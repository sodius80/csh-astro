# ServiceTitan Alternatives (2026): 6 Options Worth Comparing

ServiceTitan is serious field-service software. For a large service company, capability is rarely the question. Readiness is. The company has to be prepared for its price model, implementation work, data cleanup, office process, and management discipline.

Contractors usually search for ServiceTitan alternatives for one of two reasons. Some need scheduling, dispatch, quoting, invoicing, payments, customer communication, and reporting without buying a larger operating system too early. Others are already large enough for ServiceTitan but want a second proposal before committing.

This guide uses current official pricing pages, recent standardized CSH reviews, and fit-based buying logic. It does not crown one vendor for every contractor. ServiceTitan can be the right answer for a larger operation with call booking, memberships, payroll, technician performance, pricebook, and reporting needs. The alternatives below make more sense when the business needs a different buying path.

## 1. Jobber: best first demo for smaller service contractors

[Visit Jobber](/go/jobber-official-site/)

Jobber is usually the first ServiceTitan alternative to test when the company is not ready for an enterprise field-service rollout. It covers quotes, scheduling, dispatching, invoices, online payments, reminders, customer communication, booking, reporting, and job costing, and its buying path is easier to check before a sales call.

Current Jobber pricing lists Core at $49/month with no commitment, $39/month with a monthly one-year commitment, or $29/month with annual billing. Connect, Grow, and Plus add more users and more advanced workflows. Jobber also lists a 14-day trial with no credit card required and full Grow access during the trial.

Why it belongs on the shortlist:

- public pricing helps screen the budget before a demo
- setup is lighter than a ServiceTitan rollout
- the workflow fits many residential service teams
- the trial can be run with real jobs before a sales commitment

Where ServiceTitan may still win:

- larger dispatch centers
- deeper call booking, reporting, memberships, payroll, and commission controls
- multi-location service operations
- companies that need a true operating system beyond field-service basics

Best fit: service contractors who need a practical system now and want to wait on enterprise implementation until the business clearly needs it. See the [Jobber review](/jobber-review/) for the current plan details.

## How to run the buying split

Run the buying process in two passes. First, decide whether the company needs a lighter system, a ServiceTitan-class platform, or a middle option. Second, compare vendors that match that stage.

A small residential team should not burn weeks on enterprise demos if Jobber, Housecall Pro, or ServiceM8-style workflow solves the problem. A 30-technician business with a call center should not choose a lighter app because the first subscription quote is lower. The software has to match the operating model.

Ask every vendor for the same written answers:

1. What is the total first-year cost for the exact technician, office, and manager count?
2. What implementation work is included, and what is billed separately?
3. Which phone, SMS, marketing, payments, GPS, inventory, membership, and reporting tools are add-ons?
4. Which QuickBooks or accounting workflows are native?
5. What data can be exported if the company leaves?
6. What contract term, renewal term, and cancellation process are in the order form?
7. Which reports can an owner see without custom work?
8. Which plan contains the workflow that triggered the search?

## When ServiceTitan may still be the right answer

Do not reject ServiceTitan because it is quote-based. It may be the better fit if the company has a real call center, dedicated dispatch team, strong office management, and enough revenue impact to justify a larger implementation.

ServiceTitan deserves to stay in the final shortlist if:

- marketing attribution and call booking affect major revenue decisions
- memberships, payroll, commissions, and technician performance tracking are central to operations
- the company needs deeper reporting across departments, locations, campaigns, and technicians
- leadership is ready to clean up pricebook, accounting, customer, and job data before launch
- the written proposal makes financial sense after add-ons and implementation are included

If those conditions are not true yet, a smaller or midmarket alternative may produce a better adoption result.

## FAQ

### What is the best ServiceTitan alternative for small contractors?

Jobber is usually the best first demo for smaller residential service contractors because it has public pricing, a 14-day no-card trial, and a simpler quote, schedule, invoice, and payment workflow. Housecall Pro is the next close comparison when customer communication, reviews, booking, and residential service polish matter more.

### Which ServiceTitan alternative is closest for HVAC, plumbing, or electrical companies?

FieldEdge is the closest downmarket comparison for many HVAC, plumbing, and electrical companies because it is built around field-service dispatch, QuickBooks, agreements, pricebook, reports, and mobile licenses. Service Fusion is worth pricing if unlimited users are the bigger issue.

### Is ServiceTitan too expensive for small businesses?

Not always, but it is usually too much system for a small team that only needs quotes, dispatch, invoices, payments, and basic reporting. The issue is total adoption cost, not just subscription price. Implementation, data cleanup, office process, add-ons, and contract terms matter.

### Does ServiceTitan publish pricing?

No. The current pricing page describes per-technician pricing and Starter, Essentials, and The Works packages, but it does not publish fixed dollar amounts. Buyers need a written quote to compare it fairly against public-price alternatives.

### Should contractors replace ServiceTitan with a cheaper app?

Only if the cheaper app solves the actual workflow. A company that needs call booking, memberships, payroll, pricebook, performance reporting, and multi-location management may create more problems by underbuying. A smaller company that does not need those controls may be better off with Jobber, Housecall Pro, FieldEdge, Service Fusion, Workiz, or Zuper.

## Bottom line

Start with Jobber or Housecall Pro if the business is a smaller residential service contractor. Compare FieldEdge when you need the closest downmarket field-service option. Quote Service Fusion when user count and contract terms are the pain. Put Workiz on the list when calls, leads, and automation-heavy office workflow drive the search. Use Zuper as the flexible demo-led option for teams that want configurable field-service software. Keep ServiceTitan in the running when the business is ready for a larger operating system and the written proposal holds up against the alternatives.
