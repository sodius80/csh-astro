# ServiceTitan Alternatives (2026): 6 Options Worth Comparing

ServiceTitan is a serious field-service operating system. The issue is not whether it is capable. The issue is fit.

For many contractors, ServiceTitan enters the buying conversation before the business is ready for the cost, implementation work, process change, and office discipline that a platform like that requires. If you run a smaller service business, a growing residential team, or a company that mostly needs quotes, scheduling, dispatch, invoicing, payments, and customer communication, there are alternatives worth checking first.

## 1. Jobber — best for most smaller field-service contractors

[Visit Jobber](/go/jobber-official-site/)

Jobber is usually the first ServiceTitan alternative to check if your business is not ready for enterprise field-service software. It covers the core workflows most small and mid-sized service contractors need: quotes, scheduling, dispatching, invoices, online payments, customer communication, reminders, booking, reporting, and job costing.

Current Jobber pricing lists Core at $49/month month-to-month, $39/month with a monthly one-year commitment, or $29/month billed annually. Connect starts at $199/month month-to-month or $149/month annual and includes 5 users. Grow starts at $399/month month-to-month or $299/month annual and includes 10 users. Plus starts at $699/month month-to-month or $529/month annual and includes 15 users. The page lists a 14-day no-card trial.

Why it is a ServiceTitan alternative:

- easier buying process with published pricing
- enough operational coverage for many smaller service teams
- lower rollout burden than ServiceTitan
- quote, schedule, invoice, and payment workflow in one place

Where ServiceTitan may still win:

- larger dispatch centers
- more complex call booking and reporting
- memberships, payroll, technician performance, and multi-location controls
- businesses that need a deeper operating system, not just field-service basics

Best fit: service contractors who need a strong practical system but are not ready for a ServiceTitan-level rollout.

## 2. Housecall Pro — best customer-experience alternative

[Visit Housecall Pro](/go/housecall-pro-official-site/)

Housecall Pro is a strong alternative for residential service businesses that want online booking, customer reminders, quotes/proposals, dispatch, invoicing, payments, review management, job costing, price book, customer communication, and QuickBooks support on higher tiers.

Current pricing lists Basic at $59/month billed annually or $79 month-to-month, Essentials at $149/month annually or $189 month-to-month, and MAX at $299/month annually or $329 month-to-month. Housecall Pro advertises a 14-day no-card trial. Essentials adds QuickBooks Online/Desktop, marketing, customer equipment tracking, GPS, checklists, visual price book, and premium review management. MAX adds advanced custom reporting, onboarding, escalated support, and included sales proposal / recurring service plan tools.

Why it is a ServiceTitan alternative:

- more approachable for smaller residential teams
- strong customer communication and booking workflow
- public pricing and trial path
- good middle ground between simple scheduling software and enterprise field-service software

Where ServiceTitan may still win:

- larger teams with more complex dispatch and reporting
- call center operations
- payroll, commission, membership, and management-layer controls
- multi-branch operations

Best fit: residential service businesses that want professional field-service workflow without ServiceTitan’s buying complexity.

## 3. FieldEdge — best closest downmarket alternative

[Visit FieldEdge](/go/fieldedge-official-site/)

FieldEdge is one of the more direct ServiceTitan alternatives because it targets serious field-service companies rather than very small shops only. It is relevant for HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and similar trades that need dispatch, service agreements, QuickBooks, reporting, mobile licenses, and quote options.

FieldEdge pricing is quote-based. The pricing page lists Select, Premier, and Elite. Select includes dispatching, booking/scheduling, customer management, pricebook/flat rate, basic agreements/quotes, QuickBooks Online/Desktop, integrated payments, and 2 mobile app licenses. Premier adds advanced dispatching, multi-option quotes, advanced service agreements, 10 saved reports, prospect tracking, and 4 mobile app licenses. Elite adds unlimited saved reports, outbound call recording, Proposal Pro, MarketingEdge, consumer portal, and 6 mobile app licenses.

Why it is a ServiceTitan alternative:

- closer field-service depth than Jobber or ServiceM8
- QuickBooks Online/Desktop is listed across plans
- Premier and Elite move toward stronger reporting, agreements, proposals, and marketing workflow
- can fit businesses that need more than entry-level field service but less than ServiceTitan

Where to be careful:

- no public prices
- included mobile app licenses vary by plan
- Proposal Pro, MarketingEdge, consumer portal, and warehouse inventory details need plan-by-plan confirmation

Best fit: contractors who want a serious field-service platform but want to compare against ServiceTitan before signing a bigger proposal.

## 4. Service Fusion — best unlimited-user posture

[Visit Service Fusion](/go/service-fusion-official-site/)

Service Fusion is worth considering if ServiceTitan’s cost structure feels too heavy and you want field-service functionality with an unlimited-user posture. The current pricing page does not show the old public dollar amounts in the extracted content, so avoid stale price claims. It does say all plans include no contract requirements, personalized onboarding, unlimited support, and unlimited users.

Service Fusion lists Starter, Plus, and Pro. Starter includes customer management, estimates/jobs, scheduling/dispatching, integrated payments, QuickBooks integration, invoicing, project management, reporting, text alerts, and estimate options. Plus adds job photo uploads, inventory management, job costing, and integrated voice/text. Pro adds Open API, custom documents, e-sign documents, customer web portal, and progress/recurring invoicing.

Why it is a ServiceTitan alternative:

- unlimited-user language is attractive for growing field teams
- no-contract language lowers commitment risk if it appears in your quote
- QuickBooks, estimates, jobs, dispatch, invoices, payments, reporting, and customer management are central categories
- Pro adds API, portal, e-sign, documents, and progress/recurring invoicing

Where to be careful:

- pricing is demo-led on the current page
- GPS fleet tracking, call tracking, and other modules may be add-ons
- confirm unlimited users and contract terms in writing

Best fit: contractors that need a field-service platform but want to avoid ServiceTitan-level commitment and per-technician opacity.

Why it is a ServiceTitan alternative:

Where to be careful:

Best fit: field-service contractors who want stronger lead/call/automation tooling than entry-level apps but do not want ServiceTitan yet.

## When ServiceTitan may still be the right answer

Do not reject ServiceTitan just because it is quote-based. It may be the better fit if:

- you have a real call center or dedicated dispatch team
- you need deep reporting across technicians, campaigns, memberships, departments, and locations
- payroll, commissions, and performance tracking are central to operations
- marketing attribution and call booking affect major revenue decisions
- you have the office staff to implement and maintain the system

## Demo checklist for ServiceTitan alternatives

Use the same checklist for every vendor:

1. What is the total first-year cost, including implementation, users/technicians, add-ons, texting/phones, payments, and support?
2. How long does implementation take for a company our size?
3. Which QuickBooks or accounting workflows are native?
4. Can we export data cleanly if we leave?
5. Which features are plan-gated?
6. Are phone, SMS, GPS, marketing, inventory, service agreements, and customer portal included?
7. What contract term are we signing?
8. Which reports can owners see without custom work?

Jobber and Housecall Pro are the better first demos for smaller residential service contractors. FieldEdge is the closest downmarket field-service comparison. Keep ServiceTitan in the running only if the business is ready for a larger operating system and the written proposal makes financial sense.
