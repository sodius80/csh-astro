# Housecall Pro Alternatives (2026)

Housecall Pro is good field-service software. Contractors usually look elsewhere for a specific reason: a plan gate moved, the crew grew, QuickBooks needs changed, reporting ran thin, or the business now needs a different way to run the office and field.

This guide does not treat every tool below as better than Housecall Pro. It is a buying map for contractors who like the Housecall Pro model but need different pricing, deeper dispatch/accounting workflow, a lighter plan, or a bigger field-service platform.

## 1. Jobber - closest overall Housecall Pro alternative

[Visit Jobber](/go/jobber-official-site/)

Jobber is usually the first Housecall Pro comparison for small service shops. It covers the same day-to-day loop: quotes, scheduling, dispatching, invoicing, payments, reminders, online booking, customer communication, and reporting.

Current Jobber pricing lists Core at $49/month month-to-month, $39/month with a monthly one-year commitment, or $29/month billed annually. Connect starts at $199/month month-to-month or $149/month annual and includes 5 users. Grow starts at $399/month month-to-month or $299/month annual and includes 10 users. Plus starts at $699/month month-to-month or $529/month annual and includes 15 users. Higher tiers add automations, QuickBooks Online, job costing, advanced quote features, two-way SMS, workflow automations, pipeline, onboarding, and support benefits.

Why it is a good HCP alternative:

- same quote/schedule/invoice/payment category
- public pricing and a 14-day trial
- good fit for small residential service businesses
- strong quote follow-ups, customer communication, and online booking

Where Housecall Pro may still win:

- if you rely heavily on Housecall Pro’s customer-facing experience and current marketing/review workflow
- if your team already fits the HCP plan you are on
- if QuickBooks Desktop matters; Jobber pricing content highlights QuickBooks Online rather than Desktop

Best fit: service contractors who want Housecall Pro’s general category of software but prefer Jobber’s plan structure, interface, or quote-to-job workflow.

## 2. Service Fusion - best unlimited-user posture

[Visit Service Fusion](/go/service-fusion-official-site/)

Service Fusion is the alternative to review when Housecall Pro’s user math starts driving the bill. The current Service Fusion pricing page does not show the old public dollar amounts in the extracted content, so do not use stale flat-rate claims. What it does say matters: all plans come with no contract requirements, personalized onboarding, unlimited support, and unlimited users.

Service Fusion has Starter, Plus, and Pro tiers. Starter includes customer management, estimates/jobs, scheduling/dispatching, integrated payments, QuickBooks integration, invoicing, project management, reporting, text alerts, and estimate options. Plus adds job photos, inventory management, job costing, and integrated voice/text. Pro adds Open API, custom documents, e-sign documents, customer web portal, and progress/recurring invoicing.

Why it is a good HCP alternative:

- unlimited-user language matters as the crew and office grow
- no-contract language helps if you want less commitment risk
- covers QuickBooks, estimates, jobs, dispatch, invoices, payments, reporting, and customer management
- Pro tier has documents, e-sign, portal, and progress/recurring invoicing

Where to be careful:

- current public page is demo-led; get the exact price in writing
- GPS fleet tracking, ServiceCall.ai, and similar tools can be add-ons
- confirm whether unlimited users and no-contract terms apply to your quote

Best fit: contractors leaving Housecall Pro because user count and plan gates are pushing the cost up.

## 3. Workiz - best for phone, lead, and automation-heavy teams

[Visit Workiz](/go/workiz-official-site/)

Workiz is a strong Housecall Pro alternative when the office runs on calls, leads, scheduling, estimates, invoices, client management, online payments, local numbers, automations, and field-service communication. It is weaker as a simple cheaper replacement because the current buying path requires quote discipline.

The working pricing URL is `/pricing-plans/`, not the older `/pricing/` URL. During this update, the official page described Kickstart, Standard, Pro, and Ultimate but the extracted public page did not show fixed base dollar prices. It did show the cost drivers to pin down: Kickstart includes the first 3 users, Standard and Pro include the first 5 users, Standard extra members are listed at $46/month on annual payment or $55/month on monthly payment, Pro extra members are listed at $54/month on annual payment or $65/month on monthly payment, and Workiz Communication is sold separately.

Why it is a good HCP alternative:

- Workiz puts more weight on dispatch, phone, lead, and communication workflow
- Standard adds QuickBooks Online, custom fields, location tracking, service areas, subcontractors, and lead tracking
- Pro adds performance pay, Genius Leads, Genius Scheduling, and custom reports
- Ultimate adds service plans, sales proposals, inventory, flat rate, multi-day jobs, equipment tracking, purchase orders, and Zapier

Where to be careful:

- confirm the base package price in writing because the current public page extract did not expose fixed card prices
- Workiz Communication, card readers, phone, SMS, and AI tools can affect total cost
- extra-user pricing gets expensive quickly on larger teams

Best fit: field-service businesses that want more call, lead, dispatch, and automation workflow than HCP gives at the same budget.

## 4. FieldEdge - best QuickBooks-heavy multi-truck alternative

[Visit FieldEdge](/go/fieldedge-official-site/)

FieldEdge is not a lightweight Housecall Pro swap. It is a step into deeper field-service operations, especially for HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and other multi-truck companies that depend on QuickBooks and need stronger dispatch, agreements, reporting, and customer history.

FieldEdge pricing is quote-based. The current pricing page lists Select, Premier, and Elite. Select includes dispatching, booking/scheduling, customer management, pricebook/flat rate, agreements/quotes, QuickBooks Online/Desktop, integrated payments, and 2 mobile app licenses. Premier adds advanced dispatching, multi-option quotes, advanced service agreements, 10 saved reports, prospect tracking, and 4 mobile app licenses. Elite adds unlimited saved reports, outbound call recording, Proposal Pro, MarketingEdge, consumer portal, 6 mobile app licenses, and conditional warehouse inventory support.

Why it is a good HCP alternative:

- QuickBooks Online/Desktop is listed across FieldEdge plans
- stronger plan detail around agreements, reporting, prospect tracking, and multi-option quotes
- better fit for multi-truck service businesses than a basic plan comparison shows
- field-service academy/onboarding/support resources are included categories

Where to be careful:

- no public price table
- included mobile licenses vary by plan
- Proposal Pro, MarketingEdge, consumer portal, and warehouse inventory need plan-by-plan review

Best fit: companies outgrowing Housecall Pro because dispatch, reporting, QuickBooks, or service agreements have gotten more complex.

## 6. ServiceTitan - best bigger-system upgrade

[Visit ServiceTitan](/go/servicetitan-official-site/)

ServiceTitan is not the cheaper Housecall Pro alternative. It is the upgrade path when Housecall Pro is too light for a larger operation.

ServiceTitan’s pricing is quote-based and per-technician. The current pricing page lists Starter, Essentials, and The Works packages. Starter includes dispatching, scheduling, call booking, invoicing, and pricebook. Essentials adds mobile estimates and payroll management. The Works adds configurable payroll, advanced reporting, commission tracking, and customizable memberships.

Why it is a good HCP alternative:

- deeper call booking, dispatch, reporting, payroll, membership, and marketing/operations capabilities
- stronger fit for larger HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and multi-trade service businesses
- better when the issue is operational depth rather than price

Where to be careful:

- quote-based pricing, implementation, add-ons, and contract terms need careful review
- much heavier rollout than Housecall Pro
- too much system for many smaller contractors

Best fit: companies outgrowing HCP because they need a larger service-business operating system rather than a cheaper subscription.

## When you should stay with Housecall Pro

A lower starting price is not enough reason to switch. Stay with Housecall Pro if:

- your team fits the current plan comfortably
- the customer booking/communication/review workflow is working
- QuickBooks and equipment tracking needs are covered by your plan
- your techs already use it consistently
- the cost of retraining and data migration is higher than the plan savings

## How to shortlist Housecall Pro alternatives

Skip the feature-grid trap. Housecall Pro already covers scheduling, quotes, invoices, payments, online booking, review management, job cost tracking, price book, customer communication, and QuickBooks access on higher plans. Pick a replacement because it fixes a named problem that Housecall Pro does not solve well enough for the current business.

Write the exit reason first. If the issue is pricing at the current team size, compare Housecall Pro against Jobber, Service Fusion, and ServiceM8 with the same user count and job volume. If the issue is QuickBooks Desktop, flat-rate pricebook depth, service agreements, or office-led dispatch, compare FieldEdge and ServiceTitan. If the issue is calls, missed leads, dispatch visibility, and communication workflow, compare Workiz. If the team simply dislikes the interface, run a trial or hands-on demo before assuming a bigger system will fix adoption.

The second filter is implementation load. ServiceM8, Jobber, and Housecall Pro are easier to test because the pricing and trial paths are public. Service Fusion, FieldEdge, Workiz, and ServiceTitan require a more careful sales process. That can be fine, but bring real jobs, real QuickBooks questions, real technicians, and real cancellation questions into the demo.

The third filter is total cost. Include base subscription, users, phone or texting, payment processing, onboarding, data import, GPS, marketing, reporting, inventory, add-ons, and support. A plan can look cheaper than Housecall Pro until you add the tools that caused the search in the first place.

### Demo questions for every HCP alternative

1. Which Housecall Pro workflow are we replacing?
2. What is included at our exact user count?
3. Does QuickBooks Online or Desktop sync the way our bookkeeper needs?
4. Can technicians complete a job from dispatch to payment on their actual phones?
5. How will customer, job, invoice, note, and photo data move out of Housecall Pro?
6. What are the cancellation and renewal terms?

## Demo checklist

For every alternative, ask:

1. What is the total cost for our exact user count and job volume?
2. Which features are included vs add-on priced?
3. Does QuickBooks Online or Desktop sync the way our bookkeeper needs?
4. Can we export Housecall Pro customers, jobs, notes, photos, estimates, invoices, and payment history?
5. What does implementation require from our office team?
6. Are texting, phones, GPS, marketing, service plans, inventory, and reporting included?
7. What happens if we cancel?

## FAQ

### What is the best overall Housecall Pro alternative?

Jobber is the closest overall alternative for many small residential service businesses. It has public pricing, a 14-day trial, and a similar quote, schedule, invoice, payment, and customer communication workflow. It is not automatically better than Housecall Pro, so compare the exact plan features before switching.

### What is the cheapest Housecall Pro alternative?

ServiceM8 is the lowest published-price option in this guide, with a Free plan for one user and paid plans that start at $29/month in the USA. It is a lighter job-card system with job/month limits, so it should be tested carefully before replacing a broader Housecall Pro workflow.

### Which Housecall Pro alternative is best for many users?

Service Fusion is worth a demo when user count is the problem. Its current official page says all plans include unlimited users and that pricing stays the same whether the company has one technician and one dispatcher or a much larger office and field team. Ask for the current dollar quote and add-ons in writing.

### Which Housecall Pro alternative is best for HVAC or plumbing companies?

FieldEdge and ServiceTitan are the more serious service-trade options. FieldEdge fits QuickBooks-heavy multi-truck companies that want Select, Premier, or Elite package depth. ServiceTitan fits larger service businesses that need call booking, pricebook, dispatch, memberships, reporting, payroll, and commission controls.

### Should a small contractor leave Housecall Pro for ServiceTitan?

Usually no. ServiceTitan is quote-based and implementation-heavy. A small contractor should compare Jobber, ServiceM8, Service Fusion, Workiz, or a better-fitting Housecall Pro tier before moving to a larger operating system.

## Bottom line

Jobber is the closest overall Housecall Pro alternative. Service Fusion is worth a demo if user count is the pain. Workiz is stronger when calls, leads, and automations matter. FieldEdge is the step up for QuickBooks-heavy multi-truck operations. ServiceM8 is the lower-cost lightweight option. ServiceTitan belongs in the conversation only when the business has outgrown Housecall Pro operationally, not because the subscription feels high.
