# Housecall Pro Alternatives (2026)

Housecall Pro is good field-service software. The reason contractors search for alternatives is usually more specific: the plan gate moved, the team grew, QuickBooks needs changed, reporting is not deep enough, or the business now needs a different operating model.

This guide is not a list of tools that are always better than Housecall Pro. It is a buying map for contractors who like the idea of Housecall Pro but need a different price structure, deeper dispatch/accounting workflow, lighter pricing, or a bigger field-service platform.

## 1. Jobber — closest overall Housecall Pro alternative

[Visit Jobber](/go/jobber-official-site/)

Jobber is the closest first comparison for many contractors because it solves a similar field-service problem: quotes, scheduling, dispatching, invoicing, payments, reminders, online booking, customer communication, and reporting.

Current Jobber pricing lists Core at $49/month month-to-month, $39/month with a monthly one-year commitment, or $29/month billed annually. Connect starts at $199/month month-to-month or $149/month annual and includes 5 users. Grow starts at $399/month month-to-month or $299/month annual and includes 10 users. Plus starts at $699/month month-to-month or $529/month annual and includes 15 users. Higher tiers add automations, QuickBooks Online, job costing, advanced quote features, two-way SMS, workflow automations, pipeline, onboarding, and support benefits.

Why it is a good HCP alternative:

- same general quote/schedule/invoice/payment category
- public pricing and a 14-day trial
- good fit for small residential service businesses
- quote follow-ups, customer communication, and online booking are strong decision points

Where Housecall Pro may still win:

- if you rely heavily on Housecall Pro’s customer-facing experience and current marketing/review workflow
- if your team already fits the HCP plan you are on
- if QuickBooks Desktop is important; Jobber pricing content highlights QuickBooks Online rather than Desktop

Best fit: service contractors who want a similar category of software but prefer Jobber’s plan structure, interface, or quote-to-job workflow.

## 2. Service Fusion — best unlimited-user posture

[Visit Service Fusion](/go/service-fusion-official-site/)

Service Fusion is the alternative to review when Housecall Pro’s user math is the main issue. The current Service Fusion pricing page does not show the old public dollar amounts in the extracted content, so avoid stale flat-rate claims. What it does say is important: all plans come with no contract requirements, personalized onboarding, unlimited support, and unlimited users.

Service Fusion has Starter, Plus, and Pro tiers. Starter includes customer management, estimates/jobs, scheduling/dispatching, integrated payments, QuickBooks integration, invoicing, project management, reporting, text alerts, and estimate options. Plus adds job photos, inventory management, job costing, and integrated voice/text. Pro adds Open API, custom documents, e-sign documents, customer web portal, and progress/recurring invoicing.

Why it is a good HCP alternative:

- unlimited-user language is attractive as the team grows
- no-contract language is useful if you want less commitment risk
- QuickBooks, estimates, jobs, dispatch, invoices, payments, reporting, and customer management are core categories
- Pro tier has documents, e-sign, portal, and progress/recurring invoicing

Where to be careful:

- current public page is demo-led; get the exact price in writing
- GPS fleet tracking, ServiceCall.ai, and similar tools can be add-ons
- confirm whether unlimited users and no-contract terms apply to your quote

Best fit: contractors leaving Housecall Pro because user count and plan gates are driving the cost up.

## 3. Workiz — best for phone, lead, and automation-heavy teams

[Visit Workiz](/go/workiz-official-site/)

Workiz is a strong Housecall Pro alternative if your business cares about calls, leads, scheduling, estimates, invoices, client management, online payments, local numbers, automations, and field-service communication.

The working pricing URL is `/pricing-plans/`, not the older `/pricing/` URL. Current annual-billing cards list Kickstart at $225/month for the first 3 users, Standard at $275/month for the first 5 users, Pro at $325/month for the first 5 users, and Ultimate as custom. Standard lists additional users at $55/month on annual payment; Pro lists additional users at $65/month on annual payment. Paid plans include scheduling, automations, invoices/estimates, online payments, local number, and client management.

Why it is a good HCP alternative:

- included user counts are explicit in the pricing cards
- Standard adds QuickBooks Online, custom fields, location tracking, service areas, subcontractors, and lead tracking
- Pro adds performance pay, more automations, Genius Leads, Genius Scheduling, and custom reports
- Ultimate adds service plans, more automations, sales proposals, inventory, flat rate, multi-day jobs, equipment tracking, purchase orders, and Zapier

Where to be careful:

- the public page presents annual-billing prices; monthly pricing can differ
- communication tools and card readers can be sold separately
- additional-user pricing gets expensive quickly on larger teams

Best fit: field-service businesses that want more call/lead/automation workflow than HCP gives at the same budget.

## 4. FieldEdge — best QuickBooks-heavy multi-truck alternative

[Visit FieldEdge](/go/fieldedge-official-site/)

FieldEdge is less of a lightweight Housecall Pro replacement and more of a step into deeper field-service operations. It is especially relevant for HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and other multi-truck companies that depend on QuickBooks and need stronger dispatch, agreements, reporting, and customer history.

FieldEdge pricing is quote-based. The current pricing page lists Select, Premier, and Elite. Select includes dispatching, booking/scheduling, customer management, pricebook/flat rate, agreements/quotes, QuickBooks Online/Desktop, integrated payments, and 2 mobile app licenses. Premier adds advanced dispatching, multi-option quotes, advanced service agreements, 10 saved reports, prospect tracking, and 4 mobile app licenses. Elite adds unlimited saved reports, outbound call recording, Proposal Pro, MarketingEdge, consumer portal, 6 mobile app licenses, and conditional warehouse inventory support.

Why it is a good HCP alternative:

- QuickBooks Online/Desktop is listed across FieldEdge plans
- stronger plan language around agreements, reporting, prospect tracking, and multi-option quotes
- better fit for multi-truck service businesses than a basic plan comparison suggests
- field-service academy/onboarding/support resources are included categories

Where to be careful:

- no public price table
- included mobile licenses vary by plan
- Proposal Pro, MarketingEdge, consumer portal, and warehouse inventory are not simple across all plans

Best fit: companies outgrowing Housecall Pro because dispatch, reporting, QuickBooks, or service agreements have become more complex.

## 6. ServiceTitan — best bigger-system upgrade

[Visit ServiceTitan](/go/servicetitan-official-site/)

ServiceTitan is not a cheaper Housecall Pro alternative. It is the upgrade path when Housecall Pro is too light for a larger operation.

ServiceTitan’s pricing is quote-based and per-technician. The current pricing page lists Starter, Essentials, and The Works packages. Starter includes dispatching, scheduling, call booking, invoicing, and pricebook. Essentials adds mobile estimates and payroll management. The Works adds configurable payroll, advanced reporting, commission tracking, and customizable memberships.

Why it is a good HCP alternative:

- deeper call booking, dispatch, reporting, payroll, membership, and marketing/operations capabilities
- stronger fit for larger HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and multi-trade service businesses
- better when the issue is operational depth rather than price

Where to be careful:

- quote-based pricing, implementation, add-ons, and contract terms need careful review
- much heavier rollout than Housecall Pro
- too much system for many smaller contractors

Best fit: companies outgrowing HCP because they need a larger service-business operating system, not just a cheaper subscription.

## When you should stay with Housecall Pro

Do not switch just because another tool has a lower starting price. Stay with Housecall Pro if:

- your team fits the current plan comfortably
- the customer booking/communication/review workflow is working
- QuickBooks and equipment tracking needs are covered by your plan
- your techs already use it consistently
- the cost of retraining and data migration is higher than the plan savings

## Demo checklist

For every alternative, ask:

1. What is the total cost for our exact user count and job volume?
2. Which features are included vs add-on priced?
3. Does QuickBooks Online or Desktop sync the way our bookkeeper needs?
4. Can we export Housecall Pro customers, jobs, notes, photos, estimates, invoices, and payment history?
5. What does implementation require from our office team?
6. Are texting, phones, GPS, marketing, service plans, inventory, and reporting included?
7. What happens if we cancel?

## Bottom line

Jobber is the closest overall Housecall Pro alternative. Service Fusion is worth a demo if user count is the pain. Workiz is stronger when calls, leads, and automations matter. FieldEdge is the step up for QuickBooks-heavy multi-truck operations. ServiceM8 is the lower-cost lightweight option. ServiceTitan belongs in the conversation only if the business has outgrown Housecall Pro operationally, not just financially.
