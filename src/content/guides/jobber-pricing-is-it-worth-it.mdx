# Jobber Pricing Review (2026): Is It Worth It?

Jobber gives buyers more public pricing than many field-service platforms, but the plan table still needs careful reading. The current pricing page lists Core from $29/month on annual billing, Connect from $99/month for an individual annual plan or $149/month for a team annual plan, Grow from $149/month individual or $299/month team, and Plus from $529/month annual with 15 users included.

The Core entry price is real. It is also easy to over-read. Core can fit a solo operator. Connect is often the first plan worth pricing for a team because it adds automations, QuickBooks Online, quote and invoice follow-ups, checklists, time and expenses, and payment automation. Grow is where job costing, advanced quote customization, optional line items, two-way SMS, and custom workflow automations appear. Plus is built for larger teams that want premium support, onboarding, Marketing Suite, Receptionist, Pipeline, and API guidance.

## What Core includes and when it is enough

Core is Jobber's cheapest paid entry point. Current pricing lists it at $29/month on annual billing, $39/month with a monthly 1-year commitment, or $49/month with no commitment. Core includes one user and is positioned as the essential toolkit for a professional home service business.

The official pricing page lists online booking and scheduling, professional quotes, invoices and online payments, a professional website, reporting, and app marketplace access. That can be enough for a solo operator moving off paper estimates, manual invoices, and a basic calendar.

Core starts to feel tight when the business needs team workflow or automation. Quote follow-ups, invoice follow-ups, QuickBooks Online, checklists, time tracking, expenses, and automatic payment collection sit in Connect. Job costing, advanced quote customization, optional line items, two-way SMS, and custom workflow automations sit in Grow. If those are must-haves, the $29 price is the wrong benchmark.

Treat Core as an owner-operator plan. It can work for a lawn care operator, cleaner, handyman, pressure-washing contractor, or small trade business that wants a simple front office. It is not the full Jobber experience.

## What Connect adds and why teams start there

If more than one person is involved, Connect is usually where the buying conversation starts. Current pricing lists Connect Individual at $99/month on annual billing, $119/month with a monthly 1-year commitment, or $139/month with no commitment. Connect Team is $149/month annual, $169/month with a monthly 1-year commitment, or $199/month with no commitment, with 5 users included.

Connect matters because it moves follow-up and admin work out of someone's memory. The pricing page lists automated reminders, automatic payment collection, checklists, quote and invoice follow-ups, QuickBooks Online connection, and time and expense tracking. Those features are often the split between using Jobber as a shared digital clipboard and using it as the daily workflow for a small crew.

QuickBooks is one of the biggest plan gates. If the office needs clean accounting handoff, Connect is usually the floor. The standardized [Jobber review](/jobber-review/) notes that buyers should test sync behavior carefully because current new QuickBooks sync is commonly described as Jobber to QuickBooks for ongoing data. The integration can still be valuable, but it should be tested with real invoices, payments, customers, and service items.

Connect also earns its keep on follow-up. Many small contractors lose work because approved quotes, unpaid invoices, and customer reminders rely on memory. Automating those touches can be worth more than another report. A contractor should test quote follow-ups and invoice follow-ups during the trial before deciding whether Connect is enough.

## What Grow adds and when it pays for itself

Grow is where Jobber starts dealing with pricing choices, profit, and more advanced sales workflow. Current pricing lists Grow Individual at $149/month annual, $169/month with a monthly 1-year commitment, or $199/month with no commitment. Grow Team is $299/month annual, $349/month with a monthly 1-year commitment, or $399/month with no commitment, with 10 users included.

The official page lists advanced quote customizations, optional line items, automatic time tracking, job costing, two-way SMS, and custom workflow automations. These are not cosmetic extras. For many contractors, they decide whether Jobber can support the way the company sells and tracks work.

Job costing is usually the Grow feature that gets the closest look. It can help a service contractor see profit margin, labor, line items, and expenses at the job level. It is not the same as construction accounting or deep work-in-progress reporting, but it is often enough for a small field-service team that needs to know which services are making money.

Advanced quotes and optional line items matter when the company sells upgrades. A cleaning company might sell add-on deep cleans. A landscaper might offer mulch, trimming, and seasonal cleanup options. An HVAC or plumbing service company might present repair and replacement options. If optional upgrades regularly increase ticket size, Grow can justify its higher price.

Two-way SMS and custom workflow automations can also reduce office back-and-forth. The buyer should test whether the automations match the real customer journey: request, quote, approval, schedule, reminder, visit, invoice, payment, follow-up, and review request.

## What Plus includes and who should consider it

Plus is Jobber's highest public plan. Current pricing lists Plus at $529/month on annual billing, $599/month with a monthly 1-year commitment, or $699/month with no commitment. It includes 15 users, with additional users listed at $29/user and sales contact recommended for teams above 15.

Plus includes all Grow features plus several bundled tools and support items. The pricing page lists Jobber Marketing Suite, Jobber Receptionist, Pipeline, onboarding with a dedicated specialist, Premium Support, and guided API walkthrough for custom integrations.

Plus makes sense when the business is large enough to need support, growth tools, and a more structured rollout. A 15-user company may have office staff, field techs, managers, and sales roles using the system at the same time. At that point, onboarding and support are not fluff. They lower rollout risk.

Plus is not automatically the right plan because it has the longest feature list. If the company does not need Marketing Suite, Receptionist, Pipeline, Premium Support, or API guidance, Grow Team may be the more rational ceiling. The purchase should be tied to operating needs, not the pricing ladder.

## The 14-day trial and how to use it

Jobber's pricing page says the trial is 14 days, no credit card required, and gives access to all features on the Grow plan. That setup is useful because buyers can test the workflows that sit above Core and Connect.

The catch: a Grow trial can make Core feel smaller after signup. Before starting the trial, choose the likely target plan. If Core is the expected plan, test Core workflows. If Connect is expected, test QuickBooks, reminders, checklists, quote follow-ups, invoice follow-ups, time, and expenses. If Grow is expected, test job costing, advanced quotes, optional line items, two-way SMS, and custom automations.

Run real jobs through the trial. Use one simple service call, one estimate that needs follow-up, one invoice that needs payment reminders, and one job where cost tracking matters. Have at least one field user test the mobile app from a job site instead of only from the office.

A useful trial should prove that the office and field team will use Jobber every day without keeping a backup spreadsheet for the same work.

## Realistic Jobber budgets for contractors

A solo operator can start the budget with Core or Connect Individual. Core annual pricing is $29/month and may be enough if the business needs basic scheduling, quotes, invoices, payments, a website, reporting, and app marketplace access. Connect Individual at $99/month annual becomes more realistic if the owner needs QuickBooks, follow-ups, checklists, or time and expense tracking.

A five-person service company should price both Connect Team and Grow Team. Connect Team is $149/month annual with 5 users included. Grow Team is $299/month annual with 10 users included. The question is whether job costing, advanced quotes, optional line items, two-way SMS, and custom automations are worth the additional cost.

A ten-person team that sells add-ons or needs profitability detail should test Grow Team first. The included 10-user structure can fit a small office plus several technicians. If the company expects to use job costing and advanced quotes daily, the higher price may be easier to justify.

A 15-user or multi-location operation should compare Plus against ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro MAX, FieldEdge, and other heavier tools. Plus starts at $529/month annual, but it includes support and growth tools that may reduce the need for separate systems. The buyer should still model total cost, support needs, and implementation time.

## Jobber versus Housecall Pro pricing

Jobber and Housecall Pro both target small residential service businesses, but their price ladders work differently. Jobber has a lower public entry point with Core at $29/month annual. Housecall Pro starts at $59/month annual for Basic. That does not make Jobber cheaper for the workflow a contractor actually needs.

Housecall Pro Basic includes a different feature mix around online booking, review management, job cost tracking, and price book. Housecall Pro Essentials adds QuickBooks, equipment tracking, GPS, visual price book, and checklists. Jobber Connect and Grow split automation, QuickBooks, job costing, advanced quotes, and two-way SMS across separate steps.

Compare the workflow, not the sticker price. If the contractor needs customer communication, quote follow-ups, client hub workflow, and lighter setup, Jobber often fits well. If online booking, customer experience, review management, price book, and residential-service package controls matter more, Housecall Pro may be closer. ## Pricing traps to avoid

Don't compare Core against competitors if Core lacks the features that made Jobber attractive. Many businesses are really comparing Connect or Grow. The entry plan is useful only when the workflow is truly simple.

Don't ignore team pricing. Connect and Grow have individual and team versions. A five-person company should not budget from the 1-user individual plan unless only one person will log in. Jobber defines a user as anyone who needs to log into the system.

Don't forget annual commitment differences. Annual billing has the lowest monthly equivalent, while monthly no-commitment pricing is higher. A contractor still proving fit may accept higher monthly pricing for a short test rather than committing before rollout is proven.

Don't assume job costing solves every profitability problem. Grow adds useful job costing, but contractors with construction accounting, work-in-progress reporting, retainage, phase budgets, or complex purchase orders may still need a different system.

Don't treat add-ons and bundled tools as interchangeable. Plus includes Marketing Suite, Receptionist, Pipeline, Premium Support, and onboarding. If the company already has separate tools for those functions, the value of Plus changes.

## Buying checklist before choosing a plan

Start with the work, not the plan name. Write down the jobs Jobber must handle: lead intake, quote, approval, schedule, dispatch, reminders, field notes, photos, forms, invoice, payment, follow-up, review, QuickBooks sync, and reports.

Put each must-have feature next to a plan. Core covers the basics. Connect adds follow-ups, QuickBooks, checklists, time, expenses, and automation. Grow adds job costing, advanced quotes, optional line items, two-way SMS, and custom workflow automations. Plus adds support, onboarding, marketing, receptionist, pipeline, and API guidance.

Price the real user count. Include owner, office staff, field technicians, salespeople, managers, and any part-time admin who needs login access. Then compare that total against [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), [ServiceTitan](/servicetitan-review/), [FieldEdge](/fieldedge-review/), and other field-service platforms.

Use the trial to break the setup. Try a bad cell-service area, a customer who does not approve a quote right away, an unpaid invoice, a job with expenses, and a service call that needs photos or notes. If Jobber handles the messy examples, it will handle the easy ones.

## Bottom line

Jobber is worth it when the contractor uses the plan features every week. Core keeps the cost low for a solo operator. Connect is the practical first team plan for many small service companies. Grow is the better fit when job costing, advanced quotes, optional line items, two-way SMS, and custom automations matter. Plus is for larger teams that can use the bundled growth tools, onboarding, and support.

**CSH's call:** Most serious small teams should evaluate Connect Team and Grow Team rather than stopping at Core. Core proves Jobber can be affordable. Connect and Grow prove whether Jobber can run the business.

If the company mainly needs scheduling, quoting, customer communication, and invoices, Jobber belongs on the shortlist. If the company needs deep construction accounting, phase-based projects, enterprise dispatch, or heavy service-agreement controls, compare heavier tools before signing.

## FAQ

### How much does Jobber cost in 2026?

Jobber's current pricing page lists Core from $29/month annual, Connect from $99/month individual annual or $149/month team annual, Grow from $149/month individual annual or $299/month team annual, and Plus from $529/month annual. Monthly no-commitment pricing is higher.

### Does Jobber have a free trial?

Yes. Jobber lists a 14-day free trial with no credit card required. The trial gives access to Grow plan features, so buyers should remember which features will remain on the plan they choose.

### Which Jobber plan is best for a small team?

Connect Team is often the practical starting point because it includes 5 users and adds automations, QuickBooks Online, quote and invoice follow-ups, checklists, time, and expenses. Grow Team is better when job costing and advanced quotes matter.

### Is Jobber Core enough for contractors?

Core can be enough for a solo operator who needs basic scheduling, quotes, invoices, payments, website, reporting, and app marketplace access. It is usually too limited if the business needs QuickBooks, follow-ups, job costing, or a multi-user team workflow.

### How much are additional Jobber users?

The pricing page lists additional users at $29/user in Connect, Grow, and Plus contexts. Buyers should confirm the final user count in the checkout or quote before committing.
