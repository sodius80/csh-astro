## How to use this site

1. **Start with the workflow, not the brand.** If you are trying to fix scheduling chaos, start with scheduling or field service pages before you start comparing logos.
2. **Use reviews to understand fit.** Reviews are where the “right for / not for” line gets clearest.
3. **Use comparisons when you are down to two.** They are not broad buying guides; they are tie-breakers.
4. **Use pricing pages after fit is clear.** Cheap software that creates operational drag is still expensive.
5. **Use alternatives pages when you already know what is not working.** They are fastest when you can name the frustration first.

## Bottom line

There is no single “best contractor software” for every business. The right answer changes with trade, team size, revenue model, and whether you are doing short-cycle service work, project-based construction, or a mix of both. Use this hub as the top-level entry point, then branch into the review, comparison, or roundup that matches your actual buying problem.
