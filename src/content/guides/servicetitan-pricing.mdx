<p style="background:#fff8f0;border-left:4px solid #e67e22;padding:12px 16px;margin:16px 0;font-size:0.9em;line-height:1.6;"><strong>Pricing note:</strong> ServiceTitan does not publish public dollar amounts. Earlier versions of this guide summarized community-reported price ranges, but those figures can become stale quickly and should not be treated as current list pricing. The safest buyer advice is to use ServiceTitan's official quote process and verify every line item in writing.</p>

## Short Answer

ServiceTitan pricing is quote-based. The official ServiceTitan pricing page describes a <strong>per-technician pricing</strong> model, shows packages such as Starter, Essentials, and The Works, and uses <strong>Request Pricing</strong> calls to action instead of publishing monthly dollar amounts.

That means contractors should not compare ServiceTitan against tools like Jobber, Housecall Pro, or Service Fusion using a generic public rate. Compare your actual written ServiceTitan quote against the published competitor plan that matches your team size and workflow.

## What to Confirm in the Quote

Before signing, ask ServiceTitan sales to itemize:

<ul>
<li><strong>Base subscription:</strong> price per technician, minimum technician count, and which employees count as billable technicians.</li>
<li><strong>Package:</strong> whether the quote is for Starter, Essentials, The Works, or a custom bundle.</li>
<li><strong>Implementation/onboarding:</strong> data migration, pricebook setup, training, and launch support.</li>
<li><strong>Add-ons:</strong> marketing attribution, phones, advanced dispatch, payments, financing, payroll, reporting, and other Pro modules.</li>
<li><strong>Contract terms:</strong> initial term length, renewal language, cancellation windows, price increases, and early termination penalties.</li>
<li><strong>Payment processing:</strong> card/ACH rates, monthly minimums, and whether payment tools are required or optional.</li>
<li><strong>Integrations:</strong> accounting, payroll, inventory, call tracking, and any custom integration fees.</li>
</ul>

## Package Structure

ServiceTitan's public pricing page lists packages rather than dollar prices. It describes core capabilities such as dispatching, scheduling, call booking, invoicing, pricebook, mobile, estimates, payroll management, reporting, commission tracking, and memberships. Exact package inclusions can change, so use the quote to confirm which features are included versus sold as add-ons.

## Feature Claims Verified

The current official materials support the following high-level claims:

<ul>
<li><strong>Pricing model:</strong> per-technician pricing, sales quote required.</li>
<li><strong>Mobile:</strong> ServiceTitan's Field Mobile App supports iOS and Android and helps technicians access job details, complete forms, capture photos, create estimates, and process payments.</li>
<li><strong>Accounting:</strong> ServiceTitan publishes accounting integration materials for QuickBooks Online, QuickBooks Desktop, Viewpoint, Sage Intacct/Sage products, and related accounting workflows.</li>
<li><strong>Core FSM depth:</strong> scheduling/dispatch, invoicing/payments, inventory, marketing automation, reporting/analytics, pricebook, and service agreement workflows are part of the platform's broader positioning.</li>
</ul>

## Who ServiceTitan Usually Fits

ServiceTitan is normally a better fit for established HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and similar field-service companies with enough technicians, dispatch volume, marketing spend, and administrative capacity to benefit from enterprise-grade workflows.

It is usually a poor fit for solo operators and small shops that mainly need scheduling, estimates, invoices, payments, and customer communication. For those contractors, published-price tools like Jobber, Housecall Pro, Service Fusion, Workiz, or FieldEdge-style alternatives are easier to evaluate before a sales call.

## Bottom Line

ServiceTitan may be worth it when the operational upside from better dispatching, pricebook discipline, reporting, marketing attribution, and service agreement management exceeds the quoted first-year investment. But because there is no public dollar rate card, the only responsible pricing recommendation is: get the full written quote, compare it against your current process and alternatives, and do not treat third-party ranges as guaranteed current pricing.
