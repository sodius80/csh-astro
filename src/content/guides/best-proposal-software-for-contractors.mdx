# Best Proposal Software for Contractors (2026)

Most contractors do not need “proposal software” as a standalone category. They need one of three things:

1. a faster way to send clean estimates and collect approval,
2. a deeper estimating system that turns plan takeoffs into a professional proposal, or
3. a document workflow for longer scopes, contracts, options, and signatures.

That distinction matters because a roofing estimator, a remodeling company, and a small service contractor should not all buy the same proposal tool.

## How to choose

Before comparing products, decide what “proposal” means in your business.

- **Simple service estimate:** Use Jobber, Joist, Contractor+, or Housecall Pro-style software. You need speed, customer approval, and a path to invoice.
- **Construction estimate from plans:** Use Buildxact, JobTread, or another construction estimating platform. The proposal is only the customer-facing output; the estimate underneath matters more.
- **Long-form sales document:** Use PandaDoc or Proposify. These are stronger when the proposal needs sections, templates, terms, optional packages, e-signature, tracking, and approval workflows.
- **Roofing/exteriors proposal:** Consider Roofr, JobNimbus, AccuLynx, or the roofing-specific roundups before a generic document tool.

## Proposal demo checklist

Ask these before buying:

1. Can accepted proposals become jobs, schedules, invoices, or budgets without retyping?
2. Are e-signatures included or add-on priced?
3. Are proposal templates, optional line items, photos, and terms included in the plan you are considering?
4. Can you collect deposits or full payment from the proposal?
5. Does it sync with QuickBooks Online, QuickBooks Desktop, or your accounting system?
6. Are document sends, users, templates, or AI credits limited?
7. What happens when a proposal changes after the customer signs?
