# FollowUp CRM Alternatives for General Contractors (2026)

Most contractors shopping for a FollowUp CRM alternative are not chasing the longest feature list. They are usually trying to answer one of two questions:

1. Do we still need a construction sales CRM, just with a different setup?
2. Or have we outgrown bid follow-up and now need estimating, project management, job costing, scheduling, and client communication in the same operating system?

Those questions lead to different shortlists. FollowUp CRM is built for construction sales: bids, follow-ups, pipeline visibility, sales activity, reporting, email/calendar workflows, and construction-specific integrations. If the real pain is bid discipline, FollowUp CRM may still be the right tool. If the pain starts after the job is won, broader contractor platforms deserve the closer look.

## Should you leave FollowUp CRM?

Keep FollowUp CRM on the final shortlist if the core issue is sales discipline:

- bid dates are being missed
- estimators need reminders and a shared bid calendar
- sales managers need pipeline visibility
- email activity needs to be attached to companies, contacts, and projects
- the team needs construction-specific reporting instead of a generic CRM
- QuickBooks Online, ERP, or accounting integration is part of the sales handoff

FollowUp CRM's current pricing page lists Team, Professional, and Enterprise. Team covers construction CRM, email integration, QuickBooks Online integration, follow-ups and reminders, document storage, project tracking, a digital bid calendar, standard reporting, and live support. Professional adds automation, proposal generator/e-sign, sequences, data enhancement, ERP integration, and custom reporting. Enterprise adds advanced permissions, multiple connected systems, and shared customers.

## How to choose between CRM and operating system

Start with where the workflow breaks.

- If bids are late, follow-ups are missed, and sales managers cannot see pipeline health, stay close to FollowUp CRM.
- If estimates, budgets, change orders, purchase orders, invoices, and job costing are scattered after the job is won, look at JobTread or Contractor Foreman.
- If client selections, approvals, daily updates, and residential project communication are the issue, look at Buildertrend.
- If the company is really a service business, compare Jobber instead of forcing a GC platform into a service workflow.
- If the company is roofing or exteriors, compare JobNimbus because the sales-to-production path is trade-specific.

Do not treat this as a feature-grid exercise. A CRM replacement should improve the handoff from lead to signed work. An operating-system replacement should improve what happens after signed work becomes a job.

## Migration plan before switching

Before replacing FollowUp CRM, write a simple migration plan. Otherwise the team can buy a broader platform and drag the same messy sales process into it.

1. List the current sales stages, bid due dates, estimators, general contractors, contacts, and open opportunities.
2. Decide which fields are required in the next system and which old fields can be archived.
3. Export companies, contacts, activities, open bids, notes, files, and won/lost status.
4. Map where a won bid goes next: estimate, budget, schedule, client portal, project, invoice, or accounting.
5. Test one real opportunity from lead entry to accepted proposal and handoff.
6. Make one estimator and one project manager run the test before leadership signs.
7. Confirm cancellation, data export, onboarding, and support terms in writing.

A broader system is worth the switch only if that test run exposes a better operating process, not a longer feature menu.

## FAQ

### What is the best FollowUp CRM alternative for general contractors?

JobTread is the clearest broader alternative for general contractors that want CRM plus estimating, project management, job finances, and portals with published pricing. Buildertrend is stronger for residential builders and remodelers. Contractor Foreman is the budget-conscious construction-management option.

### Should a contractor leave FollowUp CRM for Buildertrend?

Only if the pain is broader than sales follow-up. Buildertrend makes more sense when client communication, selections, scheduling, change orders, files, financial tools, and project execution are the problem. If bid follow-up and sales reporting are still the issue, FollowUp CRM may remain a better fit.

### Is Jobber a good FollowUp CRM alternative?

Jobber is a good alternative only when the business is really a field-service operation. It fits quotes, scheduling, recurring jobs, invoices, payments, reminders, and customer communication. It is not a full general-contractor project management system.

### Which FollowUp CRM alternative has public pricing?

JobTread, Contractor Foreman, and Jobber have the clearest public pricing among the tools in this guide. Buildertrend, FollowUp CRM, and JobNimbus use request-pricing or quote-based buying paths.

### What should be tested before switching CRMs?

Test a real lead, bid, proposal, won job, handoff, customer communication, and accounting step. Also confirm data import, export, cancellation, onboarding, support, and the exact cost for the users who need access.
