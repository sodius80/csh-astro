# FollowUp CRM Alternatives for General Contractors (2026)

Most contractors looking for a FollowUp CRM alternative are not really asking, “Which CRM has the longest feature list?” They are usually asking one of two questions:

1. Do we still need a construction sales CRM, just with a different price or setup?
2. Or have we outgrown sales follow-up and now need estimating, project management, job costing, scheduling, and client communication in the same operating system?

Those are different buying decisions. FollowUp CRM is built around construction sales: bids, follow-ups, pipeline visibility, sales activity, reporting, email/calendar workflows, and construction-specific integrations. If the main pain is bid discipline, it may still be the right tool. If the pain starts after the job is won, the better alternatives are broader contractor platforms.

## Should you leave FollowUp CRM?

Stay with FollowUp CRM, or at least keep it in the final shortlist, if the core issue is sales discipline:

- bid dates are being missed
- estimators need reminders and a shared bid calendar
- sales managers need pipeline visibility
- email activity needs to be attached to companies, contacts, and projects
- the team needs construction-specific reporting instead of a generic CRM
- QuickBooks Online, ERP, or accounting integration is part of the sales handoff

FollowUp CRM’s current pricing page lists Team, Professional, and Enterprise. Team covers construction CRM, email integration, QuickBooks Online integration, reminders, document storage, project tracking, a digital bid calendar, standard reporting, and live support. Professional adds automation, proposal generator/e-sign, sequences, data enhancement, ERP integration, and custom reporting. Enterprise adds more control around permissions, multiple connected systems, and shared customers.

## 1. JobTread — best broader alternative with published pricing

[Visit JobTread](/go/jobtread-official-site/)

JobTread is the cleanest alternative when FollowUp CRM feels too sales-focused and you want one platform for estimating, customer/vendor communication, project management, job finances, and customer portals.

Current pricing is unusually clear for construction software: JobTread lists $199/month on monthly billing or $159/month annual equivalent, plus internal team-member pricing. One internal user is included. Additional internal users are priced in tiers, starting at $20/month monthly or $16/month annual for users 2–10. Vendors, subcontractors, customers, and some field crew portal users can access limited portals without counting as paid internal users.

Why it fits contractors leaving FollowUp CRM:

- sales and estimating are part of the platform, not a separate CRM silo
- estimating, budgets, job costing, change orders, purchase orders, and invoices stay closer together
- customer, vendor, and subcontractor portals are built into the workflow
- QuickBooks Online, Home Depot, EagleView, CompanyCam, and Zapier integrations are listed
- implementation, training, and support are included in the subscription

Where to be careful:

- JobTread does not offer a self-serve free trial; it points to a 30-day money-back guarantee for monthly subscriptions.
- Internal user count matters. A manager, estimator, PM, and office user can move the real monthly cost beyond the base subscription.
- It is a broader system than FollowUp CRM, so switching should include workflow cleanup, not just data import.

Best fit: general contractors, remodelers, builders, and specialty contractors that need CRM plus estimating, project management, job finances, and portals.

## 4. Jobber — best if you are more service business than general contractor

[Visit Jobber](/go/jobber-official-site/)

Jobber is not a general-contractor CRM. It is a field-service platform for quotes, scheduling, invoices, payments, customer communication, online booking, reminders, and light job operations.

That makes it a strong FollowUp CRM alternative only when the company is not really a GC workflow anymore — for example, a service division, maintenance business, landscaping company, cleaning company, handyman business, or other short-cycle residential/commercial service operation.

Current Jobber pricing starts with Core at $49/month month-to-month or $29/month on annual billing. Connect, Grow, and Plus add more users and more advanced features such as automations, QuickBooks Online, job costing, quote follow-ups, two-way SMS, pipeline, and premium support/onboarding.

Why it fits some FollowUp CRM searches:

Best fit: service contractors who thought they needed a CRM but really need scheduling, quotes, reminders, invoices, and payments.

## How to choose

Use these decision rules before booking demos:

- **If sales follow-up is still the pain:** stay close to FollowUp CRM and compare it only against construction CRM tools.
- **If estimating and project financials are the pain:** look at JobTread, Contractor Foreman, or Buildxact.
- **If client-facing project communication is the pain:** look at Buildertrend.
- **If short-cycle service operations are the pain:** look at Jobber or Housecall Pro, not GC software.
- **If roofing/exteriors production is the pain:** look at JobNimbus or AccuLynx.

## Demo checklist

Ask every vendor the same questions:

1. What is the total monthly/annual price for our exact user count?
2. Is onboarding included or separate?
3. What integrations are native, and which require Zapier, API work, or middleware?
4. Can we import companies, contacts, open bids, estimates, notes, documents, and activity history?
5. Where do accepted bids go next: job, budget, schedule, invoice, client portal, or accounting?
6. Which features are plan-gated?
7. What happens if we cancel — can we export our data cleanly?

## Bottom line

If the real problem is bid follow-up, FollowUp CRM still deserves a spot in the final shortlist. If the business now needs more than bid tracking, JobTread is the clearest broader alternative, Buildertrend is stronger for residential builders/remodelers, Contractor Foreman is the budget-friendly construction-management pick, Jobber fits service businesses, and JobNimbus fits roofing/exterior teams.
