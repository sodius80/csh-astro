<p>Most pest control companies do not need the most advanced software platform on the market.</p>

<p>What they usually need is more practical than that:</p>

<ul>
<li>route optimization that actually saves time instead of adding clicks</li>
<li>a clean way to move from lead to quote to recurring service agreement</li>
<li>automated customer communication without manual follow-ups after every job</li>
<li>pest-specific features like IPM tracking, termite inspections, and chemical compliance</li>
</ul>

<p>That is a smaller problem than a lot of "best pest control software" articles make it sound like.</p>

<p>If you are still running a solo operation doing mostly one-off residential calls, a calendar and QuickBooks might still be enough. A lot of contractors do not really have a software problem yet. They have a consistency problem.</p>

<p>But dedicated pest control software starts to make sense when one or more of these becomes true:</p>

<ol>
<li>jobs get double-booked or lost between phone calls, texts, and paper schedules</li>
<li>you need to track chemical applications and service routes for compliance</li>
<li>you are manually sending reminders and follow-ups after every job</li>
<li>commercial accounts with recurring service agreements are becoming a meaningful revenue stream</li>
<li>you are adding technicians and your current process stops scaling cleanly</li>
</ol>

<p>That is the frame I used for this guide.</p>

<p>I reviewed current pricing, compared feature sets, and cross-checked review patterns from G2, Capterra, and pest control industry discussions to see which tools actually fit small pest control companies versus which ones mainly fit larger operations.</p>

<p>Before getting into the product-by-product breakdown, this is the decision framework that matters most for pest control companies.</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Do you need a pest-specific system or a general field-service tool?</strong> If your main need is scheduling, quoting, invoicing, and customer communication, a cleaner general tool is often enough. If IPM tracking, termite inspections, and chemical compliance are core to your business, pest-specific depth matters more.</li>
<li><strong>Are you optimizing for transparent pricing or industry depth?</strong> GorillaDesk gives you clear pricing and quick setup. PestPac and FieldRoutes ask for a sales conversation but deliver pest-specific workflows that generalist tools cannot match.</li>

## 1. PestPac by WorkWave — Best for Mid-to-Large Pest Control Operations

<p>If you run a pest control company with 10+ technicians and need deep industry-specific features, PestPac is the platform that Terminix, Arrow Exterminators, and Clark Pest Control all trust.</p>

<p>It covers everything: route optimization, IPM (Integrated Pest Management) support, termite inspection modules, mobile sketch tools for mapping treatment areas on satellite imagery, digital forms, and a built-in marketing automation engine. The Multi-Branch Management feature on the Enterprise tier is what separates it from the rest — if you run multiple locations, this is built for it.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The Mobile Sketch feature is genuinely useful for pest control — technicians add diagrams to satellite imagery on-site to track sightings, treatments, and materials used. That is pest-specific functionality generalist platforms do not have. And the IPM and termite inspection modules mean compliance workflows are baked in, not bolted on.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> All pricing is custom. You will not know what it costs until you talk to a rep, which is a time investment before you even know if it fits your budget. Multiple reviewers on Capterra noted the software has "a ton of functionality, but requires highly trained staff" and is not intuitive out of the box. At least one reviewer documented compliance gaps where the system allows selecting chemical products with pest species they are not labeled for — a serious issue. Users migrating from the discontinued WorkWave Service also reported unfulfilled data migration promises.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom — requires consultation. Three tiers: Emerging (small ops), Growing (mid-tier), Enterprise (multi-branch).</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Mid-to-large pest control operations (10+ technicians) that need pest-specific features like IPM support, termite inspections, and multi-branch management.</p>

## 2. FieldRoutes — Best for Growing Pest Control Companies That Want Brand Recognition

<p>FieldRoutes rebranded from PestRoutes, and that legacy gives it strong name recognition in the pest control space. The platform is purpose-built for pest and lawn care — not a generalist field service tool trying to bolt on a pest module.</p>

<p>It includes route optimization, lead management, a customer payment portal, automated communication and reminders, and Sentricon integration — the industry-standard termite baiting system. Having Sentricon built into your scheduling and reporting workflow saves real time in the field for companies that do termite work.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The platform is purpose-built for pest control, and that shows. Sentricon integration is a differentiator that matters for termite-focused companies. The 337 reviews on Capterra averaging around 4.2/5 suggest a solid product, and the vendor claims customers see an average 21% revenue growth within 12 months — take that as marketing, not fact, but the review numbers back up general satisfaction.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Like PestPac, pricing is entirely custom — no demo, no pricing, no trying it yourself before you commit to a sales conversation. The Growth and Corporate packages list identical features on the pricing page, and it is not obvious what pushes you from one tier to the other. No free trial is visible.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom — requires demo. Two tiers: Growth (small businesses), Corporate (scaling operations).</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Growing pest control companies (5 to 20 technicians) that want pest-specific software with strong routing and communication features.</p>

## 3. GorillaDesk — Best Affordable All-Around for Small Crews

<p>At $49/month for the Essential tier, GorillaDesk is the most affordable option on this list with fully transparent pricing — no demo required, no sales call needed. With a 4.8/5 rating from 274 Capterra reviews, it is clearly the crowd favorite.</p>

<p>It covers the full workflow: CRM, invoicing, scheduling, dispatching, route optimization, customer portal, mobile app, GPS tracking, and material tracking. VoIP and SMS are built in — a feature that costs extra on most other platforms.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The pricing is transparent and affordable. You know exactly what you will pay before you sign up, and you can start with a free trial. The 4.8/5 rating across 274 reviews is the highest on this list, and the platform includes enough features to run a small pest control business end to end without needing add-ons.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> GorillaDesk is a multi-trade platform — it works for pest control, lawn care, tree service, cleaning, handyman, and more. That means it does not have IPM modules, termite inspection workflows, or chemical compliance tracking. If you run a serious pest control operation with regulatory requirements, you will outgrow it faster than PestPac or FieldRoutes. The tier feature differentiation on the pricing page is also vague beyond capacity differences.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Essential $49/mo | Professional $99/mo | Enterprise $149/mo. Annual plans available at roughly 8% savings.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small pest control crews (1 to 5 technicians) and owner-operators who want affordable, transparent pricing with a free trial and quick setup.</p>

## 4. Briostack — Budget Option, But Read the Reviews First

<p>Briostack is owned by EverPro/EverCommerce, which gives it the backing of a larger software ecosystem. The platform offers CRM, scheduling and routing, bids and diagramming, and marketing add-ons.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> It is a pest and lawn care specific platform backed by a larger company, which means more resources behind development than a standalone startup. Starting around $50/mo based on Capterra data, it is competitively priced.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> There are significant red flags here. The pricing page returns a 404 error — if a software company cannot keep their own pricing page live, that is a concern. The "Brio Tech" mobile app sits at 1.4/5 on the App Store (10 ratings) and 2.9/5 on Google Play (15 reviews), with complaints about crashes, login bugs, and GPS failures. Capterra shows 4.1/5 (69 reviews) while G2 shows just 2.3/5 (8 reviews) — that gap suggests review selection bias. And with only 1 to 2 native integrations, the ecosystem is very limited.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom — quote-based, approximately $50/mo starting per Capterra data.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Honestly, the app store ratings and pricing page being down are hard to ignore. If you are already in the EverPro ecosystem, it might make sense. Otherwise, look at GorillaDesk for a similar price point with much stronger reviews.</p>

## 5. FieldProxy — Hard to Recommend for US-Based Operations

<p>FieldProxy positions itself as a premium field service management platform with AI-powered routing and operations automation. But the numbers do not support it for US pest control companies.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The pricing model is per-jobs-per-month rather than per-user, which could work for companies with many technicians but lower job volumes. At 600 jobs/month on the Growth tier, that works out to about $1.42 per job if you are running at capacity.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> $449/month billed yearly is the floor, plus a $2,000+ implementation fee. Eleven of 12 G2 reviews are from Asia, and the 3 Capterra reviews are all from 2022. There is no meaningful evidence this platform works for US-based pest control operations. There is no monthly billing option — you are locked into a yearly commitment before you have tested the product. And the pricing page is currently blocked by a Vercel security checkpoint.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Essentials $449/mo (billed yearly, 300 jobs) | Growth $849/mo (billed yearly, 600 jobs) | Custom enterprise. Plus $2,000+ implementation fee.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> I cannot recommend this for US-based pest control companies at this time. The review data does not support it and the pricing is too high for the level of market validation available.</p>

## FAQ

### What is the best pest control software for small businesses?

<p>GorillaDesk at $49/month is the most affordable and accessible option for small pest control businesses. It has transparent pricing, a free trial, and covers the core workflow — scheduling, invoicing, routing, and a mobile app. If you need pest-specific features like IPM tracking, PestPac is the industry standard, but you will need to contact them for pricing.</p>

### How much does pest control software cost?

<p>Pricing ranges from $49/month (GorillaDesk Essential) to $449+/month (FieldProxy). PestPac and FieldRoutes use custom pricing — based on industry reports, expect $200 to $600/month for mid-size operations. Briostack starts around $50/month based on Capterra data, though their pricing page is currently down.</p>

### Do pest control companies need route optimization software?

<p>If you have 2 or more technicians covering multiple jobs per day, route optimization saves real time and fuel. The best pest control platforms (PestPac, FieldRoutes, GorillaDesk) include built-in route optimization. If you are using manual planning, you are probably leaving 30 to 60 minutes per technician per day on the table.</p>

### Can pest control software handle termite inspections and compliance?

<p>PestPac is the strongest option here — it has dedicated termite inspection modules, IPM support, mobile sketch tools for mapping treatment areas, and chemical compliance tracking. FieldRoutes includes Sentricon integration. GorillaDesk, Briostack, and FieldProxy do not have pest-specific compliance features.</p>
