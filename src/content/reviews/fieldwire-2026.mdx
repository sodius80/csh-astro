## What Fieldwire Gets Right

**Mobile plan viewing that actually works**

Fieldwire built their platform mobile-first, and it shows. Workers can pull up plans on tablets or phones, zoom into specific areas, and see markup layers without the lag that plagues desktop-focused competitors. The app automatically downloads plan versions so field crews always work from current drawings, not the outdated set someone printed last month.

Major GCs like EllisDon use Fieldwire specifically for QA/QC because the mobile plan access prevents the common problem of crews working from superseded drawings. When plans update, everyone gets the notification immediately.

**Offline access for real job sites**

Here's where Fieldwire separates from cloud-only tools. The mobile app works with 80% functionality when cell coverage drops to nothing. Workers can mark up plans, create tasks, take photos, and log progress. When connectivity returns, everything syncs automatically.

For subcontractors working in basements, rural sites, or concrete buildings that kill cell signals, this offline capability prevents lost productivity. Teams don't stand around waiting for data connections to load the latest drawings.

**Task assignment tied to drawings**

Instead of generic to-do lists, Fieldwire lets supervisors pin tasks directly to specific locations on plans. A plumbing foreman can mark "check pressure test on 2nd floor bathroom" right on the plan drawing. Workers see the task location visually, not just in text descriptions.

The system auto-hyperlinks uploaded plans, meaning you can reference "see sheet A-101" in a task and workers tap to jump directly to that drawing. Small detail, but it saves time when coordinating multi-trade work.

**Legitimate free plan**

Fieldwire's Basic plan costs nothing and includes unlimited projects with up to 3 users and 100MB storage. That's enough for small crews to test the system or handle simple coordination jobs. Most "free" construction software either limits you to one project or gives you a 30-day trial, then cuts you off.

## Where Fieldwire Falls Short

**Per-user pricing hurts growing teams**

Fieldwire charges $44 per user per month on their Pro plan. A 5-person crew pays $220 monthly. A 10-person team hits $440 monthly. For companies adding seasonal workers or managing multiple trades, the math gets expensive fast.

Competitors like Contractor Foreman charge one flat rate for unlimited users. If your team fluctuates between 5-15 people, Fieldwire's per-seat model penalizes growth.

**Change orders stuck on web app**

Field crews can create and assign tasks from mobile devices, but change orders require logging into the web application. This creates friction when supervisors spot scope changes on-site but can't document them immediately. They either wait until returning to the office or try to use the web interface on a phone, which defeats the mobile-first design.

Users consistently flag this limitation in reviews. When you're built for mobile field work, requiring desktop access for change documentation breaks the workflow.

**Not a business management system**

Fieldwire handles plan coordination and task tracking well, but offers no estimating, job costing, or financial management. You can't create quotes, track material costs, or generate invoices. It connects to some accounting systems, but those integrations are limited compared to all-in-one platforms.

Contractors who want one system for everything from bidding to billing need to look elsewhere. Fieldwire assumes you handle business operations in other software and just need field coordination.

**Free plan limitations bite quickly**

The Basic plan's 3-user and 100MB limits sound reasonable until you try coordinating a real project. Most construction plans exceed 100MB once you include all drawings, specs, and photos. Adding a fourth worker means upgrading to Pro at $44 per user monthly.

The free plan works for testing Fieldwire or very simple projects, but growing teams hit the walls fast.

## Final Verdict

**Fieldwire succeeds as a focused mobile coordination tool, not a complete business system.** The mobile-first design, offline functionality, and visual task management solve real field coordination problems. Teams working from trucks, basements, or remote sites benefit from having current plans and task lists accessible without reliable internet.

The per-user pricing model and lack of financial features limit Fieldwire's appeal for growing teams or contractors wanting consolidated business management. But for subcontractors and field crews who handle estimating and accounting elsewhere, Fieldwire delivers exactly what they need: reliable access to plans and tasks where the work actually happens.

Choose Fieldwire when mobile plan access and offline capability matter more than all-in-one business features. Skip it if you want estimating, job costing, or prefer flat-rate pricing that doesn't penalize team growth.
