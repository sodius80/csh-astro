Its strongest fit is a contractor that needs current drawings, plan markups, tasks, photos, punch lists, inspections, RFIs, and submittals in the hands of field teams. It is not trying to be a full estimating, dispatch, payroll, or accounting platform.</p>

<p>The 2026 audit changed the shape of this review. Fieldwire's official pricing and help pages now show a more complete product than the old thin review suggested: QuickBooks Online is listed as an integration, Business Plus includes RFIs/submittals/change orders/budget, and the current pricing page gives clear per-user prices. The caution is still real: the free Basic tier is capped tightly, paid plans are per user, and several serious project-management features sit only on Business Plus.</p>

<h2>Feature Deep Dive</h2>

<h3>Plan Viewing, Sheets, Files, and Photos</h3>

<p>Fieldwire starts with drawings. The Basic plan includes plan viewing, task management, specifications, files/photos, and checklists. That is enough to test whether your team likes the product, but the Basic account ceiling is small: 5 users, 3 projects, and 100 sheets.</p>

<p>Paid plans remove the published project and sheet caps. Pro, Business, and Business Plus all allow unlimited projects and sheets, which is a major reason the paid tiers make sense once you have real plan sets, multiple jobs, or more than a few people in the field.</p>

<p>The official pricing matrix lists files and photos across all plans, but the public plan limits are framed around users, projects, and sheets rather than a simple file-storage number. Business and Business Plus add more controls around the document layer, including app integrations and file access control. If your team uploads heavy photo/video records, confirm storage, export, and retention expectations before rolling it out company-wide.</p>

<h3>Tasks, Punch Lists, and Inspections</h3>

<p>Tasks are the core work-tracking unit in Fieldwire. They can be created from the Plans tab, pinned to a location, and filled with messages, attachments, photos, checklists, priority, assignee, category, status, and other task data. That works well for punch items, QA/QC notes, safety observations, and short-cycle field work.</p>

<p>Fieldwire does not need a separate punch-list module to be useful for closeout. Pro and higher tiers add reports and exports, including punch-list reporting. Business adds custom task fields and custom forms, which gives teams more structure for inspections, safety audits, daily reports, timesheets, and QA/QC documentation.</p>

<p>The practical question is not "Can Fieldwire track punch and inspections?" It can. The question is which plan gives you the structure you need. A small team can use Basic tasks and checklists. A contractor that needs formal inspection forms, signatures, and repeatable templates should expect to evaluate Business or Business Plus.</p>

<h3>Forms</h3>

<p>Custom forms are a Business and Business Plus feature. Fieldwire's help center says forms can be filled out, signed off, and submitted on both web and mobile. Form templates can cover daily reports, timesheets, inspection requests, safety audits, time-and-material tags, job hazard analysis, QA/QC inspections, and similar field documents.</p>

<p>This is one of the clearest paid-tier breakpoints. If your crew only needs plan-based tasks and photos, Pro may be enough. If your office wants standard field forms and inspection records, Business is the more realistic entry point.</p>

<h3>RFIs, Submittals, Change Orders, and Budget</h3>

<p>Business Plus is the project-management tier. It adds RFIs, full submittal management, change orders, and budget. That makes Fieldwire more credible for GCs and commercial specialty contractors than the old review suggested, but it also means the most important project-management modules are not on the lower paid tiers.</p>

<p>The distinction inside submittals matters. Fieldwire's public pricing matrix shows submittal extractor on paid tiers above Basic, while full submittal management is Business Plus. In plain English: lower paid plans may help extract a log from specifications, but Business Plus is the tier for managing submittal workflow.</p>

<p>Change orders are also Business Plus, and Fieldwire's help center currently notes that change orders are available only on the web version. That is a real workflow issue for field supervisors who spot scope changes onsite and expect the phone app to carry the full change-order process.</p>

<h3>Mobile and Offline Work</h3>

<p>Fieldwire's iOS and Android apps work online or offline for project content that has already been downloaded onto the device. Users can view downloaded content and add new content while offline; those updates sync back to the web version once the device is connected again and the mobile app is open.</p>

<p>There are two important limits. First, users need to be logged into the mobile app before losing connection because offline login is not available. Second, Fieldwire says offline functionality is not available on the Windows app and cannot be used with Surface Pro tablets. For iPad, iPhone, and Android field teams, the offline story is a genuine strength. For Windows tablet teams, it is a mismatch.</p>

<p>Smart Sync and selective sync also matter. Fieldwire can download a lot of plan, photo, video, and file data. Before a remote job or basement workday, crews need the right projects and sheets synced, and the device needs enough local storage.</p>

<h3>Integrations</h3>

<p>The current integration story is broader than the old article claimed. Fieldwire's official integration reference lists cloud and office tools such as Box, Dropbox, Egnyte, Google Drive, Google Calendar, Google Sheets, Microsoft OneDrive, Outlook, Excel, Power BI, Project, SharePoint, Teams, Smartsheet, and DocuSign. It also lists construction or business tools such as 4PS by Hilti, Hilti On!Track, QuickBooks Online, Viewpoint Spectrum, Viewpoint Vista, Truebuilt, and API-based connections.</p>

<p>That said, an integration list is not the same as deep accounting. Fieldwire remains a field and project-management tool. If QuickBooks Online is central to your books, verify exactly which records sync, whether the connection is native or needs setup, and how cost codes, change orders, budget data, and invoices flow before you choose it as the middle of your tech stack.</p>

<h3>Autodesk and Hilti Relationship</h3>

<p>Fieldwire is Fieldwire by Hilti. Hilti announced its acquisition of Fieldwire in 2021 for approximately $300 million, after previously investing in Fieldwire. That ownership matters because Fieldwire now sits inside Hilti's broader construction software direction.</p>

<p>Fieldwire is not an Autodesk product. Autodesk Build and Autodesk Construction Cloud are separate products to compare, especially if your team is coming from PlanGrid or already works inside Autodesk's design and BIM tools. Treat Fieldwire vs Autodesk as a product-fit decision, not a parent-company relationship.</p>

<h2>Where Fieldwire Falls Short</h2>

<h3>Basic Is More of a Test Bed Than an Operating Plan</h3>

<p>The free plan is useful because it is real: 5 users, 3 projects, and 100 sheets. But that ceiling is easy to hit on commercial work. One drawing set plus a few revisions can consume much of the sheet allowance. A contractor with multiple active jobs will likely need paid seats quickly.</p>

<h3>Business Plus Holds the Serious PM Modules</h3>

<p>Forms starting on Business is reasonable. RFIs, full submittal management, change orders, and budget sitting on Business Plus is a bigger buying decision. If you need only drawings and tasks, Fieldwire can be a lower-cost field tool. If you need the full project-management stack, price the Business Plus tier from the beginning.</p>

<h3>Change Orders Are Web-Only</h3>

<p>The current help documentation says change orders are available only on the web version. That does not make the module useless, but it creates friction for teams that expect the supervisor's phone or tablet to capture the entire change-order workflow onsite.</p>

<h3>It Is Not a Full Business System</h3>

<p>Fieldwire is not the tool I would choose for estimating, bid management, customer CRM, service dispatch, payroll, invoicing, or accounting as the main workflow. QuickBooks Online being listed as an integration is helpful, but it does not turn Fieldwire into a financial operating system. Most contractors will run Fieldwire beside accounting and preconstruction tools.</p>

<h2>Bottom Line</h2>

<p>Fieldwire is recommended when the central problem is field coordination: current plans, plan-based tasks, punch lists, photos, forms, RFIs, submittals, and jobsite records. The mobile/offline story is a real advantage for iOS and Android crews that prepare their projects before going into low-signal areas.</p>

<p>The buying caution is plan fit. Basic is useful but capped. Pro is for drawings, tasks, reports, and sheets at scale. Business is the jump for forms and integrations. Business Plus is where RFIs, submittal management, change orders, and budget arrive. If those modules matter, price Business Plus from day one instead of starting with the lower tiers and hoping they will be enough.</p>

<p>Choose Fieldwire if you want field crews working from current drawings with clear task ownership and structured documentation. Skip it if your bigger pain is estimating, dispatch, payroll, or accounting. In that case, compare a broader contractor platform first and add Fieldwire only if plan-based field coordination is still a separate gap.</p>
