<p>Tallie is an expense management system aimed at small-to-mid service businesses that need to automate receipts, approvals, and mileage tracking without dealing with the complexity of larger enterprise platforms. For contractors who have employees driving between jobs, buying materials, and submitting reimbursement requests, it removes a real back-office headache.</p>

<div style="background:#f4f5f7;border-left:4px solid #1C2B4B;padding:16px 20px;margin:24px 0;border-radius:0 8px 8px 0;">
<p style="margin:0 0 8px 0;"><strong style="color:#1a7a3f;">Right for:</strong> Contractors with 3-20 employees who submit receipts and mileage regularly, especially those already using QuickBooks and wanting to eliminate manual expense report headaches.</p>
<p style="margin:0;"><strong style="color:#c0392b;">Not for:</strong> Solo operators with no employees, or contractors who need expense management bundled into a broader job management or field service platform.</p>

<h2>What Tallie Gets Right</h2>

<p><strong>Receipt capture actually works.</strong> Tallie's mobile app lets employees photograph receipts and uses OCR to extract the relevant data automatically. The date, merchant, and amount populate into the expense report without manual typing. Contractors who have dealt with crumpled receipts in truck cupholders know how much time this saves when it actually functions, and user feedback indicates Tallie does.</p>

<p><strong>QuickBooks sync is the real draw.</strong> This is the integration that gets mentioned most often in positive reviews. Tallie connects bi-directionally with QuickBooks Desktop and Online, syncing expenses, customers, classifications, and bills automatically. Users report that the connection is stronger than many competing expense tools, reducing the month-end reconciliation work that usually falls on the owner or bookkeeper.</p>

<p><strong>Mileage tracking fits contractor workflows.</strong> Unlike generic mileage apps, Tallie includes GPS-enabled trip logging tied to expense reports. Contractors driving between residential jobs, supply houses, and the office can log miles within the same system where they submit receipts. The IRS rate calculation is built in, and the mileage data flows to QuickBooks for tax purposes.</p>

<p><strong>All features at every tier.</strong> Tallie does not gate capabilities behind higher-priced plans. Approval workflows, accounting integrations, mobile receipt capture, and multi-user policies are available on the entry plan. The only variable is user count. Contractors appreciate knowing they are not paying extra for basic functionality.</p>

<h2>Where Tallie Falls Short</h2>

<p><strong>The $50 monthly minimum penalizes very small teams.</strong> If you have two people submitting expenses occasionally, you are still paying $50 per month. That math works better for teams of 5-10 where the per-user cost drops closer to the actual $9 rate. Solo operators or husband-and-wife teams should probably stick to a simple spreadsheet or manual QuickBooks entry.</p>

<p><strong>Reporting depth is adequate, not exhaustive.</strong> Tallie handles basic expense categorization, merchant breakdowns, and export to spreadsheet formats. Users who need custom dashboards, accrual-based expense forecasting, or deep analytics sometimes find the reporting layer thin. For most contractor bookkeeping needs, it is sufficient. If you have a dedicated finance person managing the business, they may want more.</p>

<p><strong>It is an expense tool, not a job management system.</strong> Tallie does not track job budgets, material costs, or project profitability. It handles the back-office expense and reimbursement workflow well, but contractors who need expense data tied directly to individual job estimates or work orders will need to integrate it with their field service or project management system. Tallie is a layer on top of your accounting software, not a replacement for your core operational tools.</p>
