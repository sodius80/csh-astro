<p>Clean Smarts is the kind of software that makes you wonder why commercial cleaning companies put up with so many separate tools. It combines scheduling, time tracking, inspections, supply management, and team communication into one platform with transparent pricing that you can see before you book a demo - something surprisingly rare in this space.</p>

<p>I spent time researching Clean Smarts for this review, looking at what it offers, how it compares to alternatives, and whether it delivers on the promise of replacing redundant software. The company claims an average savings of $470 per month by eliminating overlapping tools, and with Foundation starting at $125/month for 10 users, the math has a reasonable starting point.</p>

<p><strong>Third-Party Rating:</strong> Clean Smarts holds a 4.8 out of 5 rating on the Apple App Store from 1,200+ reviews - an impressive signal of user satisfaction from the people using the app daily. Capterra shows 5 out of 5 but from only 2 reviews, which is too small a sample to draw conclusions from. The company reports 94% customer retention after six months, which aligns with the App Store sentiment but is self-reported.</p>

<h2>Feature Deep Dive</h2>

<h3>Time Tracking with GPS</h3>
<p>Employees clock in and out from their mobile phones with location-based check-ins. This eliminates buddy punching and gives office managers a real-time view of who is on site. Managers can approve time entries directly, and the data flows into the payroll add-on or can be exported for external payroll processing. The GPS geofencing is practical - it confirms a cleaner is at the correct job site without being overly intrusive.</p>

<h3>Inspections with Weighted Scoring</h3>
<p>Quality control is a core selling point for Clean Smarts. Inspectors can walk a job site, score completed tasks against weighted criteria, and log issues with photos. The inspection data builds a performance history over time that you can share with clients through the white-labeled customer portal. For cleaning companies that contract with large corporate clients (Verizon, Coca-Cola, American Airlines are listed customers), this inspection trail is essential for contract compliance.</p>

<h3>Supply Management</h3>
<p>Tracking cleaning supplies across multiple job sites is a logistical headache that Clean Smarts addresses directly. The platform tracks inventory levels, automates reorder reminders, and lets cleaners submit supply requests from the mobile app. Managers get visibility into which sites are consuming supplies faster than expected - useful data for both cost control and client billing.</p>

<h3>Scheduling and Team Communication</h3>
<p>The drag-and-drop scheduler handles shift changes, sick-day replacements, and recurring schedules with real-time notifications to affected employees. The in-app messaging keeps communication in one place rather than mixing with personal texts. Group messaging and direct messaging are both supported, with separate customer-facing message boards for client communication.</p>

<h2>What Clean Smarts Gets Right</h2>

<h3>Transparent pricing in a category that hides it</h3>
<p>Most janitorial software vendors require a demo call to see pricing. Clean Smarts publishes both tiers - Foundation at $125/month for 10 users and Expert at $200/month for 10 users - plus the per-user add-on rates. Annual billing gets you 20% off. The payroll add-on pricing is also public: $60/month + $6 per employee. This makes evaluation faster and more honest. You know what you are looking at before you talk to a salesperson.</p>

<h3>Mobile-first experience that employees actually use</h3>
<p>The 4.8 rating with 1,200+ reviews on the Apple App Store is not typical for B2B janitorial software. It suggests the mobile app is genuinely built for the field employee experience, not just a web portal squeezed into a phone screen. GPS check-ins, task lists, supply requests, and inspections all work from the phone. When employees adopt the tool willingly, the office gets cleaner data.</p>

<h3>White-labeled customer portal</h3>
<p>Clean Smarts lets you brand the customer portal with your company name, so clients see your brand - not Clean Smarts - when they check inspection reports, submit service requests, or view schedules. For cleaning companies competing on professionalism and transparency, this is a meaningful differentiator. Public surveys and revenue reports add to the client-facing toolkit.</p>

<h2>Where Clean Smarts Falls Short</h2>

<h3>Company size versus client scale</h3>
<p>Clean Smarts lists Verizon, Coca-Cola, Wells Fargo, and American Airlines as customers. Those are demanding enterprise clients. But the company itself has 2-10 employees according to LinkedIn. There is a mismatch here. For a small cleaning company with 20 employees, a 10-person vendor is fine. For a company managing contracts with Fortune 500 clients, the support infrastructure of a tiny software vendor is a real consideration - what happens if their only support person is out sick?</p>

<h3>Third-party validation is thin</h3>
<p>Two Capterra reviews is not enough for due diligence. The App Store reviews are encouraging but come from a consumer app store, not a business software review platform. The 94% retention rate is self-reported. For a product at this price point, the lack of independent business reviews is an obstacle for risk-averse buyers who need social proof before committing.</p>

<h3>Payroll is an add-on, not included</h3>
<p>The payroll module costs $60/month plus $6 per employee on top of the base platform. There is also a $200 one-time fee if you transfer mid-year from another provider. Payroll is a table-stakes feature for janitorial software - most platforms in this category either include it or charge less for it. The add-on pricing plus per-employee cost adds up quickly. A 50-person cleaning company on Foundation would pay $125 + $60 + (50 x $6) = $485/month with payroll included.</p>

<p>Clean Smarts is a solid choice for small-to-mid commercial cleaning companies that want one platform to replace the typical stack of separate scheduling, time tracking, and inspection tools.
