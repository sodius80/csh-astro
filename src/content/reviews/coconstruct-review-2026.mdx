## What Happened to CoConstruct

<p>Buildertrend acquired CoConstruct in February 2021. CoConstruct's own migration page says customers can continue CoConstruct projects and current pricing during the first phase, then move to an appropriate Buildertrend package with preferred customer pricing after the migration period.</p>

<p>The important correction: this should not be framed as simply "CoConstruct does not exist." CoConstruct pages and help content still exist, and the migration page says users retain access to active/current projects and historical project information. The safer conclusion is that CoConstruct is a legacy/unified product path, not a clean standalone choice for new buyers.</p>

## What CoConstruct Users Need to Verify

<p><strong>Pricing after migration</strong> - CoConstruct says customers move to an appropriate Buildertrend package with preferred customer pricing, but it does not publish a simple public price sheet for the final migrated account. Do not assume old CoConstruct prices or old Buildertrend tier prices are still current.</p>

<p><strong>New-project rules</strong> - The migration page says projects can still be added in CoConstruct during transition, but suggests beginning new projects in Buildertrend. After fully committing to Buildertrend, new projects can no longer be added into CoConstruct while historical access remains.</p>

<p><strong>Workflow changes</strong> - Selections, client communication, financial management, and project scheduling can remain available, but the migration may change where those workflows live and how teams interact with them.</p>

<p><strong>Support and roadmap</strong> - CoConstruct still has help content and migration support, but the strategic direction is Buildertrend. New buyers should confirm whether they are buying CoConstruct, Buildertrend, or a migration package.</p>

## Should You Switch Away from CoConstruct or Buildertrend?

<p>Only if the migration pricing, product direction, or workflow changes do not fit your business. Switching construction software carries real training and data-migration costs, so existing users should first ask for a written migration plan, feature mapping, final package price, renewal terms, and historical-data access guarantee.</p>

<p>Red flags for switching: unclear renewal pricing, no written answer on future CoConstruct access, lost selections/client-portal workflow, or a quote that forces you into Buildertrend functionality you do not use.</p>

<p>Consider alternatives like Contractor Foreman if budget is the primary concern, Projul if you want published annual flat-rate construction management, or JobTread if you want construction job costing with a base-plus-user model.</p>
