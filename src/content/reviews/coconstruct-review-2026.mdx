## What Happened to CoConstruct

<p>Buildertrend acquired CoConstruct in February 2021 and spent the following years migrating all users to the Buildertrend platform. By 2026, you cannot sign up for standalone CoConstruct - it simply doesn't exist anymore.</p>

<p>The good news: your project data survived. Contractors report that Buildertrend preserved client information, project timelines, and financial records during the migration. The less good news: workflows changed, pricing jumped dramatically, and some beloved CoConstruct features were altered or moved behind higher-tier paywalls.</p>

## What CoConstruct Users Gained in Buildertrend

<p><strong>Integrated payment processing</strong> - CoConstruct never had native payment collection. Buildertrend processes payments at roughly 2.99%, eliminating the need for separate merchant accounts.</p>

<p><strong>QuickBooks sync</strong> - Direct integration with QuickBooks Online pushes invoices, expenses, and job costing automatically. Legacy CoConstruct required manual export/import.</p>

<p><strong>Enhanced project dashboard</strong> - The unified Buildertrend interface combines scheduling, financials, and communications in one view rather than CoConstruct's separate modules.</p>

## What CoConstruct Users Lost

<p><strong>Affordable entry pricing</strong> - CoConstruct's lowest plan started at $49/mo. Buildertrend Essential begins at $399-$499/mo, pricing out small remodelers who relied on CoConstruct's budget tiers.</p>

<p><strong>Automatic selections tracking</strong> - CoConstruct automatically linked client selections to allowances and change orders. In Buildertrend, contractors must manually connect these workflows, adding administrative steps.</p>

<p><strong>Mobile app performance</strong> - Field crews report that the Buildertrend mobile app feels sluggish compared to CoConstruct's lightweight mobile experience.</p>

<p><strong>No free trial</strong> - CoConstruct offered trial periods. Buildertrend requires upfront payment, forcing contractors to commit before testing whether migrated workflows fit their needs.</p>

## Should You Switch Away from Buildertrend?

<p>Only if the pricing doesn't work or key workflows broke during migration. Switching construction software is expensive in training time and data migration risk. Most legacy CoConstruct users report acceptable satisfaction with Buildertrend despite the learning curve.</p>

<p>Red flag scenarios for switching: you're paying for Complete tier ($999+/mo) just to access selections features you had in CoConstruct's $99 plan, or your field teams consistently struggle with the mobile app.</p>

<p>Consider alternatives like Contractor Foreman if budget is the primary concern, or JobProgress for simplified project management without Buildertrend's complexity.</p>
