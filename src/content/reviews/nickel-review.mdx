<h2>Key Features: Three Pillars of B2B Payments</h2>

<h3>1. Getting Paid (Accounts Receivable)</h3>
<p>Nickel's A/R side covers the full payment collection workflow. Customers get a branded payment portal or a simple payment link. You can accept credit cards (2.9% flat), ACH bank transfers (free), or mailed checks. Partial and full refunds are supported, and you can save buyer payment info for repeat transactions. If your main problem is sending invoices rather than reducing ACH costs, compare the broader options in our <a href="/best-invoicing-software-contractors/">best invoicing software for contractors</a> guide. The real standout is <strong>one-for-one settlement</strong> - every transaction settles individually with your bank, no batch reconciliation. This is a genuine time-saver for contractors who hate matching batches at the end of the month.</p>

<h3>2. Paying Others (Accounts Payable)</h3>
<p>You can pay vendors, subcontractors, and suppliers by card, ACH, or physical check ($2 each). The approval workflow lets you set up custom approval chains before payments go out. Scheduled and recurring payments are available on Plus and Pro. For contractors who manage multiple subs and material suppliers on each job, this replaces the check-writing-and-stuffing-envelopes workflow with a few clicks.</p>

<h3>3. Trade Credit (Net Terms)</h3>
<p>This is Nickel's most differentiated feature and is exclusive to the Pro plan. You can offer digital credit applications that customers fill out online, pull credit reports and insights on applicants, set up decision workflows to approve or decline, and extend net terms up to Net 60. Nickel can also advance the payment so you get cash immediately while the customer pays on their terms. For material suppliers and subcontractors who routinely extend credit, this is a significant upgrade from the manual credit-check-and-invoice process.</p>

<h2>QuickBooks Integration: Real Differentiator</h2>

<p>Nickel's <a href="/quickbooks-online-review/">QuickBooks Online</a> integration is genuinely two-way and real-time. Create an invoice in QuickBooks and it appears in Nickel. Customer pays through Nickel - QuickBooks is updated instantly with the payment details and reconciliation data. No CSV exports, no manual entry, no "sync now" buttons.</p>

<p>This is the feature that makes Nickel a natural fit for contractors who already run their books on QuickBooks. Most payment processors require either manual reconciliation or a third-party middleware (like Zapier) to bridge the gap. Nickel does it natively.</p>

<h2>Security & Compliance</h2>
<p>Nickel operates with PCI-compliant infrastructure, SOC 2 compliance (audited quarterly), and encryption/tokenization for all payment data. Funds move through FDIC-insured holding accounts held in your business's name - not pooled with other merchants. This matters for contractors who process large transactions and want to know their money is protected and accessible.</p>

<p>Nickel is the strongest free ACH option for contractors who process B2B payments and want to eliminate processing fees. <h3>Nickel vs. Melio</h3>
<p>Both offer free ACH, but Nickel's ACH is truly unlimited (no caps) and the free plan is more feature-rich. Melio's advantage is the mobile app and slightly cheaper check mailing ($1.50 vs $2). For volume-heavy contractors who process many large transactions, Nickel's unlimited free ACH and trade credit features give it the edge.</p>

<h3>Nickel vs. QuickBooks Payments</h3>
<p>QuickBooks Payments has the advantage of being native to QuickBooks - no separate account needed. But it charges for ACH ($10/mo + $0.50/transaction for the cheapest plan) and has higher card rates (2.9% + $0.25). For contractors who process significant ACH volume, paying QuickBooks for ACH when Nickel offers it free is hard to justify. The trade-off is managing two platforms instead of one.</p>

<h3>Nickel vs. Square</h3>
<p>Square wins on mobile payments and in-person processing - it is the industry standard for on-site card swiping. Square's card rate is slightly lower (2.6% + $0.10 vs 2.9%). But Square's ACH processing charges 1% per transaction, and the $10K per-transaction cap is a problem for B2B contractors sending large invoices. Nickel is built for B2B; Square is built for retail. Use Square for the job site, Nickel for the office.</p>
