<h2>What Bitumio Does</h2>

<p>Bitumio is built for asphalt contractors from the ground up. The platform covers customer management, estimating, proposals, scheduling, job costing, automated follow-ups, and field communication. The estimating tools include map-based measurements that let you draw job areas and calculate quantities — square footage, tonnage, sealcoat coverage — without switching to a separate takeoff tool. That direct measurement-to-estimate flow is the feature that stands out most when you are used to building proposals from separate notes and spreadsheets.</p>

<p>The proposal workflow generates professional-looking documents with your pricing and scope. Once a proposal is accepted, the job moves into scheduling, where the office assigns crews and equipment. Field crews can view schedules and job details through the mobile app — and for employees who only need that view, the access is free. Completed field work feeds back into job costing so the office can compare estimated against actual cost before invoicing.</p>

<p>Automated follow-ups handle the step that many small paving companies forget: reminding customers about recommended maintenance, seasonal sealcoating, or past-due invoices. That sounds minor until you realize how many paving jobs are one-and-done because nobody followed up.</p>

<p>Bitumio lists $149 per user per month on its pricing page for admins, estimators, office managers, and foremen. That clarity matters because the alternative in the paving space is usually a custom quote, an application form, or a "contact sales" prompt. You can model what a three-user office costs — $447/month — before you ever talk to anyone. You can compare that against Projul's annual pricing or OneCrew's sales-led process without guessing.</p>

<h3>Free mobile-only crew access</h3>

<p>Field employees who only need to see schedules and job details can use the mobile app for free according to the official pricing page. For a paving company where most of the team works in the field, that can keep the software bill low while still giving every crew member the job information they need. The owner does not have to decide whether a crew lead "deserves" a paid seat just to see today's schedule.</p>

<h3>Asphalt-specific workflow</h3>

<p>Bitumio is not a generic field service management tool that you try to fit into asphalt work. Its estimating tools, proposal templates, scheduling views, and job costing categories are shaped around asphalt paving, sealcoating, and pavement maintenance. That matters when you are estimating a tonnage-based asphalt job with different production rates than a concrete project, or when your proposal needs to communicate scope for a parking lot overlay rather than a general construction remodel.</p>

<h2>Where Bitumio Falls Short</h2>

<h3>Per-user pricing scales against you</h3>

<p>$149 per seat is fair for a small office team. The trouble appears when your company has multiple people who need full platform access. An owner, estimator, office manager, and two foremen who all need to create estimates, manage schedules, and run job costs means five paid seats at $745/month. That is $8,940/year before any annual discount. Compare that to a flat-rate platform at $4,788/year for the whole company and the math shifts. Bitumio works best when you keep the paid-seat count intentional.</p>

<h3>Less natural for concrete or mixed operations</h3>

<p>If your company pours concrete, does general site work, or runs mixed trades, Bitumio's asphalt orientation may feel narrow. The estimating templates, cost categories, and workflow are built for asphalt paving and sealcoating. A concrete-heavy crew or a general contractor would be better served by broader construction software with customizable templates.</p>

<h3>Some details need a sales conversation</h3>

<p>Bitumio publishes its per-user price, but the details around annual savings, onboarding, data migration, and accounting workflow depth are not public. Buyers should get written confirmation of contract terms, support response, integration setup, cancellation, and data export before signing. The pricing page is transparent on the monthly rate but quiet on the edges that determine real total cost.</p>
