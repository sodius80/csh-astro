<p>Service Fusion is worth looking at for HVAC, plumbing, and electrical crews of 10 or more people &#8212; but only if your techs are on iOS. The legacy Android app is poorly rated on Google Play at about 2.5/5 from 489 reviews, while the newer Service Fusion By EverPro Android listing is about 2.7/5 from a very small review set; that is not a marginal problem. It is a documented, persistent operational liability for Android-majority teams.</p>
<p>The real draw here is pricing. Every plan includes unlimited users at a flat monthly rate. No per-user fees, no seat caps, no surprise overage charges as you add technicians. For a crew of 15, that math changes the economics of field service software significantly compared to platforms that charge per user.</p>
<div style="background:#f4f5f7;border-left:4px solid #1C2B4B;padding:16px 20px;margin:24px 0;border-radius:0 8px 8px 0;">
<p style="margin:0 0 8px 0;"><strong style="color:#1a7a3f;">Right for:</strong> Dispatch-heavy HVAC, plumbing, and electrical operations with 10&#8211;50 technicians who need unlimited user access, strong QuickBooks integration, and run iOS devices in the field.</p>
<p style="margin:0;"><strong style="color:#c0392b;">Not for:</strong> Android-majority crews, solo operators or teams under 10 (Jobber is cheaper and simpler), anyone who wants a free trial before committing, or contractors who need offline capability in the field.</p>
</div>
<h2>What Service Fusion Gets Right</h2>
<p><strong>The unlimited-user pricing model is the single biggest reason to consider Service Fusion.</strong> Every competitor in this range &#8212; Jobber, Housecall Pro, Workiz &#8212; charges per user or caps seats by tier. Service Fusion does not. Whether you have 5 users or 50, the price stays the same.</p>
<p>Here is what that means in practice. On current <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber</a> Grow Team pricing, annual billing is $299/month for 10 users, with $29 per additional user. On Service Fusion Starter ($208/month billed annually), you get unlimited users. For a 15-technician crew, Jobber Grow Team math is about $444/month before add-ons, while Service Fusion Starter stays at $208/month billed annually.</p>
<p>At 20 or 30 users, the gap widens. Current Jobber Grow Team math is about $589/month for 20 users and $879/month for 30 users before add-ons. Service Fusion Starter is still $208/month billed annually.</p>
<p><strong>The dispatch board is built for operations that live on scheduling.</strong> Drag-and-drop job assignment, color-coded statuses, and a visual layout that dispatchers can actually work from without squinting. For shops running 10+ trucks daily, the dispatch board is the feature that gets the most daily use, and reviewers consistently call it out as one of Service Fusion&#8217;s operational strengths.</p>
<p><strong>QuickBooks integration is genuinely strong.</strong> Two-way sync with both QuickBooks Online and Desktop &#8212; not just one-way invoice export. Customer records, payments, and job data stay connected between the field system and accounting. Multiple G2 and Capterra reviewers cite the QuickBooks connection as a primary reason they chose Service Fusion over alternatives.</p>
<p><strong>The iOS app works well.</strong> It earns a 4.6/5 rating on the App Store from about 1,420 ratings. Technicians on iPhones and iPads can access job details, capture signatures, process payments through FusionPay, and update work orders from the field. That is a meaningfully better field experience than what many mid-market platforms deliver.</p>
<p><strong>On-site invoicing and payment processing close jobs faster.</strong> Service Fusion Payments lets techs collect payment in the field and advertises card rates as low as 2.90% + $0.30 per transaction; additional fees may apply. Invoices are created and sent from the job, not batched at end-of-day. For service businesses where getting paid on the same day as the work matters, this removes a real bottleneck.</p>
<h2>Where Service Fusion Falls Short</h2>
<p><strong>The Android app is a genuine problem.</strong> The legacy Android app has a poor Google Play rating from 489 reviews; the newer By EverPro Android listing has only a small review set. That is not a few unhappy users &#8212; it is a pattern. The most common complaints: crashes during job updates, photos failing to upload on anything less than strong LTE, slow loading times even on flagship devices, and significant battery drain during all-day use.</p>
<p>One Google Play reviewer put it bluntly: &#8220;This app is a hindrance to good efficient work. It has so many issues I can&#8217;t list them here &#8212; low operating speed on a brand new S24+, unreliable saving, and it can&#8217;t be used AT ALL if you don&#8217;t have service.&#8221;</p>
<p>If your crew is mostly Android, this is not a minor detail. Service Fusion is a risk you should not take without extensive testing &#8212; and Service Fusion does not offer a free trial to let you test it.</p>
<p><strong>There is no free trial.</strong> This is unusual for the category. <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber</a> offers 14 days. <a href="/housecall-pro-review/">Housecall Pro</a> offers 14 days. Workiz advertises a 7-day no-card trial and shows a limited Lite column in its feature matrix. Service Fusion directs prospects to a free demo; paid plans start at $245/month month-to-month or $208/month billed annually, charged as one annual payment. The pricing page says plans include personalized onboarding, but I found a demo CTA rather than a public self-serve trial.</p>
<p><strong>Offline support needs verification.</strong> Multiple Android reviews report problems saving work or using the app without connectivity. If your technicians work in basements, rural properties, mechanical rooms, or buildings with weak cell signal, verify offline-mode support during the demo before committing.</p>
<p><strong>Reporting is basic.</strong> The built-in reports cover scheduling, revenue, and job status &#8212; the fundamentals. But contractors who want deeper business analytics, custom report building, or visual dashboards are going to export to Excel. Reports are also only available on desktop, not through the mobile app. For data-driven operations, this is a real limitation.</p>
<p><strong>The Starter plan is not cheap for small crews.</strong> $245/month month-to-month, or $208/month billed annually is a meaningful expense for a 3&#8211;5 person operation. The unlimited-user model only pays off once you have enough users to make per-user alternatives more expensive. For a crew under 10, <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber</a> starts at $29/month annually or $49 month-to-month and may cover similar workflows at a lower total cost with a cleaner interface.</p>

<h2>FAQ</h2>
<h3>Is Service Fusion worth it?</h3>
<p>For HVAC, plumbing, or electrical crews with 10 or more technicians on iOS devices, Service Fusion can be worth it. The unlimited-user pricing at $208&#8211;$533/month billed annually means your cost per user drops as your team grows &#8212; a 15-tech crew on the Starter plan pays roughly $14 per user per month. But for crews under 10, per-user platforms like <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber</a> are cheaper. And if your techs use Android, the legacy Android app’s 2.5/5 rating and the newer By EverPro app’s small-sample 2.7/5 rating are serious concerns to investigate before signing.</p>
<h3>How much does Service Fusion cost?</h3>
<p>Starter starts at $245/month ($208 with annual billing), Plus at $382/month ($325 annual), and Pro at $627/month ($533 annual). All plans include unlimited users. Optional add-ons such as GPS fleet tracking and ServiceCall.ai are listed, but public add-on pricing was not found. Service Fusion Payments advertises rates as low as 2.90% + $0.30, with other fees possible. No public implementation fee was found; the pricing page says personalized onboarding is included. No public free trial or free plan was found.</p>
<h3>Does Service Fusion offer a free trial?</h3>
<p>No. Service Fusion does not offer a free trial or a free plan. This is a drawback versus Jobber and Housecall Pro, which advertise 14-day no-card trials. Service Fusion’s pricing page points prospects to a free demo and says personalized onboarding is included, but I found no public self-serve trial. In practice, get hands-on demo access before committing at $245+/month.</p>
<h3>Is the Service Fusion Android app any good?</h3>
<p>No. The legacy Service Fusion Android app is rated about 2.5/5 on Google Play from 489 reviews; the newer Service Fusion By EverPro app is about 2.7/5 from 8 reviews. Common complaints include crashes during job updates, failed photo uploads, slow loading on flagship devices, heavy battery drain, and connectivity-related field issues. The iOS app, by contrast, is rated 4.6/5 from about 1,420 ratings. If your technicians are primarily on Android, this is a significant risk factor.</p>

Jobber wins for small crews with lower starting prices, a cleaner interface, a 14-day free trial, and stronger mobile apps on both platforms.
