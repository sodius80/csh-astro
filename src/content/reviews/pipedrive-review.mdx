<p>Pipedrive is a sales pipeline CRM built around one idea: every lead, deal, and quote should have a visible next step. For contractor offices where leads disappear because nobody knows who called whom or which estimate needs a follow-up, that single discipline can be the difference between closing 30% of quotes and closing 50%.</p>

<p>With <strong>$14/seat/mo</strong> as the entry point (annual billing) and a 14-day free trial that does not require a credit card, Pipedrive is the cheapest way for a contractor to get a real CRM workflow without committing to a field service platform. This review is based on Pipedrive's official pricing page, product pages, and the 8 CSH comparison and roundup pages where Pipedrive already appears against alternatives like ActiveCampaign, Zoho CRM, Keap, and Pipeline CRM.</p>

<h2>Quick verdict</h2>

<p>Pipedrive is worth it if your contractor office has a sales follow-up problem. Leads come in from the website, phone, or referral, and nobody can tell which ones were called, which quotes went out, and which customers need a second touch. Pipedrive fixes that by putting every deal in a visual pipeline with an owner, a stage, and a next activity.</p>

<p>Skip Pipedrive if your problem is operations. If you need to <a href="/jobber-review/">dispatch technicians</a>, schedule crews, create field invoices, manage work orders, or track inventory, Pipedrive is not that tool. It sits before the job is sold, not after.</p>

<p>The pricing is transparent and easy to evaluate. Four plans, published rates, annual and monthly billing options. The 14-day trial gives full access with no card, which is enough time to build a pipeline, import contacts, connect email, and see whether the team actually uses it.</p>

<h2>The pipeline: Pipedrive's core value</h2>

<p>Everything in Pipedrive revolves around the pipeline view. It is a board where deals move left to right through stages, and every deal has an owner, a value, and a next activity. That structure maps directly to a contractor sales process:</p>

<ul>
<li>New lead</li>
<li>Contacted</li>
<li>Estimate scheduled</li>
<li>Quote sent</li>
<li>Follow-up due</li>
<li>Won</li>
<li>Lost</li>
</ul>

<p>When a deal sits in "Quote sent" with no next activity for 5 days, the pipeline makes that stuck status obvious. The office manager can see it, the salesperson can see it, and nobody can pretend the follow-up happened. That visibility is the core reason to buy a CRM, and Pipedrive does it better than any tool in its price range.</p>

<p>The activity feed is the second half of the pipeline. Every call, email, meeting, and note is logged against the deal, so a manager can answer the question "what happened with that lead this week?" in two clicks instead of chasing down the salesperson.</p>

<h2>Email and automation</h2>

<p>Full email sync starts on the <strong>Growth plan</strong> ($39/seat/mo annual). That includes Gmail, Outlook, and company email with open and link tracking, so a salesperson can see when a prospect opened the quote email. Bulk email sending and automation sequences are also on Growth, which means a contractor can set up a basic follow-up sequence after a quote goes out.</p>

<p>On Lite ($14), email is limited to basic contact management. If email tracking and automated follow-up sequences matter to your sales process, Lite is not enough. Growth is the practical starting plan for an active sales operation.</p>

<p>For marketing automation beyond sales follow-up (segmented campaigns, lead scoring, reactivation sequences), Pipedrive is narrower than a platform like <a href="/activecampaign-vs-pipedrive/">ActiveCampaign</a>. The Campaigns add-on ($13.33/mo per company) extends email marketing, but it is not a replacement for a full marketing automation engine.</p>

<h2>Mobile app</h2>

<p>Pipedrive's iOS and Android apps sync cleanly with the web platform. A salesperson in the field can update deal stages, log a call, add a note, and set the next activity from their phone. The mobile experience is straightforward and does not require training, which matters for adoption in a contractor office where people are more comfortable with a phone than a desk computer.</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Zapier</strong> - connect Pipedrive to virtually any other app, including field service tools and accounting software</li>
<li><strong>QuickBooks</strong> - contact and deal sync through Zapier or third-party connectors, not native</li>
<li><strong>Mailchimp</strong> - email marketing sync for contacts and leads</li>

<p>If you need direct, native QuickBooks integration (two-way sync without Zapier), Pipedrive is not the strongest choice. See our <a href="/crm-with-quickbooks-integration/">CRM with QuickBooks integration</a> guide for tools with native accounting handoff.</p>

<h2>What Pipedrive does not do</h2>

<p>Pipedrive is a sales CRM, not an operations platform. It does not:</p>

<ul>
<li>Dispatch technicians or manage routes</li>
<li>Schedule crews or track job progress</li>
<li>Create invoices or process payments</li>
<li>Manage inventory, work orders, or production calendars</li>
<li>Handle job costing or project management after the deal is won</li>
</ul>

<p>That is not a flaw. It is the product's scope. A contractor using <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber</a> or <a href="/housecall-pro-review/">Housecall Pro</a> for operations may still use Pipedrive for sales pipeline discipline. The two tools solve different problems at different stages of the business. If you want one tool that handles everything from lead to invoice, look at full field service platforms instead.</p>

<h2>Pros and cons</h2>

<h3>What Pipedrive does well</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Lowest entry price in dedicated sales CRM.</strong> At $14/seat/mo (annual), Pipedrive undercuts every comparable CRM. For a contractor testing whether CRM discipline works for the office, the test is cheap.</li>
<li><strong>Activity-centered pipeline.</strong> The deal board plus activity feed creates natural follow-up accountability. You can see what was done, what is overdue, and what needs to happen next.</li>
<li><strong>14-day trial with no card.</strong> Full feature access, no credit card, no commitment. A contractor can build a real pipeline and test it with the team before paying anything.</li>
<li><strong>Clean, mobile-friendly interface.</strong> The web app and mobile app are intuitive. Adoption is usually the hardest part of CRM, and Pipedrive makes it easier than most.</li>
<li><strong>Transparent pricing.</strong> Four plans, published rates, annual and monthly options. No hidden tiers or "contact for pricing" walls.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Where Pipedrive falls short</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>No no-cost starter tier.</strong> The trial converts to paid at $14/seat/mo. If you want a no-cost CRM, <a href="/best-free-crm-for-contractors/">Freshsales or HubSpot Free</a> are the alternatives.</li>
<li><strong>Not an operations tool.</strong> No dispatch, scheduling, invoicing, or job management. Pipedrive handles sales, not the work after the job is sold.</li>
<li><strong>Marketing depth is limited.</strong> Email automation exists on Growth and higher, but segmentation and campaign logic are narrower than a dedicated marketing platform like ActiveCampaign.</li>
<li><strong>Per-seat pricing scales up.</strong> A 5-seat team on Growth pays $195/month annual. Lite-only teams stay cheap, but most contractors need Growth for the email and automation features.</li>
</ul>

<h2>How Pipedrive compares</h2>

<h3>Pipedrive vs ActiveCampaign</h3>
<p>If the problem is sales pipeline and follow-up discipline, <a href="/activecampaign-vs-pipedrive/">Pipedrive is the better starting point</a>. If the problem is marketing automation (quote nurture emails, seasonal campaigns, reactivation sequences, lead scoring), ActiveCampaign is stronger. Pipedrive starts cheaper ($14 vs $15) and is easier to adopt. ActiveCampaign scales by contact count, Pipedrive by seat count.</p>

<h3>Pipedrive vs HubSpot Free</h3>
<p>HubSpot's no-cost CRM edition covers 5 users with unlimited contacts, email tracking, meeting scheduling, and deal pipelines. If budget is zero, HubSpot's starter edition is the better starting point. Pipedrive's advantage is a more focused pipeline workflow, cleaner activity tracking, and email automation on Growth that HubSpot only offers on paid Starter plans.</p>

<h3>Pipedrive vs Zoho CRM</h3>
<p>Zoho CRM has a no-cost starter edition (3 users, 5,000 records) and deeper customization (custom modules, fields). Its paid Standard tier starts at $14/user/mo (annual), matching Pipedrive's Lite price. Zoho's strength is the broader ecosystem (Zoho Books, Projects, Inventory). Pipedrive's strength is a cleaner, more focused pipeline experience that is easier for a contractor team to adopt without configuration overhead.</p>

<h3>Pipedrive vs Jobber or Housecall Pro</h3>
<p>These are not competitors. <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber</a> and <a href="/housecall-pro-review/">Housecall Pro</a> are field service platforms that handle dispatch, scheduling, invoicing, and payments. Pipedrive is a sales CRM that handles leads and deals before the job is sold. Many contractors use Pipedrive alongside Jobber or Housecall Pro: Pipedrive for the sales pipeline, the field service tool for operations.</p>
