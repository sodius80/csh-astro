<p>Jobber has been the default recommendation for small field service businesses since well before any of us started writing about contractor software. That reputation is earned. It is the platform that made scheduling, quoting, invoicing, and client communication feel like they belong in the same app rather than duct-taped together across four different tools.</p>

<p>In this review, I look at Jobber's pricing as of mid-2026, walk through its key features, and evaluate where it still leads the pack and where competitors have caught up or pulled ahead.</p>

<p><strong>Third-Party Rating:</strong> Capterra users rate Jobber 4.0 out of 5 based on 2,100+ reviews. On G2, the score is 4.6 out of 5. The gap likely reflects different reviewer populations. Capterra skews toward small-to-medium businesses that use the product day-to-day. G2 draws more mid-market and enterprise reviewers who may find Jobber simpler and more focused than the alternatives they have used. I am using Capterra as the more representative source for contractors.</p>

</ul>

<h2>Feature Deep Dive</h2>

<h3>Scheduling and Dispatch</h3>
<p>Jobber's scheduling view is a drag-and-drop calendar that shows your day, week, or month at a glance. You can assign jobs to technicians, set estimated arrival windows, and send automated reminders to clients. The dispatch board shows who is available and what their day looks like. It is not advanced route optimization like you get from ServiceTitan or Verizon Connect. But for the small-to-mid size businesses Jobber serves, the calendar is fast, intuitive, and gets the job done without a training session.</p>

<h3>Quoting and Estimates</h3>
<p>Jobber's quoting tools let you build estimates from saved line items, adjust quantities and prices on the fly, and send professional-looking quotes that clients can approve with one click. Once approved, the quote converts to a job automatically. You can attach photos, terms and conditions, and optional signatures. Compared to <a href="/field-promax-review/">Field ProMax</a>, Jobber's quote builder is more polished and easier to customize without advanced configuration.</p>

<h3>Invoicing and Payments</h3>
<p>Invoicing is where Jobber really shines. You can create an invoice from a completed job, send it by email or SMS, and collect payment immediately through Jobber Payments. Customers can pay by credit card or ACH bank transfer. The invoice design is clean and professional out of the box. Jobber Payments processes at 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction, which is standard for the industry. The downside is that you cannot bring your own processor. If you already have a payment setup you like, Jobber will ask you to switch.</p>

<h3>Client Hub</h3>
<p>The client hub is Jobber's standout feature. It gives each customer a branded portal where they can view their appointment history, approve estimates, request bookings, pay invoices, and leave reviews. For a contractor running a one- or two-person office, the client hub reduces inbound calls significantly. Customers self-serve on their own time. This is one area where Jobber beats <a href="/housecall-pro-review/">Housecall Pro</a>, whose equivalent portal is not as fully featured.</p>

<h3>Mobile Apps</h3>
<p>Jobber's mobile apps for iOS and Android cover what technicians need in the field: view the daily schedule, start and complete jobs, collect payments, take photos, capture customer signatures, and log GPS mileage. The app works offline for critical functions, so a technician in a basement or rural area can still log job details and sync when they reconnect.</p>

<h2>What Jobber Gets Right and Where It Falls Short</h2>

<h3>Ease of use that actually delivers</h3>
<p>Jobber is genuinely easy to set up and use. New users can go from account creation to sending their first invoice within an hour. The interface is clean, the terminology matches how contractors actually think about their work, and the onboarding flow guides you through setup without overwhelming you. This is Jobber's enduring competitive advantage. Competitors have gotten close on features, but few match the out-of-box usability.</p>

<h3>The client hub creates real office efficiency</h3>
<p>When I talk to Jobber users, the client hub is what makes them stick with the platform. Customers can approve quotes, book appointments, and pay invoices without a phone call. For a busy shop, even 10 redirected calls per day adds up to real time saved. Jobber's reviews add-on also lets you collect and manage Google and Facebook reviews from the same portal, which helps with local SEO.</p>

<h3>Transparent, predictable pricing</h3>
<p>Jobber publishes all its prices on its website. There is no sales call required to see what a plan costs. The 14-day free trial is genuinely useful for evaluating the full product. This transparency is surprisingly rare in field service software. Competitors like ServiceTitan and Workiz hide prices behind demo requests, making upfront comparison harder.</p>

<h3>Regular updates and good support</h3>
<p>Jobber ships new features consistently. Recent additions include improved scheduling views, more payment options, and deeper QuickBooks sync. Support is responsive through email, chat, and phone during business hours. The knowledge base and help center are well organized and actually helpful.</p>

<h3>Where Jobber Falls Short</h3>

<h3>Locked into Jobber Payments</h3>
<p>Jobber does not let you use your own payment processor. You must use Jobber Payments for online transactions. If you already have a relationship with a processor or negotiate better rates elsewhere, this is a real constraint. The 2.9% + $0.30 rate is not bad, but the inability to switch or negotiate is frustrating for growing businesses.</p>

<h3>Reporting is basic</h3>
<p>Jobber's reporting covers the essentials: revenue by technician, job completion rates, invoice aging, and payment history. But it stops there. There is no custom report builder, no dashboard editor, and no drill-down analytics. If you want to analyze profitability by job type, customer lifetime value, or technician efficiency, you will export data and build those reports yourself in something else.</p>

<h3>Marketing tools cost extra</h3>
<p>The reviews add-on ($39/mo) and Campaigns add-on ($29/mo) are priced as separate subscriptions. If you want the full marketing stack - automated review requests, SMS and email campaigns, and customer follow-ups - you are paying $68/mo on top of your plan. Housecall Pro bundles more of these features into its base tiers, which makes the total cost comparison shift in Housecall Pro's favor for marketing-heavy users.</p>

<h3>No QuickBooks Desktop sync</h3>
<p>Jobber integrates with QuickBooks Online but not QuickBooks Desktop. For established contractors who have run their accounting on Desktop for years and do not want to migrate, this is a dealbreaker. Alternatives like Field ProMax support both Desktop and Online.</p>

A 5-person crew on the team version of Connect pays $169/mo. If you add the Reviews add-on ($39/mo) and Campaigns ($29/mo), realistic monthly cost for a small crew is $237-$267/mo. That is not cheap, but it replaces individual tools for scheduling, invoicing, CRM, and marketing coordination.</p>

<h2>Final Verdict</h2>

<p>Jobber remains the field service platform I recommend first to most small contractors. It is not the cheapest option. It is not the most feature-rich option. But it is the option that new users can adopt fastest and that works reliably day after day. The client hub, the quote-to-invoice-to-payment workflow, and the clean interface are genuinely hard to beat at this price point.</p>

<p>The real question is not whether Jobber is good. It is whether its limitations - locked-in payment processing, basic reporting, no Desktop QuickBooks sync - apply to your specific business. If none of those are dealbreakers, Jobber is the right answer. If any of them pinch, look at Housecall Pro or Field ProMax before you commit.</p>
