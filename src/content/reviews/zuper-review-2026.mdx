## What Zuper Gets Right

<p><strong>Intelligent scheduling automation</strong> -- Zuper's core strength appears to be matching jobs to technicians based on skills, location, and availability. For roofing contractors dealing with different roof types, heights, and material requirements, this could reduce the manual scheduling burden that kills productivity in growing operations.</p>

<p><strong>Project-based workflow design</strong> -- Unlike generic field service tools, Zuper structures workflows around projects rather than individual service calls. This aligns better with how roofing jobs actually work, where a single project involves multiple visits, material deliveries, and coordination between office and field teams.</p>

<p><strong>Customer visibility features</strong> -- The customer portal gives homeowners access to proposals, job progress updates, and payment options. For an industry where communication gaps often create friction, this transparency can help contractors stand out and reduce phone calls about job status.</p>

## Where Zuper Falls Short

<p><strong>Pricing confusion creates evaluation barriers</strong> -- Research reveals pricing points ranging from $29 to $245 per month, but without clear plan breakdowns or feature tiers. Contractors need to know what they're buying before committing time to demos and setup.</p>

<p><strong>Limited verified user feedback</strong> -- Unlike established platforms with thousands of reviews, Zuper lacks the user feedback volume that helps contractors understand real-world performance. This makes it harder to assess reliability during busy seasons or complex projects.</p>

<p><strong>Feature complexity may overwhelm smaller teams</strong> -- The all-in-one approach with AI scheduling, project coordination, and warehouse inventory tracking could be overkill for contractors who just need basic job management and aren't ready for advanced automation.</p>
