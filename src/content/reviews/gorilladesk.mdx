## What GorillaDesk Gets Right

The software includes 150+ digital business document templates covering pest inspection forms, treatment records, and recurring service agreements. Contractors report the interface makes sense immediately without forcing you to adapt HVAC workflows to pest routes.

**Transparent pricing, but priced by schedules/routes.** GorillaDesk posts its plan structure publicly: Basic starts at $49/month for the first schedule, Pro at $99/month for the first schedule, and Growth at $149/month for the first schedule. Its help center says pricing changes as you add technician schedules/routes, while admins, managers, sales users, and crew users are unlimited. No startup fees, no long-term contracts, and free data migration are meaningful advantages.

**Route optimization built for recurring pest services.** The scheduling system understands pest control's unique rhythm of monthly, bi-monthly, and quarterly recurring treatments. Contractors praise the drag-and-drop scheduling board and automatic route optimization that groups nearby customers by service day. The system handles seasonal adjustments and one-time treatments without breaking your regular route structure.

**Offline mobile capability.** Field technicians can access the current week's schedule, customer information, and service forms even without cell coverage. While limited to current week data, this addresses a real pain point for rural pest control routes where cell towers are sparse.

**Quick setup and responsive support.** Multiple G2 reviewers mention getting up and running within days, not weeks. The customer support team responds quickly and understands pest control terminology, unlike generic FSM support teams that need everything explained.

## Where GorillaDesk Falls Short

**QuickBooks Online sync reliability issues.** Multiple Reddit contractors report serious problems with the QuickBooks Online integration. One detailed warning states: "DO NOT sync QBO with Gorilla Desk! You will continue to have sync problems and your books will be a nightmare!" The issues include duplicate transactions, failed invoice transfers, and payment matching problems that require manual cleanup. This is not isolated feedback - similar complaints appear across multiple contractor forums.

**SMS costs are mandatory but separate.** GorillaDesk's core value proposition includes automated customer communication and on-my-way texts. However, these SMS features require a separately purchased bulk SMS add-on that is not included in the base pricing. For a pest control company sending route updates and appointment reminders, this transforms the $49/month headline price into a higher real monthly cost.

**Offline mode limitations create operational risk.** While GorillaDesk offers offline mobile access, it only caches the current week's data. For pest control companies running rural routes with poor cell coverage, this creates problems when technicians need to reference previous treatment records, check customer notes from last quarter, or handle emergency callbacks beyond the current week window.

**Basic reporting limits business insights.** Small pest control operators consistently mention the reporting capabilities feel limited compared to enterprise pest software like PestPac or FieldRoutes. You can track basic revenue and job completion metrics, but deeper profitability analysis, chemical usage tracking, and seasonal trend reporting require manual spreadsheet work.

**Mobile app performance issues in low-coverage areas.** Even when cell coverage exists, contractors report the mobile app syncs slowly and sometimes fails to update job statuses or upload photos until technicians return to stronger signal areas. This creates gaps in real-time job tracking that office staff rely on for customer service calls.
