## What ExaYard Gets Right

**AI automation that actually works on real plans**

ExaYard's core strength is reading PDF construction plans and automatically identifying elements to measure and count. Upload a residential floor plan, and the AI recognizes walls, doors, windows, and fixtures without manual markup. For contractors used to clicking every measurement in PlanSwift, this feels like magic.

The platform includes pre-built templates called "Blu assistants" for common residential tasks. Select "flooring takeoff" and ExaYard knows to measure room areas and calculate material needs. The electrical template counts outlets, switches, and fixtures. These templates eliminate the setup work that makes traditional takeoff software painful for smaller jobs.

**Browser-based with unlimited storage**

Unlike desktop tools that tie you to one computer, ExaYard runs in any web browser. Contractors report accessing projects from job sites on tablets, the office desktop, and home laptops without file syncing headaches. The platform stores unlimited projects in the cloud, which matters for contractors who bid dozens of jobs monthly.

Integration works through standard exports. ExaYard generates CSV files and PDFs that import into QuickBooks, Buildertrend, or whatever estimating system contractors already use. It's not trying to replace your entire workflow, just the slow takeoff part.

**Published pricing without sales theater**

ExaYard posts actual prices on their website. Starter costs $99 per user monthly with $100 in AI credits. Professional runs $249 monthly with $250 in credits and unlimited manual measurements. Enterprise requires a sales call, but at least the entry-level pricing is transparent.

Compare this to competitors who hide pricing behind "request demo" forms. Contractors can budget for ExaYard without suffering through a sales pitch about "transformation" and "solutions."

## Where ExaYard Falls Short

**Monthly credit limits create budget anxiety**

ExaYard charges AI credits for automated takeoffs. Simple residential plans consume $5-15 in credits. Complex commercial drawings can cost $50-100. The Starter plan's $100 monthly allowance handles maybe 10-20 typical residential jobs.

Contractors report watching their credit meter like a gas gauge. Heavy bidders hit limits mid-month and either pay overages or switch to manual mode. The Professional plan's $250 allowance helps, but credit management becomes a monthly chore.

This credit model makes sense for ExaYard's costs, but it creates uncertainty contractors hate. PlanSwift charges once and lets you measure unlimited plans. ExaYard's variable costs make it harder to predict monthly expenses.

**Three-day trial barely scratches the surface**

ExaYard offers a three-day free trial. That's enough time to upload a plan and see the AI work, but not enough to evaluate whether it fits your bidding workflow. Most contractors need weeks to trust automated measurements enough to submit real bids.

Compare Procore's 30-day trials or even Buildertrend's 14-day window. Three days feels stingy for a tool that requires workflow changes. Contractors end up buying before they're confident, which leads to buyer's remorse.

**Browser dependency eliminates offline work**

ExaYard requires internet connectivity for everything. No offline mode exists. Contractors visiting job sites with poor cellular coverage can't access their takeoffs. The platform becomes unusable during internet outages.

Desktop tools like PlanSwift work offline once installed. For contractors who travel frequently or work in rural areas, browser dependency is a real limitation.

**Enterprise pricing lacks transparency**

The Enterprise tier promises "custom pricing" and advanced features but requires sales contact. Larger contractors can't budget without engaging salespeople. This contradicts ExaYard's refreshing pricing transparency on the lower tiers.

## Final Verdict

ExaYard represents the shift from desktop takeoff tools to AI-powered automation. The platform delivers on its core promise of faster measurements from PDF plans, but monthly credit limits require careful budgeting.

For residential contractors bidding frequently, ExaYard's speed advantage over manual methods justifies the cost. The browser-based access and unlimited storage beat desktop tools for teams working from multiple locations.

The credit model is ExaYard's biggest weakness. Heavy bidders face monthly anxiety about usage limits. The three-day trial makes evaluation difficult. But for contractors willing to manage variable costs, the time savings are real.

**My recommendation:** Try ExaYard if you're bidding 10+ residential jobs monthly and want to eliminate manual takeoff time. Budget $150-200 monthly per user including credit overages. Skip it if you need offline capability or submit only occasional bids.
