<p>This MailerLite review covers what the platform does well for contractors, where it falls short, and when to pick a different email tool instead. MailerLite is not <a href="/field-service-software-alternatives/">contractor operations software</a>. It will not schedule technicians, dispatch crews, build invoices, or manage job costing. What it does is email marketing: newsletters, seasonal promotions, quote follow-up sequences, and customer reactivation campaigns. It does that job cleanly, affordably, and with a free plan that lets a contractor test the platform with a real list before paying anything.</p>

<p>The value proposition is simple. A contractor with a small customer list can start on the free plan (250 subscribers, 2,500 emails per month), build a newsletter template, set up a welcome sequence, and send real campaigns without entering a credit card. When the list grows or the sending volume increases, paid plans start at $10.80/mo billed yearly for 500 contacts. That is cheaper than ActiveCampaign ($15/mo for 1,000 contacts) and includes more features than Moosend at a similar price point if you need landing pages and a website builder alongside email.</p>

<p>This review is based on Atlas-verified pricing, MailerLite's official pricing page, the CSH <a href="/best-email-marketing-software-contractors/">email marketing roundup</a> where MailerLite ranks as the simplicity and value pick, and six comparison pages where MailerLite appears against ActiveCampaign, Moosend, GetResponse, Freshsales, Keap, Pipeline CRM, and Zoho CRM.</p>

<h2>Feature Deep Dive</h2>

<h3>Email editor and templates</h3>
<p>MailerLite's drag-and-drop editor is one of its strongest features. The official pricing page highlights three editor options: a drag-and-drop builder, a custom HTML editor, and a simple text editor. For most contractors, the drag-and-drop builder is the right choice. It produces clean, mobile-friendly emails without touching code, and the block-based interface means a non-technical office manager can build a professional newsletter in under an hour.</p>

<p>The catch is template variety. MailerLite's template library is smaller than Mailchimp or Constant Contact. A contractor who wants a pre-built template for a specific trade or season may need to build from a blank canvas instead. That is not a deal-breaker, but it adds setup time compared to platforms with hundreds of ready-made designs.</p>

<h3>Marketing automation</h3>
<p>The automation builder is a visual workflow editor that handles the scenarios contractors care about: welcome sequences for new subscribers, time-delayed follow-ups after a quote request, tag-based triggers that send different content to different segments, and abandoned form recovery workflows.</p>

<p>The free plan includes 3 active automations with 5 steps max. The Comfort plan expands to 50 active automations with 100 steps. The Power plan removes limits entirely with unlimited automations and multiple automation triggers. That tier structure means a contractor can start with basic welcome sequences on the free plan and scale up as follow-up logic gets more sophisticated.</p>

<p>Where it falls short: the automation logic is simpler than ActiveCampaign's. MailerLite handles linear sequences, tag triggers, and time delays well. If you need complex multi-branch split paths with scoring thresholds, conditional logic based on deal stage, or pipeline-stage-based automation, ActiveCampaign is the stronger tool. For most contractor email marketing needs, MailerLite's automation depth is enough.</p>

<h3>Landing pages, forms, and website builder</h3>
<p>MailerLite includes a landing page builder, signup form builder, and website builder on every plan. The free plan allows 1 website, 1 landing page, and 3 active forms. The Comfort plan expands to 10 websites and landing pages plus 10 signup forms. The Power plan removes all limits.</p>

<p>For a contractor who wants a simple "request a quote" landing page or a seasonal promotion page without paying for a separate tool, this is real value. The website builder includes SEO tools, click maps, password-protected pages, custom code injection, and A/B split testing. The signup forms capture customer preferences and enable targeted messaging.</p>

<h3>Integrations and API access</h3>
<p>MailerLite lists 100-plus integrations including Zapier, Make, WordPress, Shopify, WooCommerce, Stripe, Claude, and ChatGPT. API and MCP Server access are available, though sending via API or MCP requires a paid plan. For contractors, the most important integrations are usually website forms, payment systems, and Zapier connections to field service tools.</p>

<p>Check the exact tool before buying. A large integration marketplace does not guarantee a deep native connector for every contractor platform. If you need contacts to move from Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan, QuickBooks, or a niche estimating tool, verify the Zapier path or API handoff during the trial.</p>

<h3>Security and compliance</h3>
<p>MailerLite stores data in the European Union and is certified with ISO 27001. The platform is fully GDPR compliant with controls for personal data processing security. MailerLite states that it never rents, sells, or shares data with third parties. Accounts are monitored to comply with Terms of Use and Anti-Spam policy to protect shared IP reputations. For contractors handling customer email addresses, this level of compliance matters.</p>

<h2>What MailerLite Gets Right</h2>

<h3>A free plan that actually works</h3>
<p>The free plan covers 250 subscribers and 2,500 emails per month with 2 user seats, 1 website, 1 landing page, 3 active forms, and 3 active automations. That is enough to build a contact list, design a newsletter template, set up a welcome sequence, and send real campaigns without paying anything. A 14-day premium trial also unlocks advanced features with no credit card required.</p>

<p>For a contractor who has never done email marketing and wants to test the workflow before committing, this is the lowest-risk entry point in the category. ActiveCampaign has no free plan. Moosend has no free plan. MailerLite's free tier is the genuine no-cost starting point that competitors talk about but do not always deliver.</p>

<h3>The price-to-feature ratio is strong</h3>
<p>At $10.80/mo billed yearly for 500 contacts, the Comfort plan includes A/B testing, the AI writing assistant, emails that adapt content per subscriber, smart sending, and the ability to remove the MailerLite logo. Compare that to ActiveCampaign Starter at $15/mo for 1,000 contacts (annual), which has limited automation on the entry tier. MailerLite gives a contractor more email marketing features per dollar at lower contact counts.</p>

<p>The Power plan at $22.50/mo billed yearly adds unlimited emails, unlimited user seats, unlimited automations, and multiple automation triggers. For a contractor with 500 to 5,000 contacts who sends frequently, the unlimited email volume on Power is a meaningful advantage over platforms that charge per send or cap monthly volume.</p>

<h3>The editor does not get in the way</h3>
<p>The drag-and-drop editor is fast, clean, and produces emails that look good on phones. That matters for contractors because most customers read email on mobile devices. A tool that requires fighting with formatting or previewing across breakpoints adds friction that makes people stop sending. MailerLite's editor removes that friction.</p>

<h3>It can sit beside field service software</h3>
<p>MailerLite does not need to replace Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan, or another operations system. In many contractor offices, the better architecture is separate: field service software runs the job, MailerLite runs customer communication and nurture. That division keeps the dispatch team in the tool they use every day while marketing owns email campaigns and landing pages.</p>

<h2>Where MailerLite Falls Short</h2>

<h3>No CRM or pipeline tracking</h3>
<p>MailerLite is an email marketing platform, not a sales CRM. It has no deal stages, pipeline views, or contact scoring beyond email engagement metrics. If your problem is lead tracking and follow-up discipline before the quote goes out, you need a tool like <a href="/pipedrive-review/">Pipedrive</a> or <a href="/pipeline-crm-review/">Pipeline CRM</a>. MailerLite handles the marketing side; it does not manage the sales pipeline.</p>

<h3>Less automation depth than ActiveCampaign</h3>
<p>The automation builder handles welcome sequences, tag-based triggers, and time delays. It does not handle the multi-branch split paths, conditional logic based on deal stage, lead scoring thresholds, or site tracking event chains that ActiveCampaign offers. For contractors who need automated follow-up that reacts differently based on what a lead clicked, downloaded, or visited, ActiveCampaign has more depth.</p>

<h3>Template library is smaller than competitors</h3>
<p>MailerLite has fewer pre-built templates than Mailchimp, Constant Contact, or AWeber. A contractor who wants a ready-made seasonal promotion template or a trade-specific newsletter design may need to build from scratch. That is not hard with the drag-and-drop editor, but it adds setup time.</p>

<h3>Pricing scales with subscriber count</h3>
<p>The $10.80/mo entry price is excellent for 500 contacts. But MailerLite pricing scales by active subscriber count across multiple tiers. At 10,000 contacts, the Comfort plan costs significantly more. For contractors whose lists grow through customer acquisition, the cost advantage shrinks as the audience grows. Price the real contact count before signing up, not just the entry tier.</p>
