<p>PandaDoc is a document automation platform that handles proposals, quotes, contracts, agreements, and e-signatures in one system. For contractors who sell larger project scopes, commercial work, or maintenance agreements where the proposal itself is the sales asset, it does that job well. It is not contractor-specific software, and it does not replace job management, scheduling, or field-service tools.</p>

<p>This review covers who PandaDoc actually fits, how the pricing works, what the feature set delivers, and where it falls short. The pricing was verified against PandaDoc's official pricing page and cross-checked with Atlas V3 product data.</p>

<ul>
<li>You sell larger project scopes, commercial work, or packaged contracts where the proposal is the sales asset</li>

<li>You need e-signatures, document tracking, and a reusable content library for scope language and terms</li>

<h2>Feature Deep Dive</h2>

<h3>Document Editor and Template Builder</h3>

<p>The drag-and-drop document editor is PandaDoc's core strength. You build proposals, quotes, contracts, and agreements from reusable templates that include text blocks, images, pricing tables, videos, and custom branding. The editor supports conditional content, so sections appear or hide based on variables you set (project type, scope size, client industry). For contractors who maintain separate proposal templates for kitchen remodels, commercial fit-outs, and service agreements, this conditional content system prevents template sprawl.</p>

<p>The content library stores reusable clauses, terms, scope language, and approved language blocks. When a legal clause changes or a standard scope description needs updating, you edit it once in the library and every new document pulls the current version. That eliminates the problem of old terms lurking in copied Word documents from two years ago.</p>

<h3>E-Signature Workflow</h3>

<p>PandaDoc's e-signature system handles the full signature lifecycle: send, track, remind, and store. You see when a recipient opens the document, which pages they viewed, and where they paused. Automated reminders follow up with signers who have not completed the document. The audit trail records every action with timestamps and IP addresses, which matters for construction contracts where signature disputes can escalate.</p>

<p>Signers do not need a PandaDoc account to sign. They receive an email link, review the document in a browser, and apply their signature. This reduces friction for clients, subcontractors, and suppliers who do not want to create an account just to approve a scope of work.</p>

<h3>Pricing Tables and Quotes</h3>

<p>The pricing table feature lets you build interactive quote sections inside proposals. Line items can include quantities, optional add-ons, and tiered pricing. Clients can select or deselect optional items, and the total recalculates in real time. For contractors who sell packages (good/better/best tiers) or offer optional work (upgrade to premium fixtures, add a warranty package), this turns a static proposal into an interactive document that helps the client make decisions on the spot.</p>

<p>Pricing tables are only available on the Business plan and higher. The Free and Starter plans do not include them, which limits those tiers to documents without interactive quote sections.</p>

<h3>CRM Integrations and Deal Rooms</h3>

<p>PandaDoc integrates with HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, Zoho CRM, [QuickBooks Online](/quickbooks-online-review/), and other platforms. The Business plan includes CRM integrations that connect proposals to deals, so a sales rep can generate a proposal from a CRM record without manual data entry.</p>

<p>Deal rooms, available on the Business plan, create a shared space where buyers and sellers collaborate on a proposal. Clients can ask questions, request changes, and approve scope items within the document. For larger commercial contracts or design-build projects where the proposal goes through multiple revision rounds, this keeps the conversation tied to the document rather than scattered across email threads.</p>

<h3>Payment Collection</h3>

<p>Business plan and higher supports payment collection through Stripe, PayPal, and other gateways. You embed payment links directly in the document, so a client can pay a deposit or full balance without leaving the proposal. For contractors who collect deposits before starting work, or who offer payment plans, this eliminates the separate invoice-and-wait step. The client signs the contract and pays the deposit in one session.</p>

<h3>Mobile App</h3>

<p>The iOS and Android app handles document review, signing, tracking, and approval. For a contractor on a job site who needs to review and sign a subcontractor agreement, the mobile workflow works. Building a complex proposal with pricing tables and conditional content is better done on desktop. The mobile app is a companion to the desktop editor, not a replacement.</p>

<h2>What PandaDoc Gets Right</h2>

<h3>The Proposal Builder Is Genuinely Better Than Word</h3>

<p>If your current proposal process involves Word documents, PDF exports, and email attachments, PandaDoc is a clear upgrade. The drag-and-drop editor, content library, and conditional content system make proposals faster to build, easier to maintain, and more professional in presentation. The reusable templates mean you build the structure once and fill in project-specific details each time. For contractors who send 10 or more proposals per month, the time savings are real.</p>

<h3>The Free Plan Has Real Utility</h3>

<p>The Free plan at $0/mo includes the document editor, 5 eSignatures per month, up to 5 templates, real-time tracking, and 24/7 support. It does not include custom branding, pricing tables, CRM integrations, or content library. But for a contractor who needs occasional document signing (subcontractor agreements, simple scope letters, change order approvals), the free plan covers that. No credit card required, no time limit.</p>

<p>Be aware that the free plan is limited: 5 eSignatures per month, 60 free documents per year, and no custom branding. Once you need pricing tables, CRM integration, or more than 5 templates, you are on the Starter or Business plan.</p>

<h3>Tracking and Analytics Are Actionable</h3>

<p>PandaDoc's document tracking tells you when a client opens a proposal, which sections they viewed, how long they spent, and whether they forwarded it. For contractors who send proposals and then wait in silence, this is genuinely useful. You know when a client has seen the proposal, which means your follow-up timing is based on data, not guesswork. The recipient analytics (available on Business plan) add deeper insight into how recipients engage with the document.</p>

<h3>The Integration Ecosystem Is Broad</h3>

<p>PandaDoc connects to over 40 integrations including CRMs, payment gateways, cloud storage, and productivity tools. The CRM integrations with HubSpot, Salesforce, and Pipedrive are the most valuable for contractors with a defined sales process. The [QuickBooks Online](/quickbooks-online-review/) integration syncs document data with accounting, which reduces manual entry between the proposal and the invoice. For contractors already using these platforms, PandaDoc fits into the existing stack rather than creating a new data silo.</p>

<h2>Where PandaDoc Falls Short</h2>

<h3>It Is Not Contractor-Specific</h3>

<p>PandaDoc is a horizontal document automation platform. It was not built for contractors. It does not include job scheduling, dispatch, crew management, time tracking, equipment tracking, or any field-service functionality. If you need those features, you need a separate tool. A common setup for contractors is PandaDoc for proposals and contracts, paired with [Jobber](/jobber-review/) or [Buildertrend](/buildertrend-review/) for job management and operations. That works, but it means two software subscriptions and two systems to maintain.</p>

<p>If your proposals are simple quotes that your field-service tool already generates, PandaDoc may be overkill. See our [best proposal software guide](/best-proposal-software-for-contractors/) for alternatives that may fit a contractor workflow better.</p>

<h3>Per-Seat Pricing Adds Up for Teams</h3>

<p>The Business plan at $49/seat/month billed annually means a 5-person team pays $245/month, and a 10-person team pays $490/month. For a contractor evaluating document automation, the question is whether the proposal and contract workflow justifies that cost. A solo operator or small team may get by on the Free or Starter plans. But once you need CRM integrations, pricing tables, and content library, you are on Business, and the cost scales linearly with seats.</p>

<h3>Starter Plan Template Limit</h3>

<p>The Starter plan at $19/seat/month caps templates at 5. For a contractor who maintains separate proposal templates for different project types (kitchen remodel, bathroom renovation, commercial fit-out, service agreement, change order), 5 templates is restrictive. The Business plan removes the template cap and adds pricing tables, content library, and CRM integrations. Most contractors who use PandaDoc seriously will need Business, not Starter.</p>

<h3>Document Overage Fees</h3>

<p>PandaDoc charges per additional document beyond plan limits: $2 per document on Starter annual, $3.50 on Starter monthly, and $2.60 on Business monthly. For high-volume contractors sending dozens of proposals per month, these overage fees add to the monthly cost. The Free plan gives 60 documents per year; paid plans offer unlimited document uploads and eSignatures, so overage only applies to the "document" definition (any document delivered for signature, approval, or review).</p>

<h3>No Construction-Specific Document Types</h3>

<p>PandaDoc does not handle AIA forms (G702/G703), lien waivers, certified payroll, progress billing schedules, or construction-specific compliance documents. For contractors doing commercial work with payment application requirements, those documents need a different tool. PandaDoc handles the proposal and general contract workflow well but does not replace construction accounting software.</p>

<h2>Final Verdict</h2>

<p>PandaDoc is a solid document automation platform. The proposal builder, e-signature workflow, content library, and CRM integrations are genuinely useful for contractors who sell larger project scopes. The Free plan gives you a real entry point to test the editor and signature workflow before committing to paid features. The 14-day trial on paid plans requires no credit card.</p>

<p>The tradeoffs are clear. PandaDoc is not contractor-specific. It does not do job management, scheduling, dispatch, or field-service work. The per-seat pricing on Business adds up for teams. The Starter plan sits in a gap between Free and Business that most contractors will skip. And the platform does not handle construction-specific document types like AIA forms or lien waivers.</p>

<p>For contractors where the proposal and contract workflow is a real bottleneck in the sales process, PandaDoc belongs on the short list. Start with the Free plan to test the editor, then evaluate whether Business plan features (pricing tables, CRM, payment collection) justify the per-seat cost. For contractors who need simpler quoting inside an all-in-one tool, [Jobber](/jobber-review/) covers that without a separate document platform.</p>
