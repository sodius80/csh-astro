<p>Markate is a modular field service operations platform built for solo operators and very small crews. What makes it interesting in a category dominated by <a href="/reviews/jobber/">Jobber</a> ($70/mo) and <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro/">Housecall Pro</a> ($59/mo) is simple: Markate starts at <strong>$39.95/mo</strong> with annual billing, and you can add capabilities a la carte rather than paying for a full suite you might not need yet. For painting-specific needs, see our <a href="/best-software-for-painting-contractors/">best software for painting contractors</a> roundup.</p>

<p>For this review, I researched Markate's published pricing page, feature documentation, the affiliate program, Capterra reviews, and user discussions across contractor communities. This review is based on publicly available information — I have not interviewed the Markate team.</p>

<h2>Key Features: Covers the Basics Well</h2>

<h3>Job Management</h3>
<p>The centralized dashboard lets you assign tasks, log time, and track job progress. The scheduling calendar supports both single-day appointments and multi-day projects — important for painting jobs that span several days. SMS and email notifications keep the crew and the customer informed.</p>

<h3>Estimating & Proposals</h3>
<p>You can create branded estimates with flexible pricing packages and attach photos or video walkthroughs. The mobile quoting feature lets sales-oriented painters write estimates on-site and get digital signatures before leaving — a real advantage for closing jobs fast.</p>

<h3>Invoicing & Online Payments</h3>
<p>Integrated invoicing supports credit cards, ACH, PayPal, Square, and Stripe. Funds typically deposit within 1-3 business days. You can set milestone-based payment triggers with photo documentation to justify progress invoices — useful for larger painting projects with draw schedules.</p>

<h3>Mobile App</h3>
<p>The field mobile app covers on-the-go quoting, job checklists, photo documentation, check-ins, and instant client approvals or signatures. Users consistently praise the intuitive mobile interface and the ability to run most workflows from a smartphone.</p>

<h3>Customer Portal & Online Booking</h3>
<p>Clients can book services 24/7, view job status, invoices, and communicate through a secure portal. This feature is a $10/mo add-on but removes a lot of "when can you come out?" phone calls for a busy painter.</p>

<h2>Pros and Cons</h2>

<h3>What Markate does well</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Lowest entry price in the category.</strong> At $39.95/mo (annual), it is roughly half the cost of <a href="/reviews/jobber/">Jobber</a> or <a href="/reviews/housecall-pro/">Housecall Pro</a>. For a painter on a tight budget who just needs the basics, that is a meaningful difference.</li>
<li><strong>Transparent pricing.</strong> Every dollar amount is published on the website — no "contact sales" walls. You can calculate your exact monthly cost before signing up.</li>
<li><strong>Mobile-first design.</strong> The app is genuinely usable for most workflows. You do not need a desktop to run a painting business with Markate.</li>
<li><strong>Responsive support.</strong> US-based customer support via chat, phone, and email during business hours (MST). Users consistently report fast, helpful responses.</li>
<li><strong>No-contract flexibility.</strong> Month-to-month billing is available, and the 14-day free trial requires no credit card. Low commitment to start.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Where Markate falls short</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Add-on cost accumulation.</strong> The base price is deceptive if you need multiple features. Essential tools like review management, branded portal, online booking, and Zapier are all $10/mo each. Three add-ons erase the price advantage over competitors.</li>
<li><strong>Limited native reporting.</strong> Built-in analytics cover the basics (profit/loss, sales tax, demographics) but users who want custom dashboards or deep export capabilities report needing external tools.</li>
<li><strong>No painting-specific features.</strong> Unlike Jobber's paint-specific template store or Housecall Pro's industry-specific quoting, Markate is a general field service platform. No paint coverage calculators, no color matching tools.</li>
<li><strong>Non-refundable subscriptions.</strong> All payments are non-refundable, and cancellations only take effect at the end of the current billing cycle. If you pay annually and cancel month two, you do not get the remaining ten months back.</li>
<li><strong>Feature gaps vs competitors.</strong> Route optimization, AI tools, and advanced automation are missing compared to higher-priced competitors. Markate is a solid baseline tool, not an innovation leader.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Affiliate Program</h2>

<p>Markate runs an affiliate program through <a href="https://affiliate.markate.com" rel="nofollow">affiliate.markate.com</a>. The commission is a <strong>one-time $25 per sale</strong> (not recurring, not percentage-based), with a $250 minimum payout threshold. The sign-up process requires contacting Markate through their website — it is not a self-serve enrollment. Affiliates get a unique tracking link, real-time reporting on clicks and conversions, and access to banner creatives.</p>

<p>This is a small, flat-fee program compared to the recurring commission models at Housecall Pro or Jobber. For a content site, the $25 one-time payout with a $250 minimum means it takes 10 sales before seeing any revenue — a meaningful threshold for a smaller publisher.</p>

<h2>Final Verdict: Conditional — Budget-Friendly Basics, But the Add-Ons Tell the Real Story</h2>

<p>Markate is a genuinely affordable entry point for solo painters who need professional field service software and do not want to pay for features they will not use. At $39.95/mo (annual), it delivers estimating, scheduling, invoicing, payments, and a client portal — the core tools you need to look professional and get paid faster.</p>

<p>The modular add-on model is both the feature and the flaw. It keeps the base price low, which matters for a painter on a tight margin. But if you need review management, online booking, a branded portal, and Zapier — all reasonable needs for a growing painting business — you land at $69.95/mo or more, right in Jobber territory without getting Jobber's polish and depth.</p>

<p>Markate is best understood as a <strong>selective value play</strong>: buy only what you need, skip what you do not, and keep your software bill proportional to your revenue. That is a valid philosophy. But know the math before you start — the base price is not the price.</p>

<h2>Comparisons</h2>

<h3>Markate vs Jobber</h3>
<p><a href="/reviews/jobber/">Jobber</a> starts at $70/mo and includes client self-serve portal, automated follow-ups, automated review requests, and online booking in the base price. At Markate, those features are $10/mo add-ons each. A painter who needs three of them reaches the same monthly cost. Jobber's mobile app is more polished, and the QuickBooks integration is tighter. Markate wins only if you genuinely do not need the extras.</p>

<h3>Markate vs Housecall Pro</h3>
<p><a href="/reviews/housecall-pro/">Housecall Pro</a> starts at $59/mo (annual) with automated review requests and online booking included. Markate can look cheaper on paper at $39.95/mo, but adding those same features closes the gap to $59.95/mo. Housecall Pro also has stronger automated marketing tools. Markate wins for painters who want bare-bones basics at the lowest possible price.</p>
