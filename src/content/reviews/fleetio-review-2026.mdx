<p>Fleet maintenance is the kind of operational cost that quietly drains a budget. A truck breaks down, a part needs replacing, a repair shop overcharges - each event is small, but they add up fast. Fleetio approaches this problem by making maintenance the primary feature and everything else secondary. For a broader look at construction fleet tools, see our <a href="/best-equipment-tracking-software-contractors/">best equipment tracking software roundup</a>.</p>

<p>It is not a GPS tracking platform. It is not a construction-specific equipment tracker. It is a fleet maintenance management system that handles work orders, preventive maintenance schedules, parts inventory, fuel tracking, and repair shop coordination. If your main problem is keeping vehicles running and understanding what they actually cost per mile, Fleetio is worth a close look.</p>

<h2>Feature Deep Dive</h2>

<h3>Maintenance Workflow Automation</h3>
<p>The core of Fleetio is its maintenance engine. You create work orders, set preventive maintenance schedules based on mileage, engine hours, or calendar intervals, and assign them to in-house techs or external shops. The system generates automatic reminders and can escalate overdue tasks. For contractors running mixed fleets, the ability to set different PM schedules for different asset types - trucks on mileage intervals, excavators on engine hours - is useful without being over-engineered.</p>
<p>The catch is automation limits. The Essential tier includes zero automations. Professional caps at 5 automations. Premium allows up to 40. If you want automated PM scheduling across a diverse fleet, you need Professional or Premium.</p>

<h3>Outsourced Maintenance Shop Network</h3>
<p>Fleetio's integrated network of over 65,000 external repair shops across the US and Canada is a genuine differentiator. You can send work orders to shops electronically, receive cost estimates, approve or reject them in the platform, and track invoices. For fleets that do not run their own repair shops - which describes most contractors - this feature eliminates phone tag and paper invoice tracking.</p>
<p>The shop network includes automated approval workflows and real-time cost tracking. This is one of those features that sounds minor on paper but saves real time if you coordinate repairs across multiple locations.</p>

<h3>Parts and Inventory Management</h3>
<p>Fleetio tracks parts stock, sets reorder alerts based on minimum quantity thresholds, and links parts directly to work orders. This works for both in-house maintenance and outsourced repairs. You can track which parts were used on which asset and tie the cost back to the vehicle or equipment record.</p>
<p>For contractors who keep common parts (filters, belts, fluids) in stock for their shop, this prevents the "I thought we had one" surprise that delays a repair. The Premium tier adds deeper inventory controls like tire tracking and warranty monitoring.</p>

<h3>Fuel and Cost Tracking</h3>
<p>Fleetio integrates with major fuel card providers (WEX, Fuelman, Comdata) to pull transaction data automatically. You see fuel spend by vehicle, cost per mile, cost per hour, and fuel efficiency trends. Combined with the maintenance cost data, this gives a reasonably complete picture of total cost of ownership per asset.</p>
<p>This is where Fleetio's approach pays off for contractors who have never had this data. Knowing that Truck 7 costs $0.82 per mile while Truck 3 costs $0.51 per mile is actionable information that makes the decision to replace or repair a vehicle data-driven rather than gut-feel.</p>

<h3>Integrations and API</h3>
<p>Fleetio connects with over 100 telematics, fuel card, and accounting partners. The major telematics integrations include Geotab, Samsara, Verizon Connect, and Motive. On the accounting side, QuickBooks Online syncs work order costs and parts expenses. Fleetio also offers a public API for custom integrations.</p>
<p>The bring-your-own-telematics approach gives you flexibility - you are not locked into a hardware ecosystem. The trade-off is that you are managing two vendor relationships instead of one.</p>

<h2>What Fleetio Gets Right</h2>

<h3>Transparent pricing with no seat fees</h3>
<p>Fleetio publishes every dollar amount on its pricing page. $4 per vehicle per month for Essential, $7 for Professional, $10 for Premium. Unlimited users at every tier. A 14-day free trial with no credit card required. In a market where most fleet management platforms hide behind custom quotes, this is refreshing. You can calculate your annual cost in under a minute without talking to a sales rep.</p>
<p>For a 25-vehicle fleet on Essential, that is $100/month. For the same fleet on Premium, $250/month. No math required.</p>

<h3>Maintenance-first design that matches real priorities</h3>
<p>GPS tracking is flashy. Maintenance management is boring. But for most fleet operators, the boring problem - keeping vehicles running - is where the money goes. Fleetio leans into this. The interface is organized around what needs maintenance today, what is overdue, and what each asset is costing. There is no dashboard clutter from features you do not need.</p>

<h3>The repair shop network saves real time</h3>
<p>For fleets that outsource maintenance, the 65,000-shop network eliminates the back-and-forth of phone calls, email approvals, and lost paper invoices. You send a work order from Fleetio, the shop responds with an estimate, you approve it in the platform, and the invoice syncs back when the work is done. It is a small workflow improvement that compounds across dozens of repairs per year.</p>

<h3>Mixed asset support across vehicles, equipment, and tools</h3>
<p>Fleetio handles vehicles, heavy equipment, trailers, and small tools in a single platform. The Equipment Management add-on (starting around $0.50/asset/month) extends the same maintenance and tracking workflows to non-vehicle assets. For contractors who run trucks, excavators, and a shop full of tools, this avoids managing separate systems for different asset types.</p>

<h2>Where Fleetio Falls Short</h2>

<h3>No native GPS tracking</h3>
<p>Fleetio is upfront about this - it is a maintenance platform, not a GPS tracking platform. But the absence of native telematics means you cannot use Fleetio alone for theft prevention, real-time location, or geofencing. You need a separate telematics provider, which adds another subscription and another integration to manage. For contractors who want one platform for everything, this is a significant gap.</p>

<h3>Not purpose-built for construction equipment</h3>
<p>Fleetio handles vehicles and equipment well, but it does not have construction-specific features like job-site utilization tracking, equipment scheduling across projects, or integration with construction ERP systems like Procore or Viewpoint. Platforms like Tenna and HCSS Equipment360 are built for the specific workflows of construction equipment management. Fleetio is a general fleet platform that happens to work for contractor fleets.</p>

<h3>Essential plan limits and annual-only higher tiers</h3>
<p>The Essential tier caps at 100 vehicles and includes zero automations. If your fleet grows past 100 vehicles, or if you need automated PM scheduling, parts inventory management, or API access, you are on Professional at $7/vehicle/month or Premium at $10/vehicle/month - both annual commitment only. There is no month-to-month option for the tiers that include the features most growing fleets actually need.</p>

<h3>AI features are still developing</h3>
<p>Fleetio has introduced an AI Service Advisor and AI-powered insights, but these are less mature than what some competitors offer. Predictive maintenance - using machine learning to forecast failures before they happen - is not a core capability. Fleetio relies on rule-based preventive maintenance schedules rather than AI-driven anomaly detection. For most fleets, rule-based PM is sufficient. But if you are looking for the latest in predictive maintenance, Fleetio is not there yet.</p>

<h2>Final Verdict</h2>

<p>Fleetio solves the right problem - maintenance costs - with the right approach: transparent pricing, unlimited users, and a platform that centers on keeping assets running rather than tracking where they are. For contractors who run fleets of 5 to 200 vehicles and whose primary operational pain point is breakdowns, repair coordination, and understanding total cost of ownership, Fleetio is a strong choice with clear ROI.</p>

<p>The platform is less compelling if you need everything bundled: GPS, dash cams, ELD compliance, and maintenance in one subscription. Fleetio's bring-your-own-telematics model gives you flexibility but also means managing multiple vendor relationships. And if you are a construction contractor who needs construction-specific equipment tracking - job-site utilization, project-based equipment scheduling, Procore integration - you will find Fleetio's generalist approach limiting.</p>

<p>For the contractor who wants to stop guessing what their fleet costs to run and start having real data to make vehicle replacement and repair decisions, Fleetio delivers. The pricing is fair, the trial is no-commitment, and the maintenance workflows are genuinely well-designed.</p>
