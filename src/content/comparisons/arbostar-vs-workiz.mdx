# ArboStar vs Workiz: Tree Care Platform or Dispatch Tool?

ArboStar vs Workiz looks like a field service comparison, but these two platforms serve different trades and solve different operational problems. The decision is not about which feature list is longer. It is about what kind of work the business does and what kind of platform that work requires.

ArboStar is built for arborist and tree care businesses. It answers the question that established tree care companies face: how do I run CRM, scheduling, equipment tracking, field documentation, and job mapping in one system built specifically for tree work? Workiz is built for dispatch-first field service management. It answers a different question: how do I turn inbound calls into dispatched jobs fast and keep communication, routing, and payment tied to each job?

Those are different problems for different contractors. A tree care company that needs arborist CRM, live job maps, and safety documentation will not find those in a dispatch platform for locksmiths and plumbers. A service company that needs call handling, same-day dispatch, and AI answering will not get that from an arborist specialist. ## The buying split

### ArboStar is an arborist business management platform first

ArboStar's main advantage is specialization. The platform is built specifically for arborist and tree care businesses, not adapted from a general field service tool. Every module - CRM, scheduling, estimating, invoicing, equipment tracking, field documentation, job mapping - is designed around how tree care companies actually operate.

The arborist CRM records client tree history, service records, and communication history specific to tree work. For a company that returns to the same properties for pruning, removals, plant health, and storm cleanup, that depth matters. A salesperson can pull up prior work, and a crew can see photos and notes from the last visit without digging through files.

Live job maps and GPS positioning give managers a real-time view of where crews are and which equipment is tied up. Tree jobs are not interchangeable service calls. The equipment needs, crew composition, and site conditions change per job. A chipper truck, a bucket truck, and specific rigging gear may all need to arrive together at the right site. ArboStar's map and dispatch features reflect that operational reality.

Equipment tracking covers vehicles, tools, chainsaws, chippers, rigging gear, maintenance schedules, and fuel costs. For a multi-crew tree care company managing hundreds of thousands of dollars in equipment across multiple sites, that is not a nice-to-have. It is operational infrastructure.

The official pricing page lists a starting point of $250 per month, but the final quote depends on company size, license count, required features, integrations, and platform usage. Multiple-license discounts are available, and annual subscriptions include two months free. Because ArboStar requires a sales conversation to get a real price, it is harder to budget for before the demo. That is the trade-off for getting a tree-specific platform instead of a generalist tool.

Where ArboStar falls short: it is not a dispatch-first tool for service trades. A locksmith, plumber, or HVAC company would be paying for arborist-specific features they will not use. There is no built-in phone system, no AI answering, no call tracking. The mobile app covers job details, notes, photos, and schedule updates, but some users report it lacks functions available on desktop. Setup requires real configuration around crews, equipment, pricing, and accounting processes.

### Workiz is a dispatch and communication tool first

Workiz needs a different test. Picture a day where the office keeps answering calls, moving jobs around, chasing technician updates, texting customers, collecting payment on site, and checking which leads turned into booked work. Workiz is built for that reality.

The official Workiz pricing page uses request-pricing cards for its Standard, Pro, and Ultimate plans. Standard and Pro include the first 5 users. Extra members are published: Standard costs $55 per month per extra member on annual payment and Pro costs $65 per month per extra member on annual payment. The full bill depends on user count, Workiz Communication add-ons, phone and SMS usage, AI tools, payment hardware, and whether you need Ultimate-only modules like inventory, service plans, or purchase orders.

Standard is often the realistic baseline because it includes QuickBooks Online, custom fields, location tracking, service areas, subcontractor management, and lead tracking. Pro adds performance pay, Genius leads, Genius scheduling, custom reports, and 10 automations. Ultimate adds inventory management, service plans, sales proposals, purchase orders, equipment tracking, checklists, multi-day jobs, and franchise management.

Workiz Communication is sold separately. That category includes integrated phone, Genius Answering, call insights, smart messaging, ad tracking, two-way texting, call flows, call masking, and 10DLC registration. If those tools are the reason you want Workiz, do not compare the base tier alone. Ask for the all-in monthly number at your expected call volume, text volume, user count, and AI usage.

A 7-day free trial requires no credit card and lets you test the dispatch board, scheduling, and field app before talking to sales.

Where Workiz falls short: base plan dollar amounts are no longer shown as a simple public rate card. The entry paid plan is higher than simpler competitors. There are no arborist-specific features - no tree mapping, no tree service history, no arborist CRM. Equipment tracking requires the Ultimate quote. Limited integrations compared to broader field service platforms. For a comparison against a simpler dispatch tool, see our <a href="/clockshark-vs-workiz/">ClockShark vs Workiz comparison</a>.

## Pricing reality

### ArboStar: custom quote with a starting point

ArboStar uses customized pricing based on specific business needs, tools used, and platform usage. The official pricing page lists a starting price of $250 per month, but the final quote depends on company size, license count, required features, integrations, and platform usage. Multiple-license discounts are available, and annual subscriptions include two months free.

Pricing requires a sales conversation and plan selection for the final quote. That is not a reason to reject ArboStar. It is a reason to require a written quote before comparing it against Workiz. For ArboStar, the quote should separate:

- Base subscription and license count
- Required modules (CRM, scheduling, equipment tracking, field documentation, job mapping)
- QuickBooks or accounting integration setup
- Mobile app access and user roles
- Implementation, onboarding, and training costs
- Support level and 24/7 availability
- Renewal term, cancellation, and price-change language

If the sales quote cannot answer those items in writing, the comparison is not ready. ArboStar may still be the right platform for a tree care company, but the buyer cannot know the real cost without it.

### Workiz: request pricing with published add-on rates

Workiz Standard, Pro, and Ultimate all use request pricing on the official pricing page. The first 5 users are included on Standard and Pro. Extra members cost $55 per month on Standard and $65 per month on Pro when billed annually. A 7-day free trial requires no credit card.

The pricing structure means the full monthly bill depends on several variables:

- Base plan (Standard, Pro, or Ultimate) - priced per quote
- Extra members beyond the first 5 at $55 or $65 per member on annual billing
- Workiz Communication add-ons (phone, SMS, AI answering, call insights, ad tracking)
- Phone and SMS usage at your expected call and text volume
- Payment processing and hardware costs
- Ultimate-only modules if you need inventory, service plans, or purchase orders

If you need 10 users on Standard, that is 5 extra members at $55 each on annual billing, or $275 per month in extra-member fees alone, on top of the base plan price. The total cost is not knowable without a quote.

### Which is cheaper?

Neither product is cheap, and neither publishes a simple rate card. ArboStar starts from $250 per month with a custom quote. Workiz's base plan pricing is request-only, and the extra-member fees for teams beyond 5 users add up fast.

The real cost question is not a price comparison. It is whether the business does tree work or general service calls. A tree care company that needs arborist CRM, equipment tracking, live job maps, and safety documentation needs ArboStar or a similar specialist platform. A service company that needs dispatch, call handling, and routing needs Workiz. ## Where ArboStar wins

### Tree-specific CRM and service history

ArboStar's arborist CRM is built for tree-care customer management. Contacts, leads, quotes, job history, communication records, and service history are all tree-work aware. For a company managing hundreds of client properties with recurring pruning, removals, and plant health work, that depth matters. A crew arriving on site can see tree-specific notes, prior work photos, and safety documentation attached to the job. See our <a href="/best-tree-service-software/">best tree service software roundup</a> for how ArboStar compares to other arborist tools.

### Live job maps and equipment tracking

Tree work depends on more than an address and appointment time. The right crew, truck, chipper, equipment, access notes, and weather conditions affect the day. ArboStar's map and GPS positioning give managers a clearer view of where work is happening and which resources are tied up. Equipment tracking covers maintenance schedules, fuel costs, and GPS-linked assets across vehicles, tools, and rigging gear. See our <a href="/best-equipment-tracking-software-contractors/">best equipment tracking software roundup</a> for a broader view.

### Field documentation for arborist work

Safety forms, hazard notes, job photos, and crew instructions are central to tree work in a way they are not for general service calls. ArboStar's mobile workflow supports field documentation attached to specific tree jobs, not just generic job notes. For a company that needs OSHA-compliant safety records and site-specific hazard documentation, that is a meaningful differentiator.

### Estimating with field context

ArboStar's estimating workflow includes pre-entered rates, work types, job size, equipment needs, crew composition, photos, notes, and digital signatures. For tree proposals that depend on site conditions, tree species, and access constraints, that detail can reduce the gap between what sales promised and what the crew finds on site.

## Where Workiz wins

### Phone-to-dispatch workflow

Workiz is stronger when inbound calls are the business. Locksmiths, garage door companies, appliance repair teams, emergency plumbers, and same-day HVAC shops often care less about tree mapping and more about turning a call into a scheduled job fast. Workiz's local numbers, dispatch board, communication tools, and lead tracking fit that operating style better.

ArboStar has no phone system, no dispatch board for same-day service calls, and no call tracking. If your office lives on the phone, ArboStar will not solve that problem.

### Dispatch board and same-day routing

When jobs are added, moved, canceled, and reassigned during the day, the dispatch board matters. Workiz gives the office more control over who goes where next and what the technician sees before arriving. That can beat ArboStar's scheduling for teams that live in the board all day.

ArboStar has scheduling and a live job map, but it is built around tree work crews and equipment assignment, not the fast-changing, same-day dispatch flow that service companies need when call volume drives the schedule.

### Communication layer

Workiz Communication is a major part of the Workiz pitch: calls, texts, call flows, ad tracking, AI answering, smart messaging, and call insights attached to the job workflow. The caution is cost, because those tools are sold separately. Missed calls and messy texts can cost a dispatch-heavy shop real money, but only if the team uses the system every day.

ArboStar does not offer a comparable communication stack. If phone and text workflow is the operational pain point, Workiz is the right tool.

### All-in-one service workflow

Workiz combines scheduling, dispatching, client CRM, payments, local phone numbers, automations, online booking, and optional communication tools in one platform. ArboStar is focused on arborist business management. If the goal is a single platform that covers the full service workflow from call to payment for a non-tree-care trade, Workiz is the more complete tool.

### Better for Workiz's specific trades

Workiz is especially relevant for locksmiths, garage door, appliance repair, junk removal, HVAC, plumbing, and other service companies where crews run multiple short jobs per day and new demand keeps changing the schedule. ArboStar is more naturally suited to tree care companies with equipment-heavy operations and recurring property management. Workiz is the wrong fit for a multi-crew tree care company that needs arborist CRM records, live job maps, equipment tracking, safety documentation, and tree-specific field workflows. A tree care company could use Workiz for dispatch alongside ArboStar, but Workiz alone will not run a tree care business.

ArboStar is the wrong fit for a solo HVAC technician, a plumbing crew doing service calls, or a locksmith company that primarily needs call handling, same-day dispatch, and payment collection. The custom pricing, arborist-specific features, and implementation effort are wasted on operations that just need a dispatch platform. For those contractors, Workiz is the more direct solution. ## Can you use both?

Yes, and some tree care companies should. A multi-crew tree care operation running ArboStar for CRM, scheduling, equipment tracking, and field documentation might still need a dispatch and communication tool if the office handles high call volume. In that case, Workiz could run alongside ArboStar specifically for call handling, dispatch, customer communication, and payment collection. For how ArboStar compares to another tree-care specialist, see our <a href="/arbostar-vs-handoff/">ArboStar vs Handoff comparison</a>.

The question is whether ArboStar's scheduling and communication features are sufficient for the office workflow, or whether the company needs the deeper phone and dispatch tools Workiz provides: built-in phone, AI answering, call insights, two-way texting, and a dedicated same-day dispatch board. If ArboStar's CRM and scheduling cover the office workflow, adding Workiz is redundant. If it does not, the two tools complement each other.

## FAQ

### What trades is Workiz built for?

Workiz is built for HVAC, plumbing, locksmith, appliance repair, garage door, junk removal, and electrical service work. ArboStar is built for arborist services, tree care, and landscaping companies with equipment-heavy operations.
