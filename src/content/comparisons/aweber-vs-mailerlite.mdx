# AWeber vs MailerLite for Contractor Email Marketing

AWeber and MailerLite are two of the most affordable email marketing tools a contractor can choose, and both offer a free plan that works for a small list. They overlap on the basics: email campaigns, drag-and-drop editors, landing pages, signup forms, automation workflows, and subscriber management. The differences come down to template library size, free-plan subscriber limits, automation depth, and where each tool draws the line between affordability and feature access.

AWeber gives more subscribers on its free plan and a larger template library. MailerLite gives a cleaner editor, a lower paid entry point, and unlimited monthly emails on its Power tier. Neither tool is a CRM or field service platform. Both help contractors communicate with leads and customers through email.

## Quick verdict

Pick AWeber when templates and deliverability reputation are the priority. The official AWeber pricing page shows a free plan for up to 500 subscribers and 3,000 emails per month. Paid plans start with Lite at $15 per month or $12.50 per month billed annually. Plus is $30 per month or $20 per month billed annually, which includes 12x subscriber sends and behavioral automation. Done For You is $20 per month billed annually plus a $79 setup fee (regularly $599). The official page lists a 14-day free trial with no credit card required.

Pick MailerLite when value and simplicity matter more. Atlas-verified MailerLite pricing from the official pricing page shows a free plan at $0 for up to 250 subscribers and 2,500 monthly emails. At the 500-subscriber level, Comfort is $12 per month or $10.80 per month billed yearly. Power is $25 per month or $22.50 per month billed yearly, and includes unlimited monthly emails. Enterprise is custom for businesses with more than 200,000 subscribers. The official page lists a 14-day premium trial with no credit card required.

Skip both if the real problem is field operations. Neither product replaces [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), [ServiceTitan](/servicetitan-review/), or a broader [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) platform. AWeber and MailerLite help before and after the sale. They do not schedule crews, dispatch technicians, track job costing, manage routes, or invoice field work.

## The fundamental difference: templates and history vs editor and automation

AWeber is built around the idea that a contractor who starts with a large template library, reliable deliverability, and support on every plan will get campaigns out the door faster. The platform has been sending email since 1998. That history shows up in its deliverability reputation, its template catalog, and its support model. The tradeoff is that the interface and automation capabilities feel more traditional compared to newer tools.

MailerLite is built around the idea that email marketing should be affordable, clean, and easy to own. The drag-and-drop editor is one of the simplest in the category. The automation builder covers practical sequences without a steep learning curve. The tradeoff is a smaller template library and fewer legacy integrations than a tool that has been in the market for decades.

This is why feature count does not settle the comparison. AWeber has hundreds of templates and phone support on every plan. MailerLite has a cleaner editor and unlimited emails on Power. They are not competing for the same buyer. They serve contractors at different points on the simplicity-versus-depth axis.

## Free plan comparison

Both products offer a free plan, and this is where the comparison gets interesting for a small contractor.

AWeber's free plan covers up to 500 subscribers and 3,000 emails per month. That gives a contractor more room on the free tier. A plumbing company with 350 past customers and leads can use AWeber indefinitely without paying. The free plan includes the drag-and-drop builder, basic templates, landing pages, web push notifications, and ecommerce tools. Limitations include 1 email list, 3 landing pages, 3 email automations, 1 custom segment, and AWeber branding on messages.

MailerLite's free plan covers up to 250 subscribers and 2,500 monthly emails. The subscriber cap is lower, but the free plan includes 2 user seats, 1 website, 1 landing page, 3 active forms, and 3 active automations with 5 steps each. A 14-day premium trial is also available with no credit card required, giving temporary access to Comfort and Power features before reverting to free-plan limits.

For a contractor just starting to build a list, both free plans are genuinely usable. AWeber gives more subscribers before requiring a paid upgrade. MailerLite gives a slightly cleaner interface and multi-seat access on the free plan. The practical difference matters most between 250 and 500 subscribers: AWeber is free, MailerLite requires a paid plan.

## When AWeber makes more sense

AWeber makes more sense when the contractor values templates, deliverability, and a larger free-plan subscriber cap. A roofing company with 400 past customers who wants to send a monthly newsletter and seasonal reminders can stay on AWeber's free plan indefinitely. That is real savings for a small office.

Official AWeber feature pages describe a drag-and-drop email builder, AI subject line assistant, RSS-to-email, HTML emails, thousands of professional images, email templates, signup forms, WordPress plugin, subscriber tagging, ecommerce, and integrations with Facebook, PayPal, WordPress, Shopify, WooCommerce, and hundreds more. For a contractor, that maps to practical marketing work:

- send a monthly newsletter to past customers
- run a seasonal reactivation campaign before busy season
- build a landing page for one service area
- collect leads through a signup form on the website
- tag subscribers by service interest or location
- sell a digital guide or paid consultation through the platform

AWeber also includes web push notifications on every plan. The Lite plan supports up to 50,000 subscribers for web push, which can be useful for time-sensitive alerts like storm-damage inspection availability. The Plus plan adds unlimited web push, behavioral automation, email split testing, AMP emails, dynamic content, and free migration.

The tradeoff is that AWeber's automation is more basic than MailerLite's. The interface feels traditional compared to newer tools. If the contractor needs deeper multi-step automation workflows with complex branching, AWeber may not be the right fit. For a broader comparison, AWeber also appears in the [best email marketing software for contractors](/best-email-marketing-software-contractors/) roundup.

## When MailerLite makes more sense

MailerLite makes more sense when the contractor wants the email layer to be affordable, clean, and focused on automation. The office may already have leads in a spreadsheet, a CRM, or a field service platform. The missing piece is consistent communication.

Official MailerLite pages position the platform around email marketing, automation, landing pages, subscription forms, segmentation, reporting, and a drag-and-drop editor. For a contractor, that maps to practical marketing work:

- send newsletters to past customers
- create a form for a seasonal offer
- build an automated sequence after a quote request
- segment a list by service interest or location
- send maintenance reminders before busy season
- review campaign reporting before the next send

MailerLite's automation covers welcome sequences, tag-based triggers, time delays, and workflow branching. The free plan allows 3 active automations, Comfort allows 50, and Power allows unlimited with multiple automation triggers. That tiered approach means a contractor starts with basic sequences and expands as the list and needs grow.

The Power plan at $22.50 per month billed yearly is where MailerLite differentiates on volume. It includes unlimited monthly emails, unlimited automations, unlimited forms, unlimited landing pages, and unlimited user seats. That matters when the list grows and sending frequency increases. No need to monitor email caps.

MailerLite also stands out on built-in monetization. The platform supports selling digital products, paid newsletter subscriptions, and appointment bookings directly. A contractor who runs a paid consultation or sells a digital guide can do that inside MailerLite without a separate e-commerce tool.

## Automation and nurture

Both platforms handle practical email automation, but the depth differs.

AWeber's automation is straightforward. The free and Lite plans include basic autoresponders and email automations with limits on the number of active automations. The Plus plan unlocks behavioral automation, email split testing, and dynamic content. For a contractor who wants welcome sequences, tag-based triggers, and simple time-delay paths, AWeber covers the work. The limitation is that the automation builder is not as visual or flexible as MailerLite's, and contact scoring is not available.

MailerLite's automation is more visual and tiered. The free plan allows 3 active automations with up to 5 steps each. Comfort expands to 50 automations with 100 steps each. Power adds multiple automation triggers and unlimited automations. The visual workflow builder lets a contractor map out a welcome sequence, a quote follow-up path, or a reactivation campaign without technical setup. For more automation-focused options, see the [MailerLite vs Moosend](/mailerlite-vs-moosend/) comparison.

The decision signal: choose AWeber when automation needs are basic and the template library matters more. Choose MailerLite when the contractor wants a visual automation builder and room to grow from 3 workflows to unlimited.

## Templates and editor

This is where AWeber has a clear advantage. AWeber offers hundreds of professional, mobile-responsive email templates built for different industries and use cases. A contractor who does not want to design an email from scratch can pick a template, swap in their own copy and images, and send. That matters for a small office without a designer.

MailerLite has a clean drag-and-drop editor that is widely regarded as one of the simplest in the email marketing category. The template library is smaller than AWeber's, but the editor makes it easy to build a custom email quickly. The rich text editor and plain text editor give options for different comfort levels. MailerLite also offers a subscriber preference center, smart sending, and personalized content blocks on Comfort and Power.

For a contractor who wants to send the first campaign within an hour of signing up, MailerLite is the faster path to a clean email. For a contractor who wants to start from a polished template, AWeber is the better starting point.

## Landing pages and forms

Both products support lead capture. AWeber includes landing pages on every plan, with 3 landing pages on the free and Lite plans and unlimited on Plus. MailerLite includes 1 website and 1 landing page on the free plan, 10 on Comfort, and unlimited on Power.

For contractors, the tactical work is similar on day one:

- build a page for one offer
- capture a name, email, and project interest
- tag or segment the contact
- send a first response quickly
- follow up with useful reminders instead of generic blasts

AWeber includes web push notifications on every plan, which MailerLite does not offer. For time-sensitive alerts like storm-season availability or scheduling openings, web push can be a practical addition to email campaigns.

## Integrations and ecosystem

AWeber lists integrations with Facebook, PayPal, WordPress, Shopify, WooCommerce, and hundreds more. The platform has been in the market since 1998, so its integration library is mature and well-documented. Free migration is available on Plus and higher tiers.

MailerLite lists 100+ integrations including Zapier, Make, WordPress, Shopify, WooCommerce, and AI tools like Claude and ChatGPT. The ecosystem is smaller than AWeber's in raw count but covers the same practical bases for a contractor. MailerLite also supports paid newsletter subscriptions, digital product sales, and appointment bookings directly within the platform.

For most contractors, both integration libraries cover the basics: WordPress, Shopify, Zapier, and forms. The difference matters when the contractor relies on a specific integration or wants AI-assisted content creation tools built into the platform.

## Support

AWeber offers 24/7 support via chat, email, and phone on every plan, including free. That is a meaningful advantage for a contractor who may need help outside business hours. The Plus plan adds priority support.

MailerLite offers 24/7 email support on Comfort and 24/7 live chat and email support on Power. The free plan includes 24/7 email and chat support for the first 14 days only. After the trial period, free-plan support is limited. For a contractor on a free plan who needs ongoing help, AWeber has the edge.

## Contractor operations fit

Neither product should be sold internally as contractor operations software.

AWeber can help with campaigns, landing pages, forms, automations, web push, ecommerce, and subscriber management. It will not replace scheduling, estimating, dispatch, production tracking, job costing, payments, inventory, routes, or field mobile workflows.

MailerLite can help with campaigns, forms, landing pages, segmentation, automation, and reporting. It will not replace a CRM pipeline, service calendar, crew board, estimate workflow, job costing system, or invoice process.

## Where AWeber falls short

AWeber's biggest drawback in this comparison is the paid entry point. Lite at $12.50 per month billed annually is not expensive, but it sits above MailerLite Comfort at $10.80 per month billed annually. The gap is small, but for a cost-conscious contractor, MailerLite is cheaper at the first paid tier.

The second drawback is automation depth. AWeber's automation is basic relative to MailerLite's visual workflow builder or ActiveCampaign's multi-step sequences. Contact scoring, event-based triggers, and complex branching are not part of the AWeber toolkit on lower tiers. The Plus plan adds behavioral automation, but the builder itself is not as visual or flexible.

The third drawback is the interface. AWeber has been in the market since 1998, and the platform shows its age in the editor and navigation. The drag-and-drop builder works, but it does not feel as modern or clean as MailerLite's. For a contractor who values a polished interface, AWeber may feel dated. For more modern alternatives, see [AWeber vs Pipedrive](/aweber-vs-pipedrive/) or [AWeber vs Moosend](/aweber-vs-moosend/).

## Where MailerLite falls short

MailerLite's biggest drawback is the free-plan subscriber cap. At 250 subscribers, the free plan is lower than AWeber's 500-subscriber free plan. A contractor with 300 past customers and leads cannot use MailerLite's free plan and must move to Comfort at $10.80 per month billed annually. AWeber would cover that same list for free.

The second drawback is template library size. MailerLite has a smaller catalog than AWeber. A contractor who wants to start from a polished, industry-specific template will find fewer options. The drag-and-drop editor is clean, but the starting library is thinner.

The third drawback is support on the free plan. MailerLite's free plan includes 24/7 email and chat support for the first 14 days only. After that, free-plan support is limited. AWeber offers 24/7 chat, email, and phone support on every plan including free, with no time limit.

## How to choose

Start with the list size and what matters most to the office.

If the list is between 250 and 500 subscribers, AWeber is free and MailerLite is not. That alone may settle the decision. A contractor with 350 past customers and leads can use AWeber indefinitely without paying.

If the list is under 250 subscribers, both free plans work. MailerLite gives a cleaner editor and multi-seat access on the free plan. AWeber gives more templates and phone support. Pick based on whether the office values a polished interface or a larger template library.

If the list is over 500 subscribers and the contractor is paying, compare the paid tiers. MailerLite Comfort at $10.80 per month annual is cheaper than AWeber Lite at $12.50 per month annual. But check what each plan includes. MailerLite Comfort has AI writing assistant, personalized content blocks, and A/B testing. AWeber Lite has web push notifications, ecommerce tools, and a larger template library. MailerLite Power at $22.50 per month annual adds unlimited emails. AWeber Plus at $20 per month annual adds behavioral automation but caps sends at 12x subscriber count.

If unlimited email volume matters, MailerLite Power is the stronger value. If templates, phone support, and a larger free-plan subscriber cap matter, AWeber is the better fit.
