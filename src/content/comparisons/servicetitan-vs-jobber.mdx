## Where Jobber wins

### 💰 Pricing Transparency — What you see is what you pay

<p>Jobber publishes Core, Connect, Grow, and Plus tiers. Current annual starting prices are Core $29/mo, Connect $99/mo, Grow $149/mo, and Plus $529/mo, with promotional first-year and monthly/no-commitment pricing able to differ. The same page lists included users of 1, 5, 10, and 15 respectively, with extra users at $29/user. ServiceTitan does not publish dollar pricing; its pricing page says pricing is per technician and asks buyers to request pricing. Compare the written ServiceTitan quote against Jobber's plan and user math before assuming the premium platform will pay back.</p>

### 🚀 Trial-first setup — Easier to evaluate before sales

<p>Jobber can be evaluated through a 14-day trial before choosing a plan. ServiceTitan's pricing page sends buyers into a request-pricing or demo path, which is a better fit when you are ready to scope implementation, reporting, and process changes with sales.</p>

### 📱 Field workflow — Test Jobber, demo ServiceTitan

<p>The safer claim is not that one mobile app is universally better. Jobber's trial lets the office and field team test scheduling, quotes, invoices, and payments before committing. ServiceTitan lists mobile estimates in Essentials and The Works, so field workflow should be validated in the demo and written package scope.</p>

### 🆓 Free Trial — Test before you commit

<p>Jobber offers a 14-day free trial with no credit card required. ServiceTitan's public pricing page points to request pricing/demo rather than a published self-service trial.</p>

## Where ServiceTitan wins

### 📊 Advanced reporting and operations controls — Built for deeper management review

<p>ServiceTitan's public pricing page lists advanced reporting, configurable payroll, commission tracking, and customizable memberships in The Works, with payroll management in Essentials. Jobber lists job costing in Grow, so the gap is not simple availability; it is whether Jobber's reporting depth is enough for the management reviews you run.</p>

### 📈 Marketing Attribution — Know what's working

<p>ServiceTitan highlights Adaptive Marketing, describing campaign statistics that connect ad spend to incoming revenue. Jobber's pricing page emphasizes tools such as Marketing Suite and Pipeline on Plus, so do not assume parity or absence from plan names alone; compare attribution reports during the ServiceTitan demo and Jobber trial if channel ROI matters.</p>

### 📞 Call Center Management — Built for volume

<p>ServiceTitan lists call booking in every package and highlights call recording for performance review. If your office handles high call volume, validate call booking, recording, routing, and reporting in the ServiceTitan demo against Jobber's lighter booking and customer-communication workflow.</p>

### 📋 Pricebook Control — Validate flat-rate depth in demo

<p>ServiceTitan lists Pricebook in every package. For flat-rate shops, that can matter more than basic quoting, but confirm the exact pricebook controls, price updates, and package limits in the demo and written quote.</p>

## FAQ

### At what team size should I switch from Jobber to ServiceTitan?

<p>There is no universal headcount trigger. The inflection point is usually when dispatch, advanced reporting, pricebook control, call booking/review, memberships, payroll/commission tracking, and management accountability become bigger constraints than subscription cost or setup speed. At that point, ServiceTitan may justify a written per-technician quote and implementation work. Before that, Jobber's published tiers and trial are easier to validate.</p>

### Can I migrate my data from Jobber to ServiceTitan?

<p>Maybe, but confirm the scope before signing. ServiceTitan has onboarding/import documentation, but a Jobber-to-ServiceTitan migration can depend on what exports are available, which records can be mapped, and how much history or attachment cleanup is needed. Ask the sales/onboarding team to document the import fields, timing, excluded data, and cleanup responsibilities in writing.</p>

### Is ServiceTitan overkill for smaller HVAC contractors?

<p>Often, but not because of an unsupported tech-count cutoff. ServiceTitan's quoted packages make the most sense when advanced reporting, call booking/review, pricebook control, memberships, payroll/commission tracking, and implementation support are worth the sales process. A smaller HVAC shop should get a written quote and compare it against Jobber's published tiers, included users, add-user pricing, and trial results.</p>

### Why can ServiceTitan cost more than Jobber?

<p>ServiceTitan does not publish fixed plan prices; its pricing page says pricing is per technician and built around business goals. Its packages include operations features such as call booking, pricebook, mobile estimates, payroll/configurable payroll, advanced reporting, commission tracking, and customizable memberships. Jobber publishes plan prices and user allowances, so the right comparison is the written ServiceTitan proposal against Jobber's current plan, user, add-user, and promotional terms.</p>
