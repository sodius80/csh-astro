## Where Jobber wins

### 💰 Pricing Transparency — What you see is what you pay

<p>Jobber publishes its pricing on its website: $49/month (Core), $149/month (Connect), $199/month (Grow). What you see is what you pay. ServiceTitan doesn't publish pricing at all — you have to sit through a sales demo to get a quote. Based on community-reported data, contractors pay $245-$500+ per technician per month plus implementation fees of $5,000-$50,000+. For a 5-tech shop, that's $15,000-$30,000/year vs Jobber's $1,400/year.</p>

### 🚀 Ease of Use — Simpler and faster to adopt

<p>Jobber's interface is cleaner and more intuitive for office staff and field techs. The learning curve is significantly lighter. ServiceTitan requires dedicated training and typically a full-time admin to manage the system effectively.</p>

### 📱 Mobile App — Better field experience

<p>Jobber's mobile app consistently gets higher ratings from field technicians. It's faster, more intuitive, and handles the daily workflow of scheduling, job updates, and payments without complexity.</p>

### 🆓 Free Trial — Test before you commit

<p>Jobber offers a 14-day free trial with no credit card required. ServiceTitan requires a sales demo and custom quote — there's no self-service evaluation option.</p>

## Where ServiceTitan wins

### 📊 Job Costing — Built for financial visibility

<p>ServiceTitan's job costing is genuinely enterprise-grade. You can track profitability by job type, technician, material cost, and labor hour. For contractors running $2M+ in revenue, this visibility is essential for scaling profitably. Jobber's reporting is adequate for small shops but lacks this depth.</p>

### 📈 Marketing Attribution — Know what's working

<p>ServiceTitan tracks which marketing channels are driving profitable jobs. You can see the ROI of every dollar spent on advertising, SEO, or lead generation. This is a core value proposition for growing operations. Jobber does not offer marketing attribution.</p>

### 📞 Call Center Management — Built for volume

<p>ServiceTitan includes call center tools with call tracking, scripting, and performance metrics. For operations handling high call volumes, this is a genuine advantage. Jobber is not built for call center operations.</p>

### 📋 Pricebook Optimization — Flat-rate pricing at scale

<p>ServiceTitan's pricebook system is the most mature in the industry. You can optimize pricing by job type, location, technician skill level, and seasonal demand. For operations that rely on flat-rate pricing, this is a significant advantage.</p>

## FAQ

### At what team size should I switch from Jobber to ServiceTitan?

<p>There's no universal answer, but the inflection point is typically 15-20 technicians and $2M+ in annual revenue. At that scale, you're likely spending enough on marketing and managing enough operational complexity that ServiceTitan's advanced tools start generating measurable ROI. Below that, most contractors find Jobber covers 90% of their needs at 10% of the cost.</p>

### Can I migrate my data from Jobber to ServiceTitan?

<p>Yes, ServiceTitan offers data migration as part of their onboarding process. Customer records, job history, and contact information can typically be imported. However, the migration isn't instant — expect a 2-4 week process, and some data (like custom fields or attachments) may not transfer cleanly. Plan for a transition period where both systems run in parallel.</p>

### Is ServiceTitan overkill for HVAC contractors under 10 techs?

<p>In most cases, yes. ServiceTitan's core value propositions — pricebook optimization, marketing attribution, and call center management — scale with team size. A 5-tech HVAC shop will pay $15,000-$30,000/year for ServiceTitan but won't use half the features. Jobber Grow at $1,400/year covers scheduling, dispatching, quoting, and invoicing with a fraction of the complexity.</p>

### Why is ServiceTitan so much more expensive than Jobber?

<p>ServiceTitan is built for scale. It includes enterprise features like marketing attribution, call center management, advanced job costing, and pricebook optimization that small businesses don't need. The pricing reflects the depth of the platform and the cost of supporting large operations. For a 10+ tech company, the ROI can justify the cost. For smaller shops, it rarely does.</p>
