# AWeber vs Moosend for Contractor Email Marketing

In this AWeber vs Moosend comparison, both are affordable email marketing platforms built for small businesses and growing lists. They overlap on the fundamentals: email campaigns, automation workflows, landing pages, forms, segmentation, and reporting. The differences come down to pricing structure, free plan availability, trial length, and how each tool handles send volume and feature access.

That matters for contractors. A small roofing company with 200 past customers needs a different tool than a plumbing company with 2,000 newsletter subscribers. The right choice depends on three questions: Do you need a free plan? Do you want the lowest monthly Pro price? Do you prefer pay-as-you-go or a subscription?

## Quick verdict

Pick AWeber if you want a free plan you can use indefinitely. The official AWeber pricing page lists a free plan at $0 for up to 500 subscribers and 3,000 emails per month, with access to the drag-and-drop editor, templates, signup forms, landing pages, and basic automations. Paid plans start with Lite at $15 per month or $12.50 per month billed yearly, and Plus at $30 per month or $20 per month billed yearly. AWeber has been sending email since 1998, and that longevity translates into strong deliverability and responsive support across every plan tier.

Pick Moosend if you want the lowest paid Pro entry point. The official Moosend pricing page shows Pro at $7 per month when paid annually or $9 per month on monthly billing for 500 contacts. The Pro plan includes unlimited email campaigns, marketing automation workflows, subscription forms, landing pages, reporting, and SMTP. Moosend does not offer a free plan, but the 30-day free trial requires no credit card and includes full Pro feature access for up to 1,000 contacts.

Skip both if the main problem is field operations. Neither AWeber nor Moosend replaces [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), [ServiceTitan](/servicetitan-review/), or broader [field service software](/best-field-service-software/). Both tools help contractors communicate with leads and customers through email. They do not dispatch crews, route technicians, manage job costing, or run field workflows.

## When AWeber makes more sense

AWeber makes more sense when the contractor wants a free plan that lasts. Many small contractor businesses have fewer than 500 past customers, leads, and newsletter subscribers combined. For those companies, AWeber's free plan is not a trial. It is a permanent tool.

A contractor could use the free plan to:

- send a monthly newsletter to past customers
- run a seasonal reactivation campaign before busy season
- collect leads through a website form
- build a simple landing page for one service area
- send quote follow-up emails manually

The free plan has limits: 3,000 emails per month and a cap on active automations, forms, and landing pages. But for a small list, that may be enough. When the list grows past 500 subscribers, Lite at $12.50 per month billed yearly gives access to advanced message analytics, web push notifications, and up to 10x subscriber email sends. Plus at $20 per month billed yearly expands to 12x subscriber sends, unlimited automations, unlimited landing pages, advanced reporting, and AWeber branding removal.

AWeber also stands out on template library and support. The platform includes hundreds of mobile-responsive email templates, a smart designer that auto-generates branded templates, and an AI subject line assistant. Support is available 24/7 via chat, email, and phone on every plan, including the free plan. For a contractor who is not technically inclined and wants someone to talk to when something breaks, AWeber is the safest pick.

## When Moosend makes more sense

Moosend makes more sense when the contractor wants the lowest paid monthly price. At $7 per month billed annually, Moosend Pro is cheaper than AWeber Lite ($12.50) and AWeber Plus ($20). The Pro plan includes unlimited email campaigns and sends from the start, so there is no need to upgrade tiers just to send more emails at the same subscriber count.

A contractor could use Moosend Pro to:

- send unlimited email campaigns to up to 500 contacts
- build landing pages and subscription forms
- run marketing automation workflows with behavioral triggers
- segment contacts by service interest, location, or customer type
- review campaign reporting and A/B test results
- send transactional emails through SMTP

Moosend also offers something AWeber does not: pay-as-you-go email credits. For contractors who send emails seasonally or infrequently, credits start at $350 for 350,000 emails and never expire. That pricing model avoids a monthly subscription when the business only sends a few large campaigns per year. A roofing company that runs one storm-season blast and one spring inspection reminder could find credits cheaper than 12 months of any subscription.

The 30-day free trial is longer than AWeber's 14-day trial and requires no credit card. That gives a contractor more time to test automations, templates, and campaign workflows before paying. The trial includes full Pro feature access for up to 1,000 contacts, so the testing period is not feature-limited.

## Pricing comparison

Both tools are affordable, but the pricing structures differ in ways that matter for contractor budgets.

Paid plans for 500 subscribers start with Lite at $15 per month, or $12.50 per month billed yearly. Plus is $30 per month, or $20 per month billed yearly, and includes 12x subscriber email sends, unlimited automations, and advanced reporting. Done For You is $20 per month billed yearly plus a $79 setup fee (promotional pricing from $599 standard). AWeber Unlimited is $899 per month for high-volume senders.

Atlas-verified Moosend pricing lists Pro at $7 per month when paid annually or $9 per month on monthly billing for 500 contacts. The Pro plan includes unlimited email campaigns and sends. Moosend+ and Enterprise are custom-quote plans. Pay-as-you-go email credits are published separately: 350,000 credits for $350, 500,000 for $500, 750,000 for $750, and 1,000,000 for $1,000.

The simple rule: AWeber is cheaper when the list is small enough for the free plan. Moosend is cheaper when the list has outgrown free and the contractor wants the lowest monthly subscription or prefers credits over subscriptions.

## Email marketing and automation

Both platforms cover the same email marketing fundamentals: drag-and-drop editors, templates, segmentation, A/B testing, and automation workflows. The differences are in approach and feature access.

AWeber's automation covers welcome sequences, tag-based triggers, time delays, and behavioral automation. The free plan allows a limited number of active automations. Lite expands the send cap to 10x subscriber max. Plus removes automation count limits and includes unlimited landing pages, custom segments, and users. That tiered approach means a contractor starts with basic sequences and expands as the list and needs grow. AWeber also offers an AI subject line assistant, web push notifications, and a smart designer that auto-generates branded templates from a website URL.

Moosend's automation is included in full from the Pro plan. There is no tiered limit on published automation workflows. The official feature pages describe a visual workflow builder, pre-built automation templates, multi-step workflows, behavioral triggers (page views, product views, purchases), and audience discovery. For contractors, that means welcome sequences, quote follow-up paths, and reactivation campaigns can all run without worrying about an automation count limit.

The decision signal: choose AWeber when the automation needs are straightforward, the template library matters, and the tiered approach keeps costs low. Choose Moosend when the contractor wants unlimited automations from the Pro plan and behavioral triggers tied to website activity. ## Free plan vs free trial

This is the biggest structural difference between the two platforms.

AWeber offers a genuine free plan. It is not a trial. A contractor with 200 past customers and leads can use AWeber indefinitely at $0, sending up to 3,000 emails per month. The free plan includes the drag-and-drop editor, templates, signup forms, landing pages, and basic automations. The limitation is volume: 500 subscribers, 3,000 emails, and capped feature counts on automations, forms, and landing pages.

Moosend does not offer a free plan. The 30-day free trial gives full Pro access for up to 1,000 contacts with no credit card required, but it expires. After 30 days, the contractor must move to a paid Pro plan, Moosend+, Enterprise, or pay-as-you-go credits.

For a cost-conscious contractor, the math is simple. If the list is under 500 subscribers and 3,000 monthly emails, AWeber is free forever. Once the list exceeds that limit, Moosend Pro at $7 per month annual is cheaper than AWeber Lite at $12.50 per month annual.

The crossover point matters. A contractor at 600 subscribers pays $12.50 per month on AWeber Lite or $7 per month on Moosend. At 500 subscribers, the comparison is AWeber Lite at $12.50 versus Moosend Pro at $7, both billed annually. Moosend is cheaper per month, but AWeber includes web push notifications, an AI subject line assistant, and phone support that Moosend does not match at the same tier.

## Integrations and ecosystem

AWeber integrates with hundreds of third-party platforms, including WordPress, Shopify, WooCommerce, PayPal, Facebook, Zapier, and Etsy. The platform also includes a WordPress plugin for direct signup form embedding, an API for developers, and a certified experts marketplace for contractors who want to hire implementation help. Free account migration service is available to all tiers.

Moosend lists 80+ integrations, including WordPress, Shopify, WooCommerce, Zapier, and CMS platforms. The integration library is smaller than AWeber's, and the community of third-party tutorials and templates is also smaller. Moosend does support API access, SMTP, and transactional email on Pro, which gives developers more flexibility.

For most contractors, both integration libraries cover the basics: WordPress, Shopify, Zapier, and forms. The difference matters when the contractor relies on a specific integration or wants access to a larger marketplace of templates and certified experts.

## Landing pages and forms

Both platforms include landing page builders and form creation. AWeber's free plan allows limited landing pages and signup forms. Lite expands to 3 landing pages and 3 forms. Plus removes limits entirely with unlimited landing pages, forms, and automations.

Moosend's free trial allows 1 unpublished landing page and 1 unpublished subscription form. The Pro plan includes unlimited subscription forms and landing pages.

For a contractor building a simple landing page for a neighborhood campaign or a service-specific lead capture form, both tools handle the job. AWeber has a slight edge on the free plan because a contractor can publish a limited number of forms and landing pages without paying. Moosend requires a Pro subscription to publish forms and landing pages.

## Setup effort and ownership

Both tools are designed for self-service signup. Neither requires implementation services, onboarding fees, or a sales call. That makes both lighter to start than a full CRM like [Keap](/keap-vs-moosend/) or a field service platform like ServiceTitan.

AWeber's free plan lets a contractor test the platform with a real list before committing to a paid tier. The setup questions are list hygiene, consent, template selection, automation triggers, and campaign timing. One person can usually own AWeber as a marketing layer.

Moosend's 30-day trial gives a contractor full Pro access to test every feature. The setup questions are the same: list import, consent, segments, forms, landing pages, automation paths, and campaign cadence. The longer trial period gives more time to build and test before paying.

The failure mode is identical for both: the tool sits unused because nobody writes campaigns, cleans the list, reviews reports, or builds the next follow-up sequence. Buying the cheaper tool does not solve the ownership problem.

## Contractor operations fit

Neither AWeber nor Moosend should be sold internally as job management software. Both help contractors communicate with leads and customers through email campaigns, forms, landing pages, automations, and reporting. Neither runs estimates, schedules, jobs, invoices, routes, crews, or field production.

A common stack could be: field service software for jobs and scheduling, plus AWeber or Moosend for email follow-up. The right email tool depends on budget, list size, and whether the contractor values a free plan or a lower monthly price. ## Where AWeber falls short

AWeber's biggest drawback is the Lite tier's email cap. At $12.50 per month billed yearly, the Lite plan limits emails to 10 times the subscriber count. For a contractor at 500 subscribers, that means 5,000 emails per month. If the contractor sends frequently or runs multiple campaigns, they need to upgrade to Plus at $20 per month for 12x subscriber sends and unlimited automations. That is still affordable, but it is more than Moosend Pro at $7 per month, which includes unlimited sends from the start.

The second drawback is no pay-as-you-go option. Contractors who send emails only a few times per year cannot avoid a monthly or annual subscription. Moosend's credit system is better for occasional senders.

The third drawback is automation limits on lower tiers. The free plan allows a limited number of active automations. Lite allows 3. Plus is unlimited. A contractor who needs multiple parallel workflows from day one either pays for Plus or works within the cap. Moosend Pro offers unlimited published automations from the start.

## Where Moosend falls short

Moosend's biggest limitation is the lack of a free plan. A contractor with 150 past customers and a small newsletter list has to pay $7 per month on Moosend or $0 on AWeber. For very small lists, AWeber's free plan is a genuine advantage that Moosend cannot match.

The second limitation is integration depth. Moosend lists 80+ integrations compared to AWeber's larger library. The gap matters when the contractor relies on a specific tool or platform. Moosend's smaller user community also means fewer third-party tutorials, templates, and resources.

The third limitation is support access. Moosend offers email and live chat support but no phone support. AWeber provides 24/7 phone, chat, and email support on every plan, including free. For a contractor who wants to pick up the phone when something breaks, AWeber has the edge.

The fourth limitation is template library size. AWeber offers hundreds of pre-built mobile-responsive templates plus a smart designer that auto-generates branded designs. Moosend's template selection is smaller and more utilitarian.

## How to choose

Start with the list size and sending frequency.

If the list is under 500 subscribers and 3,000 monthly emails, AWeber is free. Start there. The free plan is not a trial. It is a permanent tool for a small list.

If the list is over 500 subscribers, compare the monthly prices. Moosend Pro at $7 per month annual is cheaper than AWeber Lite at $12.50 per month annual. But check what each plan includes. AWeber Lite has web push notifications, AI subject line assistant, and advanced analytics. Moosend Pro has unlimited automations, SMTP, and transactional email. Match the features to the actual need.

If the contractor sends emails only a few times per year, Moosend's pay-as-you-go credits may be cheaper than any monthly subscription. Calculate the total annual cost: 4 campaigns at 50,000 emails each would cost $350 in credits versus $84 per year on Moosend Pro or $150 per year on AWeber Lite.

If the contractor values phone support and a deep template library, AWeber has the edge regardless of price. If the contractor wants the simplest possible setup with all features available from day one, Moosend is the cleaner choice.

If both the free plan and the lowest price matter, the decision comes down to how fast the list is growing. A contractor who expects to stay under 500 subscribers for the foreseeable future should pick AWeber. A contractor who expects to pass 500 quickly should pick Moosend for the lower per-month price at scale.

## Final verdict

AWeber is the stronger choice for contractors who want a free plan they can use indefinitely, a large template library, and reliable deliverability backed by decades of email sending infrastructure. It makes the most sense when the list is small, the budget is tight, and the contractor values permanent free access plus phone support over the lowest paid price. The web push notifications and smart designer tools add value for contractors who want more than basic newsletters.

Moosend is the stronger choice for contractors who want the lowest monthly Pro price, unlimited automations from day one, and pay-as-you-go flexibility. It makes the most sense when the list has outgrown free-plan limits, the contractor sends emails regularly but wants unlimited sends from the start, or the business sends seasonally and prefers credits over subscriptions. The 30-day trial with no credit card gives enough time to test before committing.

My practical call: choose AWeber if your list is small enough for the free plan or if phone support and templates matter most. Choose Moosend if you want the lowest monthly Pro price, a longer trial, unlimited automations from Pro, or pay-as-you-go credits for occasional sending.
