# ArboStar vs ClockShark: Arborist Platform or Time Tracker?

ArboStar vs ClockShark looks like a field service comparison, but these two tools sit at different levels of the software stack and solve fundamentally different problems. The decision is not about which feature list is longer. It is about what operational bottleneck is costing the business more money and what kind of platform is needed.

ArboStar is built for arborist and tree care businesses. It answers the question that established tree care companies face: how do I run CRM, scheduling, equipment tracking, field documentation, and job mapping in one system built specifically for tree work? ClockShark is built for GPS-verified time tracking. It answers a different question: did the crew actually work the hours they claimed, and were they at the right job site when they clocked in?

Those are different problems, and buying the wrong tool for the bottleneck will leave a contractor frustrated. A tree care company that needs arborist CRM, live job maps, and safety documentation will not find those in a time tracker. A construction crew that needs to verify labor hours and sync payroll will not get that from a full arborist platform they do not need. See our <a href="/arbostar-review/">ArboStar review</a> and <a href="/clockshark-review/">ClockShark review</a> for the full product breakdowns.

## The buying split

### ArboStar is an arborist business management platform first

ArboStar's main advantage is specialization. The platform is built specifically for arborist and tree care businesses, not adapted from a general field service tool. That means every module - CRM, scheduling, estimating, invoicing, equipment tracking, field documentation, job mapping - is designed around how tree care companies actually operate.

The arborist CRM records client tree history, service records, and communication history specific to tree work. For a company that returns to the same properties for pruning, removals, plant health, and storm cleanup, that depth matters. A salesperson can pull up prior work, and a crew can see photos and notes from the last visit without digging through files.

Live job maps and GPS positioning give managers a real-time view of where crews are and which equipment is tied up. Tree jobs are not interchangeable service calls. The equipment needs, crew composition, and site conditions change per job. A chipper truck, a bucket truck, and specific rigging gear may all need to arrive together at the right site. ArboStar's map and dispatch features reflect that operational reality.

Equipment tracking covers vehicles, tools, chainsaws, chippers, rigging gear, maintenance schedules, and fuel costs. For a multi-crew tree care company managing hundreds of thousands of dollars in equipment across multiple sites, that is not a nice-to-have. It is operational infrastructure.

The official pricing page lists a starting point of $250 per month, but the final quote depends on company size, license count, required features, integrations, and platform usage. Annual billing includes two months free. Because ArboStar requires a sales conversation to get a real price, it is harder to budget for before the demo. That is the trade-off for getting a tree-specific platform instead of a generalist tool.

Where ArboStar falls short: it is not a general field service tool. A non-tree-care company - HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing - would be paying for arborist-specific features they will not use. The mobile app covers job details, notes, photos, and schedule updates, but some users report it lacks functions available on desktop. Setup requires real configuration around crews, equipment, pricing, and accounting processes.

### ClockShark is a GPS time tracking tool first

ClockShark's main advantage is focus. The platform is built for one core purpose: GPS-verified time tracking for field crews. The geofencing that competitors like <a href="/quickbooks-time-vs-clockshark/">QuickBooks Time</a> reserve for premium tiers is included in ClockShark's Standard plan at $40 per month plus $9 per user. No accounting software subscription is required to use it.

For a five-person crew, ClockShark Standard costs $76 per month total: $40 base plus $36 for four additional users at $9 each. The Pro plan adds job costing, PTO management, and shift wrap-up forms at $60 per month plus $11 per user, which comes to $104 per month for five users. The pricing is fully published on the official pricing page with no quote required.

Active-user billing is a practical advantage for seasonal contractors. ClockShark bills only for users who actually clock in during a billing period and pro-rates charges for partial days. A roofing company that brings on extra crews for summer reroofing does not pay for them in the winter months. Users can be activated and deactivated as needed.

Mock location detection catches GPS spoofing apps. That is a real problem for contractors who have had crew members clock in from home, and it is not something most time tracking tools address explicitly. ClockShark also advertises 5-star rated customer support with live help from real humans, and Pro plan users get personal onboarding with a customer success representative.

Where ClockShark falls short: it is not a dispatch platform, not a CRM, not an invoicing tool, and not a phone system. Scheduling is functional but lacks shift templates and flexible recurrence options. There is no offline mode - crews in low-connectivity areas cannot clock in without internet access. The $40 per month base fee is steep for 1-2 person operations. If the business needs dispatch, estimates, invoicing, payments, or equipment tracking, ClockShark alone will not cover it. ## Pricing reality

### ArboStar: custom quote with a starting point

ArboStar uses customized pricing based on specific business needs, tools used, and platform usage. The official pricing page lists a starting price of $250 per month, but the final quote depends on company size, license count, required features, integrations, and platform usage. Multiple-license discounts are available, and annual subscriptions include two months free.

Pricing requires a sales conversation and plan selection for the final quote. That is not a reason to reject ArboStar. It is a reason to require a written quote before comparing it against ClockShark. For ArboStar, the quote should separate:

- Base subscription and license count
- Required modules (CRM, scheduling, equipment tracking, field documentation, job mapping)
- QuickBooks or accounting integration setup
- Mobile app access and user roles
- Implementation, onboarding, and training costs
- Support level and 24/7 availability
- Renewal term, cancellation, and price-change language

If the sales quote cannot answer those items in writing, the comparison is not ready. ArboStar may still be the right platform for a tree care company, but the buyer cannot know the real cost without it.

### ClockShark: published and predictable

ClockShark Standard costs $40 per month base plus $9 per active user. Pro costs $60 per month base plus $11 per active user. The pricing is fully published on the official pricing page, and a 14-day free trial requires no credit card and includes all Pro plan features.

Active-user billing means a contractor only pays for users who actually clock in. Seasonal contractors who scale crews up and down throughout the year are not paying for idle accounts during the off-season. Invoices are pro-rated based on the days users are active. A three-year contract term applies to all ClockShark pricing plans. That is worth knowing before signing - it is a longer commitment than most SaaS tools require.

### Which is cheaper?

ClockShark is cheaper on paper and easier to budget. A five-person crew on ClockShark Standard pays $76 per month with geofencing, GPS tracking, scheduling, and payroll integrations included. ArboStar's starting point of $250 per month is a floor, not a ceiling, and the final cost depends on configuration.

The real cost question is not per-user math. It is whether the business needs a full arborist platform or just labor verification. A tree care company that needs arborist CRM, equipment tracking, live job maps, and safety documentation will need ArboStar (or a similar specialist platform) regardless of what they pay for time tracking. A construction or trade crew that only needs GPS-verified time tracking and payroll sync will find ClockShark more cost-efficient and transparent.

ArboStar's map and GPS positioning give managers a clearer view of where work is happening and which resources are tied up. Equipment tracking covers maintenance schedules, fuel costs, and GPS-linked assets across vehicles, tools, and rigging gear. ### Field documentation for arborist work

Safety forms, hazard notes, job photos, and crew instructions are central to tree work in a way they are not for general service calls. ArboStar's mobile workflow supports field documentation attached to specific tree jobs, not just generic job notes. For a company that needs OSHA-compliant safety records and site-specific hazard documentation, that is a meaningful differentiator.

### Estimating with field context

ArboStar's estimating workflow includes pre-entered rates, work types, job size, equipment needs, crew composition, photos, notes, and digital signatures. For tree proposals that depend on site conditions, tree species, and access constraints, that detail can reduce the gap between what sales promised and what the crew finds on site.

## FAQ

### Is ArboStar or ClockShark better for a tree care company?

It depends on what the tree care company needs. If the company runs multiple crews and needs arborist CRM, equipment tracking, live job maps, and safety documentation, ArboStar is the better fit. If the company primarily needs GPS time tracking and payroll sync and can handle scheduling and client management through other tools, ClockShark is more cost-effective at $40 per month plus $9 per user.

### Which is cheaper, ArboStar or ClockShark?

ClockShark is cheaper at the entry level. ClockShark Standard starts at $40 per month plus $9 per user with fully published pricing. ArboStar starts from $250 per month with a custom quote that depends on company size, licenses, and features. For any crew size, ClockShark is less expensive at entry level.

### Does ArboStar offer a free trial?

ArboStar offers a free demo, not a self-service trial. The demo is a video call with the Director of Sales that showcases platform features including CRM, scheduling, invoicing, GPS-tracking, and equipment management. ClockShark offers a 14-day free trial with no credit card required and includes all Pro plan features.

### Can ClockShark handle tree care scheduling?

ClockShark can schedule tree service jobs with drag-and-drop scheduling, job and task tracking, and geofencing. It does not offer tree-specific features like arborist CRM, equipment tracking, live job maps with crew and equipment assignment, or field documentation for safety forms and hazard notes. For basic tree company scheduling and time tracking, ClockShark works. For multi-crew tree operations with complex equipment needs, ArboStar is the better fit. ### Does ArboStar have GPS time tracking?

Yes, ArboStar includes GPS-tracking and a live job map. The mobile app provides real-time monitoring of crews and equipment locations. However, ArboStar's GPS is built for operational visibility (where are my crews and equipment), not specifically for payroll verification with features like mock location detection and active-user billing. If payroll verification is the primary need, ClockShark is more purpose-built for that workflow. ### Can I use ClockShark alongside ArboStar?

Yes. Some tree care companies run ArboStar as their primary business management platform and add ClockShark for dedicated GPS time tracking and payroll sync. This works when ArboStar's built-in GPS does not meet the company's labor verification needs or when the company needs direct integration with a payroll platform that ArboStar does not support natively. ### Does ClockShark require an accounting software subscription?

No. ClockShark is a standalone platform. No accounting software subscription is required to use it. It syncs with QuickBooks, ADP, Gusto, Xero, Sage, Paychex, and Paylocity, but those integrations are optional, not mandatory. A three-year contract term applies to all ClockShark pricing plans. That is a longer commitment than most SaaS tools require and is worth knowing before signing. ArboStar's contract term is negotiated as part of the custom quote.
