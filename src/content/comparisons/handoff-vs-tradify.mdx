# Handoff vs Tradify: AI Estimating vs Trade Job Management

Handoff and Tradify both serve trade contractors, but they target different parts of the business. Handoff is an AI-native estimating and proposal tool built for residential remodelers, roofers, and trade contractors who need to turn job walks into professional proposals fast. Tradify is a trade job management platform that handles quoting, scheduling, invoicing, payments, and accounting sync for small trade teams of 1-20 users.

Those are different problems, and picking the wrong tool will leave a gap. A remodeler losing bids because estimates take three days does not need invoicing software. A plumbing team drowning in manual invoices and scattered schedules does not need AI proposal visuals. See the <a href="/tradify-review/">Tradify review</a> for a full product breakdown. For a broader look at the category, the <a href="/best-field-service-software/">best field service software roundup</a> covers both tools and their alternatives.

## The buying split

### Handoff is an estimating and proposal tool first

Handoff's core value is what happens before the job is won. The platform turns job walks, voice notes, blueprints, and photos into AI-generated estimates, scopes of work, and professional proposals. For a residential contractor who currently uses a spreadsheet and stale templates, that is a direct hit on the slowest part of the sales process. The <a href="/best-estimating-software-for-small-contractors/">small-contractor estimating software guide</a> covers the broader category.

The pricing is published and tiered. Atlas verifies Flex at $119/month billed annually or $149 month to month, with 2 users and 50 AI usage credits. Pro is $239/month annual or $299 month to month, with 5 users, unlimited AI credits, client financing, and the AI project management suite. Scale is $719/month annual or $899 month to month, adding AI takeoffs from residential plans up to 5,000 square feet. Enterprise is custom quote for API access, custom integrations, and advanced security.

Where Handoff falls short: it is not an invoicing platform, not a payment processor, not a dispatch board, and not a full job management system. Scheduling exists within project management but is not a field-service dispatch tool. The starting price of $119/month is higher than most alternatives in this comparison. AI credit limits on Flex mean heavy users may need to upgrade to Pro quickly. And the product is focused on residential trades and remodelers, so commercial or route-based service work is not its strength. For a comparison that shows Handoff against a broader field-service platform, see <a href="/handoff-vs-zuper/">Handoff vs Zuper</a>.

### Tradify is a trade job management tool first

Tradify should be judged on whether its published pricing and straightforward job workflow fit your trade business. The official pricing page lists Lite at $47 per user per month, Pro at $51, and Plus at $61. A 14-day free trial includes all features with no credit card. That kind of pricing clarity is rare in a category where most competitors hide behind a request-a-demo wall. For another comparison that shows Tradify against a specialist tool, see <a href="/clockshark-vs-tradify/">ClockShark vs Tradify</a>.

Tradify's platform covers the trade job lifecycle: inquiries, quoting, scheduling, job tracking, invoicing, and payments. Xero and QuickBooks Online sync are available on every plan, including the entry-level Lite tier at $47/user/month. Free one-on-one training and phone support are included, not sold as add-ons. The site lists a 4.8 out of 5 rating on Trustpilot from 548 reviews.

Where Tradify falls short is estimating depth and AI. There is no AI-generated proposal tool, no voice-to-estimate, no AI takeoffs. The AI tools Tradify does have -- SmartRead and SmartWrite -- are locked to the Plus tier at $61/user/month. Reporting, purchase orders, bulk invoicing, and inventory management are also Plus-only. The integration list is shorter than Jobber, Housecall Pro, or Service Fusion. And for teams above 20 users, the per-user model can become more expensive than a platform with broad-access pricing.

## Pricing reality

### Solo operator or two-person team

For a solo operator, Tradify is the cheaper entry point. Tradify Lite costs $47/month for one user and includes quoting, invoicing, payments, scheduling, and accounting sync. Handoff Flex costs $119/month annual or $149 month to month for 2 users with 50 AI credits, a 7-day free trial, and AI estimating features.

The products are not doing the same job. If the business needs to turn job walks into professional proposals, Handoff at $119/month solves that directly. If the business needs to quote, invoice, collect payment, and sync accounting, Tradify at $47/month covers it. Some solo contractors may need both, but that means two subscriptions. For alternatives that combine estimating with job management, compare <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber</a> and <a href="/housecall-pro-review/">Housecall Pro</a>.

### Five-person team

This is where the pricing comparison gets interesting. Handoff Flex only includes 2 users, so a 5-person team needs Handoff Pro at $239/month annual or $299 month to month. That includes 5 users, unlimited AI credits, client financing, and the AI project management suite.

Tradify Lite for 5 users costs $235/month ($47 x 5). Tradify Pro for 5 users costs $255/month ($51 x 5). Tradify Plus for 5 users costs $305/month ($61 x 5). All Tradify plans include quoting, invoicing, payments, scheduling, and accounting sync.

At five users, Handoff Pro at $239/month and Tradify Pro at $255/month are remarkably close in price. But the scope is different. Handoff Pro delivers AI estimating, proposals, and project admin. Tradify Pro delivers quoting, scheduling, invoicing, payments, accounting sync, custom branding, inquiry forms, appointment reminders, quote options with online acceptance, subcontractor scheduling, and timesheets. Neither replaces the other without gaps.

### Ten-person team

At 10 users, Handoff's published plans cap out. Flex and Pro both include 5 users, and Scale also lists 5 users. For a 10-person team, Handoff would require an Enterprise custom quote. That makes pricing less predictable at this scale.

Tradify scales linearly. Lite for 10 users costs $470/month ($47 x 10). Pro costs $510/month ($51 x 10). Plus costs $610/month ($61 x 10), plus the $12/month Instant Website add-on if used. Those are known numbers, and for some teams that predictability is the deciding factor. But at 10+ users, the per-user model becomes expensive enough to justify looking at platforms with broad-access pricing or custom quotes. For teams evaluating larger platforms, the <a href="/field-service-software-alternatives/">field service software alternatives roundup</a> covers the wider category.

### Contract terms compared

Handoff Flex is available month to month at $149/month. Handoff Pro requires a 12-month commitment at $239/month annual. Scale also requires annual billing. The 7-day free trial on Flex lets you test the workflow before committing.

Tradify does not advertise a contract term on its pricing page. Monthly billing with no long-term contract is typical for per-user SaaS platforms in this category. The 14-day trial includes all features with no credit card. If contract flexibility matters, Tradify has the edge.

## Where Handoff wins

### AI estimating and proposal speed

Handoff's core differentiator is turning job walks into professional proposals. Voice-to-estimate lets a contractor describe the work out loud and Handoff builds the quote. AI-generated proposal visuals look professional enough to send directly to homeowners. Custom pricing catalogs mean the estimates use your actual labor rates, materials, and markup.

That is a practical sales workflow for residential contractors. If the current process is a spreadsheet, a stale template, and three days of follow-up, Handoff is solving the right problem. Tradify handles quoting, but its quoting is not AI-driven and does not produce the same proposal-quality output.

### Published tiered pricing

Handoff publishes Flex, Pro, Scale, and Enterprise pricing. You know what the product costs before scheduling a call. The 7-day free trial on Flex lets you test the AI estimating workflow with a real job before committing.

Tradify also publishes pricing, but the products are different in scope. Handoff's pricing reflects AI estimating and proposal capabilities. A contractor who needs AI-generated proposals will find Handoff's pricing easier to evaluate because the entry tier includes those features.

### Residential contractor focus

Handoff is built for remodelers, roofers, home builders, handymen, and trade contractors who sell project-based work to homeowners. The AI features -- voice transcription to estimate, AI proposals, AI site walkthroughs, AI takeoffs on Scale -- point directly at the residential sales workflow.

Tradify is built for small trade businesses across electrical, HVAC, plumbing, and field service. It is less specialized for residential project work and more focused on repeat job management. If the business sells residential remodels and needs professional proposals, Handoff is the closer fit. For a comparison against another residential-focused tool, see <a href="/clockshark-vs-handoff/">ClockShark vs Handoff</a>.

### Project management on higher plans

Handoff Pro adds the AI project management suite, which includes AI budget tracking, AI scheduling, AI daily logs, and AI meeting recording. Client financing is also available on Pro and above. For a residential contractor who wins the job and needs lightweight project admin, Handoff Pro covers some of that workflow without requiring a separate platform.

Tradify does not offer project management in the same way. It handles job tracking and scheduling, but its AI and reporting tools are operational, not project-management oriented.

## Where Tradify wins

### Complete trade job workflow

Tradify covers the full trade job lifecycle on every plan: inquiry capture, quoting, scheduling, job tracking, invoicing, progress invoicing, online and credit card payments, and accounting sync. On Pro, it adds custom branding, inquiry forms, appointment reminders, quote options with online acceptance, subcontractor scheduling, and timesheets.

Handoff does not do invoicing, payment collection, or online payments. If the business needs to turn inquiries into quotes, quotes into jobs, and jobs into paid invoices, Handoff alone will not solve that problem. For another angle on trade job management alternatives, see <a href="/service-fusion-vs-tradify/">Service Fusion vs Tradify</a>.

### Published per-user pricing and trial access

Tradify publishes $47, $51, and $61 per user per month. A 14-day free trial includes all features with no credit card. That matters because trade software is hard to judge from a sales call. A trade business needs to test quoting, scheduling, invoicing, payments, and accounting sync with real jobs before committing.

Handoff also offers a trial, but it is 7 days on Flex only, with 50 AI credits. Tradify's 14-day trial with all features gives more room to evaluate the full platform. And Tradify's straight per-user rate makes the total cost more predictable at scale than Handoff's tiered model with included user counts.

### Accounting sync from day one

Xero and QuickBooks Online sync are available on every Tradify plan, including Lite at $47/user/month. For electricians, plumbers, and HVAC techs who already run on Xero or QBO, that means the accounting connection works from the entry tier without upgrading.

Handoff lists QuickBooks integration in its pricing table, but does not advertise Xero sync. If your business runs on Xero, Tradify covers it at the entry tier. Handoff does not. For contractors evaluating accounting-integrated platforms, the broader <a href="/field-service-software-alternatives/">field service alternatives roundup</a> is useful context.

### Included training and support

Free one-on-one training and phone support are included with every Tradify plan. That is not a paid add-on or a higher-tier feature. For a small trade business owner who does not want to figure out a new platform alone, included training can be the difference between adoption and another unused subscription.

Handoff Pro includes a Success Coach for onboarding, which is valuable. But Tradify includes one-on-one training on every tier, including Lite at $47/user/month. For first-time software buyers, that is a practical advantage.

### Strong customer rating

Tradify lists a 4.8 out of 5 rating on Trustpilot from 548 reviews. Handoff is listed on GetApp with 42 verified reviews and positive ease-of-use sentiment. Both are positive signals, but Tradify's larger review base suggests broader customer validation across trade businesses.

## Evaluation plan

### Test Handoff for one real estimate

During the 7-day Flex trial, take one real job walk and build the estimate and proposal inside Handoff. Use your actual labor rates, materials, markup, photos, and scope notes. Test voice-to-estimate by describing the job out loud. Check whether the AI-generated proposal visuals are good enough to send without manual cleanup. Count how many AI credits the estimate consumes and whether 50 credits per month is enough for your volume.

Then compare plans: Flex at $119/month versus Pro at $239/month. Confirm whether you need unlimited AI credits, client financing, the AI project management suite, or more than 2 user seats. Test the QuickBooks integration if accounting sync matters to your workflow.

### Test Tradify on a real job

For Tradify, the 14-day trial gives full access to all features with no credit card. Use that time to run a real job end to end: capture an inquiry, create a quote, convert it to a job, schedule the work, assign a technician, send a customer update, collect payment, and sync the result to Xero or QuickBooks Online.

Then compare the tier you actually need. If reporting and purchase orders are important, budget for Plus at $61/user/month. If the core workflow of quoting, scheduling, invoicing, and payments is enough, Lite at $47 or Pro at $51 may be the better value. Test the one-on-one training session -- it is included, and it can shorten the learning curve significantly. ## Final verdict

<p><strong>My call:</strong> Handoff is the better pick when AI estimating, proposal speed, and residential contractor focus are the priority. Tradify is the better pick when published per-user pricing, a 14-day trial with all features, and a complete trade job workflow matter more than AI-generated proposals.</p>

The 14-day trial with no credit card makes that evaluation low-risk.

If you are unsure, pick the pain first. Slow estimates and proposal quality point toward Handoff. Quoting, invoicing, and job management point toward Tradify. Some trade businesses need both, and that is a valid stack -- but start with the tool that solves the bigger daily problem.

## FAQ

### Is Handoff or Tradify better for contractors?

Neither is universally better. Handoff is better for residential remodelers, roofers, and trade contractors that need AI-generated estimates, proposals, and project admin with published tiered pricing. Tradify is better for small trade teams of 1-20 users that want quoting, invoicing, scheduling, payments, and accounting sync with published per-user pricing. The right choice depends on whether estimating speed or job management is the bigger daily problem.

### Which is cheaper, Handoff or Tradify?

Tradify is cheaper at the entry level. Tradify Lite costs $47/user/month for one user. Handoff Flex costs $119/month annual or $149/month monthly for 2 users. But the products are not doing the same job -- Handoff provides AI estimating and proposals, Tradify manages quoting, invoicing, payments, and scheduling. Comparing them on price alone misses that they solve different problems. At five users, Handoff Pro at $239/month and Tradify Pro at $255/month ($51 x 5) are close in price but have very different feature sets.

### Can Handoff replace Tradify?

No. Handoff does not do quoting in the trade job management sense, invoicing, payment collection, scheduling, or accounting sync. It is an estimating and proposal tool. If your business needs to turn inquiries into quotes and quotes into paid invoices, Handoff alone will not replace Tradify. Some contractors use both: Handoff for estimating and proposals, Tradify for job management and invoicing.

### Can Tradify replace Handoff?

No. Tradify does not have AI-generated proposals, voice-to-estimate, AI takeoffs from residential plans, or residential contractor-specific estimating tools. Its AI tools (SmartRead and SmartWrite) are operational, not estimating-focused, and are locked to the Plus tier. If the business needs professional homeowner-facing proposals generated from job walks, Tradify alone will not replace Handoff.

### Do both products offer a free trial?

Yes. Handoff offers a 7-day free trial on Flex with no credit card required, including 50 AI credits. Tradify offers a 14-day free trial with all features and no credit card required. Tradify's trial is longer and includes Plus-tier features like reporting and purchase orders, giving you more room to evaluate the full platform. Handoff's trial is shorter but lets you test the AI estimating workflow with a real job.

### Does Tradify have AI estimating?

No. Tradify does not have AI-generated estimates, voice-to-estimate, or AI proposals. Its AI tools are SmartRead (which helps process documents and data) and SmartWrite, both locked to the Plus tier at $61/user/month. These are operational AI tools, not estimating or proposal tools. If AI estimating is a core requirement, Handoff is the right tool between these two.

### What should I test during the free trial?

In Handoff, test voice-to-estimate with a real job description, build a proposal from actual labor rates and materials, check whether the AI-generated visuals are good enough to send, count AI credit usage, and test the QuickBooks integration. In Tradify, run a real job end to end: capture an inquiry, create a quote, schedule the work, collect payment, and sync to Xero or QuickBooks. Then compare which tier you actually need and whether the included training session shortens the learning curve.
