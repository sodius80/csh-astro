# Clear Estimates vs Jobber: Estimating vs Field Service

Clear Estimates and Jobber look like they compete for the same contractor. Both manage customer records, handle estimates and invoices, and appear in the same comparison searches. But they are built around fundamentally different kinds of contractor work - and choosing the wrong one does not mean you picked a bad product. It means the software will fight your workflow every day instead of supporting it. For more detail, see our <a href="/clear-estimates-review/">Clear Estimates review</a> and <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber review</a>.

Clear Estimates is an estimating-first product built around a maintained line-item cost database with 13,000+ line items, 200+ preloaded project templates, and quarterly labor and material rate updates. It is designed for residential remodelers and small contractors who need the estimate to be the best part of their process - built from accurate, current line-item data rather than memory or a spreadsheet.

Jobber is a field service management platform built for businesses that run multiple same-day service calls. HVAC tune-ups, plumbing repairs, landscaping routes, cleaning appointments - work that books, completes, and closes in a single day. The features that make Jobber valuable - drag-and-drop scheduling, automated quote follow-ups, mobile payment collection, client hub - matter most when each day is a new set of stops. HVAC teams comparing this category should also read our <a href="/best-hvac-software-small-business/">best HVAC software guide</a>.

If you are a remodeler evaluating Jobber, or a service contractor evaluating Clear Estimates, this comparison will save you time. These are tools for different jobs.

## Clear Estimates vs Jobber: Quick Verdict by Contractor Type

**Residential remodelers and small contractors:** Start with Clear Estimates. Jobber does not have a line-item cost database, preloaded construction templates, quarterly rate updates, or estimating-specific reports. Jobber's quote builder handles line items, but it is not designed for the estimating depth that serious remodelers need. If your business lives and dies on detailed estimates with accurate cost data, Clear Estimates is the right architecture.

**Field service contractors - HVAC, plumbing, electrical, landscaping, cleaning:** Start with Jobber. Clear Estimates has no scheduling, no dispatch, no CRM, no mobile app for techs, and no payment collection. You would be buying an estimating-only tool when what you actually need is an operations platform that connects quotes to scheduling to invoicing to payment. Jobber's transparent pricing and 14-day trial mean you can test it this afternoon.

**The edge case:** A remodeling company that also does service work faces the hardest choice. You can pick one platform and accept that part of the business works around the software, or you can run both systems. Clear Estimates for the remodel estimates, Jobber for the service dispatch. That adds cost and data sync work, but it is sometimes the honest answer - no single tool covers both well at this price range.

## When This Software Makes Sense

Both tools earn their cost when the manual process is breaking down - but the breakdown looks different for each buyer.

Clear Estimates makes sense when the problem is estimating inconsistency. Estimates take too long because every one starts from a blank page. Pricing varies between estimators because there is no shared line-item database. Material and labor assumptions drift because nobody has time to update a spreadsheet every quarter. A remodeler handling 10-20 detailed estimates a month typically hits this wall - and that is when Clear Estimates's database and templates start paying for themselves.

Jobber makes sense when the problem is daily dispatch chaos. Jobs are falling through the cracks between booking and invoicing. Someone forgets to follow up on an unsigned estimate. A tech shows up without knowing the job scope because the notes are in a group chat. An invoice sits unpaid for two weeks because nobody remembered to send a reminder. Jobber's scheduling, quote follow-ups, automated reminders, and payment collection close those gaps without a long implementation.

## When It Does Not Make Sense Yet

Skip both if you are running fewer than 10 jobs a month and the admin load has not become genuinely painful. Software adds overhead before it adds value - you need to configure line items, customer records, payment settings, and accounting integrations before you see a return. If a spreadsheet and QuickBooks are not breaking down yet, keep them.

Clear Estimates specifically: if your work is same-day service calls with no need for line-item estimating depth, Clear Estimates is expensive overkill. The $79/mo starting price for an estimating-only tool makes no sense when all you need is a quote to send and an invoice to collect. Jobber or Joist would serve you better.

Jobber specifically: if your work is multi-week remodeling projects where the estimate is the most important document you produce all week, Jobber's quote builder will feel too light. You can force it to work with line items, but you will lack the cost database, templates, rate updates, and estimating-specific controls that make Clear Estimates valuable for that kind of work.

## Key Features Compared

### Estimating and Cost Database

This is the core differentiator. Clear Estimates maintains a 13,000+ line-item cost database covering labor and material rates across 400+ geographic areas, updated quarterly. A contractor who signs up has immediate access to priced line items for framing, drywall, painting, flooring, siding, roofing, trim, cabinets, countertops, plumbing fixtures, and dozens of other categories. Alongside the database, 200+ preloaded project templates let a remodeler select a kitchen template, adjust dimensions and materials, and have a detailed estimate in minutes.

Jobber's quote builder handles line items, optional add-ons, and digital approval with e-signature. But there is no cost database, no preloaded templates for construction scopes, no quarterly rate updates. Every line item must be entered manually or reused from a saved item list.

**Winner:** Clear Estimates. Jobber does not compete in estimating database depth. For more on estimating-specific tools, see our <a href="/best-estimating-software-for-small-contractors/">best estimating software roundup</a>.

### Scheduling and Dispatch

Jobber has the clear advantage here. The drag-and-drop calendar shows every tech's day in one view, lets you move jobs between time slots, and keeps customers updated with automated notifications. For a plumbing company booking 8-12 calls a day, the calendar view keeps the dispatch board manageable. The mobile app lets techs see their schedule, job details, and route without calling the office.

Clear Estimates has no scheduling or dispatch. It is an estimating-only product. There is no calendar, no tech assignments, no route optimization, no customer notifications about arrival times.

**Winner:** Jobber. Clear Estimates does not compete here.

### Mobile App

Jobber draws consistent praise for mobile usability. The iOS and Android apps let techs view job details, clock in and out, upload photos, add notes, fill out forms, and collect payment. Offline support is limited - timers, forms, notes, and attachments can be saved locally and synced later. For a broader mobile-first shortlist, see our <a href="/field-service-management-mobile-app/">field service management mobile app guide</a>.

Clear Estimates is primarily a web-based desktop product. There is no dedicated mobile app for field use. Contractors who need to create estimates on site from a phone or tablet will find Clear Estimates limiting.

**Winner:** Jobber. This gap is significant for contractors with field teams.

### CRM and Client Communication

Jobber includes a full client hub where customers can request work, approve quotes, review past appointments, see assigned team details, and pay invoices. Quote follow-ups can be automated, appointment reminders go out by text or email, and two-way SMS is available on Grow and higher plans.

Clear Estimates has no CRM. There is no lead tracking, no customer portal, no automated follow-ups, no appointment reminders. It is an estimating tool. Customer relationship management is handled outside the software.

**Winner:** Jobber. Clear Estimates is not designed to compete here.

### Reporting and Job Costing

Clear Estimates offers estimating-specific reports but no job costing, profitability tracking, or operational reporting. The Pro tier adds custom filtering and reports, but the scope is estimating-centric.

Jobber's reporting covers the basics well: job revenue, outstanding invoices, and tech performance on Core and Connect. Grow adds job costing with profit margin, labor, line items, and expenses. Even on Grow, the reporting is functional rather than finance-grade, but it covers a broader operational scope than Clear Estimates.

**Winner:** Jobber for operational reporting. Clear Estimates for estimating-specific data.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

**Clear Estimates** integrates with Buildertrend (via Pro tier) and CRM tools through Zapier. The Pro tier also adds property data from ATTOM. That is a narrow integration set - it covers the estimating-to-construction-management handoff but does not extend into payments, marketing, or field operations.

**Jobber** has a 70+ partner App Marketplace. Key connections: QuickBooks Online, Xero, Gusto, Mailchimp, Zapier, Square, Authorize.net, Stripe, PayPal Express. The Zapier connection handles long-tail integrations that Jobber does not connect to natively. The catch: only one payment integration can be active at a time, and there is no QuickBooks Desktop support. ## Limitations and Red Flags

**Clear Estimates:**
- Estimating only - no scheduling, dispatch, CRM, project management, or mobile app for field techs
- Cost database still needs local adjustment for actual crew rates, supplier pricing, overhead, and waste factors - it is a starting point, not a finished bid
- No digital takeoff tools for plan-based estimating
- Residential-only - commercial contractors will find the database inadequate
- Template count varies across the site (500+ on homepage, 200+ on pricing page, 160+ in comparison table)

**Jobber:**
- No built-in cost database or estimating templates - every line item is manual
- No offline mobile mode except for limited notes, forms, and attachments
- No inventory management module
- QuickBooks sync is one-way (Jobber to QuickBooks, not back)
- Tier jumps from Core to Connect to Grow are steep
- No supplier integrations for material ordering
