# Arborgold vs Jobber: Green-Industry Software Compared

<p>Jobber and Arborgold both help service businesses run their daily operations, but they serve different segments of the market. Jobber is the generalist - it works for any service trade, costs less to start, and has transparent pricing with a free trial. Arborgold is the specialist - it is built specifically for tree, lawn, and landscape companies and includes workflows that generic FSM tools do not have, but it costs more and requires an annual contract. See our <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber review</a> and <a href="/arborgold-review/">Arborgold review</a>.</p>

Put Arborgold first if you run an established tree, lawn, or landscape company and need green-industry-specific workflows - land measurements, plant health care tracking, chemical logs, and renewals - that justify a higher price and an annual contract.</p>

## Buying split: trade specificity vs generalist flexibility

The decision between Jobber and Arborgold comes down to one question: how green-industry-specific are your daily operations?

If you run general service work - HVAC, plumbing, electrical, cleaning, pressure washing - Jobber is the clear choice. It costs less, has transparent pricing, works across trades, and has a free trial. Arborgold's green-industry features would be wasted on a plumbing or electrical contractor.

If you run a tree care, lawn care, or landscaping company, the answer depends on how much of your work requires green-industry-specific tools. A landscaping company that mainly does mowing and basic maintenance may be perfectly served by Jobber. A tree care company that needs land measurements, plant health care tracking, chemical logs, and recurring renewals will hit Jobber's limits and find Arborgold's feature set more useful. For a wider landscape-specific shortlist, read the [Jobber alternatives for landscaping companies](/jobber-alternatives-landscaping-companies/) guide.

Arborgold exists because tree, lawn, and landscape operations look different from standard service calls. A tree removal involves site measurements, tree inventory, hazard assessment, crew safety, disposal, and often a renewal cycle. A lawn maintenance company needs route planning, chemical application tracking, and recurring service management. Generic FSM tools cover the basics, but the gaps accumulate.

## Pricing reality and contract questions

Jobber publishes four plan tiers with clear pricing. Core at $29/month annual or $49/month monthly for one user. Connect at $99/month annual for one user or $149/month annual for five users. Grow at $149/month annual for one user or $299/month annual for ten users. Plus at $529/month annual for fifteen users. Additional users are $29/month each. The 14-day free trial gives access to Grow plan features and requires no credit card. If the price ladder is the main concern, the [Jobber pricing guide](/jobber-pricing-is-it-worth-it/) breaks down the plan jumps in more detail.

Arborgold also publishes plan prices, but the full cost is less transparent. Starter is $129/month annual or $149/month monthly. Professional is $299/month annual or $343/month monthly. Enterprise is $499/month annual or $573/month monthly. The catch: Office Users and Mobile Users are priced separately through sales, and an annual contract is required. The monthly payment option does not mean month-to-month flexibility.

A second costing layer for Arborgold is third-party integrations. Twilio for SMS, QuickBooks, SiteOne, LandscapeHub, and SendGrid may each require separate paid subscriptions. On Jobber, QuickBooks Online sync is included on Connect and higher plans, and SMS is in the platform.

Do not compare the headline prices. Compare the fully loaded monthly cost for your actual team size - office users, mobile users, integrations, and any onboarding fees.

## Workflow fit: general scheduling vs green-industry operations

Jobber's scheduling is built for general service work. The drag-and-drop calendar, automated reminders, client communication, and mobile dispatch work well for standard service calls. Route optimization is not a core feature.

Arborgold's scheduling is built around land measurements, property mapping, and route planning that matter for tree, lawn, and landscape operations. The ability to track multiple properties, service histories, and plant or tree inventory per location is specific to green-industry work. If your daily operation involves measuring a property, cataloging trees or plants, and scheduling recurring service cycles, Arborgold offers tools that Jobber does not.

For a landscaping company that mainly does weekly mowing, both tools work. For a tree care company that needs to estimate a job based on tree measurements, track chemical applications, and manage renewal cycles, Arborgold has the edge. If the shortlist is specifically tree-care software, compare this page with the [ArboStar vs Jobber comparison](/arbostar-vs-jobber/).

## Mobile and field adoption

Both products offer iOS and Android mobile apps. The difference is in how users are priced. Jobber includes mobile access in the plan price, with transparent additional-user costs. Arborgold separates Office Users (full access) from Mobile Users (field-only access) and prices them through sales.

For a company with a large field crew and a small office staff, Arborgold's mobile user pricing model may work well - you only pay for the field access your crews need. For a company where most employees need both office and mobile access, that split can create unexpected costs.

## Where Jobber wins

### Transparent pricing and fast setup

Jobber's public pricing and 14-day free trial let any service business test the product before committing. The trial requires no credit card and gives access to Grow plan features. For a small contractor who wants to try before buying, that matters.

### Works across trades

Jobber does not require you to be a specific type of contractor. HVAC, plumbing, electrical, landscaping, cleaning, handyman services - the same platform works for all of them. That is useful for mixed-trade businesses or contractors who are not sure what the business will look like in a year.

### Lower starting cost and no contract

Jobber starts at $29/month annual for a solo operator. Even the Grow plan at $149/month annual is less than Arborgold's Starter. Month-to-month billing means no annual commitment if the tool does not work out.

### Free trial

The 14-day free trial with no credit card lets a team test real workflows before paying. That is the single strongest advantage for any small business evaluating software.

## Where Arborgold wins

### Green-industry-specific workflows

Arborgold is built for tree, lawn, and landscape operations. Land measurements, plant health care tracking, chemical application logs, tree and plant inventory mapping, recurring service renewals - these are features that generic FSM tools do not have. If these workflows are central to your business, Arborgold is the product that has them.

### Published plan structure

Arborgold publishes three plan tiers with specific feature lists. Starter covers CRM, estimating, land measurements, reporting, and crew time tracking. Professional adds job costing, renewals, material inventory, chemical tracking, and an autoprice calculator. Enterprise adds plant and tree inventory mapping, sales automation, custom templates, expense tracking, equipment maintenance, and project scheduling.

### QuickBooks Desktop support

Arborgold lists both QuickBooks Online and QuickBooks Desktop integration. That matters because many landscaping and tree-care companies still run QuickBooks Desktop, and competitors often support QuickBooks Online only.

### Scalability within green industry

A tree care company that grows from 3 crews to 30 crews can stay on Arborgold through the plan tiers. The increase in cost is predictable through the published plan structure, and the workflows are consistent.

## Where each product is the wrong fit

Jobber is the wrong fit for a tree care company that needs land measurements, plant health care tracking, chemical logs, and renewal management. Those features simply do not exist in Jobber, and workarounds would pile up over time.

Arborgold is the wrong fit for a general service contractor - HVAC, plumbing, electrical, cleaning - who does not need green-industry-specific workflows. The higher cost, annual contract, and opaque user pricing make it a poor value for businesses that would use a fraction of the feature set.

Arborgold is also a poor fit for a very small contractor or solo operator. The annual commitment, user pricing model, and third-party integration costs add overhead that a $29/month Jobber Core plan does not have.

## Evaluation plan for a fair side-by-side test

For Jobber, use the 14-day free trial. Build the real schedule, create a sample quote, send it to a test customer, convert it to a job, and have a tech run through a full day on the mobile app. Confirm which plan has the features you need and what additional users will cost.

For Arborgold, request a demo. Ask the sales team to walk through your specific workflows - land measurements, tree inventory, chemical tracking, renewals. Confirm the exact user count needed (Office Users vs Mobile Users), onboarding process, annual contract terms, and third-party integration costs.

The winner is the system your office staff and field crews will actually update without being chased. If the tool requires too much work to match your real workflow, even the best price is not worth it.

## FAQ

### Is Arborgold or Jobber better for a tree care company?

Arborgold is the better fit for tree care companies that need land measurements, tree inventory mapping, chemical application tracking, and renewal management. Jobber can handle basic scheduling and invoicing for a tree care company, but the green-industry-specific workflows that tree care operations need are in Arborgold.

### Which is cheaper, Arborgold or Jobber?

Jobber starts at $29/month annual (Core, 1 user), and the 14-day free trial costs nothing. Arborgold starts at $129/month annual (Starter) with additional user costs quoted through sales. For most small businesses, Jobber is significantly cheaper, especially when you include the free trial and month-to-month billing option.

### Does Arborgold offer a free trial?

Arborgold does not advertise a public self-serve free trial. The evaluation path is a demo with sales. Jobber offers a 14-day free trial with no credit card required.

### Does Jobber work for landscaping companies?

Yes. Jobber works well for landscaping companies that mainly do mowing, basic maintenance, and residential service work. For landscaping companies that need land measurements, plant health care tracking, chemical logs, and advanced route planning, Arborgold is the better fit.

### Does Arborgold require an annual contract?

Yes. Arborgold's pricing page says an annual contract is required, and the monthly payment option does not mean month-to-month flexibility. Confirm cancellation, renewal, and payment terms with sales before signing.

### Can I use Jobber for tree care?

You can use Jobber for tree care, and it will handle scheduling, quoting, invoicing, and customer communication. The limits will show up when you need land measurements, tree inventory, chemical tracking, or renewal workflows. For a small tree care operation that mainly needs basic office tools, Jobber may be enough. For a dedicated tree care company, Arborgold has the specific workflows.

## Final verdict

<p><strong>CSH's call:</strong> Start with Jobber if your business does general service work across any trade, values transparent pricing, and needs scheduling and invoicing that works quickly. Start with Arborgold if you run an established tree, lawn, or landscape company and the green-industry-specific workflows - land measurements, plant health care tracking, chemical logs, renewals - are central to your daily operation.</p>

<p>Use this decision rule: if your work looks like a standard service call (arrive, diagnose, fix, invoice), Jobber is the better choice. If your work starts with measuring a property, cataloging plants or trees, tracking chemical applications, and managing renewal cycles, Arborgold has the specific tools you need, and the higher cost and annual contract are more likely to be worth it.</p>

<p>Make both vendors prove the exact same sample workflows before committing. The right tool is the one your team will actually use.</p>
