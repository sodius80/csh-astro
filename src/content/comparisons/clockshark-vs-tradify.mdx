# ClockShark vs Tradify: Time Tracking vs Trade Job Management

ClockShark and Tradify are both built for trade contractors, but they solve different operational problems. ClockShark is a GPS time tracking platform that answers the question keeping construction owners up at night: are my crews at the right job site and did they actually work the hours they claimed? Tradify is a trade job management platform that answers a different question: how do I quote, schedule, invoice, and get paid for trade jobs in one simple tool?

Those are different problems, and buying the wrong tool for your bottleneck will leave you frustrated. A contractor losing money to unverified labor hours does not need quoting software. A trade business drowning in manual invoices and scattered job schedules does not need GPS geofencing. See the <a href="/clockshark-review/">ClockShark review</a> and <a href="/tradify-review/">Tradify review</a> for full product breakdowns.

## The buying split

### ClockShark is a time tracking and labor verification tool first

ClockShark's main advantage is that it is built for one thing: GPS-verified time tracking for field crews. The geofencing that competitors like QuickBooks Time reserve for premium tiers is included in ClockShark's Standard plan at $40/month plus $9 per user. No accounting software subscription required.

For a five-person crew, ClockShark Standard costs $76/month total: $40 base plus $36 for four additional users at $9 each. The Pro plan adds job costing, PTO management, and shift wrap-up forms at $60/month plus $11 per user, which comes to $104/month for five users.

Active-user billing is a practical advantage for seasonal contractors. ClockShark bills only for users who actually clock in during a billing period and prorates charges for partial days. A roofing company that brings on extra crews for summer reroofing does not pay for them in the winter months.

The mock location detection catches GPS spoofing apps. That is a real problem for contractors who have had crew members clock in from home, and it is not something most time tracking tools address explicitly.

Where ClockShark falls short: it is not a quoting tool, not an invoicing platform, not a payment processor, and not a CRM. Scheduling is functional but lacks shift templates and flexible recurrence. No offline mode means crews in low-connectivity areas cannot clock in. The $40/month base fee is steep for 1-2 person operations. A three-year contract term applies to all plans. For a broader look at time tracking options, see the <a href="/best-time-tracking-software-contractors/">best time tracking software for contractors guide</a>.

### Tradify is a trade job management tool first

Tradify should be judged on whether its published pricing and straightforward job workflow fit your trade business. The official pricing page lists Lite at $47 per user per month, Pro at $51, and Plus at $61. A 14-day free trial includes all features with no credit card. That kind of pricing clarity is rare in a category where most competitors hide behind a request-a-demo wall.

Tradify's platform covers the trade job lifecycle: inquiries, quoting, scheduling, job tracking, invoicing, and payments. Xero and QuickBooks Online sync are available on every plan, including the entry-level Lite tier at $47/user/month. Free one-on-one training and phone support are included, not sold as add-ons. The site lists a 4.8 out of 5 rating from 9,000+ ratings and reviews.

Where Tradify falls short is scale and time tracking depth. Reporting, purchase orders, bulk invoicing, and AI tools (SmartRead and SmartWrite) are locked to the Plus tier at $61/user/month. The integration list is shorter than larger competitors. Tradify does not have GPS geofencing, mock location detection, or a live crew map. There is no offline clock-in capability. And for teams above 20 users, the per-user model can become more expensive than a platform with broad-access pricing. For a wider view of the trade job management landscape, see the <a href="/best-field-service-software/">best field service software roundup</a>.

## Pricing reality

### Solo operator or two-person team

For very small crews, ClockShark usually starts too heavy. A solo operator on ClockShark Standard pays $49/month ($40 base plus $9 for one user) and gets GPS time tracking, geofencing, and scheduling - but no quoting, invoicing, or payment tools. For a one-person operation that needs to quote and invoice jobs, Tradify at $47/user/month for Lite covers quoting, invoicing, payments, scheduling, and accounting sync for less than ClockShark's base-plus-one cost.

If the business only needs a basic calendar, quotes, invoices, and reminders, also compare Tradify against <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber</a> and <a href="/housecall-pro-review/">Housecall Pro</a> before committing. ClockShark is not the right tool if quoting and invoicing are the daily workflow.

### Five-person crew

This is where the pricing comparison gets interesting. ClockShark Standard for 5 users costs $76/month: $40 base plus $36 for four additional users. That includes geofencing, GPS tracking, scheduling, and payroll integrations - but no quoting, invoicing, or payment collection.

Tradify Lite for 5 users costs $235/month ($47 x 5). Tradify Pro for 5 users costs $255/month ($51 x 5). Tradify Plus for 5 users costs $305/month ($61 x 5). All Tradify plans include quoting, invoicing, payments, scheduling, and accounting sync.

At five users, Tradify is significantly more expensive than ClockShark. But the products are not doing the same job. If the pain is unverified labor hours and crew location, ClockShark at $76/month solves it directly. If the pain is manual quoting, scattered job schedules, and chasing invoices, Tradify at $235/month replaces multiple tools.

Some contractors use both: ClockShark for GPS time tracking and Tradify for quoting, job management, and invoicing. That is a valid stack, but it means two subscriptions and two apps for the field team. For an alternative that combines time tracking with dispatch, see the <a href="/clockshark-vs-service-fusion/">ClockShark vs Service Fusion</a> comparison.

### Ten-person team

At 10 users, ClockShark Standard costs $121/month ($40 base + $81 for 9 users) and Pro costs $159/month ($60 base + $99 for 9 users). The pricing is predictable and scales linearly.

At 10 users on Tradify, Lite costs $470/month, Pro costs $510/month, and Plus costs $610/month before the $12/month Instant Website add-on if used. Those are known numbers, and for some teams that predictability is the deciding factor. But at 10+ users, the per-user model of Tradify becomes expensive enough to justify looking at platforms with broad-access pricing or custom quotes. For teams evaluating <a href="/servicetitan-review/">ServiceTitan</a> at scale, that platform is built for larger operations.

If the business needs both GPS time tracking and job management at 10+ users, compare the combined cost of ClockShark plus Tradify against an all-in-one platform like <a href="/service-fusion-review/">Service Fusion</a> or <a href="/housecall-pro-review/">Housecall Pro</a>.

### Contract terms compared

ClockShark applies a three-year contract term to all pricing plans. That is a longer commitment than most time tracking tools require. Active-user billing softens this because you only pay for users who actually clock in, but the contract term itself is a commitment.

Tradify does not advertise a contract term on its pricing page. Monthly billing with no long-term contract is typical for per-user SaaS platforms in this category. If contract flexibility matters, Tradify has the edge.

## Where ClockShark wins

### GPS time tracking depth

ClockShark's GPS layer is the core differentiator. When a worker clocks in, the system records their location with a timestamp and a map pin. Managers see a "Who's Working Now" view that shows every clocked-in employee on a live map. For a general contractor running multiple crews, that single view can replace a lot of morning phone calls.

The GPS stamp also serves as a record if a client disputes labor hours on a time-and-materials invoice. That is a practical operational benefit that job management platforms do not replicate. Tradify handles job tracking and scheduling, but it does not have GPS-stamped clock events tied to job site locations.

### Geofencing without a premium paywall

Geofencing lets you draw a virtual radius around each job site. When a worker enters or leaves that zone, ClockShark prompts them to clock in or out automatically. This feature is included in the Standard plan at $40/month plus $9 per user.

QuickBooks Time only offers geofencing on its Elite plan. Tradify does not offer geofencing at all. For a contractor whose main reason for buying software is geofenced reminders at job sites, ClockShark is the only product between these two that delivers it.

### Standalone platform with broad payroll integrations

ClockShark requires no accounting software subscription. No QuickBooks Online, no ecosystem lock-in. For contractors who use ADP, Gusto, Sage, or Paychex, or who want the flexibility to switch accounting platforms later, this removes a structural cost.

ClockShark connects to QuickBooks Online, QuickBooks Desktop, ADP, Gusto, Xero, Sage 100 Contractor, Paychex, Paylocity, and Simpro. It also supports Zapier for 1,000+ additional app connections. Tradify integrates with Xero and QuickBooks Online on all plans, but its integration list is shorter than ClockShark's and it does not connect to ADP, Gusto, Sage, Paychex, or Paylocity directly.

### Active-user billing for seasonal crews

ClockShark bills only for active users and prorates charges for partial days. A landscaping company that scales from 5 people in winter to 20 in summer does not pay for idle accounts during the off-season. Tradify charges per user per month regardless of activity, which is simpler to understand but less flexible for seasonal operations.

Xero and QuickBooks Online sync are available on every Tradify plan, including Lite at $47/user/month. For electricians, plumbers, and HVAC techs who already run on Xero or QBO, that means the accounting connection works from the entry tier without upgrading.

Free one-on-one training and phone support are included with every Tradify plan. That is not a paid add-on or a higher-tier feature. For a small trade business owner who does not want to figure out a new platform alone, included training and phone support can be the difference between adoption and another unused subscription.

ClockShark also includes live support from real humans on all plans, and Pro plan customers get personal onboarding with a customer success representative. Both products take support seriously, but Tradify's one-on-one training on every tier is a practical advantage for first-time software buyers.

## Evaluation plan

### Test ClockShark for one full labor cycle

During the 14-day ClockShark trial, run one real payroll cycle with your actual crew. Have workers clock in at real job sites, verify GPS stamps match reported locations, test geofencing prompts at your typical work radius, and confirm the payroll export works with your existing platform. Also test mock location detection by attempting to clock in from an off-site location.

Then price the plan: Standard versus Pro, monthly billing, and the three-year contract term. Confirm how active-user billing works for your seasonal headcount pattern. If job costing or PTO management matters, test those on Pro before deciding which tier to buy.

### Test Tradify on a real job

For Tradify, the 14-day trial gives full access to all features with no credit card. Use that time to run a real job end to end: capture an inquiry, create a quote, convert it to a job, schedule the work, assign a technician, send a customer update, collect payment, and sync the result to Xero or QuickBooks Online.

Then compare the tier you actually need. If reporting and purchase orders are important, budget for Plus at $61/user/month. If the core workflow of quoting, scheduling, invoicing, and payments is enough, Lite at $47 or Pro at $51 may be the better value. Test the one-on-one training session - it is included, and it can shorten the learning curve significantly.

Tradify is better for small trade teams of 1-20 users that want quoting, invoicing, scheduling, and payments with published per-user pricing. ### Which is cheaper, ClockShark or Tradify?

ClockShark is cheaper at the entry level for small teams. Standard costs $40/month plus $9 per user, which comes to $76/month for a five-person crew. Tradify Lite for five users costs $235/month ($47 x 5). But the products are not doing the same job - ClockShark tracks time, Tradify manages jobs, quotes, invoices, and payments. Comparing them on price alone misses that they solve different problems.

### Does Tradify have GPS time tracking?

No. Tradify handles job tracking and scheduling, but it does not have GPS-stamped clock in/out events, geofencing, mock location detection, or a live crew map. If GPS-verified time tracking is a core requirement, ClockShark is the right tool between these two.

### Can ClockShark replace Tradify for job management?

No. ClockShark does not do quoting, invoicing, payment collection, or job management. It is a time tracking and scheduling tool. If your business needs to turn inquiries into quotes and quotes into paid invoices, ClockShark alone will not replace Tradify. Some contractors use both tools together: ClockShark for time tracking and Tradify for job management.

### Do both products offer a free trial?

Tradify's trial includes all features across all tiers so you can test Plus-level tools like SmartRead and purchase orders before deciding which plan to buy.

### Which product has better integrations?

ClockShark has broader payroll integrations: QuickBooks Online and Desktop, ADP, Gusto, Xero, Sage, Paychex, Paylocity, and Zapier for 1,000+ additional apps. Tradify integrates with Xero and QuickBooks Online on all plans, which covers the two most common accounting platforms for small trade businesses. If you need payroll sync beyond QuickBooks and Xero, ClockShark wins on integration breadth. If Xero or QBO sync at the entry tier is enough, Tradify covers it.

### What should I test during the free trial?

In ClockShark, test GPS accuracy at your actual job sites, verify geofencing works with your typical work radius, confirm the payroll export works with your existing platform, and attempt a mock location detection test. In Tradify, run a real job end to end: capture an inquiry, create a quote, schedule the work, collect payment, and sync to Xero or QuickBooks.
