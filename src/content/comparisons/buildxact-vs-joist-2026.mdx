<p><strong>Short verdict:</strong> Joist is better for contractors who want fast, accurate estimates without learning enterprise software. Buildxact makes sense when you're running complex projects that justify the training time and monthly cost.</p>

## Key decision factors

<p><strong>Do you need live material pricing?</strong></p>
<p>Buildxact connects to supplier feeds so when lumber goes from $4 to $6 per board, your estimate updates automatically. Joist uses static pricing databases you update manually. For volatile material markets, live pricing prevents underbidding disasters.</p>

<p><strong>How complex are your projects?</strong></p>
<p>Joist handles straightforward residential work well. Buildxact includes takeoff tools, phase-based scheduling, and project management features that matter for commercial work but slow down simple jobs.</p>

## Where Buildxact wins

<p><strong>Live material pricing accuracy</strong></p>
<p>Buildxact pulls current prices from suppliers automatically. When you're estimating with lumber, concrete, or steel that swings 20% monthly, this prevents costly underbids. Contractors report sleeping better knowing estimates reflect today's market, not last month's pricing.</p>

<p><strong>Advanced takeoff capabilities</strong></p>
<p>Digital measuring tools let you calculate materials directly from plans. Upload blueprints, mark areas, and Buildxact calculates quantities. For commercial projects with complex layouts, this beats manual measurements for both speed and accuracy.</p>

<p><strong>Project management integration</strong></p>
<p>Estimates flow into scheduling, purchasing, and job costing within the same system. When you win a bid, the project setup is already done. This matters most for general contractors managing multiple phases and subcontractors.</p>

## Where Joist wins

<p><strong>Speed and simplicity</strong></p>
<p>Joist gets you from blank screen to professional estimate in minutes. Pre-built templates for common residential work mean you're customizing, not building from scratch. Contractors consistently praise the learning curve--new users estimate accurately on day one.</p>

<p><strong>Cost for small operations</strong></p>
<p>At $8 per month, Joist costs less annually than Buildxact charges monthly. For solo contractors or small crews, the feature gap doesn't justify the price jump. You get professional-looking estimates without the enterprise software budget.</p>

<p><strong>Mobile-first workflow</strong></p>
<p>Joist's mobile app handles the core estimating workflow completely. Walk a job site, enter measurements, send the estimate from your truck. Buildxact's mobile app exists but contractors report the full feature set works better on desktop.</p>

## Pricing head-to-head

<p>Buildxact starts at $149 monthly and scales up based on user count and project volume. Enterprise features like supplier integrations and advanced reporting push costs higher.</p>

<p>Joist runs $8 monthly for individual contractors, with team plans around $20-30 per user. Even for 5-person crews, you're spending less on Joist annually than Buildxact costs in two months.</p>

## Dealbreakers for each

<p><strong>Skip Buildxact if:</strong></p>
<p>You primarily handle residential work under $50K per project. The learning curve and monthly cost overwhelm the benefits. Contractors report feeling like they're "using a firehose to water a houseplant."</p>

<p><strong>Skip Joist if:</strong></p>
<p>Material costs swing dramatically in your market and you've lost money on stale pricing. Joist's manual pricing updates lag market changes by weeks. Also skip if you need detailed project management beyond estimating.</p>

## Bottom line

<p><strong>Our call:</strong> Joist wins for most contractors. The combination of speed, cost, and reliability handles residential and light commercial work better than tools costing 18x more.</p>

<p><strong>Choose Buildxact if:</strong> You're running complex commercial projects where live material pricing and integrated project management justify the cost and complexity.</p>

<p><strong>Choose Joist if:</strong> You want accurate estimates fast without enterprise software training or budgets. Works for 80% of contractor estimating needs at 5% of the enterprise cost.</p>
