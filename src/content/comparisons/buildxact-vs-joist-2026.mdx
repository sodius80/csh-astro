<p>Buildxact versus Joist for estimating. The real choice is complexity versus simplicity, with a 1,800% price difference to match.</p>

<p><strong>Disclosure:</strong> Some links on this page are affiliate links. If you sign up through one, I earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. My recommendations don't change based on that.</p>

<p><strong>Short verdict:</strong> Joist is better for contractors who want fast, accurate estimates without learning enterprise software. Buildxact makes sense when you're running complex projects that justify the training time and monthly cost.</p>

## Key decision factors

<p><strong>Do you need supplier-connected estimating depth?</strong></p>
<p>Buildxact is the stronger choice when estimating depends on digital takeoffs, dealer integration, purchase orders, and project costing. Joist is better treated as an estimates/invoices/payments app: it can help you build and send professional documents, but it is not trying to manage a full takeoff-to-purchase workflow.</p>

<p><strong>How complex are your projects?</strong></p>
<p>Joist handles straightforward residential work well. Buildxact includes takeoff tools, phase-based scheduling, and project management features that matter for commercial work but slow down simple jobs.</p>

## Where Buildxact wins

<p><strong>Supplier-connected estimating workflow</strong></p>
<p>Buildxact is stronger when estimating depends on digital takeoffs, dealer integration, purchase orders, and structured costing. That is a different job than Joist, which is built around fast estimates, invoices, payments, and document tracking rather than a full takeoff-to-purchasing workflow.</p>

<p><strong>Advanced takeoff capabilities</strong></p>
<p>Digital measuring tools let you calculate materials directly from plans. Upload blueprints, mark areas, and Buildxact calculates quantities. For commercial projects with complex layouts, this beats manual measurements for both speed and accuracy.</p>

<p><strong>Project management integration</strong></p>
<p>Estimates flow into scheduling, purchasing, and job costing within the same system. When you win a bid, the project setup is already done. This matters most for general contractors managing multiple phases and subcontractors.</p>

## Where Joist wins

<p><strong>Speed and simplicity</strong></p>
<p>Joist gets you from blank screen to professional estimate in minutes. Pre-built templates for common residential work mean you're customizing, not building from scratch. Contractors consistently praise the learning curve--new users estimate accurately on day one.</p>

<p><strong>Cost for small operations</strong></p>
<p>At $10 per month, or $100 per year, Joist Basics still costs less annually than Buildxact charges monthly. For solo contractors or small crews that mostly need estimates, invoices, payments, and simple document tracking, the feature gap may not justify the price jump.</p>

<p><strong>Mobile-first workflow</strong></p>
<p>Joist's mobile app handles the core estimating workflow completely. Walk a job site, enter measurements, send the estimate from your truck. Buildxact's mobile app exists but contractors report the full feature set works better on desktop.</p>

## Pricing head-to-head

<p>Buildxact starts at $199/month for Foundation, or $169/month when billed annually, and its current public plans list unlimited users. Pro costs $399/month ($339/month billed annually) when you need job management, schedules, and the Buildxact Onsite mobile app. Master runs $599/month ($509/month billed annually) and adds Blu Takeoff Assistant, Blu Estimate Reviewer, user access controls, and priority support.</p>

<p>Joist now lists Basics at $10/month or $100/year, Pro at $16/month or $160/year, and Elite at $32/month or $320/year. Even Joist Elite remains far below Buildxact Foundation, but the product scope is narrower: Joist centers on documents, payments, QuickBooks sync, work orders, change orders, and lightweight reporting rather than full project estimating and management.</p>

## Dealbreakers for each

<p><strong>Skip Buildxact if:</strong></p>
<p>You primarily handle residential work under $50K per project. The learning curve and monthly cost overwhelm the benefits. Contractors report feeling like they're "using a firehose to water a houseplant."</p>

<p><strong>Skip Joist if:</strong></p>
<p>Your estimates depend on takeoffs, dealer integration, purchasing, and job management rather than simple document generation. Joist is not trying to be a full construction estimating and project management system, so skip it if you need that heavier workflow.</p>

## Bottom line

<p><strong>Our call:</strong> Joist wins for most contractors. The combination of speed, cost, and reliability handles residential and light commercial work better than tools costing 18x more.</p>

<p><strong>Choose Buildxact if:</strong> You're running complex projects where takeoff depth, dealer integration, purchasing, and project management justify the cost and complexity.</p>

<p><strong>Choose Joist if:</strong> You want accurate estimates fast without enterprise software training or budgets. Works for 80% of contractor estimating needs at 5% of the enterprise cost.</p>
