# QuoteIQ vs Tradify for Trade Contractors

QuoteIQ and Tradify both publish their prices. Neither hides behind a "book a demo" wall. That alone puts them in the same shortlist for a contractor who wants to compare software without a sales call. But they answer different questions about how a trade business should pay for and run its software.

QuoteIQ is a flat-rate, AI-first field service platform for home-service contractors. Atlas verifies five published plans from $29.99/month to $699/month, with annual billing discounts and included user counts by tier. AI tools, satellite measurement, photo documentation, review automation, and payment collection are built in. Read the full [QuoteIQ review](/quoteiq-review/) for the product-level breakdown.

Tradify is a per-user, trade-specific job management app for small trade businesses. Atlas verifies three published tiers at $47, $51, and $61 per user per month, plus custom pricing for large teams. It covers the inquiry-to-payment workflow with Xero and QuickBooks sync on every plan. Read the full [Tradify review](/tradify-review/) for the source-checked feature map.

## The actual buying split

### QuoteIQ is a flat-rate, AI-and-measurement decision

QuoteIQ makes the monthly budget predictable. Its official pricing page lists Essentials at $29.99/month, Beginner at $74.99/month, Pro at $149.99/month, Elite at $299/month, and Max at $699/month. Annual billing lowers the monthly equivalent to $25, $62.50, $125, $249, and $582.50 respectively. Atlas has those numbers verified from the official pricing page.

The flat-rate model means a contractor can pick a tier based on team size and feature needs without recalculating every time a crew member joins. The plan table makes user assumptions visible: 1 user on Essentials, 2 on Beginner, 4 on Pro, 10 on Elite, and unlimited users on Max.

QuoteIQ's strongest fit is a home-service company that wants one lighter operating system instead of several point tools. The official pricing page supports estimates, invoices, scheduling, online payments, AI Virtual Call Team, consumer financing, MapMeasure Pro, QuoteIQ Cam, Review Multiplier, QuickBooks on Pro and above, job costing, inventory, routing, dispatching, EmployeeHub, and crew tools by tier.

The risk is not price mystery. It is whether the contractor uses the included tools. If the team only needs a calendar, invoices, and payments, the AI credits, satellite measurement, and photo documentation may sit unused.

### Tradify is a per-user, trade-workflow decision

Tradify asks a different question: how many people need access, and what workflow depth do they need? Its official pricing page lists Lite at $47 per user per month, Pro at $51 per user per month, and Plus at $61 per user per month. Custom pricing is available by request for large teams. Atlas has those numbers verified from the official pricing page.

That model is transparent in a different way. A 5-person plumbing team on Pro pays 5 x $51 = $255/month. A 10-person electrical team on Plus pays 10 x $61 = $610/month. The math is simple and scales with headcount.

Tradify's strongest fit is a small trade business (1-20 users) that wants the inquiry-to-payment workflow in one straightforward platform. The official pricing page supports job management, scheduling, quoting, invoicing, payments, and Xero and QuickBooks Online sync on every plan.

The risk is feature gating. Reporting, purchase orders, bulk invoicing, job tasks, and AI tools (SmartRead and SmartWrite) are locked to the Plus tier at $61 per user per month. For a team that needs any one of those features, the per-user model jumps $10 per person.

## AI comparison

### QuoteIQ's AI is built into every plan

QuoteIQ's AI story is part of the plan table. The official pricing page ties AI credits to plans: 500 monthly credits on Essentials, 1,500 on Beginner, 3,000 on Pro, 5,000 on Elite, and 8,000 on Max. AI features appear across the plan structure, including AI Autopilot, Virtual Call Team, AI Estimator, AI image tools, and related automation.

The Virtual Call Team is the most distinctive AI feature. It answers inbound calls 24/7, books appointments, and routes complex calls to the office. For a home-service contractor who misses leads because nobody answers the phone after hours, that feature alone can change the business. The AI Estimator generates quotes from property photos and scope details. MapMeasure Pro gives satellite-based property measurement for fence lines, roof areas, and surface areas.

That does not mean every contractor should trust the AI on day one. The right test is job-specific. Have the Virtual Call Team answer realistic calls. Ask AI Autopilot to perform common field actions. Use the estimator on jobs you already know how to price. Then compare the output with your current process.

### Tradify's AI is Plus-only

Tradify has added two AI-powered tools called SmartTools, available exclusively on the Plus plan at $61 per user per month. SmartRead for Bills reads supplier bills and imports costs into the system. SmartWrite generates descriptions and improves writing for quotes, invoices, and job notes.

The gating means a Pro user at $51 per user per month does not get AI tools. For a 10-person team, jumping from Pro to Plus costs $100/month more. Whether that is worth it depends on how much time SmartRead and SmartWrite save in practice.

If AI tools are a primary buying reason, QuoteIQ includes them on every plan. Tradify locks them behind the top tier. That is the clearest AI-related split in this comparison.

## Workflow depth

### QuoteIQ is strongest for home-service quoting and measurement

QuoteIQ fits the contractor whose daily loop is lead, estimate, schedule, complete work, invoice, collect payment, request review, and follow up. The product's value is that those activities sit near each other and the price is visible.

For pressure washing, fencing, landscaping, roofing service, pest control, window cleaning, and similar home-service work, that can be enough. MapMeasure Pro helps with property measurement. QuoteIQ Cam helps with photos and job reports. Review Multiplier helps after the job is done. AI Autopilot and Virtual Call Team can reduce office work if they hold up in real use.

The evaluation should stay practical. Can a technician or owner create an estimate from the job site? Can the office see the schedule? Can the company collect payment without a workaround? Does QuickBooks sync at the tier you need? Does the AI save time without creating cleanup work?

### Tradify is strongest for trade job management from inquiry to payment

Tradify fits the contractor whose daily loop is inquiry, quote, schedule, track, invoice, and collect. The official pricing page organizes all job management around a six-step process: inquiries, quoting, scheduling, job tracking, invoicing, and payments. That maps cleanly to how an electrical, plumbing, HVAC, or property maintenance business operates.

The Pro plan adds an inquiry form and email inbox, which means inquiries can arrive and be tracked inside Tradify rather than through a separate email or phone log. Quoting is available on all tiers, including Lite. Progress invoicing is available on Pro and above. Plus adds bulk invoicing for teams that process multiple invoices in batches.

Tradify does not have the measurement, photo documentation, or AI call-answering depth of QuoteIQ. If the trade needs satellite measurement for fence lines or roof areas, Tradify is not the right tool. If the trade needs a clean, trade-focused job workflow with accounting sync from day one, Tradify is the stronger pick.

## Accounting and integrations

QuoteIQ and Tradify both belong in a QuickBooks conversation, but the evidence points to different buyer types.

QuoteIQ includes QuickBooks integration on Pro and above. That is enough for a home-service company that wants estimates, invoices, payments, and customer records to move into accounting without a separate integration project. During the trial, test the sync with real item names, taxes, payments, deposits, and job records.

Tradify syncs natively with Xero and QuickBooks Online on all plans, including Lite. That is a genuine advantage over QuoteIQ, which gates QuickBooks behind the Pro tier. For a trade business where the owner handles bookkeeping and needs invoice and payment data to flow into the accounting system from day one, Tradify's approach is simpler and cheaper at the entry level.

Tradify also integrates with Stripe for online payments and Google Calendar for scheduling sync. The integration list is intentionally narrow. If your workflow depends on a specific tool that is not Xero, QuickBooks, Stripe, or Google Calendar, verify availability before committing. QuoteIQ's philosophy is to include capabilities natively (measurement, photos, reviews, AI) rather than requiring third-party subscriptions.

## Wrong-fit signals

Do not choose QuoteIQ if the business needs a deeply configured operating system, complex multi-location controls, or a broad enterprise integration plan. QuoteIQ is strongest as a clear, lighter platform for home-service contractors. It can become the wrong fit if the company is already managing multiple departments and needs custom process design.

Do not choose Tradify if the company needs inventory management or purchase orders below the Plus tier, AI tools on lower tiers, or a broader integration ecosystem than Xero, QuickBooks, Stripe, and Google Calendar. Tradify is designed for small trade businesses. Operations over 20 users that need advanced dispatching, multi-branch management, or deep reporting should look elsewhere.

Do not choose either product because the pricing model sounds simpler. Match the model to the team and the toolset. QuoteIQ's flat rate works when the included tools are used. Tradify's per-user rate works when the team is small and the workflow is trade-focused.

## Evaluation plan

### Step 1: define the job lifecycle

Write down one real job from lead to payment. Include the lead source, customer record, estimate, schedule, field assignment, photos, notes, invoice, payment, accounting sync, and review request.

If the company is measurement-heavy, add property measurement, fence lines or roof areas, and photo documentation.

If the company is trade-service-heavy, add inquiry capture, quote options, progress invoicing, purchase orders, and bulk invoicing if relevant.

### Step 2: test QuoteIQ inside the trial

Use QuoteIQ for real work during the trial. Create a customer, build an estimate, run MapMeasure Pro if relevant, schedule a job, attach photos, send an invoice, collect a test payment, and check the QuickBooks workflow if you need it. Test AI with realistic calls and field actions, not made-up prompts.

The decision should be simple: if the workflow works and the plan price fits the team size, QuoteIQ is a strong first choice.

### Step 3: test Tradify inside the trial

Start the Tradify trial on Plus to access all features, since the trial includes everything. Create a customer, send a quote, schedule a job, track it, invoice it, and test payment collection. Test the Xero or QuickBooks sync with real item names and invoices. Try SmartRead and SmartWrite with a real supplier bill.

The decision should be simple: if the workflow fits the trade and the per-user price matches the team size, Tradify is a strong pick.

### Step 4: compare the pricing models with real headcount

Calculate the monthly cost for both products at your actual team size. A 4-person team on QuoteIQ Pro pays $149.99/month. The same team on Tradify Pro pays $204/month. A solo operator on Tradify Lite pays $47/month. The same operator on QuoteIQ Essentials pays $29.99/month.

But the feature sets are not identical. QuoteIQ Pro includes QuickBooks, job costing, Client Portal, and automation. Tradify Pro includes custom branding, inquiry form, customer communications history, recurring jobs, and subcontractor scheduling. Neither product is cheaper in a vacuum. The value depends on which included features the team actually uses.
