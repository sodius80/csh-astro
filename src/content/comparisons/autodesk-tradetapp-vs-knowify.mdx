# TradeTapp vs Knowify: Which Problem Are You Solving?

Autodesk TradeTapp and Knowify both appear in construction software conversations, but they are not substitutes for each other. TradeTapp is a subcontractor prequalification and risk management platform built for general contractors inside the Autodesk Construction Cloud ecosystem. Knowify is a project management, billing, and job-cost platform built for trade contractors who run their books in QuickBooks.

That distinction matters. If a contractor compares only price, Knowify looks like the simpler buy because it publishes plan rates and TradeTapp is custom quote only. But the two tools are solving different problems. A GC who needs to vet 300 subcontractors before a bid package will not get that from Knowify. A plumbing contractor who needs AIA pay applications and progress billing tied to QuickBooks will not get that from TradeTapp.

## The fast answer

Choose <a href="/go/autodesk-tradetapp-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Autodesk TradeTapp</a> if you are a general contractor or project owner already using Autodesk Construction Cloud and your primary need is subcontractor prequalification. TradeTapp's differentiator is a 250,000+ pre-registered subcontractor network. When a sub completes qualification for one GC, that profile is available to other GCs on the network, reducing repeated data entry. The platform also automates financial health scoring, safety and insurance risk analysis, and industry benchmarking. Pricing is custom quote through Autodesk sales.

Choose <a href="/go/knowify-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Knowify</a> if you are a specialty trade contractor using QuickBooks and your primary need is project financial control. Knowify's official pricing page lists Core at $99/mo billed yearly for one included user, or $149/mo billed monthly. Advanced is $329/mo billed yearly with 10 included users, or $399/mo billed monthly. AIA pay applications and progress billing are listed on all plans, while job costing and real-time WIP reporting are on Advanced and Enterprise.

The simplest split: TradeTapp helps a GC vet subcontractors. Knowify helps a trade contractor control project finances. If you need both, you may need both tools, or you may need a different shortlist entirely.

Add-ons include Service Pro, Live Equipment Tracking, AI Dashcams, and Tool Tracking.

A trade contractor evaluating Knowify is paying for project billing and job-cost tools. The dollar amounts are not comparable without that context. ## Primary purpose and target buyer

TradeTapp is built for general contractors, project owners, and developers who manage subcontractor risk at scale. The official Autodesk construction pricing page lists TradeTapp inside the Preconstruction bundle, which includes BuildingConnected Pro (bid management), Bid Board Pro, ProEst (estimating), Forma Takeoff, and Forma Estimate. That bundle positions TradeTapp as a preconstruction tool, not an accounting or project execution platform.

Knowify is built for trade contractors. The official pricing page describes Core as designed for "bidding, executing, and invoicing fixed-price and AIA jobs while managing your team" and Advanced as adding "powerful project management and job costing tools for greater control and visibility." The industries Atlas tracks for Knowify include electrical, plumbing, HVAC, drywall, and painting trades. Knowify assumes QuickBooks is the accounting backbone and adds contractor-specific workflow around it.

A general contractor vetting subcontractors and a plumbing contractor billing AIA jobs are in different roles. The software that fits one will not fit the other.

## Subcontractor management vs project accounting

TradeTapp's subcontractor management is about qualification and risk, not project execution. GCs create customizable prequalification questionnaires that capture financial statements, safety records, insurance certificates, and compliance data. The platform automatically calculates financial ratios, runs limit-based credit assessments, and benchmarks each subcontractor against industry peers. When a risk flag triggers, the system suggests mitigation steps such as requiring higher insurance limits or adding financial covenants. The 250,000+ pre-registered network means subs who have already qualified for one GC can reuse their data when invited by another.

Knowify's subcontractor management is about project-level cost tracking. The official feature table lists subcontractor management on Advanced and Enterprise, not Core. It is designed to track subcontractor costs within a project budget, not to vet subcontractor financial health across a portfolio. A trade contractor using Knowify to manage a project with two or three subcontractors is solving a different problem than a GC using TradeTapp to vet 300 subs before a bid.

## QuickBooks and accounting integration

This is a fundamental difference. Knowify is built around QuickBooks. The official pricing page describes the QuickBooks Online integration as syncing nearly everything both ways, including customers, vendors, purchases, expenses, invoices, time, and projects. QuickBooks Online, QuickBooks Time, QuickBooks Online Payroll, and QuickBooks Payments are listed across plans. QuickBooks Desktop and Intuit Enterprise Suite are listed on Enterprise. For a trade contractor whose bookkeeper or CPA requires QuickBooks, Knowify preserves the accounting system while adding contractor workflow.

TradeTapp is not a QuickBooks-connected tool. Through Kroo Data Connectors, qualification data can sync to ERP systems like Oracle Primavera Cloud, Sage 300 CRE, and CMiC, plus accounting platforms like QuickBooks Online and Sage Intacct. But that integration is about pushing qualification data into other systems, not about running day-to-day project accounting. TradeTapp does not handle invoicing, progress billing, job costing, or financial reporting for individual projects.

If the accounting system is the decision, Knowify is the relevant comparison. If subcontractor vetting is the decision, TradeTapp is the relevant comparison. ## AIA billing and progress billing

Knowify lists AIA pay applications (G702/G703) and progress billing on all plans, including Core. For commercial subcontractors doing electrical, mechanical, drywall, or painting work, progress billing touches Schedule of Values setup, retainage, change orders, owner billing expectations, and accounting cleanup. Knowify is built around that workflow.

TradeTapp does not handle AIA billing or progress billing. It is a prequalification platform. A GC using TradeTapp to vet subs still needs a separate system (Procore, Autodesk Build, or another construction management platform) for project execution and billing. If AIA billing is a requirement, TradeTapp is not the tool.

## User model and team growth

Knowify's user model is plan-gated. Core includes 1 user, Advanced includes 10 users, and Enterprise includes unlimited users. Atlas verifies additional users at $29/mo. For a 5-person trade contractor, the real cost is not just the Core headline. For a team that needs job costing, Advanced at $329/mo annual is the practical starting point.

TradeTapp's user model is custom per agreement. The number of users who create or review qualification data, the volume of subcontractors stored and analyzed, and the depth of integration all factor into the annual cost. Buyers should get full scope in writing before evaluating cost.

## Where Autodesk TradeTapp wins

**Prequalification network.** The 250,000+ pre-registered subcontractor network is TradeTapp's real differentiator. When a sub qualifies for one GC, the profile is available to other GCs on the network. No standalone prequalification tool offers a pre-vetted pool at this scale.

**Automated financial health scoring.** Instead of manually reviewing spreadsheets, GCs get automated risk scores with supporting data. The system benchmarks each subcontractor's financial and safety metrics against a crowdsourced network of vetted trades, making qualification decisions defensible to project owners and bonding companies.

**Autodesk ecosystem integration.** For GCs already in Autodesk Construction Cloud, TradeTapp connects natively to BuildingConnected Pro, Autodesk Build (Cost Management), and Autodesk Forma. Risk flags appear alongside cost items. Qualification status shows up next to bid packages. That integration eliminates the copy-paste burden of standalone prequalification tools.

## Where Knowify wins

**Published contractor pricing.** Core and Advanced give buyers real budget numbers before a sales call. A trade contractor can model Core, Advanced, users, and add-ons before committing to a demo.

**AIA and progress billing on all plans.** For commercial subcontractors, AIA pay applications and progress billing are not afterthoughts. They are listed on Core, not gated to higher tiers.

**QuickBooks Online sync.** Knowify is designed for contractors who want to keep QuickBooks as the accounting backbone. The two-way sync covers customers, vendors, purchases, expenses, invoices, time, and projects.

**Job costing and project financial control.** Advanced and Enterprise add job costing, real-time WIP reporting, project budgets, cost-plus and T&M contracts, subcontracts, and advanced reporting. For a trade contractor asking "did this project make money?", Knowify is built around that question.

## Where each product can be the wrong fit

TradeTapp can be the wrong fit if your organization uses Procore or another non-Autodesk platform as its primary construction management system. The native data flow that makes TradeTapp valuable only works inside the Autodesk ecosystem. Kroo connectors can bridge some gaps, but the integration advantage disappears. TradeTapp is also not a fit for small contractors who need published monthly pricing, or for teams that need lightweight prequalification without enterprise complexity.

Knowify can be the wrong fit if the real problem is dispatch-first service work. Knowify has a Service Pro add-on for service departments, but a pure service company should compare dispatch-first tools like Jobber, Housecall Pro, or FieldEdge before choosing a project-accounting platform. Knowify can also be too much system for a very small contractor that only needs invoices, expenses, and basic reports. ## Evaluation plan

For TradeTapp, prepare a scope brief before contacting Autodesk sales. Know your user count, subcontractor volume, required integrations (BuildingConnected, Cost Management, or Kroo connectors), and analytics needs. During the demo, focus on whether the approval routing matches your internal process and whether the subcontractor self-service mobile interface works for your subs. Get the full scope, user assumptions, vendor volume tiers, and renewal terms in writing before evaluating cost.

For Knowify, test one real project through the platform. Build an estimate, create a contract, add a change order, create a progress invoice, log field time, add purchases and expenses, and review project costs. If job costing is the reason for the purchase, evaluate Advanced rather than assuming Core is enough. Test the mobile app with the people who will use it in the field. Confirm your QuickBooks product (Online Plus, Advanced, Desktop, or Intuit Enterprise Suite) and which integrations are included on your plan.

For both, involve the person who owns the books or the procurement process. A GC evaluating TradeTapp needs procurement alignment. A trade contractor evaluating Knowify needs the bookkeeper on board with the QuickBooks integration scope.

## FAQ

### Are Autodesk TradeTapp and Knowify the same type of software?

No. TradeTapp is a subcontractor prequalification and risk management platform for general contractors. Knowify is a project management, billing, and job-cost platform for trade contractors. They solve different problems for different buyers.

### Can Knowify replace TradeTapp for subcontractor prequalification?

No. Knowify has subcontractor management features on Advanced and Enterprise, but those are for tracking subcontractor costs within a project, not for vetting financial health, safety records, and insurance across a subcontractor network. TradeTapp's 250,000+ pre-registered sub network is the differentiator.

### Can TradeTapp replace Knowify for project billing?

No. TradeTapp does not handle AIA billing, progress billing, job costing, or QuickBooks integration for project execution. A GC using TradeTapp still needs a separate construction management platform for project billing and accounting.

### Which is cheaper, TradeTapp or Knowify?

The pricing models are not directly comparable. TradeTapp is custom quote through Autodesk sales with no published tiers. Knowify publishes Core at $99/mo annual ($149/mo monthly) and Advanced at $329/mo annual ($399/mo monthly), with users and add-ons affecting the total. Knowify gives you published prices to model before a call. TradeTapp requires a sales conversation for any cost estimate.

### Which tool is better for a general contractor?

A general contractor managing subcontractor risk at scale should evaluate TradeTapp first, especially if already in the Autodesk Construction Cloud ecosystem. A GC who also needs project execution, billing, and accounting will need a separate platform for those workflows.

### Which tool is better for a trade contractor?

A trade contractor (electrical, plumbing, HVAC, drywall, painting) using QuickBooks should evaluate Knowify first. The AIA billing, progress billing, job costing, and QuickBooks sync are built for that buyer. TradeTapp is not designed for trade contractors managing individual project finances.
