## The real buying split

### ArboStar is a tree-care specialization decision

ArboStar makes sense when the business runs tree removals, pruning, stump grinding, commercial arborist work, and emergency storm cleanup across multiple crews and sites. The official pricing page and feature copy position the platform around arborist CRM, live job maps, GPS visibility, equipment tracking, safety documentation, field notes, and tree-specific estimating.

That depth matters because tree work is not interchangeable service calls. The right crew, chipper, bucket truck, crane, rigging gear, and safety equipment must arrive together at the right site. ArboStar's map and dispatch features, equipment assignment, and crew composition tools are built for that reality. For a company that returns to the same properties for pruning, plant health, removals, and storm cleanup, the arborist CRM records client tree history, past work, and service records in ways a generalist CRM cannot. For broader tree care tool options, see the <a href="/best-tree-service-software/">best tree service software roundup</a>.

The tradeoff is price clarity and setup. ArboStar uses custom pricing from $250 per month, with the final quote affected by company size, license count, required features, integrations, and platform usage. The free demo, not a self-serve trial, is the evaluation path. That means the business has to invest time in a sales conversation before knowing the real cost.

### QuoteIQ is a price-clarity and broad-workflow decision

QuoteIQ makes the first budget pass easy. Its official pricing page lists Essentials at $29.99/month, Beginner at $74.99/month, Pro at $149.99/month, Elite at $299/month, and Max at $699/month. Annual billing lowers the monthly equivalent to $25, $62.50, $125, $249, and $582.50 respectively. Atlas has those numbers verified from the official pricing page. The 14-day free trial lets a contractor start testing immediately without a sales call.

QuoteIQ's breadth is the point. The platform covers estimates, invoices, scheduling, online payments, CRM, AI Autopilot, Virtual Call Team, AI Estimator, MapMeasure Pro satellite measurement, QuoteIQ Cam photo documentation, Review Multiplier, QuickBooks on Pro and above, job costing, routing, dispatching, inventory, and crew tools by tier. For a home-service contractor running pressure washing, fencing, landscaping, roofing service, pest control, window cleaning, or similar trades, that all-in-one approach can replace several separate subscriptions.

The tradeoff is specialization. QuoteIQ serves 50+ trades with a broad feature set, but it does not offer tree-specific CRM records, arborist service history, live job maps for tree crew dispatch, equipment tracking for chippers and bucket trucks, or safety forms tied to tree work. A tree care company using QuoteIQ would still need workarounds for equipment-heavy days and arborist-specific documentation.

## Workflow depth

### ArboStar wins when tree work is the core operation

For an established arborist company, the daily operation is not just scheduling and invoicing. It is dispatching the right crew with the right truck and equipment to the right site, documenting safety hazards and job conditions, tracking tree service history per client property, and managing estimates that depend on site conditions, tree species, and access.

ArboStar's official pages are centered on those workflows. The arborist CRM records client tree history, past work, equipment notes, and service records. Live job maps with GPS visibility help managers route tree crews and track where resources are tied up. Equipment tracking covers vehicles, chippers, bucket trucks, trailers, saws, and safety gear, including maintenance schedules, fuel costs, and GPS-linked assets. The estimating workflow includes work type, job size, equipment needs, crew composition, photos, notes, and digital signatures.

That clustering matters for tree companies. A generalist FSM can schedule a tree removal and send an invoice. It cannot track which chipper is on which site, record that a client has a hazardous oak near power lines requiring a bucket truck, or attach safety forms to a specific pruning job. For a company running 3+ crews across multiple tree sites per day, that depth is the buying reason.

### QuoteIQ wins when the daily service loop is the bottleneck

QuoteIQ fits the contractor whose daily loop is lead, estimate, schedule, complete work, invoice, collect payment, request review, and follow up. The product's value is that those activities sit near each other in one platform at a visible price.

For pressure washing, fencing, landscaping, roofing service, pest control, window cleaning, and similar home-service work, that can be enough. MapMeasure Pro helps with property measurement from satellite imagery, useful for fence linear footage, roof area, driveway surface area, and exterior cleaning square footage. QuoteIQ Cam captures GPS-tagged 4K job photos. Review Multiplier automates post-job review requests. AI Autopilot handles 35+ natural-language CRM commands. Does QuickBooks sync at the tier you need? Does AI save time without creating cleanup work? If yes, QuoteIQ is a faster and easier buying path than ArboStar for broad home-service work. ## Evaluation plan

Write one sentence: "Our biggest daily problem is _____, and our crews are _____." Do not compare $250/month (ArboStar starting point) against $29.99/month (QuoteIQ Essentials) in isolation. Compare cost against the problem solved.

ArboStar can be the better buy at a higher price if it replaces manual equipment tracking, reduces dispatch errors, and gives tree crews clear field documentation.
