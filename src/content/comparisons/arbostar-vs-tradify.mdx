# ArboStar vs Tradify for Tree Care and Trade Contractors

ArboStar vs Tradify looks like a field service comparison, but these two tools target different segments of the contractor market. The decision is not about which feature list is longer. It is about what kind of work the business does and what kind of platform fits that work.

ArboStar is built for arborist and tree care businesses. It answers the question that established tree care companies face: how do I run CRM, scheduling, equipment tracking, field documentation, and job mapping in one system built specifically for tree work? Tradify is built for small trade businesses - electricians, plumbers, HVAC techs. It answers a different question: how do I get from inquiry to payment in a simple, affordable app with pricing I can see before I sign up?

Those are different problems, and buying the wrong tool for the work will leave a contractor frustrated. A tree care company that needs arborist CRM, live job maps, and safety documentation will not find those in a trade job management app. A plumbing or electrical business that needs quoting, invoicing, and accounting sync will not benefit from paying for arborist-specific features they will never use. ## The buying split

### ArboStar is an arborist business management platform first

ArboStar's main advantage is specialization. The platform is built specifically for arborist and tree care businesses, not adapted from a general field service tool. That means every module - CRM, scheduling, estimating, invoicing, equipment tracking, field documentation, job mapping - is designed around how tree care companies actually operate.

The arborist CRM records client tree history, service records, and communication history specific to tree work. For a company that returns to the same properties for pruning, removals, plant health, and storm cleanup, that depth matters. A salesperson can pull up prior work, and a crew can see photos and notes from the last visit without digging through files.

Live job maps and GPS positioning give managers a real-time view of where crews are and which equipment is tied up. Tree jobs are not interchangeable service calls. The equipment needs, crew composition, and site conditions change per job. A chipper truck, a bucket truck, and specific rigging gear may all need to arrive together at the right site. ArboStar's map and dispatch features reflect that operational reality.

Equipment tracking covers vehicles, tools, chainsaws, chippers, rigging gear, maintenance schedules, and fuel costs. For a multi-crew tree care company managing hundreds of thousands of dollars in equipment across multiple sites, that is not a nice-to-have. It is operational infrastructure.

The official pricing page lists a starting point of $250 per month, but the final quote depends on company size, license count, required features, integrations, and platform usage. Annual billing includes two months free. Because ArboStar requires a sales conversation to get a real price, it is harder to budget for before the demo. That is the trade-off for getting a tree-specific platform instead of a generalist tool.

Where ArboStar falls short: it is not a general trade service tool. An electrical, plumbing, or HVAC company would be paying for arborist-specific features they will not use. The mobile app covers job details, notes, photos, and schedule updates, but some users report it lacks functions available on desktop. Setup requires real configuration around crews, equipment, pricing, and accounting processes. For a lighter field service alternative, see our <a href="/arbostar-vs-jobber/">ArboStar vs Jobber comparison</a>.

### Tradify is a trade job management app first

Tradify's main advantage is simplicity and transparency. The platform publishes its prices on the official pricing page: Lite at $47 per user per month, Pro at $51, and Plus at $61. No strategy session required to see the numbers. A 14-day free trial includes all features and requires no credit card.

For a 5-person team, Tradify Pro costs $255 per month total ($51 x 5). A 10-person team on Plus pays $610 per month ($61 x 10). The math is straightforward. The Instant Website add-on costs $12 per month on any plan.

Tradify covers the full trade job workflow in six steps: inquiries, quoting, scheduling, job tracking, invoicing, and payments. Xero and QuickBooks Online sync is available on every plan, including Lite at $47. That is a genuine advantage over competitors that gate accounting sync behind higher tiers or charge extra for it.

Free one-on-one training and phone support are included, not a paid add-on. Tradify holds a 4.8 out of 5 rating on Trustpilot from 548 reviews. The vendor also cites 9,000+ total ratings across platforms including app stores. For how Tradify compares to another trade-focused tool, see our <a href="/tradify-vs-workiz/">Tradify vs Workiz comparison</a>.

Where Tradify falls short: it is not built for tree care. There is no arborist CRM, no tree mapping, no equipment tracking, and no safety documentation module. Reporting, purchase orders, bulk invoicing, and AI tools (SmartRead and SmartWrite) are locked to the Plus tier at $61 per user per month. The integration list is narrow - Xero, QuickBooks, Stripe, and Google Calendar. For a broader comparison of trade tools, see our <a href="/field-service-software-alternatives/">best field service software roundup</a>.

## Pricing reality

### ArboStar: custom quote with a starting point

ArboStar uses customized pricing based on specific business needs, tools used, and platform usage. The official pricing page lists a starting price of $250 per month, but the final quote depends on company size, license count, required features, integrations, and platform usage. Multiple-license discounts are available, and annual subscriptions include two months free.

Pricing requires a sales conversation and plan selection for the final quote. That is not a reason to reject ArboStar. It is a reason to require a written quote before comparing it against Tradify. For ArboStar, the quote should separate:

- Base subscription and license count
- Required modules (CRM, scheduling, equipment tracking, field documentation, job mapping)
- QuickBooks or accounting integration setup
- Mobile app access and user roles
- Implementation, onboarding, and training costs
- Support level and 24/7 availability
- Renewal term, cancellation, and price-change language

If the sales quote cannot answer those items in writing, the comparison is not ready. ArboStar may still be the right platform for a tree care company, but the buyer cannot know the real cost without it.

## Can you use both?

It is unlikely. A tree care company running ArboStar does not need Tradify, because ArboStar covers CRM, scheduling, estimating, invoicing, and payments. The only scenario where both might coexist is if a tree care company also runs a separate trade service division (e.g., a tree care company that also does property maintenance). In that case, the trade division could use Tradify for its simpler workflow while the tree division uses ArboStar.

For most contractors, the decision is one or the other. If the work is tree care, ArboStar's specialization earns its cost. If the work is electrical, plumbing, HVAC, or property maintenance, Tradify's published pricing and trade-focused workflow are the better fit.

### Can Tradify handle tree care scheduling?

Tradify can schedule tree service jobs with its calendar view and job tracking. It does not offer tree-specific features like arborist CRM, equipment tracking, live job maps with crew and equipment assignment, or field documentation for safety forms and hazard notes. ### What trades is Tradify built for?

Tradify is built for electricians, plumbers, gas engineers, HVAC and refrigeration techs, and property maintenance services. Tradify is built for small trade businesses - electrical, plumbing, HVAC - with published per-user pricing and a clean inquiry-to-payment workflow.
