# AccuLynx vs Jobber (2026): Which One Fits Your Contractor Business?

AccuLynx and Jobber look like they compete for the same contractor. Both manage customer records, estimates, job tracking, and invoices. Both have mobile apps. Both appear in the same comparison searches. But they are built around fundamentally different kinds of work - and choosing the wrong one does not mean you picked a bad product. It means the software will fight your workflow every day instead of supporting it. For more detail, see our <a href="/acculynx-review/">AccuLynx review</a> and <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber review</a>.

AccuLynx is a roofing-specific CRM purpose-built for exterior contractors. It is designed for multi-stage projects that move through distinct phases over days or weeks: lead, inspection, estimate, insurance approval, material order, production, final payment. The features that make it valuable - ABC Supply and SRS supplier integrations, aerial measurement connections, production boards, insurance claim workflows - only make sense if your work has that structure.

Jobber is a general field service dispatch platform built for businesses that run multiple same-day jobs. HVAC tune-ups, plumbing repairs, landscaping routes, cleaning appointments - work that books, completes, and closes in a single day, where the next job is already on the calendar before the tech leaves the driveway. The features that make it valuable - drag-and-drop scheduling, automated quote follow-ups, mobile payment collection, client hub - matter most when each day is a new set of stops.

If you are a roofer evaluating Jobber, or a service contractor evaluating AccuLynx, this comparison will save you time.

## AccuLynx vs Jobber: Quick Verdict by Trade Type

**Roofing and exterior contractors:** Stop looking at Jobber. Jobber does not have supplier integrations for ABC Supply, SRS, or QXO. It does not support aerial measurements. It does not have production boards for multi-stage project tracking. It does not handle insurance claim workflows. AccuLynx has all of these by design - not as add-ons, but as the core architecture. Jobber looks cheaper on paper, but the missing capabilities mean you would be buying a tool that was not designed for how exterior projects run.

**Service contractors - HVAC, plumbing, electrical, landscaping, cleaning:** Stop looking at AccuLynx. AccuLynx's $250/mo starting price, roofing-specific workflows, and quote-only higher tiers are expensive overkill for businesses that book, visit, and close jobs in a single day. Jobber's scheduling, dispatch, quoting, and invoicing tools are built exactly for that work. The published pricing and 14-day trial mean you can evaluate the product this afternoon without talking to anyone.

**The edge case:** A mixed-trade company that does both roofing projects and service calls will face an uncomfortable choice. You can pick one platform and accept that part of the business will work around the software. Or you can run two systems, which adds cost and data sync work. Neither AccuLynx nor Jobber covers both job models well. That is not a flaw in either product - it is a reflection of how differently project-based exterior work and same-day service calls operate.

## When This Software Makes Sense

Both tools earn their cost when the office is managing too many things in text threads and spreadsheets - but the triggers are different.

AccuLynx makes sense when the problem is production visibility. The sales team is closing jobs that the production crew does not know about. Material orders go out by text with no record in the job file. Supplement approvals get tracked in a spreadsheet that nobody updates consistently. Insurance claim photos and documents are scattered across phones, email threads, and a filing cabinet. A roofing company doing $2M+ with multiple crews typically hits this wall within a few seasons - and that is when AccuLynx's supplier integrations and production boards start paying for themselves.

Jobber makes sense when the problem is daily dispatch chaos. Jobs are falling through the cracks between booking and invoicing. Someone forgets to follow up on an unsigned estimate that has been sitting for 12 days. A tech shows up without knowing the job scope because the notes are in a group chat. An invoice sits unpaid for two weeks because nobody remembered to send a reminder. Jobber's scheduling, quote follow-ups, automated reminders, and payment collection close those gaps without a long implementation.

## When It Does Not Make Sense Yet

Skip both if you are running fewer than 10 jobs a month and the admin load has not become genuinely painful. Software adds overhead before it adds value - you need to configure service items, customer records, payment settings, and accounting integrations before you see a return. If a spreadsheet and QuickBooks are not breaking down yet, keep them.

AccuLynx specifically: if your work is single-day service calls with no supplier ordering or insurance claims, AccuLynx's $250/mo starting price and roofing-specific workflows are not justified by what you would actually use. The learning curve and training period make the cost even harder to defend.

Jobber specifically: if your work is multi-week roofing or exterior projects, Jobber's service-call architecture will frustrate you. You can force it to work, but you will be building workarounds the whole time - and you will still lack the supplier integrations and production tracking that make software useful for that kind of work.

## Key Features Compared

### Scheduling and Dispatch

Jobber has the clear advantage for service-call businesses. The drag-and-drop calendar shows every tech's day in one view, lets you move jobs between time slots, and keeps customers updated with automated notifications. For a plumbing company booking 8-12 calls a day, Jobber's calendar view keeps the dispatch board manageable. The mobile app lets techs see their schedule, job details, and route without calling the office.

AccuLynx does not have drag-and-drop same-day dispatch. Its scheduling is built around the production board - tracking roofing jobs through stages like material delivery, crew assignment, and permit status. That is the right tool for managing a roofing pipeline, not routing five techs across a metro area.

**Winner:** Jobber for service calls. AccuLynx for project-stage tracking.

### Supplier Integrations and Material Ordering

This is AccuLynx's strongest differentiator. Direct connections to ABC Supply, SRS Distribution, and QXO let you create material orders directly from estimates, submit them electronically, track delivery status, and reconcile invoices without switching systems. For roofing companies with frequent supplier orders, this can remove real admin friction.

Jobber has no supplier integrations. There is no connection to ABC Supply, SRS, Beacon, or any building materials distributor. If material ordering is part of your daily workflow, this alone decides the comparison.

**Winner:** AccuLynx. Jobber does not compete here.

### Estimating and Measurements

Both handle estimates, but for different job shapes. AccuLynx integrates directly with EagleView, SkyMeasure, Hover, Geospan, and GAF QuickMeasure for aerial measurements that automatically populate estimate fields with roof area, pitch, ridges, valleys, and waste factors. The estimate builder is built around roofing-specific line items.

Jobber's quote builder handles line items, optional add-ons, and digital approval with e-signature. It works well for residential service businesses - HVAC tune-ups with repair options, landscaping cleanup with optional add-ons. But there are no aerial measurement integrations and no roofing-specific estimate fields.

**Winner:** AccuLynx for roofing estimates. Jobber for general service quoting.

### Mobile App

Jobber draws consistent praise for mobile usability. The iOS and Android apps let techs view job details, clock in and out, upload photos, add notes, fill out forms, and collect payment. Offline support is limited - timers, forms, notes, and attachments can be saved locally and synced later - but for most service routes, it works well enough.

AccuLynx's mobile app is consistently described as slower, more limited, and less intuitive than the desktop version. For roofing businesses where sales reps and project managers spend much of the day in driveways, on roofs, or between jobs, that gap matters.

**Winner:** Jobber. AccuLynx's mobile experience is its most-consistent user complaint.

### Reporting and Job Costing

AccuLynx offers stronger operational reporting for roofing - production reports, crew productivity, material cost tracking, and supplement management. But deeper profitability analysis is locked behind Pro and Elite tiers.

Jobber's reporting covers the basics well: job revenue, outstanding invoices, and tech performance on Core and Connect. Grow adds job costing (profit margin, labor, line items, expenses). But even on Grow, the reporting is functional, not finance-grade.

**Winner:** AccuLynx for roofing-specific operational reporting. Jobber for accessible, tier-based reporting.

**AccuLynx** has integrations that roofing contractors need and cannot find elsewhere: ABC Supply, SRS Distribution, and QXO for supplier ordering and pricing. EagleView, SkyMeasure, Hover, Geospan, and GAF QuickMeasure for aerial measurements. QuickBooks Online. Spotio and HubSpot for lead management.

**Jobber** has a 70+ partner App Marketplace. Key connections: QuickBooks Online, Xero, Gusto, Mailchimp, Zapier, Square, Authorize.net, Stripe, PayPal Express. ## Limitations and Red Flags

**AccuLynx:**
- No published self-serve trial - you cannot test it before calling sales
- Mobile app is noticeably weaker than the desktop product
- Pro and Elite pricing is opaque - you cannot budget without a sales call
- Features have been moving behind paid add-ons, frustrating long-term users
- Learning curve is steeper than lighter CRMs

**Jobber:**
- No offline mobile mode except for internal notes - problematic for rural or low-signal areas
- QuickBooks sync is one-way (Jobber to QuickBooks, not back)
- No inventory management module
- Tier jumps from Core to Connect to Grow are steep - the Connect Team at $199/mo is a big jump from Core at $49/mo
- No supplier integrations or roofing-specific features for exterior work
