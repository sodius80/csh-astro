# Tradify vs Workiz: Published Pricing vs Dispatch Depth

Tradify vs Workiz looks like a field service software comparison, but the products are built for different buying problems. The decision comes down to what matters more: seeing the price before you start, or getting a deeper dispatch and communication stack behind a quote.

Tradify is built for trade job management. It covers the full job lifecycle from inquiry to payment in a single platform, and it publishes its prices: Lite at $47 per user per month, Pro at $51, Plus at $61. No strategy session required to see the numbers. Workiz is built for dispatch-first field service management. It connects scheduling, dispatch, client CRM, phone tools, AI answering, route visibility, and job communication into one operating system. But you'll need a quote to see the base plan price.

Those are different value propositions. A contractor who wants to know the monthly cost before committing doesn't need a dispatch board with phone integration. A service team losing leads to missed calls and messy dispatch doesn't care about the cheapest per-user rate. See our <a href="/tradify-review/">Tradify review</a> and <a href="/workiz-review/">Workiz review</a> for the full product breakdowns.

## The buying split

### Tradify is the transparent-pricing pick

Tradify's main advantage is that you can see the price before you talk to anyone. The official pricing page lists Lite at $47 per user per month, Pro at $51, and Plus at $61. A 14-day free trial includes all features and requires no credit card. There's no "request a demo" wall between you and the numbers.

For a five-person team, Tradify Pro costs $255 per month: five users at $51 each. No base fee, no included-user cap, no communication add-on to price separately. The math is simple, and it stays simple as the team grows. A 10-person team on Plus pays $610 per month. A solo operator on Lite pays $47. You can budget from the pricing page. For more pricing context, see our <a href="/jobber-vs-workiz/">Jobber vs Workiz</a> comparison, which breaks down how a tier-based pricing model compares to per-user billing.

Xero and QuickBooks Online sync are available on every plan, including Lite at $47. That matters for trade businesses that already run their books on Xero and don't want to pay for a higher tier just to connect accounting. Tradify also includes free one-on-one training and phone support, not a paid add-on.

Where Tradify falls short: it's not a dispatch platform, not a phone system, and not a CRM with call logging. Scheduling is functional but doesn't have the dispatch board depth, route optimization, or technician location tracking that Workiz offers. There's no built-in phone system, no AI answering, no call insights. The integration list is shorter than competitors. Reporting, purchase orders, and bulk invoicing are locked to the Plus tier at $61 per user per month. AI tools (SmartRead and SmartWrite) are Plus-only. For a deeper look at the product, see our <a href="/tradify-review/">Tradify review</a>.

### Workiz is the dispatch-depth pick

Workiz should be evaluated around the office day. The official Workiz feature page puts scheduling and dispatch, invoicing, estimates, inventory, online booking, mobile app, reporting, client CRM, automations, lead source integrations, service plans, equipment tracking, location tracking, commissions, phone, SMS, AI answering, call insights, call masking, and ad tracking into the same operating system.

That changes the buying question. Workiz isn't mainly a cheaper Tradify. It's a different tool for a different problem. If jobs start as phone calls, turn into estimates, move through same-day route changes, require customer texts, and need the office to see every job conversation in one place, Workiz is closer to that problem.

Pricing needs more caution. Workiz's official pricing page currently shows Standard, Pro, and Ultimate as request-pricing plans. It publishes extra-member fees: Standard extra members cost $55 per month each on annual payment, and Pro extra members cost $65 per month each on annual payment. The page also says card readers are sold separately and subscription prices exclude applicable sales tax. Workiz Communication, phone, SMS, and AI tools can add separate spend, so the written quote matters more than the headline card.

The shorter trial gives you less time to test the dispatch workflow, phone tools, and mobile app with real jobs. For more context on Workiz's positioning against lighter tools, see our <a href="/jobber-vs-workiz/">Jobber vs Workiz</a> comparison and the <a href="/clockshark-vs-workiz/">ClockShark vs Workiz</a> comparison.

## Pricing reality

### If you want the clearest starting price

Tradify wins. The official page lists real per-user prices across three tiers plus a Custom option for large teams. There's no quote required for Lite, Pro, or Plus. The Instant Website add-on is $12 per month on any plan, and that's the only add-on to factor in.

The caution is that the lowest tier can be too limited. If a contractor needs reporting, purchase orders, bulk invoicing, job tasks, or AI tools, the real starting point isn't $47. It's Plus at $61 per user per month. But even at $61, the price is published and the math is straightforward.

### If you want the clearest all-in Workiz number

Don't stop at the Workiz pricing page. Use it as the starting checklist for a quote. Ask for the base Standard, Pro, or Ultimate price, annual versus monthly billing, included members, extra-member charges, Workiz Communication, phone and SMS usage, AI answering, call insights, card readers, taxes, onboarding, and any Ultimate-only modules.

The current verified source packet and live page agree on the important public pieces: Workiz is request-priced for core plans, it publishes extra-member rates for Standard and Pro, it separates card readers and taxes, and communication, phone, and AI tools need to be priced separately. That doesn't make Workiz a bad buy. It means you shouldn't compare Tradify's published per-user price to an incomplete Workiz quote.

### Small crew math

For a solo operator or two-person trade business, Tradify usually gets the first look. Lite at $47 per user per month gives a clear starting point, and the 14-day trial lets the owner test quoting, scheduling, invoicing, payments, and accounting sync without a sales process. A two-person team on Pro pays $102 per month total. That's easy to model.

Workiz can still fit a small crew if the phones are already the pain. A locksmith, garage door company, appliance repair team, or junk removal company may care more about call capture, same-day dispatch, and job communication than the lowest published entry price. But the request-pricing model means you won't know the base plan cost until you talk to sales. For a wider shortlist of scheduling-focused tools, see the <a href="/best-scheduling-software-contractors/">best scheduling software for contractors</a> guide.

### Growing crew math

As the team grows, the pricing models diverge. Tradify's per-user billing scales linearly: a 10-person team on Pro pays $510 per month, and on Plus pays $610. There's no base fee, no included-user cap, and no communication add-on to factor in. The cost is predictable.

Workiz becomes more interesting when the office is managing a busier job flow. Standard and Pro include the first 5 users, then charge $55 or $65 per extra member on annual payment. But the base plan price is quoted, so the total cost depends on the base rate plus extra members plus communication tools. If five, eight, or twelve people need access, ask Workiz to quote the same user count and communication stack you expect to run daily. ## Where Tradify wins

### Published pricing and longer trial

Tradify is easier to evaluate before a sales call. The pricing page lists Lite, Pro, Plus, and Custom with per-user dollar amounts, and the 14-day no-card trial gives more time to test the workflow than Workiz's 7-day trial. That matters for small contractors who don't want to spend a week in demos just to decide whether the software is in budget.

The longer trial also reduces adoption risk. Two extra days of testing can make a difference when you're trying to run quoting, scheduling, invoicing, payments, and accounting sync with real jobs before committing.

### Per-user pricing simplicity

Tradify's per-user model means there's no base fee, no included-user cap, and no separate add-on pricing for communication tools. Every user costs the same per-month rate based on the tier. A five-person team on Pro pays $255. A three-person team on Lite pays $141. The math works the same at any team size.

Workiz's model includes the first 5 users on Standard and Pro, then charges per extra member. But the base plan price is hidden behind a quote, and Workiz Communication adds a separate cost layer. The pricing is more complex to model without a written quote.

### Xero sync on all plans

Tradify includes Xero and QuickBooks Online sync on every plan, including Lite at $47. Workiz lists QuickBooks Online on Standard and higher but does not offer Xero. For trade businesses running Xero as their primary accounting platform, Tradify is the clear pick. The accounting sync covers customers, invoices, and payments.

### Free one-on-one training

Tradify includes free one-on-one training and phone support on all plans. That's not a paid add-on or a premium-tier feature. For small trade businesses that want help getting set up without paying for onboarding, this is a practical advantage.

## Where Workiz wins

### Calls, dispatch, and communication depth

Workiz is more compelling when the office day starts with the phone. The product's official feature set connects client CRM, scheduling, dispatch, location tracking, estimates, payments, calls, SMS, AI answering, call insights, and ad and source tracking. If the problem is that a customer calls, a dispatcher writes half the details down, a technician gets a stale note, and the office loses the thread, Workiz is closer to that problem.

Tradify has email tracking and appointment reminders, but it doesn't have a built-in phone system, call logging, SMS, or AI answering. The communication depth gap is real if calls and dispatch handoffs drive your business. ### Trade fit for reactive services

Workiz is especially relevant for reactive service businesses: locksmiths, garage door companies, appliance repair teams, junk removal, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and other trades where jobs often start as inbound calls and move fast. In those trades, the dispatch board is not just a calendar. It's revenue control.

Tradify is built for electricians, plumbers, gas engineers, HVAC techs, and property maintenance services. It handles the core trade workflow well, but it's not designed for the same call-driven, same-day dispatch intensity that Workiz targets. ### Built-in phone and AI tools

Workiz Communication is sold separately, but it's part of the reason to consider Workiz. The pricing page and feature pages describe an integrated phone system, calls, emails, SMS, call recording, call tags, call masking, ad tracking, AI answering, call insights, and smart messaging.

Don't compare Workiz without that layer if that's why you want Workiz. Ask for the exact package and usage assumptions. If the communication stack replaces separate phone, texting, call recording, and lead-tracking tools, it may be worth the added cost. ## Evaluation plan

### Test Tradify like a trade job shop

Use the 14-day trial around a real trade workflow. Create an inquiry, build a quote, convert it to a job, schedule the work, track time, invoice the customer, collect payment, and sync to Xero or QuickBooks Online. If reporting, purchase orders, bulk invoicing, or AI tools matter, test those on the Plus tier directly instead of assuming Lite or Pro will cover them.

Then price the real plan. A solo operator can evaluate Lite. A small trade team should usually model Pro. A team that needs reporting, purchase orders, or AI tools should model Plus at $61 per user.

### Test Workiz like a dispatch-heavy shop

For Workiz, don't evaluate only the calendar. Run the demo or trial around the phone-to-job workflow. Create an inbound lead, attach call or message history, assign the job, move the route, send a customer update, collect payment, and check what the dispatcher and technician each see after the job changes.

Then ask for a written quote that includes the base plan, included members, extra members, Workiz Communication, phone and SMS usage, AI answering, call insights, card readers, taxes, onboarding, and any Ultimate modules. The quote should make it clear what's included, what is usage-based, and what happens when the team grows.

## Final verdict

<p><strong>My call:</strong> Tradify is the better default for small trade businesses that want published per-user pricing, a 14-day no-card trial, and straightforward job management. Workiz is the better fit for call-driven teams where dispatch and communication are the operating bottleneck.</p>

Choose Tradify if you want transparent pricing, Xero sync on every plan, per-user billing simplicity, and a clean job workflow from inquiry to payment. It's the safer first system for small trade teams that don't need a dispatch board or phone system.

If you're still unsure, ask one question: what costs you more every week, not knowing the software price or not having dispatch depth? If transparent pricing wins, start with Tradify. If calls and dispatch are the bottleneck, start with Workiz.

## FAQ

### Is Tradify or Workiz better for small contractors?

Tradify is usually better for small trade contractors because it publishes per-user pricing, offers a 14-day no-card trial, and keeps the workflow focused on quoting, scheduling, invoicing, payments, and accounting sync. Workiz can still fit a small contractor if calls and dispatch are already the main pain, but you'll need a quote to see the price.

### Which is cheaper, Tradify or Workiz?

Tradify is easier to price because it publishes Lite at $47 per user per month, Pro at $51, and Plus at $61. Workiz requires a quote for Standard, Pro, and Ultimate. Yes. Workiz offers a 7-day free trial that does not require a credit card. The trial is shorter than Tradify's 14-day trial.

Yes. Tradify integrates with QuickBooks Online and Xero on all plans, including Lite at $47 per user per month.

### Does Workiz integrate with QuickBooks?

Yes. Workiz lists QuickBooks Online on Standard and higher plans. Workiz does not offer Xero integration.

Workiz is the stronger fit for dispatch-heavy teams because calls, messages, routing, technician location, job status, client CRM, phone tools, and communication history are central to the product.
