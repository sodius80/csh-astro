# ArboStar vs Handoff for Contractors

ArboStar vs Handoff isn't a typical feature-checklist comparison. These products serve different trades, different workflow stages, and different contractor profiles. Picking between them is less about which has more features and more about which one matches the work your company actually does.

ArboStar is a tree-care operating system. Its official pricing page centers on arborist CRM, live job maps, equipment tracking, scheduling, estimating, invoicing, and field documentation built specifically for tree service businesses. Handoff is an AI-native estimating and proposal platform. Its official pricing page centers on AI estimating, AI proposals, voice transcription to estimate, scopes of work, material lists, site walkthroughs, and takeoffs for residential contractors.

That distinction matters. A tree care company managing multiple crews, chipper trucks, and bucket trucks will get more from ArboStar than from a proposal builder. A remodeling contractor who sends slow, inconsistent proposals will get more from Handoff than from a field service platform designed for arborist workflows.

## The real decision

### ArboStar is a tree-care operations decision

ArboStar makes the most sense when the company runs tree removals, pruning jobs, stump grinding, and commercial arborist work across multiple sites with different crew compositions. The arborist CRM records client tree history, the live job maps help route chipper trucks and bucket trucks, the equipment tracking manages chainsaws, chippers, and rigging gear, and the field documentation captures safety forms and job photos specific to tree work.

Its official pricing page describes an all-in-one platform for tree service businesses with CRM, scheduling, invoicing, customer management, reporting, GPS tracking, live job maps, fleet management, payment processing, estimating, and a mobile app for iOS and Android. Pricing starts from $250 per month with a custom quote based on company needs, licenses, configuration, and platform usage. Multi-license discounts are available, annual subscriptions include two months free, and a free demo is available.

That's a very different buying reason from Handoff. ArboStar isn't the tool to buy because estimates take too long. It's the tool to buy when tree-specific workflows, equipment visibility, and arborist CRM depth justify a custom quote and longer setup.

### Handoff is a sales-side estimating decision

Handoff makes the most sense when the estimate is where the business leaks money. That can mean slow turnaround, inconsistent line items, weak proposal presentation, or a process where the owner is the only person who knows how to turn a site visit into a quote.

The official Handoff pricing page frames the product as an AI teammate for estimates, proposals, scopes of work, material lists, renderings, site walkthroughs, voice transcription, and takeoffs. The Flex plan includes 50 AI usage credits per month, one custom pricing catalog, AI-generated proposals, voice transcription to estimate, and two users. Pro adds unlimited AI credits, unlimited custom pricing catalogs, site walkthroughs, client financing, and five users. Scale adds AI takeoffs from residential plans up to 5,000 square feet.

Atlas verifies Handoff pricing from the official pricing page. Flex is $119 per month when billed annually, or $149 month-to-month. Pro is $239 per month when billed annually, or $299 month-to-month. Scale is $719 per month when billed annually, or $899 month-to-month. Enterprise is custom quote. The Flex plan includes a 7-day free trial.

That's a different buyer. Handoff is for the residential contractor who needs to turn a walkthrough, voice note, or photo set into a client-ready estimate and proposal faster. It's not for the tree care company that needs dispatch boards and equipment assignment.

## Where ArboStar wins

### Tree-specific CRM and service history

ArboStar's arborist CRM is built for tree-care customer management. Contacts, leads, quotes, job history, communication records, and service history are all tree-work aware. For arborist companies managing hundreds of client properties, that depth matters in a way that a general estimating tool can't match.

### Live job maps and equipment tracking

Tree work depends on more than an address and appointment time. The right crew, truck, chipper, equipment, access notes, and weather affect the day. ArboStar's map and GPS positioning give managers a clearer view of where work is happening and which resources are tied up. Equipment tracking covers maintenance schedules, fuel costs, and GPS-linked assets.

### Field documentation for arborist work

Safety forms, hazard notes, job photos, and crew instructions are central to tree work in a way they aren't for residential estimating. ArboStar's mobile workflow supports that documentation attached to specific tree jobs.

## Where Handoff wins

### Faster estimate-to-proposal workflow

Handoff wins when the company needs to turn a walkthrough, voice note, photo set, or scope into a client-ready estimate and proposal faster. That's valuable for residential contractors where speed and presentation affect whether the homeowner keeps shopping.

### Better fit for residential proposal selling

ArboStar is built for tree care operations. Handoff is aimed at the sales moment. Its official homepage and pricing language focus on residential construction, proposals, AI estimates, client portals, project admin, and protecting margins through consistent pricing and markups. That matters for remodelers, roofers, and home improvement contractors who sell trust as much as labor.

### Published pricing and free trial

Handoff Flex starts at $119 per month on annual billing with a 7-day free trial. ArboStar requires a demo and custom quote. An estimate-first residential contractor gets a clearer self-serve budget from Handoff and can test the workflow before committing.

## Where each product falls short

ArboStar is the wrong fit for a residential remodeler, roofing contractor, or home improvement company that needs faster estimates and proposals. The tree-specific features, custom pricing, and implementation effort are wasted on operations that just need better bid turnaround.

Handoff is the wrong fit for a multi-crew tree care company that needs live job maps, equipment tracking, arborist CRM records, and field documentation. It's an estimating and proposal tool, not a tree-care operating system.

Both products can be wrong for a general service contractor that needs full field service management (dispatch, invoices, payments, QuickBooks sync) across a team. ## Evaluation plan

### Test ArboStar with a real tree crew day

Don't test ArboStar as a generic estimate tool. Ask the demo team to walk through a real day with multiple crews, a chipper, a bucket truck, and a late emergency call. Confirm that the live map, equipment tracking, and arborist CRM actually reduce office-to-field friction. Get the full quoted price including license count, integrations, implementation, and support.

The pass-fail question: does ArboStar make tree crew dispatch, equipment assignment, and field documentation easier to manage than the current process?

### Test Handoff with real estimate pressure

Don't test Handoff with a fake bathroom remodel if your real jobs are roofing replacements, additions, or repair scopes. Use a recent job where proposal speed or clarity mattered. Build the estimate, generate the proposal, review the scope, check the material list, and compare the final output against what you'd normally send.

The pass-fail question: does Handoff produce a client-ready estimate faster without lowering scope quality or margin control?

## FAQ

### Is ArboStar or Handoff better for contractors?

ArboStar is better for tree care contractors whose biggest problem is managing crews, equipment, and field documentation. Handoff is better for residential contractors whose biggest problem is estimating and proposal speed. They serve different trades and workflow stages.

### Is Handoff cheaper than ArboStar?

Handoff has the lower visible entry price. Atlas verifies Handoff Flex at $119 per month when billed annually with a 7-day free trial. ArboStar starts from $250 per month with a custom quote after a demo. For a residential contractor, Handoff is the lower-risk starting point. For a tree care company, ArboStar's custom quote may include tree-specific tools that justify the higher cost.

### Does ArboStar work for residential remodeling?

ArboStar is built for tree care and arborist operations. A residential remodeler would find it overbuilt and paying for tree-specific features they won't use. Handoff is the better fit for residential estimating and proposals.

### Can Handoff handle tree service scheduling?

Handoff is an AI estimating and proposal tool. It isn't a dispatch-first field service platform. A tree care company that needs crew scheduling, live job maps, and equipment tracking should look at ArboStar, Jobber, or a general field service management tool instead.

### Which product is better for a larger team?

ArboStar is designed for multi-crew tree care operations and can scale with additional licenses. Handoff includes two users on Flex and five users on Pro, with Scale and Enterprise for larger needs. A larger tree care team should choose ArboStar. A larger residential estimating team should choose Handoff Pro or Scale.

### Should a tree care company test Handoff?

Only if the bottleneck is proposal speed rather than field operations. A tree care company that needs arborist CRM, equipment tracking, and live job maps should test ArboStar. A tree care company that also does residential work and needs faster proposals could add Handoff as a complementary tool, but the two products aren't direct substitutes.
