# ActiveCampaign vs AWeber for Contractor Email Marketing

ActiveCampaign and AWeber are both email marketing platforms built for small businesses, but they approach the job from opposite directions. ActiveCampaign is built around a visual automation builder with CRM-lite features layered in. AWeber is built around templates, deliverability, and a straightforward editor that gets a campaign out the door without a learning curve.

For a contractor choosing between them, the question is not "which has more features?" It is "what kind of email marketing do you actually need?" If the answer is automated follow-up sequences, lead scoring, and multi-step nurture workflows that respond to contact behavior, ActiveCampaign is the natural fit. If the answer is a monthly newsletter, a welcome sequence, and seasonal promotions sent from a tool that works the first time you open it, AWeber is the stronger starting point.

My call: choose <a href="/go/activecampaign-official-site/" rel="nofollow">ActiveCampaign</a> if automation depth is the priority. Choose <a href="/go/aweber-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">AWeber</a> if simplicity, a no-cost starting tier, and template-driven design matter more. Both appear in the <a href="/best-email-marketing-software-contractors/">best email marketing software for contractors roundup</a> for a broader view of the category.

## Quick verdict

Pick ActiveCampaign if automation is the job. Atlas-verified pricing lists Starter at $15/mo for 1,000 contacts when billed annually, or $19/mo on monthly billing. Plus is $49/mo annual ($59/mo monthly), Pro is $79/mo annual ($99/mo monthly), and Enterprise is $145/mo annual ($179/mo monthly), all at 1,000 contacts. Pricing scales by contact count. A 14-day free trial is available; there is no ongoing no-cost tier. The official product page positions ActiveCampaign around email marketing, automation, CRM, lead scoring, and segmentation.

Pick AWeber if simplicity and a no-cost starting tier are the priority. Atlas-verified pricing lists a no-cost tier for up to 500 subscribers and 3,000 emails per month. Paid plans start at Lite $12.50/mo billed annually for 0-500 subscribers, or $15/mo monthly. Plus is $20/mo billed annually for 0-500 subscribers, or $30/mo monthly. Done For You is $20/mo annual plus a $79 setup fee on current promo (standard $599). AWeber Unlimited is $899/mo for high-volume senders. A 14-day free trial gives access to premium features without a credit card.

Skip both if your primary need is dispatch, scheduling, or field service management. Neither ActiveCampaign nor AWeber handles technician routing, work orders, estimates, or invoicing. Those needs belong with [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), or another option from the [best field service software](/best-field-service-software/) guide.

## When ActiveCampaign makes more sense

ActiveCampaign makes more sense when the contractor has an automation problem, not a template problem. The contact list exists. The website form exists. But nobody is following up with leads automatically. The seasonal promotion goes out once and then nobody remembers to send the second email to non-openers. The welcome sequence is supposed to be five emails but only the first one ever goes out.

That is where ActiveCampaign's visual automation builder earns its keep. A contractor running a roofing company could build: someone fills out the "get a free estimate" form, they get a welcome email immediately, a case study email two days later, a pricing overview email if they opened the first one, a different follow-up if they did not, and a check-in at 30 days. All automatic. All conditional. No developer needed.

ActiveCampaign also brings CRM-lite features to the table. Contact records show list membership, tags, engagement data, deal stage, and custom fields. Lead scoring assigns points for opens, clicks, site visits, and form submissions. When a contact crosses a threshold, they can trigger an automation or move to a different list. For a contractor getting 50 website leads a month, that separates people who fill out a form and disappear from people who fill out a form and read every follow-up email.

The pricing starts lower than most combined email-CRM platforms. At $15/mo for 1,000 contacts on Starter, ActiveCampaign is accessible for contractors who are just starting to build their list and want room to grow. See the [ActiveCampaign vs Keap comparison](/activecampaign-vs-keap/) for how it stacks up against a CRM-first platform.

## When AWeber makes more sense

AWeber makes more sense when the contractor wants email marketing that works without a learning curve. The contact list exists. The newsletter content exists. But nobody is sending regularly because the tool is too complicated, or there is no budget for a paid platform yet.

AWeber has been in the email marketing space since 1998, and that longevity shows in three areas: template library, deliverability, and support. The official product page emphasizes drag-and-drop email building, hundreds of professional templates, landing pages, sign-up forms, automation workflows, and ecommerce tools. For a contractor who wants to pick a template, drop in their logo and content, and hit send, AWeber is the faster path to a professional-looking email.

The no-cost tier is the headline differentiator. A contractor with fewer than 500 subscribers can use AWeber at no cost, sending up to 3,000 emails per month. The no-cost tier includes one list, one landing page, one workflow automation, and basic features. That is a lower-risk starting point than a paid trial that converts to a monthly bill after 14 days. The official AWeber docs page confirms the no-cost option remains available.

The support model matters for contractors who are not technically inclined. AWeber offers phone, chat, and email support on every plan, including the no-cost tier. If something breaks at 9pm the night before a seasonal promotion goes out, a contractor can call and talk to a person. ActiveCampaign offers chat and ticket support on all plans, but phone support is not a standard inclusion.

## Email automation depth

This is where ActiveCampaign pulls ahead decisively. The visual automation builder is the reason most contractors choose ActiveCampaign over a simpler tool. The drag-and-drop canvas lets you connect conditions, actions, and triggers into a sequence without code. A contractor could build a conditional sequence: if a contact opens the quote follow-up email but does not click the "book a call" link, the next email takes a different path from someone who did click. That level of conditional logic runs on Starter and becomes more powerful on Plus and Professional.

ActiveCampaign supports split conditions. Different paths based on whether a contact opened, clicked, or did nothing. That is useful for contractors running seasonal promotions who want to send a second offer to non-openers and a thank-you plus upsell to openers. The automation builder also handles wait conditions, time delays, goal triggers, and site tracking events.

AWeber's automation builder covers the basics: welcome sequences, tag-based follow-ups, and basic abandoned-form workflows. The official product page lists behavioral automation, which triggers emails based on subscriber actions like opens and clicks. But the automation is not as deep as ActiveCampaign for conditional branching, multi-step journeys, or lead scoring tied to engagement. For a contractor who wants to build a simple three-email welcome sequence, AWeber handles it. For a contractor who wants to build a 10-email nurture sequence with branching logic based on behavior, ActiveCampaign is the better tool.

Decision signal: choose ActiveCampaign when complex, conditional email sequences with multiple branches are the primary use case. Choose AWeber when standard welcome sequences and periodic tag-based follow-ups are enough.

## Templates and email design

AWeber wins the template comparison. The official product page emphasizes hundreds of professional, mobile-responsive email templates. A contractor can pick a template, customize it with their branding and content, and send a professional-looking email without hiring a designer. The drag-and-drop builder is straightforward, and the template library covers newsletters, promotions, welcome emails, and seasonal campaigns.

ActiveCampaign's email editor is capable but has fewer free templates than simpler tools. The drag-and-drop builder takes more time to produce a finished email, and the template library is smaller than AWeber's. For contractors who send designed newsletters and want a large library to choose from, this is a friction point.

The practical difference: a contractor using AWeber can send a polished newsletter in 15 minutes from a template. A contractor using ActiveCampaign might spend 30 minutes on the same newsletter because the editor requires more manual layout work. The trade-off is that ActiveCampaign's automation depth compensates for the design overhead.

Both platforms let you send content that changes based on who is receiving it, AI subject line assistance, split testing, and branded click-tracking. AWeber also supports AMP emails and RSS-to-email. ActiveCampaign supports predictive sending and AI-powered send-time optimization on higher tiers.

Decision signal: choose AWeber when template-driven design and a large pre-built library save time. Choose ActiveCampaign when automation depth matters more than template selection.

## CRM and contact management

ActiveCampaign wins the CRM comparison. Contact records include list membership, tags, engagement data, deal tracking, and custom fields. The deal pipeline view shows stages, deal values, and owner assignments. Lead scoring assigns points based on email engagement, page visits, form submissions, and other behaviors. When a contact reaches a threshold, they can trigger an automation or move to a different list.

For a contractor who wants to know which contacts received which emails, who is in the active buying window, and which leads are hot based on engagement, ActiveCampaign's CRM-lite is sufficient. It does not replace a dedicated CRM like [Pipedrive](/activecampaign-vs-pipedrive/) or a field service platform, but it keeps contact history alongside campaign data in one place.

AWeber has no built-in CRM, no contact scoring, no deal pipeline, and no sales activity tracking. It is an email marketing platform, not a customer management tool. Subscribers can be tagged and segmented, but there are no deal stages, pipeline views, or activity ownership features. If the contractor needs CRM discipline, AWeber cannot provide it. See the [AWeber vs Pipedrive comparison](/aweber-vs-pipedrive/) for the email-vs-CRM trade-off.

Decision signal: choose ActiveCampaign when CRM-lite features like contact history, deal tracking, and lead scoring matter. Choose AWeber when email marketing is the only job and CRM is handled elsewhere.

## No-cost tier vs trial

AWeber’s no-cost tier is a genuine differentiator. The official AWeber docs page confirms a no-cost plan for up to 500 subscribers and 3,000 emails per month, with one list, one landing page, one workflow automation, and basic features. The no-cost tier is not time-limited. A contractor with 200 past customers can use AWeber free indefinitely and only pay when the list grows past 500.

The no-cost tier does have limitations. Downgrading from a paid plan restricts custom segments, advanced reporting, split testing, behavioral automation, and AWeber branding removal. But for a contractor who is new to email marketing and wants to test without a credit card, the no-cost tier is the lower-risk starting point.

ActiveCampaign has no permanent no-cost tier. The 14-day free trial gives full access to features, but after 14 days the contractor needs to pick a paid tier. The official pricing page offers a 30-day money-back guarantee, so a contractor who cancels within 30 days gets a full refund. But there is no indefinite no-cost option.

Decision signal: choose AWeber when a no-cost tier for a small list matters. Choose ActiveCampaign when 14 days is enough to evaluate and the contractor is ready to commit to a paid plan.

## Deliverability and support

AWeber has been sending email since 1998, and that experience translates to consistently strong deliverability. The sending infrastructure has decades of reputation built up across ISPs, which means AWeber emails are more likely to reach the inbox thanbounce. For contractors who worry about whether their newsletter actually gets delivered, AWeber's track record is reassuring.

ActiveCampaign also maintains strong deliverability, with AI-powered send-time optimization, BotSense, and reputation monitoring included in most plans. The difference is that AWeber's deliverability story is backed by 25+ years of sending history, while ActiveCampaign's is backed by AI tooling and automation.

On support, AWeber offers phone, chat, and email support on all plans, including the no-cost tier. ActiveCampaign offers chat and ticket support on all plans, with customer enablement workshops, video tutorials, and a knowledge base. Phone support is not a standard inclusion on ActiveCampaign's lower tiers. For a contractor who values the ability to call someone when something breaks, AWeber has the edge.

## Integrations and ecosystem

ActiveCampaign lists 900+ integrations through its native marketplace plus Zapier access on all plans. The ecosystem covers email, CRM, e-commerce, and productivity tools. For contractors using QuickBooks Online, Stripe, or Zapier as their operational backbone, ActiveCampaign's integration library covers those connections and more.

AWeber lists hundreds of integrations including WordPress, Shopify, WooCommerce, PayPal, Facebook, and Zapier. The integration library is smaller than ActiveCampaign's, but it covers the tools most contractors use for email marketing workflows. The WordPress plugin is particularly useful for contractors running their website on WordPress and wanting to add sign-up forms directly.

Decision signal: if a specific integration is the deciding factor, check each platform's marketplace first. For common contractor tools, both are well covered. ActiveCampaign has the broader ecosystem, but AWeber covers the essentials.

## Where ActiveCampaign falls short

ActiveCampaign's biggest limitation for contractors is complexity. The automation builder is powerful, but it takes time to learn. A contractor who just wants to send a monthly newsletter will find ActiveCampaign overkill. The Starter plan limits automation actions to 5 per sequence and restricts segmentation. To get the full automation depth, the contractor needs Plus or higher, which costs more.

Pricing scales with contact count. A contractor whose list grows from 1,000 to 5,000 contacts on Plus sees a significant monthly increase. The platform is designed to grow with you, but the price grows too.

The email editor has fewer free templates than AWeber or MailerLite. Contractors who send complex, designed newsletters may find this a friction point. For a simpler alternative, compare [ActiveCampaign vs MailerLite](/activecampaign-vs-mailerlite/). For a lower-cost option, see [ActiveCampaign vs Moosend](/activecampaign-vs-moosend/).

## Where AWeber falls short

AWeber's biggest limitation is automation depth. The automation builder covers welcome sequences, tag-based follow-ups, and basic workflows. It is not as powerful as ActiveCampaign for conditional branching, lead scoring, and complex multi-step journeys. Contractors who need sophisticated automation logic should look at [ActiveCampaign vs GetResponse](/activecampaign-vs-getresponse/) for another automation-heavy alternative.

The second limitation is CRM depth. There is no built-in CRM, no contact scoring, no deal pipeline, and no sales activity tracking. If the company needs deal owners, stage movement, and next activities, AWeber cannot provide it. Compare [AWeber vs Keap](/aweber-vs-keap/) for a CRM-led alternative.

The third limitation is the interface. AWeber has been around since 1998, and the interface feels dated compared to newer email marketing tools. The functionality is solid, but the user experience is not as polished as MailerLite or Moosend. See the [AWeber vs Moosend comparison](/aweber-vs-moosend/) for a lower-cost modern alternative.

## Evaluation plan

### Test ActiveCampaign with one real automation sequence

Do not judge ActiveCampaign from a blank account. Pick one real contractor workflow and build it:

1. Start a 14-day free trial.
2. Import a small contact segment.
3. Build a quote follow-up sequence with conditional logic: welcome email, wait, check if opened, send different follow-up based on behavior.
4. Set up a tag-based segment for "service-area-north" contacts.
5. Configure lead scoring for email opens and link clicks.
6. Send a test campaign to the segment and review the automation report.

If the conditional logic works and the automation builder feels intuitive, ActiveCampaign is doing its job. If it feels too complex for the contractor's actual needs, AWeber or MailerLite is the better fit.

### Test AWeber with one real campaign

For AWeber, build the test around a real newsletter:

1. Sign up for the no-cost plan with fewer than 500 subscribers.
2. Choose a template from the library and customize it.
3. Build a short welcome sequence for new sign-ups.
4. Create a sign-up form for the website.
5. Send a test campaign to the segment.
6. Review open rates, click rates, and deliverability.

If the templates look professional and the deliverability is strong, AWeber is a solid low-cost marketing layer. If the office needs automated follow-up sequences with branching logic, switch to ActiveCampaign.

## Bottom line for contractors

ActiveCampaign and AWeber answer different questions.

ActiveCampaign answers: "How do I automate email follow-ups, segment my list, and score leads without hiring a marketing person?" The visual automation builder is the best in this category, and the $15/mo entry price for 1,000 contacts makes it accessible at any list size. The CRM-lite features keep contact history alongside campaign data, which is useful for contractors who want to see which leads are engaging.

AWeber answers: "How do I send professional emails to my customers without a learning curve?" The no-cost tier for 500 subscribers, the template library, and the reliable deliverability make it the lower-risk starting point for contractors new to email marketing. The phone support on every plan matters for contractors who are not technically inclined.

Start with <a href="/go/activecampaign-official-site/" rel="nofollow">ActiveCampaign</a> if automation depth and CRM-lite features are the priority. Start with <a href="/go/aweber-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">AWeber</a> if a no-cost starting tier, templates, and simplicity matter more.
