# Freshsales vs Zoho CRM: Free CRM for Contractors

Freshsales and Zoho CRM are two of the few CRM platforms that offer a genuinely free edition. Both give you 3 users at no cost. Both publish transparent pricing for paid tiers. Both include AI features, visual pipelines, and mobile apps. But they solve the free CRM problem from different directions.

Freshsales is a sales CRM built around ease of adoption. The official Freshworks pricing page describes a free tier with unlimited users, built-in phone, AI lead scoring, email templates, and a visual pipeline. The pitch is simple: sign up, start tracking leads, and call prospects from inside the CRM without paying.

Zoho CRM is a configurable CRM platform built around customization depth. The official Zoho CRM pricing page lists a free edition for 3 users with contact management, workflow automation, custom email templates, and mobile apps. The pitch is different: start free, then shape the CRM around your business data model as you grow into paid tiers and the broader Zoho ecosystem.

That difference matters. A contractor who wants a working CRM by Friday should pick Freshsales. A contractor who wants to model custom fields for roof age, insurance claim status, and subcontractor relationships should pick Zoho CRM.

My call: most contractors who need a free CRM should start with <a href="/go/freshsales-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Freshsales</a> for its built-in phone, unlimited users on the free tier, and faster adoption. Choose <a href="/go/zoho-crm-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Zoho CRM</a> if you need deeper customization, already use Zoho apps, or plan to build a more structured customer system over time.

## Quick verdict

Pick Freshsales if the office needs a CRM that works immediately. The official Freshsales pricing page confirms a free plan with built-in phone (200 minutes per month), AI-based lead scoring through Freddy AI, email templates, visual Kanban pipeline views, and live chat. For a contractor, that covers the essential sales workflow: capture leads, score them, call the hot ones, track them through a pipeline, and follow up.

Pick Zoho CRM if flexibility matters more than speed. The official Zoho CRM feature page emphasizes sales force automation, custom modules, layouts, workflows, portals, business intelligence, territory management, and a connected ecosystem of Zoho apps. That depth is valuable when a contractor wants custom fields for property type, service line, subcontractor status, job source, bid stage, or account history.

Skip both if you are looking for field service operations. Neither product replaces [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), [ServiceTitan](/servicetitan-review/), or a broader [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) platform. Freshsales and Zoho CRM sit before the job is won, or beside the operations system, not in place of scheduling, dispatch, routing, inventory, or job costing. ## When Freshsales makes more sense

Freshsales makes more sense when the contractor's biggest leak is sales follow-up and the team needs a CRM working this week, not next month. The official Freshsales product page highlights Freddy AI lead scoring, Kanban views, built-in phone and email, contextual collaboration, and deal insights. That is the right shape for a contractor office that needs to know what happened with every open lead this week.

The free tier is unusually generous. According to the official Freshworks pricing page, the free plan includes:

- Up to 3 users at no cost
- Built-in phone with 200 minutes per month
- AI-based lead scoring through Freddy AI
- Visual Kanban views for contacts, accounts, and deals
- Email templates and live chat
- Contact and account management

For a contractor with a few people handling sales, that covers the essential workflow without a per-user cap that forces upgrades as the team grows. The built-in phone is particularly useful. A roofing company can call leads directly from the CRM, log the call, and move the deal forward without switching to a separate dialer.

The interface is modern and clean, with a lower learning curve than most free CRMs, which is why it ranked as the top free CRM in our [best free CRM for contractors](/best-free-crm-for-contractors/) roundup. A small team can create a pipeline like this:

- New lead
- Contacted
- Estimate scheduled
- Quote sent
- Follow-up due
- Won
- Lost

Every open deal can have an owner, an expected value, a stage, notes, email history, and a next activity. Managers can scan the board and see where work is stuck. Estimators can open the CRM and know which quote needs a call today.

## When Zoho CRM makes more sense

Zoho CRM makes more sense when the contractor wants to shape the CRM around the business, not the other way around. The official Zoho CRM feature page lists sales force automation, forecasting, pipeline management, territory management, workflows, layouts, lead management, portals, business intelligence, and deep customization.

For contractors, that flexibility can matter. A remodeler may want different modules for homeowners, designers, architects, subcontractors, and vendors. A roofing company may want custom fields for roof age, insurance claim status, property manager, inspection date, supplement status, or material preference. A commercial service company may need account hierarchies and deal records that Freshsales handles simply but Zoho can model more deeply.

Zoho CRM's free edition also includes real functionality. The official pricing page lists contact management, follow-up reminders, workflow automation, up to 10 custom email templates, tasks, meetings, and calls tracking, data import and export, standard reports, 5,000 API calls per day, and mobile apps. That is a legitimate starter CRM for 3 users.

The tradeoff is setup effort. More fields, modules, layouts, and workflows mean more decisions. Zoho CRM can fit a contractor's process more tightly, but only if someone owns configuration and cleanup. The steeper learning curve is real, and the 5,000 record limit on the free tier fills faster than expected when contacts, leads, deals, and accounts are all combined.

Zoho CRM also has the ecosystem advantage. A contractor may pair Zoho CRM with Zoho Books for accounting, Zoho Projects for job management, Zoho Invoice for billing, Zoho Desk for support, or Zoho Inventory for stock management. That ecosystem can be powerful, but the buyer should not assume the CRM edition alone replaces an operations platform.

Freshsales also advertises a 21-day free trial with no credit card required.

Atlas-verified Zoho CRM pricing lists a Free edition for up to 3 users. Flexible monthly billing is Standard $20, Professional $35, Enterprise $50, and Ultimate $65 per user per month. The official Zoho pricing page also shows a 15-day free trial and free edition.

## Free tier comparison: what you actually get

The free tier is the heart of this comparison. Both products offer one, but the shape of what you get is different.

### Freshsales free tier

The official Freshworks pricing page describes the free plan as including Kanban views, email templates, built-in phone, and live chat. The free plan covers up to 3 users with unlimited contacts. Key features include:

- Visual Kanban pipeline for contacts, accounts, and deals
- Built-in phone with 200 minutes per month
- AI-based lead scoring through Freddy AI
- Email templates and live chat
- Contact and account management
- Mobile app access

For a contractor, that is a working sales CRM. A roofing company with 3 people in the office can track leads, call them from inside the CRM, score which ones are hottest, and move them through a pipeline without paying anything.

The limitation is integrations. The free tier includes only 1 third-party integration. Workflow automation and email cadences are not available on the free tier, so follow-up structure has to be manual. The free plan is pure sales CRM with no project management, job tracking, invoicing, or field service features. For contractors who need accounting alongside CRM, see [CRM with QuickBooks integration](/crm-with-quickbooks-integration/).

### Zoho CRM free tier

The official Zoho CRM pricing page describes the free edition as a complete CRM starter kit for home businesses. Key features include:

- Contact management with leads, accounts, contacts, and deals
- Follow-up reminders
- Basic workflow automation
- Up to 10 custom email templates
- Tasks, meetings, and calls tracking
- Data import and export (up to 10 times free)
- Standard reports
- 5,000 API calls per day
- Mobile apps

For a contractor, that is a functional CRM foundation. The advantage is customization. Custom modules and fields are available on the free tier, so a contractor can model property types, job scopes, subcontractor relationships, or service lines without writing code. The mobile app lets field workers update deal status from job sites.

The limitations are storage and scale. The free edition includes 1 GB storage per user, a 300 email per day limit per user, and a 5,000 record limit across all modules combined. Those limits fill faster than expected with photos, contracts, and property documents. No AI assistant or workflow automation beyond basic rules is available on the free tier. Zia, Zoho's AI, requires an upgrade to Enterprise.

## AI features: Freddy AI vs Zia

Both platforms market AI capabilities, but the approach is different.

Freshsales includes Freddy AI on the free tier. The official product page describes Freddy as a 24/7 assistant that highlights the best leads and deals, offers insights, and writes emails. On the free plan, Freddy AI provides lead scoring that helps prioritize which leads to call first. On Pro and higher tiers, Freddy adds deal insights, sales email assistance (rephrase, expand, enhance text), and contact scoring. For contractors comparing Freshsales's AI approach with other sales CRMs, the [Pipedrive review](/pipedrive-review/) covers a platform with activity-based selling instead.

Zoho CRM includes Zia AI on paid tiers, starting at Enterprise. The official Zoho CRM page describes Zia as a multi-agent platform combining AI and human experience, with ChatGPT integration for tasks like email rewriting and anomaly detection. Zia provides predictions, recommendations, and insights on the Enterprise and Ultimate plans. The free and Standard tiers do not include Zia.

For a contractor on a free tier, this is a real difference. Freshsales gives you AI lead scoring at no cost. Zoho CRM does not. If lead prioritization matters and the budget is zero, Freshsales has the edge.

For a contractor on paid tiers, the comparison is more nuanced. Zia on Enterprise ($40/user/month annually) offers predictions, recommendations, and anomaly detection. Freddy AI on Pro ($39/user/month annually) offers deal insights, contact scoring, and email assistance. The feature sets are comparable in scope, and the choice depends more on the broader platform fit than on AI alone.

## Customization and workflows

Zoho CRM wins the customization comparison. Its official feature page emphasizes custom modules, layouts, fields, workflows, portals, Blueprint, journey orchestration, and business intelligence. For a contractor with unusual sales stages or many job types, that depth is useful.

Examples where Zoho CRM may fit better:

- a commercial contractor tracking accounts, contacts, properties, bid invites, subcontractors, and long-cycle opportunities
- a roofing company separating retail, insurance, repair, and property manager pipelines
- a remodeler tracking design consultation, scope development, selections, proposal, and preconstruction handoff
- a service company already using Zoho Books, Zoho Projects, Zoho Invoice, Zoho Desk, or Zoho Inventory

Freshsales is customizable too, but it is strongest when the process stays closer to a normal sales pipeline. Custom fields, pipeline stages, activities, reports, automations, email sync, and integrations are available on paid tiers. The free plan includes basic workflows. Growth adds custom fields and basic workflows. Pro adds multiple sales pipelines, auto-assignment rules, territory management, and sales sequences. Enterprise adds custom modules and field-level permissions.

If the goal is to make lead follow-up visible, Freshsales's simpler approach may be more than enough. If the goal is to model a complex business process inside the CRM, Zoho CRM has more room.

Decision signal: choose Freshsales when the team needs a sales board by this week. Choose Zoho CRM when the company needs a CRM model that can be shaped around multiple sales processes and custom data structures.

## Email, phone, and communication

Freshsales has the stronger built-in communication story for contractors on the free tier. The official pricing page lists built-in phone, email, and live chat on the free plan. For a contractor who wants to call leads from inside the CRM without buying a separate phone system, that is a genuine advantage.

Zoho CRM supports email integration with Gmail, Outlook, and Zoho Mail, but built-in calling requires at least the Standard tier at $14/user/month. The free edition includes basic email and task tracking but no phone system.

On paid tiers, both platforms expand their communication capabilities. Freshsales Pro adds Bring Your Own Carrier (BYOC) for phone, sales sequences, and advanced workflows. Zoho CRM Standard adds built-in calling, mass emails, and cadences for multichannel lead follow-up. Zoho CRM Professional adds email intelligence with AI insights and sentiment analysis.

For contractors, the practical question is: does the team need phone calling inside the CRM? If yes and the budget is zero, Freshsales is the only option. If the team already uses a separate phone system and just needs email + pipeline tracking, both platforms work.

## Contractor operations fit

Neither product should be sold internally as dispatch software.

Freshsales can help with sales leads, pipeline stages, contact history, email sync, reminders, reporting, and sales follow-up. It will not manage technician routes, job schedules, field production, parts, job costing, service agreements, or crew dispatch.

Zoho CRM can model more customer data and connect to more Zoho apps, but Zoho CRM by itself is still a CRM. A contractor may pair it with Zoho Books, Zoho Projects, Zoho Invoice, Zoho Desk, Zoho FSM, or outside field service software. That ecosystem can be powerful, but the buyer should not assume the CRM edition alone replaces an operations platform.

The handoff question matters more than the brand:

1. Where does a lead enter?
2. Who qualifies the lead?
3. Where is the estimate scheduled?
4. Where is the quote created?
5. When does a won opportunity become a job?
6. Which system owns scheduling, dispatch, job costing, and invoicing?
7. Which system owns the customer record after the job closes?

If most answers are sales-stage answers, either CRM can help. If most answers are dispatch or production answers, start with operations software and use CRM as a supporting tool. ## Where Freshsales falls short

Freshsales's biggest limitation is ecosystem depth. It can integrate with many tools through the Freshworks Marketplace, but Freshsales is still primarily a sales CRM. If a contractor wants one vendor to cover CRM, accounting, projects, invoices, inventory, support, and field service, Zoho has the broader family of products.

The second limitation is customization ceiling on the free tier. The free plan includes basic workflows but no custom fields, no multiple sales pipelines, and no auto-assignment rules. Those features require a paid Freshsales Growth plan or higher. Zoho CRM's free edition includes basic workflow automation and custom modules.

The third limitation is integration access. The free tier includes only 1 third-party integration. A contractor connecting QuickBooks, Zapier, or a field service tool will need to upgrade to a paid Freshsales plan. Zoho CRM's free edition includes Zapier and API access for custom connections.

## Where Zoho CRM falls short

Zoho CRM's biggest limitation is setup effort. The product can do a lot, but that also means more fields, settings, modules, workflow choices, permissions, and cleanup. A contractor that needs sales discipline now may get stuck designing the perfect CRM instead of calling leads.

The second limitation is the free tier's practical limits. The 5,000 record cap fills faster than expected with contacts, leads, deals, and accounts combined. The 1 GB storage per user fills quickly with photos, contracts, and property documents. The 300 email per day limit can be restrictive for outreach campaigns. Freshsales's free tier has no stated record limit or email sending limit.

The third limitation is communication on the free tier. Zoho CRM Free does not include built-in phone calling. A contractor who wants to call leads from inside the CRM needs at least Standard at $14/user/month. Freshsales includes 200 minutes per month on its free plan.

The fourth limitation is operations expectation. Zoho's ecosystem is broad, but Zoho CRM alone is not the same as a field service platform. If the buyer wants scheduling, routing, crew coordination, job costing, and field production, the CRM needs either connected Zoho apps or a separate operations tool.

## Bottom line for contractors

Freshsales and Zoho CRM are both legitimate free CRM choices, but the better pick depends on what the contractor is actually trying to fix.

Choose <a href="/go/freshsales-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Freshsales</a> if the business needs a free CRM with built-in phone, AI lead scoring, unlimited users, and a modern interface the team can adopt this week. It is the better default for contractors whose main problem is lead and quote follow-up, especially when calling leads from inside the CRM matters.

Choose <a href="/go/zoho-crm-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Zoho CRM</a> if the business needs a free CRM with deeper customization, custom modules, workflows, and a path into the broader Zoho ecosystem. It is the better pick for contractors who are ready to design a more structured customer system instead of only tracking sales stages, and for companies that already use other Zoho apps.

If the job is dispatch, scheduling, route management, field production, or job costing, neither should be the first purchase. Start with [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) and add CRM only where the sales process needs extra structure.
