# Jobber vs Service Fusion (2026): Compared

The difference between Jobber and Service Fusion is not really about feature lists. Both products cover the same base - scheduling, dispatching, invoicing, QuickBooks integration, and a mobile app for field techs. The real difference is the pricing model, and that model determines which team size each product fits best. For a broader look at the market, see our <a href="/best-field-service-software-alternatives-jobber-housecall-pro-servicetitan/">field service software alternatives guide</a>.

Jobber uses per-user tiers that start affordable and scale up as you add techs. Service Fusion uses flat-rate unlimited-user pricing that costs more at the entry point but does not change as your crew grows. See my full <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber review</a> and <a href="/service-fusion-review/">Service Fusion review</a> for deeper dives on each product.

That makes Jobber the better fit for small residential crews and Service Fusion the better value once you cross roughly 8 to 10 users.

## Pricing and contract reality

This is where the two products diverge most clearly. Do not compare headline prices. Compare at your real headcount.

<p><strong>Jobber</strong> lists four plans: Core at $29/month annual billing ($49 month-to-month) for one user, Connect at $99/month annual for one user or $149/month annual for the five-user team version, Grow at $149/month annual for one user or $299/month annual for the ten-user team version, and Plus at $529/month annual with fifteen users included. Additional users are listed at $29/month each. The 14-day free trial provides access to Grow features during evaluation with no credit card required.</p>

<p>Watch the feature gates carefully. Core handles basic scheduling, quotes, and invoices. Connect adds the client hub, automations, QuickBooks Online integration, time tracking, and expenses. Grow adds job costing, advanced quote customization, two-way SMS, and custom workflow automations. Plus adds Marketing Suite, Receptionist, Pipeline, premium support, and API guidance. The workflows that make Jobber feel useful are not all in Core.</p>

<p><strong>Service Fusion</strong> lists Starter at $208/month annual billing ($245 monthly), Plus at $325/month annual ($382 monthly), and Pro at $533/month annual ($627 monthly). Every plan includes unlimited users. There is no free trial. Annual billing is charged as a single payment at sign-up and saves roughly 15% compared to month-to-month pricing.</p>

<p>The unlimited-user model means a 15-person crew pays the same as a 5-person crew for the same plan. Service Fusion is more expensive than Jobber at the entry point, but becomes cheaper per user once your team crosses roughly 8 to 10 users. A 12-person HVAC company comparing Jobber's Grow at $299/month for ten users against Service Fusion Plus at $325/month with no user limit is a real comparison worth doing.</p>

## Mobile app: the real differentiator

For field service software, the mobile app matters more than the admin dashboard. The tech on site is the one who needs the tool to work.

Jobber has the stronger mobile story for most teams. Both the iOS and Android apps are well-reviewed overall. Technicians can view job details, clock in and out, upload photos, add notes, fill out forms, and collect payment. Offline support is limited to timers, forms, notes, and attachments with syncing after reconnection. The real test is whether your techs will actually use it. During the free trial, run at least one technician through a real day. Jobber usually wins by keeping that simple.

Service Fusion's mobile experience is split. The iOS app is functional and generally reliable. The Android app is a genuine risk. Scores around 2.8 out of 5 on Google Play with recurring complaints about crashes and connectivity make it hard to recommend for Android-majority crews. If your techs are on iPhones, Service Fusion's mobile app is adequate. If they carry Androids, test it carefully before signing an annual contract.

Service Fusion also has no offline mode. Technicians must have an active connection to access job details, update status, or capture payments. Jobber's limited offline support is not full offline capability, but it covers more ground than nothing.

## When Jobber makes sense

Jobber is the safer fit for small crews and buyers who want a lower entry price, cleaner trial access, and a polished mobile app on both platforms. A solo plumber or a two-truck landscaping company can start on Core at $29/month, test the workflow with the 14-day free trial, and move to Connect or Grow as the team grows. The low entry price and free trial make it the lower-risk starting point for any small residential or service crew.

The client hub is a real advantage. Customers can request work, approve quotes, review past and upcoming appointments, see assigned team details, and pay invoices or deposits. That cuts down on the "can you resend that invoice?" calls that eat office time.

Online booking lets homeowners schedule service without calling. Automated confirmations, reminders, and quote follow-ups reduce no-shows. For a company where customer communication and streamlined office workflow matter, these tools add value that does not show up on a plan card comparison.

## Where Jobber falls short

Jobber's costs scale with headcount. A ten-person crew on Grow pays $299/month annual. Add another five techs and the math pushes toward Plus at $529/month. The tier jumps from Core to Connect and Connect to Grow are steep enough that teams should map their required features before picking a plan.

Jobber does not offer inventory management, supplier integrations, or QuickBooks Desktop sync. If any of those are must-haves, Service Fusion or a more specialized platform may fit better. Jobber also lacks the operational depth needed for multi-crew dispatch-heavy workflows that Service Fusion handles naturally.

The QuickBooks integration is one-way Jobber to QuickBooks Online. It works for most small shops, but companies that need two-way sync or Desktop compatibility should confirm the workflow during the trial.

## When Service Fusion makes sense

Service Fusion is designed for teams large enough to benefit from unlimited-user pricing. A 15-person HVAC company with dispatchers, technicians, office staff, and managers fits this model naturally. One flat monthly fee covers everyone. That predictability matters when you are budgeting for a growing field team.

The dispatch board is another strong reason to consider Service Fusion. The drag-and-drop scheduling, real-time technician status, and route optimization are consistently praised. For a company where dispatch is the main operational bottleneck - scheduling multiple techs across dozens of stops per day - Service Fusion's dispatch workflow stands out.

QuickBooks Desktop support is a genuine differentiator. Service Fusion supports two-way sync with both QuickBooks Online and QuickBooks Desktop. For home service contractors who still run QuickBooks Desktop, that compatibility alone can be the deciding factor. Double-entry elimination between the dispatch system and accounting software saves real office time.

Plus and Pro tiers add features that growing teams need: job photos, inventory management, job costing, custom documents, eSignatures, customer portal, progress billing, and recurring invoicing. The unlimited-user model means all of these come at a flat price regardless of how many people need access.

## Where Service Fusion falls short

The Android mobile app is the most frequently cited risk. If your crew is Android-majority, test the mobile workflow extensively before signing an annual contract.

There is no free trial. Service Fusion's purchase path is demo-led, meaning you commit to a sales conversation before you touch the software. For buyers who prefer to test independently before talking to sales, that friction is significant. The annual billing commitment adds another layer of risk - you pay the full year up front, which makes a wrong fit expensive.

Service Fusion also has no offline mode. Technicians must have an active internet connection to access job details, update status, or capture payments. For crews working in basements, rural areas, or buildings with poor reception, that can be a real operational problem.

GPS fleet tracking and ServiceCall.ai are listed as add-ons even on higher-tier plans. The base subscription price is not the full picture. Get a written add-on cost sheet before comparing against Jobber's all-in pricing.

## QuickBooks integration comparison

Both products integrate with QuickBooks, but the depth differs meaningfully.

Jobber connects with QuickBooks Online at the Connect tier and above. The sync is one-way from Jobber to QuickBooks - jobs, invoices, payments, and customer records flow one direction. QuickBooks Desktop is not supported.

Service Fusion supports two-way sync with both QuickBooks Online and QuickBooks Desktop on every plan. For contractors who run their accounting in QuickBooks Desktop and want estimates, invoices, and payments to sync without manual reconciliation, Service Fusion has a clear advantage.

## Final verdict

Choose Jobber if you run a small to mid-size residential service business with 1-10 technicians. The transparent pricing, polished mobile app on both platforms, free trial, client hub, and online booking make it the safer starting point for most small crews. The per-user pricing means you pay more as you grow, but for a company with 2-8 techs, the math usually favors Jobber.

Choose Service Fusion if you run a larger dispatch-heavy crew with 10+ technicians and want flat-rate unlimited-user pricing. The entry price is higher and the lack of a free trial adds friction, but for teams large enough to benefit from the unlimited model, Service Fusion delivers better per-user value. The QuickBooks Desktop support is a genuine advantage if that is your accounting platform.

The crossover point is roughly 8-10 users. Below that, start with Jobber. Above that, Service Fusion is worth a serious demo.
