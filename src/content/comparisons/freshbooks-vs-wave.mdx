# FreshBooks vs Wave for Contractors

FreshBooks and Wave both target solo contractors and small service businesses, but they approach the same buyer from opposite directions. FreshBooks charges from day one and gives you a polished invoicing, time-tracking, and payment-collection workflow. Wave starts with no monthly subscription and covers basic bookkeeping records, invoices, and bills. For a deeper look at each tool, read the [FreshBooks review](/freshbooks-review/) and the [Wave comparisons](/quickbooks-online-vs-wave/) on this site.

The decision between them is not really about features. It is about whether the contractor's biggest problem is getting paid faster or keeping costs at zero while the business is still simple. A solo electrician who sends five invoices a month and needs clean records for tax time may be perfectly served by Wave Starter. A landscaper who bills hourly, tracks time in the field, and chases late payments every month will get more value from FreshBooks.

## The buying split

### FreshBooks is an invoicing-first tool for service businesses

FreshBooks is built for contractors who bill by time or project and need the invoice-to-payment workflow to be fast. The official pricing page lists Lite at $23/mo for 5 billable clients, Plus at $43/mo for 50 clients, Premium at $70/mo for unlimited clients, and Select at custom quote. A current promotional offer of 90% off for 6 months is advertised, but standard rates are what matter for long-term budgeting. Add-ons include Team Members at $11/user/mo, Advanced Payments at $20/mo, and FreshBooks Payroll at $40/mo plus $6/user/mo. A 30-day trial with no credit card and a 30-day money-back guarantee are advertised.

The core workflow is: track time, log expenses, create and send professional invoices, accept payments, and automate late-payment reminders. That makes FreshBooks the stronger choice when invoicing speed drives cash flow. The automated payment reminders, scheduled late fees, checkout links, and instant payouts are designed to shorten the gap between sending an invoice and getting paid. For a contractor whose biggest cash-flow problem is slow-paying clients, those features solve a real problem.

The limit is construction depth. FreshBooks does not do job costing, progress billing, AIA forms, inventory management, or construction-specific billing workflows. It has project profitability on Premium, but cannot track materials, labor, and subcontractor costs at the job level the way contractor accounting requires.

### Wave is a no-cost bookkeeping tool with optional paid upgrades

Wave is built for solo contractors and small businesses that need professional invoicing and basic bookkeeping without a monthly subscription. The official pricing page lists Starter with unlimited estimates, invoices, bills, and bookkeeping records at no monthly subscription cost. The payment processing rates on Starter are 2.9% + $0.60 per credit card transaction and 3.4% + $0.60 per Amex transaction.

Wave Pro costs $19/mo if billed monthly or $190/yr if billed annually. Pro adds auto-imported bank transactions via Plaid, auto-merge and categorize, unlimited digital receipts with OCR technology, automated late payment reminders, removal of Wave branding, and discounted payment rates. The Pro payment rate is 2.9% + $0 per credit card for the first 10 transactions per month, then standard rates apply.

Optional add-ons are available separately. Receipt capture can be added to Starter or Pro. Payroll is available as an add-on. Wave Advisors bookkeeping services are available for contractors who want a dedicated bookkeeper. These are modular purchases, not required for the core Starter or Pro plans.

That makes Wave the better choice when the business is still simple enough that no-cost software covers the job. A solo contractor who sends a few invoices a month, records basic bills, and needs organized numbers for tax time can start without a subscription and only upgrade to Pro when bank imports, receipts, or payment automation become worth paying for.

## Pricing reality

### If you are trying to avoid a software subscription

<p>Wave wins this category. Starter carries no monthly software subscription and still covers the basic paperwork a solo operator needs: estimates, invoices, bills, and bookkeeping records. If the business has a handful of jobs per month and no admin team, that can be enough. Wave also gives you a path to online payments, payroll, receipt capture, and bookkeeping services as add-ons rather than forcing a subscription immediately.</p>

<p>FreshBooks is not a no-cost tool. The 30-day trial is generous, but the standard product starts at $23/mo for Lite. If the only question is whether you can send invoices and keep basic books without paying a monthly subscription, Wave is the answer.</p>

### If you need invoicing automation

<p>FreshBooks includes automated payment reminders, scheduled late fees, and online checkout links on all plans, starting at $23/mo for Lite. For a contractor whose clients pay late, those features can pay for the subscription by collecting payments faster.</p>

<p>Wave only includes automated late payment reminders on Pro at $19/mo. Starter requires manual follow-up on overdue invoices. If payment reminders are the feature that saves the most time, the cost comparison is closer than it looks: FreshBooks Lite at $23/mo includes them, while Wave Starter does not.</p>

### If time tracking matters

<p>FreshBooks has a built-in timer that feeds directly into invoices. Start a timer from the mobile app, categorize it to a client or project, and add those hours to an invoice. For contractors who bill by the hour, that workflow is the core reason to choose FreshBooks over any no-cost alternative.</p>

<p>Wave does not have a comparable built-in time tracking workflow. If billing by time is central to the business, the Starter plan will not solve that problem, and the Pro upgrade at $19/mo still does not add a FreshBooks-level time-to-invoice workflow.</p>

### If payroll is part of the decision

<p>FreshBooks sells payroll as an add-on starting at $40/mo plus $6/user/mo. Wave sells payroll as an add-on starting from $25/mo to $40/mo depending on the plan.</p>

<p>If payroll is the primary accounting problem, compare the full monthly number, not just the accounting subscription. Contractors who want accounting and payroll inside one vendor should also read the <a href="/quickbooks-online-review/">QuickBooks Online review</a>.</p>

## Where FreshBooks wins

### Invoicing speed and payment collection

FreshBooks is built around the invoice-to-payment workflow. Recurring invoices, automated payment reminders, online payment links, and client portal access are included without add-ons. For service businesses where cash flow depends on invoice collection, the automated reminders alone save meaningful time.

### Time tracking that feeds invoices

Start a timer from the mobile app, categorize it to a client or project, and add those hours to an invoice in two taps. For contractors who bill by the hour, this is the workflow FreshBooks nails better than any no-cost alternative. Wave does not offer a comparable time-to-invoice path.

### Mobile app for field workflows

The FreshBooks mobile app handles the core workflows: start a timer, snap a receipt, send an invoice, check payment status. For contractors who are not sitting at a desk, that matters. The app holds strong ratings on both iOS and Android.

### Evaluation risk

A 30-day trial with no credit card and a 30-day money-back guarantee reduce the risk of testing FreshBooks with real invoices and real clients. A contractor can send real invoices, connect a real bank account, and decide whether the client limit or feature set binds before paying anything.

## Where Wave wins

### No-cost starting point

Wave Starter carries no monthly software subscription and includes unlimited estimates, invoices, bills, and bookkeeping records. That is a real advantage for a new contractor who needs professional paperwork more than advanced automation. No per-client cap, no trial expiration, no credit card required to start.

### Lower buying risk for solo operators

A one-person trade business does not always need a paid accounting system on day one. Wave lets that owner get organized, learn how invoices and expenses flow, and upgrade later if bank imports, receipts, payroll, or extra support become worth paying for.

### Add-ons instead of a full paid stack

Wave's menu is modular. Payments, payroll, receipt capture, advisors, and bookkeeping support can be added when needed. That is useful when the contractor's needs are still forming and cash preservation matters. A contractor on Starter can add receipt capture without committing to Pro at $19/mo, keeping the monthly cost lower than FreshBooks Lite while still solving a specific problem.

### Simpler than FreshBooks for very light use

FreshBooks is not hard, but it is still a paid accounting platform with client limits, plan tiers, and add-on pricing to navigate. Wave is simpler if the owner just wants to send invoices, record expenses, and hand organized numbers to a tax preparer without evaluating which plan fits.

## Where each product is the wrong choice

**Do not pick FreshBooks** if the business only needs basic invoices and simple records and cash is tight. Paying $23/mo before the workflow requires it may be premature. Also skip FreshBooks if you have more than 5 clients and are on a tight budget, since the Lite cap forces you into Plus at $43/mo. The per-user fee of $11/user/mo adds up quickly for teams, and a 3-person shop on Plus with payroll would pay roughly $123/mo before processing fees.

**Do not pick Wave** if you need built-in time tracking, automated payment reminders on the no-cost tier, or a polished mobile workflow for field time capture. Wave Starter does not include bank feed automation, receipt capture, or payment automation. If the business has outgrown manual bookkeeping, Pro at $19/mo is the necessary upgrade, and at that point the price gap with FreshBooks narrows.

Both products are the wrong choice if the business needs job costing, project profitability, progress billing, AIA forms, inventory management, or construction-specific workflows. Neither FreshBooks nor Wave is construction accounting software. ## Evaluation plan

### Step 1: define the job the software has to do

List the jobs the software must handle this month. Include estimates, invoices, bills, receipt capture, bank feeds, time tracking, expense management, payment reminders, payroll, and reporting.

If the list is mostly basic invoices and bookkeeping records, start with Wave. If the list includes time tracking, payment automation, and mobile expense capture, start with FreshBooks.

### Step 2: price the real plan

<p>For FreshBooks, do not stop at Lite if you have more than 5 billable clients. Plus at $43/mo is the more realistic comparison once double-entry accounting and bank reconciliation matter. Add $11/user/mo for each team member and $40/mo plus $6/user/mo if payroll is needed.</p>

<p>For Wave, compare Starter if the goal is staying on Starter and Pro if bank imports, receipts, and extra users matter. If receipt capture is the only missing feature, Starter can add it without upgrading to Pro.</p>

### Step 3: run one real job through the workflow

In FreshBooks, start a timer, log an expense, create an estimate, convert it into an invoice, send it, set up a payment reminder, and accept a payment. If that flow feels fast and complete, the subscription is justified.

In Wave, create an estimate, convert it to an invoice, record a bill, add an expense, and run the basic reports you would send to a tax preparer. If that covers your month, Wave may be enough. If you keep wishing bank feeds, receipts, or payment reminders were easier, compare Wave Pro at $19/mo against FreshBooks Lite at $23/mo and see which tool's workflow fits better.

The test should make the decision obvious. If FreshBooks feels fast and the payment automation saves real time, the $23/mo is worth it. If Wave covers the month and the owner never felt the absence of time tracking or payment reminders, stay on Starter and save the money.

## FAQ

### Is FreshBooks or Wave better for contractors?

FreshBooks is better for solo contractors who bill by time, need professional invoicing with automated payment reminders, and want a mobile app for field time and expense capture. Wave is better for solo contractors who need basic invoices and bookkeeping records at no monthly subscription and whose needs are simple enough that no-cost software covers the job.

### Which is cheaper, FreshBooks or Wave?

Wave is cheaper at the entry level. Starter carries no monthly software subscription and includes unlimited estimates, invoices, bills, and bookkeeping records. Wave Pro is $19/mo or $190/yr. FreshBooks starts at $23/mo for Lite with 5 billable clients, and Plus at $43/mo is the more realistic tier for active contractors.

### Does Wave have a no-cost plan?

Yes. Wave Starter carries no monthly software subscription and includes unlimited estimates, invoices, bills, and bookkeeping records. Paid add-ons include online payment processing, receipt capture on Starter, payroll, and bookkeeping services.

### Does FreshBooks have a trial?

Yes. FreshBooks offers a 30-day trial with no credit card required. All plans are available during the trial, so you can test Lite, Plus, or Premium features before committing. There is also a 30-day money-back guarantee after purchase.

### Can FreshBooks or Wave handle job costing?

No. Neither FreshBooks nor Wave is built for construction job costing. FreshBooks has project profitability on Premium, but it cannot track materials, labor, and subcontractor costs at the job level. Wave can record income and expenses but has no project cost tracking. If job costing is a requirement, compare construction accounting tools instead.

### Does FreshBooks charge per user?

FreshBooks charges $11/user/mo for each additional team member on top of the base plan. The Select plan includes two team members.

Wave handles users differently. Pro at $19/mo is required for admin, editor, or viewer access. The two products take different approaches to multi-user access.

### Which should a solo contractor choose?

A solo contractor with simple invoices and basic bookkeeping needs should usually start with Wave Starter to keep costs at zero. A solo contractor who bills by time, sends regular invoices, and needs automated payment reminders should choose FreshBooks Lite at $23/mo. The test is whether invoicing speed and payment automation save enough time to justify the subscription.
