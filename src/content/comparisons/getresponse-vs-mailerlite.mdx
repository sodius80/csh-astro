# GetResponse vs MailerLite for Contractor Email Marketing

GetResponse and MailerLite both help contractors send better email campaigns, capture leads, and automate follow-up. The right choice depends on how much marketing platform the contractor actually needs around the email layer.

GetResponse is the deeper system. It gives a contractor room to build a marketing engine around email, landing pages, forms, popups, automation, webinars, and creator-style tools. MailerLite is the cleaner value play. It covers the core email marketing work most contractors need first: campaigns, automations, forms, landing pages, segmentation, and reporting at a lower price point with a real free plan.

## Quick verdict

Pick GetResponse when marketing depth is the job. The official GetResponse pricing page shows Starter at $19 per month on monthly billing or $15.58 per month when billed annually for a 1,000-contact list. Marketer is $59 per month monthly or $48.38 per month annually. Creator is $69 per month monthly or $56.58 per month annually. Enterprise is custom priced. The official pricing page describes a 14-day free trial account with premium-feature access that reverts to a limited free version after the trial ends.

Pick MailerLite when value and simplicity matter more. Atlas-verified MailerLite pricing from the official pricing page shows a free plan at $0 for up to 250 subscribers and 2,500 monthly emails. At the 500-subscriber level, Comfort is $12 per month or $10.80 per month when billed yearly. Power is $25 per month or $22.50 per month when billed yearly. Enterprise is custom for businesses with more than 200,000 subscribers. The official page lists a 14-day premium trial with no credit card required.

Skip both if the real problem is field operations. Neither product replaces [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), [ServiceTitan](/servicetitan-review/), or a broader [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) platform. GetResponse and MailerLite help before and after the sale. They do not schedule crews, dispatch technicians, track job costing, manage routes, or invoice field work by themselves.

## The fundamental difference: platform depth vs email focus

GetResponse is built around the idea that a contractor who can connect email, landing pages, automation funnels, webinars, and lead capture into one system will follow up better than one who uses a standalone newsletter tool. The marketing automation, visual funnel builder, webinar hosting, and creator-style monetization tools all serve that broader platform ambition.

MailerLite is built around the idea that email marketing should be affordable, clean, and easy to own. The drag-and-drop editor, subscriber management, forms, landing pages, and simple automation paths serve that focus. The platform does not try to host webinars, sell courses, or replace a marketing automation suite. It tries to send better emails at a price a small contractor can justify.

This is why feature count does not settle the comparison. GetResponse has webinar tools and creator monetization that MailerLite does not need. MailerLite has a simpler editor and a lower entry point that GetResponse cannot match. They are not competing for the same buyer. They are designed for different levels of marketing ambition.

## Free plan comparison

Both products offer a free path, but the details differ in ways that matter for a small contractor.

GetResponse offers a 14-day free trial with premium-feature access. After the trial ends, the account reverts to a limited free version. The trial is useful for testing the full platform, but the reversion to a limited free account means the contractor needs to choose a paid plan to keep using the broader toolkit. The trial limitations include 1,000 unique visits per month on landing pages, 10-person webinar capacity, and GetResponse branding on messages.

MailerLite offers a free plan for up to 250 subscribers and 2,500 monthly emails with no time limit. The free plan includes the drag-and-drop editor, rich text editor, A/B testing, 100-plus integrations, 1 website, 1 landing page, 3 active forms, and 3 active automations with 5 steps each. A 14-day premium trial is also available with no credit card required, but the free plan itself is permanent, not a trial that expires.

For a contractor just starting to build a list, MailerLite's free plan is genuinely usable. It lets the office send real campaigns to a small list without paying. GetResponse's trial is better for testing the full platform before committing, but the reversion to a limited free version makes it less practical as a long-term free tier.

## When GetResponse makes more sense

GetResponse makes more sense when the contractor wants a marketing system, not just a sending tool. A roofing company might want a landing page for storm inspections, a form for financing questions, a nurture sequence after an estimate request, and a webinar-style homeowner education event before peak season. GetResponse is built for that broader pattern.

Official GetResponse feature pages describe email marketing, autoresponders, marketing automation, landing pages, popups and forms, segmentation, website builder tools, webinars, transactional emails, and analytics. For a contractor, that maps to practical front-office work:

- send automated follow-up after a quote request
- build a landing page for one service, neighborhood, or seasonal offer
- run a homeowner education webinar or virtual event
- segment contacts by service line, source, location, or intent
- send newsletters, reminders, and reactivation campaigns
- connect campaigns to a broader funnel instead of one-off email blasts

The tradeoff is that GetResponse can be more platform than a small contractor needs. If the office only wants a clean monthly newsletter and a few estimate follow-up emails, the extra depth can turn into setup work. GetResponse is strongest when someone owns marketing and will actually use the broader toolkit.

## When MailerLite makes more sense

MailerLite makes more sense when the contractor wants the email layer to be affordable, clean, and focused. The office may already have leads in a spreadsheet, a CRM, a field service platform, or an old customer list. The missing piece is consistent communication.

Official MailerLite pages position the platform around email marketing, automation, landing pages, subscription forms, segmentation, reporting, and a drag-and-drop editor. For a contractor, that maps to practical marketing work:

- send newsletters to past customers
- create a form for a seasonal offer
- build an automated sequence after a quote request
- segment a list by service interest or location
- send maintenance reminders before busy season
- review campaign reporting before the next send

MailerLite is not the deeper platform if webinars, funnels, and creator-style tools are part of the plan. Its advantage is focus. The official pricing page puts the Comfort entry point below GetResponse's Starter price, and the free plan gives a small office more room to test without paying. ## Automation and nurture

GetResponse wins when automation depth is the deciding factor. The Starter plan includes 1 custom workflow, welcome emails, and autoresponders. Marketer adds unlimited workflows, contact tagging and scoring, event-based and purchase automation, and A/B testing. That makes it a stronger fit for a contractor that wants to build a more complete funnel.

A practical GetResponse workflow could look like this:

1. A landing page captures storm-damage inspection leads.
2. A form tags the lead by service area and project type.
3. An autoresponder sends the first response.
4. A nurture sequence sends education, reminders, and proof points.
5. A webinar or virtual event moves higher-intent homeowners closer to a consultation.
6. Campaign reporting shows which subject lines and pages are working.

MailerLite can handle practical nurture. The Comfort plan supports up to 50 active automations with 100 steps each. Power adds multiple automation triggers and unlimited automations. For many small contractors, that is enough. The difference is ceiling. MailerLite fits the office that wants email follow-up to get done. GetResponse fits the office that wants email, landing pages, and events to work together as a broader marketing system.

## Landing pages and forms

Both products support lead capture. GetResponse includes unlimited landing pages, signup forms, and popups on all plans. MailerLite includes 1 website and 1 landing page on the free plan, 10 on Comfort, and unlimited on Power.

For contractors, the tactical work is similar on day one:

- build a page for one offer
- capture a name, email, and project interest
- tag or segment the contact
- send a first response quickly
- follow up with useful reminders instead of generic blasts

The difference shows up after the first campaign. If the contractor wants to expand into webinars, more advanced funnel paths, and a larger marketing stack, GetResponse has more room. If the contractor wants a lighter campaign system that one person can own without extra platform weight, MailerLite is easier to justify.

## Webinars and education campaigns

Webinars are the clearest GetResponse advantage in this comparison. The Creator plan includes live and on-demand webinars for up to 100 attendees with 3 hours of recording storage. Enterprise supports up to 1,000 attendees with 20 hours of storage and breakout rooms. For most contractors, that does not mean running a software-company webinar. It means using online education in a practical local-business way.

Examples could include:

- a roofing company explaining storm-damage inspection steps
- a remodeler walking homeowners through kitchen project planning
- an HVAC company teaching maintenance timing before summer
- a solar or insulation contractor answering common financing questions
- a specialty trade using an online session before a seasonal campaign

MailerLite can promote those events through email, landing pages, and automation, but built-in webinar hosting is not available. If webinars are part of the contractor's marketing plan, GetResponse belongs at the top of the shortlist. If webinars are a maybe-someday idea, MailerLite's lower cost may matter more.

## Ease of use and editor

MailerLite has a clear advantage in simplicity. Its drag-and-drop editor is widely regarded as one of the cleanest in the email marketing category. The rich text editor and plain text editor give options for different comfort levels. The interface prioritizes getting a campaign sent over configuring a marketing system.

GetResponse has a steeper learning curve. The same breadth that makes it useful for automation, webinars, funnels, and creator tools means more setup work before the platform feels natural. The automation builder is visual and capable, but understanding the difference between autoresponders, automation workflows, and funnel paths takes time.

For a contractor who wants to send the first campaign within an hour of signing up, MailerLite is the faster path. For a contractor who has a marketing owner and is willing to invest in setup, GetResponse's depth pays off over time.

## Contractor operations fit

Neither product should be sold internally as contractor operations software.

GetResponse can help with campaigns, landing pages, forms, popups, webinars, autoresponders, segmentation, and marketing automation. It will not replace scheduling, estimating, dispatch, production tracking, job costing, payments, inventory, routes, or field mobile workflows.

MailerLite can help with campaigns, forms, landing pages, segmentation, automation, and reporting. It will not replace a CRM pipeline, service calendar, crew board, estimate workflow, job costing system, or invoice process by itself.

## Where GetResponse falls short

GetResponse's biggest drawback in this comparison is complexity. The same breadth that makes it useful for webinars, landing pages, automation, and creator-style marketing can make it too much tool for a contractor that only wants simple email follow-up.

The second drawback is price. GetResponse Starter at $15.58 per month annually or $19 monthly is not expensive by marketing-platform standards, but it sits above the entry point of a simpler email tool. The gap grows if the buyer moves to Marketer or Creator for deeper automation, ecommerce features, webinars, or creator tools.

The third drawback is the free plan. GetResponse's trial reverts to a limited free version after 14 days, which is less practical for a contractor who wants to keep using the tool without immediately paying. MailerLite's permanent free plan is more genuinely free.

## Where MailerLite falls short

MailerLite's biggest drawback is ceiling. It can handle the practical email marketing work many contractors need, but it is not the stronger choice if the marketing plan depends on webinars, broader funnel tooling, or a more layered automation program.

The second drawback is automation ceiling. While Comfort supports 50 automations and Power supports unlimited, the depth of each automation is simpler than what GetResponse offers. Contact scoring, event-based purchase triggers, and multi-path funnels are not part of the MailerLite toolkit.

The third drawback is that affordability can hide the real operating question. Even a low-cost tool fails if nobody writes the emails, cleans the list, checks reports, and builds the next campaign. MailerLite makes the software bill easier. It does not make the marketing owner optional.

## How to choose

Start with the missing object in the business.

If the missing object is a marketing system that connects email, landing pages, automation, webinars, and lead capture into one workflow, GetResponse is the better fit. The higher monthly cost is easier to justify when the platform replaces multiple disconnected tools and there is a marketing owner who will use the broader toolkit.

If the missing object is a contact list that receives consistent emails, MailerLite is the better fit. It gives contractors a lower-risk way to improve marketing communication without rebuilding the sales process or buying a heavier platform.

If both are missing, do not buy both on day one. If the CRM-versus-marketing-platform question is closer to the real decision, [GetResponse vs Keap](/getresponse-vs-keap/) or [GetResponse vs Pipedrive](/getresponse-vs-pipedrive/) may be the better comparison. Decide which gap is more expensive. A contractor losing active quotes because nobody owns the next call should fix CRM first. A contractor with a stable sales process but no reactivation or nurture should fix email first. GetResponse is the depth pick. MailerLite is the value pick.
