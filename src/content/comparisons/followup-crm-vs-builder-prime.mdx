# FollowUp CRM vs Builder Prime: Construction CRM Comparison

FollowUp CRM and Builder Prime are both real construction CRMs, but they serve genuinely different contractor audiences. FollowUp CRM is a pre-construction sales specialist built around bid tracking, pipeline management, and follow-up automation for general contractors and commercial subcontractors. Builder Prime is an all-in-one home improvement platform covering CRM, estimating, production management, GPS time tracking, payments, and reporting in one system. Which one fits depends on whether your business is bid-heavy pre-construction sales or home improvement production.

I evaluated both tools against their published features, pricing documentation, user reviews, and real contractor workflows. See my full <a href="/followup-crm-review/">FollowUp CRM review</a> and <a href="/builder-prime-review/">Builder Prime review</a> for more detail.

## When each CRM makes sense

### FollowUp CRM

A construction sales CRM earns its cost when bids start falling through the cracks. That usually shows up as one of three things: estimates sitting unsigned for three weeks because nobody followed up, a sales team that cannot answer "where does this bid stand?" without calling two people, or a pipeline that lives in someone's head and falls apart the moment that person is busy on a job.

FollowUp CRM addresses exactly that problem. It is built around bid tracking with custom pipeline stages, a digital bid calendar, and automated follow-up sequences. The tool is narrow by design - it covers the pre-construction sales cycle only, and it is the right call when that specific workflow is the bottleneck.

The typical FollowUp CRM user is a general contractor or commercial subcontractor with 5-15 people in estimating and sales, managing 20-60 active bids across multiple GCs and clients. The accounting integration list - Foundation, Viewpoint, Sage, QuickBooks Online - reflects the back-office systems mid-size GCs actually run.

### Builder Prime

Builder Prime addresses a different pain point entirely. The home improvement contractor's problem is not bid volume - it is the gap between sales and production. A lead comes in, gets quoted, the job gets sold, and then the work needs to be scheduled, crewed, tracked, and closed out. Most home improvement contractors manage that flow with three to five separate tools: one for CRM, one for estimating, one for scheduling, one for time tracking, and one for payments.

Builder Prime replaces that stack with one platform. The Growth plan covers CRM, estimating, proposals, production management, GPS time clock, payments, and reporting for single-location businesses. The Multiply plan adds multi-location controls: separate pipelines, phone numbers, Price Books, and QuickBooks accounts per location.

The typical Builder Prime user is a home improvement contractor in roofing, siding, windows and doors, fencing, painting, concrete, or bath remodeling - someone who wants an all-in-one system, not a point solution that only solves the sales problem.

## When it does not make sense yet

Skip FollowUp CRM if your main pain point is field production, crew scheduling, material ordering, or job costing after the contract is signed. It covers pre-construction only, and post-award project management requires a second platform.

Also skip FollowUp CRM if published pricing is a requirement before you speak with anyone. The quote-only model means every plan requires a demo, and the 5-user minimum makes it expensive for small teams. The contract terms include a 60-day written cancellation notice and a mandatory offboarding meeting - review those carefully before signing.

Skip Builder Prime if your business is general construction, commercial contracting, or a trade outside the nine home improvement verticals it targets. The platform is built for home improvement workflows, and contractors outside that scope will find the feature set misaligned.

Also skip Builder Prime if solo operator simplicity is what you need. The platform's breadth comes with complexity - some users describe the interface as overwhelming compared to simpler tools like Jobber. For a one- or two-person operation, a lighter tool with published pricing will cost less and require less setup time.

## Pricing: what each tool actually costs

### FollowUp CRM

FollowUp CRM does not publish dollar amounts. The Essentials, Professional, and Enterprise tiers are priced per user per month, but actual figures require a sales conversation. Third-party estimates put the entry-level cost at approximately $4,500 per year for 5 users - roughly $75 per user per month billed annually - with a mandatory setup fee equal to 20% of the annual license cost. The 5-user minimum applies on all contracts.

There is no free trial. FollowUp CRM offers a 30-day money-back guarantee that requires completing onboarding within 30 days of signing and matching the requirements from the recorded demo. Plan to have real bids and contacts loaded from day one if you want to evaluate honestly within that window.

**What to ask for during the demo:**
- Per-user monthly rate at each tier, broken out individually
- Annual versus monthly billing difference
- Any platform, implementation, or setup fees outside the per-user rate
- Which accounting integrations are bundled versus add-ons at each tier
- Whether automation sequences are available on Essentials or only Professional and higher

## Bid tracking and sales pipeline

This is FollowUp CRM's territory, and the gap between the two tools is significant here.

**FollowUp CRM's digital bid calendar** is a dedicated construction-specific feature for tracking submission deadlines across dozens of active bids. Custom pipeline stages let you mirror your actual pre-construction workflow - lead received, estimating in progress, bid submitted, follow-up sent, awarded, lost - whatever labels reflect your team's process. Automation sequences extend that discipline: if a submitted bid goes unanswered for five days, FollowUp CRM triggers a follow-up task or email automatically.

The bid calendar is built for the volume problem: 40 active bids across different GCs, each with a different due date, decision window, and follow-up cadence. Users describe it as a "night-and-day" switch from tracking in Excel or Google Sheets.

**Builder Prime's lead management** tracks leads through configurable status pipelines and includes automated follow-up sequences. The system captures leads from multiple sources - phone calls, website forms, HomeAdvisor, Angi - and routes them through the sales process. Speed-to-lead automation is a core feature: one Builder Prime customer reported a 68% close rate increase attributed to automated lead follow-up.

The difference in emphasis is clear. FollowUp CRM treats bid tracking as the core workflow. Builder Prime treats lead management as one module within a broader system. For a GC managing bid volume, FollowUp CRM is the sharper tool. For a home improvement contractor whose lead-to-close process includes on-site estimating and production handoff, Builder Prime's lead management plus production tools is more relevant.

**Who wins on sales pipeline:** FollowUp CRM, by a meaningful margin, for any team whose primary workflow is bid volume management. Builder Prime's lead tracking is adequate for home improvement but does not match FollowUp's bid-tracking depth.

## Mobile apps and field access

This is Builder Prime's clear advantage.

**FollowUp CRM** has no native iOS or Android app. The platform is accessible through a responsive web browser on a phone, but that is not the same experience as a native app. No push notifications, no offline access, no camera integration for dropping job-site photos into contact records. For office-based estimators and salespeople, the responsive web interface is functional. For anyone who needs CRM access from a job site, the absence of a native app is a real constraint.

**Builder Prime** has native iOS and Android apps designed for field crews. Field workers can clock in and out with GPS location stamps, view job details and schedules, and complete work orders from their phones. The mobile app includes GPS-based time tracking that ties directly to payroll.

**Who wins on mobile:** Builder Prime, without question. This is a core feature for any contractor with field crews, and FollowUp CRM does not offer a competitive option.

## Estimating and proposals

**FollowUp CRM** includes a proposal generator with e-sign on the Professional tier. That covers creating a branded proposal and collecting a digital signature, but it is not a full estimating tool. For quantity takeoffs, unit-cost estimating, or detailed bid assembly from plans, FollowUp CRM requires a dedicated estimating platform alongside it.

**Builder Prime** includes a built-in estimating module with a Price Book. Sales reps can create estimates from a mobile device or tablet, pull pricing from the centralized Price Book, calculate job costs automatically, and collect digital signatures on the spot. The Price Book keeps materials, labor rates, and markup percentages consistent across the whole team.

**Who wins on estimating:** Builder Prime, for home improvement contractors needing on-site estimating with price book management. For GCs that need detailed bid assembly, both tools fall short of dedicated estimating platforms like Buildertrend, Esticom, or PlanSwift.

## Production management

This category only applies to one of the two tools.

**FollowUp CRM** does not include production management. It covers pre-construction sales only. Once a bid is awarded and the contract is signed, the job moves out of FollowUp CRM's scope. You need a separate tool for scheduling, daily field logs, material procurement, subcontract management, and job costing during the construction phase.

**Builder Prime** covers the full production lifecycle: GPS time clock with location stamps, digital work orders, subcontractor management, drag-and-drop scheduling with calendar and Gantt views, and change order management. One user reported improving gross margin from 15% to 47% after switching from Jobber, attributing it to the production visibility - knowing actual labor costs per job, tracking material usage against estimates, and catching overruns early.

**Who wins on production management:** Builder Prime. FollowUp CRM does not compete here at all.

## Who wins - and why it depends on your business

Neither tool is universally better. They are built for different contractor problems, and the right call depends entirely on what kind of construction business you run.

**Pick FollowUp CRM if:**

You run a general contracting or commercial subcontracting business where the daily operational bottleneck is bid volume. Your team of 5-15 estimators and salespeople tracks 20-60 active bids at once, and the follow-up discipline is where deals are won or lost. Your back-end accounting runs on Foundation, Viewpoint, Sage, or QuickBooks Online. The digital bid calendar, custom pipeline stages, and automated follow-up sequences will directly replace spreadsheet chaos. You are comfortable with quote-based pricing and annual contracts, and you have reviewed the cancellation terms.

FollowUp CRM is the specialist. It does one thing - pre-construction sales pipeline management - and does it better than any all-in-one platform. For the right team at the right size, that depth is worth the narrow scope.

**Pick Builder Prime if:**

You run a home improvement contracting business - roofing, siding, windows and doors, fencing, painting, concrete, or bath remodeling - and your current operation requires three to five separate tools to manage CRM, estimating, scheduling, production, payments, and reporting. You want one platform that covers the full job lifecycle from lead capture through final payment. Your field crew needs native mobile apps with GPS time tracking and photo capture. You operate multiple locations and need separate pipelines, phone numbers, and pricing per location.

Builder Prime is the all-in-one. It does not match FollowUp CRM's bid-tracking depth, but it covers the entire workflow that FollowUp CRM stops at the moment the contract is signed. For home improvement contractors, that breadth is the value proposition.

**The specific scenarios:**

- *10-person GC team tracking 40+ commercial bids* -> FollowUp CRM. The bid calendar and pipeline depth are worth the narrow scope.

- *15-person roofing company with sales and production crews* -> Builder Prime. You need production management, mobile apps, and GPS time tracking alongside CRM.

- *5-person fence company running leads on HomeAdvisor and doing on-site quotes* -> Builder Prime. The estimating module with Price Book and lead source integrations are the right fit.

- *3-person commercial sub just starting with CRM* -> Neither. FollowUp CRM's 5-user minimum is expensive; Builder Prime's home improvement focus is wrong. Start with a lighter tool like Jobber or JobNimbus.

- *Multi-location home improvement company (roofing + siding + windows)* -> Builder Prime Multiply plan. The per-location pipelines, phone numbers, and QuickBooks accounts are a genuine differentiator.

- *Pre-construction team that needs bid tracking + a separate production platform* -> FollowUp CRM for bid tracking, plus a dedicated production tool like JobTread or Buildertrend for post-award work.
