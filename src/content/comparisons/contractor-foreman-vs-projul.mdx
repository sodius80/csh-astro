# Contractor Foreman vs Projul Comparison

<p>The useful question isn't whether Contractor Foreman or Projul is better software. The useful question is which pricing model fits your operation. Contractor Foreman sells company-level plans with published user caps: Basic at $49/mo for 1 user, Plus at $166/mo for 8 users, Pro at $221/mo for 15 users, and Unlimited at $332/mo for unlimited users. Projul sells annual flat-rate plans with no per-user fees: Core at $4,788/year, Core+ at $7,188/year, and Pro at $14,388/year.</p>

<p>That pricing difference is the core decision. Contractor Foreman gives you a low entry point and a 30-day free trial to test the product before committing. Projul asks for an annual commitment upfront but removes per-user math entirely and includes a premium support package with personalized onboarding, workflow analysis, and data import. Both are construction management platforms with estimates, schedules, job costing, change orders, documents, field tools, and QuickBooks integration. The question is whether you want to start cheap and scale by tier, or pay annually and give the whole team access on day one.</p>

## Pricing: what you actually pay

<h3>Contractor Foreman pricing</h3>

<p>Contractor Foreman publishes company-level pricing by user cap. That makes budgeting easier than per-user systems, but the tier gates still matter. Annual billing is the cleanest standard comparison point:</p>

|| Contractor Foreman plan | Atlas-verified annual price | User cap | Practical read |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Basic | $49/mo, $588/year | 1 user | Real low-cost entry point, but too narrow for most teams |
| Standard | $105/mo, $1,264/year | 3 users | Small office tier if the workflow is simple |
| Plus | $166/mo, $1,997/year | 8 users | First serious comparison tier for many teams |
| Pro | $221/mo, $2,654/year | 15 users | Better when client portal, takeoffs, and larger-team needs matter |
| Unlimited | $332/mo, $3,984/year | Unlimited | Strong math if many office and field users will actually log in |

<p>The buying mistake is comparing Contractor Foreman Basic against Projul Core and stopping there. Basic is valid, but it is not the benchmark for a multi-person construction operation. A general contractor with office staff, field leads, and owner/admin access should usually model Plus, Pro, or Unlimited.</p>

<h3>Projul pricing</h3>

<p>Projul publishes three annual plans. There is no monthly billing option and no self-serve free trial. The model is flat-rate with no per-user fees and unlimited projects on every plan:</p>

|| Projul plan | Atlas-verified annual price | Practical read |
|---|---:|---|
| Core | $4,788/year | CRM, estimating, invoicing, scheduling, photo capture, eSignatures, mobile app, reporting, and premium support |
| Core+ | $7,188/year | Adds client portal, change orders, construction financials, job costing, progress billing, time tracking, Gantt charts, and QuickBooks Online |
| Pro | $14,388/year | Adds unlimited users, assemblies, geofencing, purchase orders, selections, QuickBooks Desktop, and Spanish app translation |

<p>The premium support package, valued at $4,500 on the pricing page, is included free with annual plans. It covers workflow analysis, system customization, customized training, data import, and support by phone, email, and video chat. Projul also lists a 5% Veterans discount.</p>

<h3>Real cost comparison: same team, different bill</h3>

<p>The clearest way to compare is to put the same team size against both pricing models. Here is what an 8-user construction team would pay in the first year:</p>

|| Pricing model | First-year cost | What is included |
|---|---:|---|
| Contractor Foreman Plus (8 users) | $1,997/year | Scheduling, daily logs, time cards, safety, job-costing reports, QuickBooks Online, Zapier |
| Projul Core+ (no user limit) | $7,188/year | Job costing, change orders, client portal, progress billing, time tracking, QuickBooks Online, premium support |

<p>Contractor Foreman Plus is significantly cheaper for an 8-user team. But Projul Core+ includes the premium support package, flat-rate access for unlimited users, and deeper job costing and budgeting tools. The price gap shrinks when you move to larger teams:</p>

|| Pricing model | First-year cost | What is included |
|---|---:|---|
| Contractor Foreman Unlimited | $3,984/year | All features, unlimited internal users, dedicated trainer, phone support, offline time cards |
| Projul Pro | $14,388/year | All features, unlimited users, assemblies, geofencing, purchase orders, QuickBooks Desktop, selections |

<p>Even at the top tier, Contractor Foreman Unlimited costs roughly $3,984/year versus Projul Pro at $14,388/year. Projul's premium is the no-per-user model, the support package, and the deeper construction financial controls. For very large teams, Projul's flat rate can become more attractive when adding users would push Contractor Foreman past the Unlimited tier or when the support package replaces a dedicated admin hire.</p>

## Workflow fit: where each product is stronger

<h3>Construction project management depth</h3>

<p>Contractor Foreman has broader construction administration vocabulary. Its official plans and features list includes projects, opportunities, work orders, permits, inspections, punch lists, service tickets, estimates, bid manager, change orders, invoices, purchase orders, bills, expenses, subcontracts, AIA-style invoicing, takeoffs, drawings, PDF markup, RFIs, notices, submittals, equipment and vehicle logs, daily logs, time cards, safety meetings, and job-costing reports.</p>

<p>Projul covers the core construction loop: CRM, estimating, scheduling, project management, photo capture, eSignatures, invoicing, reporting, templates, and mobile app on Core. Core+ adds change orders, client portal, construction financials, job costing, progress billing, time tracking, Gantt charts, messaging, and QuickBooks Online. Pro adds assemblies, geolocation, geofencing, purchase orders, selections, service invoicing, QuickBooks Desktop, and Spanish app translation.</p>

<p>Contractor Foreman has more construction document depth with RFIs, submittals, drawings markup, and equipment logs. Projul has more guided workflow with its one-click estimate-to-task conversion, linear project timelines, and channeled communications that replace texts and emails. If document-heavy project administration is the priority, Contractor Foreman has the edge. If connecting lead-to-estimate-to-schedule-to-field in a guided flow is the priority, Projul has the edge. For a pure document markup and estimating comparison, see <a href="/bluebeam-revu-vs-contractor-foreman/">Bluebeam Revu vs Contractor Foreman</a>.</p>

<h3>Trial and onboarding</h3>

<p>This is one of the biggest practical differences. Contractor Foreman offers a 30-day free trial with no credit card required. You can sign up, build a real estimate, create a schedule, enter a daily log, capture time, connect QuickBooks Online, and test the client portal before paying anything. That self-serve trial is a major advantage for contractors who want to evaluate the product on their own timeline.</p>

<p>Projul does not offer a public self-serve trial. The official page emphasizes booking a free demo. During the demo, you ask to see a lead become an estimate, the estimate become tasks, a field user upload photos, a crew member log time, and the office review job-cost status. The premium support package then covers workflow analysis, system customization, training, and data import after purchase.</p>

<p>Contractor Foreman is better for contractors who want to test independently. Projul is better for contractors who want guided onboarding and are comfortable with a demo-led evaluation before committing.</p>

<h3>Accounting and QuickBooks</h3>

<p>Both products integrate with QuickBooks, but the plan gates differ. Contractor Foreman lists QuickBooks Online on Plus and higher. Projul lists QuickBooks Online on Core+ and QuickBooks Desktop on Pro. Contractor Foreman discontinued QuickBooks Desktop integration effective January 1, 2026, so Desktop users should validate migration needs before buying.</p>

<p>For construction accounting, Contractor Foreman has more depth in AIA-style billing, retainage tracking, subcontracts, and bid management. Projul has more depth in real-time WIP reports, automatic job costing reports, and live construction cost data integration for estimating.</p>

## The real buying scenarios

<h3>Scenario 1: small contractor (1-3 users) wants construction PM</h3>

<p>Choose Contractor Foreman. At 1-3 users, Contractor Foreman Basic or Standard is dramatically cheaper than Projul Core at $4,788/year. The 30-day trial lets you test the real workflow before paying. Projul's annual commitment is a serious barrier for a team this small unless the flat-rate model and support package are specifically what you are looking for.</p>

<h3>Scenario 2: growing team (5-15 users) wants everyone in one system</h3>

<p>This is where the comparison gets interesting. Contractor Foreman Plus at $166/mo ($1,997/year) for 8 users or Pro at $221/mo ($2,654/year) for 15 users is still much cheaper than Projul Core+ at $7,188/year. But Projul's no-per-user model means the price does not change as you add users, and the premium support package may save onboarding time that a small team would otherwise spend self-configuring.</p>

<p>If the team values guided onboarding, the support package, and deeper job costing controls, Projul justifies the premium. If the team can own setup internally and prefers to keep costs low, Contractor Foreman Plus or Pro is the stronger value.</p>

<h3>Scenario 3: large team (15+ users) needs unlimited access</h3>

<p>Contractor Foreman Unlimited at $332/mo ($3,984/year) gives unlimited internal users every feature. Projul Pro at $14,388/year also gives unlimited users every feature plus assemblies, geofencing, purchase orders, selections, and QuickBooks Desktop.</p>

<p>The price gap is significant. Contractor Foreman is roughly $10,000 cheaper per year at the top tier. Projul's premium includes deeper financial controls, the support package, and construction-specific features like geofencing and Spanish app translation. A very large team should model both against <a href="/procore-review/">Procore</a> or another enterprise platform to confirm the feature set is sufficient before committing.</p>

<h3>Scenario 4: contractor wants to test before buying</h3>

<p>Choose Contractor Foreman. The 30-day free trial with no credit card is the decisive factor. You can build a real estimate, create a schedule, test a change order, connect QuickBooks Online, and run a job-costing report before paying a cent. Projul only offers a demo, so you are evaluating based on a guided walkthrough rather than hands-on testing.</p>

<p>Contractors who need to see the software work with their own data should start with Contractor Foreman. Contractors who want a guided evaluation and are comfortable committing annually after a demo should consider Projul.</p>

## Where each product falls short

<h3>Contractor Foreman limitations</h3>

<ul>
<li><strong>Basic is not the real team benchmark.</strong> The $49/mo plan is a 1-user entry point. Most team workflows need Standard, Plus, Pro, or Unlimited.</li>
<li><strong>User caps create upgrade pressure.</strong> Growing teams hit the 8-user Plus cap or the 15-user Pro cap and must upgrade to Unlimited at $332/mo.</li>
<li><strong>Feature gates by tier.</strong> Scheduling, QuickBooks Online, job-costing reports, takeoffs, and client portal all require higher tiers. The cheapest plan is not the full PM workflow.</li>
<li><strong>Self-guided onboarding.</strong> Training is included but varies by plan. The platform covers many modules, so inconsistent setup can create clutter quickly.</li>
<li><strong>Client portal feels utilitarian.</strong> The portal starts on Pro and is less polished than Buildertrend or higher-priced builder platforms.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Projul limitations</h3>

<ul>
<li><strong>Annual-only pricing.</strong> No monthly billing option. The $4,788/year Core plan is a higher upfront commitment than Contractor Foreman's $49/mo entry point.</li>
<li><strong>No self-serve trial.</strong> Projul offers a demo, not a free trial. Buyers cannot test the full product independently before committing.</li>
<li><strong>User access is not fully transparent.</strong> The pricing page says no per-user fees, but lists "Unlimited Users" specifically under Pro. Buyers should confirm user access rules for Core and Core+ before signing.</li>
<li><strong>Feature gates by tier.</strong> Job costing, client portal, change orders, QuickBooks Online, purchase orders, selections, and QuickBooks Desktop all require Core+ or Pro rather than Core.</li>
<li><strong>Smaller integration marketplace.</strong> Projul has fewer third-party integrations than Procore or Buildertrend. Verify critical accounting, payment, and reporting connections before choosing a plan.</li>
</ul>

## Recommendation

<p><strong>Choose Contractor Foreman</strong> if the purchase is about affordable construction project management with a low entry point, published user caps, and a 30-day free trial. It is the better first test for budget-conscious contractors, remodelers, concrete contractors, excavation contractors, masonry contractors, and specialty trades that want broad construction PM without an annual upfront commitment or a demo-led process. The 30-day trial is a real advantage for contractors who want to evaluate software with their own data before paying.</p>

<p><strong>Choose Projul</strong> if the purchase is about flat annual pricing, no per-user fees, and guided onboarding for a growing team. It is the stronger choice for contractors with 5-30 employees that want everyone in one system, predictable annual software costs, and a premium support package that includes workflow analysis, system customization, training, and data import. The annual commitment is a real barrier for smaller shops, but the flat-rate model and support package can justify the premium for the right team.</p>

<p><strong>Do not choose based only on the lowest plan.</strong> Contractor Foreman Basic and Projul Core are both real entry points, but they do not represent the working plan for every contractor. Match each product to the workflow, team size, and billing preference you will actually have after the first 90 days.</p>
