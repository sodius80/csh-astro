    Jobber and JobNimbus look like they compete for the same contractor. Both handle customer records, job tracking, estimates, and invoices. Both have mobile apps. Both appear in the same comparison searches. But they are built around completely different kinds of work — and putting the wrong one in place doesn't mean you picked a bad product. It means you'll spend the first several months fighting the software instead of using it.
    
    Jobber is a general-service dispatch platform built for businesses that run multiple same-day jobs. HVAC tune-ups, cleaning appointments, landscaping routes, pest control visits — work that books, completes, and closes in a single day, where the next job is already on the calendar before the tech leaves the driveway. JobNimbus is a project-management CRM purpose-built for roofing and exterior contractors. It's built for jobs that move through distinct stages over days or weeks: lead → inspection → estimate → insurance approval → material order → production → final payment. The biggest problem it solves is keeping sales and production talking to each other without a phone call for every job.
    
    If you're a roofer evaluating Jobber, or a cleaning company evaluating JobNimbus, this comparison will save you a month.

    ## Jobber vs JobNimbus: Quick Verdict by Trade Type
    
    Roofers: stop evaluating Jobber. Jobber doesn't have supplier integrations for ABC Supply, Beacon Pro, SRS, or QXO. It doesn't support insurance claim workflows. It doesn't have customizable production boards for multi-stage jobs. It doesn't handle the sales-to-production handoff that exterior contractors live inside every day. JobNimbus has all of that by design — not as a workaround, but as the core architecture. The products look similar on a feature checklist. They are not.
    
    Service contractors — HVAC, plumbing, landscaping, cleaning, pest control, handyman — stop evaluating JobNimbus. JobNimbus's pricing requires a sales call — no dollar amounts are published for any tier — and its board-based project structure is overkill for businesses that book, visit, and close in a single day. Jobber's scheduling, dispatch, and invoicing tools are built exactly for that work. The published pricing and 14-day trial mean you can evaluate the product this afternoon without talking to anyone.
    
   The exception: a general contractor doing both service calls and small projects will probably land in Jobber with some workflow configuration, or find a better fit in a construction-focused platform. Neither Jobber nor JobNimbus is purpose-built for new-construction project management with change orders and subcontractor coordination.
   
   ## When this software makes sense
   
   Both tools earn their cost when the office is managing too many things in text threads and spreadsheets.
   
   Jobber makes sense when jobs are falling through the cracks between booking and invoicing. Someone forgets to follow up on an unsigned estimate that's been sitting for 12 days. A tech shows up without knowing the job scope because the notes are in a group chat. An invoice sits unpaid for two weeks because nobody remembered to send a reminder. Jobber's scheduling, quote follow-ups, automated reminders, and payment collection close those gaps without a long implementation.
   
   JobNimbus makes sense when the sales team is closing jobs the production team doesn't know about until it's almost time to schedule. Insurance supplement approvals get tracked in a spreadsheet that nobody updates consistently. Material orders go out by text with no record in the job file. A roofing company with 8–10 people doing $3–5M a year typically hits this wall within the first two or three seasons — and that's when JobNimbus's production boards and supplier integrations start paying for themselves.
   
   ## When it doesn't make sense yet
   
   Skip both if you're running fewer than 10 jobs a month and the admin load hasn't become genuinely painful. Software adds overhead before it adds value — you need to configure service items, customer records, payment settings, and accounting integrations before you see a return. If a spreadsheet and QuickBooks aren't breaking down yet, keep them.
   
   Jobber specifically: if your work is project-based with multi-week timelines and subcontractor coordination, Jobber's service-call architecture will frustrate you. You can force it to work, but you'll be building workarounds the whole time.
   
   JobNimbus specifically: if you run a service-call business in HVAC, plumbing, or cleaning, the board-based workflow and opaque pricing (quote required) aren't justified by what you'd actually use. Jobber or Housecall Pro fit the job structure better and cost less to start.

   ## Pricing: what you actually pay
   
   This is the sharpest difference between the two platforms.
   
   **Jobber** publishes every price on its pricing page — no sales call required.
   
   Individual plans (1 user):
   - **Core** — $49/mo or $29/mo annual. Scheduling, quoting, invoicing, online booking, payments, reporting, app marketplace.
   - **Connect** — $139/mo or $99/mo annual. Adds automated reminders, automatic payments, job checklists and forms, quote/invoice follow-ups, QuickBooks Online sync, time and expense tracking.
   - **Grow** — $199/mo or $149/mo annual. Adds advanced quote customizations, optional line items, automatic time tracking, job costing, two-way SMS, and custom workflow automations.
   
   Team plans:
   - **Connect Team** (5 users) — $199/mo or $149/mo annual.
   - **Grow Team** (10 users) — $399/mo or $299/mo annual.
   - **Plus** (15 users) — $699/mo or $529/mo annual — includes Marketing Suite ($79 value), AI Receptionist ($99 value), and Pipeline ($49 value).
   
   Extra users beyond plan: $29/user/month. Payment processing: 2.9% + $0.30 for credit card, 2.7% + $0.30 Tap to Pay, 1% ACH, +1% for instant payouts.
   
   Watch-outs: annual prepaid plans are non-refundable mid-year. The jump from individual plans to team plans isn't a smooth ramp — a 2-person operation on Core pays $49 + $29 = $78/month, while the next logical team step (Connect Team with QuickBooks sync) is $199/month. Run the math on your exact user count before committing to a tier.
   
   **JobNimbus** doesn't publish dollar amounts for any plan. The official pricing page lists four tiers with user limits, project board limits, automation limits, and Support Integration limits — but no prices. Your actual number comes from the sales team.
   
Official plan tiers and limits:
- **Essentials** — Up to 3 users, 3 project boards, 10 automations, max 2 Support Integrations. QuickBooks and Suppliers are unlimited across all tiers.
- **Pro** — Up to 10 users, 5 project boards, 30 automations, max 5 Support Integrations. API & Webhooks are unlimited on all tiers.
- **Premium** — Up to 19 users, unlimited project boards, 100 automations, unlimited Support Integrations. Multi-location support up to 5 locations.
- **Enterprise** — 20+ users, unlimited everything. Custom pricing.
   
JobNimbus plan tiers include users: Essentials up to 3, Pro up to 10, Premium up to 19, Enterprise 20+. Optional Marketing Bundle and Communications Bundle require a quote. There are no required setup or onboarding fees; paid Professional Services are available as an optional add-on.

**Support Integrations cap** is the practical limit to watch. Official JobNimbus pricing lists QuickBooks Integration and Supplier Integrations as unlimited across all tiers. API & Webhooks are also unlimited on all tiers. The capped row is Support Integrations: max 2 on Essentials, max 5 on Pro, unlimited on Premium and Enterprise. If your workflow requires Zapier plus multiple third-party tools beyond QuickBooks and your supplier connection, count your Support Integrations against the tier limit before assuming you're covered.

**Ancillary tools are separate costs.** CompanyCam (photo documentation) and EagleView/HOVER (aerial measurements) are not included in the JobNimbus subscription. Verify pricing directly with each vendor before budgeting.
   
   ## Key features compared
   
   **Scheduling and dispatch:** Jobber has the advantage for service-call businesses. Drag-and-drop scheduling, online booking, recurring job handling, and a mobile app that field techs can use without a training session. For a plumbing company booking 8–12 calls a day, Jobber's calendar view keeps the dispatch board manageable. JobNimbus doesn't have drag-and-drop same-day dispatch. Its board view tracks jobs through project stages — it's the right tool for managing a roofing pipeline, not routing five techs across a metro area.
   
   **Estimating:** Both handle estimates, but for different job shapes. Jobber's quoting is optimized for fast approval — send an estimate, the homeowner approves online, the job appears on the schedule. Grow adds advanced customizations and optional line items. JobNimbus's estimating handles the complexity of exterior work: line-item detail, measurement imports from EagleView or HOVER, and multi-option presentations that match insurance-claim workflows.
   
   **Payments:** Jobber's payment processing rates are published and competitive (2.9% + $0.30 credit card, 1% ACH). Payment reminders and automatic collection kick in on Connect and above. JobNimbus offers payment processing with published rates: $20 one-time boarding fee, 3.2% + $0.29 per card transaction, and ACH/eCheck at 1% capped at $50. Some customers may have negotiated rates that differ — ask during the sales call.
   
   **Mobile app:** Both are solid. Jobber draws consistent praise for ease of use (144 G2 mentions). JobNimbus's App Store rating is 4.8★ from 12K ratings — field crews on roofing jobs report it handles photos, status updates, and job notes well in the field. Jobber's key limitation is offline functionality: the official Jobber community forum confirms the app doesn't support offline mode except for internal notes. For techs working in rural low-signal areas, test this during the trial.
   
   **Automation:** JobNimbus's when/if/then automation engine is more powerful — when a job stage changes, trigger a text notification, task creation, status update, or team alert. For companies managing the sales-to-production handoff across 20+ active jobs, those automations save real hours weekly. Jobber's automations (Grow and above) handle quote and invoice follow-ups, reminders, and job-specific triggers, but the logic is simpler.
   
   **Reporting:** Jobber's reporting covers the basics for most small teams — job revenue, outstanding invoices, tech performance — but 57% of Capterra reviewers flag limited depth at scale. JobNimbus offers API & Webhooks unlimited on all tiers. Premium and Enterprise add multi-location support (up to 5 on Premium, unlimited on Enterprise) and higher automation limits.

   ## Limitations and red flags
   
   **Jobber:**
   
   The offline limitation is more significant than the marketing suggests. Jobber's own community forum is explicit: the app doesn't support offline capabilities except for internal notes. For techs in rural areas, basements, or any low-signal environment, that means no job detail access, no estimate viewing, and no payment collection until signal returns. Test your actual routes during the trial.
   
   QuickBooks sync is one-way. Data flows from Jobber into QuickBooks, not back. If your accountant makes adjustments in QuickBooks — reclassifying payments, editing invoices — those changes don't appear in Jobber. Accounting reconciliation requires manual work that grows as volume increases.
   
   No inventory management. Jobber has no module for tracking parts, equipment, or warehouse stock. If that's part of operations, you'll need a separate tool or a platform like ServiceTitan that includes it.
   
   User count matters more than plan headlines suggest. A 20-person team exceeds Jobber Plus's included 15 users and requires the published extra-user fee ($29/user/mo) or a contact-sales quote. Verify your total with Jobber before committing.
   
   **JobNimbus:**
   
You can't budget for it without a sales call. The pricing page shows four tiers (Essentials, Pro, Premium, Enterprise) with official user, board, automation, and Support Integration limits, but no dollar amounts. The only way to get your actual number is to engage the sales team. This is deliberate — and it's the single most-complained-about aspect of the product across every review platform.

The multi-tier model makes it easy to underestimate real cost. Users are included per plan (Essentials 3, Pro 10, Premium 19, Enterprise 20+), but optional bundles and Professional Services can add to the total. Ask specifically for the total with your user count and any desired add-ons before the call ends.
   
   Email delivery is a current production risk. Multiple verified user reports of emails not reaching homeowners and adjusters. For contractors whose workflow depends on emailing estimates, approvals, and follow-ups, this is a liability right now. Test it explicitly during the trial. Ask the sales team for a current fix timeline.
   
   CompanyCam and EagleView/HOVER are additional costs that don't appear in the JobNimbus subscription quote. Both are separate subscriptions — verify current pricing directly with each vendor before budgeting. A 10-person crew can easily add several hundred dollars per month in ancillary tool costs before these get factored into the decision.
