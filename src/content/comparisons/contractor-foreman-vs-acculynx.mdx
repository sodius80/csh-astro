# Contractor Foreman vs AccuLynx: Budget PM vs Roofing CRM

<p>The fastest way to decide between Contractor Foreman and AccuLynx is to name the trade and the workflow. If you need an affordable construction management system for estimates, schedules, daily logs, invoices, field records, job-cost reports, documents, RFIs, submittals, and QuickBooks Online workflow, start with Contractor Foreman. If you're a roofing or exterior contractor whose jobs depend on supplier ordering, aerial measurements, production boards, claim documents, and job files, start with AccuLynx.</p>

<p>These tools can look similar in search results because both serve contractors. They aren't built around the same operating model. Contractor Foreman is a broad budget PM platform for many trades. AccuLynx is a roofing CRM and production system with supplier and measurement depth that general construction tools do not try to match.</p>

Atlas verifies AccuLynx Essential at $250/mo, with Pro and Elite requiring a custom quote. Contractor Foreman lists a 30-day free trial. AccuLynx doesn't list a public self-serve trial on the official source packet used for this comparison.</p>

## Quick verdict

<p>Pick <a href="/go/contractor-foreman-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Contractor Foreman</a> if your bigger problem is general job administration. It's the better first look for budget-conscious general contractors, remodelers, concrete contractors, excavation contractors, masonry contractors, and specialty trades that need one place for estimates, schedules, logs, time, invoices, documents, reports, and job records. The important catch is tier fit: the $49/mo Basic plan is a 1-user entry point, not the full operating workflow for a multi-person company.</p>

<p>Pick <a href="/go/acculynx-official-site/" rel="nofollow">AccuLynx</a> if roofing or exterior restoration is the business. Its official pages emphasize roofing-specific job flow, supplier direct ordering, aerial measurement ordering, material calculations, photo and document management, production workflow, and financial controls. That depth is wasted on a general contractor that does not order roofing materials every week, but it can be the reason an established roofer pays more.</p>

<p>If neither fits, compare <a href="/jobnimbus-review/">JobNimbus</a> for a roofing CRM with a stronger flexible-board reputation, <a href="/roofr-review/">Roofr</a> for roofing estimates and proposals, and <a href="/jobtread-review/">JobTread</a> for construction job costing and all-feature pricing. For a broader shortlist, use the <a href="/best-roofing-software-contractors/">roofing software guide</a> or the <a href="/best-crm-for-contractors/">contractor CRM roundup</a>.</p>

## Pricing: what you actually pay

<h3>Contractor Foreman pricing</h3>

<p>Contractor Foreman publishes company-level tiers with user caps. Annual billing is the cleanest comparison point:</p>

| Contractor Foreman plan | Atlas-verified annual price | User cap | Practical read |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Basic | $49/mo, $588/year | 1 user | True low-cost entry point, but too limited for most team workflows |
| Standard | $105/mo, $1,264/year | 3 users | Small office tier before the bigger PM jump |
| Plus | $166/mo, $1,997/year | 8 users | First serious team tier for many contractors because it adds the broader operating workflow |
| Pro | $221/mo, $2,654/year | 15 users | Better fit when takeoffs, client portal, and larger-team needs matter |
| Unlimited | $332/mo, $3,984/year | Unlimited | Best company-wide math if the whole team will actually use the system |

<p>Contractor Foreman also lists quarterly billing on Standard and higher at higher monthly equivalents. The official plans page lists a 30-day free trial. The buying mistake is comparing AccuLynx Essential against Contractor Foreman Basic. A real team should usually compare AccuLynx Essential against Contractor Foreman Plus, Pro, or Unlimited.</p>

<h3>AccuLynx pricing</h3>

<p>AccuLynx publishes one entry plan and keeps the higher tiers behind a quote:</p>

| AccuLynx plan | Atlas-verified public price | Practical read |
|---|---:|---|
| Essential | $250/mo | Published entry plan for growth-minded roofers with CRM, measurements, material calculations, proposals, supplier direct ordering, photo and document management, and basic scheduling/job tracking |
| Pro | Custom quote | Higher workflow, financial management, reporting, and automation path for established roofing companies |
| Elite | Custom quote | Multi-location, advanced production, custom workflow, and insurance restoration path |

<p>That makes AccuLynx more expensive at the entry point, but the bigger issue is not the $250 number. The bigger issue is quote scope. Ask whether users, supplier setup, implementation, SmartDocs, texting, customer portal, reporting, support, measurement costs, and renewal terms are included. If Pro or Elite is the real fit, get a written quote before building the ROI case.</p>

## Feature comparison: broad PM vs roofing depth

<h3>Project management and field records</h3>

<p>Contractor Foreman wins the broad PM category. Its official plans and features pages list a wide set of construction records: projects, opportunities, work orders, permits, inspections, punch lists, service tickets, client portal, estimates, bid manager, change orders, invoices, purchase orders, bills, expenses, subcontracts, AIA-style invoicing, time cards, documents, photos, drawings, PDF markup, RFIs, submittals, equipment and vehicle logs, reports, and integrations.</p>

<p>AccuLynx also manages jobs, documents, schedules, and production, but the shape is roofing-specific. The question is whether that specificity helps or gets in the way. A general remodeler may find AccuLynx too focused on exterior workflows. A roofer may find Contractor Foreman's broader module list less valuable than AccuLynx's supplier and production depth.</p>

<h3>Supplier ordering and materials</h3>

<p>AccuLynx wins this category decisively. The official AccuLynx feature page says contractors can access ABC Supply, SRS Distribution, and QXO catalogs and preferred pricing, then place orders from AccuLynx. The integrations page also lists those supplier connections. That's the core reason AccuLynx belongs in a roofing software decision.</p>

<p>Contractor Foreman has purchasing, bills, purchase orders, material database integrations, and job records. That can be enough for many contractors. It isn't the same as roofing supplier direct ordering tied to an exterior job file. If your material workflow is already built around ABC Supply, SRS, or QXO, AccuLynx deserves the first demo.</p>

<h3>Aerial measurements and estimating</h3>

<p>AccuLynx is also stronger for roofing measurement workflow. Its official feature page lists aerial measurement ordering for EagleView, Hover, Geospan, and GAF QuickMeasure, with reports ordered from AccuLynx and measurements used inside the job workflow. That's a roofing-specific advantage, not a generic estimating feature.</p>

<p>Contractor Foreman includes estimating, bid management, takeoffs, drawings, and PDF markup by tier. That is useful when you want a broad construction platform and basic takeoff workflow in the same place. It is not the same as a roof-measurement workflow built around aerial reports and supplier ordering. If estimating is the whole purchase, compare both tools against <a href="/best-roofing-takeoff-estimating-software/">roofing takeoff and estimating software</a> before you decide.</p>

<h3>Accounting, job costing, and financial controls</h3>

<p>Contractor Foreman is easier to budget because the relevant tier gates are public. Job-costing reports and QuickBooks Online are part of the Plus-and-higher conversation. A 5-person team can model Plus at $166/mo annual billing. A 12-person company can model Pro at $221/mo annual billing. A larger team can model Unlimited at $332/mo annual billing. That matters for small contractors that want to avoid a quote-only process.</p>

<p>AccuLynx's official plan-options page places job costs and A/R tracking, job profit forecasting, and profit and loss analysis in higher plan tiers rather than the Essential-only entry story. That doesn't make AccuLynx weak financially. It means roofing companies should not assume the $250/mo Essential plan includes the reporting depth they want. If profitability reporting is a major purchase reason, confirm the exact plan in writing.</p>

<h3>Mobile and field adoption</h3>

<p>Contractor Foreman lists apps plus a web panel, no device limit, time cards, documents, photos, forms, and field records. The risk is setup discipline. A broad PM system only works if the company decides which records matter, who owns templates, and what the field team must enter every day.</p>

<p>AccuLynx lists a mobile roofing app for iOS and Android, but user sentiment in existing CSH source material has been more cautious: the desktop workflow is where AccuLynx feels strongest, while mobile-first teams often complain about friction. That does not disqualify AccuLynx for roofers, but it changes the trial plan. Put sales reps, production managers, and field users into the demo with real jobs, not just office admins.</p>

## The real buying scenarios

<h3>Scenario 1: small GC wants one operating system</h3>

<p>Choose Contractor Foreman. If the company manages estimates in one place, schedules in another, daily logs in texts, documents in cloud folders, photos on phones, invoices in accounting software, and RFIs in email, AccuLynx isn't the natural fix. Contractor Foreman's broad construction workflow is built for that cross-trade job administration problem.</p>

<p>Budget against Plus or Pro, not Basic. Use the 30-day trial to build one actual job, make an estimate, schedule work, enter a daily log, capture time, attach photos, create a change order, check QuickBooks Online workflow, and run the report you wish you had today.</p>

<h3>Scenario 2: established roofer has supplier and production friction</h3>

<p>Choose AccuLynx. If the business orders through roofing suppliers, uses aerial reports, manages crews through production stages, tracks supplements, and needs job documents tied to the full exterior workflow, Contractor Foreman will feel too general. AccuLynx is built around that operating pattern.</p>

<p>The demo should focus on the work that makes AccuLynx worth the premium: supplier direct ordering, measurement import, estimate-to-order handoff, production visibility, documents, job costs, and reporting. If those workflows are not central to the company, AccuLynx can become expensive overhead.</p>

<h3>Scenario 3: mixed trade contractor does roofing plus remodels</h3>

<p>This is the hard case. A company that does roofing, siding, gutters, remodeling, and general construction may not love either answer. Contractor Foreman is more flexible across trades. AccuLynx is deeper for roofing. The right choice depends on the revenue center and the workflow pain.</p>

<p>If roofing production drives the profit, AccuLynx may justify its focus. If roofing is one service line and the bigger pain is coordinating many project types, Contractor Foreman is the safer first test. Do not buy AccuLynx for the whole company just because the roofing team likes the supplier workflow, and do not buy Contractor Foreman for roofing if the missing piece is direct supplier ordering.</p>

<h3>Scenario 4: owner wants transparent pricing before a call</h3>

<p>Choose Contractor Foreman first. Its pricing page lets you model user caps and annual cost before sales gets involved. AccuLynx is clearer than it used to be because Essential is published at $250/mo, but Pro and Elite still require a custom quote. If your team needs the deeper AccuLynx tier, the budget cannot be finalized from the public page alone.</p>

## Where each product falls short

<h3>Contractor Foreman limitations</h3>

<ul>
<li><strong>Basic is not the real team benchmark.</strong> The $49/mo entry price is a valid 1-user plan, but most teams should compare Plus, Pro, or Unlimited.</li>
<li><strong>It is broad, so setup matters.</strong> A platform covering schedules, logs, invoices, documents, reports, time cards, and job costs can become messy without internal process ownership.</li>
<li><strong>Roofing supplier depth is not the point.</strong> Contractor Foreman can manage purchasing and job records, but it is not built around ABC Supply, SRS, QXO, and aerial measurement workflow the way AccuLynx is.</li>
<li><strong>Feature gates can change the value story.</strong> Confirm exactly which tier includes scheduling, QuickBooks Online, job-cost reports, takeoffs, client portal, and offline time-card needs.</li>
</ul>

<h3>AccuLynx limitations</h3>

<ul>
<li><strong>It is roofing-first.</strong> That is valuable for roofers and exterior contractors, but it can be too narrow for mixed-trade or general contracting workflows.</li>
<li><strong>Higher-tier pricing is opaque.</strong> Essential is published at $250/mo, while Pro and Elite require a quote. Buyers need written details on users, add-ons, setup, reporting, and renewal terms.</li>
<li><strong>Implementation is heavier.</strong> Supplier ordering, production workflow, measurements, documents, and financial controls require training and process decisions.</li>
<li><strong>Mobile fit must be tested.</strong> Roofing teams that live on phones should validate the mobile app with sales reps, production managers, and field users before signing.</li>
</ul>

## Recommendation

<p><strong>Choose Contractor Foreman</strong> if you need affordable cross-trade construction management and can fit the published tier model. It is the better first test for contractors trying to replace scattered admin with one job record: estimates, schedules, logs, documents, invoices, time, reports, client records, and accounting workflow.</p>

<p><strong>Choose AccuLynx</strong> if roofing is the engine of the company and supplier ordering, aerial measurements, production workflow, and job-file control are the problems you are paying to solve. The $250/mo Essential plan gives a public starting point, but established roofers should expect to compare Pro or Elite with a written quote.</p>

<p><strong>Do not choose based only on price.</strong> Contractor Foreman will usually look cheaper. AccuLynx will usually look more specialized. The right choice depends on whether your pain is broad construction administration or roofing production depth.</p>
