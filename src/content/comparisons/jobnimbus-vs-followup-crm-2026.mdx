<p>JobNimbus and FollowUp CRM are both real construction CRMs, but they solve different problems. JobNimbus is built for roofing and exterior contractors that need field production workflow, supplier ordering, and mobile photo capture. FollowUp CRM is built for general contractors and pre-construction teams that need bid tracking, sales follow-up, and opportunity management. The right choice depends on whether the bigger pain point is field workflow or sales pipeline.</p>

## Buying split: field workflow vs sales pipeline

A roofing or exterior contractor should start with JobNimbus because the software is built around the messy middle of restoration work. Real jobs sit in statuses like adjuster scheduled, supplement pending, materials ordered, install ready, and final invoice. JobNimbus's Kanban boards model those stages without forcing everything into a generic sales pipeline.

A general contractor or pre-construction sales team should start with FollowUp CRM when the main daily work is tracking bids from opportunity through award, managing follow-up sequences, and nurturing leads before the job ever starts. FollowUp CRM specializes in that narrower workflow.

Do not assume one tool can easily substitute for the other. JobNimbus has CRM features, but its deepest value is in field production, not bid tracking. FollowUp CRM has job management features, but its deepest value is in pre-construction sales, not material ordering or photo documentation.

## Pricing reality and contract questions

JobNimbus does not publish exact dollar prices on its current pricing page. The public plans are Essentials, Pro, Premium, and Enterprise, each with request-pricing CTAs. The 14-day free trial is advertised separately. Treat the trial as a product-fit test; the fully loaded cost still requires a written quote by plan, users, add-ons, support, and implementation.

FollowUp CRM pricing is less transparent in public sources. One search result cites a starting price of roughly $180 per month with an annual commitment. Higher tiers require custom quotes. Because public pricing is limited, contractors should treat the $180/mo figure as a starting anchor and confirm exact tiers, user counts, contract length, and renewal terms directly with sales.

In both cases, compare the fully loaded quote: users, add-ons, implementation, support, integrations, and renewal language. Neither product is a simple low-price pick.

## Workflow fit: field production vs bid tracking

JobNimbus makes sense when the daily workflow includes job photos, material orders, measurement reports, and production scheduling. The mobile app lets crews capture GPS-tagged photos in the field and attach them directly to job records. That matters for insurance claim support, site condition documentation, and homeowner approval.

FollowUp CRM makes sense when the daily workflow is opportunity creation, bid tracking, follow-up sequences, and pre-award lead nurturing. The pipeline is built around construction sales stages, not field production. If the team spends more time in the office tracking bids than on job sites updating statuses, FollowUp CRM's specialization is the better fit.

## Mobile and field adoption

JobNimbus has the stronger mobile case by design. GPS-tagged photos, field job updates, and board movement from the job site are central to the product. The iOS and Android apps hold strong ratings across thousands of reviews.

FollowUp CRM is more office-centered. Mobile access exists, but the product is built around desktop bid tracking and sales automation. For field-first teams, this is a meaningful gap.

## Where JobNimbus wins

### Flexible field workflow

JobNimbus's Kanban boards let roofing and exterior contractors model real job stages. Custom statuses match how restoration work actually flows: lead, inspection, estimate, adjuster scheduled, supplement pending, approved, materials ordered, install ready, completed, final invoice. For teams moving 20–50+ active jobs through those stages, the board view prevents jobs from falling through the cracks.

### Supplier and measurement integrations

JobNimbus lists supplier integrations including Beacon PRO+, Roof Hub by SRS Distribution, and ABC Supply. EagleView and HOVER measurement workflows are documented. Those connections create a tighter path between inspection, estimate, material order, and production schedule. For high-volume roofing companies, that integration depth is a real time saver.

### Trial-first evaluation

The 14-day no-card trial lets teams test real jobs, custom stages, photo capture, and mobile updates before committing. That matters because CRM adoption is hard to judge from a demo alone.

## Where FollowUp CRM wins

### Bid tracking specialization

FollowUp CRM is built around construction bid pipelines. Opportunity creation, bid deadlines, follow-up reminders, and pre-award nurture sequences are the core workflow. For contractors whose revenue depends on winning bids rather than managing production, that focus is an advantage over general-purpose CRMs.

### Sales automation

FollowUp CRM includes sales follow-up automation, email sequences, and contact management tuned for pre-construction teams. If the team is losing bids because follow-up falls through the cracks, the automated reminders and pipeline visibility can make a measurable difference.

### Integration with estimating tools

FollowUp CRM is designed to integrate with estimating tools, so bid creation and opportunity tracking can stay connected. For estimators who want the sales pipeline tied to the bid spreadsheet, that integration is more natural than forcing a field-service CRM into a sales workflow.

## Where each product is the wrong fit

JobNimbus is the wrong fit for a pure sales team that never visits job sites. The supplier integrations, measurement tools, and photo workflows are wasted if the team's work is 100% bid tracking and office sales.

FollowUp CRM is the wrong fit for a roofing or exterior crew that needs material ordering, field photo capture, and production scheduling. The bid pipeline is valuable, but it does not replace field workflow tools.

Both products can be wrong for a contractor that needs a full construction management suite. For residential new construction or whole-home remodeling, compare Buildertrend. For mixed-trade service work, compare Jobber or ServiceTitan.

## Evaluation plan for a fair side-by-side test

Run a five-job test. For JobNimbus, import a few test contacts, build sample stages, create an estimate, attach photos from a phone, assign a task, and move a job through the board. Then ask sales for a written quote matching exact users, boards, automations, integrations, and support level.

For FollowUp CRM, create a few test opportunities, build a bid pipeline, set follow-up reminders, and run a sample lead through the stages. Confirm how estimating tool integrations work and how follow-up automation triggers.

The winner is the system the actual team will update without being chased. If one product creates cleaner records but the crew avoids it, the software will fail.

## Alternatives to keep on the shortlist

If JobNimbus feels too heavy for a small roofing crew, compare Jobber for lighter mixed-service workflow. If FollowUp CRM feels too narrow, compare a general-purpose construction CRM. If the company does roofing plus remodeling or custom construction, compare Buildertrend before making the final call.

## FAQ

JobNimbus is the better fit for a roofing company because it is built around field production, insurance restoration stages, supplier ordering, and mobile photo capture. FollowUp CRM is not designed for job-site workflow.

### Which is cheaper, JobNimbus or FollowUp CRM?

Do not judge either product by a headline number. JobNimbus requires a quote after the trial. FollowUp CRM lists a starting price around $180/mo with an annual commitment, but higher tiers are custom. In both cases, get a fully loaded quote.

### Does FollowUp CRM work for field crews?

FollowUp CRM is primarily a desktop sales and bid-tracking tool. Mobile access exists, but the product is not built around field photo capture, job-site updates, or material ordering.

Yes. JobNimbus advertises a 14-day free trial with no credit card required. Use it to test real job stages, photo workflow, and mobile behavior before requesting pricing.
