# ArboStar vs Jobber (2026): Tree Care vs General Field Service Compared

<p>Jobber and ArboStar serve different contractor profiles. Jobber is the generalist field service platform for small-to-mid residential service crews that need scheduling, quoting, invoicing, and client communication at a transparent price. ArboStar is the tree-care specialist built for arborist companies that need arborist CRM records, live job maps, equipment tracking, safety documentation, and field notes specific to tree work. The right choice depends on whether the business runs general service calls or complex tree crew operations. See our <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber review</a> and <a href="/arbostar-review-2026/">ArboStar review</a>.</p>

## Buying split: crew type and operational complexity

The primary question is not how many technicians you have. It is whether your work is general service calls or tree-specific operations.

Jobber makes more sense when the daily work is HVAC tune-ups, plumbing repairs, electrical service calls, landscaping maintenance, or cleaning appointments. The drag-and-drop calendar, quote-to-invoice workflow, client hub, and mobile app are built for the residential service cycle: appointment scheduled, job done, invoice sent. Nothing about that workflow requires tree-care specialization.

ArboStar makes more sense when the crew dispatches to tree removals, pruning jobs, stump grinding, and commercial arborist work. The arborist CRM records client tree history, the live job maps help route chipper trucks and bucket trucks, the equipment tracking manages chainsaws, chippers, and rigging gear, and the field documentation captures safety forms and job photos specific to tree work.

Use a simple test. If the crew handles mostly service calls that start and finish in a few hours, start with Jobber. If the crew manages equipment-heavy tree jobs across multiple sites with different crew compositions per job, start with ArboStar.

## Pricing reality and contract questions

Jobber publishes four clear plan tiers. Core starts at $29 per month billed annually or $49 month-to-month for one user. Connect starts at $99 per month annual for one user or $149 per month annual for the 5-user team version. Grow starts at $149 per month annual for one user or $299 per month annual for the 10-user team version. Plus starts at $529 per month annual with 15 users included. Additional users are $29 per month each. The 14-day free trial gives access to Grow plan features and requires no credit card. That transparency is the main pricing advantage for small teams.

ArboStar uses custom pricing with a starting point around $250 per month on its official pricing page, but requires a sales conversation for the final quote. The final price depends on company size, license count, required features, integrations, and platform usage. Annual billing offers two months free. Because ArboStar requires a sales conversation to get a real price, it is harder to budget for before the demo.

## Workflow fit: general service vs tree-specific operations

### Scheduling and dispatch

Jobber's drag-and-drop calendar, automated reminders, and mobile job updates are built for the service call cycle. For HVAC, plumbing, or electrical companies where the daily challenge is getting the right tech to the right appointment on time, Jobber's scheduling is clean and effective. It handles one-crew-one-job dispatch well.

ArboStar's scheduling goes deeper for tree crews. Live job maps, GPS visibility, route planning, and equipment assignment matter when crews need a chipper, bucket truck, and specific tools to arrive together at the right site. Tree jobs are not interchangeable service calls. The equipment needs, crew composition, and site conditions change per job. ArboStar's map and dispatch features reflect that reality.

### Customer management

Jobber's client hub gives customers a place to approve quotes, review appointments, and pay online. That is useful for any service business. But it does not track tree-specific customer history beyond what a general CRM provides.

ArboStar's arborist CRM records client tree history, past work, equipment notes, and service records specific to tree care. For a company that returns to the same properties for pruning, removals, plant health, and storm cleanup, that history matters. A salesperson can see prior work, and a crew can see photos and notes from the last visit without digging through files.

### Mobile field tools

Both products have mobile apps. Jobber's mobile app is consistently praised for its simplicity: techs can view schedules, clock in and out, add notes, upload photos, and collect payment. Limited offline support exists for timers, forms, notes, and attachments.

ArboStar's mobile app covers job details, notes, photos, schedule updates, estimates, invoices, and real-time job status. For tree crews, the difference is what the mobile app connects to: equipment assignment, crew details, safety forms, and map-based job context that goes beyond an address and a service note.

## Where Jobber wins

### Transparent pricing and fast setup

Jobber's public pricing and 14-day free trial let small teams test the product before committing. That matters because service contractors often need software quickly. Jobber can be running within days, not weeks.

### General service scheduling and client communication

The drag-and-drop calendar, automated reminders, and client hub are built for the residential service workflow. For companies where the biggest daily problem is missed appointments and billing delays, Jobber solves those problems cleanly.

### Lower entry cost for small crews

At $29 per month for Core, Jobber is accessible for solo operators and small crews. Even Connect at $99 per month for a team with client hub and QuickBooks sync is well below ArboStar's starting point.

### Strong mobile app for field techs

Jobber's mobile app gets consistent positive feedback across thousands of reviews. Techs actually use it, which is the real test. If the app does not stick with the crew, the office still works around paper or texts.

## Where ArboStar wins

### Tree-specific CRM and service history

ArboStar's arborist CRM is built for tree-care customer management. Contacts, leads, quotes, job history, communication records, and service history are all tree-work aware. For arborist companies managing hundreds of client properties, that depth matters.

### Live job maps and equipment tracking

Tree work depends on more than an address and appointment time. The right crew, truck, chipper, equipment, access notes, and weather affect the day. ArboStar's map and GPS positioning give managers a clearer view of where work is happening and which resources are tied up. Equipment tracking covers maintenance schedules, fuel costs, and GPS-linked assets.

### Field documentation for arborist work

Safety forms, hazard notes, job photos, and crew instructions are central to tree work in a way they are not for general service calls. ArboStar's mobile workflow supports that documentation attached to specific tree jobs.

### Estimating with field context

ArboStar's estimating workflow includes pre-entered rates, work types, job size, equipment needs, crew composition, photos, notes, and digital approval. For tree proposals that depend on site conditions, that detail can reduce the gap between what sales promised and what the crew finds on site.

## Where each product is the wrong fit

Jobber is the wrong fit for a multi-crew tree care company that needs live job maps, equipment tracking, arborist CRM records, and field documentation specific to tree work. The generalist scheduling and client hub will not replace the tree-specific workflows that ArboStar provides.

ArboStar is the wrong fit for a solo HVAC technician, a plumbing crew doing service calls, or a landscaping company doing basic maintenance. The custom pricing, tree-specific features, and implementation effort are wasted on operations that just need scheduling and invoicing.

Both products can be wrong for a general contractor or construction remodeler that needs phase-based project management. Those buyers should compare Buildertrend or Knowify instead.

## Evaluation plan for a fair side-by-side test

Run a one-week test with real jobs. For Jobber, build the real schedule, create a sample quote, send it to a test customer, convert it to a job, and have a tech run a full day through the mobile app. If tree-specific workflows like equipment tracking and field documentation are on your list, note whether Jobber's general approach is acceptable or whether you need more.

For ArboStar, ask the demo team to walk through a real day with multiple crews, a chipper, a bucket truck, and a late emergency call. Confirm that the live map, equipment tracking, and arborist CRM actually reduce office-to-field friction. Get the full quoted price including license count, integrations, implementation, and support.

The winner is the system that fits the type of work the business actually does. If the work is general service calls, Jobber is the right starting point. If the work is tree care with multiple crews and equipment-heavy days, ArboStar's specialization is worth the higher cost and longer setup.

## FAQ

### Is Jobber or ArboStar better for a small tree care company?

Jobber is the lower-cost starting point for a very small tree crew that mainly needs scheduling, quotes, and invoices. ArboStar becomes worth the price when the company has multiple crews, equipment tracking needs, and a desire to capture tree-specific service history.

### Which is cheaper, Jobber or ArboStar?

Jobber starts at $29 per month and publishes clear plan tiers. ArboStar starts from $250 per month with a custom quote. For any crew size, Jobber is less expensive at the entry level.

### Does ArboStar offer a free trial?

ArboStar offers a free demo rather than a self-service trial. Jobber offers a 14-day free trial with no credit card required.

### Can Jobber handle tree care scheduling?

Jobber can schedule tree service jobs like any service call. It does not offer tree-specific features like live job maps, equipment tracking, or arborist CRM records. For basic tree company scheduling, Jobber works. For multi-crew tree operations with complex equipment needs, ArboStar is the better fit.

### Does ArboStar work for general field service (HVAC, plumbing, electrical)?

ArboStar is built for tree care and arborist operations. A general HVAC or plumbing company would likely find it overbuilt and paying for tree-specific features they will not use. Jobber is the better fit for general field service.

### Can I use Jobber and add tree-care features later?

Jobber does not offer tree-specific add-ons or modules. The platform is a general field service tool. If your tree business grows into needing live maps, equipment tracking, and arborist CRM, you would migrate to ArboStar or SingleOps when that need becomes pressing.
