# MailerLite vs Moosend for Contractor Email Marketing

In this MailerLite vs Moosend comparison, both are affordable email marketing platforms built for small businesses and growing lists. They overlap on the basics: email campaigns, automation workflows, landing pages, forms, segmentation, and reporting. The differences are in pricing structure, free plan availability, trial length, and how each tool handles send volume.

That matters for contractors. A small roofing company with 200 past customers needs a different tool than a plumbing company with 2,000 newsletter subscribers. The right choice comes down to three questions: Do you need a free plan? Do you want unlimited emails? Do you prefer pay-as-you-go or a monthly subscription?

## Quick verdict

Pick MailerLite if you want a free plan you can use indefinitely. The official MailerLite pricing page lists a free plan at $0 for up to 250 subscribers and 2,500 monthly emails, with 2 user seats and access to the drag-and-drop editor, forms, landing pages, and basic automations. Paid plans start with Comfort at $12 per month or $10.80 per month billed yearly, and Power at $25 per month or $22.50 per month billed yearly. The Power plan includes unlimited monthly emails, unlimited automations, and unlimited user seats.

Pick Moosend if you want the lowest paid Pro entry point. The official Moosend pricing page shows Pro at $7 per month when paid annually or $9 per month on monthly billing for 500 contacts. The Pro plan includes unlimited email campaigns, marketing automation workflows, subscription forms, landing pages, reporting, and SMTP. Moosend does not offer a free plan, but the 30-day free trial requires no credit card and includes full Pro feature access for up to 1,000 contacts.

Skip both if the main problem is field operations. Neither MailerLite nor Moosend replaces [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), [ServiceTitan](/servicetitan-review/), or broader [field service software](/best-field-service-software/). Both tools help contractors communicate with leads and customers through email. They do not dispatch crews, route technicians, manage job costing, or run field workflows.

## When MailerLite makes more sense

MailerLite makes more sense when the contractor wants a free plan that lasts. Many small contractor businesses have fewer than 250 past customers, leads, and newsletter subscribers combined. For those companies, MailerLite's free plan is not a trial. It is a permanent tool.

A contractor could use the free plan to:

- send a monthly newsletter to past customers
- run a seasonal reactivation campaign before busy season
- collect leads through a website form
- build a simple landing page for one service area
- send quote follow-up emails manually

The free plan has limits: 2,500 monthly emails, 1 landing page, 3 active forms, and 3 active automations. But for a small list, that may be enough. When the list grows past 250 subscribers, Comfort at $10.80 per month billed yearly gives access to unlimited templates, AI writing assistant, dynamic emails, A/B testing, and up to 50 active automations.

The Power plan at $22.50 per month billed yearly is where MailerLite differentiates on volume. It includes unlimited monthly emails, unlimited automations, unlimited forms, unlimited landing pages, and unlimited user seats. That matters when the list grows and sending frequency increases. No need to monitor email caps.

MailerLite also stands out on built-in monetization. The platform supports selling digital products, paid newsletter subscriptions, and appointment bookings directly. A contractor who runs a paid consultation or sells a digital guide can do that inside MailerLite without a separate e-commerce tool.

## When Moosend makes more sense

Moosend makes more sense when the contractor wants the lowest paid monthly price. At $7 per month billed annually, Moosend Pro is cheaper than MailerLite Comfort ($10.80) and MailerLite Power ($22.50). The Pro plan includes unlimited email campaigns and sends from the start, so there is no need to upgrade tiers just to send more emails at the same subscriber count.

A contractor could use Moosend Pro to:

- send unlimited email campaigns to up to 500 contacts
- build landing pages and subscription forms
- run marketing automation workflows
- segment contacts by service interest, location, or customer type
- review campaign reporting and A/B test results
- send transactional emails through SMTP

Moosend also offers something MailerLite does not: pay-as-you-go email credits. For contractors who send emails seasonally or infrequently, credits start at $350 for 350,000 emails and never expire. That pricing model avoids a monthly subscription when the business only sends a few large campaigns per year. A roofing company that runs one storm-season blast and one spring inspection reminder could find credits cheaper than 12 months of any subscription.

The 30-day free trial is longer than MailerLite's 14-day trial and requires no credit card. That gives a contractor more time to test automations, templates, and campaign workflows before paying. The trial includes full Pro feature access for up to 1,000 contacts, so the testing period is not feature-limited.

## Pricing comparison

Both tools are affordable, but the pricing structures differ in ways that matter for contractor budgets.

Paid plans for up to 500 subscribers start with Comfort at $12 per month, or $10.80 per month billed yearly. Atlas-verified Moosend pricing lists Pro at $7 per month when paid annually or $9 per month on monthly billing for 500 contacts. The Pro plan includes unlimited email campaigns and sends. Moosend+ and Enterprise are custom-quote plans. Pay-as-you-go email credits are published separately: 350,000 credits for $350, 500,000 for $500, 750,000 for $750, and 1,000,000 for $1,000.

## Email marketing and automation

Both platforms cover the same email marketing fundamentals: drag-and-drop editors, templates, segmentation, A/B testing, and automation workflows. The differences are in depth and approach.

MailerLite's automation covers welcome sequences, tag-based triggers, time delays, and workflow branching. The free plan allows 3 active automations, Comfort allows 50, and Power allows unlimited. That tiered approach means a contractor starts with basic sequences and expands as the list and needs grow. The drag-and-drop editor is clean and produces mobile-friendly emails quickly. MailerLite also offers a subscriber preference center, smart sending, and dynamic emails.

Moosend's automation is included in full from the Pro plan. There is no tiered limit on published automation workflows. The official feature pages describe a visual workflow builder, pre-built automation templates, multi-step workflows, behavioral triggers (page views, product views, purchases), and audience discovery. For contractors, that means welcome sequences, quote follow-up paths, and reactivation campaigns can all run without worrying about an automation count limit.

The decision signal: choose MailerLite when the automation needs are straightforward and the tiered approach keeps costs low. Choose Moosend when the contractor wants unlimited automations from the Pro plan and behavioral triggers tied to website activity.

## Free plan vs free trial

This is the biggest structural difference between the two platforms.

MailerLite offers a genuine free plan. It is not a trial. A contractor with 200 past customers and leads can use MailerLite indefinitely at $0, sending up to 2,500 emails per month. The free plan includes the drag-and-drop editor, a landing page, forms, basic automations, and subscriber management. The limitation is volume: 250 subscribers, 2,500 emails, and capped feature counts on forms, automations, and landing pages.

Moosend does not offer a free plan. The 30-day free trial gives full Pro access for up to 1,000 contacts with no credit card required, but it expires. After 30 days, the contractor must move to a paid Pro plan, Moosend+, Enterprise, or pay-as-you-go credits.

For a cost-conscious contractor, the math is simple. If the list is under 250 subscribers and 2,500 monthly emails, MailerLite is free forever. Once the list exceeds that limit, Moosend Pro at $7 per month annual is cheaper than MailerLite Comfort at $10.80 per month annual.

The crossover point matters. A contractor at 300 subscribers pays $0 on MailerLite's free plan (if under 250 subscribers, though above that requires a paid plan) or $7 per month on Moosend. At 500 subscribers, the comparison is MailerLite Comfort at $10.80 versus Moosend Pro at $7, both billed annually. Moosend is cheaper per month, but MailerLite includes AI writing assistant, dynamic emails, and a subscriber preference center that Moosend does not match at the same tier.

## Integrations and ecosystem

MailerLite lists 100+ integrations on its official pages, including Zapier, Make, WordPress, Shopify, WooCommerce, and AI tools like Claude and ChatGPT. That ecosystem matters when the contractor needs to connect email marketing to a website, a store, a form builder, or an automation platform. MailerLite also supports paid newsletter subscriptions, digital product sales, and appointment bookings directly within the platform.

Moosend lists 80+ integrations, including WordPress, Shopify, WooCommerce, Zapier, and CMS platforms. The integration library is smaller than MailerLite's, and the community of third-party tutorials and templates is also smaller. Moosend does support API access, SMTP, and transactional email on Pro, which gives developers more flexibility than MailerLite's API (which is read-only on the free plan and sending-enabled on paid plans).

For most contractors, both integration libraries cover the basics: WordPress, Shopify, Zapier, and forms. The difference matters when the contractor relies on a specific integration or wants AI-assisted content creation tools built into the platform.

## Landing pages and forms

Both platforms include landing page builders and form creation. MailerLite's free plan allows 1 landing page and 3 active forms. Comfort expands to 10 websites and landing pages and 10 signup forms. Power removes limits entirely.

Moosend's free trial allows 1 unpublished landing page and 1 unpublished subscription form. The Pro plan includes unlimited subscription forms and landing pages.

For a contractor building a simple landing page for a neighborhood campaign or a service-specific lead capture form, both tools handle the job. MailerLite has a slight edge on the free plan because a contractor can publish 1 landing page and 3 forms without paying. Moosend requires a Pro subscription to publish forms and landing pages.

## Setup effort and ownership

Both tools are designed for self-service signup. Neither requires implementation services, onboarding fees, or a sales call. That makes both lighter to start than a full CRM like Keap or a field service platform like ServiceTitan.

MailerLite's free plan lets a contractor test the platform with a real list before committing to a paid tier. The setup questions are list hygiene, consent, template selection, automation triggers, and campaign timing. One person can usually own MailerLite as a marketing layer.

Moosend's 30-day trial gives a contractor full Pro access to test every feature. The setup questions are the same: list import, consent, segments, forms, landing pages, automation paths, and campaign cadence. The longer trial period gives more time to build and test before paying.

The failure mode is identical for both: the tool sits unused because nobody writes campaigns, cleans the list, reviews reports, or builds the next follow-up sequence. Buying the cheaper tool does not solve the ownership problem.

## Contractor operations fit

Neither MailerLite nor Moosend should be sold internally as job management software. Both help contractors communicate with leads and customers through email campaigns, forms, landing pages, automations, and reporting. Neither runs estimates, schedules, jobs, invoices, routes, crews, or field production.

A common stack could be: field service software for jobs and scheduling, plus MailerLite or Moosend for email follow-up. ## Where MailerLite falls short

MailerLite's biggest drawback is the Comfort tier's email cap. At $10.80 per month billed yearly, the Comfort plan limits emails to 10 times the subscriber tier ceiling. For a contractor at 500 subscribers, that means 5,000 emails per month. If the contractor sends frequently or runs multiple campaigns, they need to upgrade to Power at $22.50 per month for unlimited emails. That is still affordable, but it is more than Moosend Pro at $7 per month, which includes unlimited sends from the start.

The second drawback is no pay-as-you-go option. Contractors who send emails only a few times per year cannot avoid a monthly or annual subscription. Moosend's credit system is better for occasional senders.

The third drawback is automation limits on lower tiers. The free plan allows only 3 active automations. Comfort allows 50. Power is unlimited. A contractor who needs multiple parallel workflows from day one either pays for Power or works within the cap. Moosend Pro offers unlimited published automations from the start.

## Where Moosend falls short

Moosend's biggest limitation is the lack of a free plan. A contractor with 150 past customers and a small newsletter list has to pay $7 per month on Moosend or $0 on MailerLite. For very small lists, MailerLite's free plan is a genuine advantage that Moosend cannot match.

The second limitation is integration depth. Moosend lists 80+ integrations compared to MailerLite's 100+. The gap is not huge, but it matters when the contractor relies on a specific tool or platform. Moosend's smaller user community also means fewer third-party tutorials, templates, and resources.

The third limitation is subscriber counting. Moosend's Pro plan starts at 500 contacts, and pricing scales as the contact count grows. MailerLite's subscriber counting is also tiered, but the free plan gives more room for a small list to grow before hitting paid territory.

## How to choose

Start with the list size and sending frequency.

If the list is under 250 subscribers and 2,500 monthly emails, MailerLite is free. Start there. The free plan is not a trial. It is a permanent tool for a small list.

If the list is over 250 subscribers, compare the monthly prices. Moosend Pro at $7 per month annual is cheaper than MailerLite Comfort at $10.80 per month annual. But check what each plan includes. MailerLite Comfort has AI writing assistant, dynamic emails, and A/B testing. Moosend Pro has unlimited automations, SMTP, and transactional email. Match the features to the actual need.

If the contractor sends emails only a few times per year, Moosend's pay-as-you-go credits may be cheaper than any monthly subscription. Calculate the total annual cost: 4 campaigns at 50,000 emails each would cost $350 in credits versus $84 per year on Moosend Pro or $129.60 per year on MailerLite Comfort.

If both the free plan and the lowest price matter, the decision comes down to how fast the list is growing. A contractor who expects to stay under 250 subscribers for the foreseeable future should pick MailerLite. A contractor who expects to pass 500 quickly should pick Moosend for the lower per-month price at scale.

## Final verdict

MailerLite is the stronger choice for contractors who want a free plan they can use indefinitely and unlimited emails on the Power tier. It makes the most sense when the list is small, the budget is tight, and the contractor values permanent free access over the lowest paid price. The built-in monetization features and larger integration library add value for contractors who want to grow beyond simple newsletters.

Moosend is the stronger choice for contractors who want the lowest monthly Pro price and pay-as-you-go flexibility. It makes the most sense when the list has outgrown free-plan limits, the contractor sends emails regularly but wants unlimited sends from the start, or the business sends seasonally and prefers credits over subscriptions. The 30-day trial with no credit card gives enough time to test before committing.

My practical call: choose MailerLite if your list is small enough for the free plan or if unlimited emails on Power matter. Choose Moosend if you want the lowest monthly Pro price, a longer trial, or pay-as-you-go credits for occasional sending.
