# Freshsales vs GetResponse for Contractor Follow-Up

Freshsales and GetResponse both help contractors follow up with leads, but they solve different follow-up problems. Freshsales is a sales CRM built around deals, pipelines, phone calling, and lead scoring. GetResponse is an email marketing and automation platform built around campaigns, landing pages, forms, webinars, and customer nurture.

That difference matters because a contractor can say "we need better follow-up" and mean two very different things. One office has leads arriving but nobody knows who owns the next call, what stage each deal is in, or when to follow up. Another office has a list of past customers, old estimates, seasonal leads, and website form submissions that never receive useful emails. The first problem points toward Freshsales. The second points toward GetResponse.

## Quick verdict

Pick Freshsales if the contractor office needs a sales CRM that works at no cost. The official Freshsales pricing page confirms a free plan for up to 3 users with Kanban views, email templates, built-in phone, and live chat. The Pro tier adds Freddy AI contact scoring, sales emails, deal insights, multiple sales pipelines, sequences, and advanced workflows. For contractors, that covers the essential sales workflow: capture leads, score them, call the hot ones, track them through a pipeline, and follow up.

Pick GetResponse if the contractor office needs a marketing layer. The official GetResponse pricing page lists email marketing, autoresponders, visual automation builder, landing pages, signup forms, popups, audience segmentation, newsletters, web push, ecommerce tools, webinars, websites, and AI content generators. For contractors, that maps to old-estimate nurture, seasonal service reminders, past-customer reactivation, maintenance campaigns, neighborhood landing pages, and educational webinar follow-up.

Skip both if the real problem is field production. Neither product replaces [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), [ServiceTitan](/servicetitan-review/), or a broader [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) platform. Freshsales and GetResponse help before and around the sale. They do not schedule crews, dispatch technicians, track materials, manage job costing, build routes, or run field invoices by themselves.

## When Freshsales makes more sense

Freshsales makes more sense when the contractor has a sales follow-up problem. Leads arrive, but nobody owns the next call. Quotes go out, but follow-up depends on memory. Open opportunities sit in email inboxes and notebooks instead of a shared pipeline.

Freshsales puts that work at the center of the product. The official pricing page describes the free plan with built-in phone at 200 minutes per month, Kanban views for contacts, accounts, and deals, email templates, and live chat. The Growth tier adds contact lifecycle stages, basic workflows, contextual collaboration with Slack, product catalog, curated reports, and Freshworks Marketplace access. The Pro tier adds Freddy AI contact scoring, sales emails, deal insights, sales sequences, multiple pipelines, territory management, and advanced workflows.

For a contractor, that means the sales workflow can look like this:

- New lead enters the pipeline
- Contacted by phone (200 minutes included on the free tier)
- Estimate scheduled
- Quote sent
- Follow-up due
- Negotiation
- Won or Lost

Every open opportunity can have an owner, contact, company, stage, value, notes, email history, and next activity. That makes Freshsales a better fit for contractors with sales reps, estimators, or longer quote cycles who need to know what happened with each lead this week.

The free tier is the strongest selling point. According to the official Freshworks pricing page, the free plan includes:

- Up to 3 users at no cost
- Built-in phone with 200 minutes per month
- AI-based lead scoring through Freddy AI (confirmed on Pro tier)
- Visual Kanban views for contacts, accounts, and deals
- Email templates and live chat
- Contact and account management

The interface is modern and clean, with a lower learning curve than most free CRMs, which is why it ranked as the top free CRM in our [best free CRM for contractors](/best-free-crm-for-contractors/) roundup. For a head-to-head against another free CRM, see our [Freshsales vs Zoho CRM](/freshsales-vs-zoho-crm/) comparison.

Freshsales is strongest when the buyer needs a source of truth for open deals. It is weaker when the buyer needs newsletters, landing pages, webinars, signup forms, and broader marketing campaigns. A sales pipeline and a marketing contact list are not the same object.

## When GetResponse makes more sense

GetResponse makes more sense when the contractor has a communication problem. The business may already have names and emails from website quote forms, inspection requests, trade shows, financing conversations, old estimates, past customers, referral partners, or seasonal maintenance reminders. The issue is that those contacts sit idle because nobody has a reliable way to send useful campaigns.

That is where GetResponse fits. The official feature and pricing pages describe email marketing, autoresponders, visual marketing automation, landing pages, forms, popups, segmentation, newsletters, web push, ecommerce automation, webinars, websites, and AI content generators. In contractor terms, those features support practical follow-up work:

- send a quote-request sequence after a form submission
- build a landing page for one service, neighborhood, or seasonal offer
- remind past customers about maintenance, inspections, upgrades, or warranties
- reactivate old estimates before the busy season
- segment contacts by trade, location, service interest, lead source, or customer type
- invite leads or past customers to an educational webinar
- track campaign opens, clicks, and responses before the next send

GetResponse also includes a free account path after the 14-day trial. The official pricing page lists four paid plans at the 1,000-contact level: Starter at $15.58 per month annually or $19 per month monthly, Marketer at $48.38 per month annually or $59 per month monthly, Creator at $56.58 per month annually or $69 per month monthly, and Enterprise with custom pricing.

The Marketer tier adds unlimited automation workflows, advanced audience segmentation, contact scoring, abandoned cart recovery, sales funnels, and promo codes. The Creator tier adds webinars (up to 100 attendees), a website builder, a course creator, and premium newsletter subscriptions. Enterprise adds unlimited users, webinars up to 1,000 attendees, SMS marketing, transactional emails, and dedicated sending infrastructure.

GetResponse is strongest when the buyer has a list to communicate with. It is weaker when the buyer needs a source of truth for open deals, owners, stages, activities, and close probability. A contact list and an active sales pipeline are not the same object.

## Pricing comparison

Both products publish pricing, but the pricing unit is different. Freshsales prices around user seats and CRM plan level. GetResponse prices around contact list size, marketing plan level, and feature depth.

Atlas verifies Freshsales pricing as a free plan at $0 for up to 3 users, with paid plans billed annually: Growth at $9 per user per month (or $11 monthly), Pro at $39 per user per month (or $47 monthly), and Enterprise at $59 per user per month (or $71 monthly). The official page says the free trial lasts 21 days and does not require a credit card.

Atlas verifies GetResponse pricing at the 1,000-contact level as a free account path after the 14-day trial, plus paid plans: Starter at $19 per month monthly or $15.58 per month annually, Marketer at $59 per month monthly or $48.38 per month annually, Creator at $69 per month monthly or $56.58 per month annually, and Enterprise with custom pricing.

The simple rule: pay for Freshsales when open opportunities are the asset. Pay for GetResponse when the contact list is the asset.

## CRM and sales pipeline depth

Freshsales wins the CRM depth comparison. GetResponse can store contacts, segment audiences, trigger automation, and support lead nurture. But a contractor sales manager usually needs more than a marketing contact record. They need to know who owns the lead, what stage the deal is in, what happened last, and what has to happen next.

Freshsales puts that work at the center of the product. The official pricing page describes Kanban pipeline views, contact lifecycle stages, deal management, sales sequences, multiple pipelines, territory management, auto-assignment rules, and custom sales activities on paid tiers. The Pro tier brings Freddy AI contact scoring and deal insights.

That matters when the contractor's sales process has clear handoffs. A website lead becomes a booked estimate. The estimate becomes a proposal. The proposal needs follow-up. The deal becomes won or lost. If those stages are invisible, email marketing will not fix the root issue.

GetResponse can help before and after those stages. It can send education before the estimate and reactivation after a lost quote. But it should not be treated as the main sales pipeline for a team that needs owners, deal stages, due dates, forecast value, and manager reporting. For a deeper look at how GetResponse compares to another dedicated CRM, see our [GetResponse vs Pipedrive](/getresponse-vs-pipedrive/) comparison.

## Email marketing and automation

GetResponse wins the email marketing comparison because email and marketing automation are the product's center of gravity. The official feature page describes a drag-and-drop email creator, newsletters, autoresponders, automation workflows, landing pages, signup forms, popups, audience segmentation, reporting, and multichannel marketing features.

For contractors, that makes GetResponse a better fit for nurture campaigns:

- old estimate follow-up
- roof inspection education
- HVAC maintenance reminders
- remodeling planning sequences
- seasonal service campaigns
- financing or promotion announcements
- past-customer newsletters
- referral partner communication

Freshsales can support email, but the role is different. The free tier includes email templates and live chat. Growth brings basic workflows. Pro adds sales sequences and advanced workflows. Those tools help when salespeople need messages attached to deals. It is not the same as choosing a dedicated email marketing platform for forms, landing pages, subscriber growth, webinars, and broader campaigns.

Freshsales also has no landing page builder, no signup forms or popups, no webinar hosting, and no newsletter management. If the contractor needs any of those, GetResponse is the more natural fit.

Decision signal: if success means better campaigns, choose GetResponse. If success means better activity around each opportunity, choose Freshsales.

## Built-in phone and communication tools

Freshsales wins the phone comparison. The official pricing page confirms a built-in phone system with 200 minutes per month on the free tier. That means a contractor can call leads directly from the CRM, log the call to the deal record, and move the opportunity forward without switching to a separate dialer. The Pro tier adds Bring Your Own Carrier (BYOC), which lets the team connect an existing phone provider.

GetResponse does not have a built-in phone. SMS marketing is available on the Enterprise plan, which includes 5,000 free SMS, automation, and bulk campaigns. But there is no way to make calls, log calls to a contact record, or manage a calling workflow inside the platform.

For a contractor where calling leads is central to the sales process, Freshsales has the better built-in toolset. For a contractor where email is the primary communication channel, GetResponse has the broader feature set. For more options in either direction, see our [best CRM for contractors](/best-crm-for-contractors/) roundup or our [best email marketing software for contractors](/best-email-marketing-software-contractors/) guide.

## Webinars, landing pages, and content tools

GetResponse wins the content and marketing tools comparison. The official pricing page lists landing pages on all paid plans, unlimited newsletter sends, signup forms, popups, and a website builder on the Creator tier. Webinars are available on Creator (up to 100 attendees, 3 hours of recording storage) and Enterprise (up to 1,000 attendees, 20 hours of recording storage, breakout rooms). The Creator tier also includes a course creator and premium newsletter subscriptions.

None of those features exist in Freshsales. There is no landing page builder, no webinar hosting, no website builder, and no course creator. Freshsales is a sales CRM, not a marketing content platform.

For contractors who want to build neighborhood landing pages for SEO, host educational webinars for homeowners, or create seasonal service campaigns with dedicated signup pages, GetResponse is the only option in this comparison that can do that work.

## Contractor operations fit

Neither platform should be sold internally as contractor operations software.

Freshsales can help with lead capture, deal management, pipeline visibility, phone calling, follow-up tasks, contact records, and sales reporting. It will not create estimates, dispatch crews, schedule technicians, track inventory, manage routes, collect job photos, or run job costing.

GetResponse can help with newsletters, landing pages, forms, popups, automated nurture, webinars, segmentation, customer reactivation, and campaign reporting. It will not create estimates, dispatch crews, schedule technicians, track inventory, manage routes, collect job photos, or run job costing.

That separation can be useful. A contractor might use Freshsales for active pipeline management, GetResponse for customer communication, and a [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) or project system for the actual work. The danger is expecting one front-office tool to do all three jobs.

## Where Freshsales falls short

Freshsales's biggest limitation in this comparison is marketing depth. There is no landing page builder, no signup forms, no popups, no webinar hosting, and no newsletter management. If the office needs campaigns, content, or webinars, GetResponse is the more natural fit.

The second limitation is automation on the free tier. Basic workflows are available on Growth and above. The free tier has email templates and live chat but no workflow automation, email cadences, or sales sequences. Follow-up structure has to be manual until the team upgrades.

The third limitation is system handoff. Freshsales will usually sit beside a field service, accounting, or marketing system. That can work, but the business needs rules for when a won deal moves into operations and which system owns the customer record after the job closes.

## Where GetResponse falls short

GetResponse's biggest limitation in this comparison is CRM depth. It can support contacts, segments, forms, landing pages, and automation paths, but it is not where most contractor sales teams should manage every open quote, owner, stage, probability, and next activity. If the office needs a sales board, Freshsales is the more natural fit.

The second limitation is pricing unit. GetResponse prices by contact list size. A contractor with a large list of past customers, old leads, and seasonal contacts may pay more than expected as the list grows. Freshsales prices by user seat, so the cost scales with the sales team, not the database size.

The third limitation is feature breadth. GetResponse can do email, automation, ecommerce, websites, webinars, newsletters, creator tools, and more. That can be useful, but it can also distract a small contractor that only needs a simple lead board and phone integration.

## Can you use both?

Yes, and for some contractors that is the cleanest setup. Freshsales can own active sales opportunities. GetResponse can own broader marketing communication before and after the sale.

A practical stack could look like this:

1. Website lead enters Freshsales as a deal.
2. Salesperson qualifies the lead and calls them from the built-in CRM phone.
3. Estimator moves the opportunity through quote stages.
4. Won work moves into the field service or project system.
5. Lost quotes, old leads, and past customers move into GetResponse segments.
6. GetResponse handles newsletters, maintenance reminders, reactivation campaigns, educational sequences, and webinar invites.

That setup only works if the handoff is documented. Someone has to decide which contacts move into GetResponse, which records stay in Freshsales, how consent is handled, and how unsubscribes are respected. Without those rules, the company can end up with two disconnected databases and no clear owner.

## Final recommendation

Choose Freshsales if the business has a sales follow-up gap. It is the better fit for lead capture, deal ownership, pipeline visibility, phone calling, and follow-up discipline. The free tier for 3 users with built-in phone and Freddy AI makes it the strongest no-cost CRM option for a contractor with a small sales team.

Choose GetResponse if the business has a marketing communication gap. It is the better fit for newsletters, old-estimate nurture, past-customer reactivation, landing pages, forms, webinars, and automated campaigns. The free account path and flexible contact-based pricing make it easier to start with one marketing owner.

If you are not sure which gap is larger, audit the last 30 days of missed follow-up. If most misses were people who should have received a campaign, start with GetResponse. If most misses were active opportunities nobody owned, start with Freshsales.

<p><strong>Bottom line:</strong> Freshsales helps contractors control a sales pipeline. GetResponse helps contractors communicate with a list. The better choice is the one that fixes the specific follow-up failure in your office.</p>
