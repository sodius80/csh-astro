# AWeber vs GetResponse for Contractor Email Marketing

AWeber and GetResponse both help contractors send better email campaigns, capture leads, and automate follow-up. The right choice depends on how much marketing platform the contractor actually needs around the email layer.

AWeber is the focused email tool. It does campaigns, templates, landing pages, forms, and basic automations well, and it includes a genuine free plan that a small contractor can use indefinitely. GetResponse is the broader system. It wraps email inside a marketing suite with landing pages, conversion funnels, webinars, automation paths, and creator-style monetization tools. The comparison is not about which has more features. It is about whether the contractor needs a sending tool or a marketing engine.

## Quick verdict

Pick AWeber if you want a free plan you can use indefinitely. The official AWeber pricing page lists a free plan at $0 for up to 500 subscribers and 3,000 emails per month, with access to the drag-and-drop editor, templates, signup forms, landing pages, and basic automations. Paid plans start with Lite at $15 per month or $12.50 per month billed yearly. Plus is $30 per month or $20 per month billed yearly. AWeber has been sending email since 1998, and that longevity translates into strong deliverability and responsive support across every plan tier.

Pick GetResponse if you want a broader marketing platform. Atlas-verified GetResponse pricing from the official pricing page shows Starter at $19 per month on monthly billing or $15.58 per month when billed annually for a 1,000-contact list. Marketer is $59 per month monthly or $48.38 per month annually. Creator is $69 per month monthly or $56.58 per month annually. Enterprise is custom priced. The official page describes a 14-day free trial with premium-feature access that reverts to a limited free version after the trial ends.

Skip both if the real problem is field operations. Neither product replaces [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), [ServiceTitan](/servicetitan-review/), or a broader [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) platform. AWeber and GetResponse help before and after the sale. They do not schedule crews, dispatch technicians, track job costing, manage routes, or invoice field work by themselves.

## The fundamental difference: email tool vs marketing platform

AWeber is built around the idea that email marketing should be affordable, clean, and easy to own. The drag-and-drop editor, template library, subscriber management, forms, and automations serve that focus. The platform does not try to host webinars, sell courses, or replace a marketing automation suite. It tries to send better emails at a price a small contractor can justify.

GetResponse is built around the idea that a contractor who can connect email, landing pages, automation funnels, webinars, and lead capture into one system will follow up better than one who uses a standalone newsletter tool. The marketing automation, visual funnel builder, webinar hosting, and creator-style monetization tools all serve that broader platform ambition.

This is why feature count does not settle the comparison. GetResponse has webinar tools and creator monetization that AWeber does not need. AWeber has a simpler editor, a permanent free plan, and phone support that GetResponse does not match at the same tier. They are not competing for the same buyer. They are designed for different levels of marketing ambition.

## Free plan comparison

This is the clearest structural difference between the two platforms.

AWeber offers a genuine free plan. It is not a trial. A contractor with 200 past customers and leads can use AWeber indefinitely at $0, sending up to 3,000 emails per month. The free plan includes the drag-and-drop editor, templates, signup forms, landing pages, and basic automations. The limitation is volume: 500 subscribers, 3,000 emails, and a cap on active automations, forms, and landing pages. But for a small list, that may be enough.

GetResponse offers a 14-day free trial with premium-feature access. After the trial ends, the account reverts to a limited free version. The trial is useful for testing the full platform, but the reversion to a limited free account means the contractor needs to choose a paid plan to keep using the broader toolkit. The trial limitations include 1,000 unique visits per month on landing pages, 10-person webinar capacity, and GetResponse branding on messages.

For a contractor just starting to build a list, AWeber's free plan is genuinely usable. It lets the office send real campaigns to a small list without paying. GetResponse's trial is better for testing the full platform before committing, but the reversion to a limited free version makes it less practical as a long-term free tier.

## When AWeber makes more sense

AWeber makes more sense when the contractor wants email to be affordable, simple, and focused. The office may already have leads in a spreadsheet, a CRM, a field service platform, or an old customer list. The missing piece is consistent communication.

Official AWeber feature pages describe email marketing, drag-and-drop editors, hundreds of mobile-responsive templates, signup forms, landing pages, automations, web push notifications, tagging, and integrations with WordPress, Shopify, PayPal, and Facebook. For a contractor, that maps to practical marketing work:

- send newsletters to past customers
- create a form for a seasonal offer
- build a simple landing page for one service area
- run a reactivation campaign before busy season
- send quote follow-up emails manually or through a basic automation
- send maintenance reminders before peak season

AWeber also stands out on template library and support. The platform includes hundreds of mobile-responsive email templates, a smart designer that auto-generates branded templates, and an AI subject line assistant. Support is available 24/7 via chat, email, and phone on every plan, including the free plan. For a contractor who is not technically inclined and wants someone to talk to when something breaks, AWeber is the safer pick.

The tradeoff is that AWeber's automation is basic relative to GetResponse. The free plan allows a limited number of active automations. Lite allows 3. Plus is unlimited. A contractor who needs multiple parallel workflows, contact scoring, or event-based triggers from day one will find AWeber's depth limiting.

## When GetResponse makes more sense

GetResponse makes more sense when the contractor wants a marketing system, not just a sending tool. A roofing company might want a landing page for storm inspections, a form for financing questions, a nurture sequence after an estimate request, and a webinar-style homeowner education event before peak season. GetResponse is built for that broader pattern.

Official GetResponse feature pages describe email marketing, autoresponders, marketing automation, landing pages, popups and forms, segmentation, website builder tools, webinars, transactional emails, and analytics. For a contractor, that maps to practical front-office work:

- send automated follow-up after a quote request
- build a landing page for one service, neighborhood, or seasonal offer
- run a homeowner education webinar or virtual event
- segment contacts by service line, source, location, or intent
- send newsletters, reminders, and reactivation campaigns
- connect campaigns to a broader funnel instead of one-off email blasts

The tradeoff is that GetResponse can be more platform than a small contractor needs. If the office only wants a clean monthly newsletter and a few estimate follow-up emails, the extra depth can turn into setup work. GetResponse is strongest when someone owns marketing and will actually use the broader toolkit.

## Automation and nurture

GetResponse wins when automation depth is the deciding factor. The Starter plan includes 1 custom workflow, welcome emails, and autoresponders. Marketer adds unlimited workflows, contact tagging and scoring, event-based and purchase automation, and A/B testing. That makes it a stronger fit for a contractor that wants to build a more complete funnel.

A practical GetResponse workflow could look like this:

1. A landing page captures storm-damage inspection leads.
2. A form tags the lead by service area and project type.
3. An autoresponder sends the first response.
4. A nurture sequence sends education, reminders, and proof points.
5. A webinar or virtual event moves higher-intent homeowners closer to a consultation.
6. Campaign reporting shows which subject lines and pages are working.

AWeber's automation covers welcome sequences, tag-based triggers, time delays, and behavioral automation. The free plan allows a limited number of active automations. Lite allows 3 automations and 3 landing pages. Plus removes automation count limits and includes unlimited landing pages, custom segments, and users. For many small contractors, that is enough. The difference is ceiling. AWeber fits the office that wants email follow-up to get done. GetResponse fits the office that wants email, landing pages, and events to work together as a broader marketing system.

## Landing pages and forms

Both products support lead capture, but with different tier structures. AWeber includes limited landing pages and signup forms on the free plan, 3 on Lite, and unlimited on Plus. GetResponse includes unlimited landing pages, signup forms, and popups on all plans, including Starter.

For contractors, the tactical work is similar on day one:

- build a page for one offer
- capture a name, email, and project interest
- tag or segment the contact
- send a first response quickly
- follow up with useful reminders instead of generic blasts

The difference shows up after the first campaign. If the contractor wants to expand into webinars, more advanced funnel paths, and a larger marketing stack, GetResponse has more room. If the contractor wants a lighter campaign system that one person can own without extra platform weight, AWeber is easier to justify.

## Webinars and education campaigns

Webinars are the clearest GetResponse advantage in this comparison. The Creator plan includes live and on-demand webinars for up to 100 attendees with 3 hours of recording storage and 3 presenters. Enterprise supports up to 1,000 attendees with 20 hours of storage and breakout rooms. For most contractors, that does not mean running a software-company webinar. It means using online education in a practical local-business way.

Examples could include:

- a roofing company explaining storm-damage inspection steps
- a remodeler walking homeowners through kitchen project planning
- an HVAC company teaching maintenance timing before summer
- a solar or insulation contractor answering common financing questions
- a specialty trade using an online session before a seasonal campaign

AWeber can promote those events through email, landing pages, and automation, but built-in webinar hosting is not available. If webinars are part of the contractor's marketing plan, GetResponse belongs at the top of the shortlist. If webinars are a maybe-someday idea, AWeber's lower cost and simpler interface may matter more.

## Ease of use and editor

AWeber has a clear advantage in simplicity. Its drag-and-drop editor is designed for a contractor who wants to send the first campaign within an hour of signing up. The smart designer auto-generates branded templates from a website URL. The AI subject line assistant helps with writer's block. The interface prioritizes getting a campaign sent over configuring a marketing system.

GetResponse has a steeper learning curve. The same breadth that makes it useful for automation, webinars, funnels, and creator tools means more setup work before the platform feels natural. The automation builder is visual and capable, but understanding the difference between autoresponders, automation workflows, and funnel paths takes time.

For a contractor who wants to send the first campaign quickly and keep things simple, AWeber is the faster path. For a contractor who has a marketing owner and is willing to invest in setup, GetResponse's depth pays off over time.

## Support and ecosystem

AWeber provides 24/7 chat, email, and phone support on every plan, including the free plan. That is a meaningful advantage for a contractor who wants to pick up the phone when something breaks. AWeber also offers free account migration service, a community forum, a knowledge base, free workshops, and a certified experts marketplace for contractors who want to hire implementation help.

GetResponse offers live chat support (English: 7 AM to 11 PM, weekends 7 AM to 9 PM GMT+1), a 24/7 chatbot, and email support in 7 languages. Phone support is available on the Enterprise plan only. The support is capable, but a small contractor on a Starter plan does not get the same phone access that AWeber provides on its free tier.

AWeber also has a larger integration library, with hundreds of third-party connections including WordPress, Shopify, WooCommerce, PayPal, Facebook, Zapier, and Etsy. GetResponse lists fewer native integrations but covers the core platforms. For most contractors, both integration libraries cover the basics. The gap matters when the contractor relies on a specific integration or wants access to a larger marketplace of templates and experts.

## Contractor operations fit

Neither product should be sold internally as contractor operations software.

AWeber can help with campaigns, forms, landing pages, automations, web push notifications, and email follow-up. It will not replace scheduling, estimating, dispatch, production tracking, job costing, payments, inventory, routes, or field mobile workflows.

GetResponse can help with campaigns, landing pages, forms, popups, webinars, autoresponders, segmentation, and marketing automation. It will not replace a CRM pipeline, service calendar, crew board, estimate workflow, job costing system, or invoice process by itself.

A common stack could be: field service software for jobs, a CRM for active sales opportunities, and one of these email tools for list nurture. For more options in this category, start with the [best email marketing software for contractors](/best-email-marketing-software-contractors/) roundup.

## Where AWeber falls short

AWeber's biggest drawback in this comparison is automation ceiling. The free plan allows a limited number of active automations. Lite allows 3. Plus is unlimited, but the depth of each automation is simpler than what GetResponse offers. Contact scoring, event-based purchase triggers, and multi-path funnels are not part of the AWeber toolkit.

The second drawback is no webinar hosting. A contractor who wants to run virtual education sessions or online estimate walkthroughs through their email marketing platform will not find that capability in AWeber. That feature alone may push the decision toward GetResponse.

The third drawback is no built-in CRM or contact scoring. AWeber manages subscribers and tags, but it does not model a sales pipeline, track deal stages, or score contacts by engagement level. For a contractor who wants those capabilities, [ActiveCampaign](/best-email-marketing-software-contractors/) or a CRM-first tool may be a better fit.

## Where GetResponse falls short

GetResponse's biggest drawback is complexity. The same breadth that makes it useful for webinars, landing pages, automation, and creator-style marketing can make it too much tool for a contractor that only wants simple email follow-up.

The second drawback is the free plan. GetResponse's trial reverts to a limited free version after 14 days, which is less practical for a contractor who wants to keep using the tool without immediately paying. AWeber's permanent free plan is more genuinely free.

The third drawback is price. GetResponse Starter at $15.58 per month annually or $19 monthly is not expensive by marketing-platform standards, but it sits above AWeber Lite at $12.50 per month annually. The gap grows if the buyer moves to Marketer or Creator for deeper automation, ecommerce features, webinars, or creator tools.

The fourth drawback is support access. GetResponse offers chat and email support on standard plans, but phone support is locked to the Enterprise tier. AWeber provides phone support on every plan, including free.

## How to choose

Start with the missing object in the business.

If the missing object is a contact list that receives consistent emails, AWeber is the better fit. The free plan lets a contractor start sending real campaigns without paying. The template library and smart designer make the first campaign look professional. The phone support means help is one call away. AWeber gives contractors a lower-risk way to improve marketing communication without rebuilding the sales process or buying a heavier platform. See how AWeber compares to other email tools like [Moosend](/aweber-vs-moosend/) or a CRM like [Pipeline CRM](/aweber-vs-pipeline-crm/) for more targeted decisions.

If the missing object is a marketing system that connects email, landing pages, automation, webinars, and lead capture into one workflow, GetResponse is the better fit. The higher monthly cost is easier to justify when the platform replaces multiple disconnected tools and there is a marketing owner who will use the broader toolkit. The [best CRM for contractors](/best-crm-for-contractors/) roundup covers the CRM side if that gap is more urgent.

If both are missing, do not buy both on day one. If the CRM-versus-marketing-platform question is closer to the real decision, [GetResponse vs Keap](/getresponse-vs-keap/) or [AWeber vs Pipedrive](/aweber-vs-pipedrive/) may be the better comparison. Decide which gap is more expensive. A contractor losing active quotes because nobody owns the next call should fix CRM first. A contractor with a stable sales process but no reactivation or nurture should fix email first. AWeber is the simplicity pick. GetResponse is the depth pick.
