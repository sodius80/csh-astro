# ActiveCampaign vs Freshsales: Email Automation or Free CRM?

ActiveCampaign and Freshsales both live in the CRM category, but they solve fundamentally different problems for a contractor. ActiveCampaign is an email marketing platform with CRM capabilities attached. Freshsales is a sales CRM with basic email tools built in. The question is not which has more features. It is whether your center of gravity is email marketing or sales pipeline management.

For a contractor evaluating the two, the starting question is simple: what hurts more right now? If quotes go out and nobody follows up because the office does not have an automated email sequence, ActiveCampaign is the natural fit. If leads come in and nobody can say which one was called, what stage the deal is at, or who owns the follow-up, Freshsales is the better starting point.

## When this software makes sense

Contractors reach for combined email-CRM platforms when the spreadsheet approach stops working. Leads come in from the website, some get calls, others get emails, and nobody can say which follow-ups actually produce jobs. The seasonal promotion goes to the whole list instead of just the customers who need that service. The monthly newsletter takes a full afternoon to send.

Both ActiveCampaign and Freshsales solve parts of that problem, but from different foundations.

ActiveCampaign makes sense when email automation is the primary need. A roofing contractor who wants every "get a free estimate" website submission to trigger a five-email sequence - welcome, case studies, pricing overview, seasonal reminder, check-in at 30 days - can build that in ActiveCampaign's visual automation builder without code. The CRM side tracks who opened what and which contacts moved through which sequence. It is CRM-light but email-heavy, and that is the right balance for contractors whose main pain is follow-up communication.

Freshsales makes sense when CRM is the primary need. A contractor who wants to see every lead in a sales pipeline, know which stage each deal is at, call leads directly from the CRM, and use AI to prioritize which leads to call first will find Freshsales more structured out of the box. The email tools sit alongside the CRM foundation rather than driving the platform.

## When it does not make sense yet

Skip both if your email needs are basic - a monthly newsletter to a small list of existing customers. MailerLite at $10/month or Mailchimp's free tier covers that without paying for automation workflows you will not use. If that sounds closer to your use case, start with the [email marketing software for contractors roundup](/best-email-marketing-software-contractors/) before paying for a combined email-CRM platform.

Also skip both if your CRM need is strictly field service management - scheduling, dispatch, estimates, and invoicing. Neither ActiveCampaign nor Freshsales handles job scheduling or dispatch. You would still need a field service platform like [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), or another option from the [field service software alternatives guide](/field-service-software-alternatives/) alongside either of them. For the broader field service landscape, see [best field service software](/best-field-service-software/).

The specific risk with ActiveCampaign: if CRM depth matters more than email automation - structured pipelines, deal stages with probability-weighted forecasts, built-in phone, and multi-user sales team workflows - ActiveCampaign's CRM capabilities will feel light compared to Freshsales.

The specific risk with Freshsales: if building complex, conditional email sequences with multiple branches is your primary use case, Freshsales's email tools are basic compared to ActiveCampaign's visual automation canvas. Freshsales does not have ActiveCampaign's drag-and-drop automation builder.

## Email marketing and automation depth

ActiveCampaign's automation builder is its strongest argument. The visual canvas lets you drag conditions, actions, and triggers into a sequence without code. A contractor could build: "If someone fills out the 'get a quote' form, send a welcome email, wait three days, check if they opened it, send a case study if they did and a different follow-up if they did not." That level of conditional logic runs on Starter and gets more powerful on Plus and Professional plans.

ActiveCampaign also supports split conditions - different paths based on whether a contact opened, clicked, or did nothing. That is useful for contractors running seasonal promotions who want to send a second offer to non-openers and a thank-you plus upsell to openers.

Freshsales's email capabilities are functional but basic by comparison. The platform includes email templates, email sequences on paid tiers, and basic email tracking. A contractor can send templated follow-up emails and see whether a lead opened them. But there is no visual automation canvas, no multi-branch conditional logic, and no behavior-triggered email sequences comparable to ActiveCampaign's builder.

Decision signal: if you plan to build complex, conditional email sequences with multiple branches and want a visual editor, ActiveCampaign is the clear choice. If your email needs are straightforward templates and periodic follow-ups, Freshsales gets the job done.

## CRM functionality and contact management

Freshsales wins on CRM depth. The contact record includes pipelines, deal stages, lead source tracking, and notes. The pipeline view shows deals at each stage - new lead, contacted, quoted, negotiating, won, lost - with the ability to drag deals between stages. The no-cost tier includes contact and account management, Kanban views, and basic deal tracking. For a contractor managing a steady flow of inbound leads and wanting a visible sales funnel, Freshsales is the more structured starting point.

ActiveCampaign's CRM covers the essentials but does not go as deep. Contact records show list membership, tags, engagement data, deal stage, and custom fields. The deal tracking view shows a pipeline with stages, deal values, and owner assignments. For a contractor who mainly needs to know which contacts received which emails and who is in the active buying window, ActiveCampaign's CRM is sufficient. For a contractor who needs multi-stage lead qualification, territory-based sales tracking, or team-based pipeline management, Freshsales is the stronger system.

Decision signal: Freshsales if CRM is the center of gravity - you want to manage leads through a pipeline, assign deals to team members, and use deal stage as the primary driver. ActiveCampaign if CRM is supporting cast - you mainly need email automation and enough contact structure to segment lists.

## Built-in phone and communication

Freshsales has the stronger built-in communication story for contractors. The official pricing page lists built-in phone, email, and live chat on the no-cost plan. For a contractor who wants to call leads from inside the CRM without buying a separate phone system, that is a genuine advantage.

The no-cost tier includes 200 minutes per month of built-in phone calling. A salesperson can dial a lead from inside the CRM, log the call, attach notes, and move the deal forward without switching tools. For a roofing, HVAC, or remodeling company where speed-to-call matters, that is real value at zero cost.

On Pro and higher, Freshsales adds Bring Your Own Carrier (BYOC) for phone and advanced workflows. The communication story deepens as the team upgrades.

ActiveCampaign does not include built-in phone on any plan. The platform focuses on email, SMS (on higher tiers), and automation. A contractor who wants to call leads from inside the CRM would need Freshsales or a separate dialer alongside ActiveCampaign.

Decision signal: for contractors who want calling built into the CRM at no extra cost, Freshsales is the only option here. For contractors who already have a phone system and only need email automation, ActiveCampaign's communication tools are sufficient.

## AI features: lead scoring and automation

Both platforms market AI capabilities, but the approach is different.

Freshsales includes Freddy AI on the no-cost tier. Freddy provides lead scoring that helps prioritize which leads to call first, deal insights on Pro and higher, and email assistance (rephrase, expand, clean up text). For a contractor on a no-cost tier, AI lead scoring at no cost is a genuine advantage.

ActiveCampaign offers AI-powered automation suggestions, AI campaign builder, spam check, AI-suggested segments, and predictive sending on higher tiers. Lead scoring in ActiveCampaign assigns points based on email engagement, page visits, and form submissions. When a contact crosses a threshold, they can trigger an automation or move to a different list. However, ActiveCampaign's lead scoring requires Plus or higher - it is not available on Starter.

For a contractor comparing AI at the entry level, Freshsales gives you AI lead scoring at no cost. ActiveCampaign requires at least Plus at $49/month for lead scoring. If lead prioritization matters and the budget is tight, Freshsales has the edge.

## Segmentation and lead scoring

ActiveCampaign handles segmentation through tags, lists, custom fields, and deal stages. A contact can be tagged "spring-promotion-opened" and "service-area-north" and "estimated-over-5k" - then you can send a follow-up to the intersection of all three. That level of multi-condition segmentation is available on Starter and becomes more granular on higher plans.

Lead scoring in ActiveCampaign assigns points based on email engagement, page visits, form submissions, and other behaviors. When a contact reaches a threshold score, they can trigger an automation or move to a different list. This is useful for contractors who want to know which leads are actively researching versus passively browsing. Lead scoring is available on Plus and higher.

Freshsales handles segmentation through tags, custom fields, and deal stage. The segmentation is capable but more contact-centric - based on field values and deal status rather than email behavior. Freddy AI lead scoring on the no-cost tier helps prioritize which leads to call first based on engagement signals.

Decision signal: ActiveCampaign for event-driven segmentation based on what contacts do inside emails and on your website. Freshsales for field-value segmentation based on who contacts are - their service area, project type, deal stage.

## Pricing and plan structure

ActiveCampaign's pricing scales primarily by contact count. Atlas-verified pricing confirms annual-billed monthly prices of Starter $15/mo, Plus $49/mo, Professional $79/mo, and Enterprise $145/mo at 1,000 contacts. Month-to-month pricing is Starter $19/mo, Plus $59/mo, Professional $99/mo, and Enterprise $179/mo. Pricing increases as the contact list grows. A 14-day free trial is available. There is no permanent free plan.

Freshsales has a no-cost plan at $0 for up to 3 users. Atlas-verified pricing confirms paid plans at Growth $9/user/month billed annually ($11 monthly), Pro $39/user/month annually ($47 monthly), and Enterprise $59/user/month annually ($71 monthly). Freshsales also advertises a 21-day free trial with no credit card required.

The pricing models are fundamentally different. ActiveCampaign charges by contact count - the price grows as your email list grows, regardless of how many users you have. Freshsales charges by user - the price grows as you add salespeople, regardless of how many contacts you have.

## ActiveCampaign: what stands out

The visual automation builder is the headline feature. ActiveCampaign's drag-and-drop canvas lets contractors build multi-step sequences that respond to contact behavior in real time. If a contact opens a quote follow-up email but does not click the "book a call" link, the next email can take a different path from someone who did click. That level of conditional logic is unusual at the Starter price point.

Lead scoring works alongside automation. Contacts accumulate points for opens, clicks, site visits, and form submissions. When a contact crosses a threshold, they can be moved to a "hot lead" list, assigned to a deal stage, or removed from a nurture sequence. For a contractor getting 50 website leads a month, this separates people who fill out a form and disappear from people who fill out a form and read every follow-up email.

Segmentation by tag, list, deal stage, or custom field is available on every plan. A contractor can send a spring HVAC tune-up promotion only to contacts tagged "has-hvac-system" and "service-area-includes-heating" without creating separate lists.

The pricing is the lowest serious entry point for email automation. ActiveCampaign at $15/month for 1,000 contacts is cheaper than most dedicated email marketing platforms with comparable automation depth.

## Where ActiveCampaign falls short

The CRM is functional but not deep. If your business model is built around managing leads through a multi-stage sales pipeline with territory assignments, deal probability forecasting, and team-based pipeline management, ActiveCampaign's CRM will feel limited compared to Freshsales.

Pricing scales with contact count. A contractor whose list grows from 1,000 to 5,000 contacts on the Plus plan sees a significant monthly increase. The platform is designed to grow with you, but the price grows too.

There is no built-in phone. A contractor who wants to call leads from inside the CRM needs a separate phone system or dialer. Freshsales includes 200 minutes per month on its no-cost plan.

There is no no-cost tier. ActiveCampaign starts at $15/month. A contractor who needs a genuinely free starting point should look at Freshsales instead.

## Freshsales: what stands out

The no-cost tier is the headline feature. For up to 3 users, a contractor gets a working CRM with built-in phone (200 minutes per month), Freddy AI lead scoring, Kanban pipeline views, email templates, and live chat. That covers the essential sales workflow without a per-user cost.

The built-in phone is particularly useful for contractors. A roofing company can call leads directly from the CRM, log the call, and move the deal forward without switching to a separate dialer. For speed-to-call scenarios where the first contractor to reach the lead wins the job, that is real value at zero cost.

The interface is modern and clean, with a lower learning curve than most CRMs. A small team can create a pipeline this week, not after a multi-week configuration project. That is why it ranked as the top no-cost CRM in our [best free CRM for contractors](/best-free-crm-for-contractors/) roundup.

Freddy AI lead scoring on the no-cost tier helps prioritize which leads to call first. For a contractor getting multiple leads per day, AI-based prioritization separates the hot leads from the tire-kickers without manual scoring.

## Where Freshsales falls short

Email automation is basic. Freshsales includes email templates and sequences on paid tiers, but there is no visual automation builder, no multi-branch conditional logic, and no behavior-triggered sequences comparable to ActiveCampaign. If building complex email sequences is the primary use case, Freshsales will not deliver.

The no-cost tier's feature ceiling is real. The no-cost plan includes basic workflows but no custom fields, no multiple sales pipelines, no auto-assignment rules, and only 1 third-party integration. A contractor connecting QuickBooks, Zapier, or a field service tool will need to upgrade to Growth at $9/user/month or higher.

The 3-user cap on the no-cost tier is a limitation for growing teams. A fourth salesperson triggers a move to paid plans. The no-cost tier is a starter, not a permanent solution for a growing office.

Freshsales has no project management, job tracking, invoicing, or field service features. It is pure sales CRM. For contractors who need accounting alongside CRM, see [CRM with QuickBooks integration](/crm-with-quickbooks-integration/).

## The bottom line for contractors

ActiveCampaign and Freshsales overlap in the CRM category, but they deliver from opposite starting points.

ActiveCampaign is for the contractor whose pain is email: managing automated follow-ups, segmented campaigns, and lead scoring without a dedicated marketing person. The visual automation builder is the best in this category, and the $15/month entry price makes it accessible. But there is no free tier, no built-in phone, and the CRM is supporting cast, not the foundation.

Freshsales is for the contractor whose pain is CRM: managing leads through a pipeline, calling them from inside the CRM, and using AI to prioritize follow-up. The no-cost tier with built-in phone and Freddy AI is genuinely generous. But the email automation is basic, the no-cost tier has integration limits, and there is no visual automation canvas.

Pick <a href="/go/activecampaign-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">ActiveCampaign</a> if email automation is your center of gravity. Pick <a href="/go/freshsales-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Freshsales</a> if a no-cost CRM with phone and AI pipeline management is.
