<p>Joist versus Clear Estimates. One bundles estimating with invoicing for simple workflows, the other focuses on estimate precision for complex jobs.</p>

<p><strong>Disclosure:</strong> Some links on this page are affiliate links. If you sign up through one, I earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. My recommendations don't change based on that.</p>

<p><strong>Short verdict:</strong> Joist is better for home service contractors who want everything in one platform. Clear Estimates makes sense when you need detailed takeoffs and already handle invoicing elsewhere.</p>

## Key decision factors

<p><strong>Do you need invoicing bundled with estimates?</strong></p>
<p>Joist includes payment processing and invoice management with estimates. Clear Estimates focuses purely on estimate creation. If you're starting fresh or want to consolidate platforms, Joist saves setup time. If you already use QuickBooks or similar for invoicing, Clear Estimates won't duplicate functionality you already have.</p>

<p><strong>How complex are your material calculations?</strong></p>
<p>Clear Estimates excels at screen takeoffs and has extensive Excel integration for detailed pricing models. Joist handles basic material lists but isn't built for complex quantity calculations or multi-phase job breakdowns.</p>

## Where Joist wins

<p><strong>Complete workflow integration</strong></p>
<p>Joist covers estimates, invoicing, and payment collection in one platform. You create an estimate, convert it to an invoice when approved, and customers can pay online through the same system. This eliminates the need to export data between different tools or manually recreate approved estimates as invoices.</p>

<p><strong>Faster setup and onboarding</strong></p>
<p>Users report getting productive with Joist in hours rather than days. The interface prioritizes common workflows over feature depth, making it accessible for contractors who want estimating software that doesn't require extensive training.</p>

<p><strong>Lower barrier to entry</strong></p>
<p>At $32/month starting price versus Clear Estimates' $79/month, Joist costs less than half as much for basic functionality. For contractors doing straightforward service work, this pricing difference adds up over time.</p>

## Where Clear Estimates wins

<p><strong>Advanced takeoff capabilities</strong></p>
<p>Clear Estimates includes screen takeoff tools for measuring plans and calculating materials from blueprints. This matters for contractors who regularly bid from drawings or need precise quantity calculations for complex jobs.</p>

<p><strong>Excel integration depth</strong></p>
<p>The platform works closely with Excel for pricing models and cost databases. Contractors who have built detailed pricing spreadsheets can integrate existing work rather than starting over with a new system's pricing structure.</p>

<p><strong>Trade-specific estimate templates</strong></p>
<p>Clear Estimates offers more detailed templates for specific trades, with pre-built line items and standard assemblies that reflect real-world job structures. This saves time for contractors who do repetitive work types.</p>

## Pricing head-to-head

<p><strong>Joist:</strong> $32/month starting plan covers basic estimates and invoicing for small contractors.</p>

<p><strong>Clear Estimates:</strong> $79/month starting plan focuses on estimate creation without invoicing features.</p>

<p>The cost difference narrows if you factor in separate invoicing software costs with Clear Estimates, but Joist remains more affordable for contractors who want basic functionality.</p>

## Dealbreakers for each

<p><strong>Skip Joist if:</strong> You need detailed material takeoffs, work heavily with Excel pricing models, or already have invoicing software you prefer. The estimating features alone aren't deep enough to justify switching if you're happy with your current invoicing setup.</p>

<p><strong>Skip Clear Estimates if:</strong> You want invoicing included, prefer simple setup over feature depth, or mainly do service work that doesn't require complex material calculations. The higher price point doesn't make sense for basic estimate needs.</p>

## Bottom line

<p><strong>Our call:</strong> Joist wins for most small contractors doing home service work who want to consolidate platforms.</p>

<p><strong>Choose Joist if:</strong> You're starting fresh with business software, do straightforward service work, and want estimates, invoicing, and payments handled in one place.</p>
