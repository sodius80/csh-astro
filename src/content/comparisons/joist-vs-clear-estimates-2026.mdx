<p>Joist versus Clear Estimates. One bundles estimating with invoicing for simple workflows, the other focuses on estimate precision for complex jobs.</p>

<p><strong>Disclosure:</strong> Some links on this page are affiliate links. If you sign up through one, I earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. My recommendations don't change based on that.</p>

<p><strong>Short verdict:</strong> Joist is better for home service contractors who want estimates, invoices, online payments, and homeowner financing in one simple app. Clear Estimates makes sense when detailed line-item estimating, trade templates, quarterly rate updates, and Buildertrend/Zapier-style integrations matter more than a lightweight mobile workflow.</p>

## Key decision factors

<p><strong>Do you need invoicing bundled with estimates?</strong></p>
<p>Joist includes estimates, invoices, online payments, and homeowner financing in the same contractor app. Clear Estimates also supports payable invoices, but its strongest value is the estimating database, templates, rate updates, and Pro-tier integrations. If you're starting fresh or want a lightweight estimate-to-payment workflow, Joist is simpler. If estimating depth matters more than mobile simplicity, Clear Estimates is the more specialized tool.</p>

<p><strong>How detailed does your estimating database need to be?</strong></p>
<p>Clear Estimates publishes a large line-item database, preloaded templates, and quarterly labor/material rate updates. Joist handles estimates, invoices, payments, and client documents well, but it is not positioned as a deep estimating database for complex remodeling assemblies or recurring cost updates.</p>

## Where Joist wins

<p><strong>Complete workflow integration</strong></p>
<p>Joist covers estimates, invoicing, and payment collection in one platform. You create an estimate, convert it to an invoice when approved, and customers can pay online through the same system. This eliminates the need to export data between different tools or manually recreate approved estimates as invoices.</p>

<p><strong>Faster setup and onboarding</strong></p>
<p>Joist prioritizes common contractor workflows over feature depth: create an estimate, convert it to an invoice, collect payment, and keep moving. That makes it accessible for contractors who want estimating software that does not require extensive configuration.</p>

<p><strong>Lower barrier to entry</strong></p>
<p>Joist Basics starts at $10/month or $100/year, while Clear Estimates Standard starts at $79/month or $59/month when billed annually. For straightforward service work, Joist is the lower-cost entry point. For contractors who need Clear Estimates' database and templates, the higher subscription may still make sense.</p>

## Where Clear Estimates wins

<p><strong>Line-item estimating database</strong></p>
<p>Clear Estimates publishes a large labor/material line-item database, preloaded templates, and quarterly rate updates. This matters for remodelers and trade contractors who need a structured estimating system rather than a lightweight invoice-and-estimate app.</p>

<p><strong>Pro-tier integrations and reporting</strong></p>
<p>Clear Estimates Pro adds Buildertrend integration, CRM integrations through Zapier, custom filtering and reports, ATTOM property data, and additional users for $9/month. That makes it a better fit when estimating needs to connect with a broader construction workflow.</p>

<p><strong>Trade-specific estimate templates</strong></p>
<p>Clear Estimates offers more detailed templates for specific trades, with pre-built line items and standard assemblies that reflect real-world job structures. This saves time for contractors who do repetitive work types.</p>

## Pricing head-to-head

<p><strong>Joist:</strong> Basics is $10/month or $100/year, Pro is $16/month or $160/year, and Elite is $32/month or $320/year. Basics includes up to five documents per month; Pro and Elite add more professional document and reporting features.</p>

<p><strong>Clear Estimates:</strong> Standard is $79/month billed monthly or $59/month billed annually. Pro is $119/month monthly or $99/month annually. Franchise starts at $249/month monthly or $199/month annually, with scope-based customization pricing.</p>

<p>The cost difference is large at the entry tier, but the products are not trying to solve the exact same problem. Joist is the cheaper all-in-one workflow. Clear Estimates is the more specialized estimating database and template system.</p>

## Dealbreakers for each

<p><strong>Skip Joist if:</strong> You need a detailed estimating database, preloaded remodeling templates, quarterly rate updates, or construction-platform integrations. Joist's estimating features are useful, but the product is built around a simple estimate-to-payment workflow.</p>

<p><strong>Skip Clear Estimates if:</strong> You mainly need quick estimates, invoices, payments, and client documents in one simple app. The higher subscription makes less sense if you do not need the estimating database, templates, rate updates, or Pro integrations.</p>

## Bottom line

<p><strong>Our call:</strong> Joist wins for most small contractors doing home service work who want to consolidate platforms.</p>

<p><strong>Choose Joist if:</strong> You're starting fresh with business software, do straightforward service work, and want estimates, invoicing, and payments handled in one place.</p>

<p><strong>Choose Clear Estimates if:</strong> You need detailed line-item estimating, preloaded trade templates, recurring rate updates, and Pro-tier integrations more than a lightweight mobile estimate-to-payment workflow.</p>
