# Keap vs PandaDoc for Contractor Sales and Document Workflows

Keap and PandaDoc both sit in the pre-sale window, but they are not competing for the same job. Keap is a CRM and marketing automation platform. PandaDoc is a document automation platform. The question is not which product is better. The question is which bottleneck is costing you more money: leads going cold because follow-up is inconsistent, or deals being lost because proposals are slow, unprofessional, and missing a clean signature path.

A roofing company that gets 30 leads a month but only follows up with 10 of them has a follow-up problem. Keap addresses that. A remodeling company that produces every proposal from scratch in Word, takes a week to send it, and has no way to collect an e-signature has a document problem. PandaDoc addresses that. Some contractors have both problems - and in that case, the two products can work together.

## Quick verdict

Pick Keap if your office is losing revenue because leads go cold, follow-up is inconsistent, old customer lists sit unused, and nobody has time to manually send emails, texts, and reminders for every quote. Keap's official pricing page describes CRM, email marketing, text marketing, lead source tracking, sales pipeline, appointment scheduling, quotes, invoices, checkout forms, and payment processing inside the platform. For a contractor with a long sales cycle and repeat customers, that breadth matters. But Keap starts at $299 per month billed monthly for 2 users and 1,500 contacts, with required implementation services at $500 one-time. It is a serious investment that only pays off if you actually use the automation depth.

Pick PandaDoc if your office is losing deals because proposals look unprofessional, take too long to produce, or lack a clean e-signature workflow. PandaDoc's official pricing page lists a Free plan at $0, Starter at $19 per seat per month billed annually, Business at $49 per seat per month billed annually, and Enterprise with custom pricing. The drag-and-drop document editor, pricing tables, content library, custom branding, e-signature workflow, and payment collection are the core product. You can test it for free. The cost only scales up if you need CRM integrations and approval workflows on the Business plan.

Skip both if the problem is dispatch, scheduling, route management, crew tracking, or job costing. Neither product is a field service operations platform. You still need [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), [ServiceTitan](/servicetitan-review/), or another [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) for the work itself.

## When Keap makes more sense

Keap makes sense when the expensive bottleneck is follow-up, not document production. The lead comes in from a form, the office calls once, nobody sends a follow-up email, the quote sits for two weeks, the prospect hires someone else, and the old customer list is never reactivated. That is a communication problem, not a proposal problem.

Atlas-verified Keap pricing starts at $299 per month billed monthly for 2 users and 1,500 contacts. Required implementation services are listed at $500 one-time. Additional users are $39 per month each. Text marketing Tier 1 is included, with paid text tiers at $24, $39, $79, $139, and $279 per month. Keap also lists a text add-on at $6 per month and a local number add-on at $10 per month, with overages at $0.015 per text message and $0.01 per voice minute.

For a contractor, the practical value of Keap is that the entire customer communication loop can live in one system:

- A quote request form captures the lead
- The lead is tagged by service interest or source
- An automated email sequence sends immediately
- A follow-up task is created for the office
- Appointment links go out automatically
- The quote is sent from inside the CRM
- The quote converts to an invoice when accepted
- Old leads and past customers get nurture campaigns, seasonal promotions, and reactivation sequences

That is a broader workflow than PandaDoc is trying to cover. PandaDoc produces the document. Keap manages the communication around the whole customer lifecycle - before, during, and after the sale.

A remodeling company with a 6-week consideration cycle, a roofing company with seasonal storm leads, or a specialty trade with a repeatable quote-to-close process can use Keap to ensure no lead falls through the cracks. The automation builder supports lead nurture, quote follow-up, reactivation, review requests, and repeat-customer campaigns. That is the product's center of gravity - not the proposal document, but the communication engine around it.

## When PandaDoc makes more sense

PandaDoc makes more sense when the proposal itself is what wins or loses the deal. A roofing contractor bidding a $25,000 replacement, a remodeler presenting a $75,000 design-build scope, a commercial contractor submitting a competitive bid, or a specialty trade quoting a maintenance agreement all need the document to look professional, include accurate pricing, and close with an e-signature. That is PandaDoc's core job.

Atlas-verified PandaDoc pricing lists a Free plan at $0, Starter at $19 per seat per month billed annually or $35 monthly, Business at $49 per seat per month billed annually or $65 monthly, and Enterprise with custom per-seat or per-document pricing. The Free plan includes 5 eSignatures per month, the document editor, up to 5 templates, real-time tracking, and 24/7 support. Starter adds unlimited document uploads and eSignatures, custom branding, and the mobile app. Business adds pricing tables, CRM integrations, content library, approval workflows, deal rooms, web forms, and bulk send. Enterprise adds CPQ, workflow automation, smart content, SSO, team workspaces, Notary, and API access.

For contractors, the practical value depends on where in the sales process the document bottleneck sits:

- A roofing contractor who needs a clean replacement proposal with pricing tables and an e-signature block
- A remodeler who presents design-build scopes with images, terms, and payment schedules
- A commercial contractor who submits formal bids with scope language, mixed media, and approval workflow
- A specialty trade who quotes maintenance agreements and wants payment collection inside the document
- An HVAC company that needs change orders, scope-of-work documents, and subcontractor agreements signed quickly

PandaDoc's document editor, rich media support, custom branding, content library, and pricing tables are all built to make the proposal itself the professional asset. The e-signature workflow with real-time tracking, audit trail, and payment collection through Stripe and PayPal means the contractor can send, track, sign, and collect deposit without switching tools.

The limitation is that PandaDoc is not a CRM. It can track document status - sent, viewed, signed - but it does not manage a sales pipeline, track contacts through deal stages, assign owners to leads, or run automated email and text follow-up sequences. A contractor who needs that pipeline structure and follow-up automation will need a separate CRM. Keap is one option. Notably, PandaDoc integrates with CRMs including Pipedrive, HubSpot, and Salesforce on the Business plan and higher. If the choice is proposal automation plus a dedicated sales CRM, compare [PandaDoc vs Pipedrive](/pandadoc-vs-pipedrive/) and [PandaDoc vs Pipeline CRM](/pandadoc-vs-pipeline-crm/).

## Marketing automation vs document automation

The core difference is what each product is built around.

Keap is built around the customer record and the communication lifecycle. The workflow is: a lead enters through a form, gets tagged and assigned to a pipeline stage, triggers an automated email sequence, receives a follow-up task for the office, gets an appointment link, receives a quote, gets an invoice, and enters a nurture or reactivation campaign after the job closes. The contact record is the system of record. The automation engine drives the communication.

PandaDoc is built around the document. The workflow is: create a proposal or quote from a template, add pricing tables and rich media, send it to the prospect, track when it is opened and viewed, collect an e-signature, and optionally collect payment through Stripe or PayPal embedded in the document. The document is the system of record for the sales interaction.

A contractor might use both if the sales process justifies it. Keap manages the lead, sends automated follow-up, tracks the deal through pipeline stages, and triggers a quote or appointment. When the deal reaches the proposal stage, PandaDoc produces the professional document with pricing tables and e-signature. PandaDoc's CRM integrations on the Business plan and higher can connect the deal record to the document, so when the proposal is signed, the CRM can be updated automatically.

But if the contractor can only buy one, the question is which bottleneck is more painful. If leads are going cold and follow-up is the problem, Keap. If proposals are losing deals and document production is the problem, PandaDoc.

## Email, text, and communication depth

Keap's communication depth is its biggest differentiator. Its official pricing page lists email marketing, email broadcasts, one-to-one email messages, newsletters, AI content assistance, AI automation assistance, text marketing, automated text messaging, forms, landing pages, and campaign workflows. For a contractor with a long sales cycle, that depth matters. A roof replacement lead might need financing information, insurance claim education, a case study, a seasonal reminder, and a final check-in over 3 weeks. A remodeler might need separate sequences for kitchen leads, bathroom leads, past clients, and cold estimates. Keap can run all of that automatically.

PandaDoc does not compete on communication depth. It sends documents and tracks them. The real-time tracking shows when a prospect opens, views, and signs. That is useful - it tells the office when to follow up. But it does not send automated email sequences, text reminders, or multi-step nurture campaigns. If the contractor needs those, PandaDoc alone will not provide them.

Decision signal: Keap if campaigns, sequences, and automated follow-up are the job. PandaDoc if the document tracking and e-signature workflow are enough.

## Quotes, invoices, and payment workflow

Both products handle quotes and payments, but from different angles.

Keap handles quotes and invoices inside the CRM. A contact moves through pipeline stages, a quote is created from within the contact record, and the quote converts to an invoice when accepted. Keap's official pricing page lists quotes, invoices, checkout forms, and payment processing as platform features. That means the quote, invoice, and payment are all attached to the customer record and can be triggered by automation. A contractor could set up a sequence where a signed quote automatically generates an invoice and sends a payment link - all without manual intervention.

PandaDoc handles payment collection inside the document itself. A proposal or quote can include pricing tables, and the prospect can pay a deposit or full balance directly from the document through Stripe, PayPal, or other gateways. That is a different approach: the payment is embedded in the document workflow, not the CRM workflow. PandaDoc's pricing tables are more sophisticated than Keap's basic quote-to-ininvoice path, with line items, quantities, discounts, and taxes all rendered in a professional table format.

Decision signal: Keap if quotes and invoices should fire automatically from pipeline stages and automation rules. PandaDoc if the proposal itself should include pricing tables, payment links, and a professional presentation.

## Implementation and adoption

Keap is a setup project. Its own pricing page says required implementation services help users start with strategy consulting, data import, migration, and done-for-you services. The official implementation page lists a one-time investment of $500. Keap is powerful enough that configuration matters: tags, forms, fields, campaigns, pipeline stages, imports, automations, and email templates all need careful planning before the platform delivers value. A contractor office that is still deciding what a lead stage should be called will find Keap too much.

PandaDoc is faster to test. A contractor can sign up for the Free plan, upload an existing proposal template, customize it with branding and pricing tables, send a test document, and collect a signature without paying anything. The value is immediate if the proposal production process is currently manual or template-based in Word or Google Docs. The Business plan adds CRM integrations, content library, and approval workflows, but the core document workflow is testable for free.

The ownership test: Keap needs someone who owns the marketing and follow-up process. PandaDoc needs someone who owns the document workflow. If nobody owns either, buying software will not fix the bottleneck.

## Contractor operations fit

Neither Keap nor PandaDoc should be treated as contractor operations software.

Keap can handle CRM, email, text, sales pipeline, appointment links, quotes, invoices, checkout forms, and payments. That makes it feel closer to an all-in-one business system than PandaDoc. But it still does not replace route scheduling, technician dispatch, inventory, field mobile workflows, or job costing. A contractor using Keap may still need [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), [ServiceTitan](/servicetitan-review/), or another operations platform for the actual work.

PandaDoc is even more clearly a pre-sale document tool. It handles proposals, quotes, contracts, agreements, change orders, e-signatures, and payment collection. Once the deal is signed and the work begins, PandaDoc's job is done.

Map the handoff before buying:

1. Where does the lead enter?
2. Who owns the first follow-up?
3. Where is the quote or proposal created?
4. How does the prospect sign and pay?
5. When does the opportunity become a job?
6. Which system owns dispatch, production, invoices, and job costing?

If the bottleneck is at step 2 - consistent follow-up - Keap. If the bottleneck is at steps 3 and 4 - professional proposals and e-signatures - PandaDoc. If both are bottlenecks, consider using both tools together. If most of the pain is after step 6, neither product is the right first purchase.

## Where Keap falls short for document production

Keap's biggest limitation in this comparison is document sophistication. It can create quotes and invoices, but it does not have a drag-and-drop document editor with rich media, pricing tables, custom branding, content library, and e-signature workflow comparable to PandaDoc. A contractor who needs professional proposals with images, payment schedules, scope language, and a polished presentation will find Keap's document capabilities basic.

The second limitation is price. At $299 per month plus $500 implementation, Keap is a significant investment. If the contractor's core problem is document production - not follow-up and automation - Keap is the wrong tool to buy. PandaDoc can do that job for free on the Free plan or $19 per seat per month on Starter.

The third limitation is setup complexity. Keap touches more of the business process than PandaDoc, which means more decisions: tags, forms, fields, campaigns, pipeline stages, email templates, text sequences, and reporting. Contractors who want a tool that works immediately will find PandaDoc's self-serve Free plan more accessible.

## Where PandaDoc falls short for CRM and follow-up

PandaDoc's biggest limitation is that it is not a CRM. It can track document status - sent, viewed, signed - but it does not manage a sales pipeline with deal stages, contact records, owners, activity history, task assignment, or automated email and text follow-up sequences. A contractor who needs to see all open opportunities on a pipeline board, filter by owner, sort by expected close date, and trigger automated follow-up will not find that in PandaDoc.

The second limitation is no marketing automation. PandaDoc does not send automated email sequences, text reminders, nurture campaigns, or reactivation messages. If leads are going cold because nobody follows up, PandaDoc alone will not solve that problem.

The third limitation is document overage fees. Atlas-verified pricing lists document overage at $2 per document on Starter annual, $3.50 on Starter monthly, and $2.60 on Business monthly. For a contractor sending a high volume of proposals, those per-document fees can add up on lower-tier plans.

## Using both together

For contractors with a mature sales process, Keap and PandaDoc can work together. PandaDoc offers native CRM integrations on the Business plan ($49 per seat per month billed annually) and higher. While the direct Keap integration is not published, PandaDoc integrates with HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, and Zoho CRM - and Keap can connect through Zapier, which both platforms support.

The combined workflow would look like this:

- Keap captures the lead from a form or landing page
- Keap's automation engine sends email and text follow-up sequences
- The lead moves through pipeline stages in Keap
- When the deal reaches the proposal stage, a PandaDoc document is generated
- PandaDoc handles the professional proposal with pricing tables and e-signature
- The signed document and payment status sync back to the CRM record
- Keap continues nurturing the customer after the job closes

The combined cost for a single user would be Keap at $299 per month plus PandaDoc Business at $49 per seat per month, totaling $348 per month. That is a meaningful investment, but if the contractor is losing deals at both the follow-up stage and the proposal stage, the revenue recovered may justify the cost.

For contractors not ready to justify both, start with the bottleneck that is most painful. If leads are disappearing, start with Keap. If proposals are losing deals, start with PandaDoc. Add the other when the first bottleneck is resolved and a new one appears.

## Bottom line for contractors

Choose <a href="/go/keap-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Keap</a> if the real problem is that leads go cold, follow-up is inconsistent, old customer lists sit unused, and nobody has time to run email and text campaigns manually. Keap's CRM, automation builder, email marketing, text marketing, pipeline management, quotes, invoices, and payment processing make it the stronger platform for managing the whole customer communication lifecycle. The $299 per month starting price only makes sense if you will actually use the automation depth to replace manual work.

Choose <a href="/go/pandadoc-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">PandaDoc</a> if the real problem is that proposals and quotes look unprofessional, take too long to produce, or lack a clean e-signature and payment collection path. PandaDoc's document editor, pricing tables, content library, custom branding, e-signature workflow, and payment collection are built to make the proposal itself the sales asset. Start with the Free plan to test the editor and signature workflow, then move to Business at $49 per seat per month for CRM integrations and approval workflows.

If both follow-up and proposal production are bottlenecks, consider using both: Keap to automate the customer lifecycle and PandaDoc to produce the proposal when the deal is ready. The two products can connect through CRM integrations and Zapier.

If the problem is scheduling, dispatch, production, or job costing, skip both for now and look at a contractor operations platform first.
