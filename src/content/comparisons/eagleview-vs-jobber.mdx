# EagleView vs Jobber (2026): Two Different Tools for Roofers

<p>EagleView and Jobber serve different stages of a roofing job. EagleView delivers per-report aerial measurements that roofers use during the estimating phase: roof measurements, pitch, slope, valleys, ridges, penetrations, walls, and property reports. Jobber is a field service management platform for the operational phase: scheduling crews, sending quotes, dispatching techs, invoicing customers, and managing the daily workflow. Most roofing contractors end up using both.</p>

<p><strong>Short verdict:</strong> If your bottleneck is accurate measurements for estimating, start with EagleView. If your bottleneck is daily operations, scheduling, and getting paid, start with Jobber. Many roofers use both because they solve different problems.</p>

## The key difference: pre-work vs. during-work

### EagleView solves the measurement problem

<p>EagleView is not a business management platform. It is a measurement and data service. You order a report, EagleView processes it using aerial imagery and AI (their new Horizon engine), and you receive detailed measurements: roof area in squares, pitch, valleys, ridges, hip lengths, eave lengths, penetrations, walls, and gutter lengths. The report becomes the foundation of your material estimate, labor bid, and insurance claim.</p>

<p>Pricing is per-report with product type and speed tier options. A small Construction Roof Report (20 sq) ranges from $24.25 (Gold tier, slowest) to $32.75 (Bronze tier, fastest). Bid Perfect Residential is $18/report across all tiers. Walls, Windows and Doors Residential ranges from $67.50 (Gold) to $78 (Bronze). Commercial reports, Full House reports, and Platinum/EagleView One pricing require plan selection or sales contact.</p>

<p>The value proposition is straightforward: an accurate EagleView measurement can save more in avoided material waste than the report itself costs, especially on complex roofs where manual measurement is prone to error.</p>

### Jobber solves the operations problem

<p>Jobber is a field service management platform for the operational side of a contracting business: scheduling, dispatching, quoting, invoicing, client communication, payment collection, and basic job costing. It handles the daily workflow between the time a lead calls and the time the final invoice is paid.</p>

<p>Jobber publishes four pricing tiers. Core ($29/mo annual, $49/mo monthly) covers scheduling, quotes, invoices, and payments for a single user. Connect ($99/mo, 1 user or $149/mo team) adds the client hub, automated reminders, QuickBooks Online sync, time tracking, and expenses. Grow ($149/mo 1 user or $299/mo team) adds job costing, advanced quotes, two-way SMS, and custom workflow automations. Plus ($529/mo, 15 users) adds Marketing Suite, Receptionist, Pipeline, onboarding, and premium support.</p>

<p>The value is operational clarity: fewer missed calls, faster quoting, automated follow-ups, cleaner invoicing, and a customer portal that reduces office overhead. For a growing service business, those efficiencies compound quickly.</p>

That same roofer running a 5-person crew may spend $149-299/month on Jobber's Grow plan. The two costs serve different budgets: EagleView is a per-job cost that pays for itself in material savings, while Jobber is an operational overhead that pays for itself in office efficiency.</p>

## Where EagleView wins

### Measurement accuracy for complex roofs

<p>EagleView's aerial measurements are the industry standard for a reason. The company has been processing property imagery for over 25 years, and its database of verified property data is a competitive moat that newer measurement tools have not matched. For complex multi-plane roofs with dormers, valleys, and multiple penetrations, manual measurement error can cost real money in material waste or labor shortfalls. EagleView removes that guesswork.</p>

### Professional reports for insurance claims

<p>For roofing contractors who work with insurance claims, EagleView reports are an established part of the workflow. Insurance adjusters accept EagleView measurements as authoritative, which can accelerate claim processing and reduce disputes. The reports include detailed diagrams with ridge lengths, hip lengths, valley lengths, eave lengths, step flashings, pipe flashings, and wall dimensions that serve as the documentation layer for both the estimate and the claim.</p>

### Bid Perfect and estimating integration

<p>EagleView's Bid Perfect product takes the measurement data further by generating a line-item estimate directly from the report. At $18/report for residential, this bridges the gap between measurement and estimate in a single step. For contractors who quote multiple homes per day, that workflow shortcut can be worth more than the report price.</p>

### Speed tier flexibility

<p>EagleView's Bronze/Silver/Gold tier system lets you choose between speed and cost. Bronze is the fastest and most expensive, Silver is mid-range, and Gold is the most affordable but slowest. A contractor who needs same-day measurements for a time-sensitive bid can pay the Bronze premium. A contractor planning ahead can order Gold-tier reports and save on every one. That flexibility is useful for roofers with mixed urgency levels.</p>

## Where Jobber wins

### Complete operational workflow

<p>Jobber covers the full job lifecycle from first contact to final payment: schedule the job, send a quote, get digital approval, dispatch a tech, collect payment via the mobile app, and send follow-up requests. For a roofer whose main operational pain is keeping the office organized, Jobber addresses the daily chaos that a measurement report alone cannot fix.</p>

### Published pricing and low-risk trial

<p>Jobber publishes every tier's price and offers a 14-day free trial with no credit card required. That makes it easy to evaluate before buying. A contractor can test scheduling, quoting, invoicing, forms, and mobile workflows with real jobs before spending anything. EagleView has no equivalent trial because its pricing is per-report - you pay as you go - but the buying decision is not comparable because the products are not comparable.</p>

### Mobile app with offline support

<p>Jobber's mobile app lets technicians view job details, add photos and notes, track time, fill out forms, and collect payments from the field. Limited offline support means timers, forms, notes, and attachments work without an internet connection and sync later. For roofers working on rooftops with variable reception, that matters.</p>

### Team-friendly user model

<p>Jobber's team plans include multiple users at a predictable price. The Connect team plan covers 5 users at $149/mo annual. The Grow team plan covers 10 users at $299/mo annual. Plus covers 15 users at $529/mo annual. Additional users are $29/mo each. For a roofing company with an owner, a couple of office staff, and a few crew leads, that model is clean and predictable.</p>

## Can you use both together?

<p>The short answer is yes, and many roofing contractors do. The workflow looks like this:</p>

<ol>
<li>Get a lead or schedule a roof inspection</li>
<li>Order an EagleView report to get accurate measurements</li>
<li>Build the estimate using the EagleView data</li>
<li>Send the quote through Jobber and get digital approval</li>
<li>Schedule the crew through Jobber's dispatch calendar</li>
<li>Complete the job, collect payment through Jobber's mobile app</li>
<li>Send follow-up and review requests through Jobber</li>
</ol>

<p>EagleView handles steps 2-3 (the pre-work data and estimate foundation). Jobber handles steps 4-7 (the operational workflow). They complement each other because they operate at different points in the job lifecycle. The EagleView report feeds into the estimate that Jobber sends as a quote.</p>

## When to pick each tool

### Choose EagleView if:

- Measurement accuracy directly affects your material costs and bid competitiveness
- You work on complex commercial or high-end residential roofs
- Insurance claims are a significant part of your revenue
- You need professional property reports (walls, windows, gutters, full house)
- You already have an operational system and just need better measurement data

### Choose Jobber if:

- Your main pain point is daily operations: scheduling, quoting, dispatching, billing
- You have 1-10 techs and need a system that is quick to set up and easy for techs to use
- You want published pricing and the ability to try before buying
- Client communication and automated follow-ups would save your office time
- You do not have dedicated office staff and need a system the owner can manage

### Consider both if:

- You are a roofing contractor who needs accurate measurements AND better daily operations
- Your workflow spans from pre-work estimating through job completion
- You can budget for per-report measurement costs plus a monthly subscription

<p>If you can only start with one, start with whichever bottleneck hurts more. If inaccurate measurements keep costing you money, start with EagleView. If scheduling chaos and slow quoting keep costing you jobs, start with Jobber. Add the other when the first bottleneck is under control.</p>

## Final verdict

<p><strong>Our call:</strong> EagleView for measurement accuracy. Jobber for operational efficiency. Most roofers should evaluate both if their budget allows.</p>

<p><strong>Start with EagleView if:</strong> your estimating process is the weak link. Accurate measurements pay for themselves on complex roofs, and the professional reports support better bids and faster insurance claims. EagleView is not cheap per report, but the cost is variable and directly tied to revenue-generating activity.</p>

<p><strong>Start with Jobber if:</strong> office operations are the bottleneck. A 14-day trial lets you test whether scheduling, quoting, invoicing, and client communication solve the daily chaos without a long implementation. The published pricing and user-model transparency make it easy to budget.</p>

<p><strong>Use both if:</strong> you have the scale and the margin. Many successful roofing contractors treat EagleView reports as a per-job cost of doing accurate work and Jobber as the operational backbone that keeps the business running between jobs. They serve different purposes and together cover the full lifecycle of a roofing project.</p>
