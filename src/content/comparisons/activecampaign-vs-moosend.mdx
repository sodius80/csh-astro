# ActiveCampaign vs Moosend: Automation Power or Budget Value?

ActiveCampaign and Moosend are both email marketing platforms built for small businesses, but they take opposite approaches to pricing and feature delivery. ActiveCampaign gates features across four plans and charges more as you unlock deeper automation, CRM, and segmentation. Moosend puts every Pro feature on a single plan and charges the lowest starting price in the category.

That difference matters for contractors. A roofing company that needs CRM-led automation with lead scoring and conditional logic is looking at a different tool than a plumbing company that wants affordable newsletters, landing pages, and seasonal campaigns. The right choice comes down to three questions: Do you need a built-in CRM? Do you need advanced conditional automation? Is the lowest monthly price the deciding factor?

## Quick verdict

Pick ActiveCampaign if you need a platform that combines email marketing with a built-in CRM. The official pricing page lists four tiers: Starter at $15/mo for 1,000 contacts billed annually, Plus at $49/mo, Pro at $79/mo, and Enterprise at $145/mo. Each tier unlocks more automation actions, segmentation depth, and CRM features. The 14-day free trial lets a contractor test the visual automation builder, lead scoring, and pipeline views before committing.

Pick Moosend if you want the lowest monthly Pro entry point in the email marketing category. The official pricing page shows Pro at $7/mo for 500 contacts when billed annually or $9/mo on monthly billing. Every Pro feature is unlocked from the start: unlimited email campaigns, marketing automation workflows, landing pages, subscription forms, reporting, and SMTP. No tier-gating, no upgrade walls. The 30-day free trial requires no credit card and includes full Pro feature access for up to 1,000 contacts.

Skip both if you are trying to run field operations. Neither ActiveCampaign nor Moosend replaces [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), [ServiceTitan](/servicetitan-review/), or a broader [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) platform. Both tools help contractors communicate with leads and customers through email. They do not dispatch crews, route technicians, manage job costing, or run field workflows.

## When ActiveCampaign makes more sense

ActiveCampaign makes more sense when the contractor needs email marketing and CRM in the same system. The platform started as email marketing software and added CRM functionality over time, so the automation engine is the core strength and the CRM sits alongside it.

A contractor could use ActiveCampaign to:

- send an automated quote follow-up sequence after a website form submission
- tag contacts by service interest, territory, lead source, or job type
- score leads based on email opens, clicks, form activity, and deal status
- trigger a sales task when a lead becomes more engaged
- send seasonal campaigns to the right segment instead of the whole list
- keep email engagement and CRM contact history in the same system

The differentiation is conditional automation logic. ActiveCampaign's visual builder handles multi-step sequences with split paths: if a contact opens a quote follow-up email but does not click the booking link, the next email can take a different path from someone who did click. That level of conditional logic is available starting on the Plus plan at $49/mo.

The built-in CRM is the other factor. Contact records show list membership, tags, engagement data, deal stage, and custom fields. A contractor who wants deal tracking, pipeline visibility, and email history in one place gets that from ActiveCampaign without paying for a separate CRM. For more on how ActiveCampaign compares to CRM-first platforms, see the [ActiveCampaign vs Pipedrive](/activecampaign-vs-pipedrive/) comparison or the [ActiveCampaign vs Keap](/activecampaign-vs-keap/) comparison.

## When Moosend makes more sense

Moosend makes more sense when the contractor wants the lowest monthly price for full-featured email marketing. At $7/mo for 500 contacts billed annually, Moosend Pro is less than half the cost of ActiveCampaign Starter at $15/mo. And every Pro feature is unlocked from day one.

A contractor could use Moosend Pro to:

- send unlimited email campaigns to up to 500 contacts
- build landing pages and subscription forms
- run marketing automation workflows with behavioral triggers
- segment contacts by service interest, location, or customer type
- review campaign reporting and A/B test results
- send transactional emails through SMTP

Moosend also offers something ActiveCampaign does not: pay-as-you-go email credits. For contractors who send emails seasonally or infrequently, credits start at $350 for 350,000 emails and never expire. A roofing company that runs one storm-season blast and one spring inspection reminder could find credits cheaper than 12 months of any subscription.

The 30-day free trial is longer than ActiveCampaign's 14-day trial and requires no credit card. That gives a contractor more time to test automations, templates, and campaign workflows before paying. The trial includes full Pro feature access for up to 1,000 contacts, so the testing period is not feature-limited.

## Pricing comparison

Both platforms are affordable, but they charge for different things and gate features differently.

Atlas-verified ActiveCampaign pricing at 1,000 contacts lists annual-billed monthly prices of Starter $15/mo, Plus $49/mo, Pro $79/mo, and Enterprise $145/mo. Monthly billing is higher: Starter $19/mo, Plus $59/mo, Professional $99/mo, and Enterprise $179/mo. Pricing scales by contact count, so costs increase as the list grows beyond 1,000 contacts.

Atlas-verified Moosend pricing lists Pro at $7/mo when paid annually or $9/mo on monthly billing for 500 contacts. The Pro plan includes unlimited email campaigns and sends, marketing automation workflows, landing pages, subscription forms, reporting, and SMTP. Moosend+ and Enterprise are custom-quote plans. Pay-as-you-go email credits are published separately: 350,000 credits for $350, 500,000 for $500, 750,000 for $750, and 1,000,000 for $1,000.

The simple rule: if you need a built-in CRM and advanced automation, ActiveCampaign delivers more value per dollar despite the higher starting price. If email marketing is the only job and you want it as cheap as possible, Moosend is the stronger budget pick.

## Email automation depth

ActiveCampaign wins the automation depth comparison. The visual builder supports conditional logic, split paths, and multi-branch workflows. A contractor can build a sequence where a contact who opens a quote email gets a case study follow-up, a contact who clicks the booking link gets a confirmation, and a contact who does nothing gets a different reminder. That level of conditional logic is what makes ActiveCampaign stand out in the email marketing category.

The trade-off is feature gating. Starter at $15/mo limits automation to 5 actions per automation. Unlimited automation actions require Plus at $49/mo. Predictive sending, split automations, and conditional content require Pro at $79/mo. A contractor who needs the full automation depth is paying $49 to $79 per month, not $15.

Moosend's automation is included in full from the Pro plan. There is no tiered limit on published automation workflows. The visual builder handles welcome sequences, quote follow-up paths, reactivation campaigns, and behavior-triggered workflows based on page views and purchases. For most contractor email marketing needs, that is sufficient.

What Moosend lacks is the multi-branch split-path complexity that ActiveCampaign offers. A contractor who needs conditional logic like "if opened but not clicked, send this; if clicked, send that; if neither, send something else" will find ActiveCampaign's builder more capable. A contractor who needs straightforward welcome sequences and time-delayed follow-ups will find Moosend adequate.

Decision signal: choose ActiveCampaign if you plan to build complex, conditional email sequences with multiple branches. Choose Moosend if your automation needs are welcome sequences, follow-up timers, and behavior triggers without complex branching.

## CRM and contact management

ActiveCampaign has a built-in CRM. Moosend does not. This is the single biggest functional difference between the two platforms.

ActiveCampaign's CRM includes contact records with deal tracking, pipeline stages, lead scoring, task management, and sales reporting. A contractor can see which contacts are in which deal stage, who owns each opportunity, and what the next activity should be. The CRM is not a replacement for a dedicated sales CRM like [Pipedrive](/pipedrive-review/) or a full field service platform, but it is functional for a contractor who wants email and deal tracking in one system.

Moosend is email marketing only. There is no contact pipeline, no deal stages, no lead scoring, and no sales reporting. If a contractor needs CRM functionality, they would need to use Moosend alongside a separate CRM tool. That is not necessarily a disadvantage if the contractor already has a CRM or prefers to keep email marketing and sales pipeline separate.

## Feature gating vs all-in-one

The pricing philosophy difference is the core of this comparison.

ActiveCampaign gates features across four tiers. Starter at $15/mo includes 5 automation actions per automation, limited segmentation, and standard CRM/e-commerce. Plus at $49/mo unlocks unlimited automation actions, standard segmentation, and lead scoring. Pro at $79/mo adds predictive sending, split automations, conditional content, and advanced segmentation. Enterprise at $145/mo adds custom objects, SSO, and dedicated support. A contractor who needs lead scoring pays at least $49/mo. A contractor who needs predictive sending pays at least $79/mo.

Moosend puts all Pro features on a single plan at $7/mo. There is no tier-gating. Unlimited email campaigns, full marketing automation, landing pages, subscription forms, A/B testing, real-time analytics, AI Writer, and SMTP are all included from the first paid tier. A contractor at $7/mo gets the same feature set as a contractor at a higher contact count paying more.

The trade-off: ActiveCampaign's gated features are more advanced than what Moosend offers on any tier. Predictive sending, conditional content, and custom objects do not exist on Moosend at all. Moosend gives you everything it has for $7/mo, but what it has is a smaller feature set than ActiveCampaign's top tiers.

## Free trial comparison

Neither platform offers a permanent free plan. Both offer time-limited free trials, but the terms differ.

ActiveCampaign offers a 14-day free trial. The trial gives access to the platform's features, but the short window may not be enough time for a contractor to build automations, import a contact list, design templates, and evaluate whether the platform fits their workflow.

Moosend offers a 30-day free trial with no credit card required. The trial includes all core features for up to 1,000 contacts with unlimited email sends. Trial limitations include 1 published automation workflow, 1 unpublished landing page, 1 unpublished subscription form, 1 custom report, and 2 team members. Despite those caps, the trial gives a contractor twice as long as ActiveCampaign to evaluate the platform.

For contractors who want a permanent free starting point, neither tool is the right pick. [MailerLite](/mailerlite-vs-moosend/) offers a free plan for up to 250 subscribers and 2,500 monthly emails. That is a genuine free tier, not a trial. See the [free CRM for contractors](/best-free-crm-for-contractors/) guide for free options across the CRM category.

## Integrations and ecosystem

ActiveCampaign lists 1,000+ integrations through its native marketplace plus Zapier access on all plans. The ecosystem covers email, CRM, e-commerce, productivity tools, and AI integrations including Claude and ChatGPT via MCP. For contractors using WordPress, Shopify, Zapier, or payment processors, ActiveCampaign's integration library is comprehensive.

Moosend lists 80+ integrations including WordPress, Shopify, WooCommerce, Salesforce, and Zapier. The library is smaller than ActiveCampaign's, and the user community is also smaller, which means fewer third-party tutorials and templates. Moosend does support API access, SMTP, and transactional email on Pro, which gives developers flexibility.

For most contractors, both integration libraries cover the basics: WordPress, Zapier, and forms. The difference matters when the contractor relies on a specific integration or wants AI-assisted content creation tools built into the platform.

## Where ActiveCampaign falls short

ActiveCampaign's biggest limitation for budget-conscious contractors is price. The $15/mo Starter plan limits automation to 5 actions per automation. Lead scoring requires Plus at $49/mo. Predictive sending and conditional content require Pro at $79/mo. A contractor who needs the full feature set is paying significantly more than Moosend's $7/mo all-in price.

The second limitation is no pay-as-you-go option. Contractors who send emails only a few times per year cannot avoid a monthly or annual subscription. Moosend's credit system is better for occasional senders.

The third limitation is the learning curve. ActiveCampaign's automation builder is powerful but complex. A contractor who buys ActiveCampaign and never builds the follow-up sequences is paying for a more expensive contact database. The platform needs an owner who will maintain campaigns, tags, list hygiene, and reporting.

## Where Moosend falls short

Moosend's biggest limitation is the lack of a built-in CRM. Contractors who need deal tracking, pipeline management, and lead scoring alongside email marketing will not find those features in Moosend. ActiveCampaign includes all of those, while Moosend is strictly an email marketing platform.

The second limitation is automation depth. Moosend's automation builder handles welcome sequences, time-delayed follow-ups, and behavior-triggered campaigns well. But it does not offer the multi-branch conditional logic, predictive sending, or conditional content that ActiveCampaign provides on higher tiers. For contractors with straightforward automation needs, this is fine. For contractors who want complex, behavior-driven sequences, it is a real gap.

The third limitation is integration depth. Moosend lists 80+ integrations compared to ActiveCampaign's 1,000+. The gap matters when the contractor relies on a specific tool or platform. Moosend's smaller user community also means fewer third-party tutorials and templates.

## How to choose

Start with the CRM question. If you need a built-in CRM with deal stages, pipeline views, and lead scoring, ActiveCampaign is the only option here. Moosend does not have those features and is not trying to compete on CRM depth.

If CRM is not part of your requirement, the decision comes down to automation depth versus price. ActiveCampaign's conditional logic, split paths, and predictive sending are more advanced than anything Moosend offers. But those features require Plus at $49/mo or Pro at $79/mo, not the $15/mo Starter plan. A contractor who genuinely needs that depth should budget for the higher tier.

If the need is straightforward email marketing: newsletters, seasonal campaigns, landing pages, and basic automation, Moosend at $7/mo delivers more value per dollar. All features are unlocked, the trial is longer, and pay-as-you-go credits are available for seasonal senders. For a direct budget comparison with a free-plan competitor, see [MailerLite vs Moosend](/mailerlite-vs-moosend/).

The failure mode for both platforms is identical: the tool sits unused because nobody writes campaigns, cleans the list, reviews reports, or builds the next follow-up sequence. Buying the cheaper tool or the more powerful tool does not solve the ownership problem. Whoever owns the marketing needs to own the platform.

## Final verdict

ActiveCampaign is the stronger choice for contractors who need a marketing platform with CRM depth. The automation builder, lead scoring, segmentation, and deal tracking make it the more complete tool for a contractor who wants to run sophisticated email campaigns alongside contact management. The higher price is justified when the business uses the features that justify the upgrade.

Moosend is the stronger choice for contractors who want the lowest monthly Pro price for full-featured email marketing. At $7/mo with all Pro features unlocked, a 30-day no-card trial, and pay-as-you-go credits for seasonal senders, it delivers more value per dollar than ActiveCampaign for straightforward email marketing work. The trade-off is no CRM, no mobile app, and fewer integrations.

My practical call: choose <a href="/go/activecampaign-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">ActiveCampaign</a> if CRM and advanced conditional automation are part of your requirement. Choose <a href="/go/moosend-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Moosend</a> if email marketing is the only job and you want it at the lowest price with all features included.
