# ArboStar vs GorillaDesk: Tree Care Specialist vs Budget FSM

<p>ArboStar and GorillaDesk both appear in tree service software conversations, but they serve very different contractor profiles. ArboStar is a tree-care specialist built for established multi-crew arborist companies that need arborist CRM, live job maps, equipment tracking, and field documentation. GorillaDesk is a budget-friendly field service platform for small pest control, lawn care, and tree service crews that want transparent route-based pricing and a self-service trial. The right choice depends on crew size, work complexity, and whether tree-specific depth justifies a custom sales process. See our <a href="/arbostar-review/">ArboStar review</a> and <a href="/gorilladesk-review/">GorillaDesk review</a>.</p>

## Buying split: operational complexity and crew scale

The primary question is not just how many technicians you have. It is whether your work is complex arborist operations or simpler route-based service calls.

ArboStar makes more sense when the crew dispatches to tree removals, pruning jobs, stump grinding, and commercial arborist work. The arborist CRM records client tree history, the live job maps help route chipper trucks and bucket trucks, the equipment tracking manages chainsaws, chippers, and rigging gear, and the field documentation captures safety forms and job photos specific to tree work. These are workflows that a general field service platform does not replicate.

GorillaDesk makes more sense when the daily work is recurring route-based service: pest control treatments, lawn applications, pool service visits, or light tree trimming on a predictable schedule. The drag-and-drop calendar, automated reminders, invoice workflow, and mobile app are built for the recurring service cycle. GorillaDesk supports tree service as one of several trades, but it does not have IPM modules, termite inspection workflows, chemical compliance tracking, or arborist-specific CRM records. See our <a href="/best-pest-control-software/">best pest control software roundup</a> for pest-specific alternatives.

Use a simple test. If the crew manages equipment-heavy tree jobs across multiple sites with different crew compositions per job, start with ArboStar. If the crew runs predictable recurring routes and mainly needs scheduling, invoicing, and customer communication at a known price, start with GorillaDesk.

## Pricing reality and contract questions

GorillaDesk publishes three clear plan tiers. Basic starts at $49 per month or $539 per year for the first route or schedule, including unlimited admin users, mobile devices, invoicing, estimates, basic routing limited to 25 stops, payment processing via Stripe and Square, and 20 built-in reports. Pro starts at $99 per month or $1,089 per year and adds the customer online portal, online booking, drive matrix routing, review generation, documents with e-signatures, QuickBooks Online sync, GPS device tracking, and Zapier integration. Growth starts at $149 per month or $1,639 per year and adds map estimation tools, job magnet route optimization, sales pipeline tracking, custom fields, and multi-branch support. Additional routes or technician schedules increase the cost beyond the base price. A 14-day free trial gives full access with no credit card required.

ArboStar uses custom pricing with a starting point of $250 per month on its official pricing page, but requires a sales conversation for the final quote. The price depends on company size, license count, required features, integrations, and platform usage. Annual billing offers two months free. Multiple-license discounts are available. Because ArboStar requires a demo to get a real price, it is harder to budget for before the sales call.

## Workflow fit: specialist depth vs general simplicity

### Scheduling and dispatch

GorillaDesk's calendar handles recurring service routes well. The drag-and-drop interface, automated appointment reminders, and basic routing (25 stops on Basic, unlimited on Pro with drive matrix) work for crews that visit the same properties on a predictable cycle. The Pro plan adds GPS device tracking and drive-time routing between jobs.

ArboStar's scheduling goes deeper for tree crews. Live job maps, GPS visibility, route planning, and equipment assignment matter when crews need a chipper, bucket truck, and specific tools to arrive together at the right site. Tree jobs are not interchangeable service calls. The equipment needs, crew composition, and site conditions change per job. ArboStar's map and dispatch features reflect that reality.

### Customer management

GorillaDesk includes a customer CRM with contact management, service history, notes, and communication records. The Pro plan adds a customer online portal for 24/7 self-service and online booking from a website or portal. For a small pest or lawn company, that depth is usually sufficient. For contractors evaluating CRM options across field service products, see our <a href="/jobber-vs-housecall-pro/">Jobber vs Housecall Pro comparison</a> for a broader view of customer management approaches.

ArboStar's arborist CRM records client tree history, past work, equipment notes, and service records specific to tree care. For a company that returns to the same properties for pruning, removals, plant health, and storm cleanup, that history matters. A salesperson can see prior work, and a crew can see photos and notes from the last visit without digging through files. For a broader look at CRM options across trades, see our <a href="/best-crm-for-contractors/">best CRM for contractors roundup</a>.

### Mobile field tools

Both products have mobile apps. GorillaDesk's mobile app lets technicians view schedules, clock in and out, add notes, upload photos, and collect payment in the field. An offline mode caches current-week calendar data after the app is loaded online first, though job and invoice details may be read-only offline. For a deeper look at GorillaDesk's mobile and trial experience, see our <a href="/gorilladesk-review/">GorillaDesk review</a>.

ArboStar's mobile app covers job details, notes, photos, schedule updates, estimates, invoices, and real-time job status. For tree crews, the difference is what the mobile app connects to: equipment assignment, crew details, safety forms, and map-based job context that goes beyond an address and a service note. For a full breakdown of ArboStar's field workflows, see our <a href="/arbostar-review/">ArboStar review</a>.

### Equipment and material tracking

ArboStar has dedicated equipment tracking for vehicles, tools, chainsaws, chippers, and rigging gear. It covers maintenance schedules, fuel costs, and GPS-linked assets. For a tree care company, that matters because equipment failures and misplacement directly affect whether jobs get done.

GorillaDesk has GPS device tracking on the Pro plan and material tracking for chemical use records, but it does not have a dedicated equipment management module. For pest control companies tracking chemical application, the material tracking feature is useful. For tree companies that need to know where the chipper and bucket truck are, it falls short.

## Where ArboStar wins

### Tree-specific CRM and service history

ArboStar's arborist CRM is built for tree-care customer management. Contacts, leads, quotes, job history, communication records, and service history are all tree-work aware. For arborist companies managing hundreds of client properties, that depth matters.

### Live job maps and equipment tracking

Tree work depends on more than an address and appointment time. The right crew, truck, chipper, equipment, access notes, and weather affect the day. ArboStar's map and GPS positioning give managers a clearer view of where work is happening and which resources are tied up. Equipment tracking covers maintenance schedules, fuel costs, and GPS-linked assets. See our <a href="/best-equipment-tracking-software-contractors/">best equipment tracking software roundup</a> for a broader view of contractor tracking tools.

### Field documentation for arborist work

Safety forms, hazard notes, job photos, and crew instructions are central to tree work in a way they are not for general service calls. ArboStar's mobile workflow supports that documentation attached to specific tree jobs.

### Estimating with field context

ArboStar's estimating workflow includes pre-entered rates, work types, job size, equipment needs, crew composition, photos, notes, and digital approval. For tree proposals that depend on site conditions, that detail can reduce the gap between what sales promised and what the crew finds on site.

## Where GorillaDesk wins

### Transparent pricing and instant setup

GorillaDesk publishes three pricing tiers with exact dollar amounts. No demo required, no sales conversation, no custom quote process. Small crews can sign up for a 14-day free trial without a credit card and be running real jobs the same day. That speed and transparency is the main advantage for operators who need software quickly.

### Affordable entry for small crews

At $49 per month for the Basic plan including unlimited admin users and mobile devices, GorillaDesk is accessible for solo operators and very small crews. ArboStar starts from $250 per month and requires a sales demo, which is a higher barrier for companies just getting organized. For a direct comparison with another budget-friendly option, see our <a href="/arbostar-vs-jobber/">ArboStar vs Jobber comparison</a>.

### Multi-trade flexibility

GorillaDesk supports pest control, lawn care, pool service, tree service, cleaning, and handyman work. For companies that run multiple service lines and want one platform for all of them, GorillaDesk's generalist approach is an advantage. ArboStar is built specifically for tree care and does not serve other trades. For a broader view of field service alternatives, see our <a href="/field-service-software-alternatives/">best field service software roundup</a>.

### No contracts and free data migration

GorillaDesk has no setup fees, no contracts, and free data migration. Month-to-month billing is available, and accounts can be frozen for seasonal businesses. That flexibility is valuable for small companies that want to test software without a long-term commitment.

## Where each product is the wrong fit

ArboStar is the wrong fit for a solo pest control operator, a small lawn care crew, or a one-person tree trimmer who mainly needs scheduling and invoicing. The custom pricing, tree-specific features, and implementation effort are wasted on operations that just need a simple dispatch-to-invoice workflow.

GorillaDesk is the wrong fit for a multi-crew tree care company that needs live job maps, equipment tracking, arborist CRM records, and field documentation specific to tree work. The generalist scheduling and material tracking will not replace the tree-specific workflows that ArboStar provides. GorillaDesk is also the wrong fit for contractors requiring QuickBooks Desktop integration, since it supports QuickBooks Online only.

Both products can be wrong for a general contractor or construction remodeler that needs phase-based project management. Those buyers should compare <a href="/buildertrend-vs-coconstruct/">Buildertrend or CoConstruct</a> instead.

## Evaluation plan for a fair side-by-side test

For GorillaDesk, sign up for the 14-day free trial. Build the real schedule, add a test customer, create an estimate, convert it to a job, run a technician through the mobile app for a full day, and test QuickBooks Online sync if you use it. Note whether the routing, invoicing, and reporting cover your actual workflow. If tree-specific features like equipment tracking and arborist CRM are on your list, note whether GorillaDesk's general approach is acceptable or whether you need more.

For ArboStar, book the demo. Ask the sales team to walk through a real day with multiple crews, a chipper, a bucket truck, and a late emergency call. Confirm that the live map, equipment tracking, and arborist CRM actually reduce office-to-field friction. Get the full quoted price including license count, integrations, implementation, and support. Ask what is included at that price and whether annual billing gives two months free.

The winner is the system that fits the type of work the business actually does. If the work is recurring route-based service across pest, lawn, and light tree work, GorillaDesk is the right starting point. If the work is complex tree care with multiple crews and equipment-heavy days, ArboStar's specialization is worth the higher cost and longer setup.

## FAQ

### Is GorillaDesk or ArboStar better for a small tree care company?

GorillaDesk is the lower-cost starting point for a very small tree crew that mainly needs scheduling, quotes, invoicing, and routing. ArboStar becomes worth the price when the company has multiple crews, equipment tracking needs, and a desire to capture tree-specific service history.

### Which is cheaper, GorillaDesk or ArboStar?

GorillaDesk starts at $49 per month with published tier pricing. ArboStar starts from $250 per month with a custom quote. For any crew size, GorillaDesk is less expensive at the entry level. GorillaDesk also offers a 14-day free trial with no credit card, while ArboStar offers a demo only. For a deeper look at GorillaDesk's pest-control-specific features, see our <a href="/gorilladesk-vs-pestpac/">GorillaDesk vs PestPac comparison</a>.

### Does ArboStar offer a free trial?

ArboStar offers a free demo rather than a self-service trial. The demo is the point where buyers should ask to see estimating, live maps, QuickBooks sync, mobile field updates, and the exact quote for their crew count. GorillaDesk offers a 14-day free trial with no credit card required.

### Can GorillaDesk handle tree care scheduling?

GorillaDesk can schedule tree service jobs like any other recurring route or one-off job. It does not offer tree-specific features like live job maps, equipment tracking, or arborist CRM records. For basic tree company scheduling and invoicing, GorillaDesk works. For multi-crew tree operations with complex equipment needs, ArboStar is the better fit.

### Does ArboStar work for pest control or lawn care?

ArboStar is built specifically for tree care and arborist operations. A pest control or lawn care company would find it overbuilt and paying for tree-specific features they will not use. GorillaDesk is the better fit for multi-trade operators that serve pest, lawn, and tree customers from one platform.

### Can I use GorillaDesk and move to ArboStar later?

Yes. GorillaDesk has no contracts and month-to-month billing, so switching is operationally straightforward. If your tree business grows into needing live maps, equipment tracking, and arborist CRM, you can migrate when that need becomes pressing. GorillaDesk offers free data migration from other systems, and ArboStar includes onboarding and data migration in its setup process.

### Does GorillaDesk include SMS texting?

No. SMS texting is a separate add-on. It requires a $5 per month local number fee plus purchased SMS credit packages ($50 for 1,000 texts, $100 for 2,500 texts, or $200 for 8,000 texts). ArboStar includes communication tools within its platform, though buyers should confirm which features are included at the quoted price.

### Does ArboStar integrate with QuickBooks?

ArboStar lists QuickBooks among its integrations and also references Xero, payment apps, VoIP, messaging, Google tools, Zapier, and API options. GorillaDesk includes QuickBooks Online sync on the Pro plan and above. Neither product supports QuickBooks Desktop. Buyers should confirm whether their exact QuickBooks version and workflow are included.
