<p>Jobber and Workiz both serve small service businesses, but they are optimized for different days. Jobber is built around scheduled work, recurring service, and a cleaner all-around experience. Workiz is built around call-driven dispatch, same-day jobs, and tighter phone-to-revenue visibility.</p>

<p><strong>Disclosure:</strong> The Jobber link on this page is an affiliate link, which means ContractorSoftwareHub may earn a commission if you sign up at no extra cost to you. We do not currently have an affiliate relationship with Workiz. Our recommendation does not change based on that.</p>

<p><strong>Short verdict:</strong> If you run a typical small home service business, Jobber is the safer default. If your day starts with the phone ringing and jobs reshuffling in real time, Workiz is the more specialized fit.</p>

## Key decision factors

### Does Workiz really have a free plan?

<p>Yes. Workiz Lite is a permanent free tier for up to two users with scheduling, invoicing, and basic CRM. It is useful for testing the platform with real jobs, but the phone system, route optimization, and inventory features live higher in the stack.</p>

### Can either replace ServiceTitan?

<p>Neither Jobber nor Workiz matches ServiceTitan’s depth in pricebook management, marketing attribution, or enterprise reporting. For contractors under roughly 15 to 20 technicians, though, both cover the core workflow at a fraction of the cost. Workiz comes closer on dispatch and phone features; Jobber comes closer on quoting and customer-facing polish.</p>

### Quoting workflow and conversion tracking

<p>Jobber has the stronger quoting system. Multi-option quotes, automated follow-up, and easier online approval make it a better fit for contractors who win work through polished proposals rather than fast call handling.</p>

### Simplicity and time to value

<p>Jobber is easier to get running quickly. Import customers, set up services, connect QuickBooks, and you are scheduling jobs. Workiz is more feature-rich around dispatch, phones, and inventory, but that also means more configuration and more moving parts to learn.</p>

### Client self-service versus phone-first workflow

<p>Jobber’s Client Hub is a real advantage for repeat-customer businesses. Clients can request work, approve quotes, and pay online without calling the office. Workiz is less about self-serve portal flow and more about controlling inbound calls, dispatch, and payment collection from the phone outward.</p>

### Built-in phone system, inventory, and routing

<p>Workiz’s biggest edge is the operating stack around reactive work: integrated VoIP, call tracking, recording, IVR, route optimization, and inventory management. If the phone is the primary lead channel, that can outweigh Jobber’s cleaner general experience.</p>

## Where Jobber wins

### Better fit for scheduled and recurring work

<p>If your week is planned in advance — landscaping, recurring cleaning, maintenance visits, pest control, or other appointment-based service — Jobber’s calendar-first workflow is the cleaner match.</p>

### Easier onboarding

<p>Jobber is usually the safer recommendation for small contractors because the interface is easier to learn and the daily workflow is calmer. New hires generally get comfortable faster.</p>

### Stronger customer experience

<p>The client portal, quoting flow, and overall polish are better for businesses that care about ongoing customer relationships and office efficiency, not just dispatch speed.</p>

### Better general-purpose FSM default

<p>If you want a broad field-service platform rather than a dispatch-and-phone specialist, Jobber is the better default choice across more service business types.</p>

## Where Workiz wins

### Built-in phone system

<p>This is Workiz’s clearest differentiator. Calls are logged to the customer record, recording is available, and you get tighter visibility from first call to closed job without stitching together a separate VoIP stack.</p>

### Better for same-day, reactive dispatch

<p>Locksmiths, garage door companies, appliance repair, and emergency HVAC or plumbing teams often operate from live inbound demand rather than a tidy weekly calendar. Workiz reflects that reality better.</p>

### Stronger point-of-service collection flow

<p>For single-visit service calls, Workiz’s text-to-pay workflow is a practical advantage. The tech can finish the job, send a payment link, and get paid before leaving.</p>

### Better visibility into calls and lead sources

<p>If you care about which ads or phone leads actually turn into revenue, Workiz gives you tighter phone-centric attribution than Jobber does out of the box.</p>

## Final verdict

<p><strong>Our call:</strong> Jobber for most contractors. Workiz if your business runs through the phone.</p>

<p><strong>Choose Jobber if:</strong> you run scheduled or recurring work, want a polished all-around field service platform, and care more about ease of use, client experience, and general reliability than advanced phone features.</p>

<p><strong>Choose Workiz if:</strong> your business is driven by inbound calls, same-day dispatch, and on-the-spot payment collection. If your day starts with the phone ringing and jobs reshuffling in real time, Workiz is built more specifically for that operating model.</p>

## FAQ

### Is Jobber or Workiz better for a small service business?

<p>For most small home service businesses, Jobber is the safer default. It is easier to learn, handles scheduled and recurring work well, and offers a cleaner overall experience. Workiz is the better choice when inbound phone calls and same-day dispatch are central to the business.</p>

### Which is cheaper, Jobber or Workiz?

<p>Jobber starts lower at roughly $29/month on the Core plan for one user. Workiz’s Standard plan starts around $65/month billed annually and includes two users. Jobber is the cheaper entry point, but the real comparison depends on team size and which features you need.</p>

### Does Workiz have a built-in phone system?

<p>Yes, and it is Workiz’s biggest differentiator. The built-in phone system includes call tracking, recording, and automatic logging to the customer record. Jobber does not offer an equivalent native phone system.</p>

### Is Jobber better for landscapers than Workiz?

<p>Usually yes. Jobber’s calendar-based scheduling, recurring job support, and client portal are a better fit for landscaping companies that plan work in advance. Workiz is better suited to businesses where the day changes constantly in response to inbound demand.</p>
