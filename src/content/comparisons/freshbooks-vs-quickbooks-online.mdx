# FreshBooks vs QuickBooks Online for Contractors: Which Fits?

FreshBooks and QuickBooks Online are the two accounting tools contractors ask about most. They both handle invoicing, expense tracking, and basic financial reporting, but they're built for fundamentally different problems. FreshBooks is built for service businesses that bill by time and need the invoice-to-payment workflow to be fast. QuickBooks Online is built for growing contractors who need real accounting controls: project profitability, payroll integration, inventory, and compatibility with the tools and accountants they already use. For a deeper look at each tool, read the [FreshBooks review](/freshbooks-review/) and the [QuickBooks Online review](/quickbooks-online-review/).

The decision is not really about price, though FreshBooks starts cheaper at $23/mo versus QuickBooks at $38/mo. It's about whether the contractor's biggest bottleneck is getting paid faster or getting the books right. A solo electrician who sends five invoices a month and tracks hours on a phone may find FreshBooks Lite sufficient. A three-person roofing outfit with a bookkeeper, vendor bills, and project profitability needs will get more value from QuickBooks Plus at $115/mo.

## The buying split

### FreshBooks is an invoicing-first tool for service businesses

FreshBooks is built for contractors who bill by time or project and need the invoice-to-payment workflow to be fast. The official pricing page lists Lite at $23/mo for 5 billable clients, Plus at $43/mo for 50 clients, Premium at $70/mo for unlimited clients, and Select at custom quote. A current promotional offer of 90% off for several months is advertised, but standard rates are what matter for long-term budgeting. Add-ons include Team Members at $11/user/mo, Advanced Payments at $20/mo, and FreshBooks Payroll at $40/mo plus $6/user/mo. A 30-day trial with no credit card and a 30-day money-back guarantee are advertised. For a full breakdown, see the [FreshBooks review](/freshbooks-review/).

The core workflow is: track time, log expenses, create and send professional invoices, accept payments, and automate late-payment reminders. That makes FreshBooks the stronger choice when invoicing speed drives cash flow. The automated payment reminders, scheduled late fees, checkout links, and instant payouts are designed to shorten the gap between sending an invoice and getting paid. For a contractor whose biggest cash-flow problem is slow-paying clients, those features solve a real problem.

The limit is accounting depth. FreshBooks added double-entry accounting on Plus and above, but it isn't built for bank reconciliation automation the way QuickBooks is. It doesn't do job costing, progress billing, AIA forms, or inventory management. It has project profitability on Premium, but cannot track materials, labor, and subcontractor costs at the job level the way contractor accounting requires. There's no 1099 contractor management, and the integration ecosystem is much smaller than QuickBooks.

### QuickBooks Online is a full accounting platform with ecosystem depth

QuickBooks Online is built for contractors who need more than invoicing. Its official pricing page lists Simple Start at $38/mo for 1 user, Essentials at $75/mo for 3 users, Plus at $115/mo for 5 users, and Advanced at $275/mo for 25 users. The same page currently advertises promotional rates of 50% off for 3 months, but standard rates apply after. The page also lists a no-cost plan with severe limitations: 1 user, 2 invoices per month, and no accountant access. It isn't practical for an active contractor. The real starting point is Simple Start at $38/mo. A 30-day free trial is available. For a complete analysis, see the [QuickBooks Online review](/quickbooks-online-review/).

The core advantage is ecosystem. QuickBooks integrates with 700-plus apps including Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan, Buildertrend, AccuLynx, JobNimbus, Procore, and CoConstruct. Most field service and construction management tools have native QuickBooks sync for invoicing, payments, and customer data. If a contractor uses any operations software that needs to talk to accounting, QuickBooks is the default hub. For US contractors, it's also the platform most accountants and bookkeepers already know.

The second advantage is job costing. The Projects feature on Plus ($115/mo) and Advanced ($275/mo) tracks per-job profit and loss. Class Tracking breaks profitability down by trade, crew, or division. That's not construction-grade job costing, but it's meaningful for contractors who need to see whether individual jobs are profitable.

The third advantage is payroll. QuickBooks Payroll is a separate subscription starting at $50/mo plus $5/employee (Core), $80/mo plus $8/employee (Premium), or $125/mo plus $10/employee (Elite). Full-service payroll includes auto payroll, tax filings, and W-2s. Contractors can also use third-party payroll providers like Gusto that integrate with QuickBooks.

The limit is complexity. QuickBooks can feel bloated for a solo contractor who just needs to send a few invoices per month. The learning curve for job costing setup is real, and customer support quality varies by plan tier. A 3-person team would need Essentials ($75/mo), an active contractor with project profitability needs would need Plus ($115/mo), and a larger operation wanting advanced reporting would need Advanced ($275/mo).

## Where FreshBooks wins

### Invoicing speed and payment collection

FreshBooks is built around the invoice-to-payment workflow. Recurring invoices, automated payment reminders, online payment links, and client portal access are included without add-ons. For service businesses where cash flow depends on invoice collection, the automated reminders alone save meaningful time. QuickBooks can send invoices and accept payments, but the automated reminder and follow-up system is not as deeply integrated.

### Time tracking that feeds invoices

Start a timer from the mobile app, categorize it to a client or project, and add those hours to an invoice in two taps. For contractors who bill by the hour, this is the workflow FreshBooks nails better than any cloud accounting tool. QuickBooks doesn't include time tracking in its core plans. QuickBooks Time (formerly TSheets) is a separate add-on at $20/mo plus $8/user/mo.

### Mobile app for field workflows

The FreshBooks mobile app handles the core workflows: start a timer, snap a receipt, send an invoice, check payment status. For contractors who aren't sitting at a desk, that matters. The app holds strong ratings on both iOS and Android. QuickBooks has a capable mobile app too, but it is tuned for receipt capture, mileage tracking, and dashboard access rather than the time-to-invoice flow that service businesses need.

### Simpler learning curve

FreshBooks is designed to be usable without accounting expertise. A contractor can sign up, create a professional invoice, and send it within minutes. QuickBooks has more depth but also more complexity, especially when setting up chart of accounts, classes, and projects. For a contractor who doesn't want to learn accounting mechanics, FreshBooks is the lower-friction entry point.

### Evaluation risk

A 30-day trial with no credit card and a 30-day money-back guarantee reduce the risk of testing FreshBooks with real invoices and real clients. A contractor can send real invoices, connect a real bank account, and decide whether the client limit or feature set binds before paying anything.

## Where QuickBooks Online wins

### Project profitability and job costing

QuickBooks Projects on the Plus plan ($115/mo) and Advanced plan ($275/mo) tracks per-job income and expenses, runs profitability reports by project, and connects to time tracking. Class Tracking breaks down profit by trade, crew, or division. For contractors who need to know whether individual jobs are profitable, QuickBooks is the stronger tool. FreshBooks has project profitability on Premium ($70/mo) but with less depth and no cost-class breakdown. Neither tool does construction-grade job costing, but QuickBooks is closer.

### Accountant and bookkeeper familiarity

In the US market, QuickBooks is the accounting platform most bookkeepers and CPAs already know. If a contractor works with an outside accountant, choosing QuickBooks eliminates the friction of teaching a new system. FreshBooks is respected, but it is not the default recommendation from most US accountants. When a contractor needs a bookkeeper to review the books at month-end, QuickBooks is the system the accountant can open and navigate without training.

### Payroll ecosystem depth

QuickBooks Payroll is native, fully integrated, and comes in three tiers covering everything from basic payroll to full-service Elite with tax penalty protection, same-day direct deposit, and HR support. FreshBooks Payroll at $40/mo plus $6/user/mo is adequate for small teams but does not match the feature depth of QuickBooks Elite. For a contractor with W-2 employees, the QuickBooks payroll integration is tighter: hours flow from QuickBooks Time into payroll, payroll taxes are filed automatically, and W-2s are generated at year-end. Contractors can also use third-party providers like Gusto, which integrates with both platforms but has deeper QuickBooks integration.

### Integration ecosystem

QuickBooks integrates with over 700 apps including every major field service platform (Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan), construction management tool (Buildertrend, Procore, CoConstruct, AccuLynx), estimating tool, and payment processor. Most of those integrations support two-way sync of invoices, payments, customers, and job data. FreshBooks has a smaller integration library that covers the basics but does not match the breadth of the QuickBooks App Store. For a contractor using multiple tools, QuickBooks is the hub that connects them.

### 1099 contractor management

QuickBooks Plus and above includes built-in 1099 contractor management: track payments to subcontractors, generate 1099-NEC forms at year-end, and e-file directly from the platform. For contractors who work with multiple 1099 subcontractors, this eliminates a separate workflow. FreshBooks does not have native 1099 management.

### Inventory and purchase orders

QuickBooks Plus and above includes inventory tracking, purchase order creation, and vendor management. For contractors who buy and resell materials, track stock levels, or manage vendor relationships, this is a real advantage. FreshBooks does not have inventory management.

## Where each product is the wrong choice

**Do not pick FreshBooks** if the business needs real accounting controls: project profitability, inventory tracking, 1099 management, or construction billing workflows. FreshBooks on Plus adds double-entry and bank reconciliation, but it is not built for the monthly close or job-cost workflow the way QuickBooks is. Also skip FreshBooks if you have more than 5 clients and are on a tight budget, since the Lite cap forces you into Plus at $43/mo. The per-user fee of $11/user/mo adds up quickly for teams. A 3-person shop on Plus with payroll would pay roughly $123/mo before processing fees, and at that price point QuickBooks Plus with native payroll is the stronger value.

**Do not pick QuickBooks** if the business only needs fast invoicing and payment reminders and has no bookkeeper, payroll, or multi-user requirements. Paying $38/mo to $115/mo for an accounting platform when an invoicing tool would do the job is premature. Also skip QuickBooks if you are a solo contractor who bills by time and wants a built-in timer that feeds invoices. QuickBooks Time is a separate $20/mo plus $8/user add-on, which pushes the effective monthly cost higher than FreshBooks Premium. The learning curve for QuickBooks job costing setup is real, and a contractor who will never use Projects, Classes, or inventory is paying for features they will not touch.

Both products are the wrong choice if the business needs construction-grade job costing, progress billing, AIA forms, lien waivers, or construction-specific workflows. Neither FreshBooks nor QuickBooks is construction accounting software. ## Evaluation plan

### Step 1: define the job the software has to do

List the jobs the software must handle this month. Include estimates, invoices, bills, receipt capture, bank feeds, time tracking, expense management, payment reminders, payroll, reconciliation, project profitability, 1099 tracking, and reporting.

If the list is mostly invoicing, time tracking, and payment collection, start with FreshBooks. If the list includes project profitability, payroll, reconciliation, multi-user access, bill entry, and 1099 management, start with QuickBooks.

### Step 2: price the real plan

<p>For FreshBooks, do not stop at Lite if you have more than 5 billable clients. Plus at $43/mo is the more realistic comparison once double-entry accounting and bank reconciliation matter. Add $11/user/mo for each team member and $40/mo plus $6/user/mo if payroll is needed.</p>

<p>For QuickBooks, do not stop at Simple Start if you need project profitability or more than 1 user. Plus at $115/mo is the practical baseline for most active contractors who need Projects, inventory, or 5 users. Add $50-125/mo plus $5-10/employee for payroll, and $20/mo plus $8/user for QuickBooks Time if time tracking is needed.</p>

### Step 3: run one real job through the workflow

In FreshBooks, start a timer, log an expense, create an estimate, convert it into an invoice, send it, set up a payment reminder, and accept a payment. If that flow feels fast and complete, the subscription is justified.

In QuickBooks, connect a bank account, import and reconcile a week of transactions, set up a Project, allocate income and expenses to it, run a profit and loss report by project, and invite an accountant user. If that workflow feels solid and the project profitability data is useful, the subscription is justified.

The test should make the decision obvious. If FreshBooks feels fast and the payment automation saves real time, the $23/mo to $43/mo is worth it. If QuickBooks' project profitability and reconciliation save hours on month-end close, the $115/mo is worth it.

## FAQ

### Is FreshBooks or QuickBooks better for contractors?

FreshBooks is better for solo contractors who bill by time, need professional invoicing with automated payment reminders, and want a mobile app for field time and expense capture. QuickBooks Online is better for growing contractors who need project profitability tracking, payroll integration, inventory, 1099 management, accountant familiarity, and broad app ecosystem compatibility.

### Which is cheaper, FreshBooks or QuickBooks?

FreshBooks Lite at $23/mo is cheaper than QuickBooks Simple Start at $38/mo. But the practical comparison shifts once you need features. FreshBooks Plus at $43/mo adds double-entry accounting and bank reconciliation. QuickBooks Plus at $115/mo adds project profitability, inventory, and 5 users. The real cost depends on which tier and add-ons (payroll, time tracking) the contractor needs. FreshBooks is cheaper for solo invoicing. QuickBooks costs more but delivers more at the accounting level.

### Does FreshBooks or QuickBooks charge per user?

FreshBooks charges $11/user/mo for each additional team member on top of the base plan. QuickBooks includes tiered user counts: 1 user on Simple Start ($38/mo), 3 on Essentials ($75/mo), 5 on Plus ($115/mo), and 25 on Advanced ($275/mo). Neither charges per user the way Xero does (which includes unlimited users on every plan).

### Can FreshBooks or QuickBooks handle job costing?

QuickBooks can handle basic job costing through its Projects feature on the Plus plan ($115/mo) and above. It tracks per-job income and expenses, runs profitability reports, and connects to Class Tracking for cost breakdown by trade or crew. FreshBooks has project profitability on Premium ($70/mo) but with less depth and no cost-class breakdown. Neither is construction-grade job costing. If job-cost controls are a requirement, compare construction accounting tools instead.

### Does FreshBooks or QuickBooks include payroll?

Neither includes payroll in the base subscription. FreshBooks Payroll is $40/mo plus $6/user/mo. QuickBooks Payroll comes in three tiers: Core ($50/mo + $5/employee), Premium ($80/mo + $8/employee), and Elite ($125/mo + $10/employee). QuickBooks payroll is more fully featured with auto payroll, tax filings, and W-2 generation. Both platforms also integrate with third-party payroll providers like Gusto.

### Does FreshBooks have a trial?

Yes. FreshBooks offers a 30-day free trial with no credit card required. All plans are available during the trial, so you can test Lite, Plus, or Premium features before committing. There is also a 30-day money-back guarantee after purchase.

### Does QuickBooks Online have a trial?

Yes. QuickBooks Online offers a 30-day free trial on any paid plan. No credit card is required to start. After the trial, you choose a plan and enter payment. Promotional pricing (50% off for 3 months) is commonly available through Intuit's website.

### Does FreshBooks integrate with QuickBooks?

No. FreshBooks doesn't sync with QuickBooks. It has its own double-entry accounting engine and runs independently. If you need QuickBooks compatibility, look at tools like Knowify or Field ProMax that are built on top of QuickBooks. FreshBooks can export data, but it's not a QuickBooks add-on.

### Which should a solo contractor choose?

A solo contractor who bills by time, sends regular invoices, and needs automated payment reminders should choose FreshBooks Lite at $23/mo or Plus at $43/mo. A solo contractor who needs unlimited invoicing, project profitability, inventory, or accountant collaboration should choose QuickBooks Simple Start at $38/mo or Plus at $115/mo. The test is whether invoicing speed or accounting depth is the bigger bottleneck.
