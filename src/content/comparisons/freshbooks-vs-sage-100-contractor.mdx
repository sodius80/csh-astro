# FreshBooks vs Sage 100 Contractor: Which Accounting Fit?

FreshBooks and Sage 100 Contractor are both accounting tools, but they are not the same kind of purchase. FreshBooks is a cloud accounting platform built around invoicing speed, time tracking, and automated payment collection for service businesses. Sage 100 Contractor is a construction accounting and business management system for contractors that need job cost, certified payroll, AIA billing, and industry-specific reporting.

That difference matters because contractors often compare accounting tools without defining the actual problem. If the business needs faster invoicing and cleaner books, Sage 100 Contractor is more system than necessary. If the business needs construction financial control, FreshBooks will not go deep enough.

## The fast answer

FreshBooks wins when the accounting problem is invoicing, time tracking, and cash flow visibility. Atlas verifies FreshBooks pricing at Lite $23/mo for 5 billable clients, Plus $43/mo for 50 clients, Premium $70/mo for unlimited clients, and Select at custom pricing. The official pricing page currently shows 90% off for 6 months as a promotional offer, but contractors should use the regular prices as the budget baseline. A 30-day free trial with no credit card required makes evaluation low-risk.

For the deeper product breakdown behind that fit, read the full [FreshBooks review](/freshbooks-review/).

Sage 100 Contractor wins when the accounting system itself needs to be construction-specific. Atlas verifies Sage 100 Contractor as custom quote only. The official Sage product page gives Request info, phone, and Talk to an expert contact paths instead of a public price table. That same page says the product manages construction and service management, provides access to critical job cost and project details, offers customizable dashboards and industry-specific reports, and supports integrated estimating, exported budgets, proposals, subcontracts, and purchase orders.

The shortcut: FreshBooks is the better fit when invoicing and clean cloud books are the problem. Sage 100 Contractor is the better fit when construction financial control is the problem.

## Pricing and buying motion

These products are easiest to separate by how they are bought.

FreshBooks can be budgeted before a call. Atlas verifies four tiers: Lite at $23/mo for 5 billable clients, Plus at $43/mo for 50 clients, Premium at $70/mo for unlimited clients, and Select at custom pricing. The official pricing page currently advertises 90% off for 6 months, showing Lite at $2.30/mo, Plus at $4.30/mo, and Premium at $7.00/mo. Treat those as temporary discounts. For planning, use the regular plan prices.

Sage 100 Contractor is custom quote only in Atlas. The official Sage product page provides Request info, phone, and Talk to an expert contact paths and does not publish public monthly, annual, implementation, or license prices. That means a contractor should not compare Sage against FreshBooks as if both products have a simple plan table. The Sage quote needs to account for users, modules, deployment, data migration, implementation support, training, reporting needs, and ongoing support.

If cloud accounting is the stronger fit, the full [FreshBooks review](/freshbooks-review/) explains where it works best for contractors.

For add-on context, FreshBooks charges $11/user/mo for additional team members, $20/mo for Advanced Payments (included on Select), and $40/mo plus $6/user/mo for FreshBooks Payroll. Those costs matter for a 2- or 3-person shop evaluating real monthly spend.

## Job costing and construction billing

This is where the products separate completely.

FreshBooks does not do job costing. The Premium plan includes project profitability tracking, which shows revenue and expenses at the project level. That is not the same as tracking labor categories, material costs by line item, subcontractor costs, work-in-progress, or change order impact in the way contractor accounting requires. FreshBooks also has no progress billing, no AIA forms, no lien waivers, and no certified payroll. If a contractor's accounting workflow depends on any of those, FreshBooks is the wrong starting point.

Sage 100 Contractor starts closer to that construction-accounting need. The official Sage product page emphasizes critical job cost and project details, project status, customizable dashboards, industry-specific reports, integrated estimating, exported budgets, proposals, subcontracts, and purchase orders. For a contractor, those are the financial controls that help an owner see whether jobs are making money, whether budgets are drifting, and whether purchasing and subcontract commitments match the project plan.

The practical test: if the office needs to know job profitability at the material, labor, and subcontractor level, FreshBooks will not answer that question. If the office mainly needs faster invoicing and cleaner monthly books, Sage 100 Contractor is more system than the business needs.

## Invoicing and payment collection

FreshBooks is built around invoicing. Recurring invoices, automated payment reminders, online payment links, and a client portal are included without add-ons. Start a timer from the mobile app, categorize it to a client or project, and add those hours to an invoice in two taps. For contractors who bill by the hour, that workflow is faster than most alternatives. The FreshBooks mobile app also handles expense capture, receipt scanning, and payment status tracking from the field.

Sage 100 Contractor handles invoices within the construction accounting workflow, but invoicing speed is not the primary selling point. The system is built around job cost, project financial control, and construction-specific compliance. A contractor who needs AIA billing, progress billing, and retainage tracking will find those features built in. A contractor who mainly needs to send 30 invoices a month and get paid faster may find FreshBooks more directly useful.

## Payroll and compliance

Payroll is another dividing line, but it should be handled carefully.

FreshBooks Payroll is available as an add-on at $40/mo plus $6/user/mo. It handles basic payroll for small service businesses. If the contractor has simple W-2 employees and standard tax filing needs, the FreshBooks payroll add-on can work. It does not handle certified payroll, prevailing wage, or union reporting.

Sage 100 Contractor requires a more detailed payroll and compliance conversation. The system handles certified payroll, prevailing wage, and union reporting natively, which is the core reason contractors doing government or commercial work evaluate Sage. If those payroll requirements are important, ask Sage or the reseller to show the exact workflows during the demo and put the relevant modules and services in the quote.

A solo contractor with simple payroll may prefer FreshBooks. A commercial contractor with certified payroll requirements should evaluate Sage more seriously.

## Implementation and deployment

FreshBooks is the lighter rollout. A contractor can choose a plan, connect bank feeds, set up invoices, invite a bookkeeper, and start reconciling. The 30-day free trial lets a contractor test the product with real invoices and real clients before paying anything. There is still setup work: chart of accounts, opening balances, bank rules, invoice templates, and payroll integration. But the buying motion is self-serve and familiar.

Sage 100 Contractor is an implementation. Before signing, the contractor should bring a real chart of accounts, sample job, estimating process, budget structure, subcontract process, purchase-order workflow, payroll requirements, and reporting pack to the demo. The value comes from fitting the system to the way the contractor runs jobs and financial controls. Expect data cleanup, configuration, user training, and a more formal rollout. This is not a weakness if the business needs the depth. It is a warning against buying Sage when the real problem is simply messy bookkeeping.

## Users and team access

FreshBooks charges per user. Each plan includes one account user by default, and additional team members cost $11/user/mo on top of the base plan. For a 2-person shop on Plus, the monthly cost is $43 + $11 = $54/mo before processing fees and payroll. The Select plan includes two team members and Advanced Payments at no extra charge.

Sage 100 Contractor should be priced from a written quote that separates users, modules, deployment, hosting, training, and support. The user model depends on the modules selected and the deployment method. That does not make Sage worse. It means Sage is a scoped system purchase, not a quick subscription choice.

The wrong move is buying Sage just to avoid spreadsheet mess if the accounting workflow is still simple. The equally wrong move is buying FreshBooks because the plan table looks clean when the business actually needs construction-accounting controls.

## Where FreshBooks wins

**Invoicing speed and payment collection.** FreshBooks is built around getting invoices out fast and getting paid faster. Recurring invoices, automated payment reminders, and online payment links are included without add-ons. For service businesses where cash flow depends on invoice collection, that workflow saves meaningful time.

**Public pricing and faster evaluation.** FreshBooks gives contractors a real budget range before a sales conversation. The official pricing page shows the standard plan prices and the temporary promotional rates separately, which makes the first budget pass easier.

**Self-serve setup with a 30-day trial.** No credit card required, all plans available, 30 days to test. A contractor can send real invoices, connect a real bank account, and decide whether the client limit or feature set binds before paying anything.

**Mobile time tracking that feeds invoices.** Start a timer from the mobile app, categorize it to a client, and add those hours to an invoice. For contractors who bill by the hour, that workflow is the reason they choose FreshBooks over alternatives.

## Where Sage 100 Contractor wins

**Construction accounting depth.** Sage's official product page points directly at job cost, project details, estimating, budgets, proposals, subcontracts, purchase orders, dashboards, and industry-specific reports. That is the reason to consider Sage over a general cloud accounting platform.

**Certified payroll and AIA billing.** Sage 100 Contractor handles certified payroll, prevailing wage, union reporting, and AIA billing natively. Those are not add-on features. They are the core construction-accounting controls that justify a Sage evaluation for contractors doing commercial or government work.

**Better fit for companies outgrowing general books.** When FreshBooks project profitability reports need side spreadsheets to explain job margin, Sage becomes more relevant. The decision is not about whether Sage is easier. It is about whether the accounting system must match construction financial operations more closely.

**Sales-led scoping can reduce guessing.** Custom quote pricing is slower, but it forces the buyer to define users, modules, deployment, migration, training, support, and reporting needs before accepting a number.

FreshBooks is the wrong choice for a contractor whose accounting pain is construction-specific. If the office needs job-cost structure, construction dashboards, estimating-to-budget flow, purchase-order control, subcontract visibility, certified payroll workflows, or AIA billing, FreshBooks will not provide those. For a deeper comparison with construction accounting tools, see [QuickBooks Online vs Sage 100 Contractor](/qbo-vs-sage-100-contractor/).

Sage 100 Contractor is the wrong choice for a solo contractor or small service shop that mainly needs invoices, bills, bank feeds, and bookkeeper access. The custom quote process, implementation work, and construction-system depth are excessive if the books are still simple. That contractor would be better served by FreshBooks or [QuickBooks Online](/quickbooks-online-review/).

Both products can be wrong if the buyer is really shopping for field operations software. Neither tool replaces scheduling, dispatch, routing, technician mobile workflows, or daily work-order management. ## Evaluation plan

For FreshBooks, test the plan that matches real volume. Lite is only reasonable if 5 billable clients and basic invoicing are enough. Most active contractors should test Plus because it adds bank reconciliation, double-entry accounting reports, and accountant access. If project profitability tracking matters, test Premium. Use the 30-day free trial with real invoices, real clients, and a real bank connection. The client limit on Lite will tell you within two weeks whether Plus is the real starting plan.

For Sage 100 Contractor, build the evaluation around one real job and one real month-end close. Ask Sage or the reseller to show job cost setup, estimating into budget, subcontract and purchase-order handling, dashboards, project status, reports, payroll requirements, data migration, and implementation steps. Then request a written quote that separates software, users, modules, hosting or deployment, training, support, and renewal terms.

Do not let the cheaper-looking option win by default. Let the accounting problem choose the product.

## Bottom line for contractors

FreshBooks and Sage 100 Contractor answer different accounting questions.

FreshBooks answers: "Can we run cleaner cloud accounting with faster invoicing, public pricing, and a self-serve setup?" It is the better first choice for solo contractors, freelancers, and small service shops that bill by time or project and do not need construction-specific accounting depth.

Sage 100 Contractor answers: "Do we need construction accounting built around job cost, project details, certified payroll, AIA billing, and industry-specific reports?" It is the better fit when general accounting software no longer gives the owner reliable project financial control.

My recommendation: choose FreshBooks if invoicing speed, transparent pricing, and cloud accounting are the buying reasons. Choose Sage 100 Contractor if construction accounting depth, job cost, and compliance are the buying reasons. If the team is unsure, ask one question first: are we missing faster invoicing and cleaner books, or are we missing construction financial control?

**Is FreshBooks cheaper than Sage 100 Contractor?**

FreshBooks is easier to budget because it publishes plan prices: Lite at $23/mo, Plus at $43/mo, Premium at $70/mo, and Select at custom pricing. Sage 100 Contractor is custom quote only in Atlas and on the official product page, so the final cost depends on users, modules, deployment, implementation, training, support, and renewals.

**Does FreshBooks do job costing?**

No. FreshBooks Premium includes project profitability tracking, which shows revenue and expenses at the project level. It does not track labor categories, material costs by line item, subcontractor costs, work-in-progress, or change orders the way construction accounting requires. **Does Sage 100 Contractor replace FreshBooks?**

It can replace FreshBooks as the accounting system for contractors that need construction accounting depth. It is not a lighter invoicing tool. Treat it as a scoped construction-accounting implementation with demo, quote, migration, training, and support questions.

**Can a solo contractor use Sage 100 Contractor?**

Most solo operators under $500K annual revenue do not need Sage 100 Contractor. The custom quote process, implementation timeline, and construction-system depth are designed for small to mid-sized contractors doing $750K or more. A solo contractor billing by time would be better served by FreshBooks or QuickBooks Online.
