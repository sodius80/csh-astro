# ActiveCampaign vs GetResponse: CRM or Marketing Platform?

ActiveCampaign and GetResponse are both email marketing platforms with automation builders, but they're built for different contractor needs. ActiveCampaign is the deeper automation and CRM tool with lead scoring, deal tracking, and complex conditional sequences at a lower entry price. GetResponse is the broader marketing platform that wraps email inside landing pages, webinars, automation funnels, and a no-cost account tier.

For a contractor evaluating the two, the question is not "which has more features?" It is "what does your business actually need?" If the answer is automated quote follow-ups, lead scoring, and CRM contact tracking, ActiveCampaign is the stronger tool at a lower price. If the answer is landing pages, webinar hosting, and a broader marketing funnel system, GetResponse covers more ground. ## When this software makes sense

Contractors reach for email marketing platforms when the manual approach stops working. The leads come in from the website, some get calls, others get emails, and nobody can say which follow-ups actually produce jobs. The seasonal promotion goes to the whole list instead of just the customers who need that service. The monthly newsletter takes a full afternoon to send.

Both ActiveCampaign and GetResponse solve that problem by keeping contact data and email history in one place. The difference is the starting assumption.

ActiveCampaign makes sense when email automation is the primary need. A contractor running a roofing company who wants every "get a free estimate" website submission to trigger a five-email sequence can build that in ActiveCampaign's visual automation builder without writing code. The CRM side tracks who opened what and which contacts moved through which sequence. It is CRM-light but email-heavy, and that is the right balance for contractors who need follow-up depth.

GetResponse makes sense when the contractor needs a marketing platform, not just an email tool. A contractor who wants to build a landing page for a seasonal promotion, send a webinar invitation to past customers, run an automated follow-up sequence after the webinar, and track who signed up can do that inside one platform. The email layer is one part of a broader marketing system.

## When it does not make sense yet

Skip both if your email needs are basic - a monthly newsletter to a small list of existing customers. MailerLite at $10/month or Mailchimp's no-cost option covers that without paying for automation workflows you will not use. Also skip both if your CRM need is strictly field service management - scheduling, dispatch, estimates, and invoicing. Neither ActiveCampaign nor GetResponse handles job scheduling or dispatch. You would still need a field service platform alongside either of them.

The specific risk with ActiveCampaign: if you need webinars, advanced landing page builders, or marketing funnels as part of your email system, ActiveCampaign does not offer those. You would need to piece together separate tools for those use cases.

The specific risk with GetResponse: if CRM depth matters more than marketing platform breadth - structured sales pipelines, deal stages with probability tracking, and multi-user sales workflows - GetResponse's contact management is less structured than ActiveCampaign's CRM-lite capabilities. ## Email marketing and automation depth

ActiveCampaign's automation builder is its strongest argument. The visual canvas lets you drag conditions, actions, and triggers into a sequence without code. A contractor could build: "If someone fills out the 'get a quote' form, send a welcome email, wait three days, check if they opened it, send a case study if they did and a different follow-up if they did not." That level of conditional logic runs on Starter and gets more powerful on Plus and Professional plans.

ActiveCampaign also supports split conditions - different paths based on whether a contact opened, clicked, or did nothing. That is useful for contractors running seasonal promotions who want to send a second offer to non-openers and a thank-you plus upsell to openers. The automation depth scales with plan tier: Starter has 5 actions per automation, Plus and above have unlimited.

GetResponse's automation builder covers more than basic sequences. The visual builder supports contact scoring, event-based triggers, and multi-path funnels on Marketer and above. A contractor could build an automation that scores a contact based on email opens and landing page visits, then routes them into different sequences based on their score. That is more capable than a simple welcome series, though it requires the Marketer tier at $48.38/mo annual to unlock.

Decision signal: if you plan to build complex, conditional email sequences with multiple branches and want the deepest automation at the lowest entry price, ActiveCampaign is the clear choice. If your automation needs include landing page interactions, webinar attendance triggers, and broader marketing funnel logic, GetResponse covers that on higher tiers.

## CRM functionality and contact management

ActiveCampaign wins on CRM depth. Contact records show list membership, tags, engagement data, deal stage, and custom fields. The deal tracking view shows a pipeline with stages (new lead, contacted, quoted, won, lost), deal values, and owner assignments. For a contractor managing a steady flow of inbound leads and wanting a visible sales funnel alongside email automation, ActiveCampaign's CRM is the more structured starting point. For a head-to-head between ActiveCampaign and a dedicated sales pipeline tool, see [ActiveCampaign vs Pipeline CRM](/activecampaign-vs-pipeline-crm/).

GetResponse handles contact management but does not provide the same CRM structure. Contacts can be organized into lists, tagged, and segmented. The platform tracks engagement data like opens, clicks, and page visits on higher tiers. But there is no structured sales pipeline with deal stages, deal values, and owner assignments equivalent to ActiveCampaign's deal tracking. A contractor using GetResponse for email marketing would need a separate CRM like [Pipeline CRM](/pipeline-crm-review/) for pipeline management if they track deals through stages.

Decision signal: ActiveCampaign if CRM is a genuine need - you want to manage leads through a pipeline, track deals, and connect deal stages to email automation. GetResponse if CRM is supporting cast - you mainly need email and marketing automation and can manage contacts through tags and lists.

## Segmentation and lead scoring

ActiveCampaign handles segmentation through tags, lists, custom fields, and deal stages. A contact can be tagged "spring-promotion-opened" and "service-area-north" and "estimated-over-5k" - then you can send a follow-up to the intersection of all three. That level of multi-condition segmentation is available on Starter and becomes more granular on higher plans.

Lead scoring in ActiveCampaign assigns points based on email engagement, page visits, form submissions, and other behaviors. When a contact reaches a threshold score, they can trigger an automation or move to a different list. This is useful for contractors who want to know which leads are actively researching versus passively browsing.

GetResponse includes contact scoring on Marketer and above. Contacts accumulate points based on engagement behavior. That scoring can trigger automation paths: a contact who scores above a threshold can be routed into a "hot lead" sequence. The scoring system is capable, but it sits on the Marketer tier ($48.38/mo annual) rather than the Starter tier. ActiveCampaign includes lead scoring on Starter at $15/mo.

Decision signal: ActiveCampaign for lead scoring at the entry-level price, plus multi-condition segmentation by tag, list, deal stage, and custom field. GetResponse for contact scoring that includes landing page and webinar interactions, on Marketer and above.

## Webinars and landing pages

The landing page builder has templates geared for conversions, and contractors can use it for service-area pages, seasonal promotion pages, or lead capture forms without a separate website tool. The webinar feature includes registration pages, automated reminder emails, recording, and follow-up sequences. That is a capability ActiveCampaign cannot match without adding a separate webinar tool like Zoom or Webex.

Decision signal: if webinars or landing pages are part of your marketing plan, GetResponse is the clear choice. If those are not in your marketing toolkit, ActiveCampaign's email automation depth at a lower price is the better fit.

## How to choose

Start with three questions:

1. **Do you need webinars or landing pages as part of your email system?** If yes, GetResponse. ActiveCampaign does not offer webinars and locks landing pages behind the Plus tier. GetResponse includes landing pages on all plans and webinars on Creator and Enterprise.

2. **Do you need a built-in CRM with deal tracking?** If yes, ActiveCampaign. Contact records, deals, pipeline stages, and lead scoring are available starting at Starter. GetResponse handles contacts through lists and tags but does not provide structured pipeline management.

3. **Do you need a no-cost account you can use after the trial?** If yes, GetResponse. The limited no-cost account after the 14-day trial lets a contractor keep sending emails without a monthly bill. ActiveCampaign's trial expires and requires a paid plan.
