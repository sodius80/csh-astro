# AWeber vs Freshsales: Email or Free CRM?

AWeber and Freshsales both offer free plans, but they solve fundamentally different problems. AWeber is an email marketing platform built around campaigns, templates, landing pages, and automation. Freshsales is a sales CRM built around pipeline stages, deal tracking, phone calling, and AI lead scoring.

That difference matters. If your office has a growing contact list and nobody is sending regular emails, AWeber is the stronger fit. If your office is losing track of which estimates need follow-up calls and which leads went cold, Freshsales is the cleaner starting point. Both have free tiers, but AWeber's free plan centers on email sends for 500 subscribers while Freshsales' free plan centers on CRM functionality for 3 users with built-in phone and AI.

## Quick verdict

Pick AWeber if email is the job. The official AWeber pricing page shows a free plan for up to 500 subscribers and 3,000 emails per month. Paid plans start at Lite $12.50 per month billed annually for 0-500 subscribers, or $15 per month on monthly billing. Plus is $20 per month billed annually for the same subscriber tier. The official product pages position AWeber around email campaigns, drag-and-drop builders, landing pages, marketing automation, sign-up forms, ecommerce tools, and integrations.

Pick Freshsales if the sales process is the job. Atlas-verified Freshsales pricing lists a free plan at $0 for up to 3 users with built-in phone (200 minutes per month), Freddy AI lead scoring, visual Kanban pipeline views, email templates, and live chat. Paid plans are Growth at $9 per user per month billed annually or $11 per user per month billed monthly, Pro at $39 per user per month annually or $47 monthly, and Enterprise at $59 per user per month annually or $71 monthly. Freshsales also advertises a 21-day free trial with no credit card required.

Skip both if you are trying to dispatch technicians or run jobs after the sale. Neither AWeber nor Freshsales replaces [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), [ServiceTitan](/servicetitan-review/), or a broader [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) platform. These tools sit before the job is won, or beside operations, not in place of scheduling and dispatch. For the broader CRM landscape, see [best CRM for contractors](/best-crm-for-contractors/).

## When AWeber makes more sense

AWeber makes more sense when the contractor has a communication problem, not a pipeline problem. The contact list exists. The estimate history exists. The past-customer list exists. But nobody is sending regular emails because someone has to remember to do it manually.

That is where AWeber fits. It has been in the email marketing space since 1998, and that longevity shows in reliable deliverability, responsive support, and a template library that covers hundreds of mobile-responsive designs. For a contractor, the practical workflows are straightforward:

- Send a monthly newsletter to past customers about seasonal services
- Set up a welcome sequence for new leads who fill out a form on the website
- Run a seasonal campaign for gutter cleaning, HVAC tune-ups, or roof inspections
- Build a landing page for a promotion without hiring a developer
- Track open rates, click rates, and subscriber growth over time

The official AWeber pricing page confirms the free plan includes up to 500 subscribers and 3,000 emails per month. For a contractor just starting to build an email list, that is enough to test the waters without paying anything. The drag-and-drop email builder, AI Subject Line Assistant, and hundreds of templates mean the office does not need a designer to send professional emails.

The automation builder handles welcome sequences, tag-based follow-ups, and behavioral triggers. For a contractor who wants to automatically send a follow-up email when someone downloads a guide or fills out a contact form, AWeber's automation is sufficient without being overwhelming. See how AWeber compares to other [email marketing tools for contractors](/best-email-marketing-software-contractors/).

The limitation is CRM depth. AWeber has contact management and custom segments, but it does not have a sales pipeline, deal stages, lead scoring, or built-in phone. If the office needs to know which lead was called, which estimate was scheduled, and which quote needs a follow-up this week, AWeber is not built for that workflow. For contractors comparing AWeber to a dedicated sales CRM, see [AWeber vs Pipedrive](/aweber-vs-pipedrive/).

## When Freshsales makes more sense

Freshsales makes more sense when the contractor's biggest leak is sales follow-up and the team needs a CRM working this week, not next month, without spending money. The official Freshsales product page highlights Freddy AI lead scoring, Kanban views, built-in phone and email, contextual collaboration, and deal insights. That is the right shape for a contractor office that needs to know what happened with every open lead this week.

The free tier is unusually generous. According to the official Freshworks pricing page, the free plan includes:

- Up to 3 users at no cost
- Built-in phone with 200 minutes per month
- AI-based lead scoring through Freddy AI
- Visual Kanban views for contacts, accounts, and deals
- Email templates and live chat
- Contact and account management

For a contractor with a few people handling sales, that covers the essential workflow without a per-user cost. The built-in phone is particularly useful. A roofing company can call leads directly from the CRM, log the call, and move the deal forward without switching to a separate dialer.

The interface is modern and clean, with a lower learning curve than most free CRMs, which is why it ranked as the top free CRM in our [best free CRM for contractors](/best-free-crm-for-contractors/) roundup. A small team can create a pipeline like this:

- New lead
- Contacted
- Estimate scheduled
- Quote sent
- Follow-up due
- Won
- Lost

Every open deal can have an owner, an expected value, a stage, notes, email history, and a next activity. Managers can scan the board and see where work is stuck. Estimators can open the CRM and know which quote needs a call today.

The limitation is integrations. The free tier includes only 1 third-party integration. Workflow automation and email cadences are not available on the free tier, so follow-up structure has to be manual. The free plan is pure sales CRM with no project management, job tracking, invoicing, or field service features.

## Free tier comparison: what you actually get

The free tier is the heart of this comparison. Both products have one, but the free tiers solve different problems.

### AWeber free plan

The official AWeber pricing page describes the free plan as including up to 500 subscribers and 3,000 emails per month. Key features include:

- Drag-and-drop email builder
- AI Subject Line Assistant
- Hundreds of professional email templates
- Landing pages
- Sign-up forms
- Basic automation
- Email split testing
- Web push notifications (up to 50,000 WPN subscribers on Lite)

For a contractor, that is a working email marketing tool. A roofing company with a list of past customers can send a monthly newsletter, a seasonal promotion, or a follow-up sequence to new leads without paying anything.

The limitation is subscriber growth. Once the list exceeds 500 subscribers, the free plan no longer applies and the contractor must move to a paid plan. AWeber's pricing then scales by subscriber count, which means costs grow as the business grows its email list.

### Freshsales free tier

The official Freshworks pricing page describes the free plan as including Kanban views, email templates, built-in phone, and live chat. The free plan covers up to 3 users. Key features include:

- Visual Kanban pipeline for contacts, accounts, and deals
- Built-in phone with 200 minutes per month
- AI-based lead scoring through Freddy AI
- Email templates and live chat
- Contact and account management
- Mobile app access

For a contractor, that is a working sales CRM. A roofing company with 3 people in the office can track leads, call them from inside the CRM, score which ones are hottest, and move them through a pipeline without paying anything.

The limitation is feature depth. The free tier includes only 1 third-party integration. Workflow automation and email cadences are not available on the free tier, so follow-up structure has to be manual. Custom fields, multiple sales pipelines, and auto-assignment rules require Growth at $9 per user per month or higher.

## Email marketing vs sales pipeline

The core difference between these tools is what they are built to do.

AWeber is built to send emails. The product centers on campaigns, templates, automation sequences, landing pages, sign-up forms, and deliverability. For a contractor who wants to nurture a contact list with regular communication, AWeber covers that workflow end to end. The email builder is drag-and-drop, the template library is large, and the automation builder handles welcome sequences, tag-based follow-ups, and behavioral triggers.

Freshsales is built to manage sales pipelines. The product centers on deals moving through stages, with contacts, accounts, activities, and reporting attached. For a contractor who wants to know which lead was called, which estimate was scheduled, and which quote needs a follow-up, Freshsales covers that workflow end to end. The Kanban pipeline is visual, the built-in phone lets salespeople call from inside the CRM, and Freddy AI scores leads so the team knows which ones to prioritize.

The mistake to avoid is buying one tool and expecting it to do the other job. AWeber has contact management, but it is not a sales pipeline CRM. Freshsales has email templates, but it is not an email marketing platform with campaign builders and newsletter automation. If the office needs both email marketing and CRM, the right move is usually to pick the stronger tool for each job and integrate them, rather than forcing one to do both.

For contractors exploring either category, see [AWeber vs MailerLite](/aweber-vs-mailerlite/) for an email marketing comparison or [Freshsales vs Pipedrive](/freshsales-vs-pipedrive/) for a CRM comparison.

## AI features: AI Subject Line vs Freddy AI

Both platforms offer AI, but the application is different.

AWeber includes an AI Subject Line Assistant that helps generate subject lines for email campaigns. For a contractor who struggles with what to put in the subject line of a monthly newsletter or seasonal promotion, the AI assistant can suggest options. AWeber's AI is focused on email content, not on lead management.

Freshsales includes Freddy AI on the free tier. The official product page describes Freddy as a 24/7 assistant that highlights the best leads and deals, offers insights, and writes emails. On the free plan, Freddy AI provides lead scoring that helps prioritize which leads to call first. On Pro and higher tiers, Freddy adds deal insights, sales email assistance, and contact scoring.

For a contractor on a free tier, this is a real difference. AWeber's free plan gives you AI for email subject lines. Freshsales' free plan gives you AI for lead scoring. If the goal is to know which leads to call first, Freshsales has the edge. If the goal is to write better email subject lines, AWeber has the edge.

## Built-in phone and communication

Freshsales has the stronger built-in communication story for contractors. The official pricing page lists built-in phone, email, and live chat on the free plan. For a contractor who wants to call leads from inside the CRM without buying a separate phone system, that is a genuine advantage.

The free tier includes 200 minutes per month of built-in phone calling. A salesperson can dial a lead from inside the CRM, log the call, attach notes, and move the deal forward without switching tools. For a roofing, HVAC, or remodeling company where speed-to-call matters, that is real value at zero cost.

On Pro and higher, Freshsales adds Bring Your Own Carrier (BYOC) for phone, sales sequences, and advanced workflows. The communication story deepens as the team upgrades.

AWeber does not include built-in phone on any plan. AWeber's communication strength is email: campaigns, automation, templates, and deliverability. A contractor who wants to call leads from inside their software would need a separate phone system alongside AWeber.

## Automation and workflows

AWeber's automation is built around email sequences and behavioral triggers. The drag-and-drop automation builder handles welcome series, tag-based follow-ups, abandoned cart or form workflows, and email split testing. For a contractor who wants to automatically send a sequence of emails when someone joins the list, downloads a guide, or fills out a contact form, AWeber's automation is the core product.

Freshsales' automation is built around sales workflows and CRM processes. On the free tier, automation is limited. The free plan includes basic workflows but no custom fields, no multiple sales pipelines, and no auto-assignment rules. Those features require Growth at $9 per user per month or higher. Pro adds multiple sales pipelines, auto-assignment rules, territory management, and sales sequences. Enterprise adds custom modules and field-level permissions.

If the goal is to automate email communication, AWeber is the stronger choice. If the goal is to automate sales workflows and pipeline management, Freshsales' paid tiers are the stronger choice.

## Contractor operations fit

Neither product should be sold internally as dispatch software.

AWeber can help with email campaigns, newsletters, landing pages, sign-up forms, automation, and customer communication. It will not manage leads through a sales pipeline, track deal stages, schedule estimates, manage technician routes, create quotes, or run field service workflows.

Freshsales can help with sales leads, pipeline stages, contact history, email sync, phone calling, reminders, reporting, and sales follow-up. It will not manage technician routes, job schedules, field production, parts, job costing, service agreements, or crew dispatch.

That is not a dealbreaker if you buy the right tool for the right job. A contractor using [Jobber](/jobber-review/) or [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/) for operations may use AWeber for email marketing or Freshsales for sales CRM alongside the operations platform. The tools solve different problems at different stages of the business.

The handoff question matters more than the brand:

1. Where does a lead enter?
2. Who qualifies the lead?
3. Where is the estimate scheduled?
4. Where is the quote created?
5. When does a won opportunity become a job?
6. Which system owns scheduling, dispatch, job costing, and invoicing?
7. Which system owns the customer record after the job closes?

If most answers are email-marketing answers (newsletters, campaigns, nurture), AWeber is the right pick. If most answers are sales-pipeline answers (lead tracking, deal stages, follow-up), Freshsales is the right pick. If most answers are dispatch or production answers, start with [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) and add email or CRM as supporting tools.

## Where AWeber falls short

AWeber's biggest limitation for contractors is the absence of a sales pipeline. AWeber has contact management and segments, but it does not have deal stages, lead scoring, a visual Kanban board, or built-in phone. A contractor who needs to know which estimate needs a follow-up call today will not find that answer inside AWeber.

The second limitation is automation depth relative to dedicated marketing automation platforms. AWeber's automation handles welcome sequences, tag-based follow-ups, and behavioral triggers, but it is not as deep as [ActiveCampaign](/activecampaign-vs-aweber/) or [GetResponse](/aweber-vs-getresponse/) for complex multi-step sequences.

The third limitation is the interface. AWeber has been doing email marketing since 1998, and the product is reliable, but the interface feels dated compared to newer platforms. For contractors who prioritize a modern UI, this may be a factor.

## Where Freshsales falls short

Freshsales' biggest limitation is the absence of email marketing features. Freshsales has email templates and basic email sync, but it is not an email marketing platform. A contractor who needs to send newsletters, campaigns, or automated email sequences to a subscriber list will not find those capabilities inside Freshsales.

The second limitation is the 3-user cap on the free tier. A growing team that adds a fourth salesperson must upgrade to a paid plan at Growth $9 per user per month or higher. The free tier is a starter, not a permanent solution for a growing office.

The third limitation is ecosystem depth. Freshsales can integrate with many tools through the Freshworks Marketplace, but it is still primarily a sales CRM. If a contractor wants one vendor to cover CRM, accounting, projects, invoices, inventory, support, and field service, Freshworks does not have that breadth. See [Zoho CRM](/freshsales-vs-zoho-crm/) for a CRM with a broader ecosystem.

## Bottom line for contractors

AWeber and Freshsales are both legitimate choices with free plans, but they answer different questions.

Choose <a href="/go/aweber-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">AWeber</a> if the business needs email marketing with campaigns, templates, landing pages, and automation for a growing subscriber list. It is the better default for contractors whose main problem is communication with leads and customers, especially when the free plan for 500 subscribers is enough to start.

Choose <a href="/go/freshsales-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Freshsales</a> if the business needs a free CRM with built-in phone, AI lead scoring, and a visual pipeline the team can adopt this week. It is the better pick for contractors whose main problem is lead and quote follow-up, especially when calling leads from inside the CRM matters and the budget is zero.

If the job is dispatch, scheduling, route management, field production, or job costing, neither should be the first purchase. Start with [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) and add email marketing or CRM only where the office needs extra structure.
