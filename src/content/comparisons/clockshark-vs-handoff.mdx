# ClockShark vs Handoff Comparison

ClockShark vs Handoff looks like a field service software choice until you think about what actually costs you money every week. Then the decision gets much cleaner.

ClockShark is built for GPS-verified time tracking. It answers the question that keeps construction and field service owners up at night: "Did my crew actually work the hours they claimed, and were they at the right job site?" Handoff is built for AI-powered estimating and proposals. It answers a different question: "How do I turn more leads into signed contracts faster?"

Those are different problems, and buying the wrong tool for your bottleneck will leave you frustrated. A contractor drowning in missed hours doesn't need better proposals. A contractor who loses half their estimates to competitors who respond faster doesn't need GPS geofencing. See our <a href="/clockshark-review/">ClockShark review</a> for the full breakdown.

That difference matters more than a feature checklist. A roofer who needs to track four crews across eight job sites will get nothing from AI proposal generation no matter how polished the output. A remodeling company that sends ten estimates a week and converts two won't fix the conversion problem with GPS tracking.

## When this software makes sense

Buy this kind of software when the pain is operational, not just cosmetic. If you cannot trust the hours on your timesheets, ClockShark will pay for itself quickly. If you are losing jobs because your estimates look amateurish or take too long to prepare, Handoff will pay for itself quickly.

ClockShark makes sense when the daily mess is knowing where your people are and what they worked on. The owner or office manager needs to see who clocked in, at which job site, at what time, and whether the GPS stamp matches the reported location. For a general contractor running multiple crews, the software should make payroll verification automatic rather than a Friday-night headache.

Handoff makes sense when the daily mess is getting estimates out the door. A remodeling company needs to turn a walkthrough into a professional proposal with photos, line items, and financing options in minutes rather than hours. The AI voice-to-estimate feature lets a contractor describe the job out loud and have the software build the quote - useful for returning to the office and sending a proposal before the homeowner has moved on to the next bid. If that's the core bottleneck, compare it against the broader <a href="/best-estimating-software-for-small-contractors/">small-contractor estimating software</a> category before treating it as a time-tracking substitute.

Don't buy either tool because the demo looked polished. Buy when the workflow matches the work your business already does.

## When it does not make sense yet

Skip both for now if you're still a solo operator doing a handful of jobs a month and a spreadsheet plus QuickBooks is not slowing you down. Software adds setup work and monthly cost that a small operation may not recover.

Also skip the wrong category. If your biggest problem is collecting payments, neither ClockShark (time tracking only) nor Handoff (estimating-focused) will fix that. You may need a full-field-service platform like <a href="/housecall-pro-review/">Housecall Pro</a> or <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber</a> instead.

The danger isn't buying software too early. The danger is buying a tool built around someone else's workflow bottleneck.

## ClockShark: what stands out

ClockShark's main advantage is that it's built for one thing - GPS time tracking for field crews - and does it exceptionally well. The geofencing that competitors like <a href="/quickbooks-time-vs-clockshark/">QuickBooks Time</a> reserve for premium tiers is included in ClockShark's Standard plan at $40/month plus $9 per user.

For a five-person crew, ClockShark Standard costs $76/month total: $40 base plus $36 for four additional users at $9 each. That's a standalone price - no accounting software subscription required. The same setup with QuickBooks Time Elite would cost roughly $90/month plus the required QuickBooks Online subscription at $38/month minimum. ClockShark saves you the accounting tax.

The mock location detection is another differentiator that matters in practice. GPS spoofing apps are easy to install and hard to catch without software that specifically looks for them. ClockShark flags devices that appear to be faking their location, which is a real problem for contractors who have had crew members clock in from home.

Active-user billing is a practical advantage for seasonal contractors. ClockShark bills only for users who actually clock in during a billing period, and prorates charges for partial days. A roofing company that brings on extra crews for summer reroofing does not pay for them in the winter months.

The real-time crew map labeled "Who's Working Now" gives dispatch visibility into which crew members are clocked in and at which job site. For a general contractor running multiple crews, that single view can replace a lot of morning phone calls.

Where ClockShark falls short: no offline mode means crews in low-connectivity areas can't clock in, the $40/month base fee is steep for 1-2 person operations, and scheduling lacks the template and recurrence flexibility of purpose-built scheduling tools. For context on how it stacks up against alternatives, see our <a href="/best-time-tracking-software-contractors/">best contractor time tracking guide</a>. GPS battery drain on older smartphones is a consistent complaint in user reviews.

Capterra 4.7/5 from 1,910+ reviews and G2 4.5/5 support the strong user sentiment. The praise centers on ease of use, GPS reliability, and customer support. Complaints focus on the lack of offline mode, GPS sync issues in some environments, and the scheduling limitations.

Shortlist ClockShark if GPS time tracking for field crews is the problem. Read the <a href="/clockshark-review/">full ClockShark review</a> before committing. Verify internet coverage at your job sites before committing - no offline mode is a hard blocker for some operations.

## ClockShark pricing

ClockShark publishes two plan levels. Standard is $40/month plus $9 per user and includes geofencing, GPS time tracking, scheduling, and mobile app access. Pro is $60/month plus $11 per user and adds job costing, PTO management, and shift wrap-up forms.

For a five-person crew, Standard runs $76/month. Pro runs $104/month. The free trial is 14 days with no credit card required. ClockShark does not require any accounting software subscription to use the platform - it's standalone.

Active-user billing is worth repeating: you pay only for users who clock in during a billing period, and charges are prorated for partial days. For seasonal businesses, this can cut the winter software bill significantly.

ClockShark is best for construction and field service crews of 5-100 employees that need GPS-verified time tracking, geofencing, and job costing. ## Handoff: what stands out

Handoff's main advantage is that it thinks like a residential contractor who wins jobs by looking professional. The AI generates proposals complete with photos, line items, and financing options in a format that homeowners recognize as polished and trustworthy.

For a remodeling contractor, the sales process is where the battle is won or lost. Most homeowners are not comparing line-item pricing down to the dollar. They are comparing who responded fastest, who sent the most professional-looking proposal, and who made them feel confident about the decision. Handoff accelerates that process dramatically.

The voice-to-estimate feature is genuinely novel for contractor software. A roofer walks a roof, describes what they see out loud, and Handoff builds a quote from that audio. For a contractor who returns from a walkthrough and needs to send a proposal before the homeowner has called the next bidder, that speed is the difference between winning and losing the job. Contractors who need a more traditional takeoff-to-estimate workflow should also compare <a href="/estimate-rocket-review/">Estimate Rocket</a> and <a href="/clear-estimates-review/">Clear Estimates</a> before deciding that AI proposal generation is the right fit.

Custom pricing catalogs let contractors set their own labor and material rates so estimates stay consistent. AI-generated visuals can show what a completed project might look like, which helps homeowners visualize the work and reduces the "I did not expect it to look like that" friction that kills deals post-signature.

Where Handoff falls short: the AI credit limits on the Flex plan (50 credits per month) mean heavy users may need to upgrade quickly. The starting price of $119/month on annual billing is higher than most field service alternatives. And Handoff is focused on residential trades and remodelers - it is less proven for commercial construction, route-based service work, or larger crews.

GetApp data shows 42 verified reviews with positive ease-of-use sentiment. The reviews praise the proposal generation speed and professional output. The complaints center on pricing and the learning curve for setting up custom catalogs.

Shortlist Handoff if AI-powered estimating and proposals would meaningfully increase your close rate. It appears in our <a href="/field-service-software-alternatives/">field service alternatives roundup</a> as the top pick for AI-native estimating. Go in knowing the Flex plan's AI credit limit and plan your usage accordingly.

## Handoff pricing

Handoff publishes two plan levels. Flex is $119/month on annual billing or $149 month to month and includes 50 AI credits per month, 2 users, and 7-day free trial. Pro is $239/month on annual billing or $299 month to month and includes unlimited AI credits, 5 users, financing support, and priority support. Enterprise is custom quote for larger teams.

The AI credits are the key constraint to understand. Each proposal or estimate generation consumes credits. On the Flex plan, 50 credits per month means roughly 10-12 detailed proposals before hitting the limit. Heavy users will need the Pro plan at $239/month.

## Bottom line

ClockShark vs Handoff is a bottleneck decision first and a software decision second.

Choose ClockShark when the bottleneck is time tracking: you need to know where your crews were, when they worked, and whether the hours match the job site. It is the cleaner fit for construction, electrical, HVAC, landscaping, roofing, and painting crews of 5-100 people who need GPS-verified time stamps and geofencing in the base plan.

Choose Handoff when the bottleneck is sales: you need to turn leads into signed contracts faster with professional-looking proposals that homeowners trust. It is the cleaner fit for residential remodelers, roofers, and trade contractors who send estimates regularly and need AI-powered proposal generation.

The wrong choice will not fail on day one. It will fail in month three, when you realize the tool solved a problem you did not have while the real bottleneck is still bleeding money. Match the tool to the bottleneck before you compare feature lists.

## FAQ

### Is ClockShark or Handoff better for contractors?

ClockShark is better for contractors whose primary need is GPS-verified time tracking, geofencing, and job costing for field crews. Handoff is better for contractors whose primary need is AI-powered estimating and professional proposals for residential clients. They solve different problems and the right choice depends on which operational bottleneck costs you more money.

### Is ClockShark cheaper than Handoff?

ClockShark is cheaper at the entry level. Standard plan at $40/month plus $9 per user comes to $76/month for a five-person crew. Handoff Flex at $119/month on annual billing or $149 month to month includes 2 users and 50 AI credits per month. For pure time tracking, ClockShark is significantly more affordable. For AI-powered estimating, Handoff is the only option between the two.

### Does Handoff have time tracking?

Handoff includes basic time tracking within its project management features, but it is not a GPS time tracking tool. There is no geofencing, no clock-in/out GPS stamps, no mock location detection, and no crew map. If time tracking is your primary need, ClockShark is the better choice.

### Does ClockShark have estimating or proposal features?

ClockShark does not include estimating or proposal generation. It is a time tracking and scheduling tool. If you need AI-powered proposals and professional estimate templates, Handoff is the better choice.

### Which product has better integrations?

ClockShark has broader payroll integrations: QuickBooks (Online and Desktop), ADP, Gusto, Xero, Sage, Paychex, Paylocity, and 1,000+ apps via Zapier. Handoff integrates primarily with QuickBooks Online. ClockShark wins on integration breadth if payroll connectivity is important.

### Can I use ClockShark and Handoff together?

Yes. There is no direct integration between them, but they solve different problems and can complement each other. ClockShark handles time tracking and GPS verification. Handoff handles estimating and proposals. For a contractor who needs both operational visibility and sales velocity, using both may make sense.

### What should I test during the free trial?

In ClockShark, test GPS accuracy at your actual job sites, verify geofencing works with your typical work radius, and confirm the payroll export works with your existing platform. In Handoff, test the voice-to-estimate feature with a real job scenario, create a complete proposal with photos, and track how much faster the AI generation is compared to your current process.
