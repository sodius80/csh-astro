# Freshsales vs Pipedrive: No-Cost CRM or Paid Sales Pipeline?

Freshsales and Pipedrive are both sales CRMs, but they answer different budget questions. Freshsales asks: can a contractor get a working CRM at no cost, with phone and AI, before paying anything? Pipedrive asks: can a sales team get a focused, activity-based pipeline CRM starting at $14/seat/mo?

The answer to both questions is yes, but the right product depends on whether the contractor needs a no-cost starting point or a paid pipeline tool with deeper activity discipline.

Freshsales has a no-cost plan with built-in phone, AI lead scoring, and a modern interface. The official Freshworks pricing page confirms a no-cost plan for up to 3 users with Kanban views, email templates, built-in phone at 200 minutes per month, and live chat. For a contractor with a small office and a tight budget, that is a working CRM with no subscription.

Pipedrive is a paid sales pipeline CRM built around activity-based selling. The official Pipedrive pricing page lists four plans: Lite at $14 per seat per month, Growth at $39 per seat per month, Premium at $59 per seat per month, and Ultimate at $79 per seat per month, all when billed annually. A 14-day trial includes full access with no credit card. Pipedrive starts with paid plans after its trial.

That difference matters. A contractor who wants to test CRM discipline without spending money should pick Freshsales. A contractor who is ready to pay for a focused pipeline experience should pick Pipedrive.

Pick Freshsales if the office needs a CRM that works at zero cost. The official Freshsales pricing page confirms a no-cost plan with built-in phone (200 minutes per month), AI-based lead scoring through Freddy AI, email templates, visual Kanban pipeline views, and live chat. For a contractor, that covers the essential sales workflow: capture leads, score them, call the hot ones, track them through a pipeline, and follow up.

Pick Pipedrive if the office needs visible sales accountability and is willing to pay for it. ## When Freshsales makes more sense

Freshsales makes more sense when the contractor's biggest leak is sales follow-up and the team needs a CRM working this week, not next month, without spending money. The official Freshsales product page highlights Freddy AI lead scoring, Kanban views, built-in phone and email, contextual collaboration, and deal insights. That is the right shape for a contractor office that needs to know what happened with every open lead this week.

The no-cost tier is unusually generous. According to the official Freshworks pricing page, the no-cost plan includes:

- Up to 3 users at no cost
- Built-in phone with 200 minutes per month
- AI-based lead scoring through Freddy AI
- Visual Kanban views for contacts, accounts, and deals
- Email templates and live chat
- Contact and account management

For a contractor with a few people handling sales, that covers the essential workflow without a per-user cost. The built-in phone is particularly useful. A roofing company can call leads directly from the CRM, log the call, and move the deal forward without switching to a separate dialer.

The interface is modern and clean, with a lower learning curve than most no-cost CRMs, which is why it ranked as the top no-cost CRM in our [best free CRM for contractors](/best-free-crm-for-contractors/) roundup. A small team can create a pipeline like this:

- New lead
- Contacted
- Estimate scheduled
- Quote sent
- Follow-up due
- Won
- Lost

Every open deal can have an owner, an expected value, a stage, notes, email history, and a next activity. Managers can scan the board and see where work is stuck. Estimators can open the CRM and know which quote needs a call today.

The limitation is integrations. The no-cost tier includes only 1 third-party integration. Workflow automation and email cadences are not available on the no-cost tier, so follow-up structure has to be manual. The no-cost plan is pure sales CRM with no project management, job tracking, invoicing, or field service features. Pipedrive makes more sense when the office needs pipeline clarity and the team is ready to pay for it. The leads are coming in, but nobody can tell which ones were called, which estimates are scheduled, which quotes went out, and which customers need one more touch. Pipedrive also has the stronger mobile app story. Its iOS and Android apps sync cleanly with the web platform. ## No-cost tier vs trial: what you actually get

The no-cost tier is the heart of this comparison. Freshsales has one. Pipedrive does not. That single difference drives most of the buying decision.

### Freshsales no-cost tier

The official Freshworks pricing page describes the no-cost plan as including Kanban views, email templates, built-in phone, and live chat. The no-cost plan covers up to 3 users. Key features include:

- Visual Kanban pipeline for contacts, accounts, and deals
- Built-in phone with 200 minutes per month
- AI-based lead scoring through Freddy AI
- Email templates and live chat
- Contact and account management
- Mobile app access

For a contractor, that is a working sales CRM. A roofing company with 3 people in the office can track leads, call them from inside the CRM, score which ones are hottest, and move them through a pipeline without paying anything.

The limitation is integrations. The no-cost tier includes only 1 third-party integration. Workflow automation and email cadences are not available on the no-cost tier, so follow-up structure has to be manual. Custom fields, multiple sales pipelines, and auto-assignment rules require Growth at $9/user/month or higher.

### Pipedrive trial

Pipedrive does not have a no-cost tier. The official pricing page confirms a 14-day trial with no credit card required. After the trial, the lowest paid plan is Lite at $14 per seat per month when billed annually.

During the trial, a contractor gets full access to the platform. The office can create a pipeline, import leads, connect email, assign activities, and test whether the team actually updates deals. If the team ignores it, the failed test costs nothing in subscription fees.

The limitation is that the trial ends. A contractor who wants to keep using Pipedrive after 14 days must pay $14 per seat per month minimum. There is no fallback no-cost tier.

## Sales pipeline and deal tracking

Both products center on a visual pipeline, but the emphasis is different.

Pipedrive has the cleaner sales pipeline experience for most contractors. The product is built around deals moving through stages, with activities attached to each opportunity. That matters because contractor lead follow-up is usually a behavior problem before it is a software problem. Someone needs to call the lead. Someone needs to send the quote. Someone needs to follow up after a few days. Someone needs to mark the deal outcome.

A basic Pipedrive pipeline can mirror the real office process:

- New lead
- Contacted
- Estimate scheduled
- Quote sent
- Follow-up due
- Won
- Lost

That visual structure makes stuck work obvious. If six quotes sit in quote sent with no next activity, the office knows exactly what to do next.

Freshsales also supports pipelines, deals, contacts, and activities. The no-cost tier includes Kanban views, contact and account management, and basic deal tracking. The difference is emphasis. Freshsales's pipeline is strongest when it supports the no-cost value proposition: a working CRM at no cost with phone and AI. Pipedrive's pipeline is the center of the product, with activity tracking, smart sales feeds, forecasting, and reporting built around it.

On paid tiers, Freshsales closes the gap. Pro adds multiple sales pipelines, auto-assignment rules, territory management, and sales sequences. Enterprise adds custom modules and field-level permissions. For contractors comparing the two platforms at equal paid tiers, the pipeline experience is comparable. The difference is that Freshsales gives you a no-cost starting point and Pipedrive does not.

Decision signal: choose Pipedrive when the team needs a focused sales board with activity discipline and is willing to pay. Choose Freshsales when the team needs a working CRM at no cost and can live with manual follow-up structure on the no-cost tier.

## AI features: Freddy AI vs Pipedrive AI

Both platforms market AI capabilities, but the approach is different.

Freshsales includes Freddy AI on the no-cost tier. The official product page describes Freddy as a 24/7 assistant that highlights the best leads and deals, offers insights, and writes emails. On the no-cost plan, Freddy AI provides lead scoring that helps prioritize which leads to call first. On Pro and higher tiers, Freddy adds deal insights, sales email assistance (rephrase, expand, enhance text), and contact scoring. Enterprise adds forecasting insights.

Pipedrive added AI-powered report creation to its Lite plan. The official pricing page lists "AI-powered report creation" as a key feature on Lite, which lets users generate reports using text input or choose from pre-written prompts. However, Pipedrive does not offer AI-based lead scoring on any plan. Its AI features are focused on reporting and email tools on higher tiers, not on lead prioritization.

For a contractor on a no-cost tier, this is a real difference. Freshsales gives you AI lead scoring at no cost. Pipedrive does not, because it has no no-cost tier. If lead prioritization matters and the budget is zero, Freshsales has the edge.

For a contractor on paid tiers, the comparison is more nuanced. Freshsales Pro at $39/user/month annually offers Freddy AI deal insights, contact scoring, and email assistance. Pipedrive Premium at $59/seat/month annually offers AI-powered multi-email tools and custom scoring. The feature sets overlap but are not identical. The choice depends more on the broader platform fit than on AI alone.

## Built-in phone and communication

Freshsales has the stronger built-in communication story for contractors. The official pricing page lists built-in phone, email, and live chat on the no-cost plan. For a contractor who wants to call leads from inside the CRM without buying a separate phone system, that is a genuine advantage.

The no-cost tier includes 200 minutes per month of built-in phone calling. A salesperson can dial a lead from inside the CRM, log the call, attach notes, and move the deal forward without switching tools. For a roofing, HVAC, or remodeling company where speed-to-call matters, that is real value at zero cost.

On Pro and higher, Freshsales adds Bring Your Own Carrier (BYOC) for phone, sales sequences, and advanced workflows. The communication story deepens as the team upgrades.

Pipedrive does not include built-in phone on any plan. The official pricing page lists email sync, tracking, and automation on Growth and higher tiers, but no phone system. A contractor who wants to call from inside the CRM would need a third-party integration or a separate dialer.

For contractors who already have a phone system, Pipedrive's email sync and tracking are enough. For contractors who want calling built into the CRM at no extra cost, Freshsales is the only option.

## Customization and workflows

Pipedrive is customizable enough for most contractor sales pipelines. Custom fields, pipeline stages, activities, reports, automations, and integrations are available on paid tiers. The platform handles multiple pipelines, custom deal fields, and workflow automation without requiring deep configuration knowledge. For a contractor with a straightforward sales process, that is sufficient.

Freshsales is also customizable, but the no-cost tier has limits. The no-cost plan includes basic workflows but no custom fields, no multiple sales pipelines, and no auto-assignment rules. Those features require Growth at $9/user/month or higher. Pro adds multiple sales pipelines, auto-assignment rules, territory management, and sales sequences. Enterprise adds custom modules and field-level permissions.

## Contractor operations fit

Neither product should be sold internally as dispatch software.

Freshsales can help with sales leads, pipeline stages, contact history, email sync, reminders, reporting, and sales follow-up. It will not manage technician routes, job schedules, field production, parts, job costing, service agreements, or crew dispatch.

Pipedrive can help with lead tracking, sales accountability, quote follow-up, email history, reporting, and handoff before the job is sold. It does not manage crews, routes, technician schedules, inventory, production calendars, or field service workflows.

That is not a dealbreaker if you buy the right tool for the right job. A contractor using Jobber or Housecall Pro for operations may still use Freshsales or Pipedrive for sales pipeline discipline. The two tools solve different problems at different stages of the business.

The handoff question matters more than the brand:

1. Where does a lead enter?
2. Who qualifies the lead?
3. Where is the estimate scheduled?
4. Where is the quote created?
5. When does a won opportunity become a job?
6. Which system owns scheduling, dispatch, job costing, and invoicing?
7. Which system owns the customer record after the job closes?

If most answers are sales-stage answers, either CRM can help. If most answers are dispatch or production answers, start with operations software and use CRM as a supporting tool.

## Where Freshsales falls short

Freshsales's biggest limitation is the no-cost tier's feature ceiling. The no-cost plan includes basic workflows but no custom fields, no multiple sales pipelines, no auto-assignment rules, and only 1 third-party integration. A contractor connecting QuickBooks, Zapier, or a field service tool will need to upgrade to Growth at $9/user/month or higher.

The second limitation is ecosystem depth. Freshsales can integrate with many tools through the Freshworks Marketplace, but it is still primarily a sales CRM. If a contractor wants one vendor to cover CRM, accounting, projects, invoices, inventory, support, and field service, neither Freshworks nor Pipedrive has that breadth. See [Zoho CRM](/freshsales-vs-zoho-crm/) for a CRM with a broader ecosystem.

The third limitation is the 3-user cap on the no-cost tier. A growing team that adds a fourth salesperson must upgrade to a paid plan. The no-cost tier is a starter, not a permanent solution for a growing office.

## Where Pipedrive falls short

Pipedrive's biggest limitation is the absence of a no-cost tier. The 14-day trial gives a contractor office enough time to test, but after that, the lowest paid plan is Lite at $14 per seat per month. A tiny team that truly needs to avoid subscription cost should start with Freshsales or another no-cost CRM.

The second limitation is the lack of built-in phone. Pipedrive supports email sync and tracking but does not include a phone system on any plan. A contractor who wants to call leads from inside the CRM needs a third-party integration or a separate dialer. Freshsales includes 200 minutes per month on its no-cost plan.

The third limitation is per-seat pricing. Pipedrive starts cheap at $14 per seat per month, but every additional office user or salesperson adds cost. A 5-person team on Growth pays $195 per month. The team should price Lite, Growth, Premium, and Ultimate against the exact workflow they need before committing.

## Bottom line for contractors

Freshsales and Pipedrive are both legitimate sales CRM choices, but they answer different questions.

Choose <a href="/go/freshsales-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Freshsales</a> if the business needs a no-cost CRM with built-in phone, AI lead scoring, and a modern interface the team can adopt this week. It is the better default for contractors whose main problem is lead and quote follow-up, especially when calling leads from inside the CRM matters and the budget is zero.

Choose <a href="/go/pipedrive-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Pipedrive</a> if the business is ready to pay $14 per seat per month and wants a focused, activity-based sales pipeline with stronger deal tracking and mobile app support. It is the better pick for contractors who need visible sales accountability and are willing to invest in a paid CRM.

If the job is dispatch, scheduling, route management, field production, or job costing, neither should be the first purchase. Start with [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) and add CRM only where the sales process needs extra structure.
