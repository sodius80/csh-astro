# Freshsales vs Keap: Free CRM or Marketing Automation?

Freshsales and Keap are both CRMs, but they answer fundamentally different questions. Freshsales asks: can a contractor get a working CRM for free, with built-in phone and AI, before paying anything? Keap asks: should a contractor replace their CRM, email tool, text marketing, quote system, and invoice platform with one automated customer lifecycle engine?

The answer to both questions is yes, but the right product depends on whether the contractor needs a no-cost sales CRM or a full marketing automation platform.

Freshsales is a free CRM with built-in phone, AI lead scoring, and a modern interface. The official Freshworks pricing page confirms a free plan for up to 3 users with Kanban views, email templates, built-in phone at 200 minutes per month, and live chat. For a contractor with a small office and a tight budget, that is a working CRM at no cost.

Keap is a heavier CRM and marketing automation platform. The official Keap pricing page lists a single full-platform subscription starting at $299 per month billed monthly for 2 users and 1,500 contacts. That price includes email marketing, text marketing, sales pipeline, appointment scheduling, quotes, invoices, checkout forms, and payment processing. Keap also says implementation services are required, which adds a one-time cost.

My call: most contractors who need a no-cost sales CRM should start with <a href="/go/freshsales-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Freshsales</a> for its built-in phone, AI lead scoring, and free tier. Choose <a href="/go/keap-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Keap</a> when the problem is bigger than pipeline tracking and the office needs automated nurture, email and text campaigns, quotes, invoices, and payment workflow tied to the CRM.

## Quick verdict

Pick Freshsales if the office needs a CRM that works at zero cost. The official Freshsales pricing page confirms a free plan with built-in phone (200 minutes per month), AI-based lead scoring through Freddy AI, email templates, visual Kanban pipeline views, and live chat. For a contractor, that covers the essential sales workflow: capture leads, score them, call the hot ones, track them through a pipeline, and follow up.

Pick Keap if the sales pipeline is only one part of the problem. Keap's official pricing page describes CRM, email marketing, text marketing, lead source tracking, sales pipeline, appointment scheduling, quotes, invoices, checkout forms, and payment processing inside the platform. It is the more complete customer lifecycle platform, but it is also the heavier commitment in both price and setup.

Skip both if you are looking for field service dispatch. Neither platform replaces [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), [ServiceTitan](/servicetitan-review/), or the broader [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) category. You will still need operations software for technician scheduling, route management, job costing, and field workflows.

## When Freshsales makes more sense

Freshsales makes more sense when the contractor's biggest leak is sales follow-up and the team needs a CRM working this week, not next month, without spending money. The official Freshsales product page highlights Freddy AI lead scoring, Kanban views, built-in phone and email, contextual collaboration, and deal insights. That is the right shape for a contractor office that needs to know what happened with every open lead this week.

The free tier is unusually generous. According to the official Freshworks pricing page, the free plan includes:

- Up to 3 users at no cost
- Built-in phone with 200 minutes per month
- AI-based lead scoring through Freddy AI
- Visual Kanban views for contacts, accounts, and deals
- Email templates and live chat
- Contact and account management

For a contractor with a few people handling sales, that covers the essential workflow without a per-user cost. The built-in phone is particularly useful. A roofing company can call leads directly from the CRM, log the call, and move the deal forward without switching to a separate dialer.

The interface is modern and clean, with a lower learning curve than most free CRMs, which is why it ranked as the top free CRM in our [best free CRM for contractors](/best-free-crm-for-contractors/) roundup. A small team can create a pipeline like this:

- New lead
- Contacted
- Estimate scheduled
- Quote sent
- Follow-up due
- Won
- Lost

Every open deal can have an owner, an expected value, a stage, notes, email history, and a next activity. Managers can scan the board and see where work is stuck. Estimators can open the CRM and know which quote needs a call today.

The limitation is that Freshsales is a pure sales CRM. There is no project management, job tracking, invoicing, or field service features. Workflow automation and email cadences are not available on the free tier, so follow-up structure has to be manual. A contractor who needs automated email sequences or text campaigns will not find them here without upgrading to paid tiers. For a deeper comparison of how Freshsales stacks up against another dedicated sales CRM, see [Freshsales vs Pipedrive](/freshsales-vs-pipedrive/).

## When Keap makes more sense

Keap makes sense when marketing follow-up is the expensive bottleneck. The lead form comes in, the office calls once, nobody follows up, and the prospect goes cold. The old customer list sits unused. Seasonal promotions are sent manually or not at all. The estimator has no easy way to trigger a sequence after a quote is sent.

Keap is built to solve that. The official Keap pricing page describes a platform with CRM, email marketing, text marketing, lead source tracking, sales pipeline, appointment scheduling, quotes, invoices, checkout forms, and payment processing. The practical value is that sales and marketing actions can live around the same contact record instead of being split across Mailchimp, a spreadsheet, QuickBooks, and a basic CRM.

A remodeling company, roofing company, or specialty trade with a long consideration cycle could use Keap to:

- capture a quote request through a form
- tag the lead by service interest or source
- send an automated email sequence
- create a follow-up task for the office
- send appointment links
- send a quote
- convert the quote to an invoice
- keep nurture campaigns running for old leads and past customers

That is a broader workflow than Freshsales is trying to cover. Freshsales can track the opportunity and help the team call leads. Keap can track the opportunity and automate more of the surrounding customer communication.

## Pricing comparison

The price difference between these two products is dramatic.

Freshsales has a no-cost starter edition. Atlas-verified Freshsales pricing lists a Free plan at $0 for up to 3 users. Paid plans are Growth at $9 per user per month billed annually or $11 per user per month billed monthly, Pro at $39 per user per month annually or $47 per user per month monthly, and Enterprise at $59 per user per month annually or $71 per user per month monthly. Freshsales also advertises a 21-day free trial with no credit card required. The live Freshworks pricing page confirms all of these rates as of today.

Keap starts at $299 per month billed monthly for 2 users and 1,500 contacts, according to the current official pricing page. Additional users are $39 per month each. Keap also publishes text marketing tiers: Tier 1 is included, with higher tiers at $24, $39, $79, $139, and $279 per month. Optional text message add-on is $6 per month, local number add-on is $10 per month, and overages are $0.015 per text message and $0.01 per voice minute. Keap also says implementation services are required, and the official implementation page lists a one-time implementation investment of $500 (shown against a struck-through $1,500).

For concrete context, here is what a two-person contractor office pays:

| Scenario | Freshsales | Keap |

| Marketing automation | Basic workflows on free tier; advanced on Pro at $39 | Core feature - email campaigns, text marketing, automated sequences included |
| Quotes and invoices | CPQ add-on at $19/user/mo | Built into the platform |

| Free trial | 21 days, no credit card | Free trial available |
| Best budget fit | Teams that want phone, AI, and pipeline at zero cost | Companies replacing multiple marketing and CRM workflows |

The simple rule: if you are buying CRM because leads are disorganized and the budget is zero, Freshsales is the obvious choice. If you are buying CRM because the entire lead-to-customer communication process needs automation, Keap has a stronger argument despite the much higher price.

## Free CRM vs marketing automation: what you are actually buying

The core difference is not price. It is platform philosophy.

Freshsales is a sales CRM. Its job is to show every lead, every deal stage, every next activity, and every person responsible for follow-up. The free tier includes phone, AI scoring, and a clean pipeline. The paid tiers add custom fields, multiple pipelines, auto-assignment, and Salesforce-like customization. But the product is still fundamentally a sales tool that helps a team organize opportunities before the job is sold.

Keap is a customer lifecycle platform. Its job is to keep the customer record, email follow-up, text marketing, forms, appointment links, quotes, invoices, checkout forms, and automation campaigns in one system. The CRM pipeline exists inside that broader system. A contractor using Keap can automate the entire flow from lead capture to paid invoice.

That difference matters because it changes the buying decision. A contractor who picks Freshsales is investing in sales discipline. A contractor who picks Keap is investing in marketing automation and customer lifecycle management. The first costs nothing to start. The second costs $299 per month plus implementation.

If a contractor is not sure which problem they have, start with Freshsales. The free tier makes it easy to test CRM adoption without risk. If the office discovers that the real bottleneck is not pipeline tracking but automated follow-up and nurture, that is the signal to look at Keap.

## Built-in phone and communication

Freshsales has the stronger built-in communication story for contractors who want calling inside the CRM. The official Freshworks pricing page lists built-in phone, email, and live chat on the free plan. The free tier includes 200 minutes per month of built-in phone calling. A salesperson can dial a lead from inside the CRM, log the call, attach notes, and move the deal forward without switching tools.

On Pro at $39 per user per month annually, Freshsales adds Bring Your Own Carrier (BYOC) for phone, sales sequences, and advanced workflows. The communication story deepens as the team upgrades.

Keap handles communication differently. Its pricing page lists text marketing as a core feature, with Tier 1 included and higher tiers available. Voice minutes are charged at $0.01 per minute overage. Keap does not position itself as a phone system the way Freshsales does. Its communication strength is in automated text and email campaigns, not in making calls from the CRM.

For contractors who want to call leads from inside the CRM at no extra cost, Freshsales has the edge. For contractors who want automated text message follow-up sequences and campaigns, Keap is stronger.

## Marketing automation depth

Keap wins on marketing automation depth for contractors who actually need it. Its official pricing page lists email marketing, email broadcasts, one-to-one email messages, newsletters, AI content assistance, AI automation assistance, text marketing, automated text messaging, forms, landing pages, and campaign workflows. That is the product's center of gravity.

For contractors, this matters when the sales cycle includes education and repeated touches. A roof replacement lead might need financing information, insurance claim education, a case study, a seasonal reminder, and a final check-in. A remodeler might need separate sequences for kitchen leads, bathroom leads, past clients, and cold estimates. Keap is built for that kind of nurture system.

Freshsales handles marketing differently. The free tier includes email templates and live chat but no workflow automation or email cadences. Growth at $9 per user per month adds basic workflows. Pro at $39 adds advanced workflows, sales sequences, and auto-assignment rules. Enterprise adds custom module workflows. The automation capabilities exist on paid tiers, but they are sales-focused (sales sequences, activity rules) rather than marketing-focused (email broadcasts, text campaigns, landing pages).

Decision signal: choose Keap if campaigns are the job. Choose Freshsales if the office needs a CRM with some sales workflow automation but not a full marketing engine. For another angle on how Keap compares to a simpler CRM, see [Keap vs Pipedrive](/keap-vs-pipedrive/).

## Quotes, invoices, and payments

Keap has the stronger built-in business workflow for contractors who need quotes, invoices, and payments tied to the CRM. The official Keap pricing page lists quotes, invoices, checkout forms, and payment processing as platform features. A contractor can create a quote, send it, track whether it was opened, convert it to an invoice, and collect payment, all from the same customer record.

Freshsales does not include quotes or invoices in its standard CRM plans. The CPQ (Configure, Price, Quote) add-on is available at $19 per user per month and generates branded documents including quotes, invoices, onboarding documents, and contracts. But Freshsales does not handle payment processing, checkout forms, or invoice-to-payment workflow the way Keap does.

For a contractor who wants one system to manage leads, send quotes, invoice customers, and collect payment, Keap has the edge. For a contractor who already has QuickBooks or another invoicing system and only needs CRM, Freshsales is sufficient. For contractors exploring CRM options with accounting integration, see [CRM with QuickBooks integration](/crm-with-quickbooks-integration/).

## Customization and workflows

Both platforms offer customization, but the depth depends on the tier.

Freshsales free tier includes basic workflows but no custom fields, no multiple sales pipelines, and no auto-assignment rules. Growth at $9 per user per month adds custom fields and basic workflows. Pro at $39 adds multiple sales pipelines, auto-assignment rules, territory management, and sales sequences. Enterprise at $59 adds custom modules, field-level permissions, sandbox, and audit logs.

Keap offers customization through its automation builder, campaign workflows, custom fields, tags, and forms. Because Keap is a single-platform product (not tiered), the customization is consistent across all users. The tradeoff is that Keap's platform is heavier to configure than Freshsales's free tier.

| Feature | Freshsales Free | Freshsales Growth ($9) | Freshsales Pro ($39) | Keap ($299) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Custom fields | No | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Multiple pipelines | No | No | Yes | Yes |
| Auto-assignment | No | No | Yes | Yes |
| Workflow automation | Basic | Basic | Advanced | Advanced (campaign builder) |
| Marketing campaigns | No | No | Sales sequences | Email + text campaigns |
| Quotes | No | No | No (CPQ add-on $19) | Built in |
| Invoices | No | No | No | Built in |
| Payment processing | No | No | No | Built in |

If the goal is to make lead follow-up visible at no cost, Freshsales's simpler free-tier approach is enough. If the goal is to model a more complex customer lifecycle with automated campaigns, quotes, and invoices, Keap is the more complete platform.

## Contractor operations fit

Neither product should be treated as field service software.

Freshsales can help with sales leads, pipeline stages, contact history, email sync, reminders, reporting, and sales follow-up. It will not manage technician routes, job schedules, field production, parts, job costing, service agreements, or crew dispatch. See the [Freshsales vs Zoho CRM](/freshsales-vs-zoho-crm/) comparison for another free CRM angle.

Keap can handle customer communication, forms, appointment links, quotes, invoices, checkout forms, and payments. That makes it feel closer to an all-in-one business system than Freshsales. But it still does not replace route scheduling, technician dispatch, inventory, field mobile workflows, or job costing. A contractor using Keap may still need Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan, or another operations platform for the actual work. The [Keap vs MailerLite](/keap-vs-mailerlite/) comparison shows how Keap stacks up against a lighter email marketing tool.

That handoff is not a weakness if you choose the product for the right reason. Freshsales is for sales pipeline visibility at no cost. Keap is for marketing and customer communication automation. Field service software is for running jobs. See [best CRM for contractors](/best-crm-for-contractors/) for where each fits in the broader contractor software stack.

## Ease of setup

Freshsales is easier to test. The pricing page offers a 21-day free trial with no credit card required, and the free tier can serve as a permanent starting point for up to 3 users. A contractor office can create a few stages, import leads, connect email, assign activities, and know within a week whether the team will actually use it.

Keap is more of a setup project. Keap's own pricing page says required implementation services help users start with strategy consulting, data import, migration, and done-for-you services. The implementation page lists a one-time investment of $500. That is not a bad thing. It is a signal. Keap is powerful enough that configuration matters, and the implementation cost reflects the depth of the platform.

If your office is still debating what a lead stage should be called, start with Freshsales. If you already know your lifecycle stages and want automation built around them, Keap's heavier onboarding may be worth the effort.

## Where Freshsales falls short

Freshsales's biggest limitation is the free tier's feature ceiling. The free plan includes basic workflows but no custom fields, no multiple sales pipelines, no auto-assignment rules, and only 1 third-party integration. A contractor connecting QuickBooks, Zapier, or a field service tool will need to upgrade to Growth at $9 per user per month or higher.

The second limitation is the absence of marketing automation depth. Freshsales can handle sales sequences and basic workflows on paid tiers, but it does not have the email campaign builder, text marketing, landing pages, and automated nurture sequences that Keap offers. If marketing automation is the core need, Freshsales will not be enough.

The third limitation is the 3-user cap on the free tier. A growing team that adds a fourth salesperson must upgrade to a paid plan. The free tier is a starter, not a permanent solution for a growing office. For contractors weighing free CRM options, see [best free CRM for contractors](/best-free-crm-for-contractors/).

## Where Keap falls short

Keap's biggest drawback is price. A $299 per month billed-monthly starting point changes the buying decision. It is no longer a cheap CRM experiment. It has to replace enough manual follow-up, missed leads, separate email tools, quote workflow, and customer communication to justify the spend.

The second drawback is implementation. Keap can do more because it touches more of the customer journey. That also means more decisions: tags, forms, fields, campaigns, appointment settings, quote workflow, invoice settings, payment settings, and reporting. Contractors who want a CRM by Friday may find Keap too much.

The third drawback is fit. If all you want is a sales pipeline, Keap is more tool than you need. It can become shelfware if nobody owns the automation build. A contractor who just needs lead tracking and a clean sales board should not start here. For a lighter-weight alternative, see how Keap compares to [ActiveCampaign](/activecampaign-vs-keap/) for email-first automation.

## Bottom line for contractors

Freshsales and Keap are both legitimate CRM choices, but they answer different questions.

Choose <a href="/go/freshsales-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Freshsales</a> if the business needs a free CRM with built-in phone, AI lead scoring, and a modern interface the team can adopt this week. It is the better default for contractors whose main problem is lead and quote follow-up, especially when calling leads from inside the CRM matters and the budget is zero.

Choose <a href="/go/keap-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Keap</a> if the business needs a full marketing automation platform with CRM, email campaigns, text marketing, automated follow-up sequences, quotes, invoices, and payment processing in one system. It is the better pick for contractors who need to automate the entire customer lifecycle and have the budget for implementation.

If the job is dispatch, scheduling, route management, field production, or job costing, neither should be the first purchase. Start with [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) and add CRM only where the sales process needs extra structure.
