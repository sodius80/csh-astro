# Freshsales vs Moosend for Contractors: CRM or Email?

Freshsales and Moosend are both affordable tools that help a contractor follow up with leads and customers, but they are not built for the same job. Freshsales is a sales CRM with a free tier, built-in phone, AI lead scoring, and a visual deal pipeline. Moosend is an email marketing platform with campaigns, automation workflows, landing pages, subscription forms, and audience segmentation.

That split matters. If the office cannot tell which lead was called, which estimate is scheduled, or which quote needs a follow-up today, start with Freshsales. If the office has a list of old estimates, past customers, and seasonal buyers that never get enough email communication, start with Moosend.

Start with <a href="/go/moosend-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Moosend</a> if the missing piece is email marketing and the team already has leads somewhere but cannot communicate with them at scale.

## Quick verdict

Pick Freshsales if the office needs a CRM that works immediately and for free. The official Freshworks pricing page confirms a free plan for up to 3 users with built-in phone (200 minutes per month), AI-based lead scoring through Freddy AI, email templates, Kanban pipeline views, and live chat. Paid plans start at $9 per user per month billed annually on the Growth tier, with Pro at $39 and Enterprise at $59 per user per month annually. Freshsales also offers a 21-day free trial with no credit card required.

Pick Moosend if the office needs affordable email marketing and already has a list to work with. The official Moosend pricing page shows a 30-day free trial with no credit card required, Pro starting at $7 per month when billed annually for 500 contacts, or $9 per month on monthly billing. Moosend+ and Enterprise are custom-quote plans. The page also publishes pay-as-you-go email credits separately for occasional senders.

Skip both if you are trying to dispatch technicians, schedule jobs, route crews, or run field production. Neither Freshsales nor Moosend replaces [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), [ServiceTitan](/servicetitan-review/), or a broader [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) platform. For a wider view of where CRM fits in the contractor software stack, see [best CRM for contractors](/best-crm-for-contractors/).

## When Freshsales makes more sense

Freshsales makes more sense when the contractor's biggest leak is sales follow-up and the team needs a working CRM this week, not next month. The official Freshworks product page highlights Freddy AI lead scoring, Kanban views, built-in phone and email, contextual collaboration, deal insights, and mobile app access. That is the right shape for a contractor office that needs to know what happened with every open lead.

The free tier is unusually generous. According to the official Freshworks pricing page, the free plan includes:

- Up to 3 users at no cost
- Built-in phone with 200 minutes per month
- AI-based lead scoring through Freddy AI
- Visual Kanban views for contacts, accounts, and deals
- Email templates and live chat
- Contact and account management
- Mobile app access

For a contractor with a few people handling sales, that covers the essential workflow without a per-user cap forcing upgrades. The built-in phone is particularly useful. A roofing company can call leads directly from the CRM, log the call, and move the deal forward without switching to a separate dialer. The interface is modern and clean, which is why Freshsales ranked as the top free CRM in our [best free CRM for contractors](/best-free-crm-for-contractors/) roundup.

A small team can create a pipeline like this:

- New lead
- Contacted
- Estimate scheduled
- Quote sent
- Follow-up due
- Won
- Lost

Every open deal can have an owner, an expected value, a stage, notes, email history, and a next activity. Managers can scan the board and see where work is stuck. Estimators can open the CRM and know which quote needs a call today. For contractors comparing Freshsales with other free CRMs, the [Freshsales vs Zoho CRM](/freshsales-vs-zoho-crm/) comparison covers a different angle on the free-tier question.

## When Moosend makes more sense

Moosend makes more sense when the contractor has a communication problem, not a pipeline problem. The contact list exists. The estimate history exists. The past-customer list exists. But follow-up is inconsistent because someone has to remember to send emails manually.

That is where Moosend fits. Its official feature page describes email designs, custom landing pages, automated campaigns, marketing automation, subscription forms, audience management, segmentation, personalization, reporting, and support. For a contractor, that maps to practical marketing work:

- Send a quote-request follow-up sequence after a website form
- Invite old estimates back with a seasonal offer
- Send maintenance reminders to past customers
- Segment contacts by service interest, location, lead source, or job type
- Publish a simple landing page for a campaign
- Capture new subscribers through forms
- Review opens, clicks, and campaign reporting before the next send

Moosend is not trying to run the whole sales department. It is trying to help the company communicate better with the leads and customers already in its world. For contractors who want to see how Moosend compares to other email marketing tools, the [best email marketing software for contractors](/best-email-marketing-software-contractors/) roundup covers the broader landscape.

## Pricing comparison

Both products publish transparent pricing, but the unit is completely different. Freshsales charges per user. Moosend charges per contact count and plan level.

Atlas-verified Freshsales pricing lists a Free plan at $0 for up to 3 users. Paid plans are Growth at $9 per user per month billed annually or $11 per user per month billed monthly, Pro at $39 per user per month annually or $47 per user per month monthly, and Enterprise at $59 per user per month annually or $71 per user per month monthly. Freshsales also advertises a 21-day free trial with no credit card required.

Atlas-verified Moosend pricing lists Pro starting at $7 per month when billed annually for 500 contacts, or $9 per month on monthly billing. Moosend+ and Enterprise are custom-quote plans. The official pricing page shows the 30-day no-card trial and publishes pay-as-you-go email credit blocks separately: 350,000 credits for $350, 500,000 for $500, 750,000 for $750, and 1,000,000 for $1,000.

The simple rule: if you are paying to track leads through a sales pipeline, Freshsales is easier to justify. If you are paying to send better campaigns to a list, Moosend is easier to justify.

The pricing models also scale differently. Freshsales costs more as the team adds users. Moosend costs more as the contact list grows. A contractor with 2 salespeople and 500 contacts will pay $0 on Freshsales free or $7 per month on Moosend Pro annual. A contractor with 5 salespeople and 5,000 contacts will need Freshsales Growth at $45 per month annually for the team, while Moosend Pro scales by contact tier and the price increases as the list grows past each threshold.

## Sales CRM and pipeline depth

Freshsales wins the CRM comparison. It is a sales CRM built around leads, deals, contacts, accounts, activities, and pipeline stages. Moosend can manage audiences and automate marketing journeys, but it is not a full sales CRM built around deal stages, activity ownership, and pipeline reporting.

Freshsales's advantage is that the sales process becomes visible. A contractor can see which leads are new, which estimates are scheduled, which quotes are waiting, which follow-ups are overdue, and which deals were won or lost. Every open opportunity has an owner and a next activity. That matters because many contractor sales problems are accountability problems. Someone forgot the lead. Someone never called back. Someone sent the quote but did not follow up. Freshsales gives that work a structure.

The AI advantage is also real. Freddy AI lead scoring on the free tier helps prioritize which leads to call first. On Pro and higher, Freddy adds deal insights, contact scoring, and sales email assistance. For contractors who want to see how Freshsales compares to other CRM pipelines, the [Freshsales vs Pipeline CRM](/freshsales-vs-pipeline-crm/) comparison covers a dedicated sales CRM alternative.

Moosend can still support the sales cycle, but it does so through communication. It helps send the right message to the right segment at the right time. It does not replace the board where every open sales opportunity is tracked and assigned.

## Email marketing and automation

Moosend wins the email marketing comparison. It is the product built for campaigns, newsletter design, landing pages, subscription forms, segmentation, automation workflows, and campaign reporting. The official Moosend feature page emphasizes email designs, marketing automation, subscription forms, landing pages, audience management, segmentation, personalization, and reporting.

For a contractor, Moosend fits campaigns like:

- Roof replacement follow-up after an inspection request
- HVAC tune-up reminders before a seasonal rush
- Remodeling education emails for long-cycle prospects
- Past-customer reactivation campaigns
- Old-estimate follow-up before a promotion expires
- Newsletter-style updates for homeowners or property managers

The automation story is stronger in Moosend. Its marketing automation workflows can trigger emails based on list membership, contact behavior, form submissions, opens, clicks, and segment changes. A contractor can build a simple nurture sequence that sends a welcome email, waits three days, sends a case study, waits a week, and sends a call-to-action for a free estimate.

Freshsales is not an email marketing platform. It includes email templates, email sync, and basic email functionality for sales follow-up, but it does not have the campaign builder, list segmentation, automation workflow designer, or landing page tools that Moosend provides. A contractor who wants to run regular email campaigns to a list of 500 or more contacts should not try to do that inside Freshsales.

## Lead capture and follow-up

Moosend has the stronger native lead-capture story for marketing. Its official materials emphasize landing pages and subscription forms alongside email campaigns and automation. That makes sense when the contractor wants a simple campaign page, a sign-up form, or a lead magnet tied directly to the email list.

Freshsales has the stronger sales follow-up story after the lead becomes an opportunity. The pricing page and product pages emphasize leads, contacts, deals, activities, calendar, pipeline, automations, contact timeline, lead generation, routing, and reporting by plan. The built-in phone and AI scoring make the follow-up faster: call the hottest leads first, log the conversation, and move the deal to the next stage.

The handoff question is important:

1. Where does the lead enter?
2. Who qualifies it?
3. Where does the estimate get scheduled?
4. Who owns the quote follow-up?
5. When does the prospect move to won or lost?
6. Which system keeps communicating after the job closes?

If most answers are about forms, lists, segments, and campaigns, Moosend deserves the first look. If most answers are about owners, activities, deal stages, phone calls, and sales reporting, Freshsales deserves the first look.

A contractor could also use both. Freshsales handles the sales pipeline and call follow-up. Moosend handles the email list and campaign communication. That is a reasonable two-tool stack for a contractor who has both a sales process and a marketing list. The mistake is expecting either tool to do both jobs at once.

## Contractor operations fit

Neither product should be treated as contractor operations software.

Freshsales can help with sales leads, pipeline stages, contact history, email sync, reminders, reporting, and sales follow-up. It will not manage technician schedules, routes, field production, inventory, job costing, service agreements, or crew dispatch.

Moosend can help with newsletters, nurture campaigns, forms, landing pages, customer reactivation, quote follow-up emails, and audience segmentation. It will not dispatch technicians, route crews, build estimates, manage work orders, collect job photos, track parts, or handle field invoices.

That is not a problem if the buyer knows the role. A contractor can use Jobber or Housecall Pro for operations, Freshsales for sales pipeline, and Moosend for email marketing. The mistake is expecting one of these tools to do all three jobs. ## Where Freshsales falls short

Freshsales's biggest limitation is email marketing. It is a sales CRM, not a campaign platform. If the company mainly wants newsletters, landing pages, subscription forms, automation recipes, and segment-based email sends, Freshsales is not the right tool. Moosend is the cleaner product to evaluate first for that job.

The second limitation is free-tier integration access. The free plan includes only 1 third-party integration. A contractor connecting QuickBooks, Zapier, or a field service tool will need to upgrade to a paid Freshsales plan. The free tier also lacks workflow automation and email cadences, so follow-up structure has to be manual until the team upgrades.

The third limitation is scope. Freshsales is pure sales CRM with no project management, job tracking, invoicing, or field service features. For contractors who need accounting alongside CRM, see [CRM with QuickBooks integration](/crm-with-quickbooks-integration/).

The fourth limitation is operations fit. Freshsales can manage the opportunity before the job is won. It does not replace dispatch, technician scheduling, job costing, or field service work after the sale.

## Where Moosend falls short

Moosend's biggest limitation is CRM depth. It can support marketing follow-up and lead nurturing, but it is not where I would run a contractor sales pipeline. If the company needs deal owners, stage movement, next activities, quote follow-up, sales reporting, and manager visibility, Freshsales is the more natural fit.

The second limitation is operational handoff. Moosend can bring a lead in through a form or landing page and keep communication moving, but the business still needs somewhere to schedule the estimate, quote the job, dispatch the crew, and invoice the customer.

The third limitation is ownership. Email marketing only works when someone owns the list, segments, forms, templates, campaigns, and reporting. If nobody will build the campaigns, a low entry price does not matter much. For contractors comparing Moosend against CRM-led alternatives, the [Moosend vs Pipedrive](/moosend-vs-pipedrive/) and [Moosend vs Zoho CRM](/moosend-vs-zoho-crm/) comparisons cover different CRM angles.

The fourth limitation is integration breadth. Moosend has 80+ integrations, but its integration library is smaller than ActiveCampaign, MailerLite, or larger email marketing platforms. A contractor with a complex tech stack should check the integration directory before committing.

## Evaluation plan

### Test Freshsales with one real sales pipeline

Do not judge Freshsales from a blank account. Build the test around the sales day:

1. Create stages that match the real contractor sales process.
2. Import a few active leads or opportunities.
3. Assign owners and next activities.
4. Connect email and calendar.
5. Move a quote through the pipeline.
6. Check whether the office can see overdue follow-ups without asking around.

If the board makes lead follow-up clearer within a week, Freshsales is doing its job. If the board is clean but customers still never get nurture emails, add Moosend next.

### Test Moosend with one real campaign

Do not judge Moosend from a blank account. Pick one real contractor campaign and build it:

1. Import a small clean contact segment.
2. Create a form or landing page for one offer.
3. Build a short follow-up sequence.
4. Segment contacts by service interest or lead source.
5. Send a test campaign.
6. Review reporting and decide whether the office can maintain it.

If that feels useful and someone will own it, Moosend can be a strong low-cost marketing layer. If the team still cannot track which quote needs a call, fix CRM first with Freshsales.

## Bottom line for contractors

Freshsales and Moosend answer different questions.

Freshsales answers: "How do we stop losing sales opportunities because nobody knows the next step?" It is the better pick when the company needs deal stages, owners, activities, reminders, phone calling, AI scoring, email sync, and sales reporting. It starts free for 3 users with built-in phone and AI.

Moosend answers: "How do we communicate better with leads and customers through email campaigns, automation, forms, landing pages, and segmentation?" It is the better pick when the company has a list worth nurturing and wants a low-cost email marketing platform. It starts at $7 per month for Pro at 500 contacts.

Start with <a href="/go/freshsales-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Freshsales</a> if sales follow-up discipline is the bottleneck. Start with <a href="/go/moosend-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Moosend</a> if campaign follow-up and list marketing are the bottleneck. Use both if the office has both a sales pipeline to track and a list to communicate with.

If the job is dispatch, scheduling, route management, field production, or job costing, neither should be the first purchase. Start with [field service software](/best-field-service-software/) and add CRM or email marketing as the supporting layer.
