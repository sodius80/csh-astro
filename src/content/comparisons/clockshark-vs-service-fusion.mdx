# ClockShark vs Service Fusion: Time Tracking vs Field Service

ClockShark vs Service Fusion looks like a field service comparison, but these two products sit at different levels of the software stack and solve fundamentally different problems. The decision is not about which feature list is longer. It is about which operational bottleneck is costing you more money every week.

ClockShark is built for GPS-verified time tracking. It answers the question that keeps construction and field service owners up at night: did my crew actually work the hours they claimed, and were they at the right job site? Service Fusion is built for full field service management. It answers a different question: how do I run scheduling, dispatch, estimates, invoicing, payments, and customer management in one platform without paying per seat for every user?

Those are different problems, and buying the wrong tool for your bottleneck will leave you frustrated. A contractor drowning in unverified labor hours does not need a dispatch board and invoicing suite. A service company losing leads to disorganized dispatch and scattered paperwork does not need GPS geofencing. See our <a href="/clockshark-review/">ClockShark review</a> and <a href="/service-fusion-review/">Service Fusion review</a> for the full product breakdowns.

## The buying split

### ClockShark is a time tracking and labor verification tool first

ClockShark's main advantage is focus. The platform is built for one thing: GPS-verified time tracking for field crews. The geofencing that competitors like QuickBooks Time reserve for premium tiers is included in ClockShark's Standard plan at $40/month plus $9 per user. No accounting software subscription required.

For a five-person crew, ClockShark Standard costs $76/month total: $40 base plus $36 for four additional users at $9 each. The Pro plan adds job costing, PTO management, and shift wrap-up forms at $60/month plus $11 per user, which comes to $104/month for five users.

Active-user billing is a practical advantage for seasonal contractors. ClockShark bills only for users who actually clock in during a billing period and prorates charges for partial days. A roofing company that brings on extra crews for summer reroofing does not pay for them in the winter months.

The mock location detection catches GPS spoofing apps. That is a real problem for contractors who have had crew members clock in from home, and it is not something most time tracking tools address explicitly.

Where ClockShark falls short: it is not a dispatch platform, not a CRM, not an invoicing tool, and not a phone system. Scheduling is functional but lacks shift templates and flexible recurrence. No offline mode means crews in low-connectivity areas cannot clock in. The $40/month base fee is steep for 1-2 person operations. If you need invoicing, payment processing, estimates, and customer management alongside scheduling, ClockShark alone will not cover it. For a broader look at time tracking options, see our <a href="/best-time-tracking-software-contractors/">best time tracking software for contractors guide</a>.

### Service Fusion is a full field service management platform

Service Fusion needs a different test. Picture a day where the office is scheduling jobs, dispatching technicians, generating estimates, processing invoices, collecting payments, updating customer records, and pulling job reports. Service Fusion is built for all of that in one platform.

Service Fusion lists three tiers: Starter, Plus, and Pro. Starter includes customer management, estimates and jobs, scheduling and dispatching, QuickBooks integration, invoicing, payment processing, project management, reporting, text messaging alerts, and estimate options. Plus adds job photo uploads, inventory management, job costing, and integrated voice and text. Pro adds open API integration, custom documents, eSign documents, customer web portal, progress billing, and recurring invoicing.

The key pricing difference: Service Fusion does not show public dollar pricing on its pricing page. Visitors are directed to a demo and pricing consultation, so pricing should be treated as quote/demo-required. This is different from ClockShark's fully published per-user rate card. The trade-off is that Service Fusion includes unlimited users on every plan, which can be cheaper for larger teams than per-seat tools.

Where Service Fusion falls short: no published dollar pricing means buyers cannot evaluate cost without a demo. No public free trial was found. Android app feedback remains a risk - reviews are mixed on mobile quality. GPS fleet tracking, ServiceCall.ai, and several workflow tools are add-ons that can add cost. Small crews under 10 users will likely pay less with per-user tools like ClockShark, Jobber, or Housecall Pro.

## Pricing reality

### ClockShark: published and predictable

ClockShark Standard costs $40/month base plus $9 per active user. Pro costs $60/month base plus $11 per active user. The pricing is fully published on the official pricing page, and a 14-day free trial requires no credit card.

| Team size | ClockShark Standard | ClockShark Pro |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Solo (1 user) | $49/mo | $71/mo |
| 5 users | $76/mo | $104/mo |
| 10 users | $121/mo | $159/mo |
| 20 users | $211/mo | $269/mo |

Active-user billing means you only pay for users who actually clock in. Seasonal contractors who scale crews up and down throughout the year are not paying for idle accounts during the off-season.

A three-year contract term applies to all ClockShark pricing plans. That is worth knowing before signing.

### Service Fusion: demo-required with unlimited users

Service Fusion lists Starter, Plus, and Pro tiers on its pricing page, but does not show public dollar pricing. Visitors are directed to "Get Demo and Pricing" or "Talk to Sales." All plans include unlimited users, which is the main pricing advantage for larger teams.

The old Service Fusion pricing page previously showed published dollar amounts for each plan, but the current rendered page does not. Chris's ruling: treat Service Fusion pricing as quote/demo-required, not as published prices.

This creates two evaluation problems. First, you cannot compare cost-per-user without getting a quote. Second, add-ons like GPS fleet tracking, ServiceCall.ai, and custom documents can add cost on top of the base plan. Get a written quote that includes every add-on you need before comparing total cost.

### Which is cheaper?

For small crews (5 users or fewer), ClockShark is dramatically cheaper at $76/month for Standard. Service Fusion's unlimited-user model does not pay off until you have enough users that per-seat math exceeds the flat plan rate.

For larger teams (15+ users), Service Fusion's unlimited users can be more cost-effective than ClockShark's per-user model. At 20 users, ClockShark Standard costs $211/month. If Service Fusion's Starter plan comes in below that for unlimited users, it wins on cost. But without published pricing, you need a demo to find out.

The honest answer is that the cost comparison depends entirely on your team size and which tier you need. Get both quotes before deciding.

## Where ClockShark wins

### GPS time tracking depth

ClockShark's GPS layer is the core differentiator. When a worker clocks in, the system records their location with a timestamp and a map pin. Managers see a "Who's Working Now" view that shows every clocked-in employee on a live map. For a general contractor running multiple crews, that single view can replace a lot of morning phone calls.

The GPS stamp also serves as a record if a client disputes labor hours on a time-and-materials invoice. That is a practical operational benefit that full FSM platforms like Service Fusion do not replicate at the same depth. For a deeper dive, see our <a href="/clockshark-vs-handoff/">ClockShark vs Handoff comparison</a>.

### Geofencing without a premium paywall

Geofencing lets you draw a virtual radius around each job site. When a worker enters or leaves that zone, ClockShark prompts them to clock in or out automatically. This feature is included in the Standard plan at $40/month plus $9 per user.

Service Fusion lists GPS fleet tracking as an add-on, not as a geofencing feature tied to clock in/out events. For a contractor whose main reason for buying software is geofenced reminders at job sites, ClockShark reaches that feature at a lower total cost and with deeper GPS time tracking focus.

### Published pricing and transparent evaluation

ClockShark publishes full pricing on its pricing page. You can calculate your monthly cost before talking to anyone. A 14-day trial with no credit card lets you test the platform with your actual crew before committing. That is the kind of buying experience contractors prefer.

Service Fusion requires a demo to get pricing. That is not inherently bad - you get a one-on-one walkthrough and a custom quote - but it slows down comparison shopping and makes it harder to evaluate cost without committing time to a sales conversation.

### Standalone platform with broad payroll integrations

ClockShark requires no accounting software subscription. No QuickBooks Online, no ecosystem lock-in. For contractors who use ADP, Gusto, Sage, or Paychex, or who want the flexibility to switch accounting platforms later, this removes a structural cost.

ClockShark connects to QuickBooks Online, QuickBooks Desktop, ADP, Gusto, Xero, Sage 100 Contractor, Paychex, Paylocity, MYOB, and Simpro. It also supports Zapier for 1,000+ additional app connections. Service Fusion integrates with QuickBooks and includes invoicing and payment processing in Starter, but its payroll and accounting integration breadth is narrower.

### Active-user billing for seasonal crews

ClockShark bills only for active users and prorates charges for partial days. A landscaping company that scales from 5 people in winter to 20 in summer does not pay for idle accounts during the off-season. Service Fusion includes unlimited users on every plan, which is a different advantage - but unlimited-user pricing only matters if your team is large enough to justify the higher base cost.

## Where Service Fusion wins

### Full field service management in one platform

Service Fusion bundles scheduling, dispatch, estimates, invoicing, payments, customer management, project management, and reporting into one platform. Starter includes QuickBooks integration, invoicing, and payment processing. ClockShark has none of those capabilities at any tier.

If your operation needs more than time tracking - if you need to generate estimates, send invoices, process payments, manage customer records, and pull job reports - Service Fusion covers all of it. ClockShark will not.

### Unlimited users on every plan

Service Fusion includes unlimited users on every plan. That is the primary pricing advantage for larger teams. A 20-person operation does not pay ClockShark's $9 or $11 per user - everyone gets access. Adding office staff, dispatchers, managers, and accounting users does not increase the bill.

ClockShark's per-user model is cheaper for small teams but scales linearly. At 20 users, ClockShark Standard costs $211/month. If Service Fusion's flat plan pricing comes in lower, the unlimited-user model wins. This is where the demo-required pricing becomes a real evaluation step: you need the actual quote to know which is cheaper at your team size.

### Dispatch board and work order management

When jobs are added, moved, canceled, and reassigned during the day, the dispatch board matters. Service Fusion gives the office control over scheduling, dispatching, work orders, and fleet visibility. That can beat ClockShark for teams that live in the dispatch flow all day.

ClockShark has a scheduler, but it is built around shift assignments and time tracking, not the live same-day dispatch flow that service companies need when call volume drives the schedule.

### Invoicing, payments, and estimates

Service Fusion Starter includes estimates, invoicing, payment processing, and QuickBooks integration. For a service company that wants to generate estimates on site, invoice the customer, and process payment without switching tools, Service Fusion covers the full workflow.

ClockShark does not offer invoicing, payments, or estimates. If those capabilities matter to your operation, ClockShark alone will not solve them. You would need a separate invoicing or accounting tool alongside it.

### Built for HVAC, plumbing, and electrical service work

Service Fusion is especially relevant for dispatch-heavy HVAC, plumbing, and electrical service companies that run multiple jobs per day and need dispatch, estimates, invoicing, and customer management tied together. The flat-rate pricing with unlimited users fits larger service operations where adding per-seat costs for every technician, dispatcher, and office staff member would be expensive. For a broader look at similar platforms, see our <a href="/best-field-service-software/">best field service software</a> guide.

ClockShark is more naturally suited to construction, electrical, roofing, landscaping, and painting crews where the primary question is labor verification across multiple job sites rather than full service workflow management.

## When each platform is the wrong choice

<p><strong>Do not pick ClockShark</strong> if your operation mainly needs dispatch, estimates, invoicing, payment processing, CRM, work orders, or customer portals. ClockShark is a time tracking tool, not a field service management platform. It will feel limited if dispatch and invoicing are your daily operational pain.</p>

<p><strong>Do not pick Service Fusion</strong> if you only need GPS-verified time tracking for construction crews across multiple job sites, you want published pricing, or your team is small enough that per-user pricing is cheaper than a flat-rate plan. Service Fusion's demo-required pricing and lack of a self-serve trial also make it harder to evaluate quickly. Small crews under 10 users will almost always pay less with ClockShark or a simpler tool like <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber</a> or <a href="/housecall-pro-review/">Housecall Pro</a>.</p>

## Evaluation plan

### Test ClockShark for one full labor cycle

During the 14-day ClockShark trial, run one real payroll cycle with your actual crew. Have workers clock in at real job sites, verify GPS stamps match reported locations, test geofencing prompts at your typical work radius, and confirm the payroll export works with your existing platform. Also test mock location detection by attempting to clock in from an off-site location.

### Test Service Fusion with a real dispatch day

Service Fusion does not offer a self-serve trial, so the demo is your evaluation window. Before the demo, prepare a realistic day of work: how many jobs are dispatched, how many technicians are on the board, what invoicing and payment flow looks like, and what add-ons (GPS fleet tracking, ServiceCall.ai, custom documents, eSign) you need. During the demo, ask for the exact monthly price of your team size on the tier that includes your must-have features, including every add-on. Get it in writing.

## FAQ

### Is ClockShark or Service Fusion better for contractors?

Neither is universally better. ClockShark is better for construction and trade crews that need GPS-verified time tracking, geofencing, and payroll integration without paying for an accounting subscription. Service Fusion is better for dispatch-heavy HVAC, plumbing, and electrical teams with 10+ users that need scheduling, dispatch, invoicing, payments, and customer management in one platform with unlimited users. The right choice depends on which operational bottleneck costs you more money and what team size you run.

### Which is cheaper, ClockShark or Service Fusion?

For small crews, ClockShark is cheaper and has fully published pricing. ClockShark Standard costs $40/month plus $9 per user, which comes to $76/month for a five-person crew. Service Fusion pricing is custom quote and requires a demo, so a direct cost comparison is not possible without a quote. Service Fusion includes unlimited users on every plan, which can be cheaper for larger teams of 15+ users where per-seat math exceeds the flat plan rate. Get both quotes before deciding.

### Does Service Fusion have GPS time tracking?

Service Fusion lists GPS fleet tracking as an add-on, not as a core feature tied to clock in/out events. It is not comparable to ClockShark's purpose-built GPS time tracking, which records GPS-stamped clock in/out events, includes geofencing in Standard, has mock location detection for GPS spoofing, and shows a real-time crew map. If GPS time tracking and labor verification are the primary reasons for buying software, ClockShark is the deeper tool.

### Can ClockShark replace Service Fusion?

No. ClockShark does not have dispatch, estimates, invoicing, payment processing, CRM, or customer portals. It is a time tracking and scheduling tool. If your business needs full field service management, ClockShark will not replace Service Fusion. Some contractors may use both: ClockShark for GPS time tracking and Service Fusion for dispatch and invoicing, though that means paying for two platforms.

### Does ClockShark integrate with QuickBooks?

Yes. ClockShark connects to both QuickBooks Online and QuickBooks Desktop for payroll sync. It also integrates with ADP, Gusto, Xero, Sage 100 Contractor, Paychex, Paylocity, MYOB, and Simpro. You do not need to be a QuickBooks customer to use ClockShark. Service Fusion also integrates with QuickBooks and includes it in the Starter plan, alongside invoicing and payment processing.

### Does Service Fusion publish pricing?

No. Service Fusion lists Starter, Plus, and Pro tiers on its pricing page, but does not show public dollar pricing. Visitors are directed to a demo and pricing consultation. This is different from ClockShark, which publishes full per-user pricing on its pricing page. If transparent pricing is a priority, ClockShark wins that comparison by default.

### What should I test during the evaluation?

In ClockShark, test GPS accuracy at your actual job sites, verify geofencing works with your typical work radius, confirm the payroll export works with your existing platform, and attempt a mock location detection test. The 14-day trial gives you real-world testing with no credit card.

For Service Fusion, the demo is your evaluation window. Prepare a realistic dispatch day with your team size and required features. Ask for the exact monthly price including every add-on you need, and get it in writing. Test mobile workflow, especially if your team runs Android devices, and confirm the QuickBooks integration works with your setup.
