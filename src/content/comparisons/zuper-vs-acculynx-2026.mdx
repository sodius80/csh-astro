<p>Zuper's custom pricing model allows for deeper platform customization. Larger roofing companies can modify workflows, reporting, and integrations to match their specific processes. This flexibility appeals to companies with unique operational requirements.</p>

## Dealbreakers to consider

<p><strong>Zuper's pricing opacity</strong></p>
<p>Zuper requires demos for all pricing discussions. No published rates exist, and smaller roofing companies report feeling excluded from the sales process. If you need transparent pricing to make quick decisions, Zuper's enterprise approach won't work.</p>

<p><strong>AccuLynx's per-user cost scaling</strong></p>
<p>AccuLynx charges per user monthly, which scales expensive for larger teams. A 20-person roofing company pays $1,200-2,400 monthly just for user licenses. Companies with many field techs report this pricing model becoming prohibitive as they grow.</p>

<p><strong>Neither offers free trials</strong></p>
<p>Both platforms require sales demos and custom quotes before you can test the software. This makes it impossible to evaluate the platforms with your actual data and workflows before committing to implementation.</p>

<p><strong>Complex onboarding for both</strong></p>
<p>Users report weeks-long onboarding processes for both platforms. Data migration, crew training, and workflow setup require dedicated project management. Neither platform works for companies needing immediate deployment.</p>
