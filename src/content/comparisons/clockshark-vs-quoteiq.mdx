# ClockShark vs QuoteIQ: Time Tracking vs All-in-One FSM

ClockShark vs QuoteIQ looks like a field service software comparison until you ask what problem each tool actually solves. Then the decision gets cleaner.

ClockShark is built for one thing: GPS-verified time tracking for field crews. It answers the question that keeps construction owners up at night: "Did my crew actually work the hours they claimed, and were they at the right job site?" It does that job exceptionally well, with geofencing in the base plan, mock location detection, and payroll sync across a dozen platforms. Read our <a href="/clockshark-review/">ClockShark review</a> for the full breakdown.

QuoteIQ is a different animal entirely. It is an all-in-one field service platform that includes estimates, invoices, scheduling, online payments, AI call answering, satellite measurement, photo documentation, review automation, and customer CRM. It answers a different question: "Can I run my whole service business from one app without paying for five separate subscriptions?" See our <a href="/quoteiq-review/">QuoteIQ review</a> for the full assessment.

Those are different problems. A contractor bleeding money on unverified labor hours does not need AI call answering. A contractor drowning in separate tools for quoting, scheduling, invoicing, and measurement does not need GPS time stamps. The wrong tool for your bottleneck will feel wrong within the first month.

## The core difference

### ClockShark is a time tracking specialist

ClockShark does one thing and does it deeply. The entire product is built around GPS-verified time tracking: workers clock in at job sites, the system stamps their GPS location, geofencing prompts automatic clock in/out when they enter or leave a defined radius, and mock location detection flags GPS spoofing attempts. The "Who's Working Now" real-time crew map gives dispatch visibility into which crew members are clocked in and where.

That specialization matters. ClockShark does not try to be an estimating tool, an invoicing platform, or a CRM. It is a time tracking and scheduling tool that connects to payroll. For a general contractor running five crews across eight job sites who needs to verify labor hours against T&M billing, that focus is the entire point.

The closest <a href="/clockshark-vs-zuper/">ClockShark vs Zuper comparison</a> shows the same pattern: ClockShark wins when the problem is labor verification, loses when the problem is broader operations management. The same logic applies here.

### QuoteIQ is an all-in-one field service platform

QuoteIQ covers the full service loop: create an estimate, schedule the job, dispatch the crew, document the work with photos, invoice the customer, collect payment, and request a review. It layers AI tools on top of every step: AI Autopilot for voice-activated CRM commands, AI Estimator for generating quotes from property photos, Virtual Call Team for 24/7 call answering, and MapMeasure Pro for satellite-based property measurement.

That breadth is the point. A pressure washing company that currently pays for CompanyCam ($30/mo), ResponsiBid ($179/mo), a review tool, a scheduling tool, and a phone answering service can collapse all of that into one QuoteIQ subscription. The tradeoff is that QuoteIQ's time tracking is not GPS-verified at the level ClockShark provides. The EmployeeHub feature on Elite and Max plans includes GEO location tracking and Time Tracker Pro, but it lacks geofencing and mock location detection.

### The cost crossover

The pricing models are fundamentally different and favor different team sizes.

ClockShark is cheaper for small crews that only need time tracking. A 5-person crew on ClockShark Standard pays $76/month. QuoteIQ Pro (which includes 4 users) costs $149.99/month but covers estimates, invoicing, scheduling, payments, QuickBooks, and AI tools. If you need all of those features, QuoteIQ saves you from buying 4-5 separate subscriptions.

QuoteIQ is cheaper per user for larger teams because it has no per-user fees. A 10-person team on ClockShark Standard pays $121/month for time tracking only. The same team on QuoteIQ Elite pays $299/month but gets 10 included users, 5,000 AI credits, EmployeeHub with GPS location, routing, dispatching, inventory, and job costing. For a contractor who needs that operational breadth, $299 for 10 users is competitive. For a contractor who only needs time tracking, $121 beats $299.

The wrong question is "which is cheaper." The right question is "which problem costs me more money."

## When ClockShark makes sense

ClockShark is the right pick when the daily mess is knowing where your people are and what they actually worked on. For a general contractor running multiple crews across different job sites, ClockShark makes payroll verification automatic instead of a Friday-night headache.

The geofencing advantage is concrete. ClockShark includes geofencing in its Standard plan at $40/month plus $9 per user. Competitors like <a href="/quickbooks-time-vs-clockshark/">QuickBooks Time</a> reserve geofencing for their Elite tier plus a required QuickBooks Online subscription. For a contractor whose main reason for buying time tracking is geofenced reminders at job sites, ClockShark reaches that feature at a lower total cost.

Active-user billing matters for seasonal contractors. A roofing company that scales from 5 people in winter to 20 in summer does not pay for idle accounts during the off-season. ClockShark bills only for users who clock in, and prorates partial days.

Mock location detection is a real differentiator. GPS spoofing apps are easy to install and hard to catch without software that specifically looks for them. ClockShark flags devices that appear to be faking their location, which matters for operations where GPS trust is an ongoing issue. If you want to see how ClockShark compares against other time-tracking focused tools, the <a href="/clockshark-vs-service-fusion/">ClockShark vs Service Fusion comparison</a> covers the FSM angle.

Where ClockShark falls short for this comparison: it has no estimating, no invoicing, no payments, no AI tools, no measurement, and no CRM. If your business needs any of those, ClockShark alone will not cover the workflow. You would need to pair it with another tool, which means two subscriptions.

## When QuoteIQ makes sense

QuoteIQ is the right pick when the business needs one app for the full service loop and the owner is tired of managing 4-5 separate subscriptions that each cover one piece of the workflow.

For a 5-person pressure washing or fencing company, QuoteIQ Pro at $149.99/month covers 4 users with estimating, scheduling, invoicing, online payments, QuickBooks sync, AI credits, MapMeasure Pro for satellite measurement, QuoteIQ Cam for photo documentation, review automation, and e-signatures. Replacing those capabilities with separate subscriptions on other platforms could cost significantly more.

The AI tools are the most distinctive part of QuoteIQ's pitch. AI Autopilot gives 35+ natural-language CRM commands. The AI Estimator generates quotes from property photos and scope descriptions. The Virtual Call Team handles inbound calls 24/7 with AI, books appointments, and routes complex calls to the office. For a home-service contractor who misses leads because nobody answers the phone after hours, that feature alone can change the business. MapMeasure Pro on the Beginner plan gives satellite-based property measurement for fence lines, roof areas, and surface areas, replacing a separate ResponsiBid subscription. For broader category context in field service AI, see our <a href="/best-pressure-washing-software/">best pressure washing software roundup</a>.

Where QuoteIQ falls short for this comparison: its time tracking is not in the same league as ClockShark's. The EmployeeHub feature on Elite and Max plans includes GEO location tracking and Time Tracker Pro, but there is no geofencing, no mock location detection, and no GPS-stamped clock in/out at the job site level. For a contractor whose primary problem is unverified labor hours, QuoteIQ's time tracking is not purpose-built for that job. Also, QuoteIQ's payroll integration is limited to QuickBooks (Pro and above). If your payroll runs through ADP, Gusto, Xero, Sage, or Paychex, QuoteIQ cannot sync with those platforms directly.

## Wrong-fit signals

Do not choose ClockShark if the company needs estimates, invoices, scheduling, payments, customer management, or AI tools. ClockShark is a time tracking and scheduling specialist. If the business needs an all-in-one platform, ClockShark will leave too many gaps. A contractor who needs quoting, invoicing, and payment collection should look at <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber</a> or <a href="/housecall-pro-review/">Housecall Pro</a> instead, alongside QuoteIQ.

Do not choose QuoteIQ if the business's primary pain is unverified labor hours. QuoteIQ's EmployeeHub is a general workforce feature, not a purpose-built GPS time tracking system. If you need geofencing at the base plan, mock location detection, GPS-stamped clock in/out per job site, and real-time crew maps, ClockShark is the deeper tool. If the payroll platform is not QuickBooks, QuoteIQ cannot sync with it.

Do not choose either product because the AI or GPS language sounds impressive. Put the same real job into each system during the trial. The better choice is the one that makes your bottleneck easier to handle, not the one with the longer feature list.

## Evaluation plan

### Step 1: name the bottleneck

Write one sentence before starting a trial: "Our biggest problem is _____." If the blank is unverified labor hours, GPS spoofing, payroll verification across job sites, or knowing who was where and when, start with ClockShark. If the blank is scattered tools for quoting, scheduling, invoicing, payments, measurement, and customer communication, start with QuoteIQ.

### Step 2: test ClockShark with real job sites

Use the 14-day trial with no credit card. Have crew members clock in at actual job sites. Test GPS accuracy on the phones your crews actually use. Set up geofencing around a job site and verify it prompts clock in/out. Run the payroll export to your existing platform. Test mock location detection by attempting to spoof GPS. Check the "Who's Working Now" crew map during a real workday. Read the <a href="/clockshark-review/">full ClockShark review</a> for deeper context on what to test.

ClockShark passes if payroll verification becomes automatic and you can trust the timesheet data without manual spot-checks.

### Step 3: test QuoteIQ with a real service loop

Use the 14-day trial with a real job from start to finish. Create a customer, build an estimate using the AI Estimator, run MapMeasure Pro on a property you know, schedule the job, document it with QuoteIQ Cam, send the invoice, collect a test payment, and set up Review Multiplier. Test AI Autopilot with real customer language. Make a test call to the Virtual Call Team. Track how many AI credits the workflow consumes.

QuoteIQ passes if the job moves through the platform without needing separate tools for normal work.

### Step 4: compare by cost per solved problem

Do not compare $76 against $29.99 in isolation. Compare cost against the problem solved.

ClockShark can be the better buy for a construction crew where unverified labor hours are costing $2,000+ per month in inflated payroll or disputed T&M billing. QuoteIQ can be the better buy for a home-service contractor who is paying $400+ across five separate subscriptions that QuoteIQ could replace for $149.99.

## FAQ

### Is ClockShark or QuoteIQ better for contractors?

Neither is universally better. ClockShark is better for construction and trade crews of 5-100 users that need GPS-verified time tracking, geofencing, mock location detection, and payroll sync with published per-user pricing. QuoteIQ is better for home-service contractors of 1-50 users that want estimates, invoices, scheduling, payments, AI tools, satellite measurement, and customer management in one flat-rate platform. The right choice depends on which operational bottleneck costs you more money.

### Is ClockShark cheaper than QuoteIQ?

It depends on team size and what you need. ClockShark Standard costs $76/month for a 5-person crew. QuoteIQ Essentials costs $29.99/month for 1 user, but the Pro plan that covers 4 users costs $149.99/month. For pure time tracking, ClockShark is significantly more cost-effective. For all-in-one field service management, QuoteIQ's flat-rate pricing can be cheaper than buying 4-5 separate subscriptions, especially for teams of 4+ where the included user count covers the whole crew.

### Does QuoteIQ have GPS time tracking?

QuoteIQ's EmployeeHub feature on Elite and Max plans includes GEO location tracking and Time Tracker Pro, but it is not a purpose-built GPS time tracking tool like ClockShark. There is no geofencing, no mock location detection, and no GPS-stamped clock in/out per job site with a real-time crew map. If GPS-verified labor hours are the main reason for buying software, ClockShark is the deeper tool.

### Does ClockShark have estimating or invoicing?

No. ClockShark is a time tracking and scheduling tool. It does not include estimating, proposal generation, invoicing, payment processing, or CRM. If you need those features, QuoteIQ is the better choice, or you would need to pair ClockShark with a separate estimating and invoicing tool like <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber</a> or <a href="/housecall-pro-review/">Housecall Pro</a>.

### Which product has better payroll integrations?

ClockShark has broader payroll integrations: QuickBooks Online and Desktop, ADP, Gusto, Xero, Sage 100 Contractor, Paychex, Paylocity, MYOB, and Simpro. QuoteIQ integrates with QuickBooks on the Pro plan and above but does not support ADP, Gusto, Xero, Sage, or Paychex. If your payroll runs through any platform other than QuickBooks, ClockShark is the better fit for payroll sync.

### Can I use ClockShark and QuoteIQ together?

Yes. They solve different problems and can complement each other. ClockShark handles GPS time tracking and payroll verification. QuoteIQ handles estimating, invoicing, scheduling, payments, measurement, and customer communication. For a contractor who needs both operational visibility on labor hours and a full service-loop platform, using both may make sense. The cost would be two subscriptions, so model whether the combined price is justified by the problems solved.

### What should I test during the free trial?

In ClockShark, test GPS accuracy at your actual job sites, verify geofencing works with your typical work radius, confirm the payroll export works with your existing platform, and attempt a mock location detection test. The 14-day trial with no credit card gives you real-world testing without risk.

In QuoteIQ, use the 14-day trial with a real job from start to finish: create a customer, build an estimate with the AI Estimator, run MapMeasure Pro on a property, schedule the job, document it with QuoteIQ Cam, send the invoice, collect a test payment, and test the AI Autopilot and Virtual Call Team features. Track how many AI credits the workflow consumes to understand which tier you actually need.
