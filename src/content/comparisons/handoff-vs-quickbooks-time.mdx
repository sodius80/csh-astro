# Handoff vs QuickBooks Time for Contractors

Handoff vs QuickBooks Time is a comparison between two different bottlenecks. Handoff is for the contractor who can't turn a site visit into a clean estimate and proposal fast enough. QuickBooks Time is for the contractor who can't get accurate labor hours into QuickBooks without chasing timesheets.

That difference matters more than the feature checklist. Handoff lives before the job is won: estimating, scopes, proposal presentation, takeoffs, client approvals, and project documents. QuickBooks Time lives around the work record: clock-ins, schedules, time off, time kiosk, payroll and invoicing sync, mileage, and project time.

If you're comparing these because both touch QuickBooks, start by naming the broken workflow. If the estimate is slow, Handoff is the better test. If the timecard is unreliable, QuickBooks Time is the better test.

## The real decision

### Handoff is an estimating and proposal decision

Handoff makes sense when the business loses money before the job starts. That can mean slow estimate turnaround, inconsistent scopes, weak proposal presentation, owner-only estimating knowledge, or a process where a good lead waits days for a bid.

The official Handoff pricing page centers on AI estimating, AI proposals, voice transcription to estimate, AI-powered scopes of work, material lists, client portals, invoices and payments, QuickBooks Online sync, and AI takeoffs on the Scale plan. Flex includes 50 AI usage credits per month and two users. Pro adds unlimited AI usage credits, unlimited custom pricing catalogs, client financing, AI site walkthroughs, five users, and success-coach onboarding. Scale adds AI takeoffs from residential plans up to 5,000 square feet.

That's not a time tracking product. It's a sales and pre-production product. Handoff is useful when the owner, estimator, or sales rep needs a faster path from walkthrough to client-ready proposal.

### QuickBooks Time is a time tracking decision

QuickBooks Time makes sense when QuickBooks Online is already the accounting backbone and labor records are the pain. The official QuickBooks Time pricing page states that a QuickBooks Online account is required. That's not a footnote. It's the center of the buying decision.

Time Premium starts at $20 per month as the base fee plus $10 per user per month. Time Elite starts at $40 per month as the base fee plus $12 per user per month. The page also advertises a 50% first-three-month promotion, but evergreen buying math should use standard rates because promotions expire.

The feature set is different from Handoff. QuickBooks Time is about the workforce app, payroll and invoicing sync, time off management, alerts, photo attachments to timesheets, on-site time kiosk, mileage on higher tiers, project estimates versus actuals, project activity feed, and timesheet signatures. That's the labor record. It isn't the bid.

## Pricing reality

### Handoff pricing

Atlas has Handoff verified from the official pricing page. Flex is $119 per month when billed annually, or $149 month-to-month. Pro is $239 per month when billed annually, or $299 month-to-month. Scale is $719 per month when billed annually, or $899 month-to-month. Enterprise is custom quote.

The price matters less than the plan limits. Flex includes a 7-day free trial, 50 AI credits per month, one custom pricing catalog, AI-generated proposals, voice transcription to estimate, mobile and desktop access, and two users. Pro is the more realistic planning number for a busy estimator because it adds unlimited AI usage credits, unlimited custom pricing catalogs, and five users.

Scale is not a normal upgrade for every small contractor. It is specifically where residential plan takeoffs enter the workflow. If you don't need AI takeoffs from residential plans, compare Flex and Pro first.

### QuickBooks Time pricing

Atlas has QuickBooks Time verified from the official pricing page. Time Premium is $20 per month as the base fee plus $10 per user per month. Time Elite is $40 per month as the base fee plus $12 per user per month. A QuickBooks Online subscription is required to use QuickBooks Time.

The QuickBooks dependency is the pricing story. If the business already pays for QuickBooks Online, QuickBooks Time can be a focused add-on for payroll and labor records. If the business doesn't use QuickBooks Online, the time tracking decision also becomes an accounting platform decision.

Don't compare only the first line of each price table. Handoff Flex includes two users, but it is solving estimating and proposals. QuickBooks Time charges around a base fee plus users, but it is solving timesheets and payroll flow. The cheaper number is not the better number if it solves the wrong problem.

## Where Handoff wins

### Estimate speed and proposal quality

Handoff wins when the owner needs to turn notes, photos, voice input, or plan details into an estimate and proposal quickly. The official pricing page frames Handoff around AI estimating, AI proposals, AI-powered scopes of work, AI renderings, material lists, client portals, and approvals.

That is valuable for residential contractors because the proposal experience can change the sale. Homeowners aren't only comparing line items. They are comparing response time, scope clarity, confidence, and whether the contractor looks organized enough to trust.

### Better fit for residential sales workflow

QuickBooks Time can help track labor after work happens. It does not help a remodeler send a stronger proposal before the homeowner chooses a competitor. Handoff is better for residential trades where the estimate is a sales document, not just an internal number.

This makes Handoff a stronger test for remodelers, roofers, handymen, painters, deck builders, kitchen and bath contractors, and other businesses where the bid package affects close rate.

### Client approvals and project documents

Handoff also stretches beyond the estimate into client approvals, portals, invoices, payments, digital signatures, AI daily logs, AI scheduling, and budget tracking. Those features do not make it a full field service dispatch system, but they do make it broader than a pure estimate calculator.

If the goal is a cleaner handoff from accepted proposal to project paperwork, Handoff is closer to the workflow than QuickBooks Time.

## Where Handoff falls short

### It is not a payroll time clock

Handoff should not be used as a substitute for QuickBooks Time. It isn't built around time kiosk, payroll and invoicing sync, time off, mileage, project activity feed, or timesheet signatures. If the office needs reliable clock-ins and payroll records, Handoff doesn't solve enough of the problem.

### AI output still needs control

AI-assisted estimating does not remove the need for clean pricing catalogs, markup rules, exclusions, and review. A bad catalog can still create a bad proposal. A fast quote is only useful if the scope and margin are correct.

The buyer should test Handoff with real jobs, not demo scenarios. Build a proposal from a recent lead, check the scope, check the numbers, and decide whether the output is good enough to send with normal review.

### Plan limits shape the experience

Flex is the cheapest entry, but the 50-credit limit and two-user cap matter. Pro removes the AI credit ceiling and raises the included-user count to five. Scale adds takeoff capacity but raises the budget sharply.

If the owner expects Handoff to become the central estimating workflow, the Pro plan may be the honest planning number even though Flex is the entry price.

## Where QuickBooks Time wins

### Native QuickBooks labor flow

QuickBooks Time wins when QuickBooks Online is already the source of truth. Labor hours can support payroll, invoicing, and project tracking without moving data through a separate estimating or field service system.

That is especially useful for small contractors who trust QuickBooks, have a bookkeeper already working there, and need better time records without adopting a full operating platform.

### Time kiosk, time off, and basic workforce controls

QuickBooks Time is stronger for workforce controls. The official pricing page lists a workforce app, payroll and invoicing sync, time off management, custom alerts, photo attachments to timesheets, on-site time kiosk, and support in the time tracking plans.

Time Elite adds the field and project controls that matter to many contractors: mileage tracking, project deadlines, project estimates versus actuals, project activity feed, and timesheet signatures. Those are not Handoff features.

### Smaller adoption burden than a sales platform

A time tracking rollout still takes work, but it is more contained than changing the estimating and proposal process. The office needs users, projects or jobs, pay rules, schedules, approvals, and mobile expectations.

If the current sales process is fine and only payroll week is painful, QuickBooks Time is a narrower and cleaner rollout than Handoff.

## Where QuickBooks Time falls short

### QuickBooks Online is required

The required QuickBooks Online account is the first limitation. If the company isn't already on QuickBooks Online, QuickBooks Time forces an accounting decision before a time tracking decision.

That can be fine for a company already planning to move to QuickBooks Online. It is a poor fit for a contractor that uses QuickBooks Desktop, Xero, Sage, a payroll provider, or a field service system as the center of operations.

### It does not create better bids

QuickBooks Time won't solve slow estimates, weak scopes, generic proposals, or poor homeowner presentation. It can show labor records and support payroll flow, but it can't turn a walkthrough into a polished client-ready proposal.

If the owner says, "We are losing jobs because our quotes go out late," QuickBooks Time is not the first tool to test. Handoff is.

### It is not full field service software

QuickBooks Time should not be mistaken for a dispatch or field service operating system either. It can support scheduling and time visibility, but it is not the same as customer management, dispatch, work orders, invoices, payments, and field tech workflow in a product built around service operations.

If the problem is broader than timesheets, compare field service platforms before choosing QuickBooks Time as the workaround.

## Evaluation plan

### Test Handoff with a real estimate

Don't test Handoff with a fake project. Use a recent job where estimate speed, scope clarity, or proposal presentation mattered. Add the actual notes, photos, pricing assumptions, and scope details. Build the estimate, review the material list, generate the proposal, and decide whether it could go to a homeowner after normal review.

The pass-fail question is simple: did Handoff get a better proposal out faster without weakening margin control?

### Test QuickBooks Time through payroll week

Don't test QuickBooks Time with a few sample punches. Set up the actual workers, schedules, approval flow, project names, and pay rules. Have the team clock in from the devices they actually carry. Run the approval process and see whether payroll or invoicing cleanup gets easier.

The pass-fail question is different: did QuickBooks Time reduce timecard chasing and payroll cleanup inside QuickBooks Online?

## Final verdict

Choose Handoff if the business wins or loses money at the estimate stage. It is the stronger choice for residential contractors that need faster estimates, cleaner scopes, stronger proposal presentation, client approvals, AI takeoffs, and a smoother path from lead to signed job. The planning question is whether Flex is enough, or whether Pro is the realistic tier because unlimited AI usage credits matter.

Choose QuickBooks Time if the business already runs on QuickBooks Online and the problem is labor time. It is the stronger choice for companies that need time tracking, time kiosk, time off, payroll and invoicing sync, mileage, project time, and timesheet signatures without adopting a new estimating platform.

The cleanest rule: buy Handoff when the missing record is the estimate. Buy QuickBooks Time when the missing record is the timecard.
