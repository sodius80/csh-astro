Most field service software comparisons get lost in feature checklists. Jobber has this; Service Fusion has that. Both do scheduling. Both invoice. Both have mobile apps. But the practical reason contractors end up in one versus the other almost always comes down to one thing: how they charge.

Jobber charges per user and stacks fees as you add features. Service Fusion charges a flat monthly rate for unlimited users, regardless of whether you have four techs or forty. The "cheaper" option flips somewhere around 8–12 users, depending on what add-ons each side requires. If you're trying to figure out which one costs less for your team size, this comparison does the math in three real-world scenarios.

## The pricing philosophy clash

Jobber is built on a per-user model. You start with a base plan (Core, Connect, Grow, or Plus), and the cost grows as your team does. Add a sixth tech past the Connect Team plan's included five, and you're paying $29 extra per month. Add a few marketing add-ons and an AI receptionist on top of Grow, and your bill can look very different from the headline price.

Service Fusion flips that model. Three plans — Starter, Plus, Pro — all priced as a flat monthly fee with unlimited users. It doesn't matter if you have one dispatcher or twenty techs, the base charge stays the same. The catch: that flat rate starts at $245/month. For a solo operator or a two-person team, that's a lot compared to Jobber's $39/month Core.

The decision isn't about which software is "better." It's about which pricing structure fits your team size and growth stage.

## When this software makes sense

**Jobber** makes sense when you're running a tight team and losing money to admin gaps, not headcount. Estimates go out and nobody follows up. Invoices sit unpaid for two weeks because the reminder never got sent. A tech shows up without knowing job details because the notes are in a group chat. Jobber closes those gaps — automated follow-ups, client portal, payment reminders, schedule dispatch — and the pricing stays reasonable when your team is small.

**Service Fusion** makes sense when you've grown past the point where per-user pricing rewards you. At 12 or 15 technicians, paying per seat on Jobber adds up fast. Service Fusion's dispatch board is also built differently — it's designed for larger fleets with skills matching, priority queuing, and route coordination across multiple crews simultaneously.

## When it doesn't make sense yet

Skip both if you're handling fewer than 5–8 jobs a week and admin load isn't genuinely painful. Software adds configuration overhead before it returns value — you need to set up service items, customer records, payment integrations, and accounting sync before the first invoice goes out cleanly.

Service Fusion specifically: don't pay $245/month flat rate if you have two or three techs. The math simply doesn't work at small scale compared to Jobber or Housecall Pro. Service Fusion's pricing model assumes a team large enough to benefit from unlimited-user pricing.

Jobber specifically: if you need GPS fleet tracking on 10+ vehicles, Jobber's basic routing won't cut it. Service Fusion's Pro plan has GPS fleet tracking built in. If that's a requirement and you're running a mid-size fleet, Service Fusion starts looking more compelling even before you run the per-user math.

Pick <a href="/go/jobber-official-site/" rel="nofollow sponsored">Jobber</a> if your team is under 8–10 people. Start with Core at $39/month if you're solo. Move to Connect Team at $169/month for up to 5 users if you need QuickBooks sync and automated reminders. ## Jobber pricing: what you actually pay

Jobber publishes every price on its website — no sales call required. That transparency is worth something on its own.

**Individual plans (1 user):**
- **Core** — $39/month or $29/month annual. Scheduling, quoting, invoicing, online booking, payments.
- **Connect** — $119/month or $89/month annual. Adds QuickBooks sync, automated reminders, automatic payments, job checklists, and quote/invoice follow-ups.
- **Grow** — $199/month or $149/month annual. Adds job costing, two-way SMS, advanced quote customization, and custom workflow automations.

**Team plans:**
- **Connect Team** (5 users) — $169/month or $129/month annual.
- **Grow Team** (10 users) — $349/month or $249/month annual.
- **Plus** (15 users) — $599/month or $449/month annual — includes Marketing Suite, AI Receptionist, and Reviews add-ons.

Extra users beyond plan limits: $29/user/month. Payment processing: 2.9% + $0.30 per credit card transaction; 1% ACH.

**Add-ons that inflate the real cost:**
- AI Receptionist: $99/month (included in Plus only)
- Marketing Suite: $79/month (included in Plus only)
- Reviews: $39/month
- Campaigns: $29/month
- Referrals: $29/month

A Grow Team plan plus AI Receptionist and Marketing Suite comes to $527/month monthly for 10 users — before payment processing. The headline prices are real, but the add-on costs are where Jobber's bill grows in practice. If you need job costing and team communication tools, run your real total before comparing to Service Fusion.

The job costing lock is the biggest structural complaint. You can't get it until the Grow tier ($199+ individual, $349+ team). For a 5-person plumbing company that needs to know if jobs are profitable, that means a minimum of $169/month just to access it.

## Real-world cost: three team sizes compared

This is the comparison that actually matters.

**Three-person team (owner + 2 techs):**
- Jobber Connect Team: $169/month (5 users, QuickBooks sync, reminders)
- Service Fusion Starter: $245/month (unlimited users, base features)
- **Verdict: Jobber saves $76/month minimum.** At this size, Service Fusion's flat rate is overkill. Jobber Connect gives you QuickBooks, automated customer communication, and job management for less.

**Eight-person team (2 office staff + 6 field techs):**
- Jobber Grow Team: $349/month (10 users, job costing, two-way SMS)
- Service Fusion Starter: $245/month + typical add-ons ~$130/month = $375/month
- **Verdict: Close — Jobber slightly cheaper on paper, but depends on add-ons.** If you only need Service Fusion's base features and your team fits on Starter, Service Fusion pulls ahead. Once you add GPS and VoIP on Service Fusion, or Marketing Suite on Jobber, the gap narrows to nearly nothing. Evaluate based on which add-ons you'll actually use.

**Fifteen-plus tech operation:**
- Jobber: Grow Team ($349/mo) + 5 extra users at $29/user = $494/month — and that's before any add-ons. Plus plan at $599/month for 15 included.
- Service Fusion Pro: $627/month — unlimited users regardless of headcount.
- **Verdict: Service Fusion wins at scale.** The flat rate stops growing when you add people. At 20 techs on Jobber's Plus plan, you'd need extra-user add-ons or a custom quote. Service Fusion charges the same $627 whether you have 15 or 30 technicians.

The crossover point is somewhere between 8 and 12 users, shifting based on which add-ons each side requires. Run your actual numbers before deciding.

## Jobber: where it stands out and where it falls short

**What stands out:** Jobber's real advantage for small teams is the combination of transparent pricing, a functional free trial, and an offline mobile app that actually works. The iOS app's 4.9/5 rating across 10,000+ reviews isn't marketing — it's the clearest signal that field crews can use this tool without training and without signal. For a cleaning company, landscaping crew, or pest control operation where techs are constantly moving through dead zones, that offline functionality prevents the kinds of job-day problems that software is supposed to solve.

The customer-facing side of Jobber is also better than most competitors at this price range. Online booking, quote approval via the client portal, automated payment reminders, and two-way SMS communication on Grow and above. A 5-person plumbing company that used to chase unsigned estimates for 12 days can automate those follow-ups on Connect.

**Where it falls short:** Job costing is locked behind the Grow tier. For any operation that needs to know whether jobs are profitable — which is basically every contractor — that means paying $199/month solo or $349/month for a 10-person team. You can't get there on Core or Connect.

The add-on model stacks costs quickly. Marketing Suite ($79/mo), AI Receptionist ($99/mo), Reviews ($39/mo) — these aren't optional for a lot of growing operations. A Grow Team plan with the two main add-ons hits $527/month for 10 users. That's not far from Service Fusion territory once you factor in the unlimited-user headroom.

Routing is basic. If you need GPS fleet tracking across multiple vehicles with sophisticated route optimization, Jobber doesn't go there. You'd need a separate tool or a move to Service Fusion Pro.

**Best for:** Teams of 1–10 people in home services — HVAC, plumbing, landscaping, cleaning, pest control, handyman — who want to start quickly, evaluate on a trial, and pay proportionally to team size.

## Service Fusion: where it stands out and where it falls short

**What stands out:** The unlimited-user flat rate is the headline, and for bigger operations it's genuinely valuable. A plumbing company adding a fifth truck doesn't trigger a bill increase. An HVAC company onboarding two new office coordinators doesn't pay $58/month more. When your team is large enough to benefit from that model, it simplifies budgeting in a way per-user software doesn't.

The dispatch board is more sophisticated than Jobber's. Priority queuing, skills matching, and multi-crew coordination across a larger fleet is where Service Fusion earns its cost. For an electrical contractor running 12 techs across a metro area, a structured dispatch board reduces the dispatcher's daily triage work in ways Jobber's simpler calendar can't match.

QuickBooks Desktop integration is also worth noting specifically. Jobber syncs with QuickBooks Online and Xero but not Desktop. For contractors who've been on QuickBooks Desktop for years and aren't ready to migrate, Service Fusion's integration matters.

**Where it falls short:** No offline mode is a hard limitation for field service software in 2026. Techs working in basements, rural properties, or any dead zone lose access to job details, can't view estimates, and can't collect signatures or payments until signal returns. Multiple reviewers call this out as the platform's biggest functional gap. For a home service operation where technicians regularly work in spaces without signal, this is a disqualifier — test it on a demo route before signing up.

The Android app rating of 2.8/5 is a real problem for teams running mixed-device environments. If any of your techs are on Android, the app experience is meaningfully worse than what iOS users get, and worse than competitors like Jobber or Housecall Pro on the same platform.

No free trial means no way to evaluate the software before committing to a demo and sales conversation. For contractors shopping software on their own time — which is how most small business owners do it — that friction is real.

Some reviewers note Service Fusion hasn't kept pace with the modern FSM market. The platform does what it does well, but the UX in several areas feels dated compared to tools built more recently.

**Best for:** HVAC, electrical, and plumbing companies with 10+ technicians that need flat-rate unlimited-user pricing, structured dispatch, and GPS fleet tracking in one platform.

## Integrations

**Jobber** connects with QuickBooks Online and Desktop (two-way sync), Stripe, Square, Google Maps/Waze, Gmail, Outlook 365, and Zapier for custom automations. Zapier fills most integration gaps Jobber doesn't natively cover. The main gap is QuickBooks sync: Jobber pushes data in but doesn't pull changes back — if your accountant edits invoices in QuickBooks, those changes don't appear in Jobber.

**Service Fusion** centers its ecosystem on QuickBooks (most-cited integration), GPS fleet tracking providers, VoIP/call tracking, and built-in payment processing. The third-party app marketplace is thinner than Jobber's. If you need Zapier or flexible API connections to custom workflows, Jobber has more options.

## User sentiment

**Jobber** has the larger, more consistent review record. Common praise themes: ease of use, the offline mobile app, quote follow-up automation, and QuickBooks sync. Common complaints: add-on costs add up, per-user pricing gets expensive past 10 employees, and job costing is gated behind Grow. The iOS app's 4.9/5 rating across 10,000+ reviews is the strongest single data point — when field crews like the app, adoption is easier.

**Service Fusion** draws consistent praise for its dispatch board, QuickBooks integration, and unlimited-user pricing from shops that have grown past the per-seat model. Common complaints: no offline mode shows up repeatedly, the Android app is called out frequently, add-on costs surprise buyers, and multiple reviewers describe the platform as feeling dated compared to 2026 competitors. The platform's G2 rating (4.0–4.3/5) is respectable but sits below Jobber's across review platforms.

The per-user pricing model works in your favor at small scale.

Book the demo with your actual team size and get a complete cost breakdown — base plan plus any GPS, VoIP, and marketing tools you'd actually use — before comparing to Jobber's Grow Team total.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Jobber or Service Fusion better for a 3-person team?**

Jobber. A 3-person team on Jobber Connect Team pays $169/month and gets QuickBooks sync, automated customer communication, scheduling, quoting, invoicing, and payments. Service Fusion Starter costs $245/month minimum — and that's before add-ons. At that team size, Service Fusion's unlimited-user pricing doesn't save you anything; it just costs more.

**At what team size does Service Fusion become cheaper than Jobber?**

Roughly 8–12 users, depending on which add-ons each platform requires. A 12-tech team on Jobber Grow (10 users) would pay $349/month base plus $58/month in extra-user fees — $407/month before any add-ons. Service Fusion Starter at $245/month covers those 12 users without per-seat fees, though add-ons will push the real total higher. Run your actual numbers with both add-on lists to find your specific crossover.

**Does Service Fusion have a free trial?**

No. Service Fusion offers a demo only — you'll need to schedule a call with their sales team before seeing the product in action. This is a meaningful difference from Jobber's 14-day no-credit-card trial. If you need to evaluate field service software independently before talking to anyone, Jobber is the path.

**Does Jobber work offline?**

Yes — and this is one of Jobber's clearest differentiators over Service Fusion. Jobber's iOS app offers full offline functionality: job details, estimates, forms, and payment collection all work without signal and sync when connectivity returns. Service Fusion has no offline mode. Techs without signal can't access job information.

**What is Service Fusion's Android app rating?**

2.8/5 as of the latest data from Scout's research. This is well below Jobber's 4.6/5 Android rating and below most comparable FSM platforms. If your team runs Android devices, this is a real problem — check current reviews and test the app during any demo before committing.

**Can Jobber handle GPS fleet tracking?**

Basic routing only. Jobber integrates with Google Maps and Waze for navigation but doesn't offer advanced GPS fleet tracking across multiple vehicles. Service Fusion's Pro plan includes GPS fleet tracking as a core feature. If managing a fleet of 5+ vehicles with real-time location tracking is a requirement, Service Fusion Pro has a cleaner answer.

**Does Jobber have job costing?**

Yes, but only on the Grow tier and above. That means $199/month for a single user or $349/month for up to 10 users on the team plan. Core and Connect do not include job costing. Service Fusion adds job costing at the Plus tier ($382/month). If job costing is the reason you're looking at software in the first place, factor that tier requirement into the cost comparison.

**What integrations does Service Fusion have compared to Jobber?**

Jobber has the broader ecosystem: QuickBooks Online and Desktop, Stripe, Square, Zapier, Google Maps, Gmail, Outlook 365, and a 70+ app marketplace. Service Fusion centers on QuickBooks Online, GPS providers, VoIP/call tracking, and payment processing, with a thinner third-party marketplace. For contractors who need flexible custom integrations via Zapier or want a wider app selection, Jobber is stronger.
