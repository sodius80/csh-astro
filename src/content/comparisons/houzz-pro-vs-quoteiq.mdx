# Houzz Pro vs QuoteIQ for Contractors

Houzz Pro vs QuoteIQ is a comparison between two platforms that both mention AI but use it for completely different contractor workflows.

Houzz Pro is a design-first platform built around 3D floor planning, visual proposals, client portals, selections boards, and visibility in the Houzz marketplace. It serves roughly 70 million homeowners each month who are researching renovation projects. The software works when the contractor's bottleneck is selling through presentation - showing a homeowner what the finished space will look like before demolition starts.

QuoteIQ is a field-service-first platform with published pricing from $29.99/month to $699/month. Its official pricing page lists estimates, invoices, scheduling, online payments, MapMeasure Pro satellite measurement, QuoteIQ Cam, AI Estimator, AI Virtual Call Team, review tools, QuickBooks, routing, and monthly AI credits by plan. It works when the contractor's bottleneck is running daily quoting, invoicing, and field-service workflow with AI tools built in. ## The fundamental difference: design-sale vs AI-first field service

Houzz Pro is built around the idea that contractors who can show clients what a finished project looks like close more jobs. The 3D floor planner, mood boards, selections boards, AR walkthroughs, and the Houzz marketplace profile all serve that idea. The platform earns its cost when the sales process includes a presentation phase where the homeowner needs to see and approve before signing. If your business sells through visualization, that is where the value lives.

QuoteIQ is built around the idea that home-service contractors need quoting, scheduling, invoicing, and payment collection to happen fast, with AI tools layered in to reduce manual work. MapMeasure Pro lets a fence contractor measure linear footage from satellite imagery without a site visit. The AI Estimator generates estimates from photos. AI Virtual Call Team answers incoming calls. The platform earns its cost when daily field-service workflow runs efficiently and AI tools reduce the hours spent on manual quoting and customer communication.

This is why feature checklists do not help here. Houzz Pro has 3D design tools QuoteIQ does not need. QuoteIQ has satellite measurement, AI estimating, and published pricing Houzz Pro does not offer. They are not competing for the same workflow. They are built for different kinds of contractor businesses.

## Pricing reality and what you actually pay

Both platforms publish pricing information, but the structures are very different.

**Houzz Pro** offers a no-cost Basic tier that includes the 3D Floor Planner, estimates, and invoicing. Paid tiers are volume-based: previously published rates ranged from $99/mo annual for Essential to $1,120/mo annual for Enterprise, with additional users at $60/user/mo on Essential and Pro, $100/user/mo on Custom tiers, and included on Enterprise. As of the most recent pricing page update, Pro, Custom, and Enterprise tiers require a demo for current rates - the previously published subscription numbers are no longer visible. Advertising packages start at $499/mo and are separate from the software subscription. The 30-day Pro trial requires no credit card, but the subscription converts to annual billing after the trial ends.

**QuoteIQ** publishes five transparent plan tiers on its official pricing page: Essentials at $29.99/month (or $25/month billed annually at $299.99/year), Beginner at $74.99/month (or $62.50/month billed annually at $749.99/year), Pro at $149.99/month (or $125/month billed annually at $1,499.99/year), Elite at $299/month (or $249/month billed annually at $2,990/year), and Max at $699/month (or $582.50/month billed annually at $6,990/year). Tiers include 1, 2, 4, 10, and unlimited users respectively. All plans include a 14-day free trial, and yearly billing saves two months.

The pricing gap is real and structural. Houzz Pro's pricing is volume-based and now requires a demo for paid tiers. QuoteIQ's pricing is published, tiered, and transparent from the first visit. A fence company that wants to know exactly what software will cost before talking to sales has a clear answer with QuoteIQ. A remodeling firm that needs design presentation tools and marketplace visibility will find Houzz Pro's pricing harder to compare without a demo, but the toolset may justify the conversation.

The safer buying exercise is the same for both: write down your real user count, the features you need, and any add-ons. Then compare the monthly total against the workflow each platform is built for.

## 3D design and client presentation

This is where Houzz Pro has no real competitor in this comparison. QuoteIQ does not have a 3D floor planner, mood boards, selections boards, or AR walkthroughs. QuoteIQ's estimating is photo-based and functional, but it is not a visual presentation tool.

Houzz Pro's 3D floor planner lets a remodeler build a room layout, apply finishes and fixtures, and walk a client through an augmented reality view on a phone or tablet before any demolition starts. Contractors report that clients who were hesitant during a line-item estimate became committed after seeing a rendered version of their own space. The mood board and selections tools get client buy-in on materials during the design phase, before orders are placed. For a kitchen and bath contractor, that can reduce the mid-project change-request conversations that cost time and money. If proposals are the bottleneck but 3D design is not, compare the broader <a href="/best-proposal-software-for-contractors/">best proposal software for contractors</a> shortlist too.

If your sales process includes sitting across from a homeowner and showing them what the finished space will look like, these tools matter. If your sales process is measuring a property, generating a quote, scheduling the crew, and getting paid, QuoteIQ's photo-based AI Estimator and satellite measurement are the more relevant tools.

## AI tools and measurement

QuoteIQ has a clear advantage here. Its platform includes AI features that Houzz Pro does not offer.

MapMeasure Pro uses satellite imagery to measure properties. A fence contractor can pull linear fence footage, gate placement, and terrain data without driving to the site. This starts on the Beginner plan at $74.99/month. The AI Estimator generates quotes from photos - a contractor takes photos of the job site, and the AI produces an estimate based on visible scope. QuoteIQ Cam is a field documentation tool. AI Virtual Call Team answers incoming customer calls. Monthly AI credits scale by tier from 500 to 8,000.

Houzz Pro's AI features are built around the 3D floor planner, AR walkthroughs, and visual design tools. These are genuinely useful for presentation and client communication, but they are not estimating or field-service AI. There is no photo-based AI estimating, no satellite measurement, no AI call answering.

If AI for estimating, measurement, and call handling is the buying reason, QuoteIQ is the stronger tool. If AI for visual design and client presentation is the buying reason, Houzz Pro covers that ground.

## Scheduling and dispatch

QuoteIQ has a clearer advantage here. Its platform includes scheduling, routing, and dispatch tools designed for home-service contractors running multiple jobs per day. Route planning, recurring scheduling, and same-day changes live in the same system. If dispatch depth is the real decision, also compare <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber</a> and <a href="/service-fusion-review/">Service Fusion</a> before picking a design-first platform.

Houzz Pro has project tracking and scheduling, but it is project-based rather than dispatch-based. It tracks phases, milestones, and tasks across the lifecycle of a remodeling project. It does not have route optimization, technician dispatching, or the kind of daily scheduling depth that a service business with multiple crews needs.

If dispatch is your main bottleneck, QuoteIQ is the stronger tool. If project phase tracking is your main need, Houzz Pro covers it. If you try to use Houzz Pro for dispatch or QuoteIQ for multi-week project management, both will frustrate you.

## Mobile app: different purposes

Houzz Pro's mobile app is designed for the design and client communication side. It supports 3D floor planning with AR, client portal access, messaging, time tracking, photo capture, and offline mode. It is the tool a contractor brings to a client meeting.

QuoteIQ's mobile app is rated 4.7 out of 5 from over 4,100 reviews. It covers job details, photos, scheduling, invoicing, payments, and customer communication. It is the tool a technician or contractor uses throughout the workday.

If your techs live in the app and the day-of-job flow is what matters, QuoteIQ is the better fit. If the app needs to support client presentations and design work on site, Houzz Pro's mobile experience is more relevant.

## Marketing and lead generation

Houzz Pro has a unique advantage that no field-service tool can match: the Houzz marketplace. The platform serves roughly 70 million homeowners each month who are researching renovation projects. A Houzz Pro subscription includes an organic marketplace profile that appears in local search results. The advertising add-on provides featured placement, starting at $499/month separately from software pricing.

For a remodeling firm that wants leads from homeowners actively planning renovation work, that marketplace visibility is a genuine lead generation channel. For a fence company, pressure washing contractor, or exterior cleaning specialist doing daily service work, the Houzz marketplace is not relevant.

QuoteIQ offers review request tools and customer communication features, but it is not a marketplace. Its marketing is about retaining customers and getting reviews. Houzz Pro's marketing is about getting found by homeowners who do not know you yet.

## QuickBooks and accounting

Both platforms offer QuickBooks integration. The difference is in how field activity flows into the accounting system.

Houzz Pro offers two-way sync with QuickBooks Online for estimates, invoices, and payments. It also integrates with Gusto for payroll, Google Suite, Microsoft 365, Zoom, and Zapier for broader connections.

QuoteIQ offers QuickBooks integration on the Pro plan ($149.99/month) and above. The sync handles invoices, payments, and customer records.

Both handle the basics. Choose based on which platform your team will actually use day to day rather than on sync feature lists.

## Cancellation and billing concerns

This is a factor that specifically affects Houzz Pro. Across more than 1,000 Capterra reviews, a significant share of negative feedback names cancellation difficulty as a primary or significant complaint. Users describe automatic annual renewals triggering after a trial they understood to be monthly billing. Others report charges continuing after requesting cancellation. This pattern is consistent across multiple years of reviews.

QuoteIQ does not have this pattern in its review data. The 14-day trial requires a card, but the pricing structure is transparent and published. Canceling a monthly subscription is handled through account settings.

If you are evaluating Houzz Pro, document your trial start date, confirm the annual renewal trigger in writing, and set a calendar reminder two weeks before it expires. The billing complaints are consistent enough to treat as a real risk, not an outlier.

## Onboarding and setup

Houzz Pro requires significant setup time. Multiple Capterra users describe onboarding as more demanding than anticipated, with one reviewer reporting 50 to 80 hours for basic workflow configuration. The 3D floor planner, mood boards, selections boards, and client portal all require configuration before they add value.

QuoteIQ is faster to adopt. The default workflow works out of the box for standard home-service quoting and invoicing. MapMeasure Pro and the AI Estimator need testing with real jobs before you trust the output, but a contractor can be operational within a day or two.

If implementation speed matters, QuoteIQ has the edge. If your business can absorb a longer setup period in exchange for design-specific tools and marketplace visibility, Houzz Pro's onboarding investment may pay off over time.

## Where each product can be the wrong fit

Houzz Pro can be the wrong fit when the business does not sell through design presentation. A fence company, a pressure washing contractor, or an exterior cleaning specialist does not benefit from 3D floor plans or mood boards. The Houzz marketplace is about renovation and design projects, not service calls. Paying for software plus advertising to get visibility on a marketplace that does not serve your trade is a misallocation.

QuoteIQ can be the wrong fit when the business is project-based with multi-week timelines and change orders. A custom home builder, a design-build remodeler running three concurrent projects with subcontractor coordination, or a contractor who needs selections control and production scheduling will outgrow QuoteIQ's field-service architecture. Those buyers should look at construction-first platforms like <a href="/buildertrend-review/">Buildertrend</a>.

Both tools can be wrong for a contractor who has not yet identified whether their work is service-driven or project-driven. Figure that out before choosing software.

## Evaluation plan for the two trials

Both platforms offer trials. Use them with your actual workflow rather than clicking around an empty dashboard.

For Houzz Pro, the 30-day trial gives access to the 3D floor planner, CRM, client portal, and marketplace tools. Day one should be setup: import a client list, create a sample project, and build one 3D floor plan. Day two should test the client portal: send a test proposal to an internal contact and check whether the approval flow works. Day three should test selections and mood boards with real material options. Document the trial start date and understand the annual renewal terms before day thirty ends.

For QuoteIQ, the 14-day trial requires a card but opens full features. Day one should be setup: import customers, create services, connect a test payment path. Day two should test MapMeasure Pro: measure a real property and check the satellite output. Day three should test the AI Estimator: take photos of a real job site and see what estimate the AI produces. Day four should test quoting: build a quote and send it for online approval. Day five should test scheduling and invoicing: book a job, mark it complete, and check the QuickBooks sync.

If both platforms pass their respective tests, the decision should be clear based on which workflow matched your actual business. If neither feels right, consider that you may need a construction-first platform rather than a design-first or service-first tool.

## FAQ

### Is Houzz Pro or QuoteIQ cheaper?

QuoteIQ has the lower and more transparent entry price at $29.99/month for Essentials, with five published tiers up to $699/month. Houzz Pro has a no-cost Basic tier, but paid Pro, Custom, and Enterprise tiers now require a demo for current pricing. The full comparison depends on user count, features needed, and whether you need Houzz marketplace advertising at $499+/month. A home-service team may find QuoteIQ more cost-effective. A design-build firm may find Houzz Pro more valuable despite higher cost because the design tools and marketplace leads justify it.

### Can I use Houzz Pro for a service business?

You can, but it is not built for it. Houzz Pro does not have dispatch, route optimization, satellite measurement, or AI estimating from photos. It is built for project-based remodeling and design work. If your business runs on daily service calls, QuoteIQ or <a href="/housecall-pro-review/">Housecall Pro</a> will fit the workflow better.

### Can I use QuoteIQ for remodeling project management?

QuoteIQ can handle estimates, invoices, and scheduling for smaller remodeling work, but it is not built for multi-week phase tracking, change orders, selections management, or 3D design presentation. If those are core to your workflow, compare <a href="/buildertrend-review/">Buildertrend</a> or <a href="/houzz-pro-vs-jobber/">Houzz Pro vs Jobber</a> for alternatives.

### Which is better for QuickBooks sync?

Both offer QuickBooks Online integration. Houzz Pro offers two-way sync for estimates, invoices, and payments at all paid tiers. QuoteIQ includes QuickBooks on the Pro plan ($149.99/month) and above. Test the sync with your actual bookkeeping setup during the trial.

### Does Houzz Pro have satellite measurement or AI estimating?

No. Houzz Pro's AI features are built around 3D floor planning, AR walkthroughs, and visual design tools. It does not have satellite-based property measurement or photo-based AI estimating. QuoteIQ includes MapMeasure Pro satellite measurement starting on the Beginner plan and the AI Estimator for photo-based estimates.

### Is the Houzz marketplace worth the advertising cost?

It depends entirely on whether your trade is renovation or design work. If you are a kitchen and bath contractor or an interior designer, Houzz marketplace visibility can generate genuine leads from homeowners planning projects. The advertising package starts at $499/month on top of the software subscription. If you are a fence company, pressure washing contractor, or exterior cleaning specialist, the Houzz marketplace is not relevant to your customer base.

## Final verdict

Houzz Pro and QuoteIQ are useful products that serve different contractor businesses. Houzz Pro is the right pick for design-build remodelers, interior designers, and home pros who sell through visual presentation and benefit from Houzz marketplace visibility. The 3D floor planner, client portal, selections boards, and marketplace profile are genuine advantages for that audience. The caveats are real: annual billing, cancellation difficulty in user reviews, pricing that now requires a demo, and a longer onboarding period. But for the right business, those tools justify the cost.

QuoteIQ is the right pick for home-service contractors who want transparent published pricing, AI estimating, satellite measurement, quoting, scheduling, invoicing, and payments in one platform. The five published tiers, included user counts, 14-day trial, MapMeasure Pro, AI Estimator, and 4.7-star mobile app make it a strong starting point for small to mid-size service businesses. It is not a design tool, and it does not try to be.

The decision comes down to one question: does your revenue depend on selling through design presentation, or on quoting and running field-service jobs at speed? Answer that honestly, and the software choice follows.
