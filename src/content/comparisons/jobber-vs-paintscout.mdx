# Jobber vs PaintScout (2026): Painting Contractor Software Compared


Most painting software comparisons frame this as a head-to-head race. It's not. Jobber and PaintScout are built for different parts of the same business, and the one you need first depends entirely on where the pain lives. See our <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber review</a> for more on the operations side.

If the estimate goes out fine but the job falls apart after that - missed follow-ups, scheduling confusion, invoices that don't go out until the owner remembers - that's a Jobber problem. If the estimate itself is the issue - inconsistent pricing, slow proposals, no upgrade presentation, sales reps quoting different numbers for the same scope - that's a PaintScout problem.

The short version: Jobber wins on operations. PaintScout wins on pre-sale painting accuracy. Some painting companies eventually need both.

## Jobber vs PaintScout: Quick Verdict for Painters

**Solo painters and small residential crews (1-5 people):** Jobber is the safer first platform. The Core plan at $49/mo handles scheduling, quoting, invoicing, and online payments. Connect at $139-$199/mo (individual or team) adds automated reminders, QuickBooks Online, and quote follow-ups. You don't need painting-specific production-rate math if you're doing enough repeat work that your rates are already dialed in.

**Painting companies with a sales team or high estimate volume:** This is where PaintScout earns its cost. If two sales reps are quoting the same 1,500 sq ft interior at different prices because they're doing their own mental math, PaintScout's production-rate templates close that gap. The proposal presentation - branded, interactive, with upgrade options, photos, and e-signature - also converts better than a standard PDF estimate.

**Operations-first buyers:** If you don't have scheduling, job tracking, or invoicing sorted, PaintScout's Sales plan alone won't cover it. You'd need the Operations add-on (+$79/mo annual), and even then Jobber's ops breadth is deeper for field service. Start with Jobber, add PaintScout later if your estimating process becomes the bottleneck.

**Both tools together:** PaintScout's own integrations page lists Jobber via Zapier. Some growing painting companies run PaintScout for estimating and proposals, then Jobber for scheduling and invoicing after the job is won. It's not the cheapest stack, but it covers both gaps.

Jobber prices its plans three ways: month-to-month no commitment, monthly with a 1-year contract, and annual prepaid. The annual rate is the lowest, and the monthly no-commitment rate is the ceiling.

Scheduling, quoting, invoicing, online payments, basic reporting, app marketplace. No QuickBooks sync. No automated reminders. Fine for a solo painter who just needs the basics organized.

**Connect** - $139/mo individual / $199/mo team (monthly no-commitment) or $99/mo individual / $149/mo team (annual). Up to 1 or 5 users.
Adds automated reminders, automatic payment collection, checklists and job forms, quote and invoice follow-ups, QuickBooks Online, and time and expense tracking. This is the realistic minimum for a painting company that wants automation handling the follow-up.

**Grow** - $199/mo individual / $399/mo team (monthly) or $149/mo individual / $299/mo team (annual). Up to 1 or 10 users.
Adds advanced quote customizations, optional line items on estimates, automatic time tracking, job costing, two-way SMS, and custom workflow automations. The right tier if you want to track crew hours against job budget or run automated SMS communications.

**Plus** - $699/mo monthly / $529/mo annual (15 users).
Bundles Marketing Suite ($79 value), AI Receptionist ($99 value), Pipeline add-on ($49 value), dedicated onboarding, and Premium Support. Overkill for most painting companies; relevant if you want the full stack including outbound marketing automation.

**Extra users:** $29/mo each beyond plan-included seats. Teams over 15 contact sales.

**The pricing jump to watch:** Core to Connect is the real upgrade path for most painters. QuickBooks Online, automated reminders, and quote follow-ups all sit behind Connect. A solo painter on Core who adds one employee quickly discovers they're not just adding a $29/mo seat - they need the team Connect plan at $149-$199/mo to get the automation that makes the workflow functional.

## Estimating and Proposal Workflow

This is the clearest differentiator, and it's not close.

**Jobber** is a general-purpose quote builder. You create line items, set quantities and prices, add optional items (on Grow tier), and send the estimate for digital approval. The quote-to-job conversion is clean - customer approves, job appears on the schedule. For painting contractors whose rates are already set and whose estimates are simple enough to express as line items ("exterior painting - 2,000 sq ft - $4,500"), Jobber's quote builder works fine.

What Jobber doesn't do: room-by-room production-rate math. If an estimator needs to enter dimensions for three bedrooms, a hallway, and a kitchen, calculate wall area minus windows and doors, apply your company's production rate per square foot by paint type, and present that as a structured painting proposal with upgrade options - Jobber isn't the tool for that job.

**PaintScout** is built specifically for that workflow. The production-rate engine lets estimators enter room dimensions and surface types, and the system calculates estimates based on your company's customized production rates. Walls, trim, ceilings, cabinets, and other painting services each get their own rate. The estimate output is a branded, interactive presentation - not just a PDF - with photos, videos, optional upgrades, and add-on services built in. Customers can accept from their phone, leave questions in a chat thread, and e-sign the proposal without a call.

The follow-up layer is also better for sales teams. PaintScout tracks estimate activity (when the customer opened it, how long they spent on each section), sends automated email and SMS follow-ups on a schedule you configure, and shows close rates per estimator on a sales leaderboard. For a painting company training new sales reps or managing a team of estimators, that accountability layer has real value.

**The catch on PaintScout's estimating:** the production-rate setup takes upfront work. You need to configure your rates accurately before the system calculates correctly. The optional Success Packages exist partly because of this - the $999 Plus package includes production rate setup assistance. Budget time for that, or budget money for the package.

## Scheduling, Job Management, and Production Handoff

This is where Jobber's strength shows.

**Jobber's** scheduling is built into the core product at every tier. Drag-and-drop calendar, GPS tracking (Connect tier and above), automated appointment reminders, and online booking that lets customers schedule directly from your website or Google profile. After a job is won, the workflow is linear: estimate approved → job created on the schedule → crew assigned → job notes and photos logged in the field → invoice sent → payment collected. That handoff is what Jobber is designed around.

The mobile app is consistently praised for being usable without training. A crew lead can open the app, see their jobs, check notes, log time, take photos, and mark a job complete without a tutorial. For painting crews where the lead isn't the tech-forward member of the team, that matters.

**PaintScout's** Sales plan handles Google Calendar sync, which covers basic scheduling visibility. If you need a proper scheduling layer - drag-and-drop job board, team task management, integrated job pipelines, and lead-to-completion workflow - that's the Operations add-on at $79/mo annual.

PaintScout's Operations add-on is newer and painting-specific in its orientation, which makes sense for the audience. But if you're evaluating PaintScout against Jobber for field operations, you're comparing Jobber's core product against PaintScout's add-on. Jobber wins that comparison unless PaintScout Operations covers everything your crew needs - verify before buying.

One scenario where PaintScout Operations makes sense even if you're already using Jobber: some painting companies use PaintScout for the pre-sale workflow and a separate tool for job management. PaintScout Operations might replace the job management piece if it fits your production workflow - but it's a case-by-case call, not a default.

## Follow-Ups, Communication, and Customer Experience

**Jobber** automates the transactional layer: reminders before jobs, follow-up requests after jobs, automated invoice reminders for unpaid balances. Two-way SMS is available on Grow tier. The client hub lets customers view quotes, invoices, and job history, and pay online without calling the office. For a residential painting company where most customers are homeowners booking a one-time project, that self-service visibility reduces admin time on both sides.

**PaintScout** goes deeper on the pre-sale communication side. Automated email and SMS sequences follow up on open estimates on a schedule you configure. The interactive proposal lets customers ask questions, request changes, and chat directly within the proposal document before they sign. That's more than a follow-up reminder - it's a selling environment that mirrors how consumers interact with other service products they buy.

Estimate activity tracking shows which customers have opened the proposal, how many times, and how long they spent on the upgrade section. That information gives an estimator a real reason to call at the right moment instead of guessing. For companies where proposal conversion is a known weak spot, this is one of the more practical differentiators in PaintScout's feature set.

For a residential painting company that already uses QuickBooks and CompanyCam, Jobber connects to both without extra configuration.

**PaintScout** integrates with CompanyCam, QuickBooks Online, Zapier, Stripe, Wisetack (buy-now-pay-later for customers), Google Calendar (via Zapier), Jobber (via Zapier), HubSpot (via Zapier), Airtable (via Zapier), GoHighLevel (via Zapier), Monday.com (via Zapier), HeyPros, Workglue, and Dropbox.

The Wisetack integration is worth noting: it lets customers finance large painting projects directly through the proposal. If you're selling exterior repaints, whole-house interior projects, or commercial work where project cost is in the thousands, financing options convert better than invoicing alone. Jobber doesn't have this natively.

The Jobber-via-Zapier integration on PaintScout's list is also telling - PaintScout explicitly supports companies that use both tools. That stack (PaintScout for estimates and proposals, Jobber for operations) is a realistic setup for a growing painting company.

## Which Painting Contractors Should Choose Jobber?

Jobber is the right first platform for a painting contractor who needs operations covered. Specifically:

**If you're solo or running a small crew (1-5 people)** and your current system is text chains and a spreadsheet, Jobber connects booking, scheduling, quoting, invoicing, and payments in one place. Core at $29/mo annual handles the basics. Connect at $99/mo individual adds QuickBooks and automated reminders - the two additions that most residential painting companies actually use daily.

**If your estimates are simple enough for line items.** Interior repaints where you're working from room-based flat rates, standard exterior paint jobs where square footage times a price-per-square-foot is close enough, routine commercial work. Line-item quotes in Jobber work well at that level of specificity.

**If you want mobile-first field operations.** Jobber's mobile app is consistently rated as one of the most intuitive in the category. For painting crews where the field team updates job status, logs hours, and collects signatures from phones, Jobber's app reduces friction more reliably than most alternatives.

**If QuickBooks Online is non-negotiable.** Jobber syncs with QuickBooks Online starting at Connect. PaintScout also syncs with QuickBooks Online, but Jobber's broader ops workflow is a cleaner fit for companies that think about their business through a QuickBooks lens.

**Who should skip Jobber:** Painting companies training estimators on consistent production-rate pricing, companies where the proposal presentation is a competitive differentiator, or sales teams that need estimate activity tracking and follow-up automation built into the estimating tool itself.

## Which Painting Contractors Should Choose PaintScout?

PaintScout is the right tool for painting companies where the estimate is the problem - not the operations.

**If inconsistent estimating is costing you jobs or margin.** Two sales reps quoting the same scope at $4,200 and $5,800 is a production-rate problem, not a scheduling problem. PaintScout's production-rate engine makes the math the same for every estimator, every time.

**If your proposal is losing to competitors who present better.** A PDF attached to an email versus an interactive branded proposal with photos, upgrades, and a live chat thread - customers notice the difference. If you're in a market where homeowners compare three quotes, presentation quality affects conversion.

**If follow-up is falling through the cracks.** Manually following up on 15 open estimates is a job in itself. PaintScout automates the sequence without the office having to remember each one.

**If you have a sales team or plan to add one.** The leaderboard and close-rate tracking aren't just vanity metrics - they show which estimators are closing and which aren't. That data matters when you're managing multiple reps or training new ones.

**If you already have operations covered.** PaintScout is not the right first tool if you have no scheduling system, no invoicing process, and no client communication flow. Fix those first, or add PaintScout Operations alongside the Sales plan.

**Who should skip PaintScout:** Painters who only need basic quoting at the lowest cost, shops that want a one-platform field-service system without paying for an add-on operations layer, and buyers who don't want to invest time in production-rate configuration.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Jobber good for painting contractors?**

Yes, if the main problem is operations - scheduling, invoicing, reminders, payments, and client communication. Jobber covers all of that in one platform with transparent pricing and a 14-day trial. It's not painting-specific estimating software. Jobber's quote builder handles line-item estimates well, but it doesn't do production-rate math, room dimensions, or surface-specific pricing.

**Is PaintScout only for estimating?**

The Sales plan is primarily an estimating, proposal, and sales-automation platform. It includes payments, invoice/payment reporting, Google Calendar sync, deal pipeline, and automated follow-ups. The Operations add-on ($79/mo annual) extends PaintScout into scheduling, job management, task tracking, and production workflow. Buyers who need full field-service operations should evaluate whether Operations covers their specific workflow before dropping a tool like Jobber.

**Which is cheaper, Jobber or PaintScout?**

Model your specific team size against both pricing pages before deciding on cost alone.

It's a real setup, not just a theoretical one.
