# TradeTapp vs Bluebeam Revu: Prequalification or Takeoffs?

Autodesk TradeTapp vs Bluebeam Revu is a comparison of two construction tools that solve fundamentally different problems. TradeTapp is a subcontractor prequalification and risk management platform built for general contractors inside Autodesk Construction Cloud. Bluebeam Revu is a PDF markup, measurement, and takeoff platform built for construction teams that work from plan sets.

The distinction matters. A GC who needs to vet 300 subcontractors before a bid package will not get that capability from Bluebeam. An estimator who needs calibrated measurements, overlay comparison, and quantity takeoffs from PDF drawings will not get that from TradeTapp. Both tools can live in the same contractor's tech stack without overlapping.

## The fast answer

Choose [Autodesk TradeTapp](/go/autodesk-tradetapp-official-site/) if you are a general contractor or project owner already using Autodesk Construction Cloud and your primary need is subcontractor prequalification. TradeTapp's differentiator is a 250,000+ pre-registered subcontractor network where qualification profiles are reusable across GCs. The platform automates financial health scoring, safety and insurance risk analysis, and industry benchmarking. Pricing is custom quote through Autodesk sales with no published tiers.

Choose [Bluebeam Revu](/go/bluebeam-revu-official-site/) if your team works from PDF plan sets and needs professional markup, calibrated measurements, overlay comparison, quantity takeoffs, and collaborative document review. Bluebeam publishes four annual per-user tiers: Basics at $260/user/year, Core at $330/user/year, Complete at $440/user/year, and Max at $590/user/year (introductory pricing locks in through 2027). A 14-day free trial gives full feature access with no credit card required.

The simplest split: TradeTapp helps a GC vet subcontractors before they go to work. Bluebeam helps an estimator or PM work with the drawings on their desk. If you need both prequalification and document workflow, you likely need both tools or a broader platform like Procore.

## Primary purpose and target buyer

TradeTapp is built for general contractors, project owners, and developers who manage subcontractor risk at scale. The official Autodesk construction page lists TradeTapp inside the Preconstruction bundle, which positions it as a preconstruction tool for vetting and qualifying subcontractors before they are invited to bid.

Bluebeam Revu is built for construction professionals whose daily work runs through PDF plan sets. That includes estimators pulling quantities from drawings, project managers reviewing revision sets, GCs coordinating plan review with subs and architects, and subcontractors who need to mark up drawings on site or in the office. The tool sits beside a separate estimating, PM, or accounting system rather than replacing one.

A GC vetting 300 subcontractors before a bid and an estimator measuring areas from a PDF plan set are in different roles at different stages of the project. The software that fits one will not fit the other.

## Prequalification vs document workflow

TradeTapp's core workflow is subcontractor prequalification. GCs create customizable questionnaires that capture financial statements, safety records, insurance certificates, and compliance data. The platform automatically calculates financial ratios, runs credit assessments, and benchmarks each subcontractor against industry peers across the 250,000+ sub database. When a risk flag triggers, the system suggests mitigation steps such as requiring higher insurance limits or adding financial covenants. The 250,000+ pre-registered network means subs who have already qualified for one GC can reuse their data when invited by another GC on the network, reducing repeated data entry.

Bluebeam's core workflow is document-centric. You mark up drawings with tools that understand construction scale: calibrated measurements for length, area, perimeter, volume, angle, and count. The measurements snap to scale when you calibrate a drawing, so quantities come from actual plan geometry. The overlay and compare tool detects changes between drawing revisions automatically. Studio Sessions let multiple people mark up the same PDF in real time with attribution. The Complete tier adds Quantity Link for live Excel integration, and the Max tier adds AI-powered drawing reviews and Magic Markups.

TradeTapp does not do PDF markup, measurement, or takeoff. Bluebeam does not do subcontractor prequalification, financial health scoring, or risk benchmarking. The feature sets do not overlap.

## Where Autodesk TradeTapp wins

**Prequalification network.** The 250,000+ pre-registered subcontractor network is TradeTapp's real differentiator. When a sub qualifies for one GC, the profile is available to other GCs on the network. No standalone prequalification tool offers a pre-vetted pool at this scale.

**Automated financial health scoring.** Instead of manually reviewing spreadsheets, GCs get automated risk scores with supporting data. The system benchmarks each subcontractor's financial and safety metrics against a crowdsourced network of vetted trades, making qualification decisions defensible to project owners and bonding companies.

**Autodesk ecosystem integration.** For GCs already in Autodesk Construction Cloud, TradeTapp connects natively to BuildingConnected Pro, Autodesk Build, and Autodesk Forma. Risk flags appear alongside cost items. Qualification status shows up next to bid packages.

## Where Bluebeam Revu wins

**Industry-standard PDF markup.** Bluebeam was built for the AEC workflow in a way that general PDF editors were not. The measurement tools understand construction scales, the markup tools are set up for drawing review, and Studio handles real-time multi-stakeholder plan review that construction projects require.

**Published per-user pricing.** Bluebeam publishes exact dollar amounts for all four tiers. The feature boundary between Basics, Core, Complete, and Max is documented on their site. For a construction software buyer trying to budget, that matters when the alternative is a custom-quote sales process.

**Calibrated measurement and takeoff.** Volume, area, perimeter, angle, and count measurements tied to plan geometry are the core of what Bluebeam does. The Complete tier adds Quantity Link for live Excel sync, and the Max tier adds AI drawing reviews. No prequalification tool offers these capabilities.

**Studio Sessions.** Real-time collaborative markup with attribution and version control changes how distributed teams review drawings. GCs, architects, structural engineers, and subs can all mark up the same document simultaneously.

The native data flow that makes TradeTapp valuable only works inside the Autodesk ecosystem. It is also not a fit for small contractors who need published monthly pricing, or for teams that need lightweight prequalification without enterprise complexity. And if your primary need is document markup, measurement, or takeoff from plan sets, TradeTapp does not do that work at all.

Bluebeam Revu can be the wrong fit if you need a full project management, scheduling, invoicing, payroll, or accounting platform. ## FAQ

### Are Autodesk TradeTapp and Bluebeam Revu the same type of construction software?

No. TradeTapp is a subcontractor prequalification and risk management platform for general contractors. Bluebeam Revu is a PDF markup, measurement, and takeoff platform for construction teams that work from plan sets. They solve different problems for different parts of the workflow.

### Can Bluebeam Revu replace TradeTapp for subcontractor prequalification?

No. Bluebeam has no subcontractor prequalification, financial health scoring, risk benchmarking, or compliance tracking capabilities. It is a document and takeoff tool. If prequalification is the need, you need a prequalification platform like TradeTapp.

### Can TradeTapp replace Bluebeam Revu for PDF markup and takeoffs?

No. TradeTapp does not handle PDF markup, calibrated measurements, overlay comparison, quantity takeoffs, or collaborative document review. If your estimators work from PDF plan sets, they need a document workflow tool like Bluebeam.

### Which is cheaper, TradeTapp or Bluebeam?

The pricing models are not directly comparable. TradeTapp is custom quote through Autodesk sales with no published tiers. Bluebeam publishes four annual per-user tiers: Basics $260/user/year, Core $330/user/year, Complete $440/user/year, and Max $590/user/year (introductory). Bluebeam gives you published prices to model before a call. TradeTapp requires a sales conversation for any cost estimate.

### Do TradeTapp and Bluebeam Revu integrate with each other?

There is no direct integration between the two. They can coexist in a tech stack: TradeTapp manages subcontractor qualification data, Bluebeam manages document workflow. Both export to Excel, which can serve as a bridge for limited data exchange. TradeTapp connects natively to Autodesk Construction Cloud products. Bluebeam connects to design and document management tools. The two integration layers do not conflict.

### Should I buy TradeTapp if I already have Bluebeam Revu?

If you are a GC who needs to vet subcontractors at scale, yes. TradeTapp handles prequalification that Bluebeam cannot address. The two tools serve different stages of the project lifecycle and complement each other without overlap.

### Should I buy Bluebeam Revu if I already have TradeTapp?

If your estimators and PMs work from PDF plan sets and need professional markup and measurement tools, yes. Bluebeam handles document and takeoff workflows that TradeTapp cannot address. The 14-day free trial lets you validate the fit before committing.
