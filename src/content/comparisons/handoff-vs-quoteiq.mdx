# Handoff vs QuoteIQ for Contractors

Handoff vs QuoteIQ is not just a price comparison. It is a workflow comparison.

Both products talk about AI. Both can help a contractor create estimates. Both can connect parts of the sales process to the back office. But the buying reason is different enough that the wrong choice will feel wrong quickly.

Handoff is built around AI estimating and residential construction proposals. Its official pricing page positions Flex, Pro, Scale, and Enterprise around estimates, proposals, scopes of work, AI renderings, takeoffs, client portals, invoicing, project management, and QuickBooks Online sync. It is strongest when the contractor's pain is, "We need to turn walkthroughs, scopes, photos, and plans into better bids faster."

QuoteIQ is a broader home-service field service platform with public tiers from $29.99/month to $699/month. Its official pricing page includes estimates, invoices, scheduling, online payments, AI Virtual Call Team, MapMeasure Pro, QuoteIQ Cam, review tools, job costing, QuickBooks, routing, inventory, crew tools, and monthly AI credits by plan. It is strongest when the contractor's pain is, "We need one clearly priced app to run daily home-service workflow." ## The real buying split

### Handoff is an estimating and proposal decision

Handoff makes the most sense when the sales workflow is the constraint. A remodeler, roofer, or residential contractor walks a job, gathers scope details, takes photos, talks through the work, then has to turn that messy intake into a professional estimate and proposal before the homeowner moves on.

That is where Handoff's official pages are focused. The pricing page lists AI estimating, AI proposals, AI-powered scopes of work, AI renderings, voice transcription to estimate, custom pricing catalogs, client portals, daily logs, scheduling, meeting recording, milestone invoicing, and QuickBooks Online sync. The Scale tier adds AI takeoffs from residential plans up to 5,000 sqft and positions the workflow around larger residential jobs.

The practical read: Handoff is not trying to be the cheapest CRM. It is trying to make a contractor faster and more consistent at selling project work. If a better estimate and proposal can win one extra remodel, roof, or large trade job, the subscription math looks different.

The risk is that a company buys it for the AI promise without needing that workflow. If the business mostly needs a calendar, invoices, payment links, route planning, and basic customer records, Handoff may feel expensive compared with a broader field-service tool.

### QuoteIQ is a field-service operating app decision

QuoteIQ makes the first budget pass easier because the price table is public. Essentials is $29.99/month. Beginner is $74.99/month. Pro is $149.99/month. Elite is $299/month. Max is $699/month. Annual billing lowers those monthly equivalents to $25, $62.50, $125, $249, and $582.50 respectively.

That clarity matters for a home-service owner comparing software after jobs are done for the day. The owner can see the likely tier, included users, AI credits, and feature gates before booking a demo. QuoteIQ also covers more of the everyday field-service loop: estimate, schedule, invoice, collect payment, document the job, request a review, and keep customer history in one place.

The official pricing page supports that broader read. MapMeasure Pro starts on Beginner. QuickBooks is listed on Pro and above. Elite adds EmployeeHub, routing, dispatching, inventory, and higher AI credits. Max adds unlimited users, AI Website Builder, sales team tracking, priority support, and crew management.

The risk is assuming breadth equals fit. QuoteIQ can cover a lot, but a contractor selling complex remodels may still need deeper scope, proposal, takeoff, and project-document handling than a lighter FSM platform provides.

## Workflow depth

### Handoff wins when estimates are the product

For many residential contractors, the estimate is not paperwork. It is the sales asset. The homeowner sees the scope, the line items, the presentation, the visuals, the timing, and the payment path before choosing who to trust.

Handoff is built for that moment. The official pages describe AI estimates from scope, blueprints, or job photos; AI proposals; AI-powered scopes of work; renderings; client portals; approvals; milestone invoices; and project management. That cluster matters when the estimate has to become a polished package, not just a number.

A remodeler should test Handoff with a real walkthrough. Record voice notes. Add photos. Build a scope. Create the proposal. Check whether the output is clear enough to send to a homeowner without rebuilding it in another tool. If the answer is yes, Handoff is solving the right problem.

### QuoteIQ wins when the whole service loop matters

QuoteIQ is more useful when the company wants one app around the whole daily service loop. The job starts with a lead or customer record, becomes an estimate, gets scheduled, gets documented with photos, turns into an invoice, collects payment, and then triggers follow-up or review requests.

That is different from Handoff's proposal-first center of gravity. QuoteIQ's breadth is the point. MapMeasure Pro can help with property measurement. QuoteIQ Cam can document the job. QuickBooks on Pro and above can connect accounting. Elite and Max add more operational tools for routing, dispatching, inventory, team workflow, and crew management.

A home-service contractor should test QuoteIQ during the trial with a normal job from start to finish. If the team can create the estimate, schedule the work, document it, invoice, collect payment, and sync accounting without workarounds, QuoteIQ has passed the buying test.

## AI comparison

### Handoff's AI is closer to a sales and project assistant

Handoff's AI is centered on estimate and proposal creation. The pricing page and home page describe an AI teammate trained around residential construction, contractor pricing, project history, scopes, client portals, daily logs, scheduling, meeting notes, documents, and QuickBooks-connected payments.

That makes Handoff more specialized. It is the stronger choice when the contractor wants AI to help turn job information into a bid package and then carry that information into project documents and client communication.

The caution is usage. Flex includes 50 AI usage credits per month, while Pro and above list unlimited AI usage credits. A company that expects to use AI heavily should model the workflow on Pro or Scale rather than assuming Flex will be enough.

### QuoteIQ's AI is tied to daily field-service actions

QuoteIQ's AI is broader but less construction-proposal-specific. The pricing page lists monthly AI credits by tier and includes AI Autopilot, Virtual Call Team, AI Estimator, AI image tools, AI CoPilot, AI Smart Import, and related tools. The home page frames these as part of an all-in-one CRM and field-service platform.

That can be better for a service business that wants AI inside the daily operating app. The owner can use AI around estimates, customer communication, imports, images, calls, and internal actions rather than only formal proposals. If the larger question is how AI fits into estimates across several vendors, use the <a href="/best-estimating-software-for-small-contractors/">small-contractor estimating software guide</a> as the wider benchmark.

The caution is that AI credits are metered. Essentials includes 500 per month, Beginner 1,500, Pro 3,000, Elite 5,000, and Max 8,000. If AI call handling, estimate generation, and image generation become daily habits, the buyer should test usage during the trial instead of guessing from the feature list.

## Measurement, takeoffs, and job type

This is one of the cleanest splits in the comparison.

Handoff's measurement story is tied to residential project estimating. Scale includes AI takeoffs from residential plans up to 5,000 sqft, detailed material and labor quantities, and a faster estimating workflow for larger residential projects. That matters if the company sells bigger remodels or project work where plans, scopes, and takeoffs affect the bid. QuoteIQ's measurement story is tied to field-service estimating. MapMeasure Pro starts on Beginner and supports satellite-based property measurement. That is useful for pressure washing, fencing, exterior cleaning, landscaping, roofing service, and other trades where measuring the property helps produce a fast quote. If the trade is roofing-heavy and needs a broader software shortlist, compare the <a href="/best-roofing-software-contractors/">roofing software guide</a> as well.

If the buyer is pricing a remodel from plans, Handoff is more aligned. If the buyer is measuring a driveway, fence line, roof area, or property from satellite imagery inside a field-service CRM, QuoteIQ is more aligned.

## QuickBooks and back-office fit

Both products can belong in a QuickBooks discussion, but the proof bar is different.

Handoff's pricing page describes bi-directional QuickBooks Online sync for estimates, invoices, and payments. That is a good fit when estimates and proposals are the source of accounting activity. The buyer should confirm how estimates, accepted proposals, milestone invoices, deposits, payments, change orders, and job records map into QuickBooks before signing.

QuoteIQ lists QuickBooks on Pro and above. That is enough for many home-service businesses if estimates, invoices, payments, customer records, taxes, items, and deposits sync cleanly. The buyer should test this in the trial with real item names and payment scenarios.

Neither product should win on a checkbox. Make the accounting workflow prove itself with the same job you use for the product demo or trial.

## Wrong-fit signals

Do not choose Handoff if the company mainly needs a general field-service app with low entry cost, route planning, basic scheduling, invoices, payments, and customer records. Handoff can cover more than estimating, but its pricing and product center are strongest when proposal quality and residential construction sales workflow matter.

Do not choose QuoteIQ if the company is really buying a deep estimating and proposal engine for larger residential projects. QuoteIQ can create estimates and includes measurement tools, but Handoff is better aligned when the estimate must become a polished bid package with AI scopes, renderings, takeoffs, approvals, and project documents.

Do not choose either product because the AI language sounds impressive. Put the same real job into each system. The better choice is the one that makes your bottleneck easier to handle, not the one with the longer feature list.

## Evaluation plan

### Step 1: name the bottleneck

Write one sentence before booking a demo or starting a trial: "Our biggest problem is _____." If the blank is slow estimates, weak proposals, inconsistent scopes, or losing bigger residential bids, start with Handoff. If the blank is running estimates, scheduling, invoices, payments, measurements, photos, and customer records in one place, start with QuoteIQ.

### Step 2: test Handoff with a real estimate

Use a real residential job. Bring the scope, photos, notes, pricing assumptions, and any plans. Build the estimate, proposal, scope, client-facing document, payment path, and project handoff. If Scale is on the table, ask specifically how AI takeoffs work, how the 5,000 sqft plan limit applies, and when additional takeoff charges apply.

Handoff passes if the proposal is good enough to send, the assumptions are visible, and the owner or estimator saves real time.

### Step 3: test QuoteIQ with a real service loop

Use a real home-service job. Create the customer, estimate, measurement, schedule, photos, invoice, payment, review request, and QuickBooks sync. Test the AI features with real customer language and real service details. Watch how many AI credits the work consumes.

QuoteIQ passes if the job moves through the platform without the team needing separate tools for normal work.

### Step 4: compare by cost per solved problem

Do not compare $119 against $29.99 in isolation. Compare cost against the problem solved.

Handoff can be the better buy at a higher price if it helps win larger projects and protects margin. QuoteIQ can be the better buy at a lower price if it replaces several tools and gives the team a practical daily operating system. ## Final verdict

<strong>CSH's call:</strong> Handoff is the better choice when the contractor is trying to improve how larger residential jobs get estimated, packaged, approved, and carried into project workflow. Choose Handoff if the sentence is, "We lose money because estimates and proposals take too long or do not sell the job well enough." Choose QuoteIQ if the sentence is, "We need one clearly priced system to run quoting, scheduling, invoicing, payments, and customer workflow."

The products overlap on AI, but they do not solve the same core problem. Match the tool to the bottleneck first, then compare price.

## FAQ

### Is Handoff or QuoteIQ better for contractors?

Handoff is better for contractors whose main problem is AI estimating, proposals, scopes, client approvals, and residential construction sales workflow. QuoteIQ is better for contractors who want an all-in-one field-service platform with published pricing, estimates, invoices, scheduling, payments, measurement, photos, reviews, and AI tools.

### Is QuoteIQ cheaper than Handoff?

Yes at the entry level. QuoteIQ starts at $29.99/month monthly or $25/month as an annual-billed equivalent. Handoff starts at $119/month billed annually or $149/month month to month. The better comparison is not only entry price, though. Handoff is a proposal-first estimating platform, while QuoteIQ is a broader field-service operating app.

### Does Handoff have a free trial?

Handoff's official pricing page lists a 7-day free trial on Flex. Pro and Scale are shown with 12-month commitments on the pricing page, so buyers considering higher tiers should confirm trial, onboarding, commitment, and cancellation details before signing.

### Does QuoteIQ have a free trial?

QuoteIQ's official pricing page lists a 14-day free trial on every plan. Use that trial with a real job, not a sample workflow. Test estimates, scheduling, payments, measurement, AI credits, and QuickBooks if you need accounting sync.

### Which product is better for roofing contractors?

It depends on the roofing workflow. Handoff fits better when the roofer sells larger residential projects and wants AI estimates, proposals, scopes, takeoffs, approvals, and project documents. QuoteIQ fits better when the roofer wants a field-service app with measurement, quotes, scheduling, invoices, payments, customer records, and published pricing.

### Can Handoff and QuoteIQ replace each other?

Not cleanly. Handoff can cover more than proposals, and QuoteIQ can create estimates, but their centers of gravity are different. Handoff is stronger around proposal-first residential construction estimating. QuoteIQ is stronger around all-in-one home-service operations with transparent pricing.

### What should I test first?

Test the workflow that costs the business money. If slow estimates and weak proposals cost jobs, test Handoff with a real walkthrough and proposal. If daily operations are scattered across tools, test QuoteIQ with a full service loop from estimate to payment and QuickBooks sync.
