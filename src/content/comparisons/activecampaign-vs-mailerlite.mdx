# ActiveCampaign vs MailerLite for Contractor Email Marketing

ActiveCampaign and MailerLite are both email marketing platforms built for small businesses, but they solve different problems for contractors. ActiveCampaign is an automation powerhouse with visual sequence builders, lead scoring, and CRM-lite contact management. MailerLite is a simpler, more affordable tool with a no-cost tier and a cleaner email editor.

For a contractor evaluating the two, the question is not "which is better?" It is "what do you actually need your email tool to do?" If the answer is complex multi-step follow-up sequences with conditional branching, lead scoring, and contact pipeline tracking, ActiveCampaign is the stronger platform. If the answer is monthly newsletters, simple welcome sequences, and a no-cost tier that covers a small list indefinitely, MailerLite is the better fit. For broader context, see where both rank in the <a href="/best-email-marketing-software-contractors/">best email marketing software for contractors</a> roundup.

## Quick verdict

Pick ActiveCampaign if you want the deepest automation builder in this price range. The official ActiveCampaign pricing page lists four plans at 1,000 contacts: Starter at $15/mo billed annually, Plus at $49/mo, Pro at $79/mo, and Enterprise at $145/mo. There is no permanent free plan, only a 14-day free trial. The visual automation builder handles conditional branches, split logic, and multi-step sequences that respond to contact behavior in real time. Lead scoring, deal tracking, and CRM-lite contact records come built in. For a contractor running automated quote follow-ups, seasonal promotions, and segmented nurture campaigns, ActiveCampaign delivers more depth than MailerLite at every tier.

Pick MailerLite if you want a no-cost tier you can use indefinitely. The official MailerLite pricing page lists a no-cost plan at $0 for up to 250 subscribers and 2,500 monthly emails, with 2 user seats and access to the drag-and-drop editor, forms, landing pages, and basic automations. Paid plans start with Comfort at $12/mo or $10.80/mo billed yearly for up to 500 subscribers, and Power at $25/mo or $22.50/mo billed yearly with unlimited emails and unlimited user seats. Enterprise is custom for businesses with over 200,000 subscribers. For a contractor with a small list who mainly needs newsletters and simple follow-ups, MailerLite is cheaper, simpler, and good enough to run permanently on the no-cost tier.

Skip both if your main problem is field operations. Neither ActiveCampaign nor MailerLite handles scheduling, dispatch, estimates, or invoicing. You would still need a field service platform like [Jobber](/jobber-review/), [Housecall Pro](/housecall-pro-review/), or another option from the [field service software alternatives](/field-service-software-alternatives/) guide alongside either tool.

## When ActiveCampaign makes more sense

ActiveCampaign makes more sense when the contractor needs automation that goes beyond "send email, wait, send next email." The platform's visual canvas lets you build sequences where a contact's path depends on what they actually do: if they open the quote follow-up email and click the booking link, they get a thank-you and an upsell. If they open but do not click, they get a different follow-up. If they do nothing, they get a nudge at day five. That level of conditional logic runs on the Starter plan and gets more powerful on Plus and Pro.

A contractor could use ActiveCampaign to:

- Trigger a five-email sequence when someone fills out a "get a quote" form on the website
- Score leads based on email opens, link clicks, and page visits so the hottest prospects get called first
- Tag contacts by service area, trade type, and project size, then send targeted campaigns to specific combinations
- Track deals through a pipeline (new lead, contacted, quoted, won, lost) with automated actions at each stage
- Run seasonal promotions where non-openers get a second offer and openers get a thank-you with an upsell

Lead scoring is a key differentiator. Contacts accumulate points for engagement behavior. When a contact crosses a threshold, they can trigger an automation or move to a different list. For a contractor getting 50 website leads a month, this separates people who fill out a form and disappear from people who read every follow-up email. MailerLite does not offer lead scoring.

ActiveCampaign also includes CRM-lite functionality. Contact records show list membership, tags, engagement data, deal stage, and custom fields. The deal tracking view shows a pipeline with stages, deal values, and owner assignments. It is not a full CRM like [Pipedrive](/pipedrive-review/) or Keap, but for a contractor who mainly needs email automation with enough contact structure to segment effectively, it covers the essentials.

## When MailerLite makes more sense

MailerLite makes more sense when the contractor values simplicity and a no-cost tier. Many small contractor businesses have fewer than 250 past customers, leads, and newsletter subscribers combined. For those companies, MailerLite's no-cost tier is not a trial. It is a permanent tool.

A contractor could use the no-cost tier to:

- Send a monthly newsletter to past customers about seasonal services
- Run a spring reactivation campaign before busy season starts
- Collect leads through a website signup form
- Build one landing page for a service area
- Send quote follow-up emails manually

The no-cost tier has limits: 250 subscribers, 2,500 monthly emails, 1 landing page, 3 active forms, and 3 active automations. For a small list, that may be enough. When the list grows past 250 subscribers, Comfort at $10.80/mo billed yearly gives access to unlimited templates, AI writing assistant, personalized content blocks, A/B testing, and up to 50 active automations. Power at $22.50/mo billed yearly includes unlimited monthly emails, unlimited automations, unlimited forms, and unlimited user seats.

MailerLite's drag-and-drop email editor is cleaner and faster to produce a finished email than ActiveCampaign's editor. The template library is smaller than Mailchimp or Constant Contact, but the editor itself is more intuitive. For a contractor who wants polished newsletters without spending an hour on design, MailerLite gets the job done faster. Contractors comparing affordable email tools may also want to read our [Moosend review](/moosend-review/).

MailerLite also stands out on built-in monetization. The platform supports selling digital products, paid newsletter subscriptions, and appointment bookings directly. A contractor who runs a paid consultation or sells a digital guide can do that inside MailerLite without a separate e-commerce tool.

## Email marketing and automation depth

This is where the two platforms diverge most sharply.

ActiveCampaign's automation builder is its strongest argument. The visual canvas lets you drag conditions, actions, and triggers into a sequence without code. A contractor could build: "If someone fills out the quote form, send a welcome email, wait three days, check if they opened it, send a case study if they did and a different follow-up if they did not, wait seven more days, and if still no response, create a follow-up task." That level of conditional logic is unusual at the Starter price point.

ActiveCampaign also supports split conditions: different paths based on whether a contact opened, clicked, or did nothing. That is useful for contractors running seasonal promotions who want to send a second offer to non-openers and a thank-you plus upsell to openers. The automation depth scales with plan tier: Starter has 5 actions per automation, Plus and above have unlimited.

MailerLite's automation covers the basics: welcome sequences, tag-based triggers, time delays, and workflow branching. The no-cost tier allows 3 active automations, Comfort allows 50, and Power allows unlimited. That tiered approach means a contractor starts with simple sequences and expands as needs grow. But the automation builder does not support the conditional branching depth that ActiveCampaign offers. There is no lead scoring, no behavior-based path splitting beyond basic triggers, and no deal-stage automation.

The decision signal: choose ActiveCampaign when you plan to build complex, conditional email sequences with multiple branches and want a visual editor that makes that easy. Choose MailerLite when your email needs are straightforward welcome sequences, tag-triggered follow-ups, and periodic campaigns.

## CRM and contact management

ActiveCampaign includes CRM-lite functionality that MailerLite does not have at all. Contact records in ActiveCampaign show list membership, tags, engagement history, deal stage, custom fields, and email activity. The deal tracking view shows a pipeline with stages (new lead, contacted, quoted, won, lost), deal values, and owner assignments. For a contractor who wants to see where each lead is in the buying process and connect that to email automation, ActiveCampaign's CRM is functional out of the box.

MailerLite has no built-in CRM. Contact data in MailerLite includes subscriber information, tags, segments, and engagement metrics, but there is no pipeline, no deal stages, and no sales workflow management. A contractor using MailerLite would need a separate CRM for pipeline management, or they would track deals manually. For a head-to-head against a CRM-first tool, see [MailerLite vs Pipeline CRM](/mailerlite-vs-pipeline-crm/).

This matters for contractors who want one system for email marketing and basic contact management. If you currently use a spreadsheet or a basic CRM alongside your email tool and want to consolidate, ActiveCampaign's built-in CRM can replace the spreadsheet. MailerLite cannot. For a comparison of CRM-first platforms, see [ActiveCampaign vs Keap](/activecampaign-vs-keap/) or the [best CRM for contractors](/best-crm-for-contractors/) roundup.

## Segmentation and lead scoring

ActiveCampaign handles segmentation through tags, lists, custom fields, and deal stages. A contact can be tagged "spring-promo-opened" and "service-area-north" and "estimated-over-5k" and you can send a follow-up to the intersection of all three. That multi-condition segmentation is available on Starter and becomes more granular on higher plans.

Lead scoring in ActiveCampaign assigns points based on email engagement, page visits, form submissions, and other behaviors. When a contact reaches a threshold, they can trigger an automation or move to a different list. For a contractor getting regular website leads, this separates people who fill out a form and disappear from people who fill out a form and read every follow-up email.

MailerLite handles segmentation through tags, segments, and custom fields. The segmentation is capable for email-centric criteria: which contacts opened which campaigns, which tags they have, which forms they filled out. But there is no lead scoring, no behavior-based scoring, and no automatic list movement based on engagement thresholds.

The decision signal: ActiveCampaign for event-driven segmentation based on what contacts do inside emails and on your website, plus lead scoring to prioritize follow-up calls. MailerLite for basic tag-based segmentation that covers most small-list needs.

## Email editor and template quality

MailerLite has the cleaner email editor. The drag-and-drop builder is intuitive, produces mobile-friendly emails quickly, and the block-based approach makes it easy to build a professional newsletter without design experience. The template library is smaller than some competitors, but the editor itself compensates with speed and ease of use.

ActiveCampaign's email editor is capable but takes more time to produce a finished email. The template selection is more limited on lower tiers, and the drag-and-drop builder has a steeper learning curve. For contractors who send complex, designed newsletters regularly, this is a minor friction point. For contractors who mainly send text-focused follow-up sequences and automated campaigns, the editor is adequate.

The decision signal: if the email editor experience matters as much as the automation, and you want polished emails built quickly, MailerLite is the better choice. If the automation builder matters more than the email editor, ActiveCampaign's editor gets the job done.

## No-cost tier vs no no-cost tier

This is the biggest structural difference between the two platforms.

MailerLite offers a genuine no-cost tier. It is not a trial. A contractor with 200 past customers and leads can use MailerLite indefinitely at $0, sending up to 2,500 emails per month. The no-cost tier includes the drag-and-drop editor, 1 landing page, 3 active forms, 3 active automations, and subscriber management. The limitation is volume: 250 subscribers, 2,500 emails, and capped feature counts.

ActiveCampaign does not offer a no-cost tier. The 14-day free trial gives full access to test automations and features, but it expires. After 14 days, the contractor must move to a paid plan starting at $15/mo for 1,000 contacts when billed annually.

ActiveCampaign and MailerLite are both email marketing platforms designed to manage campaigns, contacts, and automation workflows. The fundamental difference is that MailerLite allows businesses to send emails at no monthly cost under its entry-level tier, while ActiveCampaign requires a paid subscription after the trial period ends.

For most contractors, both integration libraries cover the basics: WordPress, Zapier, and forms. ## Where ActiveCampaign falls short

ActiveCampaign's biggest drawback is the lack of a no-cost tier. A contractor with 150 past customers and a small newsletter list has to pay $15/mo on ActiveCampaign or use MailerLite at no cost. For very small lists, MailerLite's entry-level tier is a genuine advantage that ActiveCampaign cannot match.

The second drawback is pricing complexity. ActiveCampaign charges by contact count, and the monthly cost increases as the list grows. A contractor whose list grows from 1,000 to 5,000 contacts sees a significant monthly increase. The platform is designed to grow with you, but the price grows too.

The third drawback is the email editor. ActiveCampaign offers fewer free templates than MailerLite, and the drag-and-drop builder takes more time to produce a finished email. For contractors who send designed newsletters and care about the editor experience, MailerLite is faster and cleaner.

## Where MailerLite falls short

MailerLite's biggest limitation is automation depth. The automation builder handles welcome sequences, tag-based triggers, and time delays, but it does not support the conditional branching, split logic, and behavior-based path splitting that ActiveCampaign offers. For contractors who need complex multi-step sequences, MailerLite will feel restrictive.

The second limitation is the lack of a built-in CRM. There is no pipeline, no deal stages, and no sales workflow management. A contractor using MailerLite for email marketing still needs a separate system for contact management and pipeline tracking.

The third limitation is no lead scoring. MailerLite does not assign points based on engagement behavior, and there is no automatic way to separate hot leads from passive subscribers. For contractors getting regular inbound leads who want to prioritize follow-up calls based on engagement, ActiveCampaign's lead scoring is a meaningful differentiator.

## How to choose

Start with three questions:

1. **Do you need complex automation?** If yes, ActiveCampaign. If your email sequences need conditional branches, split logic, and behavior-based paths, ActiveCampaign's visual builder is the right tool. If your sequences are simple welcome emails and periodic follow-ups, MailerLite handles that.

2. **Do you need a no-cost tier?** If your list is under 250 subscribers and you want no monthly cost indefinitely, MailerLite is the only option. ActiveCampaign's 14-day trial expires.

3. **Do you need CRM and lead scoring?** If you want contact records, deal tracking, pipeline stages, and engagement-based lead scoring in one platform, ActiveCampaign includes that. MailerLite does not.

If all three answers point to MailerLite (simple automation, small list, no CRM needed), pick MailerLite. If any answer points to ActiveCampaign (complex automation, growing list, CRM needed), the $15/mo entry point is justified by the feature gap.

## Final verdict

ActiveCampaign and MailerLite overlap in category but not in depth. ActiveCampaign is the automation platform with CRM-lite contact management, lead scoring, and a visual sequence builder that handles complex conditional logic. MailerLite is the simpler email tool with a no-cost tier, a cleaner editor, and a lower paid entry point.

ActiveCampaign is for the contractor whose pain is follow-up: managing automated sequences, scoring leads, and tracking deals through a pipeline alongside email campaigns. The $15/mo entry price is the lowest in the category for a platform with this feature set. It makes sense when the business has outgrown basic newsletter tools and needs real automation depth.

MailerLite is for the contractor whose pain is cost and simplicity: a small list, monthly newsletters, and a tool that does not get in the way. The no-cost tier is permanent, not a trial. The paid tiers are affordable. The editor is clean. It makes sense when the list is small, the budget is tight, and the contractor values ease of use over automation complexity.
