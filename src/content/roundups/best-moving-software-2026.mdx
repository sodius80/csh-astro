## Do You Need This Yet?

Moving company software pays for itself when you're managing enough crews that spreadsheets can't keep up with inventory, scheduling conflicts, and billing disputes. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a single-truck operation booking moves through phone calls and paper BOLs. Basic scheduling apps still work fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're losing money on lost inventory claims, dispatch conflicts between trucks, or paperwork delays that kill customer satisfaction.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Elromco. The all-in-one platform covers CRM to electronic BOL and invoicing without forcing you to juggle multiple subscriptions, and the 400+ moving company user base proves it handles real-world workflows.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Elromco – Best Overall

<p>Elromco delivers the complete moving company tech stack in one platform. CRM, quoting, dispatch, electronic BOL, invoicing, and accounting all connect without third-party integrations. Over 400 moving companies use it as their primary system.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The all-in-one approach eliminates the software juggling most moving companies deal with. Electronic BOL system is built specifically for moving workflows, not adapted from general logistics software.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Custom pricing only with no transparent rates published. The comprehensive feature set may overwhelm single-truck operations that just need basic scheduling.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Professional $289/mo, Enterprise $399/mo. Published on website.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Moving companies wanting complete platform from lead capture to final accounting</p>

### 2. SmartMoving – Established Brand

<p>SmartMoving focuses on operations management and customer relations with real-time business insights. The platform has built brand recognition in the moving industry and delivers core CRM functionality moving companies need.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Established brand with industry recognition gives confidence to moving companies making their first software purchase. Real-time business insights help with decision making during busy seasons.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Custom pricing with no public rates makes budgeting difficult. May lack advanced features that newer platforms offer.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote; SmartMoving lists plan features but requires a demo for current dollar pricing.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Established moving companies wanting recognized platform with proven track record</p>

### 3. Movegistics – Mobile-First

<p>Movegistics emphasizes mobile workflows with apps for onsite estimates and electronic bill of lading. The platform includes 2-way texting, live chat, and QuickBooks integration to connect with existing accounting systems.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Mobile estimates let crews quote onsite without returning to office. Electronic BOL system reduces paperwork delays that cost moving companies time and customer satisfaction.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Custom pricing only with no transparency. Less brand recognition than SmartMoving may concern companies wanting established vendor.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Plan-based pricing is not published as fixed dollars; request a quote for AI survey packs or the full CRM plan. Text messaging is listed at $50 per 1,000 messages.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Moving companies wanting mobile apps for field estimates and electronic documentation</p>

### 4. MoversTech CRM – Budget Option

<p>MoversTech CRM targets smaller moving companies with daily operations management, automated communication, and customer experience tools. The platform keeps things straightforward for companies that don't need advanced features.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Designed specifically for smaller moving operations without overwhelming feature bloat. Automated communication reduces manual follow-up that smaller companies often struggle with.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Less feature depth than full platforms means growing companies may outgrow it quickly. Still uses custom pricing despite targeting budget-conscious buyers.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Predictable monthly plans are advertised, but dollar amounts are not published; request Growth or Professional plan pricing.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Smaller moving companies on tight budgets needing basic CRM and dispatch</p>

### 5. Pipedrive – General CRM

<p>Pipedrive offers lead management, workflow automation, and communication tools in a general CRM platform. Easy to use for sales-focused moving companies but lacks moving-specific features like dispatch or inventory tracking.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Transparent per-user pricing model lets companies budget accurately. Easy interface means faster team adoption without extensive training.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Not moving-specific means missing dispatch features, inventory tracking, and electronic BOL that moving companies actually need daily.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Lite starts at $14/seat/month billed annually; Growth is $39/seat/month billed annually. Pipedrive offers a 14-day trial.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Moving companies wanting scalable CRM but handling dispatch and inventory with other tools</p>
