<p>Most small HVAC companies do not need the biggest HVAC software platform on the market. They need something the office and the techs will actually use every day.</p>

<p>That usually comes down to a few practical things:</p>

<ul>
<li>a dispatch board that stays organized instead of becoming a daily mess</li>
<li>a clean handoff from estimate to job to invoice</li>
<li>customer communication that does not depend on someone remembering every follow-up</li>
<li>enough visibility into maintenance agreements, technician workload, and cash flow</li>
</ul>

<p>That gap is smaller than many "best HVAC software" articles make it sound.</p>

<p>If you are still running a small residential shop with a couple of techs, a simple calendar, QuickBooks, and a reliable invoicing flow may be enough for now. Plenty of contractors do not have a software problem yet. They have a consistency problem.</p>

<p>Dedicated HVAC software starts to make sense when one or more of these becomes true:</p>

<ol>
<li>dispatch gets sloppy because jobs live in texts and phone calls</li>
<li>service agreement tracking needs to be part of the workflow, not an afterthought</li>
<li>quotes, jobs, and invoices keep getting entered by hand</li>
<li>customer communication is inconsistent enough to hurt reviews</li>
<li>the company is getting big enough that reporting and process matter more than speed alone</li>
</ol>

<p>That is the frame for this guide.</p>

<p>For this Phase 3 update, we reviewed current pricing, compared feature sets, and cross-checked standardized CSH reviews for Jobber, Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan, FieldEdge, and Service Fusion to separate small-HVAC fit from larger-office fit.</p>

## Do You Need This Yet?

<p>Maybe not. If the shop is still simple and under control, there is no prize for adding another system.</p>

<div class="need-check">
<p class="need-do">You probably do if:</p>
<ul class="need-list">
<li>jobs get double-booked, delayed, or lost in the shuffle</li>
<li>you are doing enough maintenance work that agreement tracking is becoming messy</li>
<li>someone in the office is retyping the same information into multiple systems</li>
<li>you want automated review requests and customer follow-ups</li>
<li>you are adding techs and need visibility into who is doing what</li>
</ul>
<p class="need-dont">You may not yet if:</p>
<ul class="need-list">
<li>you are still running 1-2 techs with a calendar and QuickBooks</li>
<li>most of your work is straightforward residential service calls</li>
<li>you do not have service agreements or maintenance plans yet</li>
<li>a simple dispatch board or shared calendar is still holding together</li>
</ul>
</div>

## How to Choose

<p>Start with the bottleneck you feel every week:</p>

<p><strong>If you need the simplest path from lead to invoice:</strong> Jobber is the clean all-in-one choice for small crews. It covers scheduling, quoting, dispatching, invoicing, payments, and recurring maintenance scheduling without acting like a full HVAC operations suite. For a smaller team, that restraint is useful because the interface stays simple.</p>

<p><strong>If customer communication and reviews are your growth engine:</strong> Housecall Pro is the stronger fit. It has built-in automated review requests, appointment reminders, mobile field tools, and customer equipment tracking on higher plans. If new work comes from Google reviews and word-of-mouth, those follow-ups matter more than another back-office setting.</p>

<p><strong>If you are scaling past 10 techs:</strong> ServiceTitan is the enterprise option. It has dispatch optimization, flat-rate pricebook tools, marketing attribution, and deeper reporting. Setup is sales-led, and pricing and contract terms come through a sales quote. Wait on it until you have outgrown everything else.</p>

<p><strong>If you need HVAC-specific service agreement management:</strong> FieldEdge is purpose-built for HVAC. It handles equipment tracking per location, service agreements, and flat-rate pricing in the field. The tradeoff: the mobile experience is less polished, and quote-only pricing means you need sales confirmation.</p>

## Pricing Reality for HVAC Buyers

<p>HVAC software pricing is hard to compare because the starter plan is often not the plan a growing shop ends up using. Jobber and Housecall Pro publish prices and free trials, but the HVAC workflow you care about may sit behind higher-tier features such as QuickBooks, job costing, customer equipment, reminders, marketing, or review tools. ServiceTitan and FieldEdge require pricing conversations, so judge the quote against implementation lift and office capacity as well as the monthly number.</p>

<p>For a small HVAC company, start with user math: how many people need to log in every week? A two-tech company with one dispatcher can often stay on Jobber or Housecall Pro without much complexity. A larger service department with dispatchers, CSR roles, managers, comfort advisors, installers, and technicians has to model seats, included users, mobile licenses, onboarding, support, add-ons, payment processing, and annual commitments before comparing vendors.</p>

<p>Watch Service Fusion closely. Its public page emphasizes unlimited users, month-to-month availability, annual savings, onboarding, and support, but the current pricing-page extraction did not expose stable plan dollar amounts. That makes a fixed price from secondary sources risky. Treat Service Fusion as a demo-confirmed pricing conversation unless the current official page shows exact monthly and annual totals during your visit.</p>

<p>Separate software cost from payment processing cost, too. HVAC shops taking deposits, final payments, and membership renewals through the platform should ask for card-present, keyed, ACH, chargeback, and payout timing details. A lower monthly plan can still cost more if payment fees are high or the bookkeeper has to clean up sync errors every week.</p>

## Demo Checklist for HVAC Shops

<p>Do not let the demo live in a perfect sample account. Make each vendor walk through a normal HVAC day: a no-cool call comes in, the dispatcher assigns the closest tech, the tech reviews equipment history, the customer approves a repair, the office invoices the job, the payment syncs to accounting, and the maintenance-agreement reminder is scheduled. If that chain is hard to follow, the feature list is not enough.</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Dispatch:</strong> Can the office see technician availability, job status, travel time, and urgent calls without opening three screens?</li>
<li><strong>Equipment:</strong> Can the tech see past systems, model numbers, warranties, photos, prior recommendations, and service-agreement coverage before arriving?</li>
<li><strong>Pricing:</strong> Can the company use a pricebook or flat-rate menu without creating inconsistent tech-by-tech pricing?</li>
<li><strong>Accounting:</strong> Does the QuickBooks sync handle customers, invoices, payments, items, classes, and corrections in the way the bookkeeper needs?</li>
<li><strong>Customer experience:</strong> Can the platform send confirmations, on-my-way texts, review requests, estimates, invoices, and membership reminders without manual copy-paste work?</li>
</ul>

## Stage-Based Shortlist

<p><strong>One to five techs:</strong> Look at Jobber first if clean operations and lower entry cost matter most. Look at Housecall Pro first if customer communication, review requests, and residential service presentation matter more.</p>

<p><strong>Five to fifteen techs:</strong> Compare Housecall Pro, FieldEdge, and Service Fusion. At this size, service agreements, QuickBooks depth, user math, mobile adoption, and reporting start to matter more than the cheapest entry plan.</p>

<p><strong>Fifteen-plus techs or a dedicated call center:</strong> Bring ServiceTitan into the conversation when the business needs stronger dispatch controls, marketing attribution, call booking, pricebook governance, memberships, reporting, and implementation support. It is not the cheapest path, but it can be the more complete operational fit once the office is large enough to use it.</p>

## Product Reviews

### 1. Jobber

<p>Jobber is a practical all-in-one for small HVAC crews that want the day to run cleaner. Scheduling, quoting, dispatching, invoicing, payments, and recurring maintenance scheduling all live in one interface, and the setup does not overwhelm your techs.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The scheduling board is drag-and-drop, color-coded by job type, and updated in real time. Quotes turn into jobs and then invoices without re-entering data. Client communication covers confirmations, on-my-way texts, and follow-up requests.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Jobber is not deeply HVAC-specific. It supports recurring maintenance jobs and invoices, but it does not replace a full HVAC equipment module, service-agreement system, or dedicated flat-rate pricebook. If those are core to the business, you will eventually outgrow it.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core starts at $29/month with annual billing or $49 month-to-month. Connect is $99/month annually or $139 monthly, Grow is $149/month annually or $199 monthly, and Plus is $529/month annually or $699 monthly. Extra users are $29/user/month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small HVAC companies (1-10 techs) doing residential service work who need clean daily operations more than trade-specific features.</p>

### 2. Housecall Pro

<p>Housecall Pro is the Jobber alternative to watch when customer experience matters most. Its pricing and feature pages emphasize review management, customer communication, online booking, mobile field work, and customer equipment tracking on higher plans.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Techs can access schedules, update job statuses, collect payments, and send photos from the field. Automated review requests sit inside the post-job workflow, which can support local reputation growth.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The lowest published price is annual Basic. Essentials, MAX, extra users, and add-ons can raise the total quickly, and the scheduling board is functional but not as polished as Jobber's.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic is $59/month annually or $79 month-to-month; Essentials is $149/month annually or $189 monthly; Max is $299/month annually or $329 monthly.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> HVAC companies where customer reviews and field technician experience matter more than the absolute lowest price.</p>

### 3. ServiceTitan

<p>ServiceTitan is the enterprise option in this list. It is built for HVAC companies doing $1.5M+ in revenue with 10+ techs. The analytics, marketing attribution, and flat-rate pricebook are deep, but pricing and rollout need sales confirmation.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Marketing attribution ties campaigns and trackable phone numbers back to booked-job revenue. The dispatch board supports schedule optimization and route planning. Flat-rate pricing books with upsell prompts are built into the tech mobile app. Customer lifetime value tracking helps show which customers are worth investing in.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Implementation is sales-led, so this is not a same-day self-service setup. Pricing is quote-only, and contract terms require sales confirmation. For small shops, it is overkill.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote. ServiceTitan does not publish a current public dollar rate card; confirm technician counts, modules, implementation, and contract terms directly.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> HVAC companies with 10+ techs doing $1.5M+ revenue who need enterprise-grade analytics and have outgrown simpler tools.</p>

### 4. FieldEdge

<p>FieldEdge is the HVAC-specific pick. It handles equipment tracking per location, service agreements, and flat-rate pricing in the field.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Equipment history per customer location helps techs see what systems they are servicing before they arrive. The workflow includes service agreement management. Flat-rate pricing books integrate with the tech mobile app.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The mobile experience is not as polished as Jobber or Housecall Pro. Implementation takes more setup time, and FieldEdge does not publish fixed dollar pricing on its current pricing page.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote. FieldEdge lists Select, Premier, and Elite plans with 2, 4, and 6 included mobile licenses respectively, but the actual dollar price varies by team size and add-ons and requires a demo.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> HVAC companies with service agreement programs that need per-location equipment tracking and flat-rate pricing integration.</p>

### 5. Service Fusion

<p>Service Fusion sits between lighter SMB field-service tools and enterprise platforms for complexity and pricing. It gives growing HVAC companies more trade-specific depth than many generalist tools without the heaviest enterprise footprint.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> It has a useful mix of scheduling, dispatching, and invoicing, plus HVAC-specific features like service agreement tracking. The pricing is mid-range and includes unlimited users.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> It is not as polished as the top contenders. Reviews are mixed on mobile app quality and customer support responsiveness.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Service Fusion publishes Starter, Plus, and Pro tiers, but the current pricing page content available to crawlers does not expose stable dollar amounts. Plus and Pro add features such as inventory, job costing, integrated voice/text, API access, custom documents, e-signatures, customer web portal, and recurring billing. All plans include unlimited users, with GPS fleet tracking and other tools sold as add-ons. Confirm current annual and monthly totals directly before buying.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Growing HVAC companies that need more than basic scheduling but are not ready for ServiceTitan-level complexity.</p>

## Bottom Line

<p>Short version:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Jobber</strong> is the cleanest all-in-one for small crews. Start here if you want scheduling, quoting, and invoicing in one place without HVAC-specific complexity.</li>
<li><strong>Housecall Pro</strong> wins on customer communication and the field tech mobile experience. Choose it if reviews and customer follow-ups drive your growth.</li>
<li><strong>ServiceTitan</strong> is the enterprise option. Only consider it when you have 10+ techs and have outgrown simpler tools.</li>
<li><strong>FieldEdge</strong> is the HVAC-specific play. Use it when service agreements and equipment tracking are core to your business model.</li>
</ul>

<p>For most small HVAC contractors, the shortlist should start with Jobber or Housecall Pro. ServiceTitan and FieldEdge matter once the business has grown past what simpler tools can handle.</p>

## FAQ

### Should I use a general field-service tool or HVAC-specific software?

<p>For most small HVAC companies, a general field-service tool like Jobber covers the essentials well. You only need HVAC-specific software if you have service agreements, equipment tracking requirements, or flat-rate pricing needs that general tools cannot handle.</p>

### Can a small HVAC shop start with Jobber?

<p>Yes. Jobber is designed for small service businesses and handles scheduling, quoting, dispatching, invoicing, and payments cleanly. Many HVAC companies start here and only upgrade when they outgrow it.</p>

### What makes Housecall Pro valuable for HVAC customer experience?

<p>Built-in automated review requests, appointment reminders, and mobile field tools. If your business grows through Google reviews and word-of-mouth, these features can support local reputation and follow-up consistency.</p>

### When would I consider FieldEdge?

<p>When you need HVAC-specific features like per-location equipment history, service agreement management, and flat-rate pricing books integrated into the field tech workflow.</p>

### Is ServiceTitan a good fit for small HVAC shops?

<p>Generally no. ServiceTitan is built for larger HVAC companies with roughly 10+ techs and seven-figure revenue. The implementation complexity and quote-only pricing make it overkill for smaller operations.</p>
