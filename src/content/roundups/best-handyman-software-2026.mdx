## Do You Need This Yet?

Handyman software pays for itself when you're doing enough volume that manual scheduling and invoicing starts eating into billable hours. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a solo handyman handling 3-5 jobs per week and tracking everything in your phone notes. Simple invoicing apps still work fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're spending more time on paperwork than repairs, missing appointments, or losing track of which customers still owe money.

If you're in the middle, growing but not overwhelmed, start with Jobber. Its current pricing page lists Core from $29/month, with higher tiers adding users and automation as you grow.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Jobber - Best overall value

<p>Jobber delivers solid field service management without breaking the budget. Covers scheduling, estimates, invoicing, client management, and route optimization in one package. Built for service trades who need dependable software that works.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Users consistently report that Jobber rarely crashes or glitches, making it dependable for daily operations. Route optimization actually cuts drive time between jobs.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Takes a general FSM approach rather than handyman-specific features. You'll pay for some capabilities you might not use.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core starts at $29/mo on Jobber's current pricing page. Connect, Grow, and Plus tiers add more users and automation, and annual/promotional pricing changes the effective first-year cost.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Handymen who want affordable, reliable FSM that scales as they grow</p>

### 2. Housecall Pro - Best for customer communication

<p>Housecall Pro excels at keeping customers in the loop with two-way SMS, email updates, and online booking that lets clients schedule themselves. Strong dispatch and invoicing round out the package.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Two-way SMS communication keeps customers informed throughout jobs without constant phone calls. Online booking reduces scheduling back-and-forth.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Costs more than Jobber without delivering clearly superior value. Feature set can feel excessive for simple handyman operations.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic is listed at $59/mo when billed annually or $79 month-to-month. Essentials is $149/mo annual or $189 month-to-month; Max is $299/mo annual or $329 month-to-month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Handymen who want strong customer communication and online booking</p>

### 3. Projul - Best for growth planning

<p>Projul bridges the gap between handyman work and full construction projects. Estimating, scheduling, project management, and job costing tools work for both small repairs and larger jobs.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Handles both current handyman tasks and future construction work without requiring a platform switch. Strong estimating tools help price jobs accurately.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Annual pricing of $4,788 makes it expensive for solo handymen. Many features are overkill for basic repair work.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core starts at $4,788/year. Core+ and Pro cost more, but Projul keeps the model annual and does not charge per user.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Growing handymen planning to become full contractors</p>

### 4. Workiz - Best for lead integration

<p>Workiz focuses on lead management with integrations for major lead platforms. Scheduling, dispatch, and invoicing handle the rest of your workflow once leads come in.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Built specifically for businesses that get leads from Angi, Thumbtack, and Google Local Services. Good lead tracking and follow-up tools.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Less established brand recognition than competitors. Workiz now publishes paid tiers, and the entry paid plan is much higher than Jobber or Housecall Pro for a solo handyman.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Workiz lists Kickstart at $225/month with annual billing for the first 3 users, Standard at $275/month annual for the first 5 users, and Pro at $325/month annual for the first 5 users. Ultimate remains quote-based.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Handymen who get leads from Angi, Thumbtack, Google Local Services</p>

### 5. Basic Invoicing Tools - Cheapest option

<p>QuickBooks and Square offer basic invoicing and payment collection for handymen who don't need scheduling or job management. Works for very small operations.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Cheapest entry point if you only need invoicing: Square can start with no monthly subscription, while QuickBooks Simple Start uses promotional and standard rates. Simple invoicing and payment collection without complexity.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No scheduling, dispatch, or job management features. Most handymen outgrow these tools within a year.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> QuickBooks Simple Start is currently advertised at $19/mo for the first three months before the standard $38/mo rate. Square Invoices is included in Square Free with payment processing fees, while Square Plus is $49/mo per location with a 30-day trial.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Solo handymen who just need invoicing</p>

## FAQ

<p><strong>Do I need handyman-specific software or can I use general field service management?</strong></p>
<p>General FSM works fine for most handymen. The job workflow is similar to other service trades. Handyman-specific features are nice but not essential.</p>

<p><strong>What's the difference between flat pricing and per-user pricing?</strong></p>
<p>Flat pricing costs the same whether you have 1 or 10 users. Per-user pricing scales up as you add team members. Flat is better for growing teams, per-user is fairer for solo operations.</p>

<p><strong>Should I start with a basic invoicing tool and upgrade later?</strong></p>
<p>Only if you're truly solo and plan to stay that way. Most handymen outgrow basic invoicing within a year. Starting with proper FSM software avoids the hassle of switching later.</p>
