## Do You Need This Yet?

A CRM with QuickBooks integration pays for itself when you're sending enough quotes and invoices that double data entry is stealing an hour or more each week. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're billing a handful of repeat customers with the same scope each month. Your current workflow is fine until volume or complexity increases.
- **You need it now** if you're entering customer info into two systems, invoices aren't matching your books, or your accountant is asking for data you can't easily export.

If you're in the middle, growing but stable, start with Jobber. It combines CRM, scheduling, and invoicing in one platform, quote follow-up automation can revive stale quotes, and the QuickBooks Online sync reduces manual accounting entry on eligible plans.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Jobber -- Best All-in-One CRM + Field Service


<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The quote follow-up automation. Set it once and Jobber can send a follow-up text after a quote goes out with no response. Jobber's current QuickBooks help docs describe the newer QuickBooks Online sync as one-way from Jobber to QuickBooks for clients, products and services, invoices, payments, refunds, tips, payouts, and optional timesheets.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No visual pipeline view like a kanban board showing leads moving through stages. Lead source tracking is basic. You cannot easily tie marketing spend to revenue. If you want deep sales analytics, you need a separate tool.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core starts at $29/month with annual billing or $49 month-to-month. QuickBooks sync is a higher-tier feature, so most contractors comparing accounting integration should price Connect, Grow, or Plus based on user count, billing term, and add-ons.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Service contractors who want CRM functionality built into their scheduling and invoicing workflow without managing a separate system.</p>

### 2. Housecall Pro -- Best for Lead Follow-Up in Residential


<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Automated review requests and follow-up sequences help keep customers engaged after the first job. The visual pipeline means you can see at a glance how many quotes are pending, approved, or lost.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> More expensive than Jobber at every tier. The Android app is weaker than iOS, which matters for mixed teams. Some features require higher-tier plans.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic is $59/month annually or $79 month-to-month; Essentials is $149/month annually or $189 monthly; MAX is $299/month annually or $329 monthly, with additional users at $35/month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Residential service contractors with significant inbound lead volume who want pipeline visibility and lead source attribution.</p>

### 3. Method CRM -- Best for QuickBooks-First Operations


<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Method is built around QuickBooks rather than treating accounting as an add-on integration. The current Method pricing page emphasizes patented two-way sync for QuickBooks Online and Desktop, plus customer portals, lead management, estimates, invoices, and workflow automation depending on tier.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No native field service scheduling or dispatching. If you need to send crews to job sites, you still need a separate field service tool. The interface is functional but dated. The mobile app is limited compared to Jobber or Housecall Pro.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> CRM QuickStart is $35/user/month, CRM Pro is $59/user/month, and CRM Enterprise is $97/user/month. Method also advertises promotional pricing and a 10-day no-card trial, so compare regular price rather than only the temporary discount.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Contractors who live in QuickBooks and want a CRM that mirrors their accounting data structure exactly.</p>

### 4. ServiceTitan -- Best for Enterprise CRM + Marketing ROI

ServiceTitan's Marketing Pro Ads pages describe Google Ads, Google Analytics, Dynamic Call Tracking, and full-funnel marketing analytics. <p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Expensive custom pricing with no public dollar rate card. Long onboarding and training required. Overkill for contractors with under 15 techs. CRM features are buried inside a massive platform.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote. ServiceTitan does not publish a current public dollar rate card; confirm modules, implementation, user assumptions, and QuickBooks sync requirements in writing.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Large service contractors who need CRM, marketing attribution, and enterprise reporting tied to QuickBooks at scale.</p>

## FAQ

<p><strong>Do I need a separate CRM if my field service app has customer management?</strong></p>
<p>For most small contractors, Jobber or Housecall Pro's built-in customer management is sufficient. You need a dedicated CRM only if you have a sales team doing complex pipeline management or multiple marketing campaigns.</p>

<p><strong>How does the QuickBooks integration actually work?</strong></p>
<p>Sync direction depends on the vendor and the version of the integration. Jobber's newer QuickBooks Online integration is mainly one-way from Jobber to QuickBooks, while Housecall Pro's current help docs also describe the main ongoing sync as Housecall Pro to QuickBooks. Method is more QuickBooks-native because it is designed around QuickBooks Online and Desktop rather than treating accounting as a small add-on.</p>

<p><strong>Can I use Method CRM without QuickBooks?</strong></p>
<p>Method is designed specifically for QuickBooks. Without QuickBooks, there is little reason to choose it over a general CRM.</p>

<p><strong>Will a CRM really help me close more jobs?</strong></p>
<p>If your current follow-up process is "I meant to call them back," then yes. Automated quote follow-ups can recover jobs that would otherwise go cold, but the lift depends on lead quality, response speed, and close process.</p>
