## Do You Need This Yet?

Pool & spa software pays for itself when you're running enough routes that manual chemical logs and drive-time planning start stealing hours from your week. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're servicing just a handful of pools and can remember the chemical routine from memory. A paper log and basic GPS still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're spending two hours a day planning routes that software could optimize in minutes, or if missed chemical readings are turning into callbacks.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Skimmer. It's built for pool service routes, with an LSI calculator and chemical dosage tracking tied to each body of water, plus route optimization that cuts drive time by up to 25%.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Skimmer – Best for Pool Service Routes

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Built-in LSI calculator and chemical dosage tracking tied to each body of water, plus one-click route optimization that cuts drive time by up to 25%. The platform was designed by pool pros who understand the difference between alkalinity and pH.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Skimmer recently doubled pricing from $1 to $2 per serviced location for many users. A longtime user with 129 clients wrote on Capterra, "Longtime Skimmer user and I'm leaving! My per-location monthly fee is going from $1 to $2 - a 100% rise." High-volume operators also report random app crashes on Android after recent updates.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> $1 per serviced location/month on Getting Started (starts at $49/month) or $2 per serviced location/month on Scaling Up (starts at $98/month). Skimmer bills once per serviced location even if that address has multiple bodies of water.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Dedicated pool service companies running 20 to 1,000+ weekly stops that want pool-native workflows.</p>

### 2. Jobber – Best for Mixed-Service Contractors

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Flexible scheduling and invoicing across multiple trade types, making it ideal for contractors who offer pool service alongside landscaping, pressure washing, or handyman work. The interface is clean and field crews can learn it quickly.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No built-in chemical tracking or water test logging; contractors must use custom text fields. A Reddit contractor noted, "Paying 199/mo for jobber and it has definitely helped me organize... but 199 for something that isn't easy to send pictures." Two-way client texting is locked behind the Grow plan at $239/month.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Plans start at $29/month under Jobber's annual promotional pricing, with standard monthly and team pricing varying by plan. Use Jobber's pricing page for the current Core, Connect, Grow, and Plus totals before buying.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Contractors who run pool service as one of several offerings and need a single platform for mixed trades.</p>

### 3. Housecall Pro – Best for Marketing-Heavy Multi-Service Teams

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Automated review requests, Google Local Services Ads integration, and built-in postcard/email marketing that helps contractors generate referrals. The platform focuses on growing your customer base.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No chemical tracking, no LSI calculator, no pool equipment database. Contractors on Capterra call the Basic plan "essentially useless" because it lacks QuickBooks sync, GPS tracking, and route optimization. A Trustpilot reviewer wrote, "When we called, we couldn't talk to a human anymore for tech support. They would only relate through AI."</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic is $59/month billed annually or $79 month-to-month; Essentials is $149/month annually or $189 month-to-month; MAX is $299/month annually or $329 month-to-month, with additional users at $35/month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Growing home service businesses that do pool work alongside HVAC, plumbing, or cleaning and need strong marketing automation.</p>

### 4. Pool Office Manager – Best Purpose-Built Pool Platform

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Designed exclusively for seasonal pool service companies since 2014, with a built-in chemical calculator, route optimization, and the ability to turn users on and off to reduce off-season bills.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Very limited verified review presence makes broad quality assessment difficult. Mobile app receives low ratings. Additional users cost $25/month after the first user. Compared to Skimmer, it has a smaller public review footprint and less modern UX polish.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> $125/month first user | $25/month additional users.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Medium to large pool service companies (30 to 200 pools) that want pool-specific software and don't need a polished consumer-grade mobile app.</p>

### 5. ServiceTitan – Best for Large Multi-Location Enterprises

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Deep analytics, enterprise CRM, and marketing automation built for large field service operations with complex dispatch needs. The reporting capabilities are unmatched for large operations.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> ServiceTitan uses sales-quoted per-technician pricing, which can be expensive for pool contractors compared with pool-native tools. The platform is not built for pool-specific workflows like chemical tracking or pool construction draw schedules, so a pool company needs to confirm the enterprise FSM depth justifies the quote.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote; ServiceTitan publishes per-technician pricing but not dollar amounts.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Large pool and spa enterprises with 20+ employees or multiple locations that need enterprise-grade reporting and dispatch.</p>

## FAQ

<p><strong>Do I need pool-specific software or can general field service software work?</strong></p>
<p>It depends on your business. If you only do pool service and track chemicals regularly, pool-specific software like Skimmer saves hours per week. If you do pool work alongside other trades, general platforms like Jobber or Housecall Pro make more sense.</p>

<p><strong>Why did Skimmer raise prices so much?</strong></p>
<p>Skimmer doubled pricing from $1 to $2 per serviced location for many users in late 2025. They cited infrastructure costs and new features. Some longtime users are switching to alternatives, while others accept the increase because the pool-specific features save them time.</p>

<p><strong>What's the real cost of Housecall Pro for pool contractors?</strong></p>
<p>The Basic plan at $59/month billed annually lacks QuickBooks sync and GPS tracking. Pool contractors that need those features should compare Essentials at $149/month annually ($189 monthly) or MAX at $299/month annually ($329 monthly), plus $35/month for each additional user.</p>
