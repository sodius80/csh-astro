## Do You Need This Yet?

Pool & spa software pays for itself when you're running enough routes that manual chemical logs and drive-time planning start stealing hours from your week. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're servicing just a handful of pools and can remember the chemical routine from memory. A paper log and basic GPS still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're spending two hours a day planning routes that software could optimize in minutes, or if missed chemical readings are turning into callbacks.

If you're in the middle, growing but not overwhelmed, start with Skimmer. Getting Started covers route building and basic service reporting; Scaling Up adds LSI/dosing, LaMotte integration, recurring work orders, route optimization, required checklist items, unlimited service texts, and live phone support.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Skimmer – Best for Pool Service Routes

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Skimmer separates route basics from heavier pool-service operations. Getting Started includes route builder, service reports, customer portal, invoicing, online payments, and photo limits. Scaling Up adds LSI and dosing powered by Orenda, LaMotte integration, recurring work orders, route optimization, required checklist items, unlimited service texts, and live phone support.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The $1/location Getting Started plan has a $49/month minimum and does not include Scaling Up features such as route optimization, quotes/estimates, job management, recurring work orders, unlimited service texts, required checklist items, LaMotte integration, or live phone support. Enterprise pricing is custom above 1,000 pools.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Getting Started is $1 per serviced location/month with a $49/month minimum and 49 included locations. Scaling Up is $2 per serviced location/month with a $98/month minimum, 49 included locations, and a first-month-free promo for new users. Skimmer bills once per serviced location even if that address has multiple bodies of water.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Dedicated pool service companies up to 1,000 serviced locations on published plans; Enterprise pricing is custom above 1,000 pools.</p>

### 2. Jobber – Best for Mixed-Service Contractors

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Flexible scheduling and invoicing across multiple trade types, making it ideal for contractors who offer pool service alongside landscaping, pressure washing, or handyman work. The interface is clean and field crews can learn it quickly.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No pool-native chemical tracking or water-test logging. Team features, two-way SMS, and add-ons vary by plan, so confirm the current Core, Connect, Grow, and Plus totals before buying.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core starts at $29/month billed annually or $49 month-to-month. Jobber offers a 14-day no-card trial; team seats and add-ons vary by plan.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Contractors who run pool service as one of several offerings and need a single platform for mixed trades.</p>

### 3. Housecall Pro – Best for Marketing-Heavy Multi-Service Teams

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Automated review requests, Google Local Services Ads integration, and built-in postcard/email marketing that helps contractors generate referrals. The platform focuses on growing your customer base.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No pool-specific chemical tracking, LSI calculator, or pool equipment workflow. Basic does not list QuickBooks sync or employee GPS tracking; contractors needing those features should compare Essentials or MAX.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic is $59/month billed annually or $79 month-to-month; Essentials is $149/month annually or $189 month-to-month; MAX is $299/month annually or $329 month-to-month, with additional users at $35/month. Housecall Pro offers a 14-day no-card trial.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Growing home service businesses that do pool work alongside HVAC, plumbing, or cleaning and need strong marketing automation.</p>

### 4. Pool Office Manager – Best Purpose-Built Pool Platform

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Designed exclusively for seasonal pool service companies since 2014, with a built-in chemical calculator, route optimization, and the ability to turn users on and off to reduce off-season bills.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Very limited verified review presence makes broad quality assessment difficult. Additional users cost $25/month after the first user, QuickBooks integration adds $25/month, and the pricing page lists a $500 activation fee with a dated waiver note that should be verified before buying. Compared to Skimmer, it has a smaller public review footprint and less modern UX polish.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> $125/month for the first user, $25/month for each additional user, and $25/month for QuickBooks integration. The pricing page lists a $500 activation fee with a dated waiver note, so verify whether activation is currently charged before signing.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Medium to large pool service companies (30 to 200 pools) that want pool-specific software and don't need a polished consumer-grade mobile app.</p>

### 5. ServiceTitan – Best for Large Multi-Location Enterprises

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Deep analytics, enterprise CRM, and marketing automation built for large field service operations with complex dispatch needs. The reporting depth is strongest when a pool business also needs broader field-service operations management.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> ServiceTitan uses sales-quoted per-technician pricing, which can be expensive for pool contractors compared with pool-native tools. The platform is not built for pool-specific workflows like chemical tracking or pool construction draw schedules, so a pool company needs to confirm the enterprise FSM depth justifies the quote.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote; ServiceTitan publishes per-technician pricing but not dollar amounts.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Large pool and spa enterprises with 20+ employees or multiple locations that need enterprise-grade reporting and dispatch.</p>

## Bottom Line
