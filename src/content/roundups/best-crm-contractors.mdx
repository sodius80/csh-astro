<p>Most contractors don't think they need a CRM until they lose a job they forgot to follow up on, or realize they have no idea which lead source is actually bringing in good work. A CRM — even a basic one — fixes the follow-up problem and starts giving you data on where your business actually comes from.</p>

<p><strong>Disclosure:</strong> Some links on this page are affiliate links. If you sign up through one, I earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. My recommendations don't change based on that.</p>

<p><strong>Right for:</strong> Service contractors who are losing jobs to forgotten follow-ups and have no visibility into which lead sources produce the best work. Even basic CRM features in a tool like Jobber solve this problem for most small shops.</p>

<p><strong>Not for:</strong> Contractors with a complex B2B sales cycle managing large commercial accounts and multiple decision-makers per deal. That work needs a purpose-built sales CRM, not a field service tool with CRM features tacked on.</p>

## Do You Need This Yet?

CRM software pays for itself when you're sending enough quotes that manual follow-up starts leaking jobs. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're sending a handful of quotes a month and can remember to call back without a list. Your phone's notes app still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you've lost jobs because you forgot to follow up, or you have no idea which lead source actually produces revenue.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Jobber. The built-in quote follow-up automation sends a text a few days after an unanswered quote, which alone recovers enough jobs to cover the subscription while you figure out what else you need.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Jobber – Best All-in-One (CRM + Field Service)

<p>Jobber's CRM features aren't marketed as a CRM, but they function as one for most small service contractors. Every client has a profile with full history: past jobs, quotes, invoices, notes, and communication log. You can see all open quotes that haven't been accepted, filter by status, and follow up from the same platform you dispatch from.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The quote follow-up automation. Set it once and Jobber automatically sends a follow-up text a few days after a quote goes out with no response. Multiple reviewers describe winning jobs they had assumed were dead simply because an automated text went out at the right time.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No visual pipeline view (like a kanban board showing leads moving through stages), or lead source tracking tied to marketing spend. If you want to know which Google Ads campaign is producing profitable jobs, you need a separate analytics layer.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core $29/mo | Connect $149/mo | Grow $199/mo.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Service contractors who want CRM functionality built into their existing scheduling and invoicing workflow without managing a separate system.</p>

### 2. Housecall Pro – Best for Lead Follow-Up in Residential

<p>Housecall Pro has a pipeline feature that shows leads and estimates in stages — new lead, estimate sent, won, lost. For a residential service contractor who gets a lot of inbound leads from Google and needs to track where each one stands, this visual pipeline is more useful than Jobber's list-based quote tracking.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Lead source tracking. You can track which campaign or referral source produced a lead, see conversion rates by source, and start making decisions about where to spend your marketing money based on actual data.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The pricing tiers jump sharply — most contractors end up on the $129/mo Essentials plan to unlock the automated follow-up sequences.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic $49/mo | Essentials $129/mo | MAX $299/mo.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Residential service contractors with significant inbound lead volume who want pipeline visibility and lead source attribution.</p>

### 3. Pipeline CRM – Best Standalone CRM

<p>Pipeline CRM (formerly PipelineDeals) is a dedicated sales CRM that some contractors use alongside their field service software when the built-in CRM features of tools like Jobber aren't enough. It's built for managing deal pipelines, contact histories, and sales activity tracking in more depth than a field service tool provides.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Depth for complex sales pipelines. If you have a dedicated sales rep managing a pipeline separately from field operations, this separation makes sense.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Integration question is the main issue. Pipeline CRM doesn't connect natively to most field service tools, so you'd be managing two separate systems with some data duplication.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Start $25/user/mo | Develop $33/user/mo | Grow $49/user/mo.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Contractors with dedicated sales staff managing a complex B2B pipeline who need more CRM depth than field service tools provide.</p>

### 4. HubSpot Free – Best Free Starting Point

<p>HubSpot's free CRM tier is genuinely capable for basic lead and contact management. You get contact records, deal pipelines, email tracking, and basic reporting at no cost. For a contractor who wants to start managing leads in something more structured than a spreadsheet without spending money yet, it works.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> It's free and capable for basic needs. A solid way to start structured lead management without committing to a paid platform.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> HubSpot doesn't connect to your field service workflow. You'd be managing leads in HubSpot and jobs in a separate tool, which creates data silos. The free tier has limits that push you toward paid tiers quickly.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Free (core CRM) | Starter from $20/mo | Professional from $890/mo.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Contractors who want to start managing leads in a structured system at no cost and aren't ready to commit to an all-in-one platform yet.</p>

### 5. ServiceTitan – Best for Complex Operations

<p>ServiceTitan has the most complete CRM feature set in the field service space — marketing attribution down to the call level, customer lifetime value tracking, automated marketing campaigns, and full pipeline visibility.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Enterprise-grade marketing attribution. For a larger service operation where understanding the ROI on every marketing channel matters, it's the most capable platform on this list.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The price and implementation complexity put it out of reach for most small contractors. This is the CRM conversation for a 15-tech shop doing $3M+ in revenue, not a 5-tech shop trying to stop losing jobs to forgotten follow-ups.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom ($500-$700+/mo).</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Large field service operations where marketing attribution and customer lifetime value data directly inform business decisions.</p>

## Bottom Line

<p>If you're running a small service operation and losing jobs to poor follow-up, <strong>Jobber's</strong> built-in CRM features solve the problem without adding another system. The automated quote follow-up alone is worth it.</p>

<p>If you're doing significant inbound lead volume from multiple sources and want to know which ones actually convert, <strong>Housecall Pro's</strong> pipeline and lead attribution tools give you that visibility.</p>

<p><strong>HubSpot Free</strong> if you want to start structured lead management at no cost. <strong>Pipeline CRM</strong> if you have a dedicated sales operation separate from field work. <strong>ServiceTitan</strong> when you're managing a large operation and need enterprise-grade marketing attribution.</p>
