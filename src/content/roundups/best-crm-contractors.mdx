<p>Most contractors don't think they need a CRM until they lose a job they forgot to follow up on, or realize they have no idea which lead source is actually bringing in good work. A CRM — even a basic one — fixes the follow-up problem and starts giving you data on where your business actually comes from.</p>

<p><strong>Disclosure:</strong> Some links on this page are affiliate links. If you sign up through one, I earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. My recommendations don't change based on that.</p>

<p><strong>Right for:</strong> Service contractors who are losing jobs to forgotten follow-ups and have no visibility into which lead sources produce the best work. Built-in CRM features in a tool like Jobber can solve the first problem for many small shops, while Housecall Pro or ServiceTitan may make more sense when pipeline and attribution depth matter.</p>

<p><strong>Not for:</strong> Contractors with a complex B2B sales cycle managing large commercial accounts and multiple decision-makers per deal. That work needs a purpose-built sales CRM, not a field service tool with CRM features tacked on.</p>

## Do You Need This Yet?

CRM software pays for itself when you're sending enough quotes that manual follow-up starts leaking jobs. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're sending a handful of quotes a month and can remember to call back without a list. Your phone's notes app still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you've lost jobs because you forgot to follow up, or you have no idea which lead source actually produces revenue.

If you're in the middle - growing but not overwhelmed - start with Jobber. The built-in quote follow-up automation on Connect and higher plans reduces the chance that unanswered quotes disappear into a notebook or inbox while you figure out what else you need.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Jobber – Best All-in-One (CRM + Field Service)

<p>Jobber's CRM features are built into its field service workflow rather than sold as a standalone CRM. Every client has a profile with past jobs, quotes, invoices, notes, and communication history. You can see open quotes, filter by status, and follow up from the same platform you dispatch from.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The quote follow-up automation. Jobber's help center says quote follow-ups can send email or text reminders for quotes that are still awaiting response, with up to two reminders on Connect and higher plans.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Jobber's separate Pipeline tool is included on Plus, while lower tiers rely more on quote and request tracking than a full sales-board workflow. If you want lead-source reporting tied to campaign performance, compare Housecall Pro, ServiceTitan, or a standalone CRM.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core is $29/mo when billed annually or $49 month-to-month for one user. Connect starts at $99/mo annual for one user or $149/mo annual for the team version, Grow starts at $149/mo annual for one user or $299/mo annual for the team version, and Plus starts at $529/mo annual with 15 users included. A 14-day free trial is available with no credit card required.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Service contractors who want CRM functionality built into their existing scheduling and invoicing workflow without managing a separate system.</p>

### 2. Housecall Pro – Best for Lead Follow-Up in Residential

<p>Housecall Pro publishes a dedicated Pipeline feature that gives teams a visual workflow for opportunities, lead intake, mobile updates, and automated follow-ups. Its help center also documents lead-source tracking on customers, jobs, and estimates. For a residential service contractor with heavy inbound lead volume, that is a clearer CRM layer than Jobber's lower-tier quote tracking.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Lead source tracking. Housecall Pro lets teams assign lead sources to customers, jobs, and estimates, and its mobile leads workflow tracks open, won, and lost leads.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The pricing tiers jump sharply. Contractors needing deeper marketing, follow-up, or reporting features may need Essentials at $149/mo annual or MAX at $299/mo annual.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic $59/mo annual or $79 monthly. Essentials $149/mo annual or $189 monthly. MAX $299/mo annual or $329 monthly, with additional users listed at $35/mo.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Residential service contractors with significant inbound lead volume who want pipeline visibility and lead source attribution.</p>

### 3. Pipeline CRM – Best Standalone CRM

<p>Pipeline CRM (formerly PipelineDeals) is a dedicated sales CRM that some contractors use alongside their field service software when the built-in CRM features of tools like Jobber aren't enough. It's built for managing deal pipelines, contact histories, and sales activity tracking in more depth than a field service tool provides.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Depth for complex sales pipelines. If you have a dedicated sales rep managing a pipeline separately from field operations, this separation makes sense.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Integration question is the main issue. Pipeline CRM doesn't connect natively to most field service tools, so you'd be managing two separate systems with some data duplication.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Start is $25/user/mo with annual billing or $29/user/mo monthly. Higher Pipeline CRM tiers scale per user; confirm Develop/Grow pricing against the current pricing page before signing.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Contractors with dedicated sales staff managing a complex B2B pipeline who need more CRM depth than field service tools provide.</p>

### 4. HubSpot Free – Best Free Starting Point

<p>HubSpot's free CRM tier is capable for basic lead and contact management. You get free foundational CRM tools for up to two users, with paid Starter seats published from $9/mo/seat when billed annually. For a contractor who wants to start managing leads in something more structured than a spreadsheet without spending money yet, it works.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The free tier gives small teams a no-card starting point for structured lead management before they commit to a paid platform.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> HubSpot doesn't connect to your field service workflow. You'd be managing leads in HubSpot and jobs in a separate tool, which creates data silos. The free tier has limits that push you toward paid tiers quickly.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Free CRM tools are available. HubSpot Starter seats now publish around $9/mo for many hubs, while Professional pricing varies by hub and seat count.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Contractors who want to start managing leads in a structured system at no cost and aren't ready to commit to an all-in-one platform yet.</p>

### 5. ServiceTitan – Best for Complex Operations

<p>ServiceTitan's CRM-adjacent strength is revenue attribution and front-office visibility: campaign ROI scorecards, unique phone numbers tied to booked revenue, call booking workflows, customer communication, and marketing automation add-ons.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Enterprise-grade marketing attribution. For a larger service operation where understanding campaign ROI matters, ServiceTitan has deeper revenue-attribution tools than small-business CRMs.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The price and implementation complexity put it out of reach for most small contractors. This is the CRM conversation for a larger multi-tech operation with marketing attribution needs, not a small shop trying to stop losing jobs to forgotten follow-ups.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote only; ServiceTitan requires a personalized demo/pricing request and does not publish current dollar tiers.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Large field service operations where marketing attribution and customer lifetime value data directly inform business decisions.</p>

## Bottom Line

<p>If you're running a small service operation and losing jobs to poor follow-up, <strong>Jobber's</strong> built-in client history and quote follow-up features may solve the problem without adding another system.</p>

<p>If you're doing significant inbound lead volume from multiple sources and want better visibility into what converts, <strong>Housecall Pro's</strong> Pipeline and lead-source tools are the stronger fit.</p>

<p><strong>HubSpot Free</strong> if you want to start structured lead management at no cost. <strong>Pipeline CRM</strong> if you have a dedicated sales operation separate from field work. <strong>ServiceTitan</strong> when you're managing a large operation and need campaign ROI tied to booked revenue.</p>
