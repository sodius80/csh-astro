<p>Most contractors build their business on referrals and word of mouth. That works until the referral pipeline slows down, or a competitor starts sending seasonal promotion emails that stay top of mind while your company name gathers dust in old inboxes.</p>

<p>Email marketing does not have to mean daily broadcasts or complicated funnels. For a contractor with a list of past customers and prospects, the tools on this page handle three jobs: send a professional monthly newsletter, automate follow-ups on unanswered quotes, and track which emails actually produce calls and bookings. If the bigger issue is pipeline tracking, compare these tools with the <a href="/best-free-crm-for-contractors/">best free CRM software for contractors</a> before paying for a marketing platform.</p>

<p><strong>Disclosure:</strong> Some links on this page are affiliate links. If you sign up through one, I earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. ContractorSoftwareHub's recommendations do not change based on that.</p>

<p><strong>Right for:</strong> Contractors who have a growing list of past customers and want to stay top of mind, send seasonal promotions, or automate quote follow-ups. Any trade  -  plumbing, electrical, HVAC, roofing, landscaping, general contracting  -  benefits from regular communication with past clients.</p>

<p><strong>Not for:</strong> Contractors who do not plan to send regular emails or who have fewer than 50 customer contacts. At that scale, a simple text message or phone call is still the most effective follow-up.</p>

## Do You Need This Yet?

<p>Email marketing pays off when you have a customer list and no system for communicating with them. Two quick checks:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>You don't need it yet</strong> if you have fewer than 50 customer emails and most of your work comes from repeat clients who call you directly. Your phone is still your best marketing tool at that scale.</li>
<li><strong>You need it now</strong> if you have a list of past customers who rarely hear from you, you are sending quote follow-ups from your personal email, or competitors are sending seasonal offers while your inbox is quiet.</li>
</ul>

<p>If you are in the middle  -  growing, but not drowning  -  start with MailerLite. Their free plan lets you test the workflow before committing.</p>

<p>ActiveCampaign is the upgrade path when you outgrow basic automation and need deeper segmentation or CRM integration. If QuickBooks sync matters as much as email automation, use the <a href="/crm-with-quickbooks-integration/">CRM with QuickBooks integration guide</a> as a second filter.</p>

## Getting Started with Email Marketing as a Contractor

<p>Most contractors overthink the first email. A monthly newsletter with a seasonal tip, a recent project photo, and a "call us when you need X" line is enough to start. The tools above handle the technical part. The strategy part is simpler than most people expect: send consistently, track what gets opened, and adjust based on what produces calls.</p>

<p>Start with one automated sequence first: a welcome email for new contacts who fill out your website form. That single automation pays for the tool by itself if it converts even one extra lead per quarter. Add a monthly newsletter after the automation is running. Add seasonal promotions or maintenance reminders when you have momentum.</p>

## FAQ

### Do contractors need a separate email marketing tool, or can they use their CRM?

<p>Some CRMs  -  like ActiveCampaign or Keap  -  include email marketing. Others  -  like <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber</a> or <a href="/housecall-pro-review/">Housecall Pro</a>  -  have basic communication features but not full campaign tools. If your CRM already sends marketing emails, test that first. If it does not, or if the email features are limited, a dedicated email marketing tool is worth the separate subscription.</p>

### What is the best email marketing tool for a contractor with a small list?

<p>MailerLite offers a free tier that covers 250 subscribers with 2,500 emails per month - enough to test with a small real list before upgrading.</p>

<p>AWeber offers a 14-day free trial with no credit card required.</p>

### How many emails should a contractor send per month?

<p>One newsletter per month plus automated follow-ups for new leads is a good starting point. Seasonal promotions (spring HVAC tune-ups, fall gutter cleaning, winter pipe insulation) add another 2-4 emails per year. Consistency matters more than frequency  -  a monthly email that always arrives is better than bursts of emails followed by months of silence.</p>

### What is the most important email automation for a contractor?

<p>A welcome sequence for new contacts. When someone fills out your website form or calls for a quote, an automated welcome email thanks them, sets expectations for next steps, and delivers a relevant resource (seasonal maintenance tip, recent project gallery, or service area overview). That single sequence captures leads who would otherwise slip through the cracks.</p>

### Can I import my existing customer list into these tools?

<p>Yes, all five tools support CSV import of existing contacts. Most also offer manual entry, API imports, and integrations with common CRMs and form builders. Check the import limits on free plans before uploading a large list  -  some free tiers cap total contacts or monthly email volume.</p>
