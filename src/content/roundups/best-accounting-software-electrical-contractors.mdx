<p>Electrical contractors have a specific accounting problem that general small business software usually handles badly: job costing. Tracking labor, materials, permits, and subcontractors at the job level — and knowing whether each job actually made money — is the thing most accounting tools either skip entirely or bolt on as an afterthought.</p>

<p><strong>Right for:</strong> Electrical contractors who need job costing, payroll integration, and a bookkeeper or CPA who already knows the software. QuickBooks wins this category for most shops.</p>

<p><strong>Not for:</strong> Electrical GCs running multi-phase commercial projects with AIA billing, certified payroll, or union reporting. That work needs Sage 100 Contractor or Foundation — and a dedicated accounting staff member to run it.</p>

## Do You Need This Yet?

Electrical contractor accounting software pays for itself when you're doing enough volume that manual job costing and progress billing starts eating into billable hours. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a solo electrician doing residential service calls and tracking expenses in a basic spreadsheet. Simple invoicing still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're juggling multiple active jobs and can't tell whether each one actually made money after labor, materials, and permits.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with QuickBooks Online Plus. Your bookkeeper or CPA already knows it, and the "Projects" feature gives you enough job costing to figure out if your workflow needs something deeper like Sage 100 Contractor later.

## Product Reviews

### 1. QuickBooks Online – Best Overall

<p>QuickBooks Online is the default answer for electrical contractors for one practical reason: your bookkeeper or CPA almost certainly knows how to use it. When you need help at tax time or want to bring in outside accounting support, QBO removes the friction of training someone on a niche platform.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The job costing features in the Plus and Advanced tiers ("Projects" in QBO) work reasonably well. You can track income and expenses by job, run a profitability report per project, and connect it to time tracking.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The learning curve for job costing specifically. The feature exists but isn't intuitive, and the reports don't break out labor versus materials the way a construction-specific tool would.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Current QuickBooks Online public pricing shows Simple Start promoted from $19/mo, Essentials $75/mo, Plus $115/mo, and Advanced $275/mo before short-term discounts. For this article, the key tier is Plus because that is where most electrical contractors start using Projects/job-costing workflows.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Electrical contractors who already have a bookkeeper using QuickBooks or want accountant-friendly books with adequate job costing.</p>

### 2. FreshBooks – Best for Solo and Small Electrical Shops

<p>FreshBooks is built for service businesses where invoicing, time tracking, and client communication are the daily workflow. If you're a solo electrician or running 2 people and your main accounting need is sending professional invoices and getting paid faster, FreshBooks is easier to use than QuickBooks from day one.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Much easier to use than QuickBooks from day one for basic invoicing and time tracking needs.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Doesn't do job costing well. FreshBooks has project tracking, but it's not designed for tracking materials, labor, and subcontractor costs at the job level. Also watch the client limits on lower tiers — the Lite plan caps at 5 active clients.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> FreshBooks was showing promotional pricing of Lite $6.90/mo, Plus $12.90/mo, and Premium $21/mo during the audit, with a 30-day money-back guarantee. Treat those as promo prices and verify the post-promo monthly rate before switching.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Solo electricians or 2-person shops focused on invoicing speed and cash flow visibility over detailed job costing.</p>

### 3. Wave – Best Free Option

<p>Wave is free for accounting and invoicing. For an electrical contractor just starting out or running a very lean operation who can't justify a software subscription yet, Wave handles invoicing, expense tracking, and basic financial reporting at no cost.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Genuinely free — no time limits, no user caps on the free accounting tier.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No meaningful job costing, payroll add-on costs extra, and customer support on the free tier is limited.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Starter accounting/invoicing is free; Pro is $19/mo. Wave Payroll is listed at $40/month plus $6 per active employee or contractor for new U.S. payroll customers.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Solo electricians who need basic invoicing and bookkeeping at no cost until revenue justifies a paid tool.</p>

### 4. Sage 100 Contractor – Best Job Costing for Mid-Size Firms

<p>Sage 100 Contractor (formerly Sage Master Builder) is construction-specific accounting software built with job costing at its core. For an electrical contractor running $1M+ in revenue with multiple active projects, the job costing and WIP reporting in Sage 100 is meaningfully better than what you'll get from QuickBooks.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Handles certified payroll, union tracking, AIA billing formats, change orders connected to accounting, and job cost reporting by phase — real needs for electrical firms doing commercial work.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Cost and implementation are barriers. Recent third-party pricing ranges are closer to $115-$200 per user per month, and Sage 100 Contractor is usually sold and implemented through Sage partners or resellers. Interface is dated and learning curve is steep.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Recent third-party pricing ranges cluster around $115-$200 per user/month, but full pricing depends on reseller, modules, hosting, and implementation scope.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Electrical contractors doing $750K+ in annual revenue who need proper construction job costing, certified payroll, and are ready to invest in real implementation time.</p>

### 5. Knowify – Best for Connecting Estimating to Accounting

<p>Knowify is a hybrid tool built specifically for trade contractors — part project management, part job costing, integrated with QuickBooks. Electrical contractors who need to connect estimates, change orders, subcontractor management, and job costing in one system without buying entirely separate tools find it more integrated than QBO alone.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Change order workflow is cleaner than managing it manually in QBO. Tracks approved changes against the original estimate and flows that into billing.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Doesn't replace QuickBooks — it sits on top of it, adding another subscription.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Knowify Core is $99/mo on annual billing or $149/mo monthly; Advanced is $249/mo annual or $311/mo monthly, with Enterprise quoted.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Electrical contractors who use or want to use QuickBooks but need better job costing and change order tracking built on top of it.</p>

## Bottom Line

<p>Most electrical contractors should start with <strong>QuickBooks Online Plus</strong>. It's not perfect for job costing, but it's good enough for most operations under $750K, and the ecosystem of bookkeepers and CPAs who know it makes outside accounting support easy to find.</p>

<p>Solo electricians or 2-person shops: <strong>FreshBooks</strong> is simpler for invoicing. <strong>Wave</strong> if you're not ready to pay for software yet.</p>

<p>If you're past $750K and running multiple commercial projects simultaneously, <strong>Sage 100 Contractor</strong> is worth the investment. The job costing and certified payroll features solve real problems that QuickBooks doesn't handle properly.</p>

<p><strong>Knowify</strong> makes sense if you want better project tracking and change order management layered on top of QuickBooks without replacing your accounting system entirely.</p>
