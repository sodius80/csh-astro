## Do You Need This Yet?

Bookkeeping software pays for itself when you're invoicing enough customers that manual tracking starts costing you tax deductions and billable time. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a solo operator with under 5 invoices per month and simple expenses. Wave's free accounting still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if your accountant sends cleanup bills, you're missing deductions, or you have employees and 1099 subcontractors to track.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with QuickBooks Online Plus. Your accountant probably already knows it, and the project profitability feature gives you visibility into job costs before you need to hire a full-time bookkeeper.

## Product Reviews

### 1. QuickBooks Online -- Best Overall for Contractors


<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> More accountants use QuickBooks than any other platform. If you switch accountants, the new one probably knows it already. Payroll is native and integrates directly with your time tracking.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Price climbs quickly. Simple Start at $30/mo lacks bill management and time tracking. You need Plus at $85/mo for project profitability. The interface can feel busy for contractors who just want to send invoices and reconcile bank feeds.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Simple Start $30/mo | Essentials $55/mo | Plus $85/mo | Advanced $200/mo. Payroll adds $45-125/mo depending on plan.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Contractors who want the deepest reporting, widest accountant compatibility, and the option to add payroll without switching platforms.</p>

### 2. Xero -- Best for Growing Teams


<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Unlimited users on every plan means your bookkeeper, accountant, and project manager can all access the books without driving up cost. Bank rules automation learns your recurring transactions and categorizes them automatically.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Fewer U.S. accountants know Xero compared to QuickBooks. Payroll requires a third-party add-on in most states, which adds complexity. The reporting is good but not as customizable as QuickBooks.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Early $15/mo | Growing $42/mo | Established $78/mo.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Contractors who want clean reporting, unlimited users, and a modern interface without per-user fees.</p>

### 3. FreshBooks -- Best for Invoice-Heavy Businesses


<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The invoice-to-payment pipeline is the best in this group. Automated reminders go out without manual scheduling. Clients can pay directly from the invoice via credit card or ACH.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No true job costing or project profitability reports. You can tag expenses to a client but not allocate labor and materials to a specific job. Not ideal for contractors who need to know whether Job #47 made money.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Lite $15/mo | Plus $30/mo | Premium $55/mo | Select custom pricing.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Service contractors who send many invoices and want automated payment reminders and client management.</p>

### 4. Wave -- Best Free Option


<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Free accounting, invoicing, and receipt scanning. You only pay for payment processing and payroll. For solo operators with simple needs, this is genuinely cost-free bookkeeping.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No job costing. Payroll is expensive relative to competitors. Limited reporting depth means your accountant may still need to adjust entries at year-end.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Free for accounting and invoicing. Payment processing at 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction. Payroll starts at $40/mo plus $6 per employee.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Solo contractors with simple needs who want professional invoicing and basic reporting at no cost.</p>

## Bottom Line

<p>For most small contractors, QuickBooks Online is the safe choice because your accountant knows it, it scales with your business, and the project profitability feature justifies the Plus plan cost. Xero is the better choice if you hate QuickBooks' interface and need multiple users without paying per seat. FreshBooks works for service contractors who live and die by invoicing speed. Wave is a legitimate free option for solo operators who do not need job costing.</p>

## FAQ

<p><strong>Do I need separate bookkeeping software if I already have a field service CRM?</strong></p>
<p>Most field service CRMs handle invoicing but lack true double-entry bookkeeping. If you file taxes as a business entity or have employees, you need dedicated bookkeeping software.</p>

<p><strong>Can I use Wave for free forever?</strong></p>
<p>Wave's accounting and invoicing are free. You pay only for payment processing and payroll. For solo operators with simple needs, this is genuinely cost-free bookkeeping.</p>

<p><strong>Which bookkeeping software has the best QuickBooks integration?</strong></p>
<p>This question usually means which field service CRM integrates with QuickBooks. Most major CRMs including Jobber, Housecall Pro, and ServiceTitan offer QuickBooks integration.</p>

<p><strong>How much should a small contractor budget for bookkeeping software?</strong></p>
<p>Expect $30-85 per month for the software itself, plus $40-200 per month if you hire a bookkeeper. Doing it yourself saves money but costs time.</p>
