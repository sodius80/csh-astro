## How to Choose Garage Door Software

The garage door industry uses general field service management software because no garage door specific platform offers compelling advantages over established FSM tools.

**Start with your biggest pain point.** If you're losing emergency repair calls because customers can't reach you, prioritize platforms with strong communication features like Housecall Pro. If scheduling conflicts between repairs and installations create chaos, focus on dispatch optimization like ServiceTitan offers.

**Consider your growth trajectory.** Jobber works well for shops with 1-5 techs who want room to grow. ServiceTitan makes sense when you have 5+ techs and need enterprise workflow management. Don't buy more software than you'll use in the next 12 months.

**Factor in true costs.** ServiceTitan uses quote-only per-technician pricing, so a 4-person crew needs a written quote before comparing it with Jobber or Housecall Pro. Those simpler tools have transparent pricing and similar core functionality for most garage door contractors.

**Test the mobile experience.** Your techs will use these platforms on phones and tablets while working on garage doors. Poor mobile interfaces create frustration and reduce adoption. Most platforms offer free trials - use them.

## Do You Need This Yet?

Garage door software pays for itself when you're handling enough emergency repairs and installation calls that manual dispatch starts losing jobs. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're doing 1–2 garage door jobs a month with a paper calendar and a single truck. A simple online calendar still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're missing emergency repair calls because of scheduling conflicts, or your techs don't know which truck carries the right spring inventory.

If you're in the middle—busy but not chaotic—start with ServiceTitan. Its dispatch board handles both emergency repairs and scheduled installs without creating conflicts, and dynamic pricing tools help you capture margin on after-hours calls while you figure out what else your workflow needs.

<p><strong>What's the most important feature for garage door contractors?</strong></p>
<p>Dispatch and scheduling flexibility. Garage door contractors deal with both emergency repairs that need immediate response and scheduled installations that require specific time blocks. Your software needs to handle both workflow types without creating scheduling conflicts.</p>

<p><strong>How much should I expect to pay for garage door software?</strong></p>
<p>Budget $50-100 per user per month for solid platforms like Jobber or Housecall Pro. ServiceTitan uses quote-only per-technician pricing and includes enterprise features most small shops don't need. Get the quote in writing before comparing it with transparent options.</p>
