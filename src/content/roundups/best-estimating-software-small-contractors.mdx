<p>Small contractors — the 1 to 5 person shop doing residential remodels, additions, or service work — don't need the same estimating tools that a GC managing a $5M commercial project uses. The enterprise platforms are either overkill or just too expensive to justify when you're doing 15 to 40 jobs a year.</p>

<p><strong>Right for:</strong> Small residential contractors, remodelers, and single-trade shops doing 10 to 50 jobs a year who need professional-looking estimates out the door fast and some job costing on the back end.</p>

<p><strong>Not for:</strong> GCs managing multiple subs, commercial bids, or takeoff-heavy work. Those operations need STACK, PlanSwift, or Procore Estimating.</p>

## Do You Need This Yet?

Estimating software pays for itself when you're doing enough volume that manual takeoffs and spreadsheet reconciliation start eating into your evenings. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a one or two person shop doing a handful of small jobs a year and your paper quotes look professional enough. Joist's Basics plan or even a well-organized spreadsheet still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if estimates that used to take an hour are now taking three, or if you can't tell whether the job made money until you've reconciled spreadsheets at the end.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Buildxact. It connects estimating to actual job costing, so you'll know if you're profitable before the job is finished instead of after.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Buildxact – Best Overall for Small Contractors

<p>Buildxact is purpose-built for small residential builders and remodelers. The combination of estimating, job costing, scheduling, and basic project management in a single tool at a price that doesn't require a finance conversation first is what puts it at the top of this list.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The estimate-to-job tracking connection. Knowing whether the job made money against the estimate without reconciling spreadsheets at the end is what Buildxact gets right that simpler tools don't.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Learning curve. As one reviewer put it: "Took me a solid two weeks to get comfortable, but now estimates that used to take me 3 hours take 45 minutes."</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Foundation $199/mo monthly or $169/mo annual; Pro $399/mo monthly or $339/mo annual; Master $599/mo monthly or $509/mo annual. Buildxact offers a 14-day trial.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small residential builders and remodelers who want estimating connected to job costing and basic project management in one place.</p>

### 2. Joist – Best Low-Cost Option

<p>Joist is where a lot of small contractors start, and for good reason. The Basics plan lets you send a limited number of estimates and invoices each month, collect e-signatures, and accept payments. It's not deep, but for a one or two person shop just getting off paper estimates, it works with almost no learning curve.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Low-cost estimating and invoicing without a construction-management learning curve. Pro and Elite include a 14-day trial before you commit.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Joist stays in the "estimates and invoicing" lane — it doesn't do real job costing or project management. If you outgrow the basic estimate-send-invoice workflow, you'll outgrow Joist.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basics $10/mo or $100/year; Pro $16/mo or $160/year; Elite $32/mo or $320/year. Pro and Elite trials run 14 days.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Contractors just getting off paper quotes who need a low-cost way to send professional estimates and collect e-signatures.</p>

### 3. Houzz Pro – Best for Residential Remodelers

<p>Houzz Pro combines estimating with a full client experience platform — mood boards, project timelines, a client portal, and invoice tracking. For a residential remodeler where the sales process involves multiple client touchpoints before a contract is signed, the integrated client presentation tools are a real differentiator.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The client management side — tracking where each prospect is in the sales process, presenting design concepts alongside the estimate, maintaining a professional client portal through the project.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Higher monthly cost and less developed project management features than competitors at a similar price point.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Houzz Pro's public pricing currently centers on Pro at $249/mo, with Custom/Ultimate quote-based tiers above that. A free basic plan and 30-day trial are available.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Residential remodelers who compete on design and client experience, not just on price.</p>

### 4. Jobber – Best for Service-Based Contractors

<p>Jobber isn't a pure estimating tool, but for service contractors (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, landscaping) where the estimate is a quote for a service call or small job rather than a multi-phase construction project, it handles estimating as part of a broader field service workflow.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The quoting feature lets you build line-item quotes, get customer approval by text or email, and convert directly to jobs without entering data again.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> If you're a GC or remodeler doing construction estimates with detailed takeoffs and material cost tracking, Jobber is not the right tool.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core starts at $49/mo on monthly billing, with annual-commitment and promotional pricing shown lower on Jobber's pricing page. Connect, Grow, and Plus add more users and quoting/automation features.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Single-trade service contractors who want quoting integrated into scheduling and invoicing, not separate estimating software.</p>

### 5. Clear Estimates – Best Built-In Cost Database

<p>Clear Estimates is a simple estimating tool with a built-in database of construction costs that updates quarterly. For a small contractor who doesn't want to build and maintain their own cost library from scratch, having a starting point for labor and material costs per trade is genuinely useful.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Built-in cost database that updates quarterly saves real time on estimate creation.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Doesn't connect to job costing or project management. It saves time on estimates but doesn't replace a separate system for tracking job actuals.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Standard is $79/mo monthly or $59/mo billed annually. Clear Estimates also offers Pro and franchise tiers, plus a 30-day free trial.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small contractors doing residential remodel or repair work who want a cost database to build estimates from, without the complexity of a full project management platform.</p>

## Bottom Line

<p><strong>Buildxact</strong> is the right answer for most small residential contractors. If you're doing remodels, additions, or small new builds and you want estimates that connect to actual job tracking, it covers the workflow without requiring enterprise-level commitment.</p>

<p>Start with <strong>Joist</strong> if you're coming off paper estimates and need the simplest low-cost entry point. Graduate to Buildxact once the volume justifies the subscription.</p>

<p><strong>Houzz Pro</strong> if your sales process is design-heavy. <strong>Clear Estimates</strong> if you want a cost database without paying for tools you won't use. <strong>Jobber</strong> if you're a service contractor — the quoting-to-job workflow is more practical than standalone estimating software.</p>
