## Do You Need This Yet?

Appliance repair software pays for itself when you're doing enough volume that manual scheduling and parts tracking starts eating into billable hours. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're handling 1–2 service calls a week with a phone calendar and a parts bin you can see from your truck. A shared group text still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're double-booking calls, losing track of which technician carries compressor or thermostat parts, or recreating flat-rate prices from memory on every job.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—compare ServiceTitan against Jobber and Housecall Pro instead of assuming the enterprise tool is automatically the right fit. ServiceTitan is the strongest appliance-specific option here, but its quote-only pricing means a smaller shop should validate total cost before building around it.

## Product Reviews

### 1. ServiceTitan – Best for growing shops

<p>ServiceTitan positions itself specifically for appliance repair contractors, offering scheduling, dispatch, mobile tools, estimating, invoicing, and inventory tracking in one platform.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> ServiceTitan markets itself as the appliance repair leader with mobile app features that technicians actually use in the field. The platform includes flat-rate pricing tools designed for appliance jobs.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> ServiceTitan uses quote-only per-technician pricing, making it hard for smaller shops to benchmark cost before a sales process. Solo operators will find this overkill for their needs.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote; ServiceTitan publishes per-technician pricing but not dollar amounts.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Appliance repair companies with 3+ techs ready for enterprise FSM.</p>

### 2. Housecall Pro – Best for customer communication

<p>Housecall Pro offers scheduling, dispatch, invoicing, and strong customer communication tools including two-way SMS and online booking for appliance repair businesses.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Two-way SMS keeps customers informed about technician arrival times, and online booking lets customers schedule appliance repairs directly. Users report good customer retention with these communication features.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Higher published entry price than Jobber Core on month-to-month billing. Housecall Pro has customer equipment tracking on higher tiers, but it does not include a preloaded appliance parts catalog or dedicated parts inventory module.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic starts at $59/mo for 1 user; Essentials is $149/mo for up to 5 users; Max is listed at $299/mo promotional / $329/mo standard with additional users at $35/mo.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Appliance repair shops wanting strong customer communication.</p>

### 3. Jobber – Best value

<p>Jobber provides core field service management including scheduling, estimates, invoicing, and client management at a lower price point than specialized appliance platforms.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Good value for money with reliable scheduling, estimating, and invoicing tools. Users report the platform is dependable for core FSM needs.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No appliance-specific features or parts catalogs. No parts inventory tracking for appliance components like motors or control boards.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core is $49/mo month-to-month for 1 user, with annual billing and 1-year commitment options lowering the effective monthly price. Connect, Grow, and Plus add more users and automation.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small appliance repair shops needing solid FSM at lower cost.</p>

### 4. Zoho FSM – Budget option

<p>Zoho FSM offers scheduling, dispatch, work orders, and mobile apps as a cost-effective field service solution, though without appliance-specific features.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Cost-effective option for small businesses with basic FSM features including scheduling, dispatch, and mobile app access for technicians.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Less brand recognition in the appliance repair space. General FSM platform without appliance-specific tools or parts tracking.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Zoho FSM has a free plan for up to 30 appointments/month, appointment-based paid tiers, and a 15-day no-card trial. The public page should be checked during signup because paid-tier dollar amounts can be region/currency dependent.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small appliance repair businesses needing cost-effective FSM.</p>

### 5. Dynamics 365 Field Service – Enterprise only

<p>Microsoft Dynamics 365 Field Service provides AI scheduling, IoT integration, ERP/CRM integration, and predictive maintenance for large appliance repair operations.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> AI-powered scheduling optimization and IoT integration for connected appliance diagnostics. Deep ERP and CRM integration for enterprise workflows.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Per-user annual pricing and Microsoft ecosystem complexity put it out of reach for most small shops. Implementation usually requires dedicated admin or partner support.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Dynamics 365 Field Service is listed at $105/user/month paid yearly; the Field Service Contractor license is $50/user/month paid yearly. Resource Scheduling Optimization is an additional $30/resource/month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Large appliance repair chains with ERP integration needs.</p>

## Bottom Line

<p>ServiceTitan fits appliance repair shops with multiple technicians that can justify a sales-quoted enterprise platform for appliance-specific features and flat-rate pricing tools. Smaller operations should choose Jobber for reliable FSM starting at $49/month-to-month on Core (lower with annual billing), or Housecall Pro starting at $59/month on Basic if customer communication is a priority. Skip Dynamics 365 unless you're running a large appliance repair chain with Microsoft admin support.</p>

<p>None of these platforms solve the parts inventory problem completely. You'll still need to manually add appliance components or integrate with a separate parts management system.</p>

## FAQ

<p><strong>Do these platforms include appliance parts catalogs?</strong></p>
<p>No. None of these platforms include pre-loaded appliance parts catalogs. Technicians must manually add parts or integrate with separate inventory systems to track components like motors, thermostats, and control boards.</p>

<p><strong>Which platform is best for solo appliance repair techs?</strong></p>
<p>Jobber offers strong value for solo operators with Core at $49/month on month-to-month billing and lower annual options. ServiceTitan requires a sales quote and is generally too heavy for single-person shops.</p>

<p><strong>Can I track appliance parts inventory in these systems?</strong></p>
<p>ServiceTitan includes inventory tracking features. Housecall Pro offers customer equipment tracking on higher tiers, but not a preloaded appliance parts catalog or dedicated parts inventory module. Jobber also lacks appliance-specific parts inventory, so most shops will still need to track components separately or integrate with a dedicated inventory system.</p>
