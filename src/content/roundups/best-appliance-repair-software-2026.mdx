## Product Reviews

### 1. ServiceTitan – Best for growing shops

<p>ServiceTitan positions itself specifically for appliance repair contractors, offering scheduling, dispatch, mobile tools, estimating, invoicing, and inventory tracking in one platform.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> ServiceTitan markets itself as the appliance repair leader with mobile app features that technicians actually use in the field. The platform includes flat-rate pricing tools designed for appliance jobs.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The per-tech pricing starts at $245/month, making it expensive for smaller shops. Solo operators will find this overkill for their needs.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> $245-$398 per technician per month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Appliance repair companies with 3+ techs ready for enterprise FSM.</p>

### 2. Housecall Pro – Best for customer communication

<p>Housecall Pro offers scheduling, dispatch, invoicing, and strong customer communication tools including two-way SMS and online booking for appliance repair businesses.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Two-way SMS keeps customers informed about technician arrival times, and online booking lets customers schedule appliance repairs directly. Users report good customer retention with these communication features.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> More expensive than Jobber for similar core features. No appliance parts catalog or inventory tracking capabilities.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Around $65 per user per month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Appliance repair shops wanting strong customer communication.</p>

### 3. Jobber – Best value

<p>Jobber provides core field service management including scheduling, estimates, invoicing, and client management at a lower price point than specialized appliance platforms.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Good value for money with reliable scheduling, estimating, and invoicing tools. Users report the platform is dependable for core FSM needs.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No appliance-specific features or parts catalogs. No parts inventory tracking for appliance components like motors or control boards.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Around $49 per user per month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small appliance repair shops needing solid FSM at lower cost.</p>

### 4. Zoho FSM – Budget option

<p>Zoho FSM offers scheduling, dispatch, work orders, and mobile apps as a cost-effective field service solution, though without appliance-specific features.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Cost-effective option for small businesses with basic FSM features including scheduling, dispatch, and mobile app access for technicians.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Less brand recognition in the appliance repair space. General FSM platform without appliance-specific tools or parts tracking.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote required.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small appliance repair businesses needing cost-effective FSM.</p>

### 5. Dynamics 365 Field Service – Enterprise only

<p>Microsoft Dynamics 365 Field Service provides AI scheduling, IoT integration, ERP/CRM integration, and predictive maintenance for large appliance repair operations.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> AI-powered scheduling optimization and IoT integration for connected appliance diagnostics. Deep ERP and CRM integration for enterprise workflows.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Enterprise pricing puts it out of reach for small shops. Complex implementation requires dedicated IT resources and months of setup time.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote for enterprise deployments.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Large appliance repair chains with ERP integration needs.</p>

## Bottom Line

<p>ServiceTitan wins for appliance repair shops with 3+ technicians who can justify the $245/tech monthly cost for appliance-specific features and flat-rate pricing tools. Smaller operations should choose Jobber for reliable FSM at $49/user/month, or Housecall Pro at $65/user/month if customer communication is a priority. Skip the enterprise options unless you're running a large appliance repair chain.</p>

<p>None of these platforms solve the parts inventory problem completely. You'll still need to manually add appliance components or integrate with a separate parts management system.</p>

## FAQ

<p><strong>Do these platforms include appliance parts catalogs?</strong></p>
<p>No. None of these platforms include pre-loaded appliance parts catalogs. Technicians must manually add parts or integrate with separate inventory systems to track components like motors, thermostats, and control boards.</p>

<p><strong>Which platform is best for solo appliance repair techs?</strong></p>
<p>Jobber offers the best value for solo operators at around $49/month. ServiceTitan starts at $245/tech/month, making it too expensive for single-person shops.</p>

<p><strong>Can I track appliance parts inventory in these systems?</strong></p>
<p>ServiceTitan includes inventory tracking features. Housecall Pro and Jobber do not have built-in parts inventory management. You'll need to track appliance components separately or integrate with a dedicated inventory system.</p>
