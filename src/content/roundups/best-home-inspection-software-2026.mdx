## Do You Need This Yet?

Home inspection software pays for itself when you're doing enough inspections that manual report writing starts eating into your evenings and weekends. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're doing 1–2 inspections a month with a basic template and Word documents. A simple photo upload tool still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're spending 3+ hours writing reports after every inspection, or agents are asking for faster turnaround times you can't meet.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Spectora. It's the only platform that combines report writing with scheduling and payment processing, so you're not juggling three subscriptions while you figure out what your workflow needs.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Spectora – Best All-in-One

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Spectora's web-based reports are built for buyers and agents to read online, and the platform handles the inspection business cycle from scheduling through payment collection. Spectora's pricing page also lists offline mobile report writing and unlimited report creation on paid plans.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> At $109 monthly, it's expensive for solo inspectors who only need report writing. Community complaints mention occasional bugs and workflow changes, so inspectors should test their own template setup before committing. Spectora's cancellation help says your account remains active and past reports remain accessible after cancellation, but you cannot schedule new inspections until you resubscribe.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> $109/month or $1,090/year. Additional inspectors are $99/month or $999/year. The trial includes two free inspections, with email/SMS capabilities reserved for paid plans.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Established solo inspectors and multi-inspector firms wanting business management and report writing in one place.</p>

### 2. Home Inspector Pro (HIP) – Best Multi-Platform Flexibility

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> True cross-platform support means you can work across Windows, Mac, iPhone, iPad, and Android. The template editor gives you granular control over your reports, and HIP's help center documents AI translation support for English, French, Canadian French, Spanish, German, Portuguese, and Tagalog.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The learning curve is steep compared to simpler competitors. Advanced scheduling, text and email automation, and additional third-party integrations require the HIP Office tier at $89 monthly combined cost.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic $74/month | HIP Office $89/month combined | One-time license $999 plus Office subscription required.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Tech-comfortable inspectors who work across multiple devices and need maximum template control.</p>

### 3. HomeGauge – Best for Desktop-First Workflows

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The desktop application provides deep customization options that mobile-first platforms cannot match. U.S.-based support maintains a strong reputation among experienced users who have invested years in custom templates.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The mobile experience can lag behind native cloud competitors, and HomeGauge is now part of the Spectora family, which reduces the amount of independent competition in the inspection-software category.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> $89/month after a 30-day free trial, according to the current HomeGauge pricing page.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Experienced inspectors who prefer desktop-heavy workflows and have invested time in custom templates.</p>

### 4. Tap Inspect – Best iOS-Only Simplicity

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The iOS-native interface is built around completing and sharing reports before leaving the job site. Pay-per-job pricing at $7.50 per inspection gives you flexibility without monthly commitments, and Tap Inspect's public pages also list scheduling, agreements, payments, reminders, and a client portal.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> iOS-only support means no Android compatibility, which limits team growth and device flexibility. Per-job pricing becomes expensive above roughly 12 inspections monthly. It is also a weaker fit if your inspectors need a desktop report writer.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Pay-per-report is sold in blocks of 20 for $150 ($7.50 each). Unlimited is $90/month plus $45 per additional inspector. The trial covers the first five inspections.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Solo inspectors fully embedded in Apple ecosystem who want fast mobile reporting without business management bloat.</p>

### 5. PalmTech – Best for Narrative Reports

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> PalmTech supports narrative comments and customizable report templates, while its current feature pages also promote scheduling, agreements, payments, AI comment help, and report delivery tools.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> PalmTech's public feature page notes that live data sync requires an internet connection, so inspectors who regularly work in weak-signal areas should test offline workflows before switching. At $50 per user per month, pricing is simple for a solo inspector but can climb for teams.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> $50/month per user or $500/year.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Inspectors who strictly prefer narrative, room-by-room reporting and do not need modern mobile automation.</p>
