## Product Reviews

### 1. Spectora – Best All-in-One

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Real estate agents routinely praise Spectora reports for their clean, readable format. The platform handles the entire inspection business cycle from scheduling through payment collection, eliminating the need for separate subscriptions.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> At $109 monthly, it's expensive for solo inspectors who only need report writing. One Reddit user said "Spectora frequently made me furious with the bugs and its inability to save my comments the way I wanted them saved." The subscription model means you lose access to past reports if you cancel.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Pro plan $109/month with scheduling and payments included.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Established solo inspectors and multi-inspector firms wanting business management and report writing in one place.</p>

### 2. Home Inspector Pro (HIP) – Best Multi-Platform Flexibility

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> True cross-platform support means you can start a report on Windows, continue on iPad, and finish on Android. The template editor gives you granular control over every aspect of your reports, making it ideal for commercial and specialty inspections.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The learning curve is steep compared to simpler competitors. Full functionality requires the HIP Office tier at $89 monthly combined cost, and you cannot send Repair Request Lists without satisfying contract-upload requirements.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic $74/month | HIP Office $89/month combined | One-time license $999 plus Office subscription required.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Tech-comfortable inspectors who work across multiple devices and need maximum template control.</p>

### 3. HomeGauge – Best for Desktop-First Workflows

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The desktop application provides deep customization options that mobile-first platforms cannot match. U.S.-based support maintains a strong reputation among experienced users who have invested years in custom templates.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> A G2 review called HomeGauge "mired in 20th-century technology, missing necessary features, and has pathetic support." The mobile experience lags behind native cloud competitors, and the platform is now owned by the same parent company as Spectora.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> HomeGauge One $89/month with cloud delivery included.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Experienced inspectors who prefer desktop-heavy workflows and have invested time in custom templates.</p>

### 4. Tap Inspect – Best iOS-Only Simplicity

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The iOS-native interface lets inspectors complete reports before leaving the job site. Pay-per-job pricing at $7.50 per inspection gives you flexibility without monthly commitments.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> iOS-only support means no Android compatibility, which limits team growth and device flexibility. Per-job pricing becomes expensive above roughly 12 inspections monthly. Reddit users noted "cheaper but with lower quality reports compared to Spectora."</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> $7.50 per job (blocks of 20 for $150) | Unlimited $90/month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Solo inspectors fully embedded in Apple ecosystem who want fast mobile reporting without business management bloat.</p>

### 5. PalmTech – Best for Narrative Reports

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The room-by-room workflow produces narrative reports that read like professional letters rather than checkbox summaries. This format has strong appeal with attorneys and lenders who handle property disputes.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> InterNACHI forum users describe the app as "the most awkward app that I use" and say "the program may have been written 10+ years ago and it hasn't kept up with the new technology." The mobile app requires internet to generate PDFs, which fails in basements or rural areas.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> $50/month per user or $500/year.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Inspectors who strictly prefer narrative, room-by-room reporting and do not need modern mobile automation.</p>

## Bottom Line

<p><strong>For most inspectors:</strong> Spectora earns the top recommendation despite its price. The reports impress real estate agents, and the all-in-one platform eliminates juggling multiple subscriptions.</p>

<p><strong>Need maximum flexibility:</strong> Home Inspector Pro handles any device and gives you complete template control, making it ideal for commercial work or complex inspections.</p>

<p><strong>Prefer desktop workflows:</strong> HomeGauge remains solid for experienced users, but the acquisition by Spectora's parent company raises questions about future development.</p>

<p><strong>Want simple and cheap:</strong> Tap Inspect works well for part-time inspectors in the Apple ecosystem, but iOS-only support limits growth.</p>

<p><strong>Avoid:</strong> PalmTech unless you specifically need narrative formatting. The technology feels dated compared to modern alternatives.</p>

## FAQ

<p><strong>Why are home inspection software prices rising?</strong></p>
<p>Industry consolidation plays a major role. Radian Capital now owns both Spectora and HomeGauge, while Porch Group owns HIP and PalmTech. With fewer independent competitors, prices have increased across the board. HomeGauge users saw cloud service prices jump from $39/month to $59/month after the acquisition.</p>

<p><strong>Can I access my old reports if I cancel my subscription?</strong></p>
<p>This varies by vendor. Spectora requires an active subscription to view past reports, creating vendor lock-in. HIP offers a one-time license option but still requires an Office subscription for cloud delivery. HomeGauge and PalmTech have different policies. Always export your reports before switching platforms.</p>

<p><strong>Which software works best in areas with poor cell signal?</strong></p>
<p>HomeGauge and HIP handle offline work better than cloud-first competitors. Spectora requires internet for most functions. PalmTech's mobile app needs internet to generate PDFs, which is problematic in basements. Tap Inspect works offline but syncs when connection returns.</p>
