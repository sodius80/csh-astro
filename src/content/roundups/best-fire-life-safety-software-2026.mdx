## How to Choose Fire & Life Safety Software

Fire protection software isn't like other trades. You're not managing reactive service calls, you're managing scheduled compliance work with strict NFPA requirements.

Look for platforms that handle inspection-first workflows: recurring inspection scheduling, deficiency tracking with photos and notes, NFPA-compliant reporting, and customer notification systems for upcoming inspections.

Avoid general field service platforms that treat fire inspections like regular service calls. The compliance tracking and reporting requirements are too different.

## Do You Need This Yet?

Fire & life safety software pays for itself when you're doing enough volume that manual inspection logs and NFPA compliance tracking start creating liability gaps. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're only handling a handful of small inspections a month and your clients accept basic PDF reports. A general FSM platform or even a well-managed spreadsheet still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're juggling recurring inspection schedules across multiple facilities, tracking deficiencies by hand, or if a missed compliance deadline could cost you a contract.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Inspect Point. It has the clearest named fire-protection plan structure in this group and handles scheduling, deficiency tracking, and compliance reporting in one workflow.
