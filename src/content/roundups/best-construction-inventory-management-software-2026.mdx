## Do You Need This Yet?

Inventory software pays for itself when you're doing enough volume that lost materials or time spent searching starts eating into profits. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a solo contractor or small crew who can mentally track what's in the truck and warehouse. Basic organization and labeled bins still work fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're regularly buying materials you already have, spending more than 30 minutes per week looking for tools, or managing multiple job sites with shared inventory.

If you're in the middle, growing but not overwhelmed, start with Sortly. Its visual approach minimizes the learning curve while you figure out what inventory workflows actually matter for your business.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Sortly – Best Visual Tracking

<p>Sortly turns inventory tracking into a visual process that actually makes sense for contractors. Instead of spreadsheets and part numbers, you photograph items and organize them visually.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The photo-based approach eliminates the biggest barrier to contractor adoption. Instead of typing "3/4 inch copper elbow" you just snap a picture and drop it in the right folder.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Limited integration with construction-specific software means it works best as a standalone tool rather than part of a connected workflow.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Free plan includes 100 unique items and 1 user. Advanced is $49/month month-to-month or $24/month as a first-year annual promotional equivalent, billed $288/year; the yearly discount changes after the first year. Ultra is $149/month monthly or $74/month on yearly billing and includes purchase orders. Premium is $299/month monthly or $149/month on yearly billing and includes QuickBooks Online. Enterprise is custom quoted. Paid plans advertise 14-day trials.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small contractors who need simple, visual inventory management without complex job costing integration.</p>

### 2. Zoho Inventory – Best for Multi-Location

<p>Zoho Inventory offers solid multi-location tracking at contractor-friendly pricing. It handles basic inventory needs well but requires workarounds for construction-specific requirements.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The free plan (50 orders/month, 1 user, 2 locations) and $29/org/month Standard plan (billed annually) make it accessible for growing teams. Multi-location support works well for contractors managing both warehouse and job site inventory, with extra users available as $7.50/org/month annual-billing add-ons and extra locations/orders available as paid add-ons.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Not purpose-built for construction workflows means you'll spend time configuring it to work with your specific needs rather than getting construction-ready features out of the box.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Free plan includes 50 orders/month, 1 user, and 2 locations. Standard starts at $29/org/month billed annually. Zoho advertises a 14-day trial. Additional users are $7.50/org/month on annual billing, with add-ons available for extra locations and order volume.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Growing contractors with multiple job sites or warehouses who need affordable multi-location tracking.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Higher annual starting price and construction-specific scope may be too much if all you need is a simple tool-and-material list.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Annual-only plans are Core at $4,788/year, Core+ at $7,188/year, and Pro at $14,388/year. There are no per-user fees; annual commitment, onboarding, and support are included.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Established contractors who need construction-specific inventory features and can justify the higher price with time savings.</p>

## FAQ

<p><strong>Do I need inventory software as a small contractor?</strong></p>
<p>You need it when lost materials or time spent searching for tools starts costing more than $100-200 per month. If you're still tracking everything mentally or on paper without problems, you're probably not there yet.</p>

<p><strong>What's the difference between general inventory software and construction-specific tools?</strong></p>
<p>General tools like Sortly and Zoho track what you have and where. Contractor platforms like Projul make more sense when materials, purchase orders, estimates, job costing, and QuickBooks workflows need to live in one operating system.</p>

<p><strong>Can these tools track tools and equipment, not just materials?</strong></p>
<p>Sortly and Zoho Inventory can track both consumable materials and reusable assets like tools and equipment. Projul is better framed as a contractor operations platform where material workflows connect to jobs, purchase orders, estimates, and job costing rather than a standalone tool-tracking app.</p>

<p><strong>How do these integrate with existing contractor software?</strong></p>
<p>Integration varies widely. Zoho connects well within the Zoho ecosystem. Sortly works well as a standalone visual inventory tool, with QuickBooks Online available on Premium. Projul connects material workflows with estimates, purchase orders, job costing, QuickBooks, and project management in a contractor operations platform.</p>

<p><strong>What about larger contractors with complex inventory needs?</strong></p>
<p>Larger contractors often need enterprise solutions like Procore, which uses custom quotes based on product mix and annual construction volume (ACV), rather than publishing a simple self-serve price. Procore includes unlimited users, data, and support, and Field Productivity is priced by FTE. The tools in this roundup work best for smaller contractors that need inventory control before they need a full enterprise suite.</p>
