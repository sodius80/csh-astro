## Do You Need This Yet?

Inventory software pays for itself when you're doing enough volume that lost materials or time spent searching starts eating into profits. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a solo contractor or small crew who can mentally track what's in the truck and warehouse. Basic organization and labeled bins still work fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're regularly buying materials you already have, spending more than 30 minutes per week looking for tools, or managing multiple job sites with shared inventory.

If you're in the middle--growing but not overwhelmed--start with Sortly. Its visual approach minimizes the learning curve while you figure out what inventory workflows actually matter for your business.

## FAQ

<p><strong>Do I need inventory software as a small contractor?</strong></p>
<p>You need it when lost materials or time spent searching for tools starts costing more than $100-200 per month. If you're still tracking everything mentally or on paper without problems, you're probably not there yet.</p>

<p><strong>What's the difference between general inventory software and construction-specific tools?</strong></p>
<p>General tools like Sortly and Zoho track what you have and where. Construction-specific tools like Projul also connect inventory to jobs, track material usage against estimates, and integrate with other contractor software.</p>

<p><strong>Can these tools track tools and equipment, not just materials?</strong></p>
<p>Yes, all three handle both consumable materials and reusable assets like tools and equipment. Sortly excels at visual tool tracking, while Projul offers more detailed equipment management features.</p>

<p><strong>How do these integrate with existing contractor software?</strong></p>
<p>Integration varies widely. Zoho connects well within the Zoho ecosystem. Projul integrates with common contractor tools. Sortly has limited integrations but works well as a standalone solution.</p>

<p><strong>What about larger contractors with complex inventory needs?</strong></p>
<p>Larger contractors often need enterprise solutions like Procore, which ranges from $10,000 to $50,000+ annually. The tools in this roundup work best for contractors with 1-20 employees.</p>
