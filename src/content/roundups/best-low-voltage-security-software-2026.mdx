## Do You Need This Yet?

Security contractor software pays for itself when you're managing enough access control, camera, and alarm installations that manual scheduling and separate security system management starts causing coordination gaps. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're handling a few small installs a month with a basic calendar and your security hardware manufacturer tools. A notebook and phone still work fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're running separate systems for dispatch and security hardware, or your techs are showing up without the right access credentials or camera setup knowledge.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Genetec Security Center if the security-system side is the bottleneck. It natively unifies access control, video surveillance, ALPR, communications, and intrusion workflows, so you stop juggling separate security dashboards while your install volume increases.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Genetec Security Center – Best for unified security

<p>Genetec Security Center SaaS is built for contractors and integrators managing access control, video, intrusion, intercom, and speaker connections. Unlike general FSM tools, it is a security-system platform first, with published per-connection SaaS pricing and channel-partner quoting for final configuration.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Genetec publishes Security Center SaaS connection pricing by connection type: access control, video, intrusion, intercom, and speaker. That gives integrators a real starting point before hardware, storage, retention, and channel-partner configuration are quoted.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Security Center SaaS pricing is transparent by connection, but hardware, storage, partner quoting, and implementation can still make Genetec too heavy for small alarm-only shops. Complex setup requires security-system expertise.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Security Center SaaS lists access-control connections at $99/year on Standard or $149/year on Premium; video, intrusion, intercom, and speaker connections list at $149/year on Standard or $199/year on Premium. Hardware and final configuration still require a certified channel partner quote.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Security contractors needing true integration across access control, video, and intrusion systems</p>

### 2. ServiceTitan – Best FSM for security

<p>ServiceTitan offers mature field service management specifically designed for trade contractors. Security companies get solid scheduling, dispatch, estimating, and invoicing tools, plus customer communication features that work well for service calls.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Proven FSM platform with strong contractor focus. Excellent dispatch and scheduling capabilities handle the complexity of security service calls, from routine maintenance to emergency repairs.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No security-specific features or access-control/video integrations. Quote-only per-technician pricing can be hard to compare against lighter FSM tools, and it is built for general trades rather than security specialization.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote; ServiceTitan describes per-technician pricing but does not publish dollar amounts.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Growing security companies that need proven field service management with strong dispatch capabilities</p>

### 3. Jobber – Best value FSM

<p>Jobber delivers reliable field service management at a lower published entry price than enterprise platforms. Security contractors get scheduling, estimates, invoicing, and client management tools that handle the business side without adding security-system management.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Low published entry pricing works for smaller security contractors that only need basic FSM. Reliable platform with good customer support covers core scheduling, quoting, invoicing, and communication without unnecessary complexity.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Zero security-specific features or integrations. It's a general FSM tool with limited customization for specialized security workflows.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core starts at $29/month before promotional discounts. Higher tiers add users and automation features; verify current annual/promo terms at checkout.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small security shops wanting reliable field service management without the enterprise price tag</p>

### 4. AMAG Technology – Access control focused

<p>AMAG Technology focuses on Symmetry access-control and security-management solutions. Security contractors should treat it as an access-control/security-system platform, not as dispatch, invoicing, or customer-management software.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> AMAG is built around physical-security management, especially access-control needs. Contractors should confirm video, alarm, licensing, and support scope through an AMAG partner or current price list.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Enterprise-focused with complex pricing structure. Less market presence than established competitors, and limited field service management features.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote with enterprise-focused pricing.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Security contractors specializing in access control and video integration projects</p>

### 5. HID Global – Hardware-centric

<p>HID Global is best known for physical access-control credentials, readers, cards, mobile access, and related hardware. Contractors should treat it as an access-control hardware and identity stack, not a field-service management platform.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> HID covers physical access-control credentials, readers, cards, mobile access, and related systems. That is valuable when credentials and hardware are the project center, but it does not replace dispatch, estimating, or invoicing software.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Hardware-focused platform with no scheduling, invoicing, or customer management. This is credential management, not field service software.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom pricing with unclear cost structure.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Security contractors focused on access control credentials and physical security hardware</p>

## Bottom Line

<p>Security contractors face a choice. Genetec Security Center is the strongest unified security platform here, and its SaaS pricing now publishes per-connection starting points, but hardware and partner configuration still make it a serious security-system project. ServiceTitan and Jobber handle field service well but ignore the security-specific side. AMAG and HID Global focus on access-control and credential niches but lack broader FSM capabilities.</p>

<p>For most small security contractors, Jobber provides reliable field service management at reasonable cost. Growing shops with dispatch complexity should consider ServiceTitan. Shops managing complex, integrated security systems across multiple sites should evaluate Genetec with a certified channel partner.</p>
