## Do You Need This Yet?

Solar software pays for itself when you're designing enough systems that manual shading analysis and permit plan prep start eating into your sales time. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're only doing a few small residential installs a year and your utility or AHJ accepts basic drawings. A free tool with standard roof assumptions still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if permits are getting rejected for inaccurate shading calculations, or if financing partners are questioning your production estimates.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Aurora Solar. Its Basic plan now gives one user 50 projects/month at $159/month, while Premium adds LIDAR-assisted modeling and bankable shade reports for teams that need stronger design confidence.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Aurora Solar – Best for Precision Design and Sales

<p>Aurora Solar leads on design precision with AI-powered 3D modeling and LIDAR shading analysis. The platform can generate automated solar permit plan sets directly from a sales proposal, which contractors report saves hours of engineering time.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Aurora publishes clear Basic and Premium pricing, includes 50 projects per month for 1 user on both tiers, and positions Premium around LIDAR-assisted modeling, bankable shade reports, and battery modeling. Enterprise adds custom users/projects, APIs and integrations, advanced permissions, and premium services.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Premium design/drafting services, Nearmap imagery, AI-assisted site models, and Enterprise features can change the real bill beyond the Basic/Premium headline. Teams should also test complex-roof modeling and support responsiveness before standardizing on Aurora.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic is $159/user/month or $1,620/year; Premium is $259/user/month or $2,640/year; Enterprise is custom. Basic and Premium include 50 projects/month for 1 user, and on-demand site models start at $9.99.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Mid-sized to large residential and commercial installers who need bankable accuracy and have dedicated design staff.</p>

### 2. OpenSolar – Best Free All-in-One

<p>OpenSolar offers a permanently free core platform with no licensing fees, funded through hardware and finance partnerships. It includes design, proposal creation, and basic CRM tools in one package.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> OpenSolar explains its free model directly: the core platform is funded by hardware, finance, distributor, permitting, and education partners instead of installer license fees. That keeps the design, proposal, and basic business workflow accessible for small installers.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> OpenSolar remains free for core use, but API and webhook access now runs through wallet products with a trial and paid charges after the trial. Teams that need external integrations, raw data access, or custom connected workflows should price that separately before assuming the whole stack is free.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core platform is free for solar professionals. API and webhook access use API Access or Raw Data API Access wallet products after a trial, and optional partner or imagery services may add costs.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small to mid-sized solar companies, new installers, and growing teams that need core design and sales tools without overhead.</p>

### 3. HelioScope – Best for Commercial and Utility-Scale

<p>HelioScope specializes in commercial and utility-scale design with bankable energy yield simulations. The drag-and-drop interface handles multi-MW ground-mount and commercial rooftop arrays effectively.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> HelioScope publishes C&I-focused Basic and Pro plans with per-license project limits. Basic covers 10 projects/month and designs up to 1.25 MW DC; Pro adds LIDAR-assisted modeling, AI-based obstruction detection, financial modeling, P90/P95/P99 values, and designs up to 5 MW DC.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> HelioScope is C&I-first, licensed per user, and limited to 10 projects/month on Basic and Pro monthly plans. Residential teams or companies that need a full CRM/sales operations suite will usually need another system around it.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic is $159/license/month or $1,620/year for 1 user and 10 projects/month. Pro is $259/license/month or $2,640/year. Enterprise is custom per-project pricing with unlimited users and higher capacity.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Commercial design teams, engineers, and solar companies specializing in C&I or utility-scale projects.</p>

### 4. Solargraf – Best for Enphase-Centric Installers

<p>Solargraf offers an annual solar design, proposal, permitting, financing, and CRM platform with project and user limits by plan. The public site emphasizes residential and commercial design workflows, automated permitting, interactive proposals, integrated financing, and CRM/workflow integrations.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The pricing page is unusually specific for this category: Starter, Small Business, Teams, and Enterprise publish annual prices, included users, included projects, and extra project/user costs. All plans include the core design, proposal, permitting, financing, and CRM toolset, while Enterprise includes public API access.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Solargraf is annual, project-limited, and user-limited below Enterprise. Public API access is included on Enterprise, but other plans list it as a $4,000 annual add-on. Test plan-set output, email/proposal delivery, and CRM integration needs during the demo before committing.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Starter is $2,799/year for 2 users and 240 projects. Small Business is $4,799/year for 4 users and 480 projects. Teams is $6,399/year for 6 users and 720 projects. Enterprise is $12,999/year with unlimited users and 1,500 projects. Extra projects run $9-$11 each depending on tier, and public API access is a $4,000 annual add-on unless you are on Enterprise.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Residential installers that want proposal, permitting, financing, and CRM workflows in one annual platform and can forecast project/user volume before buying.</p>

### 5. Solo (GoSolo) – Best for High-Volume Residential Sales

<p>Solo provides solar proposal software with annual platform plans, build-it-yourself Studio workflows, instant On-Demand proposals, and pay-as-you-go Concierge/Flex proposal production. It is built more around high-volume residential sales motion than C&I engineering depth.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Solo gives sales organizations three paths: build proposals internally in Solo Studio, generate simpler proposals instantly through On-Demand, or use Concierge/Flex for done-for-you proposal production. Flex pricing is useful when volume swings and you do not want a full platform contract.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Solo's annual platform plans start at $800/month, so small installers can outgrow the budget quickly. Flex lowers the commitment but does not include Solo Studio or On-Demand access, and the platform remains more residential-sales oriented than C&I design oriented.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Annual platform plans start at Core $800/month, Pro $1,600/month, and Elite $3,200/month. Concierge Flex is separate pay-as-you-go pricing: $32/proposal for 1-99 monthly proposals, $27 for 100-499, and $24 for 500+; revisions are $10 each.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> High-volume residential sales groups and small-to-medium installers with lean teams who want fast, done-for-you proposals.</p>

Evaluate Solargraf when annual project/user pricing and proposal-to-permitting workflow fit your volume. Solo works for high-volume residential shops that want Studio, On-Demand, or Concierge/Flex proposal production.</p>
