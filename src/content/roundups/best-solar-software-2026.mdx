## Do You Need This Yet?

Solar software pays for itself when you're designing enough systems that manual shading analysis and permit plan prep start eating into your sales time. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're only doing a few small residential installs a year and your utility or AHJ accepts basic drawings. A free tool with standard roof assumptions still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if permits are getting rejected for inaccurate shading calculations, or if financing partners are questioning your production estimates.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Aurora Solar. It can generate automated permit plan sets directly from a sales proposal, which saves hours of engineering time and is used by 80% of the top 75 U.S. installers.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Aurora Solar – Best for Precision Design and Sales

<p>Aurora Solar leads on design precision with AI-powered 3D modeling and LIDAR shading analysis. The platform can generate automated solar permit plan sets directly from a sales proposal, which contractors report saves hours of engineering time.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Aurora's automatic modeling handles complex roof geometries and generates bankable production estimates that financing partners accept. Wood Mackenzie data shows 80% of the top 75 U.S. installers use Aurora, indicating strong industry trust.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> A surveying technician on G2 noted, "Their automatic modeling feature is either spot on or missing a lot. Sometimes the trees could be represented more accurately." Trustpilot reviews cite slow customer service on billing issues, with two-week response times. Premium pricing scales quickly with add-ons, and there's no built-in CRM.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic $159/mo per user | Professional and Enterprise require sales quotes.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Mid-sized to large residential and commercial installers who need bankable accuracy and have dedicated design staff.</p>

### 2. OpenSolar – Best Free All-in-One

<p>OpenSolar offers a permanently free core platform with no licensing fees, funded through hardware and finance partnerships. It includes design, proposal creation, and basic CRM tools in one package.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> A contractor on FeaturedCustomers reported, "We were paying thousands of dollars per year on a design tool that was way slower and less accurate. Now all of our team use OpenSolar." The zero-cost entry point eliminates budget barriers for new installers.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> U.S. utility integrations can be limited compared to paid competitors. Custom branding and proposal styling feel basic. Starting April 2026, API access and third-party connector fees may create hidden costs for teams needing integrations.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Free forever | API and connector fees apply for advanced integrations.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small to mid-sized solar companies, new installers, and growing teams that need core design and sales tools without overhead.</p>

### 3. HelioScope – Best for Commercial and Utility-Scale

<p>HelioScope specializes in commercial and utility-scale design with bankable energy yield simulations. The drag-and-drop interface handles multi-MW ground-mount and commercial rooftop arrays effectively.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Engineers value the sharable reports and simulation depth for large arrays. The platform generates bankable energy yield data that EPCs and developers trust for project financing.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> HelioScope is not built for residential projects or small-scale design. It requires additional platforms to manage the full sales process. The layout process feels manual compared to newer automated tools, and one-user-per-license limits team collaboration.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic $159/mo (10 projects limit) | Professional and Enterprise tiers available.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Commercial design teams, engineers, and solar companies specializing in C&I or utility-scale projects.</p>

### 4. Solargraf – Best for Enphase-Centric Installers

<p>Solargraf offers deep native integration with Enphase IQ batteries and microinverters, including direct push to Enphase commissioning tools. It focuses on fast, phone-friendly proposal creation for residential installers.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The Enphase integration allows installers to push designs directly to commissioning tools and field apps. Interactive web quotes and financing integration help close sales faster.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> A Software Advice reviewer wrote, "I hate it. I constantly have problems with plansets." Users report glitchy 3D modeling that bogs down computers, unreliable email delivery from the app, and support wait times that have grown longer. Enphase ownership creates bias toward its own hardware.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Starter $2,799/year (2 users, 240 projects) | Additional project fees apply beyond tier limits.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Residential installers who primarily sell Enphase equipment and want fast, phone-friendly proposal creation.</p>

### 5. Solo (GoSolo) – Best for High-Volume Residential Sales

<p>Solo provides a managed design service with DNV-certified production estimates and lender-ready proposals delivered in as fast as 15 minutes. The platform includes built-in financing tools for residential installers.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The managed service model removes design work from internal teams while maintaining accuracy. Solo's production estimates achieved 97% accuracy in an independent validation of 1,899 systems, according to SurgePV.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Solo does not offer a self-service trial, forcing contractors into a sales process before evaluation. Pricing escalates quickly with monthly costs ranging from $1,600 to $8,000 or more. The managed service model removes design control from in-house teams, and the platform is residential-only.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core $800/mo | Flex $32 per proposal | Enterprise requires sales quotes.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> High-volume residential sales groups and small-to-medium installers with lean teams who want fast, done-for-you proposals.</p>
