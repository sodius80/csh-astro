## Do You Need This Yet?

Glass & glazing software starts to make sense when custom orders, supplier lead times, and fragile material handling create mistakes a generic scheduling app cannot catch. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a small shop doing mostly standard installs with predictable inventory. A general field service app or even a paper log still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you've ever lost a job because glass arrived at the wrong site, or if you're tracking custom orders across multiple suppliers in spreadsheets.

If you're in the middle—growing but not buried in admin yet—start with Glazier or Smart Glazier. Both are built around glass workflows, while Projul is the safer fit when predictable flat construction pricing matters more than trade-specific glass modules.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Glazier – Best All-in-One Glass Operations

<p>Glazier builds quoting, scheduling, purchase orders, field operations, payments, and QuickBooks integration around glass-company workflows. The public site emphasizes residential/service teams and commercial contract teams rather than generic field service.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Glass-specific quoting, purchase orders, job timelines, field apps, and QuickBooks integration are presented as native parts of the workflow rather than workarounds in a general FSM tool.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The vendor does not publish a simple monthly rate card. Small shops need a demo and quote before they can compare it fairly against Smart Glazier Design, GlassManager, or Projul.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote. The public site offers demos and ROI calculators, but not a published plan table.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Established glass contractors who need integrated quoting, scheduling, field operations, purchasing, and billing.</p>

### 2. Smart Glazier – Best for Design

<p>Smart Glazier combines glass design, quoting, modules, field management, scheduling, ordering, invoicing, and reporting. The current pricing page lists Smart Glazier Design from $150/month and shows larger Business examples that cost more depending on modules and users.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Integrated glass design tools connect directly to quoting, with optional modules and broader Business workflows for shops that need more than design-only quoting.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Pricing depends heavily on modules, office users, and whether you need Design only or the fuller Business product. That makes quote comparison harder than the headline $150/month starting point suggests.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Smart Glazier Design starts at $150/month for one user with the glass designer and one additional design module. Smart Glazier Business starts around $230/month and can run higher depending on modules, integrations, and office/field users.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Glaziers who need design capabilities integrated with business management and want modular shop workflows beyond design-only quoting.</p>

### 3. Projul – Best Flat Pricing

<p>Projul offers construction field service management with estimating, scheduling, field updates, job costing, and flat annual pricing. It is not glazing-specific, but teams can configure job stages and field records around custom order workflows when predictable construction pricing matters more than trade-specific glass modules.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Flat annual pricing with no per-user fees makes budgeting predictable for teams that need many office and field users.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> General construction focus means missing glazing-specific design and fabrication modules. Annual-only pricing also creates a larger upfront commitment than monthly tools.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Projul Core starts at $4,788/year. Core+ is $7,188/year and Pro is $14,388/year. Projul does not currently publish monthly billing, and the no-per-user model is the main budget advantage.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Growing glazing teams who want predictable costs and solid custom order tracking without glazing-specific requirements.</p>

### 4. FeneVision – Enterprise Only

<p>FeneVision provides ERP capabilities for design, production, and supply chain management in fenestration. The platform targets manufacturers rather than field service contractors, making it complex and expensive for most glazing businesses.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> ERP coverage for fenestration manufacturing workflows from design through production and supply chain management.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Built for manufacturers, not field service contractors. Complex implementation and expensive licensing make it overkill for install-only glazing businesses.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quotes only, typically enterprise-level investments.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Large fenestration manufacturers who need full ERP capabilities, not field service contractors.</p>

### 5. Jobber – Budget Pick

<p>Jobber delivers standard field service management with scheduling, estimates, invoicing, and client management. While reliable and well-supported, it doesn't address glazing-specific needs like custom glass order tracking.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Reliable platform with good support and proven track record. Lower entry cost makes it accessible for small operations that only need generic scheduling, quotes, and invoices.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No glazing-specific features or custom glass order tracking. Higher Jobber tiers add more users and automation, so costs can climb as the team grows.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core starts at $29/month with annual billing on Jobber's current pricing page. Higher tiers, team plans, add-ons, and billing terms change the total.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small glazing shops focused on simple install scheduling who don't need custom order complexity.</p>

## FAQ

<p><strong>Do I need glazing-specific software or will general field service work?</strong></p>
<p>If you handle custom glass orders with lead times and fragile material tracking, glass-specific software like Glazier or Smart Glazier gives you workflow coverage that generic field service tools usually miss. General platforms like Jobber work for simple install scheduling but miss much of the order complexity.</p>

<p><strong>What's the difference between per-user and flat pricing?</strong></p>
<p>Per-user pricing scales with your team size. Smart Glazier Design starts at $150/month, while Business starts around $230/month and can run higher once modules and office users are added. Projul Core starts at $4,788/year with no per-user fees; Core+ and Pro cost more, but the flat annual model can be easier to budget for teams that need many users.</p>

<p><strong>Can these platforms handle custom glass cutting and ordering?</strong></p>
<p>Glazier and Smart Glazier include glass-specific quoting and order workflows. Projul can be configured around custom order stages, but it is not glazing-specific. Jobber and FeneVision don't focus on this workflow.</p>
