## Do You Need This Yet?

Excavation software pays for itself when you're running enough equipment that manual hour logging and fuel tracking starts hiding which jobs are actually profitable. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a solo operator with one excavator and track hours on a clipboard. Simple logging still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're running multiple machines across different sites and can't tell whether a job made money after fuel, operator wages, and maintenance costs.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Projul. The field time tracking, daily logs, and job-costing workflow give you visibility into true costs before you commit to a heavy-civil enterprise system.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Projul – Best published-price job costing option

<p>Projul is a general construction management platform with estimating, scheduling, time tracking, daily logs, job costing, and budgeting. Its own FAQ lists excavation and grading contractors among supported specialties, but it is not a dedicated heavy-civil production system.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Projul publishes flat-rate annual pricing, avoids per-user fees, and emphasizes field time tracking, mobile updates, live construction costs, and job-costing/budgeting visibility.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> It is still general construction software. Excavation contractors that need heavy-civil production quantities, equipment-specific workflows, and pay-item tracking should compare it against HeavyJob before committing.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core starts at $4,788/year. Core+ is $7,188/year, and Pro is $14,388/year. Projul says it does not currently offer a monthly billing option.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Excavation contractors who want published-price job costing, time tracking, daily logs, and mobile field updates without an enterprise quote</p>

### 4. Contractor Foreman – Best for budget-conscious

<p>Contractor Foreman provides project planning, scheduling, billing, and estimating on fixed-fee plans with published user limits. It lacks excavation-specific features.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Affordable fixed-fee pricing and a low $49/month Basic entry point. User limits are published by tier, and a 30-day free trial is available.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Contractor Foreman's equipment and vehicle logs are lighter than dedicated heavy-civil production tracking, and the platform is not excavation-specific.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic starts at $49/month for 1 user on annual billing; Standard is $105/month for 3 users; Plus is $166/month for 8 users. Contractor Foreman offers a 30-day free trial.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Budget-conscious excavation contractors needing basic project management without heavy-civil production tracking</p>

### 5. Estimator360 – Best for project tracking

<p>Estimator360 focuses on construction estimating and project tracking. It is less established in excavation than HCSS, but it publishes per-location pricing instead of hiding everything behind a quote.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Construction estimating capabilities with project tracking features. Good for contractors who prioritize tracking over equipment management.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> It is less excavation-specific than HCSS or dedicated heavy-civil systems, and per-location pricing can add up for multi-location operations.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Contractors and lumberyards plan is $250/month per location on monthly billing or $200/month per location billed annually. Modular builder plans are higher, and Estimator360 offers a 21-day trial.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Excavation contractors needing basic project tracking and estimating capabilities</p>

## FAQ

<p><strong>Do I need excavation-specific software or will general construction software work?</strong></p>
<p>General construction software can work if it includes equipment tracking and job costing. Excavation contractors need to track equipment hours, fuel costs, and production rates more than office-based trades. Look for software that handles time-and-material billing and equipment management.</p>

<p><strong>What's the most important feature for excavation contractors?</strong></p>
<p>Equipment tracking. Machine hours, fuel, operator wages, and maintenance costs can turn a profitable-looking excavation job into a loss. Without tracking those costs against estimates, you can't tell which jobs are actually profitable. Job costing comes second, followed by scheduling and billing.</p>

<p><strong>How much should I expect to pay for excavation software?</strong></p>
<p>Published entry points range from Contractor Foreman's $49/month Basic plan when billed annually to Projul's $4,788/year Core plan and Estimator360's $250/month per-location monthly plan. HeavyJob and Procore require custom quotes, so large heavy-civil teams should expect a sales process rather than a public checkout price.</p>
