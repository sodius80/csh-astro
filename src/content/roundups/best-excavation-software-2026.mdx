## FAQ

<p><strong>Do I need excavation-specific software or will general construction software work?</strong></p>
<p>General construction software can work if it includes equipment tracking and job costing. Excavation contractors need to track equipment hours, fuel costs, and production rates more than office-based trades. Look for software that handles time-and-material billing and equipment management.</p>

<p><strong>What's the most important feature for excavation contractors?</strong></p>
<p>Equipment tracking. A single excavator costs $150-$200 per hour to operate. Without tracking equipment hours against job estimates, you can't tell if projects are profitable. Job costing comes second, followed by scheduling and billing.</p>

<p><strong>How much should I expect to pay for excavation software?</strong></p>
<p>Budget-friendly options start around $50/month. Professional-grade software with equipment tracking runs $4,000-$6,000 per year. Enterprise solutions require custom quotes that can reach $10,000+ annually depending on company size.</p>
