<h2>Do You Need This Yet?</h2>

<p>A free CRM makes sense when the team knows follow-up is the problem but nobody is ready to spend $50-100/month or more on a field service platform yet. Two quick checks:</p>

<p>- <strong>You don't need it yet</strong> if you manage 5-10 open quotes from memory, every prospect has been called back this week, and the owner personally owns every deal. A simple task list still works at that scale.</p>
<p>- <strong>You need it now</strong> if quotes sit unanswered for more than a week and nobody can say which lead source produces your best work. A free CRM creates structure without spending money.</p>

<p>If you are in the middle - growing, but not drowning - start with Freshsales or HubSpot Free. Set up the pipeline stages, enter 10 active deals, and see whether the team updates deal status after a week. If they do, the CRM is working. If they do not, the problem is process, not software.</p>

<h2>Free CRM vs Field Service CRM - Where the Line Is</h2>

<p>The most common mistake contractors make with free CRMs is expecting them to do field service work. A free CRM handles leads, contacts, pipelines, and follow-up. It does not handle scheduling, dispatch, invoicing, payments, job costing, or crew management. If you sign up for HubSpot expecting to send invoices from it, you will be disappointed.</p>

<p>The opposite mistake is skipping a free CRM entirely and paying $50-100/month for a field service tool when all you need is better follow-up. If you are a 1-3 person operation sending 20 quotes a month and losing a third of them to missed follow-up, a free CRM for three months will pay for itself in recovered jobs before you outgrow it.</p>

<p>Some contractors use both: a free CRM for sales pipeline and lead tracking, and a field service tool for operations. That works when the team can maintain two systems. It does not work when data lives in one tool and decisions are made from the other without reconciliation.</p>

<h2>Implementation Plan for Free CRM Users</h2>

<p>The first project should be smaller than most contractors expect. Do not start with every feature. Start with the follow-up rule: every lead needs an owner, a stage, a next action, and a due date. If those four fields are maintained, the CRM will prevent most lost follow-up before any advanced features are added.</p>

<p><strong>Week one:</strong> Set up pipeline stages: new lead, appointment scheduled, quote sent, follow-up due, won, lost, dormant. Enter every open lead from the last 30 days.</p>
<p><strong>Week two:</strong> Connect your business email to the CRM. Set up one lead capture form on your website if you have one.</p>
<p><strong>Week three:</strong> Review every lead older than seven days. Call the ones with no activity in the last week.</p>
<p><strong>Week four:</strong> Check which lead source produced the most won deals. Decide whether the CRM is helping enough to keep using it or whether it is time to upgrade to a paid tier or field service tool.</p>

<h2>When to Upgrade from Free to Paid</h2>

<p>Free tiers have hard limits. Watch for these signals:</p>

<p>- <strong>User limit hit.</strong> Freshsales is the only option with unlimited free users. If you outgrow HubSpot's 5-user or Zoho's 3-user limit, you need a paid tier or a different free CRM.</p>
<p>- <strong>Automation need.</strong> Free tiers do not include workflow automation. If the team is manually sending follow-up emails that could be automated, the time saved is worth the $9-20/user/mo upgrade.</p>
<p>- <strong>Integration need.</strong> Freshsales and HubSpot limit integrations on their free tiers. If you need CRM-to-QuickBooks sync, CRM-to-Jobber connection, or custom API workflows, the paid tier may be cheaper than manual data entry.</p>
<p>- <strong>Storage or record limit.</strong> Zoho's 5,000-record limit and 1 GB storage cap will hit for any contractor managing contacts, leads, deals, and subcontractors. HubSpot's unlimited contacts makes this less of an issue for most contractors.</p>

<h2>Where Each CRM Is a Wrong Fit</h2>

<p><strong>Freshsales</strong> is the wrong fit if you need more than one integration on the free tier. The single-integration cap means you cannot connect both QuickBooks and a marketing tool without upgrading.</p>

<p><strong>HubSpot Free</strong> is the wrong fit if you expect it to manage crews, appointments, invoices, or job costing. It is a good free CRM starting point, not field service software.</p>

<p><strong>Zoho CRM</strong> is the wrong fit if you have more than three people selling. The 3-user free tier limit means most contractor teams will hit the user cap quickly.</p>

<p><strong>Pipeline CRM</strong> is the wrong fit if the office wants one system for dispatch and invoicing. It is a sales CRM - the contractor still needs a clear handoff into field operations.</p>

<p><strong>Pipedrive</strong> is the wrong fit if your sales process is relationship-driven with long trust-building phases. The activity-based scoring creates pressure to log calls and emails that may not reflect how commercial contractor sales actually work.</p>

<h2>FAQ</h2>

<h3>What is the best free CRM for a contractor with multiple sales people?</h3>
<p>Freshsales, because it offers unlimited users on the free tier. HubSpot Free caps at 5 users and Zoho at 3 users. If the team has more than 5 people touching leads, Freshsales is the only genuinely free option that does not require an upgrade for headcount alone.</p>

<h3>Can I use HubSpot for my roofing or HVAC company?</h3>
<p>Yes, if you use it for lead tracking and pipeline management. No, if you expect it to dispatch techs, send invoices, or manage job costs. HubSpot is a sales CRM, not field service software. Many roofing and HVAC contractors use HubSpot alongside Jobber or Housecall Pro - HubSpot for the sales pipeline, the field service tool for operations.</p>

<h3>How long can a contractor realistically use a free CRM?</h3>
<p>Most contractors will hit a free-tier limit within 6-18 months. The most common trigger is needing automation (workflow sequences), more users, or integrations with other tools. The right approach is to start with a free tier, build the follow-up habit, and upgrade only when the limits genuinely cost you time or money.</p>

<h3>Are there any free CRMs that also handle invoicing and scheduling?</h3>
<p>Not on this list. Bitrix24's free tier (12 users) includes CRM, projects, invoicing, and time tracking, but it has a steep learning curve and its interface is cluttered. Zoho Invoice (free, up to 500 invoices per year) is an add-on to Zoho CRM that covers invoicing, but scheduling and dispatch still require separate tools.</p>

<h3>Should I start with a free CRM and upgrade, or pay for Jobber from day one?</h3>
<p>Start free if your problem is lead tracking. Pay for Jobber from day one if your problem is scheduling and dispatching. If you send quotes but struggle with follow-up, a free CRM for 3-6 months will pay for itself. If you need a calendar, crew assignments, and invoicing on day one, skip the free CRM and go straight to a field service platform.</p>

<h2>Bottom Line</h2>

<p><strong>Freshsales</strong> is the best all-around free CRM for contractor teams. The unlimited user cap on the free tier is unmatched, and the built-in phone and AI scoring make it genuinely useful, not just free.</p>

<p><strong>HubSpot Free</strong> is the best choice for contractors with a website generating leads, because email tracking, meeting scheduling, and contact enrichment turn visitors into tracked deals.</p>

<p><strong>Zoho CRM</strong> is the best pick for contractors who want customization depth and plan to grow into accounting and project management tools.</p>

<p><strong>Pipeline CRM</strong> and <strong>Pipedrive</strong> are better suited for contractors with dedicated sales staff who can justify a paid trial after the free period ends.</p>

<p>None of these tools replace field service software. If you need scheduling, dispatch, and invoicing alongside CRM, a free CRM should be your second system, not your only one.</p>
