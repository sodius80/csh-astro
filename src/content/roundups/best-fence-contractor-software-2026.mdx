## Do You Need This Yet?

Fence software pays for itself when you're doing enough volume that manual estimating and scheduling start eating into billable hours. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a solo operator doing fewer than 10 jobs per month and your customers don't expect instant digital quotes. Pen and paper, plus a simple calendar, still work fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're losing jobs because competitors send quotes faster, you're double-booking crews, or you're manually calculating linear-foot pricing more than twice a week.

If you're in the middle - growing but not overwhelmed - start with QuoteIQ, but do not stop at the $29.99/mo Essentials headline if satellite measurement is the feature you need. MapMeasure Pro starts on Beginner at $74.99/mo.

The important pricing nuance is plan fit: Essentials is the $29.99/month entry CRM, while Beginner at $74.99/month adds MapMeasure Pro, QuoteIQ Cam, Review Multiplier, and more AI credits.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> MapMeasure Pro gives fence contractors a way to measure property lines from satellite imagery before a site visit. <p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The $29.99 Essentials plan does not include MapMeasure Pro, QuoteIQ Cam, Review Multiplier, job costing, QuickBooks integration, or the heavier AI/tooling allowances that many fence companies will want. <p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Essentials starts at $29.99/mo for 1 user and includes a 14-day trial with a card required. <p><strong>Best for:</strong> Growing fence companies that want AI-powered tools and can justify the monthly cost through improved efficiency.</p>

### 2. Jobber - General field service for small fence crews

<p>Jobber serves field service businesses across multiple trades, including fencing operations. The platform provides solid scheduling, invoicing, and customer management features but lacks fence-specific tools like linear-foot calculations or material tracking.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Jobber offers proven reliability with thousands of field service companies. The scheduling system handles recurring maintenance work well, and customer communication tools keep clients informed about appointment times and job progress.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Fence contractors must adapt their workflows to generic field service tools. No linear-foot pricing means manual calculations, and material tracking requires workarounds since the software wasn't built for fence-specific inventory needs.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core starts at $29/mo with annual billing or $49 month-to-month on Jobber's public pricing page; higher Connect, Grow, and Plus tiers vary by included users, billing choice, and promotions.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small service businesses that handle fencing alongside other trades like landscaping or general repairs.</p>

### 3. FieldFuze - Low-cost field service management

<p>FieldFuze is a field service management platform built by Toricent Labs. Core at $49/month covers CRM, estimates, invoices, payment processing, and photo uploads with 3 seats included. Pro at $349/month adds job scheduling, e-signatures, time tracking, crew management, inventory, change orders, QuickBooks integration, and 15 seats.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The seat structure. Pro includes 15 seats at $349/mo, while many competitors charge per user or per technician. ServiceTitan requires a sales quote, so FieldFuze is easier to budget before a demo. On FieldFuze Core, a solo operator gets 3 seats for $49, or roughly $16 per person. That's competitive for small crews, though not free.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> FieldFuze is a general FSM, not a fence specialty tool. There's no linear-foot quoting, no pre-loaded post or panel databases, and no fence-specific material calculations. You'll need to build custom templates for fence work.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core is $49/mo with 3 seats and payment processing. Pro is $349/mo with 15 seats, scheduling, e-signatures, time tracking, crew management, inventory, change orders, and QuickBooks integration. Enterprise is $799/mo with 25 seats, and additional seats are $8.99 each. No setup fees or contracts are listed.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Fence companies that need a full operations platform and want more seats per dollar than competitors like Jobber or ServiceTitan.</p>

### 4. Builder Prime - CRM + estimating + production for fence

<p>Builder Prime combines CRM, estimating, and production management for fence contractors. The platform offers drag-and-drop scheduling calendars and customizable proposal templates but requires custom pricing discussions rather than transparent monthly rates.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The drag-and-drop calendar makes scheduling multiple crews across different job sites visual and manageable. Customizable proposal templates let fence contractors present professional estimates for different fence types and materials without starting from scratch each time.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Custom pricing means no upfront cost transparency, making budget planning difficult. The feature set may be more than smaller fence operations need, especially companies focused purely on installation rather than design-build projects.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom pricing requires sales consultation.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Larger fence operations managing multiple crews and complex projects where comprehensive CRM features justify custom pricing.</p>

### 5. Houzz Pro - Design-build with lead gen

<p>Houzz Pro focuses on design-build contractors who want lead generation bundled with project management. The platform works for fence contractors who handle design consultation and custom installations but offers less field-focused functionality than specialized alternatives.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Built-in lead generation from the Houzz marketplace connects contractors with homeowners actively looking for fence projects. Design tools help present custom fence options during the sales process.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> At $249/month for the Pro plan, Houzz Pro costs more than fence-specific alternatives while providing fewer field management tools. The platform focuses on the sales and design phase rather than day-to-day installation workflows.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> The Pro plan is published at $249/mo; Custom and Enterprise tiers require sales conversations, and advertising packages start separately.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Design-build fence firms that prioritize lead generation and custom project sales over field efficiency tools.</p>

## Bottom Line

For fence-specific estimating, QuoteIQ is the purpose-built choice, but the real entry point depends on which features you need: Essentials is $29.99/mo, while MapMeasure Pro satellite measurement starts on Beginner at $74.99/mo. For general scheduling and invoicing, FieldFuze Core at $49/mo includes 3 seats, while Jobber Core starts lower but includes only one user and scales through tier/user limits. Jobber remains a safe, established option for multi-trade contractors, starting at $29/month on Core with annual billing or $49 month-to-month before higher-tier features.

Avoid paying for features you won't use. Most fence contractors need estimating, scheduling, and invoicing - not complex CRM systems designed for large construction companies.
