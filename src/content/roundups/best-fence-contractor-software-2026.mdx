## Product Reviews

### 1. FieldFuze - Free with payment-processing monetization

<p>FieldFuze offers unlimited users, estimates, CRM, scheduling, and invoicing at no monthly cost. The company makes money through payment processing fees rather than subscription charges, making it accessible for fence contractors just starting out or those with seasonal cash flow.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> This is genuinely free software, not a limited trial. Fence contractors get full access to estimating, customer management, scheduling, and invoicing without user limits or feature restrictions. The revenue model through payment processing (2.9% fees) means no monthly bills during slow seasons.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> As a smaller platform, FieldFuze has fewer third-party integrations than established competitors. Contractors who need specific accounting software connections or advanced reporting may find limitations.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Free plan includes all core features. Revenue through payment processing at 2.9% per transaction.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> New fence companies with tight budgets or seasonal businesses that want to avoid monthly subscription fees.</p>

### 2. QuoteIQ - Per-linear-foot pricing + satellite measurement

<p>QuoteIQ specifically targets fence contractors with linear-foot pricing calculations and satellite measurement tools for fence layouts. The software tracks post and panel inventory while providing AI-powered features for estimating and job management.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The satellite measurement feature lets contractors calculate fence runs without site visits, while built-in linear-foot pricing handles the math fence companies actually use. Post and panel inventory tracking prevents the common problem of showing up to jobs without enough materials.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> At $29.99/month, QuoteIQ costs more than basic alternatives. The AI features, while useful, require a learning curve that may slow down contractors used to traditional estimating methods.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Starts at $29.99/mo with fence-specific features included.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Growing fence companies that want AI-powered tools and can justify the monthly cost through improved efficiency.</p>

### 3. Jobber - General field service for small fence crews

<p>Jobber serves field service businesses across multiple trades, including fencing operations. The platform provides solid scheduling, invoicing, and customer management features but lacks fence-specific tools like linear-foot calculations or material tracking.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Jobber offers proven reliability with thousands of field service companies. The scheduling system handles recurring maintenance work well, and customer communication tools keep clients informed about appointment times and job progress.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Fence contractors must adapt their workflows to generic field service tools. No linear-foot pricing means manual calculations, and material tracking requires workarounds since the software wasn't built for fence-specific inventory needs.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Plans range from $39/mo to $599/mo based on team size and features needed.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small service businesses that handle fencing alongside other trades like landscaping or general repairs.</p>

### 4. Builder Prime - CRM + estimating + production for fence

<p>Builder Prime combines CRM, estimating, and production management for fence contractors. The platform offers drag-and-drop scheduling calendars and customizable proposal templates but requires custom pricing discussions rather than transparent monthly rates.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The drag-and-drop calendar makes scheduling multiple crews across different job sites visual and manageable. Customizable proposal templates let fence contractors present professional estimates for different fence types and materials without starting from scratch each time.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Custom pricing means no upfront cost transparency, making budget planning difficult. The feature set may be more than smaller fence operations need, especially companies focused purely on installation rather than design-build projects.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom pricing requires sales consultation.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Larger fence operations managing multiple crews and complex projects where comprehensive CRM features justify custom pricing.</p>

### 5. Houzz Pro - Design-build with lead gen

<p>Houzz Pro focuses on design-build contractors who want lead generation bundled with project management. The platform works for fence contractors who handle design consultation and custom installations but offers less field-focused functionality than specialized alternatives.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Built-in lead generation from the Houzz marketplace connects contractors with homeowners actively looking for fence projects. Design tools help present custom fence options during the sales process.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> At $149/month, Houzz Pro costs more than fence-specific alternatives while providing fewer field management tools. The platform focuses on the sales and design phase rather than day-to-day installation workflows.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Starts at $149/mo for lead generation and project management features.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Design-build fence firms that prioritize lead generation and custom project sales over field efficiency tools.</p>

## Bottom Line

For new fence companies, FieldFuze wins on pure economics with genuinely free core features. Growing operations should consider QuoteIQ for fence-specific tools like linear-foot pricing and satellite measurement. Jobber works for multi-trade contractors who need reliable general field service management.

Avoid paying for features you won't use. Most fence contractors need estimating, scheduling, and invoicing - not complex CRM systems designed for large construction companies.
