## How to Choose

<p><strong>Site-based vs per-user pricing?</strong></p>
<p>Commercial janitorial operations work better with site-based pricing since you manage locations, not individual employees. Swept and SweepOps offer this model.</p>

<p><strong>Compliance requirements?</strong></p>
<p>If you work with property management companies or commercial clients requiring formal cleaning standards, look for platforms with documented inspection and checklist workflows like SweepOps.</p>

<p><strong>Bidding capabilities?</strong></p>
<p>Companies actively pursuing new contracts need integrated bidding tools. Janitorial Manager specializes in this area.</p>

## Do You Need This Yet?

Janitorial software pays for itself when you're managing enough sites that scheduling crews, tracking supplies, and running inspections start eating into your mornings. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're managing just a few small locations and your crews can remember the cleaning checklist. A basic scheduling app or even a group text still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're running inspections across multiple buildings, losing track of supply orders, or if clients are asking for compliance reports you can't produce quickly.

If you're in the middle, growing but not overwhelmed, start with Swept. It's purpose-built for commercial cleaning operations, with site-specific instructions and quality control tools that general FSM platforms don't include.

## FAQ

<p><strong>What's the difference between janitorial software and general FSM?</strong></p>
<p>Janitorial software includes site-specific features like security protocols, cleaning checklists, supply tracking, and inspection workflows. General FSM platforms focus on scheduling and invoicing but miss the operational details commercial cleaning requires.</p>

<p><strong>Do I need different software for residential vs commercial cleaning?</strong></p>
<p>Yes. Residential maid services can use general platforms like Jobber or Housecall Pro. Commercial janitorial operations need site-based management, compliance tracking, and security protocols that require specialized software.</p>

<p><strong>Why don't most janitorial platforms show pricing?</strong></p>
<p>Most target larger customers with complex site configurations. Swept, SweepOps, and Clean Smarts are the clearest exceptions with published starting pricing for smaller operations.</p>
