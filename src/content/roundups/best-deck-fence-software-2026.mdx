## Do You Need This Yet?

<p>Deck and fence software pays for itself when you're doing enough volume that manual material calculations and crew scheduling starts eating into build time. Two quick checks:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>You don't need it yet</strong> if you're a solo operator doing 2-3 jobs per month and can track materials on the back of an invoice. Basic estimating still works fine at that scale.</li>
<li><strong>You need it now</strong> if you're losing track of which crew is where, running out of materials mid-job, or spending evenings doing takeoffs you should have priced in the field.</li>
</ul>

<p>If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with FieldRate's free tier for deck estimating and a simple spreadsheet for scheduling. It's genuinely free for up to 3 takeoffs per month, so you can figure out your workflow needs before committing to a paid platform.</p>

## Product Reviews

### 1. FieldRate – Best for Deck Estimating

<p>FieldRate is the only tool on this list built from the ground up for deck contractors. It ships with pre-loaded material pricing for Trex, TimberTech, AZEK, pressure-treated lumber, and other brands deck builders use daily. You can build a complete estimate on your phone during the initial consultation and share a professional proposal before you leave the homeowner's driveway.</p>

<p>The free tier includes 3 takeoffs per month with basic estimating. For most solo operators just starting out, that's enough to test the workflow. When volume picks up, FieldRate Pro at $39 per month unlocks unlimited takeoffs, IRC structural auto-sizing, cut-optimized material lists, branded PDF exports, and full offline mode.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> FieldRate handles the math that generic estimating spreadsheets miss. It accounts for decking board spacing, joist spans, rail post spacing, and stair stringer counts—details that matter when you're pricing a composite deck at $50-75 per square foot. Cut optimization alone can save 8-12% on material costs on larger jobs by reducing waste from standard lumber lengths.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> FieldRate is estimating software, not a full business management platform. It won't handle invoicing, payment processing, or crew scheduling. Fence contractors will find limited use here—it's built for decks, not perimeter fencing or chain-link installs. you'll need to pair it with a general FSM like FieldFuze Core or Jobber for the operational side of your business.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Free for 3 takeoffs/month. Pro at $39/mo (annual) or $49/mo (month-to-month) for single user with unlimited takeoffs. Business at $79/mo adds team collaboration for up to 5 members.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Deck contractors who need fast, accurate estimating with pre-loaded material databases and code-compliant structural calculations.</p>

### 2. FieldFuze – Best Low-Cost FSM

<p>FieldFuze is a field service management platform built by Toricent Labs, a company founded by contractors who got tired of paying per-user fees for tools that didn't fit how trades actually work. It covers CRM, estimates, invoices, payment processing, scheduling, inventory, time tracking, and QuickBooks integration.</p>

<p>The entry point is Core at $49 per month, which gives you CRM, estimates, invoices, payment processing, and photo uploads with 3 seats included. Additional seats run $8.99 each. For a two-person deck crew (you plus one installer), that's a reasonable starting price point.</p>

<p> Most contractors outgrow Core quickly. the Pro plan at $349 per month is where FieldFuze competes directly with ServiceTitan and Jobber. It includes 15 seats—enough for a crew of 10 plus office staff—plus job scheduling, e-signatures, time tracking, crew and contracts management, inventory, change orders, recurring jobs, leads pipeline, PDF merging, audit log, and two-way QuickBooks sync. Compared to ServiceTitan's $398+/mo starting point with just one seat and a $5,000+ setup fee, FieldFuze Pro is significantly more accessible.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The seat structure. Pro includes 15 seats at $349/mo, while competitors charge per-user. A 10-person crew on ServiceTitan would cost roughly $398 base plus $250-500 per additional tech—easily $2,500-4,000 monthly. On FieldFuze Pro, the same crew costs $349, or roughly $35 per person. That's not free, but it's competitive.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> FieldFuze is a general FSM, not a deck or fence specialty tool. You'll need to build your own material templates, and there's no pre-loaded lumber or composite pricing. For fence contractors, there's no per-linear-foot quoting or gate hardware databases. It works well enough if you set it up manually, but it won't match FieldRate's estimating speed out of the box.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core $49/mo, Pro $349/mo, Enterprise $799/mo. No setup fees, no contracts, month-to-month billing.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small to mid-sized deck and fence companies that need a full operations platform at a lower per-user cost than ServiceTitan or Jobber.</p>

### 3. Projul – Best Flat-Rate Construction Platform

<p>Projul was built by Kurt Clayson, a contractor who got tired of per-user pricing models that penalized growing crews. The result is flat-rate annual pricing with no per-user fees and no onboarding costs.</p>

<p>Core at $4,788 per year covers unlimited projects, CRM, estimates, e-signatures, invoicing, payment processing, lead capture forms, photo capture and markup, project management, reporting, scheduling, task management, templates, and premium support. For a 5-person deck crew, that's roughly $80 per person per month—competitive with general FSM tools but with construction-specific features built in.</p>

<p>Core+ at $7,188 per year adds unlimited subcontractors, change orders, a client portal, construction financials, job costing and budgeting, Gantt charts, linear project timelines, messaging, progress billing, time tracking, and QuickBooks Online integration. This is where Projul pulls ahead for contractors managing multi-phase projects with change orders.</p>

<p>Pro at $14,388 per year is for established operations. It adds unlimited users, assemblies (grouped material and labor items), automated client reminders, geofencing for time tracking, photo reports, purchase orders, QuickBooks Desktop integration, selections (allowing clients to pick finishes), and Spanish app translation.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The flat-rate model eliminates the scaling penalty. A 10-person crew and a 50-person crew both pay the same annual fee. Users report an average 32% profit increase, which Projul attributes to faster estimating, better job costing, and reduced administrative overhead. The premium support package is included free with annual plans and covers personalized workflow analysis, system customization, training, and data import.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Projul is general construction management, not deck or fence specific. There's no pre-loaded decking material database or fence-line measurement tool. Weather tracking is available but basic compared to dedicated construction weather apps. The upfront annual payment can strain cash flow for newer operations.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core $4,788/yr, Core+ $7,188/yr, Pro $14,388/yr. All annual plans include premium support. No monthly billing option.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Growing deck and fence companies that want predictable software costs and construction-specific features like job costing, change orders, and progress billing.</p>

### 4. BuildBook – Best for Residential Builders

<p>BuildBook positions itself as modern construction management software for residential home builders and remodelers. It competes in the same space as BuilderTrend and CoConstruct but with a simpler interface and transparent pricing.</p>

<p>The Solo plan runs approximately $99 per month and targets individual builders handling one to three projects at a time. The Business plan at roughly $349 per month is built for established companies with multiple crews and concurrent projects. Both tiers include unlimited projects, unlimited clients and subcontractors, and access to all features.</p>

<p>BuildBook covers project scheduling, client communication, photo and document management, estimating, selections, change orders, and warranty tracking. The selections feature is particularly useful for fence contractors offering multiple styles—wood privacy, vinyl, aluminum, or chain-link—since clients can pick materials and styles within the platform.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The unlimited-projects model means you won't hit caps as you grow. The interface is clean and modern, with less learning curve than heavier platforms like Procore. For fence contractors doing residential remodels alongside their fence work, BuildBook handles both trades in one system.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> BuildBook is not fence or deck specific. There's no per-linear-foot pricing automation, no pre-loaded lumber or composite databases, and no structural sizing calculations. Compared to FieldRate's deck estimating speed or Projul's job costing depth, BuildBook sits in the middle—good for project management, light on specialty estimating.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Solo ~$99/mo, Business ~$349/mo. Annual plans typically include a discount. All features included, no add-ons or seat limits.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Fence contractors and residential remodelers who want modern project management with client-facing features like selections and photo galleries.</p>

### 5. Jobber – Best General FSM Value

<p>Jobber is a well-established field service management platform with a strong reputation for reliability and customer support. It's built for general trades—HVAC, plumbing, landscaping, cleaning—and works adequately for deck and fence companies that don't need specialty estimating.</p>

<p>Jobber's pricing starts around $49 per user per month for basic scheduling, estimates, invoicing, and client management. Higher tiers add route optimization, automated notifications, and QuickBooks integration. Compared to FieldFuze Core at $49/mo for 3 seats, Jobber's per-user model is more expensive for multi-person crews but more affordable for solo operators.</p>

<p>For deck and fence work specifically, Jobber handles the operational side—scheduling crews, sending invoices, tracking payments—but won't help with material takeoffs, board counts, or fence-line measurements. You'll need to handle estimating externally in Excel or a specialty tool like FieldRate.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Reliability and support. Jobber has been around since 2011, has thousands of customers, and offers phone, email, and chat support. The platform rarely goes down, integrations are well-maintained, and the mobile app is polished. For contractors who prioritize stability over specialty features, Jobber delivers.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No deck or fence-specific features. No material databases, no structural sizing, no fence-line measurements. The per-user pricing model gets expensive as crews grow—a 5-person crew on Jobber's mid-tier plan can easily run $250-400 per month, while FieldFuze Pro covers 15 seats for $349.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Starting around $49/user/mo. Free trial available.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Solo deck and fence operators or very small shops that need basic scheduling, invoicing, and client management without specialty estimating.</p>

## Bottom Line

<p>For deck estimating, FieldRate is the clear choice. The free tier handles 3 takeoffs per month, and Pro at $39 gives you unlimited estimates with pre-loaded material pricing and structural auto-sizing. No other tool on this list matches its deck-specific accuracy.</p>

<p>For full business management, FieldFuze Core at $49/mo is the lowest-cost entry point among platforms that handle scheduling, invoicing, and CRM. Pro at $349/mo undercuts ServiceTitan and Jobber on per-user cost for crews of 5 or more.</p>

<p>For growing operations that hit the limits of basic FSM tools, Projul's flat-rate annual pricing ($4,788-14,388/yr) eliminates per-user scaling penalties and adds construction-specific features like job costing, change orders, and progress billing.</p>

<p>Avoid general tools like Jobber unless you're a solo operator who only needs basic scheduling and invoicing. For the same per-user cost as Jobber, FieldFuze Core gives you 3 seats and payment processing built in.</p>
