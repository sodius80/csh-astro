<p>GC estimating is a different problem than subcontractor estimating. You’re not just doing takeoffs on your own scope. You’re managing bid packages from multiple subs, leveling bids to compare apples to apples, tracking scope gaps, and assembling a complete project cost picture from pieces that don’t always fit cleanly together. The software needs to handle that coordination, not just generate numbers.</p>

<p><strong>Right for:</strong> GCs running competitive commercial or multi-phase residential bids who need bid leveling, sub coordination, and scope gap tracking built into the workflow.</p>

<p><strong>Not for:</strong> Single-trade subs who just need faster measurements. Simpler takeoff tools cost less and do the job without the overhead.</p>

## Product Reviews

### 1. Procore Estimating – Best for Large Commercial GCs Already on Procore

<p>If your operation is already running Procore for project management, the estimating module makes real sense. Bid packages connect directly to drawings, RFIs, and submittals. When a scope item changes, it flows through the whole project. For a commercial GC managing multiple active bids on complex projects, that integration is worth something.</p>

<p>If you’re not already in the Procore ecosystem, the math doesn’t work. The estimating module only delivers its value as part of the full platform, and the full platform is a significant investment. Implementation runs $5,000 to $15,000 on top of the subscription, and that’s before training your team.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Once the system is set up, it’s genuinely hard to go back to anything else. The integration between bid management and active project execution is unmatched.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The ramp-up time. Reviewers mention 3 to 6 months before the estimating module feels natural, and several mention that the value doesn’t click until the whole team is using it consistently.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Enterprise only (no published rates). Implementation cost is additional.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Commercial GCs at $10M+ annual revenue already committed to Procore across the whole operation.</p>

### 2. Buildertrend – Best for Residential GCs and Custom Home Builders

<p>Buildertrend’s estimating is built around the residential workflow, and it shows. The change order approval process through the client portal is the standout feature. Homeowners get a clean, professional interface to review and approve changes, which cuts down on the back-and-forth that kills residential project margins.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The client tools are worth the price. Buildertrend is a client communication and project management tool with estimating built in, not the other way around.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The pricing story is complex. Essential gets you the basics ($499/mo), but the features that actually make it worth it start appearing in the Advanced tier at $799 and the Complete tier at $1,099. By the time you add what you actually need, you’re looking at $9,600 to $13,000 a year.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Essential $499/mo | Advanced $799/mo | Complete $1,099/mo.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Residential remodeling GCs and custom home builders who need client-facing tools connected to their estimates and change orders.</p>

### 3. STACK – Best Cloud-Based Takeoff Without Full PM Software

<p>STACK does one thing well: fast digital takeoffs from PDF plans in a browser, with no software to install and no hardware requirements beyond a laptop. For a GC doing competitive bidding across multiple project types who doesn’t need (or want) a full project management platform attached, this is a clean option.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The pricing is straightforward compared to most of the competition. The free plan covers basic measurements. Professional starts at $149/mo and unlocks the features most GCs actually need for serious bid work.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> STACK is a takeoff tool, not a full estimating system. It handles the measurement side well, but the cost database and bid assembly side is less developed than dedicated estimating platforms.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Free plan available | Starter $69/mo | Professional from $149/mo.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Competitive bidding GCs who need faster takeoffs and cloud storage for plans, without committing to a full PM platform.</p>

### 4. PlanSwift – Best Dedicated Takeoff Tool for High-Volume Estimators

<p>PlanSwift is the tool contractors reach for when they’re doing a lot of takeoffs and speed matters. The desktop software is purpose-built for plan measurement and quantity extraction. Users who have learned it well report completing takeoffs 3 to 5 times faster than manual methods.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The business model is a perpetual license at roughly $1,749 per seat. You own the software. For a GC who estimates regularly and wants to own the tool rather than pay forever, the perpetual license makes financial sense within two years.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> It’s desktop-based Windows software with a UI that hasn’t changed much in years. There’s no mobile access, no cloud storage for your plans, and no collaboration features. It feels dated compared to browser-based competitors.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> ~$1,749 perpetual license per seat (subscription options available).</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> GCs with dedicated estimators doing high-volume bids who need speed and precision over collaboration features.</p>

### 5. Sage Estimating – Best for Enterprise Operations in the Sage Ecosystem

<p>Sage Estimating is built for large commercial GCs who are already using Sage 300 Construction & Real Estate for financials. The integration between estimating and accounting is the value proposition. When an estimate becomes a project, the cost codes flow through without manual entry.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> For an operation doing tens of millions in revenue with a full accounting department, that integration matters deeply.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The implementation experience. Sage Estimating typically requires a certified consultant to set up, adding $5,000 to $15,000 before anyone on your team has touched the software. The learning curve is significant—several reviewers mention 6 months before the system felt natural.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Enterprise pricing (contact Sage for a quote; typically $3,000+/year).</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Large commercial GCs ($50M+ revenue) already running Sage 300 who need estimating connected directly to their financial system.</p>

## How to Choose

<p>The most expensive mistake GCs make with estimating software is buying an enterprise platform before they need it. Here’s the honest framework:</p>

<ul>
<li>If you’re doing residential work, custom homes, or design-build projects under $5M a year, <strong>Buildertrend</strong> is probably the right answer. The client tools pay for themselves.</li>
<li>If you’re doing competitive commercial bids and need fast takeoffs without committing to an ecosystem, <strong>STACK</strong> at $149 a month handles the measurement side well.</li>
<li>If you estimate a lot of jobs and speed is the bottleneck, <strong>PlanSwift's</strong> perpetual license model is worth the upfront cost. You’ll recoup it within a year if you’re pricing regularly.</li>
</ul>

## Bottom Line

<p>For most GCs reading this, the right starting point is STACK or Buildertrend depending on whether your work skews commercial/bidding or residential/client-facing. Both have free trials. Both are usable within a week. Neither requires an implementation consultant.</p>

<p>Procore and Sage are real tools for the right operation. Just make sure you’re at the stage where you can absorb the implementation cost and the learning curve without it disrupting active projects.</p>
