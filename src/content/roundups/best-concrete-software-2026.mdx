## Do You Need This Yet?

Concrete software pays for itself when you're doing enough volume that manual pour scheduling and crew coordination starts costing you slabs. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a one-truck flatwork crew and your biggest scheduling challenge is a whiteboard in the shop. A shared calendar still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're coordinating multiple pours per week across different batch plants and weather windows, and a missed lead time or cold joint is costing you rework.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Projul. The flat-rate pricing won't punish you for adding crew members, and the weather-window pour scheduling helps you avoid the costly mistakes that come from scaling too fast without coordination.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Projul – Best All-in-One for Small to Mid-Size Concrete Contractors

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The flat-rate pricing model at $4,788/year for unlimited users eliminates the per-seat penalties that hurt concrete contractors with large crews. One G2 reviewer reported, "I cancelled three other subscriptions" after switching to Projul's all-in-one platform.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Smaller integration marketplace compared to enterprise competitors like Procore. G2 and Capterra reviewers note a learning curve during the first one to two weeks while office staff sets up estimate templates.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> $4,788/year flat rate for unlimited users.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Two-truck flatwork crews up to mid-size structural concrete companies that want CRM, estimating, scheduling, job costing, and invoicing in one platform without per-user penalties.</p>

### 2. Fieldwire – Best Field Coordination and Plan Management

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The offline-first mobile plan viewer works without cell coverage, crucial for concrete crews working in areas with poor signal. The sheet comparison feature helps catch design changes between plan revisions.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No built-in estimating, invoicing, or job costing capabilities. Users on Capterra and Software Advice report outgrowing the platform quickly once they need financial tracking beyond basic budgeting. Several reviews note customer support response times have changed since the Hilti acquisition.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Free for up to 5 users and 3 projects | Pro $39/user/month | Business $59/user/month | Business Plus $89/user/month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Mid-size concrete contractors focused on field coordination, punch lists, and mobile documentation rather than office-based estimating or accounting.</p>

### 3. eSUB – Best for Commercial Subcontractors

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Built specifically for subcontractor workflows with strong change order and time-and-materials tracking from GCs. Software Advice reviewers give high marks for ease of use, especially for field staff with limited computer experience.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Per-user pricing gets expensive for larger crews typical in concrete work. The interface is functional but less polished than competitors, according to reviews on Software Advice and Softabase. Separate fees are required to integrate with accounting solutions.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> $49-$85 per user/month depending on plan level.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Commercial concrete subcontractors working for multiple GCs who need mobile field management, submittal tracking, and RFI workflows tailored to sub workflows.</p>

### 4. STACK – Best for Cloud Takeoff and Estimating

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> True cloud-based digital takeoff with real-time collaboration on plans and integrated estimating spreadsheet. G2 and Software Advice users praise the speed and accuracy of cloud takeoff.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> One Software Advice user noted, "Importing material prices is VERY cumbersome." No dedicated mobile app for takeoff (mobile browser is read-only). General construction focus means concrete-specific calculations for rebar and post-tension are not as thorough as purpose-built tools.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Start plan $2,599/year per user | Grow/Build plans $5,499+/year per user.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Estimators and project managers at concrete companies that bid 10+ commercial projects monthly and need multi-user cloud collaboration on complex plans.</p>

### 5. Procore – Best for Large Commercial Operations

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Deepest feature set of any platform, with 500+ integrations, unlimited users, and strong RFI/submittal management for complex commercial projects. Enterprise users call it the industry standard.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Consistently described as overkill for small to mid-size contractors. One specialty concrete sub on Reddit reported, "We currently use Procore but they offer more tools than we use and they are increasing our cost to $30k a year. Feels like we are paying too much." A Capterra user noted, "There is a large learning curve. The software is not intuitive."</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quotes only; typically $10,000+/year based on annual construction volume. One Capterra reviewer running $8M annual work was quoted over $15,000/year.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Large commercial GCs and concrete subs managing multi-million dollar projects with dozens of subcontractors who need enterprise-grade reporting and owner-facing documentation.</p>
