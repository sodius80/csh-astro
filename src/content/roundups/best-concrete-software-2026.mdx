## Do You Need This Yet?

Concrete software pays for itself when you're doing enough volume that manual pour scheduling and crew coordination starts costing you slabs. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a one-truck flatwork crew and your biggest scheduling challenge is a whiteboard in the shop. A shared calendar still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're coordinating multiple pours per week across different batch plants and weather windows, and a missed lead time or cold joint is costing you rework.

If you're in the middle -- growing but not overwhelmed -- start with Projul. The flat-rate annual pricing won't punish you for adding crew members, and its estimating, scheduling, job-costing, and QuickBooks options help you avoid the costly mistakes that come from scaling too fast without coordination.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Projul -- Best All-in-One for Small to Mid-Size Concrete Contractors

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Projul publishes flat-rate annual pricing starting at $4,788/year with no per-user fees, which avoids the per-seat penalties that hurt concrete contractors with large crews. The plan structure covers contractor workflows like estimating, scheduling, project management, invoicing, and reporting at entry level, while higher tiers add job costing, change orders, client portal, purchase orders, and QuickBooks integration.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Smaller integration marketplace compared to enterprise competitors like Procore. Office staff should expect setup work around estimating templates, job-cost categories, and QuickBooks workflows before the system feels natural.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> $4,788/year Core, $7,188/year Core+, and $14,388/year Pro; Projul says plans have no per-user fees.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Two-truck flatwork crews up to mid-size structural concrete companies that want CRM, estimating, scheduling, job costing, and invoicing in one platform without per-user penalties.</p>

### 2. Fieldwire -- Best Field Coordination and Plan Management

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The mobile plan viewer and task workflow are useful for concrete crews working in areas with poor signal. Paid tiers add reports, sheet compare, project templates, forms, integrations, RFIs, submittals, and change orders as teams move up the plan ladder.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No built-in estimating, invoicing, or job costing capabilities. Users on Capterra and Software Advice report outgrowing the platform quickly once they need financial tracking beyond basic budgeting. Several reviews note customer support response times have changed since the Hilti acquisition.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic free | Pro $39/user/month billed annually | Business $64/user/month billed annually | Business Plus $89/user/month billed annually.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Mid-size concrete contractors focused on field coordination, punch lists, and mobile documentation rather than office-based estimating or accounting.</p>

### 3. eSUB -- Best for Commercial Subcontractors

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Built specifically for subcontractor workflows with strong change order and time-and-materials tracking from GCs. Software Advice reviewers give high marks for ease of use, especially for field staff with limited computer experience.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> eSUB does not publish simple self-serve pricing on its current site, so budgeting requires a quote. The interface is functional but less polished than competitors, according to reviews on Software Advice and Softabase, and accounting integration costs should be confirmed during the sales process.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Quote required. Current third-party directory pages list eSUB pricing as contact-vendor or no public pricing, so verify current cost directly with eSUB instead of relying on stale per-user estimates.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Commercial concrete subcontractors working for multiple GCs who need mobile field management, submittal tracking, and RFI workflows tailored to sub workflows.</p>

### 4. STACK -- Best for Cloud Takeoff and Estimating

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> True cloud-based digital takeoff with real-time collaboration on plans and integrated estimating spreadsheet. G2 and Software Advice users praise the speed and accuracy of cloud takeoff.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> One Software Advice user noted that importing material prices can be cumbersome. STACK now publishes Takeoff & Estimate pricing at $249-$299/user/month billed annually, and contractors should confirm mobile workflow limits before relying on it for field-heavy estimating.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Takeoff & Estimate pricing starts at $249/user/month billed annually for Premium and $299/user/month billed annually for Pro. Build & Operate is priced separately, starting at $599/user/year for Essential, with a 30-day trial account advertised for Build & Operate.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Estimators and project managers at concrete companies that bid 10+ commercial projects monthly and need multi-user cloud collaboration on complex plans.</p>

### 5. Procore -- Best for Large Commercial Operations

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Broad enterprise feature set with 500+ integrations, unlimited users, and RFI/submittal management for complex commercial projects.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Consistently described as overkill for small to mid-size contractors. Procore prices by product mix and annual construction volume, so the only reliable way to compare it is to get a written quote against the exact modules you need.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote only. Procore says pricing depends on the products you need and annual construction volume, with unlimited users, unlimited data, and support included in annual contracts.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Large commercial GCs and concrete subs managing multi-million dollar projects with dozens of subcontractors who need enterprise-grade reporting and owner-facing documentation.</p>
