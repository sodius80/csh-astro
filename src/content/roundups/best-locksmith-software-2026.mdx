## Do You Need This Yet?

Locksmith software pays for itself when you're doing enough volume that juggling emergency dispatches and scheduled installs starts costing you calls. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a solo locksmith taking walk-ins and occasional mobile calls, tracking jobs in your phone. Basic Google Calendar still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're missing emergency calls while you're on a job, double-booking rekeys, or losing money because you can't dispatch efficiently.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Housecall Pro. It handles both real-time emergency dispatch and scheduled appointments in one platform, and the two-way customer communication keeps you from losing jobs when you're hands-deep in a lock repair.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Housecall Pro – Best Overall

<p>Housecall Pro delivers real-time dispatch and scheduling that handles both emergency lockouts and scheduled installations. Two-way communication keeps customers updated on tech arrival times.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Industry reviews consistently call it the #1 choice for locksmith dispatch, with real-time updates that work well for emergency calls.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No locksmith-specific features like key tracking or lock inventory management. You're paying general FSM pricing without trade customization, and teams beyond one user move into higher tiers or add-on seats.</p>

### 2. SchedulingKit – Best for Solo Operations

<p>SchedulingKit combines emergency and scheduled job management with an AI receptionist that handles calls when you're busy with a lockout. Solo locksmiths can start with the free tier.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The AI receptionist feature helps capture calls when you're hands-deep in a lock repair, and the free tier lets solo operators test without commitment.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Limited features for multi-tech operations and less brand recognition than established players in the field service space.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Free plan includes 2 members, 3 event types, and 100 bookings/month. Paid plans start at $10/seat/month on annual billing or $12 month-to-month, with a 14-day Business trial.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Solo locksmiths who need help managing calls and want to start without upfront costs.</p>

<p>Synchroteam provides scheduling, dispatch, and route optimization for field service operations. Unlike many FSM tools, it publishes Standard and Premium per-user pricing, with route optimization reserved for Premium.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Route optimization features help when you're running multiple jobs in a day across different areas.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Still not locksmith-specific, and route optimization requires Premium. The interface and feature set skew toward general field service rather than emergency locksmith workflows.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Standard is shown as $49/user/month or $39/user/month on annual billing. Premium is shown as $79/user/month or $64/user/month on annual billing. A 14-day free trial is advertised.</p>

## Bottom Line

<p>Housecall Pro wins for most locksmith contractors who need proven dispatch and customer communication. SchedulingKit makes sense for solo operators who want AI call handling without upfront costs. Jobber offers solid value for small teams that don't need emergency-specific features.</p>
