<p>Small contractors do not all mean the same thing when they say estimating software. A remodeler pricing a bathroom, a designer selling a visual renovation package, and a service contractor quoting a repair need different tools. This shortlist focuses on three products that cover those three paths: <a href="/clear-estimates-review/">Clear Estimates</a>, <a href="/houzz-pro-review/">Houzz Pro</a>, and <a href="/jobber-review/">Jobber</a>.</p>

## How to Choose Between These Three

<p>Start with the estimate you actually write. If the estimate is a remodel scope with labor, materials, markup, repeatable line items, and homeowner-ready proposal language, Clear Estimates is the cleanest fit. The database and templates are the reason to buy it. You still need to tune numbers to your market, crew, supplier pricing, and waste factors, but you are not starting from a blank sheet every time.</p>

<p>If the estimate is part of a visual sale, Houzz Pro makes more sense. A design-build contractor may need the customer to understand options, selections, 3D plans, payment flow, and project communication before signing. Houzz Pro can support that broader sales motion. The tradeoff is cost and breadth. It is easy to pay for more workflow than you need if your estimates are simple.</p>

<p>If the estimate is usually a service quote, Jobber is the practical choice. HVAC, plumbing, electrical, landscaping, handyman, cleaning, pressure washing, and similar service businesses often need quote follow-up, schedule handoff, invoice timing, payment collection, and customer history more than a construction cost database. Jobber is strongest when the quote is one step in that service workflow.</p>

<p>Do not buy the cheapest plan just because the starting number looks friendly. The usable plan is the one that includes your real workflow. Clear Estimates Standard may cover a remodeler well. Houzz Pro Essential may be too light if you need advanced project tools. Jobber Core may be enough for simple quotes, but quote follow-ups, job costing, two-way SMS, automations, and larger teams sit higher in the plan ladder.</p>

## Product Reviews

<h3>1. Clear Estimates - Best focused estimating tool for small remodelers</h3>

<p>Clear Estimates is the closest match for the phrase small contractor estimating software. It is not trying to be a full field-service system, CRM, or project management suite. The value is a maintained estimate-building environment: line items, templates, pricing updates, proposal documents, invoices, and optional integrations.</p>

<p>The official pricing page lists Standard at $79 per month monthly or $59 per month annually. Pro is $119 monthly or $99 annually. Franchise starts at $249 monthly or $199 annually, with final pricing based on customization. Standard includes unlimited estimates and customers, a 13,000+ line item database, 200+ preloaded templates, quarterly labor and material rate updates, payable invoices, and no contract. The same pricing page also states a 30-day free trial.</p>

<p>That combination is why Clear Estimates is the top pick here. A small remodeler does not always need dispatch, route planning, marketing tools, or client dashboards. Often the real pain is building a consistent number fast enough to send while the customer is still warm. Clear Estimates directly attacks that problem.</p>

<p>The caution is local accuracy. A cost database is a starting point, not a substitute for knowing your crews, suppliers, waste, markup, and scope language. Treat the database as a maintained baseline, then tune it until it reflects how your company actually builds.</p>

<h3>2. Houzz Pro - Best for design-heavy residential estimates</h3>

<p>Houzz Pro is a better fit when estimating is part of a bigger homeowner sales process. Its pricing page shows a broad product: estimates, proposals, invoices, payments, CRM, QuickBooks integration, 3D floor plans, selections, project schedules, daily logs, budgets, bid management, marketing tools, and client collaboration. That is a lot more than a quote builder.</p>

<p>Atlas-verified pricing shows Free Basic Tools, Essential at $149 per month monthly or $99 per month annually, Pro at $249 monthly or $159 annually, several Custom tiers, Enterprise at $1,600 per month, advertising from $499 per month, and additional users at $60 per user per month. The page also separates software from advertising, which matters for budgeting.</p>

<p>The best Houzz Pro buyer is a design-build remodeler, interior designer, or residential contractor whose sale depends on presentation. If 3D planning, selections, proposals, client communication, payments, and Houzz visibility help close work, Houzz Pro can make sense as the center of the sales workflow.</p>

<p>The wrong buyer is a contractor who only needs to send a quote. In that case, Houzz Pro's breadth becomes overhead. You may pay for client presentation, marketing, and project collaboration features when the actual need is a tighter estimating process.</p>

<h3>3. Jobber - Best for service contractors that quote in the field</h3>

<p>Jobber is not a construction estimating engine, and that is the point. For many service contractors, the estimate does not need a remodel cost database or takeoff workflow. It needs to become a scheduled job, customer record, invoice, payment request, reminder, and follow-up process without the office retyping everything.</p>

<p>The official pricing page lists Core from $29 per month on annual billing for one user or $49 monthly, Connect from $99 per month annually for one user or $149 annually for five users, Grow from $149 annually for one user or $299 annually for ten users, and Plus at $529 annually for fifteen users. Monthly no-commitment rates are $49, $199, $399, and $699. Jobber also promotes a 14-day free trial with no credit card.</p>

<p>Core includes professional quotes, invoices, online payments, a website, reporting, and app marketplace access. Higher plans add automated reminders, quote and invoice follow-ups, QuickBooks Online, time and expense tracking, job costing, two-way SMS, workflow automations, and marketing tools. That is why Jobber is a strong fit for service companies.</p>

<p>Do not force Jobber into the wrong job. If you need assemblies, detailed takeoffs, or a remodel cost library, use Clear Estimates or a construction estimating product. If you need service quoting tied to schedule and payment workflow, Jobber is the better test.</p>

## Final Verdict
