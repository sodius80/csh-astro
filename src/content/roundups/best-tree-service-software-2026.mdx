## Do You Need This Yet?

Tree service software pays for itself when you're running enough crews that manual scheduling and equipment tracking starts costing you removals and billable hours. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a solo climber managing a single route and tracking equipment maintenance on a clipboard. Basic invoicing still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're double-booking crews, losing track of which chipper needs maintenance, or can't tell which PHC program customers are due for service.

If you're in the middle, growing but not overwhelmed, start with ArboStar. It handles arborist-specific equipment tracking and offline mobile functionality that many general field-service platforms do not emphasize, so you can manage multiple crews with less risk of losing field data when cell coverage drops.

## Product Reviews

### 1. ArboStar: Best Arborist-Built Platform

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Equipment and fleet management with GPS/live job map tracking for vehicles, tools, and equipment, including maintenance scheduling and fuel/cost oversight alongside active job data. ArboStar also publishes offline field functionality for time tracking, forms, estimates, leads, and job progress updates.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No self-service trial is listed, and ArboStar publishes a $250/month starting point rather than fixed tiers. That pricing makes the most sense when multiple crews can use the equipment, GPS, reporting, and offline field features. Solo operators should confirm whether the monthly minimum and setup effort are justified before committing.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> From $250/month for a tailored plan; final pricing requires a custom quote and varies by license count and configuration.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Mid-size to larger tree care companies (3+ crews) that want deep industry-specific workflows and equipment tracking.</p>

### 2. Jobber: Best Generalist Field Service Platform

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Client Hub self-service portal where customers approve quotes, view appointments, and pay invoices without calling the office. Public review feedback often praises Jobber's customer-facing tools and ease of use, though it remains a generalist platform rather than tree-specific software.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Crew-side mobile experience gets clunky. Multiple reviewers note field staff struggle to log time and find assigned work quickly. Team pricing, payment processing, and add-ons can climb quickly as you add crews, so quote your actual user count instead of relying on the solo starter price.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core starts at $29/month with annual billing or $49 month-to-month. Connect, Grow, team plans, Plus, extra users, and add-ons change the total; use Jobber's current pricing page for your exact crew size.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small to mid-size tree services that want a proven, generalist platform with strong customer-facing tools.</p>

### 3. Arborgold: Best for Tree Inventory and PHC

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Tree and plant inventory mapping with GPS coordinates, species tracking, DBH measurements, and condition notes. Includes an Auto-Price calculator for systematic estimating tied to labor, materials, and equipment costs.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Software Advice and Software Finder reviewers describe the software as feeling outdated, with email deliverability problems in some accounts. Public review feedback also mentions slow performance and mobile limitations, so teams should test proposal delivery and field workflows before committing.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Starter is commonly listed at $129/month on annual billing or $149 month-to-month. Professional and Enterprise pricing varies by billing context and package, so confirm the current quote and add-on user costs before buying.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Established tree, lawn, and landscape companies doing $500K+ annually that need detailed plant health care tracking and multi-year renewal management.</p>

### 4. SingleOps: Best for PHC Program Workflows

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Recurring and multi-stage workflows purpose-built for the green industry, including linked plant-health-care programs where follow-up services auto-generate after a removal. Job-level cost tracking shows profitability while work is in progress.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Public App Store feedback includes iOS stability complaints, including proposal-drafting problems and lost work. Annual billing is cheaper, but SingleOps also charges more for additional office or sales users.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Essential is $220/month or $200/month annually; Plus is $385/month or $350/month annually; Premier is $550/month or $500/month annually. Additional office/sales users and add-ons can increase the total.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Green-industry companies managing complex PHC programs and multi-day jobs, provided they have dedicated admin staff to handle the learning curve.</p>

### 5. GorillaDesk: Best Budget Option for Small Crews

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Per-route pricing with unlimited free admin users. A solo operator or two-person tree crew pays for routes, not seats, which keeps costs lower than per-user competitors.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> GorillaDesk is a general field service platform with strong pest, lawn, pool, and cleaning positioning, so tree-specific features are thin. User feedback notes slow mobile syncing, limited offline access, and an invoice design that some reviewers prefer to replace with accounting software output.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic $49/month per route | Pro $99/month | Growth $149/month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Solo operators and small crews (1-3 routes) that need simple scheduling and invoicing at a low published entry price.</p>

## FAQ

<p><strong>Do I need tree-specific software or will general field service software work?</strong></p>
<p>General platforms like Jobber work fine for basic scheduling and invoicing. Tree-specific platforms like ArboStar and Arborgold add value when you need equipment tracking, plant health care programs, or detailed tree inventory management.</p>

<p><strong>What's the real cost difference between per-user and per-route pricing?</strong></p>
<p>For a solo operator, GorillaDesk's $49/month per route and Jobber's $29/month annual Core plan are both low-entry options, but they price growth differently. GorillaDesk charges by route/schedule; Jobber changes by plan, included users, extra users, and add-ons. Price your actual crew count before deciding.</p>

<p><strong>Why don't these platforms offer longer free trials?</strong></p>
<p>Tree service software requires significant setup time for customer lists, service templates, and crew schedules. Most vendors prefer demos over trials to ensure proper onboarding and reduce abandonment rates.</p>
