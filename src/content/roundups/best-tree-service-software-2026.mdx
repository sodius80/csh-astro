## Do You Need This Yet?

Tree service software pays for itself when you're running enough crews that manual scheduling and equipment tracking starts costing you removals and billable hours. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a solo climber managing a single route and tracking equipment maintenance on a clipboard. Basic invoicing still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're double-booking crews, losing track of which chipper needs maintenance, or can't tell which PHC program customers are due for service.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with ArboStar. It handles arborist-specific equipment tracking and offline mobile functionality that generic platforms can't match, so you can manage multiple crews without losing your field data when cell coverage drops.

## Product Reviews

### 1. ArboStar – Best Arborist-Built Platform

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Equipment management module with GPS-integrated tracking for every vehicle, chainsaw, and chipper, including maintenance history and repair costs alongside active job data. Users on Software Advice consistently praise the arborist-specific design and responsive support.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No self-service trial available, and pricing isn't transparent on the vendor website. As one ArboristSite user noted: "ArborStar would be my first choice if we were running 3+ crews," suggesting solo operators may find the cost hard to justify. Mobile app lacks some desktop functions according to Software Finder feedback.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Starter $150/month per user | Professional plans require custom quotes.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Mid-size to larger tree care companies (3+ crews) that want deep industry-specific workflows and equipment tracking.</p>

### 2. Jobber – Best Generalist Field Service Platform

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Client Hub self-service portal where customers approve quotes, view appointments, and pay invoices without calling the office. Reviewers across G2 and Capterra call this the biggest admin time-saver.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Crew-side mobile experience gets clunky. Multiple reviewers note field staff struggle to log time and find assigned work quickly. Long-term users report pricing has escalated sharply while support quality declined. Annual costs for a 10-user team can approach $10,000 with add-ons.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core $39/month | Teams Connect $169/month | Teams Grow $349/month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small to mid-size tree services that want a proven, generalist platform with strong customer-facing tools.</p>

### 3. Arborgold – Best for Tree Inventory and PHC

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Tree and plant inventory mapping with GPS coordinates, species tracking, DBH measurements, and condition notes. Includes an Auto-Price calculator for systematic estimating tied to labor, materials, and equipment costs.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Capterra and Software Advice reviewers describe the software as feeling outdated, with email deliverability problems. As one verified Capterra user reported: "Emails with proposals and invoices are only received by clients about 50% of the time... The software tells you 'email sent successfully,' but that's often a lie."</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Starter $129/month | Professional $299/month | Enterprise $499-$573/month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Established tree, lawn, and landscape companies doing $500K+ annually that need detailed plant health care tracking and multi-year renewal management.</p>

### 4. SingleOps – Best for PHC Program Workflows

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Recurring and multi-stage workflows purpose-built for the green industry, including linked plant-health-care programs where follow-up services auto-generate after a removal. Job-level cost tracking shows profitability while work is in progress.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> App Store reviewers report the iOS app crashes frequently when drafting proposals and lacks auto-save, leading to lost work. One reviewer called it "a fundamentally unreliable, unstable, bug-ridden application." Contractors on ArboristSite complain about system lag and poor customer service.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Essential $220/month | Plus $385/month | Premier $550/month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Green-industry companies managing complex PHC programs and multi-day jobs, provided they have dedicated admin staff to handle the learning curve.</p>

### 5. GorillaDesk – Best Budget Option for Small Crews

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Per-route pricing with unlimited free admin users. A solo operator or two-person tree crew pays for routes, not seats, which keeps costs lower than per-user competitors.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Roughly 89 percent of Capterra reviewers are in pest control, and the platform's DNA shows. Tree-specific features are thin. User feedback notes slow mobile syncing, limited offline access, and an invoice design that looks less professional than QuickBooks.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic $49/month per route | Pro $99/month | Growth $149/month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Solo operators and small crews (1-3 routes) that need simple scheduling and invoicing at the lowest possible entry price.</p>

## FAQ

<p><strong>Do I need tree-specific software or will general field service software work?</strong></p>
<p>General platforms like Jobber work fine for basic scheduling and invoicing. Tree-specific platforms like ArboStar and Arborgold add value when you need equipment tracking, plant health care programs, or detailed tree inventory management.</p>

<p><strong>What's the real cost difference between per-user and per-route pricing?</strong></p>
<p>For a solo operator, GorillaDesk's $49/month beats Jobber's $39/month once you add the second user. For teams of 3+ users, per-user pricing usually costs less than multiple route fees.</p>

<p><strong>Why don't these platforms offer longer free trials?</strong></p>
<p>Tree service software requires significant setup time for customer lists, service templates, and crew schedules. Most vendors prefer demos over trials to ensure proper onboarding and reduce abandonment rates.</p>
