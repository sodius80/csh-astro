## Do You Need This Yet?

Restoration software pays for itself when you're handling enough claims that moisture documentation and insurance workflows start becoming a bottleneck. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a startup doing mostly direct-pay work with simple photo documentation. A shared drive and a basic job board still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're missing moisture reading deadlines, losing track of equipment on jobs, or if your Excel sheets are costing you money on insurance claims.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start by demoing PSA and Albi side by side. PSA is stronger when accounting and production need to live together; Albi is easier to price publicly because its $60/$100 user model and $6,000 annual minimum are published.

## Product Reviews

### 1. PSA (Canam Systems) – Best ERP for Multi-Branch Operations

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Real-time job costing connects field work directly to accounting, and PSA's restoration-specific modules keep jobs, CRM, accounting, analytics, and customer communication in one stack. One Capterra reviewer noted, "We are much more organized using PSA than we ever were before."</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Contractors report that exporting reports to Excel is frustrating and requires heavy manual manipulation. The desktop accounting module is not cloud-based, which feels outdated compared to modern platforms.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Canam Systems does not publish current PSA list pricing on its site. Software Advice lists PSA starting at $325/month, but treat that as a third-party listing and confirm the live quote, user assumptions, contract term, and onboarding scope with PSA.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Medium to large restoration companies with 6+ users, multi-branch oversight, or franchise groups that need accounting tied directly to production.</p>

### 2. Xcelerate Restoration Software – Best for Water-Damage-Focused Shops

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The "master job" feature links related projects for the same customer, and the interface is widely described as more intuitive than legacy restoration platforms. Setup time is measured in days, not weeks.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Users and competitors report that Xcelerate is not highly customizable, forcing contractors into preset KPIs and workflows. It lacks native field moisture mapping, relying on integrations instead.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Xcelerate's operating-software site routes buyers to a strategy session and does not publish current job-management prices. Do not rely on older $55/$85 per-user figures without a written quote; its separate Growth website/marketing product has its own published packages starting at $299/month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Mid-size companies (1-5 trucks, $500K-$2M revenue) focused primarily on water damage mitigation that want structured workflows without complex setup.</p>

### 3. Albiware – Best for Customization & Mobility

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Highly customizable workflows, CRM automation, mobile documentation, and restoration-specific user roles. The current FAQ publishes the important pricing constraint clearly: a $6,000 minimum annual subscription met through Base and Pro users.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No XactAnalysis integration due to Verisk integration policies. Per-user pricing gets expensive for larger teams, and some contractors note that complex automations require Zapier connections.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Albi publishes a $6,000 minimum annual subscription. Base users are $60/user/month and Pro users are $100/user/month; confirm your exact Base/Pro mix before comparing it with flat-rate platforms.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Growing mid-market restoration companies that need flexibility, strong mobile capabilities, and month-to-month contract terms.</p>

### 4. DASH by Next Gear (CoreLogic) – Best for TPA & Insurance-Centric Shops

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Deep restoration job-management infrastructure for carrier-heavy and TPA work: configurable workflows, compliance tasks, equipment, documentation, timesheets, and the broader CoreLogic/Next Gear ecosystem. Verify any specific Xactimate or XactAnalysis workflow you need during the demo rather than assuming it is included by default.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The infrastructure is nearly 20 years old. Users report occasional slowness, lagging, and app crashes during photo capture. One Software Advice reviewer noted, "Dash is very easy to navigate, but the only complaint is that it can be slow."</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> CoreLogic Next Gear does not publish current DASH list pricing on the product page. Software Advice lists DASH from $595/month, while Albi's comparison content cites $750–$1,150/month plus onboarding; treat both as third-party references and confirm your quote directly.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Large restoration companies doing heavy TPA and preferred vendor work where carrier integration matters more than modern interface design.</p>

### 5. JobNimbus – Best Sales-Oriented Option for Smaller Teams

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Highly intuitive job board and photo documentation, plus built-in invoicing and payment collection. The sales-led evaluation path means teams should confirm trial or sandbox access in writing before relying on it.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> JobNimbus lacks restoration-specific features like moisture mapping, equipment tracking, or IICRC compliance prompts. Multiple reviewers report frequent mobile app crashes and describe the email system as unreliable.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote. JobNimbus now publishes plan tiers and limits but not dollar amounts; pricing depends on CRM plan, role-specific project-management users, and add-ons. Confirm any trial, sandbox, or demo access in writing.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Smaller restoration or exterior teams that care more about visual sales/job boards than moisture mapping, equipment tracking, and carrier-specific restoration workflows.</p>

## Bottom Line

<p>PSA wins for established companies that need accounting tied to production. Xcelerate makes the most sense for water damage shops that want structured restoration workflows and are willing to get a quote. Albiware is the easiest of the restoration-specific platforms to price because it publishes the $60/$100 user model and $6,000 annual minimum. DASH is the right demo when carrier and TPA workflow depth trumps everything else. JobNimbus works for sales-oriented smaller teams, but do not treat older third-party dollar estimates as current pricing.</p>

<p>The biggest mistake is choosing based on features alone. Pick based on your revenue stage, contract flexibility needs, and how much of your work flows through insurance carriers.</p>

## FAQ

<p><strong>What's the difference between restoration software and general contractor software?</strong></p>
<p>Restoration platforms include moisture mapping, equipment tracking, IICRC compliance prompts, and insurance claim documentation that general construction tools lack. They're built for water, fire, and mold remediation workflows.</p>

<p><strong>Do I need restoration-specific software if I only do 20% restoration work?</strong></p>
<p>Probably not. If restoration is a small part of your business, a general contractor platform with good photo documentation might be sufficient. Consider restoration software when insurance claims become 40%+ of your revenue.</p>

<p><strong>Which platform integrates best with Xactimate?</strong></p>
<p>DASH/Next Gear is the strongest demo candidate for carrier-heavy and TPA workflows, but verify the specific Xactimate or XactAnalysis handoff you need during the sales process. PSA and Xcelerate also emphasize restoration integrations. Albiware states it lacks XactAnalysis integration due to Verisk policies.</p>
