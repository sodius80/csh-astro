## Do You Need This Yet?

Remodeling software pays for itself when you're running enough concurrent projects that manual selections tracking and homeowner communication starts eating into margin. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're doing one project at a time with a simple spreadsheet and group texts. A shared Google Drive still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're losing track of cabinet choices homeowners approved three weeks ago, or your change orders are sitting unsigned because clients never see them.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Buildertrend. The selections portal and homeowner client communication tools keep homeowners informed without the constant phone calls, so you can focus on production while you figure out what other systems you need.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Buildertrend – Best for established remodelers

<p>Buildertrend offers a broad remodeling package with estimating, selections portal, homeowner portal, warranty management, change orders, scheduling, and QuickBooks sync. The selections and client communication tools are the reason remodelers should evaluate it ahead of general construction software.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The selections portal lets homeowners view and approve finishes, fixtures, and materials online. That is the practical remodeling workflow difference versus general construction tools.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Quote-based pricing makes it harder for small shops to compare total cost up front. Complex setup can take weeks, and you should confirm onboarding, package scope, and renewal terms before signing.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom quote. Buildertrend now describes subscription pricing as tailored to company needs, feature requirements, and business goals rather than publishing the old flat plan rates.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Remodelers with 6+ staff running multiple concurrent projects who can justify the monthly cost with better client communication.</p>

### 2. RedTeam Flex – Best for integrated estimating

<p>RedTeam Flex provides integrated project management with estimating workflows, financials, document control, and contract management. It is strongest for remodelers who bid complex projects and can live with a system aimed more at commercial contractors.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The estimating module connects bid data to project management and job-cost tracking, so teams can avoid re-entering project financials after a job is awarded.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> RedTeam's current pricing page describes monthly pricing based on Active Project Value instead of publishing a stable public monthly tier. Implementation packages start at $2,750, and the system leans commercial rather than residential, missing remodeling-specific features like selections management.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> APV-based monthly quote. RedTeam says Active Project Value is the aggregate value of in-progress projects that drives the monthly fee. Implementation and onboarding packages start at $2,750.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Remodelers who bid complex projects and need tight integration between estimating and project management.</p>

### 3. Projul – Best flat-rate pricing

<p>Projul starts at $4,788/year for Core. The pricing page emphasizes no per-user fees, unlimited projects, and included onboarding/support. Core+ adds client portal, job costing and budgeting, time tracking, progress billing, and QuickBooks Online integration; Pro adds selections and QuickBooks Desktop.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Flat $4,788 annual starting pricing with no per-user fees means team growth does not create the same seat-count math as per-user products. The mobile app covers field workflows such as time tracking and photo uploads.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Built as general construction software, not remodeling-specific. The Core entry plan does not include the full remodeler workflow: client portal and job costing start with Core+, while selections require Pro.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core starts at $4,788 per year. Core+ and Pro are higher annual plans for client portal, job costing, QuickBooks, and selections needs.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small-to-mid remodelers who want predictable annual costs and good mobile functionality for crews.</p>

### 4. Houzz Pro – Best for solo operators

<p>Houzz Pro combines client management, project tools, marketing, and basic estimating with lead generation capabilities. It works well for solo remodelers who need to attract clients and manage basic project workflows.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Built-in lead generation from the Houzz platform means potential clients can find you and request quotes directly. The client-facing project tools help with basic communication.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Light project management features work for simple jobs but not complex remodels. Not designed for multi-crew operations or detailed job costing.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Houzz Pro publishes a Pro plan at $249/month with 1 seat included and additional users at $60/user/month. Custom and Enterprise plans require a sales conversation; targeted advertising packages start separately at $499/month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Solo remodelers who need lead generation and basic project management in one platform.</p>

### 5. Contractor Foreman – Best budget option

<p>Contractor Foreman provides fixed-fee construction management plans with published user limits. The $49/mo Basic plan is the entry point, while scheduling and fuller project-management features require higher tiers.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The low $49/month Basic entry point and fixed-fee upgrade path. User limits are 1, 3, 8, 15, or unlimited depending on tier, so price the tier that matches your actual team size.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> General construction software without remodeling-specific features. No selections portal or advanced homeowner communication tools.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic starts at $49/month for 1 user on annual billing; the Unlimited tier removes the internal user cap.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Budget-conscious remodelers who need basic construction management without remodeling-specific features.</p>

## FAQ

<p><strong>Do I need remodeling-specific software or will general construction software work?</strong></p>
<p>General construction software works for simple remodeling, but established remodelers benefit from features like selections portals and homeowner communication tools. If you're managing client choices for finishes and fixtures, remodeling-specific features save significant time.</p>

<p><strong>What's the biggest difference between these platforms?</strong></p>
<p>Homeowner communication capabilities. Buildertrend has more client-facing remodeling workflow than options like Contractor Foreman, which treat homeowners like any other contact. The difference matters when clients live in the space you're remodeling.</p>

<p><strong>Should I pay for software if I'm only doing 2-3 projects per year?</strong></p>
<p>Probably not. The monthly costs of comprehensive remodeling software make sense when you're running multiple concurrent projects. Solo operators doing occasional remodels can manage with basic tools and upgrade when volume increases.</p>
