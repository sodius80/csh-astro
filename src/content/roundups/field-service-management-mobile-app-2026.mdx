## Do You Need This Yet?

A field service management app pays for itself when you're dispatching enough crews that text-message scheduling and paper invoices start creating real problems. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a solo operator who books three jobs a week by text and your customers never ask for status updates. A simple calendar and invoicing tool still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're sending crews to the wrong address, losing invoices in truck dashboards, or fielding customer calls asking where the tech is.

If you're in the middle—growing but not buried in admin yet—start with Jobber. The mobile app covers the core field workflow, pricing is public, and team plans can cover small crews before you need to look at enterprise tools.

## Product Reviews

### 1. Jobber -- Best All-in-One for Small to Mid-Size Contractors


<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Jobber publishes strong current iOS and Android mobile ratings on its mobile-app page. The field app supports job details, forms, photos, time tracking, signatures, invoices, payments, and customer communication, while the client hub lets customers approve quotes and pay online without calling you.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Pricing changes materially by billing term, team plan, user count, and add-ons. The online booking widget is basic compared to Housecall Pro, and reporting is good but not as deep as ServiceTitan.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core starts at $29/month with annual billing. Connect, Grow, team plans, Plus, extra users, billing term, promotions, and add-ons change the total; verify the current mix against your user count before buying.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Service contractors who want scheduling, quoting, invoicing, and client management in a mobile app with broad field workflow coverage.</p>

### 2. Housecall Pro -- Best for Automation and Marketing


<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The automated review request alone justifies the cost for many contractors. One additional review per month often pays for the software. The price book and upselling tools are the best in this group.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Housecall Pro can be more expensive than Jobber at comparable user counts. Test iOS and Android workflows during the trial, and compare payment terms directly because card rates and payment mix change the real cost.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Basic is $59/month annually or $79 month-to-month; Essentials is $149/month annually or $189 monthly; MAX is $299/month annually or $329 monthly, with additional users at $35/month.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Residential service contractors who want automated review requests, price books, and customer financing built into the platform.</p>

### 3. ServiceTitan -- Best for Enterprise Operations


<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> ServiceTitan publishes packages around dispatching, scheduling, call booking, invoicing, pricebook, mobile estimates, reporting, marketing attribution, and larger operating teams.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> ServiceTitan uses sales-led, per-technician pricing. Implementation, modules, training, and data migration can make the total much higher than small-shop tools, so confirm timeline and scope in the quote. The mobile app requires more training than Jobber or Housecall Pro.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Sales-led, per-technician custom quote. ServiceTitan does not publish a fixed public dollar rate card; confirm modules, implementation, and user/technician assumptions directly.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Large service contractors with 20+ techs who need dispatching, call booking, and reporting at an enterprise level.</p>

### 4. Workiz -- Best for Quick Setup


<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Fast setup and a lighter interface than enterprise platforms. Workiz publishes annual pricing for Kickstart, Standard, and Pro, while Ultimate is still handled through sales.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Mobile polish is more subjective than the feature checklist. Workiz advertises offline field access with sync after reconnecting, but integrations and reporting remain lighter than larger competitors.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Workiz publishes annual pricing at $225/month for Kickstart with the first 3 users, $275/month for Standard with the first 5 users, and $325/month for Pro with the first 5 users. Ultimate is quote-based; confirm extra users, phone/AI features, communications, and billing term in writing.</p>

## FAQ

<p><strong>Do I need a field service app if I only have one crew?</strong></p>
<p>Probably not yet. A solo operator or single crew can often manage with text messages and a simple invoicing tool. Add FSM software when dispatch errors start costing you jobs.</p>

<p><strong>Which app has the best offline mobile functionality?</strong></p>
<p>Do not buy on a generic offline checkbox. Jobber supports offline work for job forms/checklists, visit details/notes, and time tracking with later sync. Housecall Pro supports offline viewing for schedule and job details, but says offline editing is not currently supported. ServiceTitan and Workiz both advertise offline field functionality. Test your exact offline workflow—schedule access, forms, signatures, payments, and sync behavior—during the trial or demo.</p>

<p><strong>Can I switch FSM platforms without losing customer data?</strong></p>
<p>Most platforms offer CSV exports for customers and job history, but formatting varies. Plan for 1-2 days of cleanup when switching. Jobber and Housecall Pro offer migration assistance.</p>

<p><strong>Do these apps integrate with QuickBooks?</strong></p>
<p>Jobber syncs with QuickBooks Online. Housecall Pro lists QuickBooks Online and Desktop on Essentials and higher. ServiceTitan lists QuickBooks Online and Desktop integrations. Workiz pricing highlights QuickBooks Online, while its QuickBooks integration page advertises Online and Desktop; confirm Desktop support and required plan before buying.</p>
