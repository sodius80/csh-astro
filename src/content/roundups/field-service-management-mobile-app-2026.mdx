## Do You Need This Yet?

A field service management app pays for itself when you're dispatching enough crews that text-message scheduling and paper invoices start creating real problems. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're a solo operator who books three jobs a week by text and your customers never ask for status updates. A simple calendar and invoicing tool still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're sending crews to the wrong address, losing invoices in truck dashboards, or fielding customer calls asking where the tech is.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Jobber. The mobile app is genuinely field-friendly, techs usually pick it up within a day, and it scales cleanly up to about 15 users before you need to look at enterprise tools.

## FAQ

<p><strong>Do I need a field service app if I only have one crew?</strong></p>
<p>Probably not yet. A solo operator or single crew can often manage with text messages and a simple invoicing tool. Add FSM software when dispatch errors start costing you jobs.</p>

<p><strong>Which app has the best offline mobile functionality?</strong></p>
<p>Jobber and Housecall Pro both handle limited offline use. Techs can view schedules and capture signatures without signal, though syncing requires connectivity. ServiceTitan requires more consistent connection.</p>

<p><strong>Can I switch FSM platforms without losing customer data?</strong></p>
<p>Most platforms offer CSV exports for customers and job history, but formatting varies. Plan for 1-2 days of cleanup when switching. Jobber and Housecall Pro offer migration assistance.</p>

<p><strong>Do these apps integrate with QuickBooks?</strong></p>
<p>Jobber, Housecall Pro, and ServiceTitan all offer QuickBooks integration. Workiz integrates with QuickBooks Online but not Desktop.</p>
