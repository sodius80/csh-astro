## Do You Need This Yet?

Paving software pays for itself when you're managing enough asphalt or concrete jobs that manual estimating and crew scheduling starts cutting into production time. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're handling 1–2 small driveway jobs a month with basic spreadsheets and a calculator. A simple invoice app still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're losing bids to math errors, missing crew schedule conflicts, or tracking tonnage and equipment hours on paper.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Projul. The flat annual rate covers unlimited users, so a 5-person crew costs $399/month regardless of headcount. Compare that to Bitumio at $149/user/mo ($745 for the same crew), and the flat-rate model wins on cost for most operations.

## FAQ

<p><strong>What's the difference between paving software and general construction software?</strong></p>
<p>Paving software includes trade-specific features like asphalt cost databases, temperature tracking, and tonnage calculations. General construction software works for paving but lacks these specialized tools. Projul bridges the gap with construction-specific workflows at flat-rate pricing.</p>

<p><strong>Do I need paving-specific software or will general construction software work?</strong></p>
<p>General construction software works fine for basic scheduling and invoicing. Choose paving-specific software like Bitumio only if you do complex asphalt estimating or need specialized cost databases. For most crews, Projul at a flat annual rate is the better value.</p>

<p><strong>Why is most paving software expensive?</strong></p>
<p>Bitumio charges $149/user/mo (roughly $745/mo for a 5-person crew), while Projul covers unlimited users for $4,788/yr ($399/mo). The flat-rate model is almost always cheaper for multi-person crews. PavementSoft requires application-based pricing, which adds friction for smaller operations.</p>
