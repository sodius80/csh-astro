## Do You Need This Yet?

Paving software pays for itself when you're managing enough asphalt or concrete jobs that manual estimating and crew scheduling starts cutting into production time. Two quick checks:

- **You don't need it yet** if you're handling 1–2 small driveway jobs a month with basic spreadsheets and a calculator. A simple invoice app still works fine at that scale.
- **You need it now** if you're losing bids to math errors, missing crew schedule conflicts, or tracking tonnage and equipment hours on paper.

If you're in the middle—growing but not overwhelmed—start with Bitumio. It's the only purpose-built asphalt estimating tool here, with built-in cost databases and map measurement tools, so your bids get more accurate while you figure out scheduling workflows.

## FAQ

<p><strong>What's the difference between paving software and general construction software?</strong></p>
<p>Paving software includes trade-specific features like asphalt cost databases, temperature tracking, and tonnage calculations. General construction software works for paving but lacks these specialized tools.</p>

<p><strong>Do I need paving-specific software or will general construction software work?</strong></p>
<p>General construction software works fine for basic scheduling and invoicing. Choose paving-specific software like Bitumio if you do complex asphalt estimating or need specialized cost databases.</p>

<p><strong>Why is most paving software custom priced?</strong></p>
<p>Paving contractors vary widely in size and needs, from solo operators to large crews. Custom pricing lets vendors match features and costs to specific business requirements.</p>
