<h2>Product Reviews</h2>

<h3>1. Field ProMax -- Best for QuickBooks-connected small trade teams</h3>

<p><a href="/field-promax-review-2026/">Read the full Field ProMax review →</a></p>

<p>Field ProMax treats QuickBooks as the center of gravity, not an afterthought. Founded in 2015 in Rochester, Minnesota, the platform offers scheduling, dispatch, estimates, invoicing, and a customer approval portal with native QuickBooks Desktop and Online sync. Revenue reportedly grew 50% year-over-year, reaching $1M in 2024.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Transparent pricing at $99-239/mo, a 14-day free trial with no credit card, and QuickBooks integration that actually works. For HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and roofing shops with 1-12 techs, this is a credible alternative to overpriced enterprise platforms.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The user interface is dated, and the mobile app gets mixed reviews. There is no enterprise tier for teams beyond 12 users. Contractors who care about modern UI or deep CRM should consider Jobber or ServiceTitan instead.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Trade shops already using QuickBooks who want affordable, functional scheduling and mobile billing.</p>

<h3>2. Tallie -- Best for AI-powered estimating on a budget</h3>

<p><a href="/tallie-review-2026/">Read the full Tallie.io review →</a></p>

<p>Tallie.io is an AI-powered field service platform for small contractors. Founded by a developer who built it for his painting-contractor best friend, it offers estimating, invoicing, scheduling, CRM, and lead tracking in just six screens. The headline feature is natural language estimate generation -- describe "12x15 room, two coats, ceiling too" and the system calculates square footage, pulls live Home Depot prices, and generates a scope of work.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> AI-powered natural language estimating is genuinely differentiated -- describe the job in plain English and get a professional quote with live Home Depot pricing. The $29/mo Pro plan includes 3 seats, making it one of the most affordable entries in the category. Change order tracking is built in. The six-screen design removes the bloat that overwhelms contractors on larger platforms.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Very early-stage -- approximately 100 businesses claimed, with zero independent reviews on G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, or Reddit. No native mobile app (web-based only). QuickBooks integration depth is unverified. No enterprise runway -- growing teams must migrate eventually.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small service businesses with 1-3 users who want AI-powered estimates and simple scheduling at a low monthly cost.</p>

<h3>3. BuildOps -- Best for commercial MEP contractors</h3>

<p>BuildOps is a cloud-based service management suite built specifically for commercial mechanical, electrical, and plumbing service firms. It handles job scheduling, work order management, inventory tracking, and invoicing with a focus on recurring maintenance contracts rather than one-time construction builds.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Automated service contract renewals, MEP-specific KPI dashboards, and field-technician mobile access designed for commercial environments. The company closed a $127M Series C in March 2025, indicating serious scaling and long-term viability.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Enterprise-focused with custom pricing, limited independent third-party reviews, and a narrow trade focus. Not designed for residential or small-scale operators.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Commercial MEP service firms managing ongoing building maintenance contracts and needing trade-specific dashboards.</p>

<h3>4. GreenLite -- Best for permitting acceleration</h3>

<p>GreenLite automates construction permitting by ingesting municipal code databases, extracting required documents, and auto-populating permit applications with AI-generated checklists. The system tracks approval status in real time and alerts stakeholders to revisions. Early adopters report 30-40% reductions in permit turnaround times.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> AI-driven checklist generation reduces the manual legwork of permit preparation. Real-time tracking replaces phone-tag with building departments. The company raised a $49.5M Series B in September 2025.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Municipal coverage is patchy -- the value varies dramatically by jurisdiction. Independent third-party reviews are limited. Contractors doing simple single-jurisdiction projects may not get enough benefit.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> General contractors and developers working across multiple jurisdictions where permitting delays regularly impact schedules and budgets.</p>

<h3>5. Attentive.ai -- Best for AI-powered jobsite safety</h3>

<p>Attentive.ai applies computer vision to jobsite safety monitoring. The platform identifies PPE compliance, flags hazards in real time, and generates automated incident reports. It also surfaces productivity analytics to identify schedule inefficiencies across multiple crews.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Computer-vision safety detection addresses a real, expensive problem. The $30.5M Series B in November 2025 signals investor confidence in the market need. Automated compliance documentation could reduce both liability and insurance costs.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Significant hardware requirements make it impractical for small sites. Independent user reviews are scarce. ROI depends on existing safety program investment -- this amplifies a program rather than creating one from scratch.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Mid-to-large construction firms already investing in safety programs and needing technology to enforce and document compliance at scale.</p>
