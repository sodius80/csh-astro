<h3>1. Field ProMax -- Best for QuickBooks-connected small trade teams</h3>

<p><a href="/field-promax-review-2026/">Read the full Field ProMax review →</a></p>

<p>Field ProMax treats QuickBooks as the center of gravity, not an afterthought. Founded in 2015 in Rochester, Minnesota, the platform offers scheduling, dispatch, estimates, invoicing, and a customer approval portal with native QuickBooks Desktop and Online sync. Revenue reportedly grew 50% year-over-year, reaching $1M in 2024.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Transparent pricing at $99-239/mo, Premium additional users listed at $25/user, and QuickBooks integration that actually works. Field ProMax does not expose a self-serve trial on its current pricing page, so confirm trial and implementation terms during the demo. For HVAC, plumbing, electrical, and roofing shops with 1-12 included users, this is a credible alternative to overpriced enterprise platforms.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The user interface is dated, and the mobile app gets mixed reviews. Published tiers top out at 12 included users, so larger teams need to verify add-on costs and fit with sales. Contractors who care about modern UI or deep CRM should consider Jobber or ServiceTitan instead.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Trade shops already using QuickBooks who want affordable, functional scheduling and mobile billing.</p>

<h3>2. Tallie -- Best for AI-powered estimating on a budget</h3>

<p>Tallie.io is an AI-powered field service platform for small contractors. Founded by a developer who built it for his painting-contractor best friend, it offers estimating, invoicing, scheduling, CRM, and lead tracking in just six screens. The headline feature is natural language estimate generation -- describe "12x15 room, two coats, ceiling too" and the system calculates square footage, pulls live Home Depot prices, and generates a scope of work.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> AI-powered natural language estimating is differentiated -- describe the job in plain English and get a professional quote with live Home Depot pricing. Current public pages show a $29/mo Starter tier and a $79/mo Pro tier with 3 seats, while also using waitlist/beta language. Change order tracking is built in. The simple design removes the bloat that overwhelms contractors on larger platforms.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Very early-stage -- approximately 100 businesses claimed, with zero independent reviews on G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, or Reddit. The pricing page uses waitlist language, so access and trial terms should be confirmed before relying on it. QuickBooks integration depth is unverified. No enterprise runway -- growing teams must migrate eventually.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Small service businesses that want AI-powered estimates and simple scheduling at a low monthly cost.</p>

<h3>3. BuildOps -- Best for commercial MEP contractors</h3>

<p>BuildOps is a cloud-based service management suite built specifically for commercial mechanical, electrical, and plumbing service firms. It handles job scheduling, work order management, inventory tracking, and invoicing with a focus on recurring maintenance contracts rather than one-time construction builds.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Automated service contract renewals, MEP-specific KPI dashboards, and field-technician mobile access designed for commercial environments. The company closed a $127M Series C in March 2025, indicating serious scaling and long-term viability.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Enterprise-focused with custom pricing, limited independent third-party reviews, and a narrow trade focus. Not designed for residential or small-scale operators.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Commercial MEP service firms managing ongoing building maintenance contracts and needing trade-specific dashboards.</p>

<h3>4. GreenLite -- Best for permitting acceleration</h3>

<p>GreenLite automates construction permitting and private plan review by scanning plan sets, identifying code issues, and surfacing jurisdiction-specific guidance. Its September 2025 Series B announcement says the platform accelerates permit timelines, but contractors should treat ROI claims as vendor-reported until verified in their own jurisdictions.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> AI-driven checklist generation reduces the manual legwork of permit preparation. Real-time tracking replaces phone-tag with building departments. The company raised a $49.5M Series B in September 2025.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Municipal coverage is patchy -- the value varies dramatically by jurisdiction. Independent third-party reviews are limited. Contractors doing simple single-jurisdiction projects may not get enough benefit.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> General contractors and developers working across multiple jurisdictions where permitting delays regularly impact schedules and budgets.</p>

<h3>5. Attentive.ai -- Best for AI-powered takeoffs</h3>

<p>Attentive.ai applies AI to automated takeoffs for construction and field-service teams. The platform is positioned around Beam AI/Automeasure workflows for landscaping, paving, snow, facilities maintenance, general contractors, subcontractors, and suppliers that need bid-ready quantities faster.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Manual takeoffs are still a real bottleneck for teams bidding outdoor, facilities, and construction work. Attentive.ai publicly claims 1K+ companies, 3.5K+ users, 4.4M+ acres measured, 90K+ workable sheets, and 365K+ takeoffs, which makes it more mature than many AI-only contractor tools.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Custom pricing and demo-led evaluation make cost hard to benchmark publicly. Independent user reviews are limited, and ROI depends on bid volume and takeoff complexity rather than general field-service needs.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Contractors and construction teams with enough takeoff volume to justify automation.</p>
