<p>Roofing software has a few requirements that don't come up in other trades. Aerial measurement, because nobody is hand-measuring a 12/12 pitch. Insurance documentation for storm work, because the supplement process is its own skill set. Photo organization that's actually tied to the job, not buried in someone's camera roll. And a sales process that often happens in a driveway while the homeowner is standing next to you looking at the damage.</p>

<p>The tools that handle these things well are mostly roofing-specific. Here's what's worth using.</p>

<p><strong>Right for:</strong> Roofing contractors who need aerial measurement, insurance claim workflows, or a CRM built around the roofing sales process.</p>

<p><strong>Not for:</strong> General contractors who occasionally do roofing work. A general FSM tool like Jobber handles scheduling and invoicing fine without the roofing-specific overhead.</p>

## Do You Need This Yet?

<p>The honest answer: maybe not, if your operation is still very simple.</p>

<div class="need-check">
<p class="need-do">You probably do if:</p>
<ul class="need-list">
<li>you are doing insurance work and need supplement tracking</li>
<li>aerial measurements are becoming a regular part of your workflow</li>
<li>leads are falling through the cracks between phone calls and estimates</li>
<li>photos and documents are scattered across phones and cloud storage</li>
<li>you are running a sales team and need visibility into the pipeline</li>
</ul>
<p class="need-dont">You may not yet if:</p>
<ul class="need-list">
<li>you are still running a small crew with mostly service calls</li>
<li>you do not do insurance or storm restoration work</li>
<li>a calendar and QuickBooks are still holding together</li>
<li>you measure roofs manually or outsource occasionally</li>
</ul>
</div>

## How to Choose

<p>Here is the practical way to think about roofing software selection:</p>

<p><strong>If you need a full CRM and project management system:</strong> JobNimbus is built for roofing contractors. The sales pipeline tracks leads from first contact through signed contract. Job boards show where every project stands. The insurance supplement workflow is built in, which matters if you're doing any storm work.</p>

<p><strong>If you need fast, affordable aerial measurements:</strong> Roofr is the answer. Individual reports run $10-15, and subscription plans bring costs down further. The built-in proposal tool lets you turn a measurement into a signable proposal on the spot.</p>

<p><strong>If you are scaling past $2M in revenue:</strong> AccuLynx covers the full lifecycle — lead management, aerial measurements, material ordering from suppliers, project management, and financial reporting. It is the most comprehensive roofing-specific platform.</p>

<p><strong>If you need maximum measurement accuracy:</strong> EagleView has the best accuracy on complex roofs with multiple pitches, dormers, and valleys. For commercial or high-end residential work where measurement errors mean real money, it is the default choice.</p>

<p><strong>If you want simple scheduling without insurance workflows:</strong> Jobber handles scheduling, invoicing, and client communication well for roofing contractors doing service calls and maintenance work without storm restoration complexity.</p>

## Product Reviews

### 1. JobNimbus

<p>JobNimbus was built for roofing contractors, and that focus shows throughout the product. The sales pipeline tracks leads from first contact through signed contract. Job boards show where every project stands. Documents, photos, and measurements attach to each job. The insurance supplement workflow is built in, which matters if you're doing any storm work.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Contractors who commit to JobNimbus and actually use the CRM side describe it as the piece that finally made their sales process visible. Leads were not falling through the gaps anymore.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Setup time. Multiple reviewers mention that JobNimbus requires real configuration to work well for your specific workflow. One reviewer running a 10-person storm restoration company described spending three weeks on setup before it felt right.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Connect $149/mo, Grow $299/mo, Manage $449/mo (annual pricing).</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Roofing contractors doing insurance work or managing a sales team who need a CRM connected to job management.</p>

### 2. Roofr

<p>Roofr's core product is aerial roof measurement reports, and the pricing is aggressive compared to EagleView. Individual reports run $10 to $15. The subscription plans bring that cost down further at volume. For a contractor pulling 20 to 30 measurements a month, the subscription model makes financial sense quickly.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> The built-in proposal tool. Contractors can turn a measurement report into a professional proposal that the homeowner signs on the spot. Several reviewers specifically mention this as the feature that changed their close rate.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Accuracy is the honest limitation. EagleView is more accurate on complex roofs with multiple pitches. For standard residential roofs, Roofr is close enough that the cost difference does not justify EagleView for most operations.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Starter $49/mo, Essentials $99/mo, Pro $169/mo.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Residential roofing contractors who need affordable aerial measurements and want a proposal tool built in.</p>

### 3. AccuLynx

<p>AccuLynx is the most comprehensive roofing-specific platform on this list. It covers the full lifecycle: lead management, aerial measurements, material ordering directly from suppliers, project management, and financial reporting. For a roofing company that has outgrown a basic CRM and wants everything in one place, AccuLynx is worth evaluating seriously.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Material ordering directly from suppliers is a genuine differentiator. When measurements and material lists flow directly into purchase orders, you save hours of manual work and reduce ordering errors.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> The mobile app needs work — several contractors mention it as noticeably weaker than the desktop version. Customer service response times have been inconsistent based on reviews from the past 18 months. Pricing is not published and requires a demo conversation.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Custom pricing (requires demo). Reported $200-400/mo for small to mid-size operations.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Roofing contractors doing $2M+ in annual revenue who want a single platform for the whole business, including material ordering.</p>

### 4. EagleView

<p>EagleView has the best measurement accuracy of any aerial service on this list, and that reputation is earned. The reports are detailed and the accuracy on complex roofs with multiple pitches, dormers, and valleys is measurably better than alternatives. For commercial roofing or high-end residential where measurement errors mean real money, EagleView is the default choice.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Unmatched accuracy on complex roofs. The premium comes through in pricing, but for complex work, it often pays for itself in avoided material waste.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> Individual reports run $25 to $40 depending on the package. For straightforward residential roofs, that premium is hard to justify compared to Roofr.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Per-report pricing ($25-40) or annual subscription plans.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Roofing contractors doing complex commercial or high-end residential work where measurement accuracy directly affects material costs.</p>

### 5. Jobber

<p>Jobber isn't roofing-specific, but it's worth including for one specific situation: a roofing contractor who primarily does service calls and maintenance work, not storm restoration or complex insurance claims. For that type of operation, Jobber's scheduling, invoicing, and client communication tools handle the job well without the complexity and cost of roofing-specific platforms.</p>

<p><strong>What stands out:</strong> Clean scheduling, quoting, invoicing, and payments in one interface. Strong mobile app. No roofing-specific overhead.</p>

<p><strong>Where it falls short:</strong> No supplement tracking, no insurance documentation workflows, no adjuster-facing tools. If any significant portion of your work involves insurance claims, look elsewhere.</p>

<p><strong>Pricing:</strong> Core $49/mo, Connect $149/mo, Grow $199/mo.</p>

<p><strong>Best for:</strong> Roofing contractors doing service calls and maintenance without insurance workflows.</p>

## Bottom Line

<p>Here is the honest breakdown:</p>

<ul>
<li><strong>JobNimbus</strong> is the best all-in-one for roofing CRM and project management. Start here if you need visibility into your sales pipeline and handle insurance work.</li>
<li><strong>Roofr</strong> wins for fast, affordable aerial measurements with a built-in proposal tool. Choose it if measurements are your primary need.</li>
<li><strong>AccuLynx</strong> is the most comprehensive platform for larger operations. Only consider it when you have outgrown basic CRMs and need material ordering integration.</li>
<li><strong>EagleView</strong> is the accuracy leader for complex roofs. Choose it for commercial or high-end residential work where measurement precision matters.</li>
<li><strong>Jobber</strong> is the simple option for contractors doing service calls without insurance complexity.</li>

## FAQ

### Do I need roofing-specific software?

<p>If you do insurance work, storm restoration, or need aerial measurements regularly, yes. Roofing-specific tools handle supplement tracking, measurement workflows, and insurance documentation that general FSM tools cannot.</p>

### Can I use Jobber for roofing?

<p>Yes, if you primarily do service calls and maintenance work without insurance claims. Jobber handles scheduling, invoicing, and client communication well for straightforward roofing operations.</p>

### Is Roofr accurate enough?

<p>For standard residential roofs, Roofr is close enough that the cost difference does not justify EagleView for most operations. For complex roofs with multiple pitches, EagleView remains more accurate.</p>

### How much does AccuLynx cost?

<p>AccuLynx requires a demo for pricing. Based on contractor reviews, expect $200-400/mo for small to mid-size operations, potentially more with additional users.</p>
